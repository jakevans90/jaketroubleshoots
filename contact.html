About & Contact

Welcome to Jake Troubleshoots — a growing collection of real-world clinical engineering troubleshooting guides.

Who I Am

Hey, I’m Jake. I’ve been troubleshooting electronics for about 15 years, and for most of that time I’ve worked as a biomedical equipment technician in clinical environments.

I’ve spent my career fixing everything from simple devices to complex systems, usually under time pressure and with patient care on the line.

Why This Site Exists

Over the years, I noticed something that kept repeating itself: when equipment fails in a clinical setting, stress goes up fast — and logical troubleshooting often goes out the window.

Service manuals are great at explaining how to replace parts, but they rarely teach how to think through a failure. They also don’t always match the real-world problems we see in hospitals.

I created this site to bridge that gap. These guides aren’t just about fixing equipment — they’re about building a troubleshooting mindset that works under pressure.

What Makes These Guides Different

Instead of only listing repair steps, these guides focus on:

  • Logical troubleshooting paths
  • Common failure patterns seen in real clinical use
  • Simple checks before expensive parts are replaced
  • Clear symptom-based diagnosis
  • What usually causes wasted time and misdiagnosis

The goal is not just to fix today’s problem, but to make the next problem easier to solve.

Troubleshooting & Documentation

In clinical engineering, troubleshooting and documentation go hand in hand. A repair isn’t finished until someone else can understand what failed, what was checked, and what corrected the issue.

These guides also aim to model good documentation habits — not just “replaced part,” but meaningful notes that explain symptoms, causes, and outcomes.

Who This Site Is For

This site is built for clinical engineers and biomedical technicians who:

  • Want clearer, real-world troubleshooting guidance
  • Feel overwhelmed when equipment fails under pressure
  • Learn better from logic and examples than dense manuals
  • Want to improve both troubleshooting and documentation skills
