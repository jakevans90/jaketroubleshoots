Kendall SCD 700 Series

One or More Sleeves Not Inflating

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through simple checks when one or more sleeves connected to a Kendall SCD 700 Series unit are not inflating properly.

This may present as:

  • One leg inflates but the other does not
  • Both sleeves connected but neither inflates
  • Partial inflation or weak compression
  • Machine running but no pressure delivered

These steps focus on the most common and easily verified causes before assuming an internal pump or valve failure.

You do not need advanced technical skills — just follow the steps and observe carefully.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Confirm the Unit Is Powered and Running

  • Ensure the device is powered on.
  • Confirm there are no active alarms.
  • Verify the therapy mode is started (not just powered on).

The unit may be powered but therapy not initiated.

2. Check Sleeve and Tubing Connections

  • Confirm tubing is fully seated into the correct port.
  • Listen/feel for a “click” when inserting connectors.
  • Inspect for partially disconnected tubing.
  • Swap ports (left/right) to see if the issue follows the sleeve or stays with the port.

Loose or partially seated connectors are the most common cause of one sleeve not inflating.

3. Inspect the Tubing for Kinks or Damage

  • Check the entire length of tubing for sharp bends.
  • Ensure tubing is not pinched under bed rails or wheels.
  • Look for cracks, tears, or punctures.

Even a small leak can prevent proper inflation.

4. Inspect the Sleeves Themselves

Look for:

  • Visible tears
  • Separated seams
  • Worn Velcro preventing proper wrap

Ensure sleeves are wrapped snugly but not overly tight. A sleeve that cannot seal properly may not hold pressure.

5. Swap Sleeves

  • Disconnect both sleeves.
  • Swap the left sleeve to the right port and vice versa.
  • Observe whether the issue follows the sleeve or stays with the port.

If the problem follows the sleeve → sleeve is likely defective.
If the problem stays on the same port → internal valve or channel issue suspected.

6. Check for Alarm Messages

  • Leak detected
  • Disconnected tubing
  • Pressure fault

Record any alarms exactly as shown.

7. Listen for Pump Activity

  • When therapy starts, you should hear the internal pump cycling.

If the pump runs but no inflation occurs → possible internal valve leak or manifold issue.
If no pump sound at all → possible internal motor or board issue.

If the Sleeve Still Does Not Inflate

If the issue persists after completing the steps above, you have already:

  • Verified the unit is powered and therapy started
  • Checked tubing connections
  • Inspected for kinks and leaks
  • Examined sleeves for damage
  • Swapped sleeves to isolate the problem
  • Checked for alarms

The issue is likely internal and may involve:

  • Internal valve failure
  • Pump motor issue
  • Pressure sensor malfunction
  • Internal manifold leak

These cannot be corrected through bedside checks.

Next Steps

  • Remove the unit from clinical service
  • Clearly label it as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation by Clinical Engineering

Knowing when to escalate is part of good troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

Example: “One sleeve not inflating; right leg no compression.”

Cause

Examples:
“Tubing and sleeve inspected, swapped ports, issue remained on right port. Suspected internal valve failure.”
or
“Swapped sleeves and problem followed sleeve. Sleeve found leaking.”

Resolution

Examples:
“Replaced defective sleeve; unit returned to service.”
or
“Removed device from service; sent for bench evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Which side affected (left/right)
  • Alarm messages present (yes/no + exact wording)
  • Sleeve swapped (yes/no)
  • Issue followed sleeve or stayed with port
  • Tubing condition
  • Pump audible (yes/no)
  • Final disposition

Final Thought

Most sleeve inflation problems are caused by:

  • ✔ loose tubing connections
  • ✔ kinked lines
  • ✔ damaged sleeves
  • ✔ improperly wrapped cuffs

Completing these steps ensures you have:

  • ✔ ruled out accessory failure
  • ✔ protected patient safety
  • ✔ documented properly
  • ✔ avoided unnecessary device swaps

That is successful troubleshooting.
