GE Healthcare Aisys

Alarm: ACB HW WATCHDOG

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Aisys

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm on a GE Healthcare Aisys anesthesia machine. This alarm may appear with system malfunction behavior, failed startup, watchdog timeout, unexpected reset, checkout failure, or unreliable anesthesia machine operation.

An ACB hardware watchdog alarm should be treated as a serious system-level fault because it may indicate the Anesthesia Control Board is not operating reliably enough for clinical use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not continue using the anesthesia machine for active patient care with an unresolved ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm.
  • If the machine is connected to a patient, have anesthesia staff move the patient to another anesthesia machine or approved backup ventilation method.
  • Remove the Aisys from patient use if the alarm occurs during setup, startup, checkout, or operation.
  • Use an alternate anesthesia machine if the room is needed for a case.

Expected outcome: Patient care is supported by a safe, functional anesthesia system before troubleshooting continues.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact alarm displayed is ACB HW WATCHDOG.
  • Observe whether the alarm occurs during boot-up, power-on self-test, system checkout, immediately after restart, or intermittently during use.
  • Check for associated symptoms such as system malfunction message, failed startup, frozen display, ventilator unavailable, checkout failure, unexpected reset, or loss of normal machine operation.
  • Document whether the alarm is repeatable or was only reported one time by staff.

Expected outcome: The reported condition is confirmed before deeper troubleshooting or repair escalation.

Check Power Source First

  • Verify the anesthesia machine is connected to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Confirm the power cord is fully seated at the wall outlet and at the machine.
  • Check that the outlet is not controlled by a wall switch or affected by a failed emergency power circuit.
  • If safe and allowed by facility procedure, test the outlet with another device or approved outlet tester.
  • Move the machine to a known-good outlet if unstable power is suspected.

Expected outcome: The machine receives stable AC power and simple power-related causes are ruled out.

Inspect for Obvious Physical or Environmental Issues

  • Inspect the machine exterior for visible damage, fluid intrusion, impact damage, loose panels, or signs of overheating.
  • Check for unusual odor, abnormal heat, fan noise changes, or evidence that the machine was recently moved or struck.
  • Look for anything blocking vents or restricting airflow around the machine.
  • Do not open the unit or perform internal disassembly during basic troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: No obvious external damage or environmental cause is found.

Perform a Controlled Reboot

  • Confirm the machine is not being used on a patient.
  • Power the Aisys down using the normal shutdown process when possible.
  • Wait briefly before restoring power.
  • Power the system back on and observe startup behavior.
  • Do not repeatedly power cycle the unit without purpose.

Expected outcome: The machine boots normally and the ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm does not return.

Repeat Startup Observation

  • After reboot, observe whether the alarm returns during boot-up, self-test, or checkout.
  • Watch for repeated system malfunction messages, failed startup, frozen screens, or unexpected resets.
  • Record whether the alarm clears temporarily or returns consistently.

Expected outcome: The issue is identified as either cleared after reboot or repeatable during startup or checkout.

Run Required Checkout Before Returning to Service

  • If the alarm clears, perform the required anesthesia machine checkout.
  • Confirm ventilation, monitoring, gas delivery, alarm function, and system readiness are normal.
  • Do not return the machine to service based only on the alarm disappearing.
  • If checkout fails or the alarm returns, remove the machine from service.

Expected outcome: The machine passes checkout without ACB HW WATCHDOG or other system malfunction alarms before clinical use.

Determine Whether the Issue Is External or Device-Related

  • Review whether AC power was verified.
  • Confirm the power cord was checked and secure.
  • Confirm no obvious physical damage, fluid intrusion, overheating, or airflow issue was found.
  • Confirm the alarm was observed or reproduced.
  • Confirm a controlled reboot was attempted.
  • If the alarm returns or the system remains abnormal, treat the condition as an internal device fault.

Expected outcome: External and simple causes are ruled out before repair escalation.

If the Problem Persists

If the ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm returns after reboot, external causes such as power source problems, loose power connection, visible damage, overheating, and basic environmental issues have been ruled out.

A persistent ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm may indicate a likely failure involving the Anesthesia Control Board hardware watchdog function or related ACB operation.

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from service.
  • Label the machine Out of Service.
  • Do not allow clinical use until the machine is repaired and fully tested.
  • Send the unit for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Escalate for Anesthesia Control Board evaluation or replacement if the alarm persists after service review.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. This alarm is not something to work around during patient care.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this alarm on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup anesthesia machine or approved backup ventilation method first. An unresolved system malfunction alarm should always be treated as a patient safety risk.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Aisys anesthesia machine reported ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm during startup/checkout. Staff reported system malfunction and machine was removed from patient use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm confirmed during startup. AC power source and power cord were checked. No obvious external damage or environmental issue found. Controlled reboot attempted, but alarm returned."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed anesthesia machine from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for repair/bench evaluation. Anesthesia Control Board evaluation recommended due to persistent ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

The ACB HW WATCHDOG alarm should be handled as a serious system malfunction. Start with patient safety, confirm the alarm, check simple external causes, attempt a controlled reboot, and escalate when the alarm persists. Clear documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
