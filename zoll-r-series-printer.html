ZOLL R Series Defibrillator

Printer Not Printing / Paper Jam

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

ZOLL

Model

R Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through simple checks when a ZOLL R Series defibrillator’s printer will not print or reports a paper jam. These steps focus on the most common and easiest things to verify first before assuming the printer or device has failed.

You do not need advanced technical skills to perform these checks.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Check the Paper Roll

  • Ensure there is a paper roll installed in the printer.
  • Confirm that the paper is inserted correctly with the leading edge feeding into the printer mechanism.
  • Replace the roll if it is missing or empty.

2. Inspect for Paper Jams

  • Open the printer cover and remove any crumpled or stuck paper.
  • Check for small bits of torn paper that could block the printer path.
  • Make sure the paper path is clear before closing the cover.

3. Verify Printer Door is Closed Properly

  • The printer will not operate if the cover or door is not fully closed.
  • Close the printer door until it clicks into place.

4. Try a Known-Good Paper Roll (If Available)

  • If the printer still fails to print, replace the paper roll with a known-good roll.
  • Attempt a test print or generate a report from the device.

5. Observe the Device Display

  • Note any error messages, such as: “Paper Jam”, “Printer Error”, or “Replace Paper”.
  • Record the message for documentation and trend tracking.

If the Printer Still Does Not Work

If the printer still will not print after completing the steps above, that’s okay — you’ve already addressed the most common causes.

You have already:

  • confirmed paper is installed correctly
  • cleared any jams
  • closed the printer cover properly
  • tried a known-good paper roll

The best next step is to:

  • remove the printer from clinical use if necessary
  • label it as Out of Service
  • escalate for repair or bench evaluation

Recognizing when to escalate prevents further damage and ensures documentation is accurate.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the user.

Example: “Printer will not print / paper jam.”

Cause

What you observed or determined during troubleshooting.

Example: “Paper roll installed, but printer still displays jam error. Paper path cleared and cover closed. Suspected printer mechanism failure.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Replaced paper roll and cleared jam; printer now functional.”
or
“Tagged printer out of service and sent for repair.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Paper roll installed: yes / no
  • Jam cleared: yes / no
  • Printer cover closed: yes / no
  • Test print performed: yes / no
  • Display message
  • Final status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Printer errors often stem from paper jams or misfeeds. Addressing the simplest causes first protects the device and ensures accurate reporting.

Completing these steps means you have:

  • ✔ ruled out common issues
  • ✔ protected patient safety
  • ✔ documented properly
  • ✔ avoided unnecessary guesswork

That is successful troubleshooting.
