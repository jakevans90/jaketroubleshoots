Kendall SCD 700 Series

Power Issue – Will Not Turn On

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through simple checks when a Kendall SCD 700 Series unit does not power on. These steps focus on the most common and easily verified causes before assuming an internal failure.

You do not need advanced technical skills — just follow the steps and observe carefully.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Confirm Power Source

  • Ensure the unit is plugged into a live AC outlet.
  • Test the outlet with another device to confirm power availability.
  • If using the battery option (if applicable), verify batteries are installed correctly and fully charged.

A disconnected, depleted, or incorrect power source is the most common reason the unit won’t power on.

2. Check the Power Switch

  • Ensure the power switch is fully in the “ON” position.
  • Listen or look for any indicator lights.
  • Sometimes the switch may feel “on” but have poor internal contact.

3. Inspect the Power Cord and Connectors

  • Examine the cord for frays, cuts, or exposed wires.
  • Confirm all connectors are clean, free from debris, and fully seated.
  • Replace any damaged cord immediately — do not operate with exposed wires.

4. Test With a Known-Good Outlet or Battery

  • Plug the unit into a different, verified outlet.
  • If battery-operated, swap with a fully charged battery pack if available.

This eliminates the power source as the cause.

5. Observe Indicator Lights or Sounds

  • Kendall SCD 700 units often have LED indicators or audible beeps.
  • No lights may indicate a blown fuse, internal PCB failure, or battery problem.
  • Note any unusual behaviors (clicks, beeps, flickering) when attempting to power on.

6. Check Safety Interlocks

Ensure compression sleeves and tubing are correctly connected. Some units have interlocks that prevent power-on if accessories are not properly seated.

7. Consider Internal Fuses or Circuitry

Certain SCD 700 units have an internal fuse accessible through a service panel. Only inspect if you are trained or authorized; improper handling can be hazardous. If unsure, skip and escalate to Clinical Engineering or the manufacturer.

If the Device Still Won’t Power On

If the unit remains unresponsive after completing all steps above, you have already:

  • Confirmed the power source
  • Verified switch operation
  • Inspected the cord and connectors
  • Tested alternative outlets or batteries
  • Checked indicator lights and accessory connections

At this point, the issue is likely internal (fuse blown, PCB failure, or battery fault) and cannot be corrected through bedside checks.

Next Steps:

  • Remove the unit from clinical service
  • Clearly label it as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation by Clinical Engineering or Cardinal Health

Knowing when to escalate is part of good troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

Example: “Kendall SCD 700 will not power on; no indicator lights.”

Cause

Example: “Power source verified, switch functional, cord and connectors intact, unit remains unresponsive. Suspected internal electronics failure (fuse or PCB).”

Resolution

Examples:

  • “Removed device from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for repair.”
  • “Tagged for bench evaluation by CE department.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • AC or battery powered
  • Outlet verified (yes/no)
  • Power switch behavior
  • Indicator lights observed (none, blinking, solid)
  • Any unusual sounds when powering on
  • Final disposition (returned to service, sent for repair)

Final Thought

Not every power issue can be corrected at the bedside. Completing these steps ensures you have:

  • ✔ ruled out simple external causes
  • ✔ protected patient safety
  • ✔ documented properly
  • ✔ avoided unnecessary guesswork

That is successful troubleshooting.
