Kendall SCD 700 Series

Pressure Too Low or Inconsistent

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through simple checks when a Kendall SCD 700 Series unit delivers pressure that appears too low, weak, inconsistent between cycles, or uneven between legs.

These steps focus on the most common and easily verified causes before assuming internal pump, valve, or pressure sensor failure.

You do not need advanced technical skills — just follow the steps and observe carefully.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Confirm Therapy Mode and Settings

  • Verify the correct therapy mode is selected (calf vs. thigh).
  • Confirm therapy has fully started and is cycling normally.
  • Ensure no standby or paused state is active.

Incorrect mode or incomplete startup can mimic low pressure.

2. Check Sleeve Application

  • Ensure sleeves are wrapped snugly and evenly.
  • Avoid loose wrapping.
  • Confirm correct sleeve size for the patient.

Oversized sleeves often feel like low pressure even when functioning normally.

3. Inspect Tubing for Leaks or Kinks

  • Check entire tubing length for cracks or pinholes.
  • Inspect for loose fittings.
  • Look for sharp bends or kinks.
  • Ensure tubing is not pinched under bed rails or equipment.

Even a small air leak can significantly reduce delivered pressure.

4. Check Connections at the Ports

  • Fully reseat tubing connectors.
  • Listen for air escaping at connection points.
  • Gently wiggle connectors while unit is cycling to check for intermittent leaks.

Loose fittings are a common cause of inconsistent pressure.

5. Swap Sleeves and Tubing

  • Swap left and right sleeves.
  • Replace tubing with a known-good set if available.

If the problem follows the sleeve → sleeve likely leaking.
If the problem follows the tubing → tubing defective.
If the issue stays with the same port → possible internal valve issue.

6. Listen to the Pump Cycle

  • Pump should sound consistent during inflation.
  • Weak, struggling, or unusually loud pump sounds may indicate internal wear.
  • Rapid cycling without building pressure may indicate leak or valve issue.

7. Check for Alarms or Error Messages

  • Leak detected
  • Pressure fault
  • System error

Record exact alarm wording if present.

8. Compare Both Channels

  • Swap tubing between ports.
  • Observe whether low pressure follows the accessory or remains with the port.

If Pressure Remains Low or Inconsistent

If the issue persists after completing the steps above, the likely causes include:

  • Internal pump wear
  • Valve manifold failure
  • Pressure sensor drift
  • Internal air leak within the unit

These cannot be corrected through bedside troubleshooting.

Next Steps

  • Remove the unit from clinical service
  • Clearly label it as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to escalate is part of good troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

Example: “Compression pressure weak and inconsistent on left leg.”

Cause

Examples:
“Sleeves and tubing inspected and swapped; issue remained on left port. Suspected internal valve or pump degradation.”

or

“Issue followed sleeve when swapped. Sleeve found leaking.”

Resolution

Examples:
“Replaced defective sleeve; unit returned to service.”
“Removed device from service; sent for bench evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Which side affected (left/right/both)
  • Alarm messages (exact wording)
  • Sleeve size used
  • Sleeve swapped: yes / no
  • Tubing replaced: yes / no
  • Pump audible and consistent: yes / no
  • Final disposition

Final Thought

Most low-pressure complaints are caused by loose tubing connections, micro-leaks in sleeves, incorrect sleeve sizing, or partially seated connectors.

Completing these steps means you have:

  • ✔ ruled out accessory causes
  • ✔ protected patient safety
  • ✔ documented properly
  • ✔ avoided unnecessary device replacement

That is successful troubleshooting.
