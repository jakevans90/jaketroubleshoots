GE Healthcare MAC VU360

Printer Issue – Not Printing / Poor Print Quality

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

MAC VU360

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through steps to identify and resolve issues when the MAC VU360 printer either does not print at all or produces poor-quality printouts.

These steps focus on the most common and easiest checks first, before advancing to more involved troubleshooting.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Confirm the Printer Is Enabled

  • Access the MAC VU360 menu and verify that the internal printer function is enabled.
  • If disabled, turn the printer on through the settings menu.
  • Try a controlled print command to test.

2. Check Paper Supply

  • Open the printer compartment and confirm correct paper is loaded (thermal paper if applicable).
  • Ensure paper is clean, flat, and correctly oriented.
  • Replace any paper that looks old, curled, or dirty.

3. Inspect Printer Roll and Sensors

  • Check the paper roll for tears or dust buildup.
  • Clean the paper path and sensors with a soft dry cloth or air duster.
  • Verify that sensors firmly detect paper presence.

4. Check Print Quality Settings

  • Navigate to print quality settings in the system menu.
  • Ensure contrast/density settings are at default or appropriate levels.
  • Try increasing density slightly if output is faint.

5. Perform a Printer Self-Test

Print the internal self-test page:

  • Access the printer test option from the system menu.
  • Observe if the self-test prints correctly.
  • If self-test fails or comes out poorly, suspect printer hardware or thermal head issue.

6. Check for Error Messages

  • Watch the monitor for any printer error codes.
  • Record the exact message for documentation.
  • Consult service manual for the specific code meaning.

If the Printer Still Does Not Work

If the issue persists after the steps above, the problem is likely internal (printer mechanism, thermal print head, connection board, etc.).

At this point basic bedside checks are exhausted and further service evaluation is required.

You have already:

  • verified printer settings
  • checked paper supply
  • cleaned sensors and path
  • attempted quality adjustments
  • triggered a self-test

The next steps are to:

  • remove the device from clinical use
  • tag it Out of Service
  • send it for bench service evaluation

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the user.

Example: “Printer will not print or prints very light and poor quality.”

Cause

What you observed or determined during troubleshooting.

Example: “Printer self-test fails and output is blank with appropriate paper loaded. Paper feed and sensors checked; no error resolved. Suspected thermal head failure.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Removed unit from service and sent for repair.”
or
“Printer mechanism replaced on bench and tested successfully.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Paper type and source
  • Printer self-test result
  • Error messages/codes
  • Steps already attempted
  • Final status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Troubleshooting printing issues often comes down to confirming proper consumables, settings, and basics before assuming an internal failure. Documenting every step improves handoff and repair accuracy.

That is successful troubleshooting.
