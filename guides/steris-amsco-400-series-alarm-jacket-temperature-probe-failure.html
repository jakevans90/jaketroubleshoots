STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Jacket Temperature Probe Failure – RTD probe JT output outside normal range

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Alarm: Jacket Temperature Probe Failure – RTD probe JT output outside normal range condition, which causes the sterilizer to abort the cycle due to invalid jacket temperature readings. This prevents proper heating and compromises sterilization control and safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service immediately if this alarm occurs
  • Do not use any instruments from an aborted cycle

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows: Jacket Temperature Probe Failure (Alarm 213)
  • Observe if the cycle automatically aborts
  • Check if jacket temperature reads 0.0°F or an abnormal value
  • Determine if the alarm occurs at startup or during heat-up

Inspect Accessible Probe Wiring Connections

  • Check visible wiring connections associated with the jacket temperature probe
  • Look for loose, damaged, or disconnected wiring

Check for Signs of Moisture or Damage

  • Inspect accessible areas for moisture intrusion or corrosion
  • Look for physical damage near probe connection points

Perform a Power Reset

  • Power OFF the sterilizer
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Power ON and re-evaluate

Isolate the Source of the Problem

  • If wiring issues are found, suspect accessory or wiring-related causes
  • If alarm persists with good wiring, suspect probe failure (RTD JT)
  • If probe and wiring appear intact, consider calibration drift or control issue
  • Look for fixed, erratic, or out-of-range temperature readings
  • Check if alarm returns immediately after reset

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks are complete and the alarm remains, internal causes are likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for repair or qualified bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot temperature-related alarms on an active patient load. Always move instruments to a backup sterilization method before removing the unit from service.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed Alarm 213 – Jacket Temperature Probe Failure. Cycle aborted with jacket temperature reading 0.0°F."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"RTD probe signal out of range. External wiring verified intact, indicating likely failed probe or calibration/control issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Unit removed from service. Power cycled with no change. Tagged out and sent for repair and probe evaluation/calibration."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Temperature probe alarms directly impact sterilization accuracy and patient safety. Always rule out simple wiring issues first, but recognize that persistent probe alarms typically indicate sensor or control failure requiring escalation. Proper documentation supports safe and efficient repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
