Medtronic Nellcor PM100N

Battery Not Charging or Reduced Runtime

Asset Type

Pulse Oximeter

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Nellcor PM100N

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Nellcor PM100N battery does not charge properly or has reduced runtime. These issues are often caused by external factors such as power supply problems, loose connections, or battery wear before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify AC Power Source

  • Confirm the outlet is functional using a known working device.
  • Check if the unit powers on with AC connected.
  • Why: A faulty outlet or no AC input will prevent battery charging.

Inspect Power Adapter and Cord

  • Ensure the correct Medtronic power adapter is being used.
  • Check for frayed cables, bent connectors, or loose connections.
  • Reseat both ends of the power cord firmly.
  • Why: Damaged or incompatible adapters can prevent proper charging.

Check Charging Indicator

  • Observe the battery or charging indicator on the device.
  • Confirm whether it shows charging status when plugged in.
  • Why: Helps determine if the device is recognizing external power.

Power Cycle the Device

  • Turn the unit off, disconnect AC power, wait 30 seconds, then reconnect and power on.
  • Why: Resets minor firmware or charging logic issues.

Test with Known Good Power Supply (if available)

  • Swap with a confirmed working PM100N power adapter.
  • Why: Rules out power supply failure without opening the device.

Inspect Battery Seating (if externally accessible)

  • If the battery compartment is accessible, ensure the battery is properly seated.
  • Look for signs of corrosion, damage, or swelling.
  • Why: Poor contact or battery degradation can prevent charging.

Evaluate Runtime

  • Fully charge the device (if possible), then run it on battery power.
  • Compare runtime to expected performance.
  • Why: Determines if the battery is holding a charge or has degraded.

Check for Heat or Unusual Behavior

  • Monitor for excessive heat near the battery area during charging.
  • Note any unusual smells or warning messages.
  • Why: Indicates potential battery failure or internal charging issues.

If the Problem Persists

If the battery still does not charge or runtime remains significantly reduced after completing these steps, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely due to:

  • Battery end-of-life
  • Internal charging circuit failure

At this point, the device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and aligns with proper Clinical Engineering practices.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot battery or power issues while the device is in use on a patient. Always transition the patient to another monitor before removing the device from service.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Unit reported not charging and shutting off quickly when unplugged.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Battery would not hold charge; device powered on with AC but lost power immediately when unplugged. Verified with known good power adapter.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Removed from service and sent for battery replacement and further evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified functional
  • Power adapter inspected or swapped
  • Charging indicator status observed
  • Battery condition (swelling, corrosion, loose fit)
  • Runtime test results
  • Any heat, odor, or abnormal behavior
  • Final device status (removed from service or returned to use)

Final Thought

Battery and charging issues are often straightforward when approached methodically. Always start with external power checks before suspecting internal failure. Prioritizing patient safety and documenting findings clearly ensures efficient repair decisions and reliable equipment performance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
