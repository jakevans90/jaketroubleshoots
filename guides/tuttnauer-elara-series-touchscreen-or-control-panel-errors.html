Tuttnauer Elara Series

Touchscreen or Control Panel Errors

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

Tuttnauer

Model

Elara Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of touchscreen or control panel malfunction, ensuring cycle control, operator input, and patient instrument safety are maintained.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Staff Safety

  • Confirm the sterilizer chamber is not pressurized and no personnel are near the door before interacting with the control panel. Prevent burns or injury.

Power Cycle the Device

  • Action: Turn off the sterilizer, wait 30–60 seconds, then power back on.
  • Expected Outcome: Temporary touchscreen glitches or frozen displays may clear.

Inspect the Touchscreen Surface

  • Action: Ensure screen is clean, dry, and free of debris or condensation.
  • Expected Outcome: Proper touch response restored if dirt or moisture was blocking contact.

Check Connections

  • Action: Inspect visible connectors between the control panel and main PCB for secure seating.
  • Expected Outcome: Loose cables can cause intermittent input or error messages.

Verify Electrical Supply

  • Action: Confirm the sterilizer is connected to a stable outlet and line voltage is within manufacturer specs.
  • Expected Outcome: Voltage fluctuations can cause touchscreen or panel errors.

Observe Error Messages

  • Action: Record any displayed error codes or unusual behavior.
  • Expected Outcome: Specific codes may indicate sensor, panel, or firmware issues.

Test Alternate Inputs

  • Action: If available, use physical buttons or external interface to navigate menus.
  • Expected Outcome: Confirms whether the touchscreen or the panel logic is at fault.

Firmware / Software Check

  • Action: Verify device firmware is up to date; if not, update per manufacturer guidance.
  • Expected Outcome: Resolves software-related touchscreen errors.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have been exhausted; the issue may be internal (panel PCB or touchscreen failure).

Remove the device from service.

Label “Out of Service” and send for manufacturer repair or bench evaluation.

Stopping here prevents further damage and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot the touchscreen during an active sterilization cycle. Move instruments to another sterilizer if immediate processing is required.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Touchscreen intermittently unresponsive and displays error messages during cycle selection."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Device firmware outdated; no external connection or power issues found."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Power cycled, cleaned touchscreen, verified connections, documented error codes, and sent device for manufacturer firmware update and evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Power outlet verified and stable
  • Screen cleaned and dry
  • Error codes recorded
  • Touch response inconsistent
  • Device status: Out of Service pending repair

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting balances safety, logic, and escalation. Systematic checks rule out common external causes, while timely removal from service preserves patient safety and prevents further equipment damage. Accurate documentation supports repair and regulatory compliance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
