Verathon GlideScope

Blade Camera / Image Quality Issues

Asset Type

Video Laryngoscope

Manufacturer

Verathon

Model

GlideScope

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the GlideScope video laryngoscope blade produces poor, blurry, distorted, or no image on the monitor. It focuses on external, easily verifiable causes—such as lens contamination, connection issues, or monitor settings—before assuming internal camera failure. The goal is to restore clear visualization for safe patient use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check the Power and Monitor

  • Ensure the monitor is powered on and fully charged (if battery-operated) or connected to power.
  • Confirm the monitor input matches the GlideScope source.
  • Why: Image issues can appear if the monitor is not functioning properly.

Inspect the Blade Lens

  • Examine the blade tip lens for debris, condensation, blood, or coating residue.
  • Clean gently using a soft, lint-free cloth with manufacturer-approved disinfectant.
  • Why: Even a small smudge can blur or obscure the image.

Check the Cable Connection

  • Verify the blade-to-handle connection is secure; remove and reconnect if loose.
  • Inspect the cable for kinks, frays, or visible damage.
  • Why: Poor connection or damaged cable can cause intermittent or distorted video.

Test With Another Compatible Blade

  • Swap the suspected blade with a known-working blade on the same handle and monitor.
  • Why: Helps determine if the issue is blade-specific or handle/monitor-related.

Test With Another Monitor

  • If available, connect the same blade and handle to a different monitor.
  • Why: Confirms whether the monitor itself is causing image issues.

Check the Environment

  • Ensure the device is not exposed to extreme lighting conditions that can affect camera visibility.

If the Problem Persists

If the above steps do not restore image quality, the issue is likely internal (camera sensor or electronics).

  • Remove the device from service immediately.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send the blade or handle for manufacturer repair or bench evaluation.

Stopping before internal disassembly is proper and safe troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not use a blade with poor image quality on a patient.
  • Move the patient to another functional GlideScope or alternative airway device before attempting further troubleshooting.
  • Always prioritize patient safety and clear visualization.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“GlideScope blade shows blurry, distorted, or no image.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed smudged lens, loose connection, or tested blade confirmed the image issue.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Cleaned lens, secured connections, tested with known-good blade and monitor. If problem persists, removed from service and sent for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Lens cleanliness verified
  • Blade and handle connections checked
  • Cable inspected for damage
  • Image tested on multiple monitors
  • Final device status: Out of Service if unresolved

Final Thought

Thorough, logic-based troubleshooting ensures patient safety and efficient escalation. Proper documentation using the CCR method allows for tracking issues, reducing repeated problems, and demonstrating effective Clinical Engineering intervention.

That is successful troubleshooting.
