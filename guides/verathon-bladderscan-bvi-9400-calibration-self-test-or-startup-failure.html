Verathon BladderScan BVI 9400

Calibration, self-test, or startup failure

Asset Type

Bladder Scanner

Manufacturer

Verathon

Model

BladderScan BVI 9400

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting BladderScan BVI 9400 startup, self-test, or calibration problems caused by power, probe, battery, cradle, or user-accessible setup issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the BladderScan BVI 9400 while it is being used for active patient assessment.

Action:

  • Remove the scanner from patient use if it will not start, fails self-test, displays calibration-related errors, or gives unreliable scan behavior.
  • Use another bladder scanner or approved clinical assessment method if bladder volume measurement is needed.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not delayed or based on an unreliable device.

Why it matters: A bladder scanner that fails startup, self-test, or calibration checks may produce inaccurate or unavailable measurements.

Verify the Reported Failure

Confirm exactly what the device is doing before assuming a hardware failure.

Action:

  • Power the unit on and observe the startup sequence.
  • Check whether the issue is unit will not power on.
  • Check whether the issue is startup freezes.
  • Check whether the issue is self-test fails.
  • Check whether the issue is calibration message appears.
  • Check whether the issue is probe is not recognized.
  • Check whether the issue is screen is blank or dim.
  • Check whether the issue is device powers off unexpectedly.
  • Check whether the issue is error returns after restart.

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified and documented.

If the unit starts normally after restart and passes checks, return it to service only after confirming normal scan operation.

Check Battery Charge and Battery Seating

Low or unstable battery power can cause startup and self-test problems.

Action:

  • Confirm the battery is properly installed and seated.
  • Place the unit on the charging base or connect it to the approved charging setup.
  • Allow the battery to charge if the charge level is low.
  • Check for charging indicators on the unit or charger.

Expected outcome: The unit powers on normally and completes startup with adequate battery power.

If the issue resolves after charging or reseating the battery, document the finding and stop.

Inspect the Charging Base and Power Supply

A charging or power issue may appear as a scanner failure.

Action:

  • Verify the charger is plugged into a known-good outlet.
  • Inspect the power adapter, cord, and charger contacts for damage, looseness, bent pins, fluid residue, or corrosion.
  • Test the outlet with another device or outlet tester if appropriate.
  • If available, try a known-good compatible charger or charging base.

Expected outcome: The scanner charges consistently and powers on normally.

If the unit only fails with one charger or outlet, remove the faulty accessory from service.

Inspect the Probe and Cable Connection

The BVI 9400 depends on the probe and cable connection for proper startup and functional checks.

Action:

  • Inspect the probe, cable, connector, and strain relief for visible damage.
  • Look for bent pins, cracked housings, dried gel, fluid intrusion, or loose connections.
  • Confirm the probe connector is fully seated.
  • Disconnect and reconnect the probe if the design allows normal user-level reconnection.

Expected outcome: The device recognizes the probe and completes startup or self-test.

If the probe or cable is damaged, remove the device or accessory from service and send it for repair evaluation.

Check for Contamination or Fluid Intrusion

Fluid residue can interfere with buttons, contacts, charging, or probe communication.

Action:

  • Inspect the scanner body, probe, charger contacts, and battery area for cleaning fluid, gel buildup, residue, corrosion, or signs of liquid entry.
  • Clean only according to approved facility and manufacturer cleaning practices.
  • Allow the device to dry fully before retesting.

Expected outcome: External contamination is removed and the unit starts normally.

If fluid intrusion, corrosion, or unusual residue is found inside user-accessible areas, stop troubleshooting and escalate.

Check Display, Buttons, and Keypad Response

Startup may appear failed if the display or buttons are not responding correctly.

Action:

  • Confirm the power button responds normally.
  • Check whether buttons feel stuck, damaged, unusually soft, or non-responsive.
  • Observe whether the screen backlight turns on, flickers, or remains blank.

Expected outcome: Controls respond normally and the display shows the startup screen.

If buttons are stuck or the display remains blank despite confirmed power, remove the unit from service.

Restart the Device

A temporary software lockup may clear with a proper restart.

Action:

  • Power the unit off.
  • Wait at least 30 seconds.
  • Power the unit back on.
  • Observe whether startup and self-test complete normally.

Expected outcome: The device boots normally and no self-test or calibration error returns.

If the failure returns repeatedly, continue troubleshooting.

Confirm the Device Is Not Being Used Outside Normal Conditions

Environmental or handling issues can contribute to startup or calibration failures.

Action:

  • Confirm the scanner is dry, clean, and at normal room temperature.
  • Do not test immediately after the device has been stored in a very cold, hot, or humid area.
  • Allow the unit to acclimate if it was recently transported or stored outside the clinical area.

Expected outcome: The device starts and self-checks normally under normal operating conditions.

If the problem resolves after acclimation and drying, document the condition found.

Attempt User-Level Calibration Only If Approved

Do not perform service-level calibration steps unless your department is trained, authorized, and has the required tools.

Action:

  • Check whether the issue requires routine user-accessible calibration or a calibration verification process.
  • Use only approved calibration accessories, targets, or procedures available to Clinical Engineering.
  • Do not bypass calibration warnings or clear errors without correcting the cause.

Expected outcome: The scanner completes the approved calibration or verification process successfully.

If calibration fails again, remove the unit from service.

Check for Repeated or Intermittent Startup Failure

Intermittent failures should be treated seriously even if the device occasionally starts.

Action:

  • Power cycle the device several times.
  • Confirm whether the failure happens every time or only intermittently.
  • Check whether the problem changes when the probe cable, charger connection, or battery is gently repositioned.

Expected outcome: The device starts consistently without cable movement, power interruption, or repeated errors.

If startup depends on cable position, battery movement, or repeated attempts, the device should not be returned to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the BladderScan BVI 9400 still fails calibration, self-test, or startup after basic external checks, the likely causes may include internal battery connection issues, probe electronics failure, display/control failure, charging circuit problems, software corruption, or internal system board failure.

At that point:

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Do not use it for patient scanning.
  • Send it for repair, bench evaluation, or vendor service according to facility process.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue using a bladder scanner that cannot reliably complete startup, self-test, or calibration checks.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot bladder scanner startup or calibration failures during active patient care. Move clinical staff to another working scanner first. A scanner that fails self-test or calibration should be treated as unreliable until the issue is corrected and normal operation is verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Verathon BladderScan BVI 9400 would not complete startup and displayed a calibration or self-test failure before scanning."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Clinical Engineering found the scanner battery charged and the outlet tested good, but the device continued to fail startup/self-test after probe reconnection and restart."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the BladderScan BVI 9400 from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for bench evaluation/vendor repair due to persistent startup/self-test failure."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior, including any calibration or self-test message displayed
  • Accessories swapped, including charging base, power supply, battery, probe, or cable
  • Power behavior, including outlet tested and confirmed good, battery charge level or charging status, charging base indicator behavior, power supply condition, and whether the failure occurs at startup or during scan setup
  • Environmental factors, including unusual heat, smell, fluid residue, corrosion, storage temperature, humidity, or whether the issue is constant or intermittent
  • Indicator lights, including charging base indicator behavior, display behavior, and button/keypad response
  • Final device status, including whether the device was returned to service, removed from service, labeled Out of Service, or sent for repair evaluation

Final Thought

Calibration, self-test, and startup failures should be approached logically. Confirm patient safety first, then rule out battery, charging, probe, cable, connector, and environmental causes. If the device still cannot pass startup or self-check, remove it from service and document the failure clearly so repair can start with useful information.

That is successful troubleshooting.
