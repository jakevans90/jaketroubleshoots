B. Braun Outlook 400ES

Battery Will Not Charge Or Runtime Is Too Short

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

B. Braun

Model

Outlook 400ES

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Outlook 400ES battery charging, short runtime, poor charge retention, or power-source related battery complaints.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

If the pump is connected to a patient and battery operation is unreliable, do not continue troubleshooting on the active patient.

Transfer the infusion to another verified pump or connect the pump to approved AC power if clinically appropriate and safe.

Expected outcome: Patient therapy is maintained without depending on a questionable battery.

Why it matters: Infusion pumps must maintain reliable power during transport, power interruptions, or bedside use.

Confirm the Reported Battery Complaint

Ask clinical staff what was observed:

  • Pump would not charge
  • Battery icon did not increase
  • Pump shut down quickly on battery
  • Low battery alarm occurred shortly after unplugging
  • Pump only works on AC power
  • Battery runtime is shorter than expected

Expected outcome: The complaint is clearly identified before testing.

If the issue was caused by normal low battery use and the pump charges normally afterward, document and return to service if all checks pass.

Inspect the Pump Exterior Before Power Testing

Check the pump housing, battery compartment area, power connector, pole clamp area, and keypad/display for damage, cracks, fluid intrusion, residue, or signs of impact.

Expected outcome: No physical damage, contamination, swelling, odor, or evidence of liquid exposure is found.

If damage, fluid intrusion, unusual heat, or burnt smell is present, remove the pump from service and send it for repair evaluation.

Verify the AC Power Source

Plug the pump into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.

Confirm the outlet has power and is not controlled by a wall switch, power strip, loose receptacle, or failed emergency power circuit.

Expected outcome: The pump powers normally from AC power and indicates AC connection or charging behavior.

If the pump charges normally from a different outlet, the issue is external to the pump. Stop and document the outlet-related finding.

Check the Power Cord and Connection

Inspect the AC power cord for cuts, bent blades, loose strain relief, cracked insulation, or intermittent connection.

Reseat the cord firmly at the pump and outlet.

If available, test with a known-good compatible power cord.

Expected outcome: The pump detects AC power consistently without flickering, intermittent charging, or power loss when the cord is gently repositioned.

If a known-good cord resolves the issue, replace the defective cord and stop.

Allow Adequate Charging Time

Leave the Outlook 400ES connected to AC power long enough for the battery to recover from a low or depleted state.

Confirm that the charge indicator, battery icon, or charging status behaves normally.

Expected outcome: Battery charge level increases over time, and low battery indications clear.

If the battery was deeply discharged and recovers after charging, verify runtime before returning the pump to service.

Check for Intermittent AC Detection

With the pump connected to AC power, gently move the cord near the outlet end and pump connection while watching for AC power loss, display changes, alarms, or charge interruption.

Do not stress the connector or force movement.

Expected outcome: AC power remains stable.

If AC detection drops in and out, remove the pump from service for power inlet, connector, or internal power-path evaluation.

Verify Battery Runtime Under Safe Test Conditions

After charging, disconnect AC power and operate the pump on battery in a non-patient test setup according to facility practice.

Monitor for early low battery alarms, unexpected shutdown, rapid battery indicator drop, or failure to remain powered.

Expected outcome: The pump operates on battery without premature shutdown or immediate low battery alarm.

If runtime is too short after a full charge, suspect an aged or failed battery. Remove from service for battery replacement or bench evaluation.

Check Battery Age and Maintenance History

Review service history for the battery replacement date, prior low runtime complaints, repeated charging issues, or failed battery tests.

Expected outcome: Battery age and maintenance history support reliable operation.

If the battery is old, has repeated complaints, or fails runtime testing, replace it per facility procedure or send the pump for battery service.

Perform Final Functional Verification

After correcting an external issue or replacing a battery through the proper service process, verify:

  • Pump powers on with AC
  • Pump recognizes AC power
  • Battery charges
  • Pump runs on battery
  • No power-related alarms occur
  • Display and keypad respond normally
  • Final safety and operational checks pass per facility procedure

Expected outcome: Pump passes functional checks and is safe to return to service.

If any power, charging, or runtime issue remains, do not return the pump to clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If outlet power, AC cord condition, charging time, AC detection, and external inspection have been ruled out, the issue is likely related to the battery, charging circuit, power inlet, internal power supply path, or control electronics.

The pump should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for battery replacement, repair, or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. An infusion pump with unreliable battery power should not be returned to patient care until charging and runtime are verified.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot battery charging or runtime complaints while the pump is delivering therapy to an active patient. Move the infusion to another verified pump first, or ensure the pump is safely connected to reliable AC power under clinical direction.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the B. Braun Outlook 400ES battery would not hold a charge and the pump displayed a low battery alarm shortly after being unplugged."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Pump powered normally on AC, outlet and power cord tested good, but battery runtime was short after extended charging."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for battery replacement and bench verification before return to use."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed good
  • AC power cord inspected or swapped
  • Battery icon or charge indicator behavior
  • Low battery alarm timing
  • Approximate runtime after full charge
  • Whether pump operates normally on AC power
  • Whether pump shuts down immediately on battery
  • Any intermittent AC detection
  • Battery replacement history
  • Unusual heat, odor, damage, or fluid exposure
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery complaints on an infusion pump should be handled carefully because unreliable backup power can interrupt therapy during transport or power loss. Start with external power, cord, connection, and charging checks before assuming internal failure. If runtime remains poor after basic causes are ruled out, remove the pump from service and document the finding clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
