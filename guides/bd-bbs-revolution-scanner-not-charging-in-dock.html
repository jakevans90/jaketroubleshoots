BD BBS Revolution

Scanner not charging in dock

Asset Type

Bladder Scanner

Manufacturer

BD

Model

BBS Revolution

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting BBS Revolution charging issues caused by dock power, scanner seating, contacts, cables, battery, or external damage.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the bladder scanner is not being used for an active patient assessment.

If the unit is needed for patient care, provide clinical staff with another verified bladder scanner before troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without relying on a scanner with uncertain battery status.

Confirm the Reported Charging Problem

Ask staff what they observed.

Check whether the issue is:

  • Scanner does not show charging when placed in the dock
  • Battery icon does not change
  • Scanner powers off quickly after docking
  • Dock has no power indicator
  • Charging is intermittent
  • Scanner only charges when held at an angle
  • Scanner does not power on after being docked

Expected outcome: The exact failure mode is confirmed before parts are blamed.

Verify the Dock Has Power

Check that the charging dock is plugged into a known working outlet.

If the dock uses an external power supply, confirm the power supply is firmly connected at both the wall outlet and the dock.

Expected outcome: Dock power is present and the charging issue is not due to an unplugged or failed outlet.

If reconnecting power restores charging, stop and document the loose or disconnected power source.

Test the Wall Outlet

Plug another device or an outlet tester into the same receptacle.

Avoid assuming the scanner or dock has failed until the outlet is confirmed.

Expected outcome: The outlet provides proper power.

If the outlet is dead or controlled by a switch, move the dock to a verified outlet and retest.

Inspect the External Power Supply and Cord

Look for:

  • Bent connector pins
  • Damaged insulation
  • Strain at the cable ends
  • Loose barrel connector
  • Cracked power supply housing
  • Signs of overheating or smell

Expected outcome: The power supply and cable are physically intact.

If damage, heat, or odor is found, remove the charger from service and do not continue using it.

Confirm the Scanner Is Seated Correctly in the Dock

Place the BBS Revolution scanner into the charging dock and verify it sits fully and evenly.

Check for obstruction from labels, cleaning residue, debris, or misalignment.

Expected outcome: The scanner makes proper contact with the charging dock.

If reseating the scanner restores charging, stop and document improper seating or obstruction.

Inspect Charging Contacts

Inspect the scanner and dock charging contacts for:

  • Dirt
  • Fluid residue
  • Corrosion
  • Bent contacts
  • Worn contact surfaces
  • Broken or recessed contact points

Clean external contact surfaces only according to facility-approved cleaning practices and manufacturer-compatible disinfectants.

Expected outcome: Charging contacts are clean, dry, and physically intact.

If bent, broken, corroded, or recessed contacts are found, remove the unit or dock from service for repair evaluation.

Check for Cleaning or Fluid Intrusion Issues

Ask whether the scanner or dock was recently cleaned, soaked, sprayed heavily, or stored wet.

Moisture around charging contacts can prevent charging and may create an electrical safety concern.

Expected outcome: No moisture or fluid contamination is present.

If liquid intrusion is suspected, remove the device and dock from service and send for evaluation.

Allow Time for Charging Response

If the battery is deeply discharged, place the scanner in the powered dock and allow it to remain seated long enough to see whether the charging indicator appears.

Expected outcome: The scanner begins showing charging status after being docked.

If there is still no indication of charging after a reasonable observation period, continue troubleshooting.

Check for Intermittent Charging

Gently observe whether the charging indicator changes when the scanner is lightly repositioned in the dock.

Do not force the scanner, bend contacts, or apply pressure to make it charge.

Expected outcome: Charging remains stable without needing to hold the scanner in a specific position.

If charging only occurs at certain angles, suspect worn dock contacts, scanner contact damage, or dock alignment failure.

Compare With a Known Good Dock or Power Supply, If Available

If another compatible BBS Revolution charging dock or approved power supply is available, test the scanner using the known good setup.

Also test a known good scanner on the suspect dock if available.

Expected outcome: The issue follows either the scanner, dock, or power supply.

If the scanner charges on another dock, the original dock or power supply is likely the problem.

If another scanner charges on the same dock, the original scanner or battery is likely the problem.

Inspect the Scanner Battery Behavior

After attempting to charge, power on the scanner and observe battery status.

Look for:

  • Battery level does not increase
  • Battery drains quickly
  • Battery icon is missing or abnormal
  • Scanner shuts off immediately when removed from dock
  • Battery or charging error message

Expected outcome: Battery status responds normally to charging.

If the battery does not accept or hold charge after external causes are ruled out, the scanner should be evaluated for battery or internal charging circuit failure.

Check for Physical Damage

Inspect the scanner and dock for drops, cracks, loose housings, damaged alignment features, or broken plastic around the charging area.

Expected outcome: No visible damage affects docking or charging.

If damage is present, remove the affected component from service and send for repair evaluation.

Perform a Functional Check After Charging Is Restored

If charging resumes, allow the unit to charge and then verify basic operation.

Confirm:

  • Scanner powers on
  • Battery level displays normally
  • Charging indicator behaves correctly
  • Unit remains powered after removal from dock
  • No charging alarms or error messages return

Expected outcome: The scanner charges and functions normally before being returned to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the BBS Revolution still does not charge after verifying the outlet, dock power, power supply, scanner seating, contacts, moisture condition, and known-good accessories, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely related to the scanner battery, dock electronics, charging contacts, or internal charging circuitry.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not keep a bladder scanner in clinical use if its charging reliability is uncertain.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot charging issues while the device is needed for an active patient scan. Provide staff with another verified bladder scanner first. A scanner with unreliable charging may fail during rounds, delay bladder volume assessment, or create unnecessary repeat testing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the BD BBS Revolution bladder scanner would not charge when placed in the docking station."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet power was present, but the scanner only charged intermittently when repositioned in the dock, indicating a likely dock contact or alignment issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the dock and scanner from service, labeled them Out of Service, and sent the system for bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • Dock power indicator status
  • Power supply inspected
  • Scanner seated fully in dock
  • Charging contacts inspected
  • Contacts cleaned and dried
  • Known-good dock or power supply tested
  • Battery icon or charging indicator behavior
  • Any charging error message
  • Evidence of fluid exposure
  • Physical damage to scanner or dock
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Charging problems should be approached logically from the outside in. Confirm power, docking, contacts, and accessory condition before suspecting internal battery or charging failure. Patient safety comes first, and clear CCR documentation helps show why the device was returned to use or removed for repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
