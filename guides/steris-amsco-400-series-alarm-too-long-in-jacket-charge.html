STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Too Long In Jacket Charge – Jacket did not reach required temperature within allotted time

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: Too Long In Jacket Charge indicating the sterilizer jacket failed to reach required temperature within the expected time, delaying or preventing proper cycle operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not use the sterilizer while this alarm is active
  • Do not consider any loads sterile if the cycle did not complete

Expected: No instruments are processed under fault conditions
Why it matters: Proper jacket temperature is critical for steam generation and sterilization effectiveness

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows: “Too Long In Jacket Charge”
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup or preheat phase)
  • Check if jacket temperature is rising slowly or not at all
  • Confirm the unit remains in alarm until temperature is reached

Expected: Alarm is clearly present and tied to heating delay
Why it matters: Confirms a true heating or steam supply issue

3. Check Steam Supply Valve

  • Verify the facility steam supply valve is fully open
  • Ensure no manual shutoff valves are partially closed

Expected: Steam supply valve is fully open
Why it matters: Restricted steam flow prevents jacket heating

4. Verify Adequate Steam Pressure

  • Confirm steam pressure is at least 50 PSIG
  • Check facility gauges or consult engineering staff
  • Look for recent steam outages or pressure drops

Expected: Steam pressure is within normal range
Why it matters: Low pressure slows or prevents heating

5. Inspect Steam Supply Piping

  • Check for closed inline valves
  • Inspect for kinks or restrictions
  • Look for visible leaks or condensation buildup

Expected: Steam path is unobstructed
Why it matters: Flow restrictions reduce heat transfer efficiency

6. Observe Heating Response

  • Reset the alarm after correcting any issues
  • Monitor jacket temperature rise

Expected: Temperature rises steadily to setpoint
Why it matters: Confirms whether issue was external or internal

7. Identify Likely Internal Causes

  • Steam regulator malfunction
  • Solenoid valve (S09) failure
  • Control system calibration drift

Expected: Alarm persists despite proper steam supply
Why it matters: Indicates internal component or control issue

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out:

  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or delay instrument processing on an active patient. Always move to a backup sterilizer before addressing equipment issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Alarm: Too Long In Jacket Charge. Sterilizer not reaching temperature and unable to start cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Steam supply valve partially closed causing insufficient steam pressure to jacket."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Opened steam supply valve fully, verified pressure ≥50 PSIG, reset alarm, and confirmed normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Alarms involving jacket heating are most often tied to steam supply issues. Always start with external checks before escalating. Proper troubleshooting ensures safe operation and prevents unnecessary repairs.

That is successful troubleshooting.
