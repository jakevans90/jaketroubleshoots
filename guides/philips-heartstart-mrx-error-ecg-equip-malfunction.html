Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: ECG Equip Malfunction

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: ECG Equip Malfunction on the Philips HeartStart MRx. This condition may indicate unreliable ECG monitoring due to electrodes, leadwires, the ECG patient cable, the ECG input port, configuration, or internal ECG monitoring hardware.

An ECG equipment malfunction should be treated as a monitoring reliability issue because it may affect rhythm display, lead detection, alarm function, and clinical decision-making.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the HeartStart MRx is connected to a patient and ECG monitoring is unreliable, transfer monitoring to another device immediately.
  • Do not rely on the MRx for ECG monitoring until the malfunction is corrected, cleared, and verified.
  • If defibrillation, pacing, or emergency monitoring may be needed, provide a fully functional backup defibrillator/monitor.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring is maintained using a reliable alternate monitor or defibrillator before troubleshooting continues.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact displayed message is Error: ECG Equip Malfunction.
  • Note whether the error appears on startup, after connecting the ECG cable, during monitoring, intermittently when the cable is moved, or continuously regardless of cable connection.
  • Check for no ECG waveform, leads-off messages that do not clear, artifact, unstable ECG tracing, incorrect lead display, or ECG monitoring unavailable.
  • Expected: The error is reproducible or clearly documented before parts or accessories are replaced.

Remove the Device From Active Monitoring Use

  • If ECG monitoring cannot be trusted, remove the MRx from clinical monitoring service.
  • Notify clinical staff that the device should not be used for ECG monitoring until evaluated.
  • Expected: The device is not used for patient monitoring while the ECG hardware condition is unresolved.

Check ECG Electrodes First

  • Inspect the ECG electrodes for expiration, dried gel, poor skin contact, loose placement, or contamination.
  • Check whether electrodes are placed over hair, lotion, moisture, broken skin, or incorrect locations.
  • Replace electrodes with new, in-date electrodes if patient-side testing is appropriate.
  • Expected: ECG signal returns and the error does not recur if the issue was electrode-related.

Check Leadwire Connection and Condition

  • Inspect each ECG leadwire from the patient end back to the trunk cable.
  • Look for bent snaps, loose clips, broken clips, frayed insulation, stretched wires, fluid contamination, or intermittent signal when moved.
  • Reconnect all leadwires firmly.
  • Expected: Leadwires are intact, clean, and securely connected.

Inspect the ECG Patient Cable

  • Check the ECG patient cable for cuts, kinks, crushed sections, exposed conductors, loose strain relief, connector damage, or signs of fluid intrusion.
  • Gently move the cable while observing whether the error appears, clears, or changes.
  • Expected: The cable does not cause intermittent ECG failure when moved.

Try a Known-Good ECG Cable

  • Replace the ECG cable with a known-good compatible ECG cable.
  • Reconnect electrodes or use an ECG simulator if available.
  • Power cycle or restart the device if needed to see whether the error clears.
  • Expected: The ECG Equip Malfunction clears if the original ECG cable was defective.

Inspect the ECG Port on the MRx

  • Inspect the ECG input port on the HeartStart MRx.
  • Check for bent pins, broken connector housing, loose port fit, debris, corrosion, fluid contamination, or damage from forced cable insertion.
  • Do not probe aggressively or force a connector into a damaged port.
  • Expected: The ECG port is clean, intact, and holds the ECG cable securely.

Check for Cleaning or Fluid-Related Damage

  • Ask whether the unit was recently cleaned, exposed to fluids, or used in a high-moisture environment.
  • Inspect the ECG connector area and cable ends for residue or moisture.
  • Allow the device and accessories to dry if minor external moisture is suspected, following facility policy.
  • Expected: No fluid, residue, or contamination is affecting the ECG cable or ECG port.

Test With an ECG Simulator

  • Connect a known-good ECG cable to an ECG simulator.
  • Select a standard rhythm and verify whether the HeartStart MRx displays a stable ECG waveform.
  • Check multiple leads if available.
  • Expected: The MRx displays a clean, stable ECG waveform without the ECG Equip Malfunction message.

Check Configuration and Lead Selection

  • Verify the device is set to the appropriate ECG source and lead selection.
  • Confirm the displayed lead matches the connected cable setup.
  • Determine whether the issue is limited to one lead, one leadwire, or one cable configuration.
  • Expected: The device is configured correctly for the connected ECG cable and lead set.

Run an Operational Check

  • Run the HeartStart MRx Operational Check according to facility procedure.
  • Review the result for ECG-related failures or repeated equipment malfunction messages.
  • Expected: The device passes ECG-related testing after external accessories are confirmed good.

Review Device Status or Service Information

  • If available to Clinical Engineering, review device status or service information for ECG-related failures.
  • Look for repeated ECG hardware, monitoring, or equipment malfunction entries.
  • Expected: Logs either confirm a repeated ECG hardware issue or show the error was isolated to an accessory condition.

Isolate the Failure

  • If the error clears with new electrodes, suspect electrode condition or skin contact.
  • If the error clears with known-good leadwires, suspect leadwire failure.
  • If the error clears with a known-good ECG cable, suspect ECG cable failure.
  • If the error changes when the connector is moved, suspect the cable connector or ECG port.
  • If the error remains with known-good accessories and simulator testing, suspect internal ECG monitoring hardware or related connection failure.

Stop When Resolved

  • If the error clears after replacing electrodes, leadwires, or the ECG cable, repeat functional testing.
  • Verify stable ECG waveform, correct lead display, no recurring ECG Equip Malfunction message, appropriate ECG alarm behavior, and passing Operational Check if required by policy.
  • Return the device to service only after ECG function is verified.

If the Problem Persists

If the ECG Equip Malfunction remains after checking electrodes, leadwires, the ECG cable, ECG port, configuration, and simulator testing, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent ECG equipment malfunction may indicate a device-side failure involving the ECG monitoring circuitry, internal cable path, connector assembly, or related PCA connections.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue troubleshooting on an active clinical device or return the unit to service with intermittent ECG monitoring.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot ECG hardware problems while the device is actively being relied on for patient monitoring. Move the patient to a backup monitor or defibrillator first, then evaluate the HeartStart MRx.

A defibrillator/monitor should not be returned to service if ECG monitoring is intermittent, noisy, or associated with unresolved equipment malfunction messages.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx reported Error: ECG Equip Malfunction. Staff reported ECG monitoring was unavailable or intermittent and the device could not be trusted for patient rhythm monitoring."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"ECG cable, leadwires, electrodes, ECG port, and setup were evaluated. Known-good ECG cable and simulator testing did not clear the malfunction, indicating likely internal ECG monitoring hardware or related PCA connection failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from clinical use and labeled Out of Service. Referred unit for bench evaluation and repair of ECG monitoring circuitry and related internal connections. Device not returned to service pending repair and verification."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

ECG Equip Malfunction should be treated as a patient monitoring reliability issue. Start with the external ECG path, confirm the complaint with known-good accessories, and escalate only after simple causes are ruled out. Clear documentation protects patients, staff, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
