Hillrom TotalCare

Scale Drift or Inaccuracy

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

TotalCare

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses inaccurate or drifting weight readings, including power, connection, sensor, and environmental factors before assuming internal scale failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the bed is unoccupied or the patient is safely moved to another bed. Never troubleshoot scales with a patient on the bed.

Check Bed Level and Placement

  • Ensure the bed is on a firm, level surface. Uneven floors can cause scale drift or false readings. Expect stable weight readings after leveling.

Inspect Power and Connections

  • Verify the bed is properly plugged in and power is stable. Loose or intermittent connections can affect scale electronics. Expected outcome: no intermittent power warnings.

Examine Scale Sensors and Housings

  • Check under-bed load cells and wiring for visible damage, debris, or misalignment. Ensure sensors are seated correctly. Outcome: sensors clean, aligned, and unobstructed.

Verify Mattress and Accessories

  • Remove or reposition mattress and any overlay. Improper placement can interfere with load cell readings. Outcome: scale reading stabilizes.

Check Calibration Status

  • If the bed allows electronic calibration, verify it has not been disrupted. Perform calibration per manufacturer guidelines if safe. Outcome: accurate weight reading restored.

Observe for Environmental Factors

  • Ensure no items (IV poles, carts) are in contact with the bed frame. Vibrations or floor movement can affect readings. Outcome: scale readings stabilize when environment controlled.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. Likely internal sensor or board issue. Bed should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for manufacturer repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop protects patient safety and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Always move patients to a stable bed before troubleshooting. Avoid weight-based interventions until scale accuracy is confirmed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed scale drifting; weight readings inconsistent."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Load cell misalignment and loose mattress placement identified."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Bed leveled, sensors inspected and reset, mattress repositioned; scale reading stabilized."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and stable
  • Mattress and overlay placement verified
  • Indicator lights normal
  • Load cells physically intact
  • Final device status: in service or sent for repair

Final Thought

Accurate weight measurements are critical for patient care and medication dosing. Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures patient safety, prevents unnecessary repair, and documents findings for accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
