Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x11A3 Right Pendant Button Stuck (Moderate)

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0x11A3 indicating a stuck right-side pendant button causing unintended bed movement or loss of normal pendant control.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from active patient use if unintended movement is occurring
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed if necessary
  • Ensure no risk of uncontrolled bed movement

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm that Error 0x11A3 is displayed on the bed interface
  • Observe if the bed moves without input
  • Check if a specific function is continuously active
  • Determine if the issue occurs at startup or during use

Inspect the Right Pendant for Physical Obstruction

  • Check for debris or dried fluids around buttons
  • Press buttons to confirm proper movement and return
  • Ensure no object is pressing against the pendant

Clean the Pendant Surface

  • Use approved disinfectant wipes to clean the pendant
  • Dry thoroughly after cleaning
  • Re-test button responsiveness

Check Pendant Cable and Connection

  • Inspect cable for cuts, pinching, or strain damage
  • Ensure connector is fully seated
  • Reseat connection if accessible

Power Cycle the Bed

  • Turn off or unplug the bed
  • Wait briefly before restoring power
  • Restart and check if error clears

Isolate Pendant vs Bed Issue

  • Swap with a known-good pendant if available
  • Determine if the error follows the pendant or remains with the bed

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0x11A3 continues after all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Likely internal pendant failure or control circuit fault
  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Replace the pendant or send for repair/bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a bed with active patient use. Always transfer the patient to a safe device before evaluating control-related issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x11A3 with right pendant causing continuous or unresponsive control input."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Right pendant button found sticking due to internal failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Inspected and cleaned pendant; issue persisted. Removed bed from service and replaced pendant."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Stuck control inputs are safety-critical issues. Always begin with simple external checks, but escalate quickly when faults persist. Proper documentation and timely removal from service ensure patient safety and effective repairs.

That is successful troubleshooting.
