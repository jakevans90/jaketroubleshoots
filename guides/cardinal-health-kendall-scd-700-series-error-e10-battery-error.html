Cardinal Health Kendall SCD 700 Series

Error: E10 Battery Error

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists with troubleshooting Error: E10 Battery Error, which indicates unsafe battery operation. This condition can result in loss of battery backup and potential device shutdown during patient therapy.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from patient use if battery reliability is in question
  • Transfer therapy to a known working SCD unit
  • Ensure continuous DVT prophylaxis is maintained

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the unit and confirm Error: E10 Battery Error is displayed
  • Observe when the error appears (startup or during operation)
  • Check if the device fails to run on battery
  • Look for alarms or service indicators

Check AC Power Operation

  • Plug the device into a known-good outlet
  • Verify AC power indicator is present
  • Confirm device operates normally while connected to AC power

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Ensure the power cord is fully seated
  • Inspect for cuts, fraying, or bent prongs
  • Test with a known-good power cord if available

Power Cycle the Device

  • Turn the device off
  • Unplug from AC power
  • Wait 30 seconds
  • Reconnect and power on

Evaluate Battery Function (Non-Invasive Check)

  • Unplug AC power while off-patient
  • Observe if the device powers off immediately or behaves abnormally
  • Confirm whether the battery supports operation

Determine Isolation

  • If the error persists after reset, the issue is internal
  • If the device only works on AC power, battery failure is likely
  • If the battery is not recognized or does not hold charge, replacement is required

If the Problem Persists

After external checks are completed, a persistent Error: E10 Battery Error indicates a failed or unsafe battery pack.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Replace battery pack if supported or send for repair

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a battery-related fault on an active patient. Always move the patient to a functioning device before evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device displaying E10 Battery Error and not operating on battery power during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery pack failure indicated by system; device unable to maintain operation without AC power."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified AC operation, confirmed battery failure, removed device from service and sent for battery replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery-related errors like E10 are safety-driven faults designed to prevent unsafe operation. Proper troubleshooting involves confirming the issue, ruling out external causes, and escalating appropriately for repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
