Berchtold Chromophare Surgical Light

Control Panel or Wall Controller Malfunction

Asset Type

Surgical Light

Manufacturer

Berchtold

Model

Chromophare Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the control panel or wall controller of a Berchtold Chromophare surgical light is malfunctioning. Symptoms may include unresponsive buttons, erratic light intensity changes, or complete failure to control the light. The focus is on external checks and easily verifiable issues before escalating to repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power to the System

  • Ensure the surgical light and wall controller are receiving power.
  • Check for tripped breakers, blown fuses, or unplugged connectors.
  • Why: Lack of power is the most common external cause of unresponsive controls.

Inspect Wall Controller Connections

  • Confirm the wall controller is properly connected to the light system.
  • Check for loose, damaged, or kinked cables.
  • Why: Poor connections can prevent signals from reaching the light head.

Check for Visible Damage or Contamination

  • Examine the control panel and wall controller for cracks, liquid spills, or stuck buttons.
  • Clean with manufacturer-approved wipes if necessary.
  • Why: Debris or liquid ingress can interfere with electrical contacts.

Test Button Functionality

  • Press each button on the control panel and wall controller while observing the light response.
  • Identify if specific functions are unresponsive or intermittent.
  • Why: Helps isolate whether the issue is with specific buttons or the entire controller.

Swap Controllers (if available)

  • If a spare wall controller or control panel is available, swap it to see if the problem persists.
  • Why: Confirms whether the issue lies with the controller or the light head/system.

Check Indicator Lights or Error Codes

  • Observe any status LEDs on the wall controller or light head.
  • Reference manufacturer troubleshooting codes if present.
  • Why: Indicator lights can provide diagnostic clues without opening the device.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have ruled out power, connections, and visible damage. The issue is likely internal to the control panel, wall controller, or light interface board.

Action:

  • Remove the surgical light from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send for manufacturer repair or bench evaluation.

Note: Continuing use without repair may compromise surgical lighting and patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot a malfunctioning surgical light while in use over an active patient.
  • Move surgical cases to a functioning light before testing.
  • Always confirm adequate lighting is available to maintain patient safety.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Wall controller buttons are unresponsive; light intensity cannot be adjusted.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Power verified, connections intact, no visible damage. Wall controller appears to be internally malfunctioning.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for manufacturer evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Power outlet checked and confirmed live
  • Cables inspected and reseated
  • Control panel buttons tested individually
  • Indicator LEDs monitored for error codes
  • Wall controller swap attempted if spare available
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Clinical troubleshooting should follow a logical, stepwise approach, prioritizing patient safety and external checks first. Proper documentation ensures continuity of care and supports timely repair by the manufacturer. Escalation is not failure; it is proper engineering judgment.

That is successful troubleshooting.
