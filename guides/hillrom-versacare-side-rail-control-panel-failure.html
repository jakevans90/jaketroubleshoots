Hillrom VersaCare

Side Rail Control Panel Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

VersaCare

What This Guide Helps With

Side rail controls not responding, intermittent button function, or complete panel failure due to cable, lockout, or power-related issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Ensure the patient is safe and the bed is in a stable position.
  • If controls are needed, use alternate controls (footboard or caregiver pendant if available).

Why it matters: Loss of side rail control can prevent repositioning and create patient risk.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Press multiple buttons on the affected side rail panel.
  • Check if:
    • All buttons are unresponsive
    • Only certain functions fail
    • The opposite side rail works normally

Expected: Clear confirmation of full or partial panel failure

Why it matters: Helps determine if the issue is isolated or system-wide

Check for Lockout or Control Restrictions

  • Inspect the caregiver control panel (usually at footboard).
  • Verify that side rail controls are not locked out.

Expected: Controls enabled and functional

Why it matters: Lockout is a common and easily overlooked cause

Test Bed Power and System Functionality

  • Confirm the bed is plugged into a known working outlet.
  • Check for power indicators and test other bed functions (hi/lo, backrest, etc.).

Expected: Bed powers and other functions operate normally

Why it matters: Confirms issue is not related to overall power loss

Inspect Side Rail Cable and Connection Points

  • Visually inspect the cable running from the side rail to the bed frame.
  • Look for:
    • Pinched or damaged wiring
    • Loose or disconnected connectors
    • Signs of wear near hinge points
  • Gently reposition the rail while testing buttons.

Expected: No damage and no change in function when moving cable

Why it matters: Repeated rail movement commonly causes cable fatigue or breaks

Swap or Compare with Opposite Side Rail (If Applicable)

  • If both rails are similar, compare function with the working side.
  • If connectors are accessible and identical, swap connections (if safe and allowed).

Expected: Issue follows the panel if faulty

Why it matters: Helps isolate panel vs. bed interface issue

Check for Fluid Intrusion or Physical Damage

  • Inspect the control panel for:
    • Cracked buttons or housing
    • Sticky or unresponsive keys
    • Signs of fluid ingress (cleaning solutions, spills)

Expected: Panel is clean, intact, and dry

Why it matters: Fluid intrusion is a common cause of keypad failure

Perform a Power Reset

  • Unplug the bed from AC power.
  • Wait 30–60 seconds, then reconnect power.

Expected: Panel may recover if issue was software-related

Why it matters: Resets internal control modules

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely due to internal failure of the side rail control panel or associated wiring harness.

Remove the bed from service.

Label the device Out of Service.

Send for repair or further bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and ensures proper repair handling.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot side rail controls while a patient is actively relying on bed positioning. Always transfer the patient to another bed before continuing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Side rail control panel unresponsive; unable to adjust bed from patient side."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed side rail control panel due to worn internal keypad/cable fatigue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and sent for repair; confirmed issue isolated to side rail assembly."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Lockout status checked
  • Opposite rail functionality confirmed
  • Cable condition inspected
  • Panel physical condition noted
  • Power reset attempted
  • Final device status (in service or removed)

Final Thought

Side rail control failures are often caused by external factors like lockouts, cable wear, or fluid exposure. A structured approach ensures simple issues are corrected quickly while recognizing when internal failure requires escalation. Always prioritize patient safety and document clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
