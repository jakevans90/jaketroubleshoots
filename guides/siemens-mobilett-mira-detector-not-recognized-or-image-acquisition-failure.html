Siemens Healthineers MOBILETT Mira

Detector not recognized or image acquisition failure

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

MOBILETT Mira

What This Guide Helps With

Detector recognition, wireless detector communication, exposure workflow, and image acquisition failures on a MOBILETT Mira mobile X-ray system.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot detector recognition or image acquisition problems during an active patient exam unless it is safe to stop.

Action:

  • Remove the MOBILETT Mira from patient use if the detector is not recognized, exposures cannot be acquired, or images are not reliably appearing.
  • Use another mobile X-ray unit if imaging is clinically urgent.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not delayed or dependent on unreliable imaging equipment.

Why it matters: A detector or acquisition failure can cause repeat exams, delayed diagnosis, or unnecessary radiation exposure if troubleshooting is attempted during patient care.

Verify the Reported Problem

Confirm whether the issue is detector recognition, exposure acquisition, image display, or image transfer.

Action:

Ask the technologist what happened:

  • Detector not listed or not detected
  • Detector connected but not ready
  • Exposure button works but no image appears
  • Image appears but is blank or corrupted
  • Image acquired but does not save or send
  • Error appears only with one detector

Check the displayed system message, detector status icon, battery indicator, and acquisition screen behavior.

Expected outcome: The failure type is clearly identified before swapping parts or escalating.

Confirm the Correct Detector Is Being Used

Verify that the detector belongs with the MOBILETT Mira system or is approved/configured for that unit.

Action:

  • Check the detector ID, label, or department assignment.
  • Confirm staff did not accidentally use a detector from another room, another mobile unit, or a different imaging system.

Expected outcome: The system is being tested with the correct detector.

If the correct detector is recognized, stop troubleshooting and document the mismatch.

Check Detector Battery Charge

A low or depleted detector battery can prevent recognition or image acquisition.

Action:

  • Check the detector battery indicator, charging cradle, or system status display.
  • Install a known-charged detector battery if applicable.
  • Allow the detector to charge if the battery is low.

Expected outcome: The detector powers on and becomes available to the MOBILETT Mira.

If the issue resolves after charging or battery replacement, document the low battery condition.

Power Cycle the Detector Safely

A detector may fail to reconnect after sleep mode, battery change, transport, or wireless interruption.

Action:

  • Power off the detector if it has a power switch.
  • Wait at least 30 seconds.
  • Power it back on and allow time for the system to rediscover it.
  • If the detector uses a removable battery, reseat the battery while following local handling procedures.

Expected outcome: The detector reconnects and shows ready status.

Inspect the Detector for Physical Damage

Check for obvious signs of damage before continuing.

Action:

  • Inspect the detector housing, corners, connector areas, labels, battery compartment, and contact surfaces.
  • Look for cracks, fluid intrusion, bent contacts, loose covers, missing labels, or evidence of a drop.

Expected outcome: The detector appears physically intact and safe to continue testing.

If physical damage is found, remove the detector from service and send it for repair evaluation.

Verify Detector Placement and Wireless Communication Conditions

Wireless detector communication can be affected by distance, shielding, storage position, or interference.

Action:

  • Place the detector near the MOBILETT Mira.
  • Avoid placing the detector behind lead shielding, inside storage compartments, under metal surfaces, or far from the system during testing.
  • Confirm the unit is not in an area with unusual wireless restrictions or known network interference.

Expected outcome: The detector is recognized when positioned close to the system.

If recognition improves only at close range, document possible wireless communication weakness and escalate for further evaluation.

Check Detector Docking, Charging, or Tether Connections

If the system uses a docking slot, charging bay, or detector cable, check those external connection points.

Action:

  • Inspect the docking contacts, charging contacts, detector cable, and connector ends.
  • Look for debris, bent pins, loose connectors, damaged cable jackets, or contamination.
  • Reseat the detector or cable firmly.
  • Try a known-good detector cable if one is available.

Expected outcome: The detector communicates normally after reseating or replacing an external connection.

If the issue resolves with a cable swap, replace the faulty cable and document it.

Restart the MOBILETT Mira System

A workstation or acquisition software issue may prevent detector recognition.

Action:

  • End any active exam if safe.
  • Perform a normal shutdown.
  • Wait for the system to fully power off.
  • Restart the MOBILETT Mira and log back into the imaging workflow.
  • Check whether the detector appears as ready.

Expected outcome: The system reloads the acquisition software and recognizes the detector.

If restart resolves the issue, monitor for recurrence and document the software recovery.

Confirm Exam and Acquisition Workflow Settings

The system may not acquire an image if the wrong exam, detector, body part, or acquisition mode is selected.

Action:

  • Start a test workflow using an appropriate non-patient test process per site policy.
  • Confirm the correct detector, exam type, receptor selection, and exposure mode are selected.
  • Check that the system is not waiting for confirmation, patient selection, detector selection, or positioning input.

Expected outcome: The detector status changes to ready and the system allows acquisition.

If the issue was workflow selection, document the incorrect selection and return the unit to service after successful test acquisition.

Check Exposure Inhibit or Readiness Messages

Image acquisition may fail because the system is not actually ready to expose.

Action:

Check for messages related to:

  • Detector not ready
  • Battery low
  • Exposure inhibited
  • Generator not ready
  • Door, brake, or positioning interlock
  • System not prepared
  • Communication error
  • Workstation or acquisition error

Expected outcome: Any blocking condition is identified before assuming detector failure.

If a non-detector interlock is causing the issue, troubleshoot that issue separately.

Test With a Known-Good Detector if Available

This helps separate a detector problem from a system-side problem.

Action:

  • Use another compatible, properly assigned detector if available.
  • Confirm the test detector is charged and approved for the MOBILETT Mira.
  • Attempt recognition and a test acquisition according to site policy.

Expected outcome:

  • If the known-good detector works, the original detector is likely faulty.
  • If neither detector works, the issue is likely system-side communication, software, or acquisition hardware.

Test the Suspect Detector on Another Compatible System if Allowed

Only perform this if your site allows detector cross-testing and the detector is properly configured for the other system.

Action:

  • Test whether the suspect detector is recognized by another compatible Siemens system.
  • Do not force pairing, reconfiguration, or service-level changes without proper authorization.

Expected outcome:

  • If the detector fails on multiple systems, suspect detector failure.
  • If the detector works elsewhere, suspect the MOBILETT Mira system, pairing, wireless path, or acquisition workstation.

Check Image Appearance After Acquisition

If the detector is recognized but the image acquisition fails or produces unusable images, separate acquisition from image quality.

Action:

Perform a controlled test image according to site policy.

Check whether the image is:

  • Missing
  • Delayed
  • Blank
  • Severely artifacted
  • Not associated with the exam
  • Present locally but not saved or transferred

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to acquisition, detector readout, workstation processing, or image routing.

If the image is acquired locally but does not send, troubleshoot PACS/network/worklist separately.

Review Recent Changes

Detector recognition failures often appear after a battery change, detector swap, software restart, network change, or physical damage event.

Action:

Ask whether there were recent:

  • Detector drops
  • Battery replacements
  • Software updates
  • Network changes
  • Department moves
  • New detector assignments
  • Previous intermittent failures
  • Cleaning or fluid exposure events

Expected outcome: A likely trigger is identified and documented for repair follow-up.

If the Problem Persists

If the detector is charged, correctly assigned, physically intact, close to the system, properly connected, and the MOBILETT Mira still does not recognize it or acquire images, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

The problem may involve the detector electronics, wireless communication module, detector pairing/configuration, acquisition workstation, software, generator/acquisition synchronization, or internal system communication path.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated test exposures or attempt internal repair during clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot detector acquisition failures on an active patient when repeat exposure or delayed imaging is possible. Move the patient exam to another working mobile X-ray system first, then troubleshoot the MOBILETT Mira in a controlled non-patient setting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Technologist reported that the Siemens MOBILETT Mira would not recognize the wireless detector and could not acquire patient images."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Detector battery and external connections were checked; detector remained unavailable after restart and was not recognized during controlled testing."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"MOBILETT Mira and detector were removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation due to suspected detector communication or acquisition system fault."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact detector recognition message displayed
  • Whether the detector appeared in the acquisition software
  • Detector battery status
  • Detector ID or serial number
  • Whether another detector was tested
  • Whether the suspect detector worked on another system
  • Docking or tether cable condition
  • Wireless signal or location-related behavior
  • Any recent drops or fluid exposure
  • Whether exposure was inhibited or image acquisition completed
  • Whether images appeared locally
  • Whether images sent to PACS
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Detector and acquisition problems should be approached logically: protect the patient first, confirm the exact failure, check battery and communication basics, verify workflow settings, and only then suspect internal failure. Clear CCR documentation helps the next technician or vendor quickly understand what was tested and why the unit was removed from service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
