Linet Eleganza

Actuator or Positioning Motor Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Linet

Model

Eleganza

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of slow, unresponsive, or non-functioning bed positioning motors (hi-lo, backrest, knee gatch) including power, connections, and mechanical obstructions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the patient is safely transferred or fully supported. Do not operate or troubleshoot actuators while the patient is on the bed.

Check Power Supply

  • Verify the bed is plugged into a live outlet.
  • Check battery backup charge if equipped.
  • Expected Outcome: Bed should respond to controls if power is adequate.

Inspect Power Cords and Connectors

  • Examine for loose, damaged, or disconnected cables to actuators and control panels.
  • Expected Outcome: Restoring proper connections may resume normal function.

Test Control Panel Commands

  • Operate each motor function individually (hi-lo, backrest, knee gatch).
  • Listen for actuator noise or observe movement.
  • Expected Outcome: If one actuator fails while others work, the issue is likely isolated.

Check for Mechanical Obstructions

  • Inspect bed frame, joints, and mattress positioning for interference.
  • Expected Outcome: Freeing any obstruction may restore movement.

Cycle Actuators Without Load

  • With no patient, run each motor to full range to observe operation.
  • Expected Outcome: Detect slow, uneven, or non-functioning actuators, confirming mechanical or motor issues.

If the Problem Persists

External checks cleared. Likely internal actuator or motor failure. Actions:

  • Remove bed from service.
  • Label “Out of Service.”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to escalate preserves patient safety and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Always reposition or transfer patients before troubleshooting bed movement. Avoid relying on partially functioning actuators for patient support.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed backrest and hi-lo motors unresponsive."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Actuators failed to move after verifying power and connections."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Bed removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • Battery status
  • Control panel response
  • Actuator noise or lack thereof
  • Any unusual sounds, heat, or smell
  • Final bed status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is the priority. Always troubleshoot logically from external causes first. Document findings thoroughly and escalate when internal motor failure is suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
