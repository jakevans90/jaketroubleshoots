Laborie Portascan 3D

Battery not holding charge

Asset Type

Bladder Scanner

Manufacturer

Laborie

Model

Portascan 3D

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Portascan 3D short battery runtime, charging failure, shutdowns, or battery indication problems from external causes.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the Portascan 3D is not being used for an active patient scan.

If the unit shuts down during use or cannot remain powered long enough to complete a scan, provide clinical staff with another available bladder scanner.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not delayed by a device with unreliable battery power.

Confirm the Reported Battery Problem

Ask staff what they observed.

Common reports may include:

  • Battery drains quickly
  • Unit shuts off after charging
  • Battery icon does not increase
  • Scanner only works while connected to power
  • Charging indicator does not appear
  • Device powers off during startup or scanning

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified before replacing parts or accessories.

Check the External Charger and Power Adapter

Inspect the charger, power supply, and cord for cuts, bent pins, loose strain relief, broken connectors, or signs of overheating.

Connect the charger firmly to the scanner or charging base, depending on the facility setup.

Expected outcome: The charger should connect securely and show normal charging behavior.

If the charger is damaged or excessively warm, remove it from service and test with a known-good compatible charger if available.

Verify the Wall Outlet

Plug the charger into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.

Avoid power strips, loose wall outlets, switched outlets, or outlets controlled by bed or headwall switches.

Expected outcome: The scanner begins charging normally from a verified power source.

If the device charges normally from another outlet, document the original outlet concern and stop troubleshooting the scanner.

Check the Charging Indicator

Observe the Portascan 3D display or charging light while connected to power.

Look for a battery icon, charging symbol, LED, or other indication that charging has started.

Expected outcome: The device should indicate that external power is connected and the battery is charging.

If there is no charging indication with a known-good outlet and charger, continue troubleshooting.

Inspect Charging Contacts and Connector Area

Check the scanner charging port, cradle contacts, and battery contact area if externally accessible.

Look for debris, bent contacts, corrosion, fluid residue, cracked plastic, or loose fit.

Do not probe aggressively or disassemble internal battery compartments beyond normal user-accessible areas.

Expected outcome: Contacts and connectors are clean, dry, aligned, and secure.

If contamination is present, clean only according to facility-approved cleaning practices and manufacturer guidance.

Reseat the Scanner on the Charging Base

If the Portascan 3D uses a docking or charging cradle, remove and reseat the scanner.

Make sure it sits fully in the cradle and is not blocked by a cover, strap, label, or debris.

Expected outcome: The charging indicator appears consistently when the scanner is docked.

If charging starts after reseating, monitor briefly, document the cradle seating issue, and stop.

Test With a Known-Good Charger or Dock

If another compatible charger, power supply, or cradle is available, test the scanner with it.

Do not mix chargers unless compatibility is confirmed by facility records, labeling, or manufacturer documentation.

Expected outcome: The scanner should charge normally with a verified compatible charging setup.

If the device charges normally with another charger or dock, remove the failed accessory from service.

Allow a Full Charge Cycle

Leave the scanner connected to verified power long enough for a normal charge cycle.

After charging, power the unit on and observe whether the battery indicator shows a reasonable charge level.

Expected outcome: The battery level should rise and remain stable long enough for normal clinical use.

If the battery still drains quickly after a full charge, continue.

Check for Storage or Use Pattern Issues

Ask staff whether the scanner has been left off the charger, stored unplugged, or used repeatedly without being returned to the charging base.

Battery complaints are sometimes caused by workflow rather than equipment failure.

Expected outcome: The scanner’s charging routine is confirmed.

If the issue is related to charging habits, educate staff and document the finding.

Observe Runtime Under Normal Use

With the battery charged, power on the scanner and perform a basic functional check using normal startup and scan preparation screens.

Do not use an active patient for testing unless clinical staff require it and it is safe.

Expected outcome: The scanner remains powered without sudden shutdown, rapid battery drop, or low-battery alarm.

If the scanner shuts down quickly or the battery drops abnormally fast, suspect battery deterioration or internal charging circuitry.

Check for Heat, Smell, or Physical Battery Concerns

Inspect the scanner and charger for unusual heat, swelling, odor, discoloration, or melted plastic.

Expected outcome: No abnormal heat, smell, swelling, or damage is present.

If any of these are found, stop troubleshooting immediately, remove the device from service, and escalate for repair evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the Portascan 3D still will not hold a charge after verifying the outlet, charger, cradle, charging contacts, charge cycle, and normal use pattern, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely related to the battery pack, charging circuit, internal power connection, or power management electronics.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, battery replacement evaluation, or bench service per facility policy

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated bedside testing on a bladder scanner with unreliable power.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot battery runtime on an active patient care request if it delays scanning. Provide staff with another bladder scanner first, then evaluate the suspect unit away from patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Laborie Portascan 3D battery would not hold charge and the scanner powered off shortly after removal from the charger."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet and charger operation, but the scanner battery level dropped rapidly after a full charge cycle."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the Portascan 3D from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for battery and charging system evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Outlet tested
  • Charger or power adapter inspected
  • Known-good charger tested
  • Charging base or dock tested
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Battery level before and after charging
  • Runtime observed after full charge
  • Any sudden shutdowns
  • Unusual heat, smell, swelling, or discoloration
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery complaints should be approached logically before assuming internal failure. Verify power, charger, contacts, docking, and use pattern first. If the scanner still cannot maintain charge, remove it from clinical use and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
