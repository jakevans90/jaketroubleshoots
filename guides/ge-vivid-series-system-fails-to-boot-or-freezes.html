GE Healthcare Vivid Series

System Fails to Boot or Freezes

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Vivid Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting boot failures, freezing during startup, or system lockups caused by power, peripherals, or software-related issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the system from clinical use before troubleshooting.
  • If actively being used, transfer the patient to another imaging system.

Why it matters: A frozen or non-booting system cannot safely support diagnostic imaging.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the system and observe behavior:
  • No power at all
  • Stuck on splash screen
  • Freezes during startup
  • Random lockups after boot

Expected: Issue is consistent and reproducible.

Why it matters: Confirms whether this is a true system fault or intermittent issue.

Check Power Source and Connections

  • Verify system is plugged into a known working outlet
  • Check power cord for damage or loose connection
  • Confirm power strip (if used) is functional
  • Observe system power indicators

Expected: Stable power with normal indicator lights.

Why it matters: Power instability is a common cause of boot failure and freezing.

Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Shut down the system completely (if responsive)
  • Disconnect AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect and power on

Expected: System boots normally.

Why it matters: Clears temporary software or memory faults.

Disconnect External Accessories

  • Remove all non-essential connections:
  • Probes
  • USB devices
  • Network cables
  • Printers or external monitors
  • Attempt to boot with minimal configuration.

Expected: System boots without freezing.

Why it matters: Faulty probes or peripherals can cause boot delays or lockups.

Inspect Probe Ports and Connections

  • Check probe ports for bent pins or debris
  • Reconnect one probe at a time after boot

Expected: No physical damage; system remains stable.

Why it matters: Damaged probe connectors can interfere with system initialization.

Check Cooling and Ventilation

  • Ensure vents are not blocked
  • Listen for internal fans during startup
  • Check for excessive heat

Expected: Fans operate normally and airflow is unobstructed.

Why it matters: Overheating can cause freezing or prevent proper boot.

Observe for Error Messages or Codes

  • Note any error messages on screen
  • Identify if the system stops at a specific stage

Expected: Any errors are documented for escalation.

Why it matters: Helps determine if issue is software, hardware, or storage related.

Attempt Alternate Power Source (If Available)

  • Plug the system into a different known-good outlet.

Expected: Same or improved behavior.

Why it matters: Rules out facility power issues.

If the Problem Persists

If the system continues to fail to boot or freeze after external checks:

  • Likely internal issue (hard drive, motherboard, power supply, or software corruption)
  • Remove the system from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or advanced bench evaluation

Proper troubleshooting includes knowing when to stop and escalate.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a freezing or boot-failing ultrasound system on an active patient. Always move the patient to a functioning system before proceeding.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ultrasound system reported as not booting and freezing on startup screen."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"System continued to freeze during boot with all peripherals disconnected, indicating likely internal hardware or software failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for vendor repair and diagnostics."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Boot failures and system freezes are often caused by simple external issues like power or connected accessories. Always rule those out first before escalating. Patient safety and proper documentation remain the priority.

That is successful troubleshooting.
