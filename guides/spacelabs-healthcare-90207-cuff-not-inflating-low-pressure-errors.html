Spacelabs Healthcare 90207

Cuff Not Inflating or Low Pressure Errors

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Spacelabs Healthcare

Model

90207

What This Guide Helps With

Cuff not inflating, weak inflation, or low pressure errors caused by leaks, tubing issues, cuff problems, or pump-related faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the cuff from the patient before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: No active measurement during troubleshooting.
  • Why it matters: Prevents discomfort and inaccurate readings during testing.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Start a manual BP measurement and observe behavior.
  • Check if the pump attempts to inflate, if there is pressure buildup, and if there are immediate error messages.
  • Expected: Clear understanding of whether this is no inflation, slow inflation, or pressure loss.
  • Why it matters: Helps differentiate between leak vs pump vs control issue.

Inspect the Cuff for Leaks or Damage

  • Check the cuff bladder and outer material.
  • Look for tears, punctures, or worn Velcro.
  • Gently squeeze the cuff to feel for escaping air.
  • Swap with a known good cuff.
  • Expected: No air leaks; cuff holds pressure.
  • Why it matters: Damaged cuffs are the most common cause of low pressure errors.

Check the Air Hose and Connections

  • Inspect tubing from cuff to monitor.
  • Ensure hose is fully connected at both ends.
  • Look for cracks, kinks, or loose fittings.
  • Swap tubing if available.
  • Expected: Secure, airtight connection with no visible damage.
  • Why it matters: Even small leaks prevent proper pressure buildup.

Verify Proper Cuff Size and Application

  • Ensure cuff size matches patient arm circumference.
  • Apply snugly, not loose.
  • Align artery marker correctly.
  • Expected: Proper fit allows accurate pressure readings.
  • Why it matters: Loose or incorrect cuff fit can mimic pressure loss.

Check for Obstructions in the Air Path

  • Ensure tubing is not pinched under equipment or bedding.
  • Check connectors for debris or blockage.
  • Expected: Unrestricted airflow.
  • Why it matters: Blockages prevent inflation or cause slow pressure rise.

Listen for Pump Operation

  • Initiate a reading and listen closely.
  • Observe if pump motor sound is present or absent and if sound is weak or abnormal.
  • Expected: Audible pump engagement during inflation.
  • Why it matters: No sound may indicate pump failure or internal issue.

Power Cycle the Device

  • Power off completely.
  • Remove and reinsert batteries if applicable.
  • Power back on and retest.
  • Expected: System resets and attempts normal inflation.
  • Why it matters: Clears minor control or firmware faults.

If the Problem Persists

If cuff, tubing, connections, and setup have been verified and the issue remains, the problem is likely internal (pump, pressure sensor, or valve failure).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and saves troubleshooting time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot cuff inflation issues on an active patient. Move the patient to a functioning device to ensure accurate and timely blood pressure monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit reported with cuff not inflating and low pressure error during BP cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Air leak identified in cuff tubing causing inability to maintain pressure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced cuff and tubing assembly; verified proper inflation and normal BP readings."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Cuff swapped with known good
  • Tubing inspected/replaced
  • Pump audible or silent
  • Any visible leaks or damage
  • Error messages displayed
  • Device passed/failed functional test
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Start with the simplest components—cuff and tubing—before assuming device failure. Most inflation issues are external. Escalate appropriately once those are ruled out, and always prioritize patient safety and accurate documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
