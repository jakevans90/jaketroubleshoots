Siemens Healthineers MOBILETT Elara Max

Detector not detected, not charging, or wireless connection fault

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

MOBILETT Elara Max

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting detector recognition, charging, docking, battery, and wireless communication issues before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the MOBILETT Elara Max is not being used for an active patient exam.

If the detector fault occurs during an exam, have clinical staff stop the study, protect the patient, and move to another available imaging device if imaging is urgent.

Expected outcome: No patient imaging depends on a detector with unreliable communication or charge status.

Confirm the Exact Detector Complaint

Ask the technologist what happened and when the issue appeared.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Detector not detected
  • Detector detected intermittently
  • Detector not charging
  • Detector battery low or depleted
  • Wireless connection lost
  • Detector unavailable for exposure
  • Detector connected but not ready
  • Issue occurs with one detector or multiple detectors

Expected outcome: The fault is narrowed to recognition, charging, battery, wireless communication, or detector readiness.

Check Whether the Detector Is Assigned to the Correct System

Confirm the detector belongs with, or is paired to, the MOBILETT Elara Max being used.

If multiple mobile X-ray systems or wireless detectors are in the area, verify the detector has not been swapped between systems.

Expected outcome: The detector is associated with the correct mobile unit and not being searched for by the wrong system.

Inspect the Detector Externally

Remove the detector from patient use and visually inspect it.

Check for:

  • Cracked housing
  • Bent or damaged corners
  • Liquid contamination
  • Damaged charging contacts
  • Missing labels or asset tags
  • Signs of being dropped
  • Unusual heat, smell, or swelling

Expected outcome: The detector has no obvious physical damage that would make continued use unsafe.

If damage, swelling, overheating, or liquid intrusion is found, stop troubleshooting and remove the detector from service.

Check Detector Battery Status

Review the detector battery indication on the system display, detector indicator lights, or charging interface if available.

If the detector battery is low, place the detector on the approved charger or docking location and allow it to charge.

Expected outcome: The detector shows charging activity and eventually reaches a usable charge level.

If the detector is fully discharged, it may need time on charge before it can communicate reliably.

Verify the Detector Is Properly Seated for Charging

Place the detector fully into the charging bay, holder, or docking location according to normal site workflow.

Confirm it is not slightly misaligned, blocked by a cover, or prevented from making contact by debris.

Expected outcome: Charging begins and the system recognizes the detector charge status.

Inspect Charging Contacts and Dock Area

With the device out of clinical use, inspect the detector charging contacts and charging bay contacts.

Look for:

  • Dirt or film on contacts
  • Bent contacts
  • Broken spring contacts
  • Foreign objects in the dock
  • Damaged detector cover interfering with seating

Expected outcome: Charging surfaces are clean, aligned, and able to make contact.

Do not scrape contacts aggressively or use unapproved cleaners. If contacts are damaged, escalate for repair.

Confirm the Mobile X-Ray System Has Adequate Power

Check that the MOBILETT Elara Max is powered on normally and has adequate system battery charge.

If the mobile system itself is low on charge, connect it to a known-good AC outlet and allow the system to stabilize.

Expected outcome: Detector charging or wireless communication is not being affected by the mobile system’s own low-power condition.

Check the AC Outlet and Power Cord

If detector charging depends on the mobile system being connected to AC, verify the cart is plugged into a working hospital-grade outlet.

Check:

  • Power cord fully seated
  • No visible cord damage
  • Outlet has power
  • No tripped breaker or switched outlet issue
  • Charging indicator on the mobile unit

Expected outcome: The mobile system is receiving AC power and can support detector charging.

Restart the Detector Workflow From the User Interface

Have the technologist exit the exam setup or detector selection screen and reselect the detector if possible.

If the detector is listed but not ready, select it again or refresh the detector status using normal operator workflow.

Expected outcome: A temporary detector selection or readiness state clears without further action.

Power Cycle the Mobile System Safely

Confirm no patient exam is active and no unsaved images are pending.

Perform a normal shutdown and restart of the MOBILETT Elara Max.

Avoid hard power interruption unless normal shutdown is not possible and local policy allows it.

Expected outcome: The system software reloads and attempts to re-establish detector communication.

If the detector is recognized after restart, verify readiness before returning to clinical use.

Check Wireless Environment and Distance

Keep the detector near the MOBILETT Elara Max during troubleshooting.

Remove unnecessary physical barriers between the detector and mobile unit when possible.

Consider whether the issue occurs only in one room, hallway, department, or area with heavy wireless traffic.

Expected outcome: Detector communication improves when distance and interference are reduced.

Check for Detector Sleep or Timeout Behavior

If the detector has been idle, confirm whether it needs to be awakened by normal movement, placement, or selection from the system.

Some wireless detector issues are caused by a detector being asleep, deeply discharged, or not active in the current exam workflow.

Expected outcome: The detector wakes and becomes available after proper selection or charging.

Compare With Another Known-Good Detector If Available

If site workflow allows, test the MOBILETT Elara Max with another compatible known-good detector.

Also test the suspect detector on its normal charging location or approved workflow.

Expected outcome:

  • If another detector works, the original detector, battery, charging contacts, or pairing may be the issue.
  • If no detector works, the mobile system, wireless interface, charging bay, or software may be the issue.

Check for Recent Changes

Ask whether anything changed before the issue started.

Examples include:

  • Detector swapped between systems
  • Mobile system recently moved departments
  • Wireless network changes
  • Software update
  • Detector dropped
  • Battery recently replaced
  • Charging bay damaged
  • Issue started after cleaning

Expected outcome: Recent changes help identify whether the issue is environmental, accessory-related, or system-related.

Verify the Error Message or Status Code

Record the exact detector message shown on the system.

Examples may include detector not available, detector not ready, wireless connection lost, battery low, charging fault, or detector communication failure.

Expected outcome: The work order contains specific fault information instead of a vague “not working” description.

Perform a Limited Functional Check

Once the detector is recognized and charged, perform a non-patient readiness check according to site policy.

Confirm:

  • Detector is detected
  • Battery status is acceptable
  • Wireless status is stable
  • Detector shows ready
  • No active detector error remains
  • System allows normal exam setup

Expected outcome: The detector and mobile system are ready for clinical use.

If the issue returns during readiness checks, remove the device or detector from service.

If the Problem Persists

If the detector is still not detected, will not charge, loses wireless connection, or remains unavailable after external checks, common user-accessible causes have been ruled out.

The device or detector should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Likely areas for repair evaluation may include detector battery, detector charging contacts, charging bay, wireless communication hardware, detector pairing/configuration, internal power path, or system software.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue clinical use with an unreliable wireless detector.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot detector recognition or charging problems during an active patient exposure workflow. Move the patient to another available system first, especially if the detector disconnects, reports low battery, or becomes unavailable during exam setup.

Maintain therapy continuity by protecting patient imaging workflow, preserving completed images, and using a backup imaging device before continuing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Technologist reported the MOBILETT Elara Max wireless detector was not detected and would not become ready for portable chest exams."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Detector battery was fully depleted and the detector was not fully seated in the charging bay, preventing charging contact."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated detector in charger, verified charging indication, allowed sufficient charge time, restarted system, and confirmed detector was recognized and ready for use."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact detector error message
  • Detector serial number or asset tag
  • Whether the issue affects one detector or multiple detectors
  • Detector battery level
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Whether the detector was fully seated in the charger
  • Condition of charging contacts
  • Whether the detector was recently dropped or cleaned
  • Wireless signal or connection status
  • Room or department where issue occurred
  • Whether another detector was tested
  • Whether AC power was connected to the mobile system
  • Final device and detector status

Final Thought

Detector issues on a mobile X-ray system should be approached logically: protect the patient first, confirm the complaint, check battery and charging conditions, verify detector assignment, and rule out wireless or external setup problems before escalating. Accurate CCR documentation helps separate a simple charging issue from a true detector, wireless, or system fault.

That is successful troubleshooting.
