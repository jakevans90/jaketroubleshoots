Puritan Bennett 840

Alarm: NO O2 SUPPLY Loss of oxygen source

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Puritan Bennett

Model

840

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Alarm: NO O2 SUPPLY Loss of oxygen source condition, indicating loss of oxygen input and inability to deliver oxygen-enriched ventilation to the patient.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient
  • Provide alternate ventilation (bag-valve mask or backup ventilator) if needed
  • Confirm adequate oxygenation is maintained

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the NO O2 SUPPLY alarm is displayed
  • Observe if alarm is continuous or intermittent
  • Check for drop in delivered FiO₂
  • Note when the alarm occurs (startup vs operation)

Check Oxygen Source Connection

  • Ensure oxygen hose is securely connected to wall outlet
  • Ensure hose is firmly connected to ventilator inlet
  • Inspect for loose or disconnected fittings

Verify Wall Oxygen Supply

  • Test wall outlet with a known-good device or flowmeter
  • Confirm oxygen flow is present
  • Ensure wall valve is open and active

Inspect Oxygen Supply Hose Condition

  • Check for kinks, cracks, or visible damage
  • Replace with known-good hose if needed

Check Oxygen Inlet Filter

  • Inspect filter for blockage or contamination
  • Replace if dirty or clogged

Check Pressure Switch Connections

  • Inspect externally accessible tubing or wiring
  • Ensure connections are secure

Run Extended Self-Test (EST)

  • Perform EST per facility procedure
  • Confirm no oxygen supply-related failures are reported

If the Problem Persists

If all external oxygen sources, hoses, filters, and connections are confirmed functional and the alarm remains, an internal ventilator fault is likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot oxygen supply alarms on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup ventilation method before investigating.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator alarming NO O2 SUPPLY and unable to deliver oxygen."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Oxygen hose disconnected from wall outlet causing loss of supply."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reconnected oxygen hose and verified proper oxygen delivery. Alarm cleared."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Oxygen supply alarms must be treated as critical failures. Always verify external gas sources first and escalate promptly when unresolved. Proper troubleshooting ensures patient safety and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
