Drager Babyleo TN500 IncuWarmer

Heater Output / Temperature Regulation Fault

Asset Type

Infant Incubator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Babyleo TN500 IncuWarmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting heater output or temperature regulation faults caused by setup, airflow, sensor, accessory, or environmental issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

If the Babyleo TN500 is in use with a patient and there is a heater output or temperature regulation fault, do not continue troubleshooting while relying on the affected unit for thermal support.

Move the infant to another approved warming or incubator device according to clinical procedure.

Expected outcome: Patient temperature support is maintained on a known-good device.

If this resolves the immediate care concern, stop and document the action taken.

Confirm the Reported Fault

Review the display and confirm the exact message, alarm, or temperature regulation behavior reported by staff.

Note whether the issue involves:

  • Low temperature alarm
  • High temperature alarm
  • Heater output not increasing
  • Temperature overshoot
  • Inconsistent air temperature
  • Skin temperature not matching expected trend
  • Fault appears during prewarming, incubator mode, radiant warmer mode, or transition between modes

Expected outcome: The fault type and operating mode are clearly identified.

Check the Current Operating Mode and Temperature Setpoint

Confirm whether the unit is operating in incubator mode, radiant warmer mode, manual mode, air temperature control, or skin temperature control.

Verify that the selected setpoint matches the clinical expectation and is not unusually low or high.

Expected outcome: The temperature regulation issue is not caused by an incorrect mode or setpoint selection.

If correcting the mode or setpoint resolves the issue, return the device to clinical staff if appropriate and stop.

Inspect for Open Panels, Access Doors, or Canopy Position Issues

Check that the canopy, access doors, side panels, hand ports, and mattress area are properly closed and seated.

An open panel or incomplete canopy closure can cause heat loss and unstable temperature regulation.

Expected outcome: The patient compartment is properly enclosed for the selected mode.

If closing or reseating a panel restores normal temperature control, document the finding and stop.

Check for Airflow Obstruction

Inspect the mattress area, air vents, warm air paths, and surrounding surfaces for blankets, positioning aids, tubing, labels, or supplies blocking airflow.

Do not remove clinically required positioning items without clinical approval.

Expected outcome: Air circulation paths are clear and the device can regulate temperature normally.

If removing an obstruction resolves the issue, document what was found and stop.

Verify Environmental Conditions Around the Unit

Check whether the Babyleo is positioned near a draft, HVAC vent, open door, exterior wall, direct sunlight, portable fan, or other heat-producing equipment.

Environmental changes can make the device appear to underheat, overheat, or regulate inconsistently.

Expected outcome: The incubator is not being affected by external airflow or heat sources.

If relocating the unit or correcting the environmental issue resolves the problem, stop and document.

Inspect the Skin Temperature Probe and Placement

If skin temperature control is being used, inspect the skin temperature probe for damage, contamination, loose connection, or improper placement.

Confirm that the probe is secured properly and has good contact with the intended monitoring site.

Expected outcome: The temperature probe is connected, intact, and positioned correctly.

If a loose or misplaced probe caused the fault, correct the setup, verify stable temperature response, and stop.

Swap the Skin Temperature Probe if Available

If the fault appears related to skin temperature feedback, test with a known-good compatible skin temperature probe.

Expected outcome: The unit responds normally with a known-good probe.

If replacing the probe resolves the issue, remove the defective probe from service and document the accessory failure.

Check for Contamination or Moisture Near Sensors and Air Paths

Inspect accessible areas for spilled fluid, cleaning residue, condensation, lint, adhesive residue, or visible contamination.

Do not open internal covers or perform deep disassembly during frontline troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: No external contamination is affecting temperature sensing or airflow.

If contamination is found externally, clean according to facility-approved procedures and reassess.

Allow the Device to Stabilize After Setup Changes

If the canopy, mode, setpoint, humidity, mattress position, or probe placement was recently changed, allow the device a reasonable stabilization period while monitoring displayed temperatures and alarms.

Expected outcome: Temperature readings trend toward the selected setpoint without repeated faults.

If the device stabilizes and alarms clear, document the adjustment and stop.

Check Power Source and Startup Behavior

Confirm the power cord is fully seated, the outlet is functioning, and the device is not operating with unstable power.

Observe whether the heater fault appears immediately at startup or only after heating begins.

Expected outcome: The unit has stable power and completes startup without recurring heater-related faults.

If power correction resolves the issue, document the outlet or cord condition and stop.

Compare Displayed Temperature Trends

Compare the displayed air temperature, skin temperature, and set temperature trends if available.

Look for obvious mismatch, sudden jumps, no change during heating, or readings that do not match the clinical environment.

Expected outcome: Temperature feedback appears logical and changes gradually.

If readings are erratic or physically unrealistic, suspect probe, sensor, or internal regulation fault and continue to escalation.

Restart Only When Safe and Appropriate

If the device is not supporting a patient, power the unit off, wait briefly, and restart it.

Observe whether the same heater output or temperature regulation fault returns.

Expected outcome: The unit restarts normally and does not repeat the heater fault.

If the fault clears and does not return during observation, document the restart and monitor per facility policy.

Remove From Service if the Fault Repeats

If the heater output fault, temperature regulation fault, unstable temperature behavior, or sensor-related temperature issue returns after external checks, do not continue attempting repeated resets.

Expected outcome: The unit is treated as unreliable for infant thermal support until evaluated.

If the Problem Persists

If the problem persists after checking setup, panels, airflow, environment, probes, accessories, power, and restart behavior, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely related to an internal heater control, temperature sensing, calibration, wiring, airflow, or control system fault.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A neonatal warming device with unreliable temperature regulation should not be returned to patient use until it has been fully evaluated and verified.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot heater regulation issues while the infant depends on the affected Babyleo TN500 for temperature support. Move the patient to another safe warming device first, then troubleshoot only when patient care is protected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Drager Babyleo TN500 was alarming for temperature regulation and not maintaining the selected air temperature."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the canopy access panel not fully closed, causing heat loss and unstable temperature control."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Closed and verified all access panels, observed temperature recovery toward setpoint, and returned the unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact alarm or fault message displayed
  • Alarm behavior
  • Operating mode at time of fault
  • Set temperature and displayed air temperature
  • Skin temperature reading if used
  • Skin probe condition and placement
  • Accessories swapped, including whether the skin probe was swapped with a known-good probe
  • Canopy, hand ports, and panels checked
  • Airflow obstruction found or ruled out
  • Environmental factors, including drafts, vents, sunlight, or room conditions noted
  • Power behavior, including whether the outlet was tested and power cord condition was checked
  • Whether fault occurred at startup or during heating
  • Indicator lights, display messages, or heater status shown during the fault
  • Any unusual smell, heat, fan noise, or silence
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Temperature regulation faults on an infant incubator require a patient-safety-first approach. Start with setup, enclosure, airflow, environment, probes, and power before suspecting internal failure. If the Babyleo TN500 cannot reliably maintain temperature after external causes are ruled out, remove it from service and document the troubleshooting clearly using the CCR method.

That is successful troubleshooting.
