ICU Medical Plum 360 Infusion Pump

Door or Cassette Errors / Not Recognized

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting an ICU Medical Plum 360 infusion pump that fails to recognize the cassette or reports door errors. Symptoms may include:

  • Pump displays “Close Door,” “Door Error,” or “Cassette Not Detected” messages.
  • Pump fails to start infusion due to recognition issues.
  • Intermittent or consistent error messages even when the cassette is correctly inserted.

Most recognition issues are caused by external factors such as misaligned cassettes, debris in the door mechanism, or connector contact issues. Internal failures are less common but possible.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Pump Power

  • Ensure the pump is powered on.
  • Check battery charge or AC connection. A low battery may prevent proper sensor operation.

Inspect the Cassette

  • Confirm the correct ICU Medical Plum 360 cassette is used.
  • Check for visible cracks, deformation, or foreign material.
  • Make sure the cassette is fully seated in the pump.

Check Door Mechanism

  • Open and close the pump door several times to clear any mechanical obstruction.
  • Inspect for debris, fluid residue, or misaligned latches that could prevent proper closure.

Clean Contact Points

  • Wipe electrical contacts on both the cassette and pump with a lint-free cloth.
  • Ensure no corrosion or moisture is present. Proper contact is critical for recognition.

Reinsert and Test

  • Insert the cassette again, making sure it clicks into place.
  • Observe if the pump now recognizes the cassette.

Swap Components if Available

  • If another compatible cassette is available, test with it to rule out a defective cassette.
  • If the door or latch mechanism seems faulty, test another pump to isolate the issue.

If the Problem Persists

All external causes (cassette seating, door mechanism, contact cleaning) have been ruled out.

The error may be due to an internal sensor or board failure.

Action:

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label “Out of Service.”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Clinical Engineering should document findings and avoid further use until repaired.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to another functioning pump first.
  • Only handle the cassette and pump when the patient is safely connected to another device.
  • Keep replacement cassettes nearby for rapid switch if needed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Pump displays ‘Door Error’ and does not recognize cassette; infusion cannot start.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed cassette seated correctly, cleaned contacts, door mechanism checked; problem persists with multiple cassettes.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Power source tested (battery and AC)
  • Door mechanism inspected and cleaned
  • Cassette(s) tested
  • Error message displayed exactly as shown
  • Pump removed from patient safely

Final Thought

Door or cassette recognition errors are often caused by simple mechanical or contact issues. Stepwise, logic-based troubleshooting ensures patient safety and avoids unnecessary internal disassembly. Proper CCR documentation captures both the problem and your actions, supporting departmental record-keeping and cost avoidance. Knowing when to escalate is a key part of effective Clinical Engineering.

That is successful troubleshooting.
