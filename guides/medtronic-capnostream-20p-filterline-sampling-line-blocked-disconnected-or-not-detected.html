Medtronic Capnostream 20p

FILTERLINE / SAMPLING LINE BLOCKED, DISCONNECTED, OR NOT DETECTED

Asset Type

Capnography Monitor

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Capnostream 20p

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting FilterLine, sampling line, blockage, disconnection, or detection issues affecting CO₂ monitoring on the Capnostream 20p.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure patient safety first.

If the Capnostream 20p is actively monitoring a patient and CO₂ monitoring is required, move the patient to another working monitor before troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Patient ventilation monitoring continues without interruption.

Why it matters: A sampling line problem can prevent reliable EtCO₂, respiration rate, apnea, and no-breath alarm monitoring.

Verify the reported issue.

Confirm the exact message or symptom being reported. Look for conditions such as:

  • FilterLine not detected
  • Sampling line disconnected
  • Sampling line blocked
  • No CO₂ waveform
  • Missing EtCO₂ value
  • No breath detection
  • Intermittent CO₂ readings

Expected outcome: The reported issue is confirmed before replacing parts or removing the unit from service.

Inspect the FilterLine connection at the monitor.

Check that the FilterLine is fully seated in the Capnostream 20p CO₂ port. Remove and reconnect it firmly.

Expected outcome: The monitor recognizes the FilterLine and begins sampling normally.

If this resolves the issue, document the loose connection and stop.

Inspect the full sampling line path.

Check the line from the monitor to the patient for kinks, pinching, stretching, crushing, fluid contamination, or disconnection.

Expected outcome: The sampling path is open and physically intact.

Why it matters: A blocked or restricted sampling line can prevent the monitor from pulling a proper sample.

Check for patient-end disconnection or incorrect placement.

Verify the cannula, airway adapter, or sampling connection is properly attached and positioned for the patient setup being used.

Expected outcome: The patient connection is correct and able to deliver a valid breath sample to the monitor.

Replace the FilterLine with a known-good compatible line.

Install a new or known-good Medtronic-compatible FilterLine. Avoid reusing a line that appears clogged, wet, contaminated, or damaged.

Expected outcome: The monitor detects the replacement FilterLine and CO₂ monitoring resumes.

If this resolves the issue, the original FilterLine was likely blocked, damaged, expired, contaminated, or defective.

Check for moisture or contamination.

Inspect the removed FilterLine and connector area for moisture, secretions, condensation, medication residue, blood, or visible contamination.

Expected outcome: No contamination is present at the monitor port or sampling line connector.

Why it matters: Moisture or debris can cause blockage alarms, detection problems, or unreliable CO₂ readings.

Confirm the correct accessory type is being used.

Verify staff are using the correct FilterLine type for the clinical application, such as nasal/oral sampling, intubated airway sampling, or pediatric/neonatal use where applicable.

Expected outcome: The accessory matches the intended patient setup and monitoring mode.

Why it matters: An incorrect sampling accessory can cause poor breath detection or unreliable readings.

Restart the monitor if the issue appears after accessory replacement.

If the FilterLine has been replaced and the connection is correct, power the monitor off and back on when safe to do so.

Expected outcome: The monitor initializes normally and recognizes the sampling line.

If this resolves the issue, document that the issue cleared after replacing the line and restarting the monitor.

Test with a known-good setup off patient.

Use a known-good FilterLine and a safe test setup to confirm the monitor can detect sampling activity. Do not perform testing on an active patient unless clinically safe and approved.

Expected outcome: The monitor detects the line and displays appropriate CO₂ response or sampling behavior.

Inspect the CO₂ inlet area externally.

Check the monitor’s FilterLine port for visible damage, broken plastic, loose fit, debris, or signs of fluid exposure. Do not insert tools deeply into the port or perform internal disassembly during basic troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: The port appears intact and holds the FilterLine securely.

Remove from service if detection or blockage errors continue with known-good lines.

If multiple compatible FilterLines produce the same blocked, disconnected, or not detected condition, suspect an internal sampling system, port, pump, sensor, or pneumatic issue.

Expected outcome: The device is removed from clinical use and sent for bench evaluation or repair.

If the Problem Persists

If the Capnostream 20p continues to show FilterLine, sampling line blocked, disconnected, or not detected errors after checking the connection, replacing the FilterLine, inspecting the line path, confirming the patient setup, and testing with a known-good accessory, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

At this point, the issue may involve the internal sampling pump, CO₂ inlet, pneumatic pathway, detection hardware, or internal CO₂ module. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot sampling line problems on an active patient if CO₂ monitoring is clinically required. Move the patient to another working monitor first. A missing or unreliable CO₂ waveform can delay recognition of apnea, hypoventilation, airway obstruction, or disconnection.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported the Capnostream 20p displayed a sampling line blocked/not detected message and would not display a reliable CO₂ waveform."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the installed FilterLine kinked near the bed rail and not allowing proper sampling."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced the FilterLine with a compatible known-good line, verified the monitor detected the line, confirmed CO₂ waveform returned, and returned the unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact displayed message or alarm
  • Whether the issue was blocked, disconnected, or not detected
  • FilterLine replaced
  • Correct accessory confirmed
  • Patient-end connection checked
  • Sampling line checked for kinks or moisture
  • CO₂ waveform present or absent
  • EtCO₂ value present, missing, or unstable
  • Respiration rate present or missing
  • Monitor restarted
  • Known-good FilterLine tested
  • CO₂ port condition
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Sampling line troubleshooting should stay logical and external first. Confirm patient safety, verify the message, check the connection, inspect the line, replace the FilterLine, and confirm the monitor can sample correctly. If the problem follows the monitor after known-good accessories are used, remove it from service and document the work clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
