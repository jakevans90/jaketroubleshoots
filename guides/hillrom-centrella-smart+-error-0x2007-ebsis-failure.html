Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x2007 EBSIS Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0x2007 EBSIS Failure indicating a system-level communication or control failure, which may result in loss of bed movement, control functions, or alarm capability.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use if critical functions are affected
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed
  • Ensure patient is not dependent on malfunctioning equipment

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error 0x2007 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup vs during operation)
  • Check for loss of movement, controls, or communication
  • Note any additional alarms or abnormal behavior

3. Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Unplug the bed from AC power
  • Wait at least 60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart the bed

4. Check Power Source and Input Stability

  • Test the wall outlet with another device
  • Ensure the power cord is fully seated and not damaged
  • Confirm stable and continuous power supply

5. Inspect External Cables and Connections

  • Check visible cables for damage, pinching, or wear
  • Ensure all accessible connectors are firmly seated
  • Look for loose or disconnected wiring

6. Check for Environmental or Physical Issues

  • Inspect for fluid intrusion or contamination
  • Look for signs of impact damage
  • Ensure electronics areas are clean and dry

7. Isolate the Failure

  • Confirm error persists after power cycle
  • Verify no external power or cable issues are present
  • Identify continued loss of system functionality

If the Problem Persists

If external causes have been ruled out and Error 0x2007 persists, the issue likely indicates an internal communication or control failure within the EBSIS subsystem.

  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the System Control Board (SCB)

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a bed with active system errors while a patient is using it. Always transfer the patient to a backup device before continuing diagnostics.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x2007 EBSIS Failure with loss of control functions during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Error persisted after power cycle and external checks, indicating internal communication failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and sent for repair. System Control Board replacement required."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

System-level errors like EBSIS failures require a structured approach—rule out simple external causes first, then escalate quickly when internal failures are suspected to maintain patient safety.

That is successful troubleshooting.
