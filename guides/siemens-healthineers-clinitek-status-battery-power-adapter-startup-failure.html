Siemens Healthineers CLINITEK Status+

Battery / Power Adapter / Startup Failure

Asset Type

Urine Analyzer

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

CLINITEK Status+

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting CLINITEK Status+ power, battery, adapter, charging, or startup problems caused by external or user-accessible issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the analyzer is not being used for active patient testing.

If testing is needed, have staff use another available urine analyzer or follow the facility’s downtime procedure.

Expected outcome: Patient testing is not delayed by troubleshooting.

Why it matters: A urine analyzer with unstable power may shut down during testing and affect result availability.

Verify the Reported Power Complaint

Ask clinical staff what was observed:

  • Unit will not power on
  • Unit powers on then shuts off
  • Battery will not charge
  • AC adapter not recognized
  • Screen is blank
  • Startup freezes or does not complete

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified before parts or settings are blamed.

Check the AC Outlet

Plug another known-working low-risk device or outlet tester into the same outlet.

Confirm the outlet is live and not controlled by a wall switch, power strip, or failed UPS.

Expected outcome: The outlet supplies stable power.

If the outlet is dead or intermittent, relocate the analyzer or report the facilities issue. If this resolves the problem, stop.

Inspect the Power Adapter Externally

Verify the correct Siemens-approved power adapter is being used for the CLINITEK Status+.

Inspect the adapter brick, wall plug, strain relief, and DC output cord for:

  • Cuts
  • Cracks
  • Bent connector
  • Loose strain relief
  • Burn marks
  • Unusual heat or smell

Expected outcome: Adapter and cable appear intact and appropriate for the analyzer.

If damaged, remove the adapter from service and replace it with an approved adapter. If this resolves the issue, stop.

Check the DC Power Connection at the Analyzer

Confirm the power plug is fully seated in the analyzer.

Gently wiggle the connector while watching for power flicker, charging indication, or screen activity. Do not force the connector.

Expected outcome: The connector remains stable and does not cause intermittent power.

If the analyzer powers on only when the connector is held in a certain position, suspect a damaged adapter plug or analyzer power jack. Remove the unit from service for bench evaluation.

Try a Known-Good Approved Power Adapter

If available, connect a known-good compatible power adapter from another working CLINITEK Status+.

Expected outcome: The analyzer powers on and remains stable.

If the analyzer works with the known-good adapter, replace the original adapter. If the issue remains, continue troubleshooting.

Check Battery Condition and Seating

Power the unit off and disconnect AC power before checking the battery compartment.

Verify the battery is installed correctly, seated securely, and not swollen, leaking, corroded, or physically damaged.

Expected outcome: Battery is properly installed and physically safe.

If the battery is swollen, leaking, or damaged, do not continue using it. Remove the analyzer from service and follow facility battery handling procedures.

Attempt AC-Only Startup

If safe and allowed by facility procedure, remove the battery or leave the battery disconnected, then connect the analyzer to the approved AC adapter.

Attempt to power the analyzer on.

Expected outcome: The analyzer starts on AC power.

If the analyzer starts on AC only, suspect a depleted, failed, or incorrectly seated battery. Replace the battery if appropriate and retest.

Allow Time for a Deeply Discharged Battery

If the analyzer has been unused or stored for a long period, connect it to AC power and allow charging time before retesting startup on battery.

Expected outcome: Charging indication appears and the analyzer eventually powers on normally.

If there is no charge indication or the battery still fails after adequate charging time, suspect battery failure or internal charging circuit failure.

Check for Startup Behavior

Observe what happens when the power button is pressed:

  • No lights or display
  • Brief flash then shutdown
  • Beep without display
  • Startup screen appears but freezes
  • Analyzer boots only on AC
  • Analyzer boots only on battery

Expected outcome: Startup behavior helps separate adapter, battery, display, and internal power faults.

Inspect for Environmental or Fluid Exposure

Look for signs of urine, cleaning fluid, corrosion, residue, or contamination around the power jack, battery area, keypad, display, and strip table area.

Expected outcome: No evidence of liquid ingress or contamination.

If fluid exposure is suspected, remove the analyzer from service. Do not continue applying power until it is evaluated.

Perform a Controlled Restart

Disconnect AC power, power the unit off, wait briefly, reconnect AC power, and attempt startup again.

Expected outcome: The analyzer completes normal startup.

If the analyzer starts normally after restart and remains stable, return to service only after verifying basic operation and documenting the event.

Confirm Basic Operation After Power Is Restored

Once the analyzer powers on, confirm:

  • Display is readable
  • Keypad or touchscreen input responds
  • Date and time are correct
  • Battery or AC status appears normal
  • No startup errors are displayed
  • Strip table/feed area initializes normally

Expected outcome: Analyzer is powered, stable, and ready for normal operational checks.

If the unit powers on but displays repeated errors, freezes, or loses settings, remove it from service for further evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the CLINITEK Status+ still will not power on, will not charge, shuts down unexpectedly, or fails startup after outlet, adapter, cable, battery, and connection checks have been completed, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as a power input jack, charging circuit, battery interface, display power issue, internal connection fault, or main electronics problem.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated power cycling or adapter swapping if there is heat, smell, fluid exposure, intermittent power, or suspected internal failure.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a power or startup issue while the analyzer is needed for active patient testing. Move testing to another analyzer or follow downtime procedures first. A device that loses power during testing may interrupt workflow, delay results, or create uncertainty about completed patient or QC tests.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported the CLINITEK Status+ would not power on and the battery appeared not to charge."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet power was good, but the original AC adapter had an intermittent DC connector and the analyzer powered normally with a known-good adapter."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced the faulty power adapter, confirmed stable startup on AC power, verified battery charging indication, and returned the analyzer to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed live
  • Power strip or UPS used, if applicable
  • Correct adapter verified
  • Known-good adapter tested
  • Battery removed, reseated, or replaced
  • Battery condition checked for swelling, leakage, or corrosion
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Startup screen behavior
  • Any beeps, error messages, or blank screen condition
  • Evidence of fluid exposure, residue, heat, or odor
  • Whether unit works on AC only, battery only, both, or neither
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed from service

Final Thought

Power problems should be approached logically and safely. Start with the outlet, adapter, cable, and battery before suspecting internal failure. If the analyzer remains unstable or shows signs of damage, remove it from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
