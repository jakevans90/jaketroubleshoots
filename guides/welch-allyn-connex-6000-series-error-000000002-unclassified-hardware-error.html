Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000002 Unclassified Hardware Error

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000002 indicating hardware fault causing startup failure, intermittent operation, or loss of monitoring capability.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use. Transition to another monitoring device.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring continues safely on alternate equipment.
  • Why it matters: Hardware-related errors can lead to unreliable or failed vital sign readings.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error #000000002 appears. Observe when it occurs (startup, during measurement, or intermittently).
  • Expected: Error is consistently displayed or reproducible.
  • Why it matters: Confirms this is a true system-reported hardware condition, not a transient glitch.

3. Perform a Full Power Reset

  • Shut down the device. Remove AC power and battery (if accessible). Wait 30–60 seconds, then reconnect and restart.
  • Expected: Device boots normally without error.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary faults and resets internal hardware communication.
  • If resolved → Return to service
  • If error persists → Continue troubleshooting

4. Check Power Source and Power Components

  • Verify the device is plugged into a known working outlet. Inspect power adapter and cord for damage or looseness.
  • Expected: Stable power with no interruption.
  • Why it matters: Power instability can trigger hardware-related system errors.
  • Try a different outlet and/or known-good power adapter if available

5. Inspect Battery Condition (if equipped)

  • Remove and reseat the battery. Check for swelling, overheating, or poor connection.
  • Expected: Battery seats properly with no visible damage.
  • Why it matters: Faulty batteries can cause internal hardware communication errors.
  • If questionable → Remove battery and operate on AC only, then retest

6. Disconnect All Accessories and Modules

  • Remove all connected accessories (SpO₂ probe, NIBP hose, temperature probe, external modules). Restart the device with nothing connected.
  • Expected: Device powers on without error.
  • Why it matters: A faulty accessory can present as a hardware error.
  • If error clears → Reconnect accessories one at a time to isolate the cause

7. Inspect Physical Condition of the Device

  • Check for signs of damage (drops, cracks, liquid intrusion, debris in ports).
  • Expected: No visible damage or contamination.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage often correlates with internal hardware faults.

8. Verify Software Stability (if accessible)

  • Observe for freezing, lag, or abnormal behavior before/after the error appears.
  • Expected: Stable interface and normal operation.
  • Why it matters: Some hardware errors are triggered by failed communication between internal components and software.

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and Error #000000002 continues:

  • This strongly indicates an internal hardware failure (e.g., main board, internal communication bus, or subsystem failure)
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for vendor repair or qualified bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a monitor displaying hardware-related errors for patient care. Always transfer monitoring before attempting troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device displaying Error #000000002 (unclassified hardware error), unable to reliably monitor vital signs."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"External causes ruled out (power source verified, accessories removed, battery checked). Error persists, indicating likely internal hardware failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair/bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior and frequency
  • Accessories swapped during troubleshooting
  • Power behavior during operation
  • Environmental factors (liquid exposure, drops)
  • Indicator lights or display behavior
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Hardware error codes like this are critical indicators—not just alerts. Focus on ruling out external causes quickly, then escalate appropriately to protect patient safety and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
