Stryker S3

Error: N/C Battery Low

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting the N/C Battery Low message indicating inadequate nurse call battery voltage and potentially unreliable nurse call signaling. Because the affected function supports patient requests for assistance, the bed should not be relied upon for nurse-call-dependent care until the condition is corrected and Nurse Call operation is verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the bed is being used for a patient whose care depends on the bed's Nurse Call function, remove the bed from that clinical application until the condition is corrected.
  • Provide an alternate verified nurse call method or transfer the patient to a bed with confirmed Nurse Call operation according to facility procedure.
  • Do not troubleshoot the issue while a patient is relying on the affected bed to request assistance.

Expected: The patient is not relying on a bed with a potentially unavailable Nurse Call function.

Why it matters: A failed or unreliable nurse call connection can delay a patient's ability to request assistance.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Associated Behavior

  • Confirm that the display shows: N/C Battery Low.
  • Verify that the bed is equipped with the Nurse Call option.
  • Document whether the message appears at startup, remains continuously displayed, or occurs intermittently.
  • Determine whether Nurse Call is not activating, operates intermittently, or whether the message returns after power cycling.
  • Note any other nurse call or bed function concerns reported by clinical staff.

Expected: The displayed message is confirmed on a Nurse Call-equipped bed and the associated behavior is identified.

Why it matters: This confirms a Nurse Call battery-related condition rather than an unrelated bed issue.

3. Confirm Bed Power and Basic Operating Condition

  • Verify that the bed is connected to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Confirm that the bed powers on and otherwise operates normally.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, and visible bed connections for damage, looseness, contamination, or signs of overheating.

Expected: The bed has stable power and no obvious external power-related damage is present.

Why it matters: Although the message concerns the Nurse Call battery, stable bed power should be confirmed before further evaluation.

4. Inspect the Nurse Call Interface and External Connections

  • Inspect the nurse call cable, plug, receptacle, and accessible connection points.
  • Check for loose or disconnected connections, bent or damaged pins, frayed or pinched cable sections, fluid contamination, or broken connector housings.
  • Reseat accessible nurse call connections as appropriate.
  • Observe whether the N/C Battery Low message remains present after accessible connection checks.

Expected: Nurse Call interface connections are secure and physically intact.

Why it matters: A damaged or poorly connected nurse call interface can contribute to unreliable operation and should be corrected before further isolation.

5. Inspect the Nurse Call Battery Condition and Connection

  • With the bed removed from patient use, inspect the nurse call battery and its accessible associated connections.
  • Check for a loose battery connection, damaged wiring or connector, corrosion, contamination, swelling, leakage, or other battery damage.
  • Confirm that the battery is correct for the Nurse Call system and was not previously left disconnected.
  • Treat the displayed message as an indication that the nurse call battery voltage has fallen below the required threshold of 5 V.

Expected: The battery connection is secure and the battery is either identified as low or damaged, or an accessible connection issue is identified.

Why it matters: This message most directly indicates insufficient Nurse Call backup battery voltage.

6. Replace the Low Nurse Call Battery as Indicated

  • If the battery is low, damaged, aged, disconnected, or unable to maintain adequate operating voltage, replace it with the correct approved battery for the bed's Nurse Call system.
  • Ensure the replacement battery connection is fully seated.
  • Confirm that associated wiring is safely routed and not pinched or damaged.
  • Observe whether the N/C Battery Low message clears after correction.

Expected: A correct, properly connected battery is installed and the low-battery message no longer appears.

Why it matters: The message is expected to resolve once adequate Nurse Call battery voltage is restored.

7. Verify Nurse Call Operation After Correction

  • Confirm that N/C Battery Low is no longer displayed.
  • Connect the bed to the appropriate nurse call test setup or approved clinical interface.
  • Activate Nurse Call from the bed.
  • Confirm the call is correctly received and annunciated at the appropriate destination.
  • Repeat testing as needed to confirm reliable operation before returning the bed to service.

Expected: The message is cleared and Nurse Call operates normally and consistently.

Why it matters: Clearing the message alone is not sufficient; the affected patient communication function must be verified.

8. Stop When Resolved

  • If correcting the battery connection or replacing the nurse call battery clears the message and Nurse Call passes functional testing, document the repair.
  • Return the bed to service according to department procedure only after reliable Nurse Call operation is confirmed.

If the Problem Persists

If N/C Battery Low remains displayed after confirming the bed is equipped with Nurse Call, inspecting external connections, and replacing or correcting the nurse call battery, common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent message may indicate a fault in the nurse call battery circuit, charging or monitoring path, wiring, or associated control electronics.

Remove the bed from service for nurse-call-dependent care, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation according to facility procedure.

Do not return a bed to service when its Nurse Call function cannot be verified as reliable.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Do not evaluate a nurse call battery fault while a patient is relying on that bed for assistance requests. Move the patient to a backup bed with verified Nurse Call operation, or provide an approved alternate verified call method, before troubleshooting begins.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displayed N/C Battery Low. Nurse Call function considered unreliable pending evaluation because the displayed message indicates low nurse call battery voltage."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Nurse call battery found below acceptable operating condition and unable to maintain required voltage, resulting in the N/C Battery Low message. External nurse call cable and accessible connections inspected with no additional damage noted."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from nurse-call-dependent use, replaced nurse call battery, secured associated connections, confirmed message cleared, and verified Nurse Call activation operated normally before returning bed to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

The N/C Battery Low message should be treated as a patient communication safety concern, not only a battery replacement issue. Safe troubleshooting confirms the displayed condition, rules out visible connection problems, restores adequate battery power, verifies Nurse Call operation, and escalates the bed for repair when reliable function cannot be confirmed.

That is successful troubleshooting.
