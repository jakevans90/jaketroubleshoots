Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: NBP Equip Malfunction

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot NBP Equip Malfunction on the Philips HeartStart MRx when the defibrillator cannot reliably perform non-invasive blood pressure measurement. This condition may prevent NBP readings from completing, cause cuff inflation failure, or make the NBP subsystem unreliable for patient monitoring.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the HeartStart MRx is being used on a patient and NBP monitoring is needed, transfer blood pressure monitoring to another approved device.
  • Do not rely on the MRx NBP function while this error is active.
  • Continue using a backup monitor or alternate blood pressure method according to facility protocol.
  • Expected outcome: Patient monitoring continues without depending on a failed NBP subsystem.
  • Why it matters: The defibrillator may still support other functions, but the NBP function should be considered unreliable until evaluated.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows Error: NBP Equip Malfunction.
  • Observe when the error occurs: during startup, when NBP is selected, during cuff inflation, during measurement, or intermittently after movement or transport.
  • Check whether the NBP pump attempts to run, whether the cuff inflates, and whether the measurement aborts.
  • Expected: The error is reproducible or clearly observed.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported NBP hardware condition instead of a user setup issue.

Check the NBP Cuff

  • Inspect the cuff for tears, worn hook-and-loop material, damaged bladder, contamination, or incorrect sizing.
  • Install a known-good compatible cuff and attempt an NBP measurement.
  • Expected outcome: The NBP measurement completes without the error if the original cuff was the cause.
  • Why it matters: A leaking or damaged cuff can mimic NBP system failure by preventing proper pressurization.

Check the NBP Hose and Tubing

  • Inspect the NBP hose for cracks, kinks, flattened sections, loose fittings, or contamination inside the connector.
  • Replace the hose with a known-good compatible hose if available.
  • Expected outcome: The cuff inflates normally and the error clears if the hose or tubing was leaking or restricted.
  • Why it matters: The NBP module depends on a sealed pneumatic path. Leaks or restrictions can cause failed pressure generation or abnormal pressure sensing.

Inspect the NBP Connector at the MRx

  • Check the NBP port on the HeartStart MRx for damage, looseness, debris, cracked plastic, bent fittings, or poor hose engagement.
  • Reconnect the NBP hose firmly and verify it locks or seats correctly.
  • Expected outcome: The connector is secure and does not leak or disconnect during inflation.
  • Why it matters: A poor external connection can cause failed NBP operation before the internal module is suspected.

Check for External Leak Behavior

  • Start an NBP cycle using a known-good cuff and hose.
  • Listen for air leaks at the cuff, hose, connector, and patient-end fitting.
  • Watch for weak inflation, rapid deflation, or repeated failed attempts.
  • Expected outcome: The pneumatic path holds pressure normally during inflation.
  • Why it matters: Leak-related failures should be ruled out before replacing or repairing the NBP module.

Check for Physical Damage or Fluid Exposure

  • Inspect the MRx around the NBP connector and side housing for impact damage, residue, cleaning-fluid intrusion, or signs the unit was dropped.
  • Look for cracked housing, loose fittings, sticky residue, or evidence of contamination near the pneumatic connection.
  • Expected outcome: No obvious external damage or contamination is present.
  • Why it matters: Damage near the NBP port may indicate a connector, tubing, or module problem requiring bench repair.

Confirm the Issue Is Not Accessory-Related

  • Repeat the test using a known-good cuff and known-good hose.
  • If the error clears, remove the failed accessory from service and complete functional verification before returning the MRx to use.
  • Expected outcome: The MRx performs NBP correctly with known-good accessories.
  • Why it matters: This isolates the issue to the cuff or hose instead of the defibrillator.

Evaluate for Internal Tubing or Module Failure

  • If the error remains with known-good accessories, suspect an internal NBP pneumatic or hardware failure.
  • Possible causes include internal tubing leak, internal tubing disconnection, failed NBP pump, failed pressure sensor, or failed NBP module hardware.
  • Do not open the device or attempt internal repair during field troubleshooting.
  • Expected outcome: External causes have been ruled out, and the failure appears internal to the MRx.

Stop When Resolved

  • If replacing the cuff or hose resolves the issue, document the failed accessory.
  • Complete an NBP functional check, clean the unit as needed, and return it to service only after the error does not recur.
  • Expected outcome: NBP measurements complete successfully without recurrence of the error.

If the Problem Persists

If Error: NBP Equip Malfunction remains after checking the cuff, hose, connector, and external pneumatic path, external causes have been ruled out.

Remove the Philips HeartStart MRx from service.

Label the device Out of Service.

Send the defibrillator for repair or bench evaluation. Persistent NBP Equip Malfunction may indicate an internal pneumatic leak, failed pump, failed pressure sensor, or NBP module hardware failure. After repair, required NBP functional checks and calibration should be completed before returning the defibrillator to service.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot NBP malfunction on an active patient when blood pressure monitoring is clinically required. Move the patient or NBP monitoring task to a backup device first, then evaluate the MRx only after patient care is supported by reliable equipment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx reported Error: NBP Equip Malfunction. Staff reported NBP would not complete measurements and cuff inflation failed or aborted during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Checked NBP cuff, hose, connector, and external pneumatic path. Known-good cuff and hose did not clear the error. Findings indicate likely internal NBP hardware malfunction."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed defibrillator from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for bench evaluation and repair. NBP functional checks and calibration required before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

NBP Equip Malfunction should be approached logically: protect the patient first, verify the error, rule out cuffs, hoses, and external leaks, then escalate if the failure remains with known-good accessories. Clear CCR documentation helps show that external causes were ruled out before repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
