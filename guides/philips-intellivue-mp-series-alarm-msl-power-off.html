Philips IntelliVue MP Series

Alarm: MSL Power Off

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

IntelliVue MP Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot MSL Power Off INOP conditions on Philips IntelliVue MP Series patient monitors when attached IntelliVue measurement devices may be drawing too much power from the monitor. This condition can affect connected MMS devices, measurement modules, parameter availability, and overall monitoring reliability.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the Philips IntelliVue MP Series monitor is not the only active source of patient monitoring before disconnecting modules or cycling power.
  • If the patient requires continuous monitoring, have clinical staff transfer monitoring to another working monitor first.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring remains uninterrupted.
  • Why it matters: The MSL Power Off condition may affect connected measurement devices and monitoring reliability.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact INOP message displayed is: MSL Power Off.
  • Observe whether it appears at startup, after connecting an MMS or module, intermittently during use, only with a specific device attached, or after moving or reconnecting the monitor setup.
  • Check whether attached devices lose function, stop communicating, or disappear from the monitor display.
  • Expected: The INOP is confirmed and tied to attached device power loading.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported condition instead of a general communication or setup issue.

Remove the Monitor From Active Use Before Isolation

  • If the alarm is active on a patient monitor, remove the monitor from patient use before disconnecting modules or performing power cycling.
  • Label or track the monitor according to department process while troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Troubleshooting is performed safely away from active patient care.

Disconnect All MMS and Attached MSL Devices

  • Disconnect all attached MMS devices, measurement modules, or MSL-connected accessories from the monitor.
  • Inspect each connection point for bent pins, loose fit, contamination, liquid residue, cracked connector housings, damaged cables, or damaged module shells.
  • Expected: All external connected devices are removed and available for inspection.
  • Why it matters: The MSL Power Off INOP may be caused by attached devices drawing too much power from the monitor.

Power Cycle the Monitor With No Attached Devices

  • Turn the monitor off, wait briefly, then power it back on with the attached devices still disconnected.
  • Confirm whether MSL Power Off clears.
  • Expected: The monitor powers up without the MSL Power Off INOP.
  • Why it matters: If the message clears with all devices removed, the issue is likely caused by one attached device or accessory load.

Reconnect Devices One at a Time

  • Reconnect one MMS or attached device at a time.
  • After each device is connected, allow the monitor to recognize it and watch for recurrence of MSL Power Off.
  • Continue until all devices are reconnected or until the INOP returns.
  • Expected: The monitor remains stable, or the specific device causing recurrence is identified.
  • Why it matters: This isolates whether the issue follows one attached device or occurs with multiple devices.

Identify a Suspect Device or Combination

  • If MSL Power Off returns after connecting a specific module or MMS, disconnect that device and test again.
  • If available, try that same suspect device on another compatible monitor or try a known-good equivalent device on the affected monitor.
  • Expected: The issue follows the suspect attached device, or the monitor itself remains suspect.
  • Why it matters: This separates an accessory or device power issue from a monitor-side power or MSL problem.

Check for Overloaded Configuration

  • Review the connected setup and determine whether too many devices are attached at once.
  • Remove nonessential modules and confirm whether the monitor operates normally with a reduced configuration.
  • Expected: The monitor operates normally when the load is reduced.
  • Why it matters: The INOP points to excessive power demand from attached devices.

Inspect for Physical or Liquid Damage

  • Inspect the monitor connection area, MMS, modules, and attached cables for evidence of impact or fluid exposure.
  • Do not continue using any accessory or device with visible damage, burning smell, heat, corrosion, or liquid contamination.
  • Expected: Damaged components are removed from use.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage can cause abnormal current draw and trigger MSL power protection.

Stop When Resolved

  • If disconnecting a faulty attached device or reducing the connected load clears the MSL Power Off INOP, keep the suspect device out of service.
  • Return the monitor only if it operates normally with approved accessories.
  • Verify normal startup, parameter recognition, and alarm behavior before returning the monitor to service.
  • Expected: Monitor functions normally without recurrence of the INOP.

If the Problem Persists

If MSL Power Off remains active with all attached devices disconnected, or if the INOP returns with multiple known-good devices, external and common causes have been ruled out.

This strongly indicates a monitor-side power, MSL interface, or internal hardware fault.

Remove the monitor from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot MSL power issues on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup monitor first, then isolate modules and attached devices safely. Maintain therapy and monitoring continuity, and avoid relying on a monitor with unresolved power-related INOPs.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips IntelliVue MP Series monitor displayed MSL Power Off INOP. Attached measurement devices intermittently lost function after connection."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Excessive power draw suspected from attached MSL or MMS device. INOP cleared after disconnecting all devices and recurred when one attached device was reconnected."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed monitor from patient use, disconnected all attached devices, power cycled monitor, and reconnected devices one at a time. Identified suspect attached device and removed it from service. Monitor verified operational with known-good configuration."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact alarm behavior, including whether MSL Power Off occurred at startup or after device connection.
  • Accessories swapped, including MMS devices, measurement modules, MSL-connected accessories, or known-good equivalents.
  • Power behavior, including whether the INOP cleared after power cycling with all attached devices disconnected.
  • Environmental factors, including liquid exposure, contamination, impact, heat, burning smell, or damaged connection points.
  • Indicator lights, missing parameters, communication loss, or attached devices disappearing from the monitor display.
  • Final device status, including whether the monitor was returned to service, the suspect device was removed, or the monitor was sent for repair.

Final Thought

The MSL Power Off INOP should be treated as a power-loading condition until proven otherwise. Start by protecting the patient, remove attached devices, power cycle the monitor, and reconnect devices one at a time. If the problem follows a connected device, remove that device from service. If it stays with the monitor, escalate for repair. Clear CCR documentation helps show what was isolated and why the device was or was not returned to use.

That is successful troubleshooting.
