STERIS AMSCO 4085

Error: BATT FUSE

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

STERIS AMSCO 4085

What This Guide Helps With

Error: BATT FUSE indicating battery fuse missing or blown, resulting in loss of battery operation or failure to power on without mains connection.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the table from patient use if battery operation is required or movement is limited
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning table if necessary

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the table and confirm “BATT FUSE” is displayed
  • Check if the table only operates when connected to mains power
  • Observe if battery-powered functions are unavailable

Confirm Mains Power Connection

  • Plug the table into a known working outlet
  • Verify power indicator is illuminated
  • Attempt movement while plugged in

Inspect External Power Components

  • Check power cord for damage or loose connections
  • Ensure cord is fully seated
  • Test with an alternate outlet if needed

Check Battery Charging Behavior

  • Leave table plugged in for several minutes
  • Observe for charging indicators
  • Unplug and test for battery operation

Inspect Accessible Fuse Area (If Applicable)

  • Check for presence of battery fuse if externally accessible
  • Look for signs of blown fuse (darkened or broken filament)

Stop if Resolved

  • If fuse replacement restores function, clear the error
  • Verify battery operation by unplugging and testing movement
  • Return the table to service

If the Problem Persists

If the BATT FUSE error remains after confirming power and fuse condition, external causes have been ruled out.

  • Remove the table from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before evaluating battery or power-related issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Table displaying BATT FUSE error and only operates when plugged into mains."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery fuse found missing or blown, preventing battery circuit operation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified mains power function, inspected fuse condition, and removed unit from service for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery fuse errors indicate a loss of backup power capability, which is critical for safe surgical positioning. Always confirm external power conditions first, then escalate when internal faults are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
