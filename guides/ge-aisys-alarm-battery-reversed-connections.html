GE Healthcare Aisys

Alarm: BATTERY REVERSED CONNECTIONS

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Aisys

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the BATTERY REVERSED CONNECTIONS alarm on a GE Healthcare Aisys anesthesia machine. This alarm may appear with a No battery backup message and indicates the machine is detecting incorrect battery voltage polarity or a battery connection problem.

A battery backup alarm should be treated as a patient safety issue because the anesthesia machine may not support normal operation during a loss of mains power.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from patient use if battery backup cannot be confirmed.
  • If the machine is in use, notify clinical staff and follow facility procedure for transferring the patient to a safe anesthesia setup.
  • Do not rely on the anesthesia machine during a power interruption while this alarm is active.
  • Confirm patient care is supported by equipment with reliable backup power before troubleshooting.

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact alarm displayed is BATTERY REVERSED CONNECTIONS.
  • Confirm whether the screen also displays No battery backup.
  • Observe whether the alarm appears at startup, after battery replacement, after service, after moving the machine, or intermittently during use.
  • Check whether the unit otherwise powers normally from AC mains power.

3. Connect to Known Good AC Power

  • Verify the anesthesia machine is plugged into a working hospital-grade outlet.
  • Confirm the power cord is fully seated at the machine and wall outlet.
  • Check that the machine powers normally on AC mains power.
  • Do not continue troubleshooting battery backup concerns while also dealing with an uncertain AC power source.

4. Remove the Device From Service Before Battery Inspection

  • Power the machine down according to facility procedure before accessing battery connections.
  • Label the device unavailable for patient use while battery backup is suspect.
  • Use appropriate precautions when handling battery wiring.
  • Do not allow the machine to return to clinical use until battery backup status is acceptable.

5. Inspect Battery Connections

  • Check the battery terminals and wiring connections.
  • Look for reversed positive and negative connections.
  • Check for loose battery terminals, disconnected battery leads, damaged wiring, corrosion, or signs of recent battery service.
  • Verify that the correct battery type is installed according to facility procedure.
  • Correct obvious connection issues only if they are within your facility’s Clinical Engineering scope and procedure.

6. Check for Recent Service or Battery Replacement

  • Ask whether the alarm started after battery replacement, power supply service, internal battery inspection, transport, relocation, or a previous repair attempt.
  • Review recent work order notes if available.
  • Pay close attention to any service event where battery wiring may have been disturbed.

7. Reconnect and Restart the System

  • After confirming the battery connections are correct and secure, reconnect power and restart the Aisys.
  • Observe startup behavior.
  • Confirm whether the BATTERY REVERSED CONNECTIONS alarm clears.
  • Confirm the No battery backup message is no longer present.

8. Confirm Battery Backup Status

  • Verify the machine no longer reports no battery backup.
  • Allow the unit to remain powered and observe for repeat alarms.
  • Follow facility procedure for checking battery backup readiness before returning the unit to service.
  • Do not treat one cleared alarm as the full repair until battery backup status is confirmed acceptable.

9. Stop When Resolved

  • If the alarm clears after correcting or securing the battery connection, document the issue and correction.
  • Confirm the machine passes required operational checks.
  • Return the device to service only after battery backup status is acceptable.
  • The likely cause was a service-accessible battery connection issue, not a confirmed internal device failure.

If the Problem Persists

If the BATTERY REVERSED CONNECTIONS alarm continues after AC power, battery connections, polarity, terminals, and recent battery service have been checked, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent reversed battery connection alarm may indicate a battery harness issue, damaged battery connector, incorrect battery installation, battery voltage sensing problem, power supply fault, internal charging circuit fault, or controller/power management failure.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not keep the anesthesia machine in service with an unresolved No battery backup condition.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this alarm on an active patient unless patient safety is already secured. Move the patient to a backup anesthesia machine or approved alternate ventilation/anesthesia setup first. A machine reporting no battery backup should not be trusted during procedures where loss of mains power could interrupt anesthesia delivery.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Aisys anesthesia machine displayed BATTERY REVERSED CONNECTIONS alarm with No battery backup message."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery backup alarm confirmed. AC power verified. Battery leads inspected for reversed polarity, loose terminals, damaged wiring, corrosion, and recent service-related connection error."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service, inspected and secured battery connections, restarted system, confirmed BATTERY REVERSED CONNECTIONS alarm cleared, and verified battery backup status before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A battery reversed connection alarm should be treated as a patient safety and backup power issue, not just a nuisance message. Start with safe removal from use, confirm the alarm, check the simple battery connection causes first, and escalate if the machine still reports no battery backup after wiring is verified. Clear documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the facility.

That is successful troubleshooting.
