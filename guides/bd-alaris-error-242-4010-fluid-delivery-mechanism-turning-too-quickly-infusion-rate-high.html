BD Alaris

Error: 242.4010 Fluid delivery mechanism turning too quickly (infusion rate high)

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 242.4010 indicates the fluid delivery mechanism is rotating faster than expected, suggesting a high infusion rate condition. This can lead to over-infusion, inaccurate delivery, or automatic pump shutdown to protect the patient.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the infusion immediately
  • Clamp the tubing
  • Transfer the patient to another verified working pump

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm Error 242.4010 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup, start of infusion, or during infusion)
  • Listen for rapid or irregular motor sounds
  • Watch for unexpected high flow or immediate alarm conditions

3. Check Infusion Programming

  • Verify infusion rate (mL/hr)
  • Confirm VTBI settings
  • Review guardrails or drug library limits
  • Ensure no accidental high-rate entry

4. Inspect IV Set Installation

  • Confirm correct BD Alaris-compatible administration set is used
  • Ensure tubing is fully seated in the pumping channel
  • Verify cassette (if applicable) is properly latched
  • Reload the set and close the door securely

5. Check for Free Flow or Low Resistance

  • Inspect for open clamps or bypass conditions
  • Check for gravity-assisted flow conditions
  • Ensure the line is properly connected and not disconnected downstream

6. Inspect Pump Channel Condition

  • Check for fluid contamination or residue
  • Look for debris interfering with the mechanism
  • Inspect for visible wear or damage

7. Power Cycle and Retest

  • Turn the device off and wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power it back on
  • Load a new set if available
  • Perform a test infusion off patient

If the Problem Persists

If Error 242.4010 continues after all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Likely internal motor control or encoder feedback failure
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot or continue infusion on an active patient when high-rate errors occur. Always move the patient to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"BD Alaris pump displaying Error 242.4010 with high infusion rate alarm during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified correct programming and IV set installation; error persisted indicating motor/encoder control issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed module from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

High infusion rate errors indicate a loss of control over fluid delivery accuracy. After ruling out setup and external causes, escalate quickly to ensure patient safety and maintain device reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
