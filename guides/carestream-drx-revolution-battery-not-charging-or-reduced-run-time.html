Carestream DRX-Revolution

Battery Not Charging or Reduced Run Time

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

Carestream

Model

DRX-Revolution

What This Guide Helps With

Battery charging issues, short run time, docking/AC power problems, charger faults, or battery-related mobile X-ray readiness concerns.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the mobile X-ray system from clinical use if battery status is low, charging is unreliable, or the unit may shut down during imaging.

Action:

Use another mobile X-ray unit for patient exams until the charging or run-time issue is corrected.

Expected outcome:

Patient imaging is not interrupted by unexpected power loss.

Why it matters:

A mobile X-ray system with unreliable battery operation may fail during transport, positioning, exposure setup, or image processing.

Verify the Reported Battery Issue

Confirm whether the problem is truly a battery issue or a power connection issue.

Action:

Ask clinical staff what they observed:

  • Unit will not charge
  • Battery percentage does not increase
  • Unit shuts down quickly after unplugging
  • Unit only works while plugged in
  • Charging indicator does not appear
  • Battery drains faster than normal
  • Error message appears during startup or charging

Expected outcome:

The reported symptom is clearly identified before replacing parts or escalating.

Check the AC Power Source

Start with the wall outlet or charging location.

Action:

Plug the DRX-Revolution into a known-good hospital-grade outlet. Confirm the outlet has power using an outlet tester or another known-working device if appropriate.

Expected outcome:

The unit recognizes AC power and begins charging.

If resolved:

Document the failed outlet or power source and stop troubleshooting the mobile unit.

Inspect the Power Cord and Plug

Check for external damage before assuming internal charger failure.

Action:

Inspect the power cord for cuts, crushed insulation, bent blades, loose strain relief, damaged plug ends, or signs of overheating.

Expected outcome:

The cord should be intact, secure, and free of heat damage.

If damage is found:

Remove the cord from use and replace it with the correct approved power cord.

Verify the Cord Is Fully Seated

A loose AC connection can mimic a battery or charger problem.

Action:

Confirm the power cord is fully inserted at both the wall outlet and the mobile X-ray system power inlet.

Expected outcome:

The charging indicator or AC power status should appear normally.

If resolved:

The issue was a loose or partially seated power connection.

Check for Charging Indicators

Confirm whether the system sees incoming AC power.

Action:

Observe the display, battery icon, power indicator, or charging status when the unit is connected to AC power.

Expected outcome:

The system should indicate that AC power is connected and charging is active.

If no charging status appears:

Continue checking external power, power cord, inlet condition, and system status.

Inspect the AC Power Inlet Area

Look for obvious external damage at the power entry point.

Action:

With the unit unplugged, visually inspect the AC inlet area for looseness, cracked plastic, bent contacts, debris, discoloration, or signs of overheating.

Expected outcome:

The inlet should be secure and undamaged.

If damage, heat, or burning smell is present:

Remove the unit from service and escalate for repair.

Allow Adequate Charging Time

A deeply discharged unit may need time on AC power before normal operation is restored.

Action:

Leave the unit connected to a verified AC source for an appropriate charging period. Monitor whether the battery percentage increases.

Expected outcome:

Battery level should gradually rise.

If battery level does not increase:

Continue troubleshooting.

Confirm the Unit Is Not Being Used Heavily While Charging

Charging may appear slow if the system is powered on, processing images, or being used repeatedly.

Action:

Ask whether the unit was being charged while actively used. Place the unit in a normal charging state and allow it to charge without repeated exams or movement.

Expected outcome:

Battery charge should increase more predictably when the system is not under active load.

Check for Battery Age or History

Reduced run time is often related to battery age, usage, or charge cycle history.

Action:

Review service history, prior battery replacements, repeated battery complaints, and any recent work orders for charging or run-time issues.

Expected outcome:

You may identify a pattern suggesting battery degradation rather than a one-time charging issue.

Check for Environmental or Storage Issues

Mobile X-ray battery performance can be affected by poor charging habits or storage conditions.

Action:

Ask whether the unit is regularly left unplugged, stored away from charging locations, or used heavily without full recharge cycles.

Expected outcome:

Operational habits may explain reduced run time.

If resolved by workflow change:

Educate the department to return the unit to AC power when not in use.

Perform a Controlled Run-Time Check

If the battery charges but drains quickly, verify the complaint under controlled conditions.

Action:

Fully charge the unit, disconnect from AC power, and observe battery percentage drop during normal idle and operational use.

Expected outcome:

Battery level should decline at a reasonable rate.

If the battery drops rapidly:

Battery degradation, internal power draw, or charging system issues are likely.

Restart the System if Charging Status Appears Frozen

A software status display issue may make charging appear abnormal.

Action:

Safely power down and restart the unit while connected to verified AC power.

Expected outcome:

Battery and AC power status should refresh normally.

If resolved:

Monitor the unit and document that the charging display recovered after restart.

Check for Related System Errors

Battery and charging problems may appear with additional system alerts.

Action:

Look for battery, charger, AC input, power supply, thermal, or system communication messages on the display.

Expected outcome:

Any related messages should be documented exactly as shown.

If errors are present:

Include the exact wording in the work order and escalate if the error persists.

Verify Final Device Readiness

Before returning the unit to service, confirm it can charge and operate reliably.

Action:

Confirm:

  • AC power is detected
  • Battery level increases while charging
  • Unit remains powered on when unplugged
  • Battery run time is acceptable for clinical use
  • No charging or power errors remain
  • No unusual heat, smell, or noise is present

Expected outcome:

The DRX-Revolution is safe to return to clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If the Carestream DRX-Revolution still will not charge, only operates on AC power, loses battery charge rapidly, shows recurring battery errors, or has signs of overheating, the common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as a degraded battery pack, charging circuit problem, power supply issue, battery communication fault, or internal power management failure.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated clinical use of a mobile X-ray system with unreliable battery operation.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot battery or charging problems while the unit is needed for an active patient exam. Move the imaging request to another mobile X-ray system first. A mobile X-ray unit that may shut down unexpectedly should not be trusted for patient care until battery operation is verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported that the Carestream DRX-Revolution would not hold a charge and had reduced run time during portable exams."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified AC outlet and power cord were good, but the unit charged slowly and battery level dropped rapidly after disconnecting from AC power."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the DRX-Revolution from service, labeled it Out of Service, documented battery behavior, and sent the unit for bench evaluation of the battery and charging system."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed good
  • Power cord inspected or swapped
  • Charging indicator present or absent
  • Battery percentage before and after charging
  • Approximate run time after full charge
  • Whether unit works on AC power
  • Whether unit shuts down when unplugged
  • Any battery, charger, or power error messages
  • AC inlet condition
  • Unusual heat, smell, or sound
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery complaints on a mobile X-ray system should be handled carefully because they directly affect device readiness, transport reliability, and exam completion. Start with the outlet, cord, charging status, and operating conditions before assuming an internal failure. If the unit still cannot charge or maintain run time, remove it from service and document the findings clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
