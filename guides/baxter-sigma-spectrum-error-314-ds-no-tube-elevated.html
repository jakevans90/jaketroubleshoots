Baxter SIGMA Spectrum

Error: 314 DS no-tube elevated

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Baxter

Model

SIGMA Spectrum

What This Guide Helps With

Error 314 indicates the downstream (DS) pressure sensor is reading high even when no tubing is installed. This prevents the pump from passing self-check and safely operating due to unreliable occlusion detection.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use immediately
  • Transfer infusion to a backup pump if therapy is active
  • Confirm patient care is not interrupted

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and observe startup
  • Confirm Error: 314 DS no-tube elevated is displayed
  • Check if the error occurs during startup or with no tubing installed
  • Determine if the issue is consistent or intermittent

Inspect Pump Channel for Obstruction or Contamination

  • Open the pump door and inspect the tubing channel
  • Look for dried fluid, debris, or residue
  • Clean using approved methods if contamination is present

Check for Mechanical Obstruction or Misalignment

  • Inspect pumping fingers and internal components
  • Ensure nothing is restricting movement
  • Verify components return to normal position when door is opened
  • Look for visible damage or warping

Perform Power Cycle

  • Turn the pump off
  • Disconnect from AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Restart the pump

Test Without Tubing Installed

  • Close the door with no tubing loaded
  • Power on the pump
  • Observe if the error is still present

Load Known-Good Tubing Set

  • Install a new Baxter-approved IV set
  • Ensure proper alignment and loading
  • Verify normal operation without error

If the Problem Persists

If Error 314 continues after cleaning, inspection, and testing with no tubing and known-good tubing, external causes have been ruled out.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a pump on an active patient. Always transition the patient to a backup infusion device before evaluating alarms or errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 314 DS no-tube elevated during startup; unable to initiate infusion."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Downstream pressure sensor reading high with no tubing present; no external obstruction or contamination found."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned pump channel and verified proper operation. Error persisted; removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Pressure sensor errors directly impact infusion safety and occlusion detection. Always rule out contamination and setup issues first, then escalate promptly when abnormal readings persist.

That is successful troubleshooting.
