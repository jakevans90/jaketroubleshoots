Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000005 Watchdog POST

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000005 during startup indicating watchdog POST failure, resulting in boot failure, freezing, or repeated restart cycles.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use immediately.
  • Expected: Patient is transferred to another monitoring device.
  • Why it matters: Device may fail to boot or operate reliably.

Verify the Reported Error/Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the device and observe startup. Confirm Error #000000005 appears.
  • Note if the unit freezes, reboots, or fails to load.
  • Expected: Error is consistently displayed during power-on self-test (POST).
  • Why it matters: Confirms a system-level failure during initialization rather than a user or accessory issue.

Perform a Complete Power Reset

  • Turn the unit off, unplug from AC power, and remove the battery (if accessible).
  • Wait 60 seconds, then reconnect and power on.
  • Expected: Device boots normally if error was caused by a temporary fault.
  • Why it matters: Clears transient watchdog or firmware lock conditions.
  • If resolved → Return device to service
  • If error persists → Continue troubleshooting

Check Power Source and Stability

  • Plug the device into a known-good outlet. Avoid power strips or shared circuits.
  • Expected: Stable power during startup with no interruption.
  • Why it matters: Voltage instability can trigger watchdog failures during POST.
  • If power issue found → Correct and retest
  • If power is stable → Continue

Inspect External Connections and Accessories

  • Disconnect all accessories (SpO₂ probes, NIBP hoses, temperature probes, USB devices, modules).
  • Attempt to boot with nothing connected.
  • Expected: Device boots without external interference.
  • Why it matters: Faulty peripherals can interfere with system initialization.
  • If device boots → Reconnect accessories one at a time to identify faulty component
  • If error persists → Continue

Observe for Environmental or Physical Issues

  • Inspect for signs of overheating, liquid intrusion, or physical damage.
  • Ensure vents are unobstructed.
  • Expected: No visible damage or overheating condition.
  • Why it matters: Environmental stress can affect system watchdog behavior.

Attempt Alternate Power Method

  • If available, attempt to power the unit using battery only or AC only (whichever was not previously used).
  • Expected: Different power source may allow successful boot.
  • Why it matters: Helps isolate internal power regulation vs system board issue.

If the Problem Persists

If Error #000000005 continues after all external checks:

  • External and accessory-related causes have been ruled out
  • The watchdog POST failure indicates a system-level hardware or firmware issue (mainboard or control circuitry)

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a monitor that fails POST. Always transfer the patient before troubleshooting to avoid loss of vital sign monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit fails to boot with Error #000000005 (Watchdog POST). Device intermittently restarts and does not reach operating screen."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Watchdog POST failure persists after power reset, accessory removal, and power source verification. Likely internal system board or firmware fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service, labeled out of service, and sent to Biomed/repair vendor for further evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm or error behavior during startup
  • Accessories disconnected or tested individually
  • Power source used (AC vs battery) and stability
  • Environmental conditions (heat, fluid exposure)
  • Indicator lights or display behavior
  • Final device status (removed from service, sent for repair)

Final Thought

Watchdog POST errors indicate the system cannot reliably monitor its own operation. After ruling out power and external causes, escalation is the correct and safe decision.

That is successful troubleshooting.
