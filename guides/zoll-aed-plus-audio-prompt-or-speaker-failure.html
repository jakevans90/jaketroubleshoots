ZOLL AED Plus

Audio Prompt or Speaker Failure

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

ZOLL

Model

AED Plus

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses missing, distorted, or absent audio prompts, focusing on speaker function, device settings, and external obstructions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the AED is not in active use. If a patient requires resuscitation, provide a backup AED. Never troubleshoot on a patient.

Check Volume Settings

Locate the AED volume control (if adjustable). Ensure it is not muted or set too low. Expected outcome: audible prompt when powered on.

Perform a Power Cycle

Turn the AED off, wait 10 seconds, then turn it back on. Observe if the audio prompt initiates. Sometimes software resets resolve minor glitches.

Inspect Speaker Area

Check for dust, debris, or obstruction over the speaker grille. Clean carefully if needed. Expected outcome: unobstructed sound output.

Verify Battery Status

Low or improperly seated batteries can reduce speaker performance. Confirm battery is recognized and fully charged. Expected outcome: normal audio prompts resume.

Check Pads and Connections

Some AEDs may suspend audio if pads are not connected properly. Ensure pads are installed and connectors fully seated. Expected outcome: device recognizes pads and enables prompts.

Test with Built-In Self-Test or Demo Mode

If available, activate the AED’s self-test or demo mode to confirm speaker function without patient involvement. Expected outcome: audio prompts audible.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. The speaker may be internally faulty:

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop protects patient safety and preserves the device.

Clinical Use Tip

Always ensure another AED is available for patient care. Do not rely on an AED with suspected audio failure during resuscitation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"AED audio prompts not audible during device power-on."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Speaker malfunction or obstruction confirmed after external checks."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery checked and fully charged
  • Volume setting verified
  • Pads properly connected
  • No debris in speaker grille
  • Indicator lights normal
  • Device removed from service

Final Thought

Patient safety requires ensuring functional AED audio prompts. Troubleshoot logically from simple external causes to escalation. Proper documentation ensures future reference and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
