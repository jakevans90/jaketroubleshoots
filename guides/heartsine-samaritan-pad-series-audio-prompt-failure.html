HeartSine Samaritan PAD Series

Audio Prompt Failure

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

HeartSine

Model

Samaritan PAD Series

What This Guide Helps With

No voice prompts, low volume, or unclear audio during operation, which may prevent proper user guidance during emergency response.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the device is needed for a patient, immediately replace it with another AED.
  • Do not rely on a unit with failed audio prompts.
  • Expected: Patient care continues with a functioning device
  • Why it matters: Audio prompts are critical for safe AED operation, especially for untrained users

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the AED and listen for voice prompts.
  • Observe:
    • No sound at all
    • Distorted or faint audio
    • Intermittent audio output
  • Expected: Clear, audible voice prompts during startup
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is consistent or intermittent

Check Environment for Noise Interference

  • Move the device to a quiet area and repeat the test.
  • Expected: Audio should be clearly audible in a normal environment
  • Why it matters: Loud environments can be mistaken for audio failure

Inspect Speaker Area

  • Check the speaker opening for:
    • Dirt or debris
    • Tape, labels, or obstruction
    • Physical damage
  • Clean gently if needed.
  • Expected: Speaker opening is clear and unobstructed
  • Why it matters: Blocked speakers can significantly reduce or eliminate sound output

Check Battery / PAD-Pak Status

  • Verify the installed PAD-Pak is:
    • Properly seated
    • Not expired
    • Not showing a replace indicator
  • Replace with a known good PAD-Pak if available.
  • Expected: Device powers on normally with full audio prompts
  • Why it matters: Low or failing battery can affect speaker performance

Observe Status Indicator

  • Check the device status LED (ready indicator).
    • Green = Ready
    • Red or flashing = fault condition
  • Expected: Green ready indicator
  • Why it matters: Internal faults may include audio subsystem issues

Perform a Functional Self-Test (if applicable)

  • Allow the unit to complete its self-test cycle or initiate a manual test if available.
  • Expected: No errors and normal audio prompts
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the device detects internal faults

Swap with Known Good Unit (If Available)

  • Compare behavior with another Samaritan PAD unit.
  • Expected: Known good unit produces normal audio
  • Why it matters: Helps isolate device-specific failure vs user perception

If the Problem Persists

After ruling out external causes such as obstruction, environment, and PAD-Pak issues, the problem is likely internal (speaker failure or audio circuitry). Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. Do not return the unit to clinical use without reliable audio prompts.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an AED with suspected audio failure while it is assigned for patient use. Always replace the device first to maintain emergency readiness.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"AED reported with no audible voice prompts during routine check."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal speaker failure confirmed after verifying unobstructed speaker, good PAD-Pak, and failed audio during self-test."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent to vendor for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • PAD-Pak status verified
  • Speaker opening inspected and cleared
  • Status indicator state (green/red)
  • Audio behavior (none, faint, distorted)
  • Self-test results
  • Environmental noise ruled out
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Audio prompts are essential for safe AED operation, especially in high-stress emergencies. Always prioritize patient safety, verify simple external causes first, and escalate promptly when core functionality is compromised. Proper documentation ensures traceability and supports equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
