Philips PageWriter TC70

Touchscreen Misalignment

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the PageWriter TC70 touchscreen is misaligned or unresponsive to touch input accurately. Symptoms may include the cursor appearing in the wrong location relative to touch, difficulty selecting buttons, or inconsistent response across the screen. Most causes are external, including calibration drift, screen contamination, or loose connections, rather than internal board failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure the device is powered properly

  • Confirm the monitor is connected to a reliable AC outlet or fully charged battery.
  • Reason: Low or unstable power can cause touchscreen controllers to behave erratically.

Clean the touchscreen surface

  • Use a lint-free cloth with manufacturer-approved cleaner to remove fingerprints, smudges, or residue.
  • Reason: Dirt or oil can interfere with capacitive touch accuracy.

Check for screen protector or overlay issues

  • Remove any screen protectors, films, or external covers.
  • Reason: Added layers can alter touch sensitivity and misalign input detection.

Verify touchscreen calibration

  • Access the calibration utility from the system settings (if available).
  • Follow the on-screen instructions to recalibrate the touchscreen.
  • Reason: Calibration drift over time is a common cause of misalignment.

Inspect external connections

  • Ensure the touchscreen ribbon cable (if externally accessible) is seated properly.
  • Reason: Loose connections can lead to offset or erratic touch responses.

Restart the device

  • Power down, wait 30 seconds, and power back on.
  • Reason: Temporary software glitches can affect touch input mapping.

Update device software/firmware

  • Confirm the device is running the latest version of PageWriter software.
  • Reason: Touchscreen driver updates may correct misalignment bugs.

If the Problem Persists

External factors and calibration issues have been ruled out. The touchscreen controller or digitizer may be internally failing.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send for bench evaluation or repair by Philips-authorized service.

Knowing when to stop is part of proper troubleshooting and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt calibration or adjustment on a patient actively attached to leads.
  • Move the patient to another device for monitoring while troubleshooting.
  • Ensure hands are dry and the surface is clean before touching the screen for calibration.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
User reported touchscreen misalignment causing inaccurate selections on the PageWriter TC70.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed that touch input did not correspond with on-screen location; screen appeared clean but calibration was offset.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Cleaned touchscreen, verified no screen protector interference, recalibrated device, and restarted. Problem persisted. Device labeled Out of Service and sent for service.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Device powered from verified AC outlet
  • Screen cleaned and visually inspected
  • Calibration utility accessed and completed
  • No unusual sounds, flickering, or heat observed
  • Device status: Out of Service pending repair

Final Thought

Proper patient monitoring relies on precise touchscreen input. Logical troubleshooting—starting with simple cleaning and calibration—protects patient safety and ensures escalation occurs only when necessary. Clear documentation preserves accountability and supports future maintenance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
