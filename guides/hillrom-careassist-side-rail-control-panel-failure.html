Hillrom CareAssist Hospital Bed

Side Rail Control Panel Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

CareAssist

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses unresponsive or partially working side rail controls caused by power, lockouts, cable issues, or panel failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the patient is safe and not dependent on the side rail controls.
  • Move the patient if necessary before troubleshooting.

Verify Bed Has Power

  • Check that the bed is plugged into a working outlet and the main power indicator is on.
  • If no power is present, restore power and retest the panel.
  • A dead panel may simply indicate loss of power.

Check for Lockout/Disable Settings

  • Inspect caregiver control panel or footboard controls for lockout settings.
  • Ensure side rail controls are not disabled.
  • Lockouts are a common cause of “failed” panels.

Test All Side Rail Panels

  • Check each side rail control panel individually.
  • If only one panel is unresponsive, issue is likely isolated to that panel or its cable.
  • If all panels fail, suspect power, communication, or control board issue.

Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Visually inspect the affected side rail panel:
  • Cracked buttons or housing
  • Fluid intrusion or residue
  • Loose or damaged mounting
  • Damage may indicate panel failure requiring replacement.

Check Cable Routing at Hinge Points

  • Inspect wiring where the side rail pivots and connects to the bed frame.
  • Look for pinched, stretched, or severed cables
  • Movement over time commonly damages wiring in these areas
  • If damage is found, remove from service.

Reseat Accessible Connections

  • If accessible without disassembly, check and reseat connectors at the base of the side rail or junction points.
  • Loose connections can cause intermittent or total failure
  • Retest after reseating

Cycle Bed Power

  • Unplug the bed, wait 30 seconds, then restore power.
  • This may reset control electronics
  • Retest panel function after reboot

Test Function from Alternate Controls

  • Attempt the same functions using caregiver controls or other panels.
  • If functions work elsewhere, confirms localized panel or wiring issue
  • If functions fail everywhere, broader system issue is likely

Evaluate for Intermittent Operation

  • Operate the panel multiple times and observe:
  • Delayed response
  • Inconsistent function
  • Partial button failure
  • These symptoms often indicate internal panel wear or cable fatigue.

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks, power verification, and connection inspections have been completed, the issue is likely internal to the side rail control panel or associated wiring.

Remove the bed from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Do not attempt internal electronic repair in the field.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot side rail controls while a patient is relying on them for repositioning or safety. Always transfer the patient to another bed first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Right-side rail control panel unresponsive; no bed movement from patient controls."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Side rail panel cable damaged at hinge point causing loss of signal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and sent for side rail cable/panel replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and power confirmed
  • Lockout settings checked
  • Which side rail panel failed (left/right/head/foot)
  • Other panels functional or not
  • Visible damage to panel or cable
  • Intermittent vs complete failure
  • Final device status (in service or removed)

Final Thought

Side rail control failures are often caused by simple issues like lockouts or cable wear at pivot points. Always start with external checks and verify functionality across multiple controls. Knowing when to stop and escalate protects both the patient and the equipment, while clear documentation ensures efficient repair follow-up.

That is successful troubleshooting.
