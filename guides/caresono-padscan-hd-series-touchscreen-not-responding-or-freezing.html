Caresono PadScan HD Series

Touchscreen Not Responding or Freezing

Asset Type

Bladder Scanner

Manufacturer

Caresono

Model

PadScan HD Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting an unresponsive or frozen PadScan HD touchscreen caused by power, screen contamination, software freeze, accessory, or charging issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the bladder scanner while it is being used for an active patient assessment.

Action:

  • Remove the PadScan HD from clinical use and have staff use another bladder scanner if an immediate scan is needed.

Expected outcome:

Patient care continues without delay while the device is checked safely.

If this resolves the clinical need, stop and document the device status.

Verify the Reported Touchscreen Issue

Confirm whether the screen is completely frozen, delayed, inaccurate, or only unresponsive in certain areas.

Action:

  • Observe the display and attempt basic menu selections. Note whether the device responds to any touch input, physical buttons, or power controls.

Expected outcome:

The failure mode is confirmed before assuming touchscreen hardware failure.

Check for Screen Contamination or Moisture

Bladder scanners are often cleaned frequently, and residue or moisture can affect touchscreen response.

Action:

  • Inspect the screen for cleaning residue, gel, disinfectant film, cracks, liquid, or debris. Clean the screen using approved facility cleaning methods and allow it to dry fully.

Expected outcome:

Touch response returns if the screen was affected by residue or moisture.

If the touchscreen responds normally after cleaning and drying, return the device to service after function testing.

Remove Gloves or Test With a Bare Finger if Safe

Some touchscreens may respond poorly to certain gloves, wet gloves, or heavy pressure.

Action:

  • If infection control conditions allow, test the touchscreen with a clean, dry bare finger or a compatible stylus if available.

Expected outcome:

The screen responds normally if the issue is related to glove contact or touch technique.

If this resolves the issue, educate staff and document the finding.

Check Battery Charge and Power Status

Low battery or unstable power can cause freezing, delayed response, or abnormal startup behavior.

Action:

  • Place the PadScan HD on its charger or connect the approved power adapter. Confirm charging indicators or battery status if visible.

Expected outcome:

The unit powers normally and touchscreen response improves after sufficient charging.

If the issue was caused by low battery, allow the unit to charge and verify normal operation before returning it to service.

Perform a Controlled Power Cycle

A temporary software freeze may clear after a proper restart.

Action:

  • Power the unit off using the normal power button if possible. Wait at least 30 seconds, then power it back on.

Expected outcome:

The system boots normally and the touchscreen responds.

If the touchscreen works after restart, perform a test scan or function check before returning the device to service.

Check for a Frozen Application or Startup Screen

Determine whether the scanner is frozen during startup, at the main menu, during scanning, or after saving results.

Action:

  • Observe when the freeze occurs. Note whether it happens every time, intermittently, or only after a specific function such as scanning, printing, exporting, or entering patient information.

Expected outcome:

The issue pattern helps separate a simple software lockup from a recurring system fault.

Disconnect External Accessories

Connected accessories can occasionally contribute to system lag or freezing.

Action:

  • Remove any connected printer, USB cable, docking connection, network cable, or peripheral accessory if present. Restart the scanner and test the touchscreen again.

Expected outcome:

Touchscreen function returns if an external accessory or connection was causing the system to hang.

If the scanner works normally without the accessory, inspect or replace the accessory before reconnecting it.

Inspect the Charger, Dock, and Power Adapter

Poor charging contacts or unstable power may cause intermittent freezing.

Action:

  • Check the charger, dock, power adapter, and charging contacts for damage, looseness, corrosion, bent pins, or debris.

Expected outcome:

Charging hardware appears intact and the unit receives stable power.

If damaged charging hardware is found, remove the affected accessory from service and replace or repair it.

Check for Physical Damage

A dropped scanner may have internal display or touchscreen damage even if the screen is still visible.

Action:

  • Inspect the scanner housing, screen, probe area, handle, and seams for cracks, impact marks, loose parts, or signs of liquid intrusion.

Expected outcome:

Physical condition is confirmed before returning the device to patient care.

If damage is found, remove the scanner from service and send it for repair evaluation.

Allow the Device to Recover From Environmental Conditions

Extreme cold, heat, or moisture can affect screen response and system performance.

Action:

  • If the device was stored in a cold room, hot area, transport cart, or recently cleaned heavily, allow it to sit at normal room temperature and dry fully before retesting.

Expected outcome:

Touchscreen response returns if the issue was environmental.

Perform a Basic Functional Check

After the screen responds, confirm that the device is not only touch-responsive but also usable.

Action:

  • Navigate through the main screen, start a test workflow if allowed by facility practice, confirm buttons respond correctly, and verify the unit does not freeze again.

Expected outcome:

The PadScan HD operates normally without freezing or delayed touchscreen response.

If function is normal, return the device to service and document the action.

If the Problem Persists

If the touchscreen remains unresponsive, freezes repeatedly, only works in certain screen areas, or does not recover after cleaning, charging, accessory removal, and restart, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

At that point, the likely causes may include touchscreen digitizer failure, display assembly issue, internal connection fault, software corruption, or main control hardware failure. Clinical Engineering should not continue repeated resets or return the unit to use if the screen cannot be trusted.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a frozen bladder scanner during an active patient scan. Have staff move to another available bladder scanner first. A touchscreen that freezes during patient entry, scanning, or result review can delay care and may cause incomplete or unreliable documentation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Caresono PadScan HD bladder scanner touchscreen was frozen and would not respond during patient setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Screen had disinfectant residue and moisture present, and the touchscreen responded normally after cleaning, drying, and restarting the unit."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned and dried the touchscreen, power cycled the PadScan HD, verified normal menu response and basic function, and returned the scanner to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery level or charging status
  • Whether the unit was on the charger or running on battery
  • Whether the touchscreen was completely frozen or partially responsive
  • Whether the issue occurred at startup, during scanning, or after saving results
  • Screen condition, including residue, gel, cracks, or moisture
  • Cleaning performed before the issue occurred
  • Charger, dock, or power adapter condition
  • Any connected accessories removed during testing
  • Whether a power cycle restored operation
  • Whether the issue returned after restart
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A frozen touchscreen on a bladder scanner should be handled logically and safely. Start with patient care needs, then check simple external causes such as screen contamination, low battery, charger issues, accessories, and temporary software lockup. If the PadScan HD continues to freeze or does not respond reliably, remove it from service and escalate for repair. Clear CCR documentation helps show what was checked, what was found, and why the final decision was made.

That is successful troubleshooting.
