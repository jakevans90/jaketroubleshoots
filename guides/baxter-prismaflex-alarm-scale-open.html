Baxter PrismaFlex

Alarm: Scale Open

Asset Type

Continuous Renal Replacement Therapy (CRRT)

Manufacturer

Baxter

Model

PrismaFlex

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the Scale Open alarm during PrismaFlex priming when a scale cannot fully close or the machine cannot detect the closed scale condition.

This alarm prevents setup from continuing until the scale, bag, carrying bar, and closure detection are correct.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the PrismaFlex is not actively supporting a patient before troubleshooting this alarm.
  • Because this alarm appears during priming, do not proceed to patient connection until the scale closes correctly and the alarm clears.
  • If therapy is urgently needed, provide clinical staff with another ready CRRT system according to facility procedure.
  • Expected: The patient is not dependent on a machine with an unresolved priming alarm.
  • Why it matters: CRRT setup depends on correct bag positioning, scale function, and alarm detection before therapy begins.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the displayed alarm is Alarm: Scale Open.
  • Verify that the alarm occurs during priming.
  • Identify which scale is involved, if indicated by the display or staff report.
  • Observe whether the scale physically remains open.
  • Observe whether the scale appears closed but the alarm remains active.
  • Check whether a bag, bar, handle, tray, or other object is preventing closure.
  • Check whether the alarm returns after pressing CONTINUE.
  • Expected: The Scale Open alarm is reproducible or clearly observed during priming.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a true scale closure problem instead of a workflow issue or incomplete priming step.

Check for Obstruction Around the Scale

  • Inspect the scale area for anything preventing full closure.
  • Check for bag tubing caught behind or beside the scale.
  • Check for a bag edge trapped between the scale and machine.
  • Look for packaging, caps, clamps, or loose accessories in the scale path.
  • Verify that fluid bags are not hanging incorrectly or resting against the machine frame.
  • Inspect for visible damage or contamination around the scale hinge or latch area.
  • Correct any obstruction and gently push the scale toward the machine until it locks closed.
  • Expected: The scale closes fully and the alarm clears after pressing CONTINUE.
  • Why it matters: A physical obstruction is the most common and easiest cause to correct during setup.

Verify Bag Positioning

  • Confirm the correct bag is on the correct scale.
  • Confirm the bag is hanging freely.
  • Make sure the bag is not twisted, folded, wedged, or pressing against the machine.
  • Verify the tubing is routed without pulling the bag off-center.
  • Confirm the bag is not interfering with the scale closing motion.
  • Reposition the bag as needed, then close the scale and press CONTINUE.
  • Expected: The bag hangs freely and does not prevent the scale from locking closed.
  • Why it matters: Incorrect bag placement can make the scale appear faulty when the actual issue is mechanical interference.

Check the Carrying Bar and Bar Tray

  • Inspect the carrying bar and bar tray for proper positioning.
  • Confirm the carrying bar is centered.
  • Confirm the bar tray is seated correctly.
  • Verify the carrying bar is not off-center or angled.
  • Confirm the bag is not pulling the bar sideways.
  • Check that the bar is not bent, loose, or obstructed.
  • Correct the carrying-bar alignment and attempt to close the scale again.
  • Expected: The carrying bar sits correctly and allows the scale to fully close.
  • Why it matters: An off-center carrying bar can prevent the scale from reaching the closed position, even if the bag itself appears correctly installed.

Confirm Carrying-Bar Handle Orientation

  • Verify the carrying-bar handle is rotated down toward the floor.
  • If the handle is left in the wrong orientation, rotate it fully downward as intended.
  • Close the scale again after correcting the handle position.
  • Expected: The handle is rotated down, the scale locks closed, and the alarm clears.
  • Why it matters: Incorrect handle orientation can physically block full closure or prevent the closure sensor from detecting the correct position.

Confirm the Scale Locks Closed

  • After correcting bag, bar, tray, and handle position, push the scale toward the PrismaFlex until it locks closed.
  • Do not slam the scale.
  • Use steady pressure and confirm the scale is seated evenly.
  • Press CONTINUE when prompted.
  • Expected: The PrismaFlex accepts the closed scale condition and continues priming.
  • Why it matters: The device must confirm scale closure before setup can continue safely.

Isolate Whether the Issue Is Mechanical or Sensor-Related

  • If the scale physically closes but the alarm remains, determine whether the problem is likely mechanical or sensor-related.
  • Check whether the scale feels loose or does not latch normally.
  • Check whether the scale springs back open.
  • Check whether the closure point feels blocked.
  • Check whether the display still reports Scale Open even when the scale is fully seated.
  • Compare with another scale that closes normally, when appropriate.
  • Expected: The issue is narrowed to obstruction, alignment, latch problem, or sensor detection failure.
  • Why it matters: A scale that closes mechanically but is not detected by the machine may indicate a failed scale sensor.

Check Scale Sensor Status in Diagnostics

  • If external causes are ruled out and the alarm remains, use the PrismaFlex diagnostic function available to Clinical Engineering.
  • Access Diagnose Screen – Scale Diagnose.
  • Check whether the affected scale closure sensor changes state when the scale is opened and closed.
  • Expected: The scale sensor status changes correctly when the scale is moved between open and closed positions.
  • Why it matters: If the sensor does not respond, the machine cannot confirm scale closure even when the scale is physically closed.

Stop When Resolved

  • If repositioning the bag, correcting the carrying bar, rotating the handle downward, removing an obstruction, or properly locking the scale clears the alarm, return the PrismaFlex to the priming workflow.
  • Allow clinical staff to continue setup.
  • Confirm the alarm does not return.
  • Document the corrective action in the work order.
  • Expected: The Scale Open alarm clears and priming continues normally.

If the Problem Persists

If the scale is correctly loaded, unobstructed, aligned, latched closed, and the carrying-bar handle is rotated down, but the PrismaFlex still reports Scale Open, external and common setup causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Scale Open alarm may indicate a scale closure sensor, latch, wiring, or scale assembly failure.

Remove the PrismaFlex from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. If the scale sensor fails testing in Diagnose Screen – Scale Diagnose, replace the affected scale assembly according to service procedure.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first.

Do not continue priming or connect a patient to the PrismaFlex while a Scale Open alarm is unresolved. Use another CRRT system if therapy needs to begin before the machine is corrected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"PrismaFlex reported Alarm: Scale Open during priming. Staff unable to continue setup because the scale would not close or the machine did not detect closure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Scale bag and carrying bar area inspected. Bag position and carrying-bar alignment corrected. Carrying-bar handle was not fully rotated down toward the floor, preventing proper scale closure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Repositioned bag, centered carrying bar, rotated handle downward, pushed scale closed until locked, and pressed CONTINUE. Alarm cleared and priming resumed normally. Device returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Scale Open alarm should be approached as a setup and safety issue first. Most causes are external: bag position, obstruction, carrying-bar alignment, or handle orientation. If those are corrected and the machine still cannot detect closure, escalation for scale sensor or assembly repair is appropriate. Clear documentation protects the patient, the clinical team, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
