Philips Ingenuity Series

Gantry Rotation or Vibration Faults

Asset Type

CT Scanner

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

Ingenuity Series

What This Guide Helps With

Gantry rotation faults, vibration, abnormal noise, or scan interruption caused by external obstruction, setup issues, or mechanical rotation concerns.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

Stop the scan if gantry vibration, abnormal noise, rotation fault, or unexpected gantry behavior occurs.

Action:

  • Remove the patient from the scanner table when safe to do so. Do not continue scanning until the fault is evaluated.

Expected outcome:

Patient is safely removed from the affected equipment and moved to an alternate scanner if imaging is urgent.

Why it matters:

Gantry rotation issues can affect patient safety, image quality, and equipment integrity.

2. Verify the Reported Fault

Confirm the exact reported behavior before resetting or clearing anything.

Action:

  • Review the operator console message, fault code, scan phase, and whether the issue occurred during startup, positioning, scout, or active scan.

Expected outcome:

The failure pattern is documented clearly.

Why it matters:

A vibration during gantry acceleration may point in a different direction than a rotation fault at startup.

3. Check for External Obstruction Around the Gantry

Inspect the gantry opening and surrounding area.

Action:

  • Look for linens, positioning straps, contrast tubing, ECG leads, patient belongings, table accessories, or foreign objects near the gantry bore or rotating path.

Expected outcome:

No item is contacting or interfering with the gantry area.

If obstruction is found, remove it, verify the area is clear, and attempt system operation only if no damage, noise, or vibration remains. If resolved, stop troubleshooting.

4. Inspect the Patient Table Position and Accessories

Confirm the table is not mispositioned or causing contact during gantry movement.

Action:

  • Check table height, longitudinal position, head holder, arm boards, immobilization devices, and accessories.

Expected outcome:

The table and accessories are positioned correctly and are not contacting the gantry.

Why it matters:

Incorrect table or accessory setup can mimic a gantry mechanical fault.

5. Confirm Room and Gantry Area Are Clear

Make sure no external equipment is interfering with scanner operation.

Action:

  • Verify IV poles, injector lines, oxygen tubing, portable equipment, cables, and cleaning supplies are clear of the gantry and table travel path.

Expected outcome:

The scanner area is clear and safe for movement.

If interference is found and corrected, retest only if the scanner shows no abnormal noise or vibration.

6. Listen for Abnormal Mechanical Noise

Assess the type and timing of the sound.

Action:

  • From a safe distance, note whether the sound is grinding, scraping, knocking, thumping, rhythmic vibration, or high-pitched bearing-like noise.

Expected outcome:

Noise is either absent after external checks or remains present and is documented.

Why it matters:

Persistent mechanical noise after external causes are ruled out suggests internal gantry drive, bearing, belt, brake, or rotating assembly concerns.

7. Check for Visible Vibration or Imbalance

Observe the gantry during a safe non-patient test sequence only if allowed by site procedure.

Action:

  • Watch for shaking, uneven rotation, sudden stops, or vibration that increases with speed.

Expected outcome:

Gantry motion is smooth and stable.

If vibration remains, stop testing and remove the scanner from service.

8. Review Console Messages and System Status

Check for active or recurring gantry-related faults.

Action:

  • Review the operator console for rotation fault messages, gantry drive errors, tilt/position errors, emergency stop history, or scan abort messages.

Expected outcome:

Fault information is captured for service documentation.

Why it matters:

Recurring gantry faults usually require Philips service diagnostics rather than continued frontline troubleshooting.

9. Check Emergency Stop and Gantry Safety Interlocks

Verify that an emergency stop or safety circuit was not activated.

Action:

  • Inspect gantry, console, and room emergency stop buttons. Confirm none are engaged. Check door/interlock indicators if applicable.

Expected outcome:

Emergency stop and safety interlock status is normal.

If an emergency stop was engaged, follow facility reset procedure and verify no unsafe condition caused the activation.

10. Power Cycle Only if Safe and Appropriate

If there is no abnormal noise, vibration, burning smell, visible damage, or active safety concern, perform a controlled restart per site procedure.

Action:

  • Coordinate with CT staff, stop scanning activity, and perform the approved shutdown/restart sequence.

Expected outcome:

Temporary software or drive initialization faults clear.

If the gantry fault returns, do not continue repeated resets.

11. Do Not Continue Scanning With Persistent Gantry Vibration

If abnormal vibration, scraping, grinding, repeated scan aborts, or gantry rotation faults continue, stop use.

Action:

  • Remove the scanner from clinical service and notify imaging leadership and service support.

Expected outcome:

The scanner is not used until evaluated.

Why it matters:

Continuing to operate a CT scanner with possible gantry rotation instability can worsen damage and create patient safety risk.

If the Problem Persists

If obstruction, patient setup, accessories, room equipment, emergency stop status, and basic system restart have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal to the gantry rotation system.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for Philips service repair or qualified bench/system evaluation

Possible internal causes may include gantry drive components, brake issues, bearing wear, belt or motor problems, encoder feedback faults, slip ring concerns, cooling-related gantry electronics faults, or rotating assembly imbalance.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue scan attempts when abnormal gantry motion or vibration remains.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot gantry rotation faults with a patient inside the scanner bore. Remove the patient first, secure alternate imaging if needed, and only perform checks when the gantry and room are safe. Maintain therapy continuity by moving the patient to a backup scanner or alternate imaging pathway before troubleshooting continues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"CT staff reported a Philips Ingenuity Series CT scanner aborted during scan setup with gantry rotation fault and noticeable vibration from the gantry."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"External obstruction, table accessories, room equipment, and emergency stop status were checked with no external cause found; vibration remained during safe system check."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Scanner was removed from service, labeled Out of Service, imaging leadership was notified, and Philips service was requested for gantry rotation system evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet or power source status if checked
  • Exact console fault message or error code
  • When the fault occurred: startup, scout, scan, or shutdown
  • Whether vibration was visible or only audible
  • Type of noise: grinding, scraping, knocking, thumping, or humming
  • Whether table position or accessories were involved
  • Emergency stop/interlock status
  • Whether the fault cleared after restart
  • Any unusual heat, smell, smoke, or visible damage
  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed from service

Final Thought

Gantry rotation and vibration faults should be handled carefully because they involve patient safety, image quality, and major mechanical scanner components. Clinical Engineering should rule out external causes first, avoid repeated scan attempts, escalate when abnormal motion remains, and document the fault clearly for service follow-up.

That is successful troubleshooting.
