Newport HT70 Plus

Battery Not Charging or Shortened Runtime

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Newport

Model

HT70 Plus

What This Guide Helps With

Ventilator battery not charging, rapid battery drain, short runtime, or unit only operating on AC power.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the ventilator is in use, immediately transition the patient to a backup ventilator before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient remains safely ventilated
  • Why it matters: Battery failure can lead to sudden loss of ventilation if AC power is interrupted

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Disconnect AC power and observe battery behavior:
  • Does the unit shut off immediately?
  • Does it run briefly then shut down?
  • Is there a battery or charging alarm?
  • Expected: Clear understanding of battery performance
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether this is a charging issue, battery capacity issue, or both

Check AC Power Source

  • Reconnect AC power and verify outlet is functional (test with another device)
  • Confirm ventilator powers on normally with AC connected
  • Expected: Stable AC operation
  • Why it matters: The battery will not charge if AC power is unstable or absent

Inspect Power Cord and Adapter

  • Check the AC power cord and external power supply for damage
  • Look for fraying, bent pins, or loose connections
  • Ensure connections are fully seated at both ends
  • Expected: Secure and undamaged connections
  • Why it matters: Intermittent power prevents proper battery charging

Confirm Charging Indicators

  • Observe the ventilator’s display or indicators
  • Verify battery charging icon is present
  • Check for absence of charging-related alarms
  • Expected: Clear indication that battery is charging
  • Why it matters: Lack of charging indication may point to power supply or internal charging circuit issues

Allow Adequate Charging Time

  • Leave the ventilator plugged into AC power for sufficient time
  • Expected: Battery charge level increases over time
  • Why it matters: Deeply discharged batteries may take time before showing improvement

Perform a Battery Runtime Check

  • After charging, disconnect AC power and monitor runtime
  • Expected: Runtime meets expected duration for the model
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the battery holds a charge or is degraded

Inspect Battery Compartment (If Accessible Externally)

  • Check battery seating and contact condition
  • Look for corrosion, debris, swelling, or damage
  • Expected: Clean, secure battery connection
  • Why it matters: Poor contact or battery damage can prevent charging or reduce capacity

Check for Environmental Factors

  • Verify the unit is used and stored within normal temperature ranges
  • Expected: Device operating within recommended conditions
  • Why it matters: Extreme temperatures can affect battery charging and performance

If the Problem Persists

If all external power sources, connections, and battery conditions have been verified, the issue is likely internal (battery failure or charging circuit issue).

Remove the ventilator from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or battery replacement.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a questionable battery for patient transport. Always confirm adequate battery runtime before moving a patient off AC power.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator battery not charging and unit shuts off when unplugged."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery failed to hold charge after full charging cycle."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed ventilator from service and sent for battery replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Power cord condition checked
  • Charging indicator status observed
  • Runtime duration measured
  • Battery compartment condition
  • Any alarms or warnings displayed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery issues on ventilators are high-risk because they directly impact life support during transport or power loss. Always rule out external power causes first, but escalate quickly when battery reliability is in question. Proper documentation ensures trends are tracked and patient safety is maintained.

That is successful troubleshooting.
