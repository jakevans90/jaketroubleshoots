GE Healthcare LOGIQ P Series

System Fails to Boot or Freezes

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

LOGIQ P Series

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses system startup failure or freezing caused by power issues, peripherals, software lockups, or environmental factors before assuming internal hardware failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the system is not in active clinical use. Move the patient to another functioning ultrasound system before troubleshooting.

Verify Power Source

Check that the system is plugged into a known-good hospital-grade outlet. Test the outlet with another device.

Expected Outcome: System receives stable power. No power may indicate outlet or power cord issue.

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

Ensure the power cord is fully seated at both the wall and system. Look for damage or loose connections.

Expected Outcome: Secure connection eliminates intermittent power or startup failure.

Check Power Indicator Lights

Observe system LEDs when attempting to power on.

  • No lights: likely power input issue
  • Lights but no boot: possible system hang or internal issue

Why it matters: Helps differentiate between power vs. system-level failure.

Perform a Hard Power Reset

Turn the system off, unplug from power, wait 60 seconds, then reconnect and power on.

Expected Outcome: Clears temporary faults or system lockups.

Disconnect External Peripherals

Remove all probes, USB devices, printers, and network cables. Attempt to boot with minimal configuration.

Expected Outcome: System boots normally if a peripheral was causing the freeze.

Check for Overheating or Vent Blockage

Inspect vents and fans for dust buildup or obstruction. Ensure adequate airflow around the unit.

Expected Outcome: Overheating conditions resolved; system may boot normally after cooling.

Observe Boot Behavior

Watch for:

  • Manufacturer logo appearing and freezing
  • Error messages
  • Repeated restart cycles

Why it matters: Provides clues to software vs. hardware failure.

Attempt Alternate Power Cycle

If freezing occurs during use, perform a controlled shutdown (if possible), then restart. If unresponsive, perform a forced shutdown and restart once.

Expected Outcome: Temporary software freeze may clear.

Check Battery (If Equipped)

If the unit has an internal battery or UPS function, verify it is not causing startup issues.

Expected Outcome: Faulty battery may prevent proper boot.

If the Problem Persists

If the system still fails to boot or continues freezing after these checks, common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (e.g., power supply, motherboard, or storage failure).

Remove the system from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Proper troubleshooting includes recognizing when escalation is necessary.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot boot or freezing issues while the system is connected to a patient. Always transition clinical care to another device before beginning diagnostics.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ultrasound system would not boot and froze on startup screen."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"System freeze persisted after power reset; no external peripherals connected; likely internal hardware or software fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent for further evaluation and repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Power cord inspected/replaced
  • Indicator lights behavior during startup
  • Peripherals disconnected
  • Boot screen behavior or error messages
  • Fan operation or overheating signs
  • Any unusual noises or delays
  • Final device status (Out of Service)

Final Thought

System boot failures should always be approached from the outside in—starting with power, connections, and environment. Avoid assuming internal failure too early, but recognize when escalation is appropriate. Patient safety and clear documentation are critical throughout the process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
