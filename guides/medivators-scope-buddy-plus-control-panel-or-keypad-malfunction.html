Medivators Scope Buddy Plus

Control Panel or Keypad Malfunction

Asset Type

Endoscope Flushing System

Manufacturer

Medivators

Model

Scope Buddy Plus

What This Guide Helps With

Unresponsive buttons, incorrect inputs, frozen interface, or inability to start cycles due to control panel or keypad issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot while the system is actively connected to a scope in use
  • Disconnect and move to a backup flushing method if needed

Expected: No patient impact or delay in reprocessing workflow
Why it matters: Prevents contamination risk or delays in scope turnaround

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt multiple button presses
  • Observe if any buttons respond intermittently
  • Check for frozen screen or delayed input

Expected: Issue is consistent or clearly observed
Why it matters: Confirms true panel failure vs user operation error

Inspect for Surface Contamination or Damage (Most Common Cause)

  • Visually inspect for moisture, chemical residue, or debris
  • Clean the keypad using approved wipes (per facility protocol)
  • Dry thoroughly before retesting

Expected: Buttons respond normally after cleaning
Why it matters: Contamination is a frequent cause of keypad failure

Perform a Power Cycle (Reset Control Interface)

  • Power OFF the unit
  • Disconnect from AC power for 10–15 seconds
  • Reconnect and power ON

Expected: Control panel resets and responds normally
Why it matters: Clears temporary system or interface glitches

Check Power Stability and Indicators

  • Verify unit is plugged into a known-good outlet
  • Check power indicator lights
  • Avoid shared outlets with high-load equipment

Expected: Stable power with normal indicator behavior
Why it matters: Inconsistent power can cause interface issues

Test Full Keypad Functionality

  • Press each button individually
  • Note any buttons that do not respond or respond incorrectly

Expected: All buttons respond correctly
Why it matters: Helps identify partial keypad failure vs full panel issue

Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Look for cracks, worn buttons, or lifted membrane
  • Check for signs of repeated chemical exposure damage

Expected: No visible physical damage
Why it matters: Physical degradation often requires replacement

If the Problem Persists

If the control panel or keypad remains unresponsive after external checks and reset attempts, internal failure (keypad membrane, control board, or interface circuitry) is likely. Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and saves troubleshooting time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a partially functioning keypad. If inputs are inconsistent, cycles may not run correctly, increasing risk of improper scope flushing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Control panel unresponsive; unable to start flushing cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Keypad membrane failure confirmed after cleaning and power cycle did not restore function."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Power cycle performed
  • Keypad response behavior (which buttons failed)
  • Presence of moisture or residue
  • Any visible physical damage
  • Indicator light status
  • Final device status (in service vs out of service)

Final Thought

Control panel issues should always be approached by ruling out simple causes like contamination and power instability first. When those are eliminated, prompt escalation is the correct and safe decision. Clear documentation ensures continuity and supports repair efficiency.

That is successful troubleshooting.
