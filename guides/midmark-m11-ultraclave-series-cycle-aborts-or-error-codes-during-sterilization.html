Midmark M11 UltraClave Series

Cycle Aborts or Error Codes During Sterilization

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

Midmark

Model

M11 UltraClave Series

What This Guide Helps With

Cycle stopping mid-run, error codes, or aborted sterilization caused by door seal, water level, power, or loading-related external issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not rely on this unit for active sterilization. Remove from service if needed.
  • Expected: Alternate sterilization method available.
  • Why it matters: Incomplete sterilization compromises patient safety.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Run a cycle and observe when it aborts. Note any displayed error codes or alarms.
  • Expected: Cycle stops at a repeatable stage with a specific error.
  • Why it matters: Identifies whether failure is related to fill, heat, pressure, or door.

Check Power Source Stability

  • Ensure the unit is plugged into a dedicated, working outlet. Avoid power strips.
  • Expected: Stable power with no interruptions.
  • Why it matters: Voltage drops or interruptions can cause cycle aborts.
  • If unstable power is found → correct and retest.

Inspect Door Closure and Gasket

  • Check that the door is fully closed and latched. Inspect gasket for debris, cracks, or misalignment.
  • Expected: Tight seal with no visible gaps or damage.
  • Why it matters: Poor sealing prevents pressure buildup and triggers aborts.
  • If debris is found → clean and retest.
  • If gasket damaged → replace before further use.

Check Water Level in Reservoir

  • Verify the reservoir is filled to the proper level with distilled water.
  • Expected: Proper water level within marked range.
  • Why it matters: Low water prevents proper steam generation and causes errors.
  • If low → fill with distilled water and retest.

Inspect Water Quality

  • Confirm only distilled water is used. Look for mineral buildup or discoloration in the chamber.
  • Expected: Clean chamber with no scaling.
  • Why it matters: Poor water quality can affect sensors and heating efficiency.
  • If buildup is present → clean chamber per facility protocol and retest.

Check Chamber Loading and Tray Placement

  • Ensure instruments are not overloaded and trays are properly seated.
  • Expected: Proper spacing for steam circulation.
  • Why it matters: Overloading restricts airflow and can trigger cycle faults.
  • If overloaded → reduce load and retest.

Inspect Drain and Filter Area

  • Check for debris in the chamber drain or filter screen.
  • Expected: Clear drain path with no obstruction.
  • Why it matters: Blocked drainage affects pressure and cycle completion.
  • If debris is found → clean and retest.

Observe for Abnormal Sounds or Indicators

  • Listen for unusual noises (clicking, no heating sound) and check indicator lights.
  • Expected: Normal operation sounds and no persistent alarms.
  • Why it matters: Helps identify heating or sensor-related failure patterns.

Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit off, wait 30 seconds, then restart and run a test cycle.
  • Expected: Unit resets and completes cycle normally.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary faults or control glitches.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (heater, pressure sensor, control board, or solenoid).

Remove the unit from service, label Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and wasted time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a sterilizer intended for active instrument processing. Always use a backup sterilization method before testing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer cycle aborting mid-cycle with error code during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Low water level and debris found in drain restricting proper cycle function."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Filled reservoir, cleaned drain/filter, and verified successful cycle completion."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Water level and quality checked
  • Door gasket condition
  • Chamber cleanliness
  • Drain/filter condition
  • Error codes observed
  • Alarm behavior
  • Final operational status

Final Thought

Start with simple external factors like power, water, and sealing before assuming internal failure. Proper troubleshooting protects patients, saves time, and ensures accurate repair decisions. Strong documentation supports long-term reliability and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
