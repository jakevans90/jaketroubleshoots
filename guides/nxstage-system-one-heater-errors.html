NxStage System One

Heater or Temperature Regulation Errors

Asset Type

Hemodialysis (HD) Machine

Manufacturer

NxStage

Model

System One

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting heater or temperature regulation errors on an NxStage System One HD machine. These errors typically indicate that the machine is unable to maintain proper dialysate temperature, which can be caused by external factors such as power instability, heater cartridge connection issues, or blocked fluid pathways. Internal heater failures are possible but should only be addressed after external causes are ruled out.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

  • Do not operate the machine with a patient connected if heater errors are present.
  • Move the patient to a functioning HD machine.
  • Rationale: Prevents hypothermia or thermal injury.

Check Power Supply

  • Verify the machine is plugged into a dedicated outlet.
  • Test the outlet with a known-working device or outlet tester.
  • Inspect the power cord for damage or loose connections.
  • Rationale: Fluctuating or insufficient power can cause heater errors.

Inspect Heater Cartridge and Connections

  • Check that the heater cartridge is properly seated in its slot.
  • Ensure connectors are clean, dry, and free from corrosion.
  • Rationale: Poor electrical contact can prevent proper heating.

Verify Dialysate Flow Path

  • Ensure dialysate cartridges or bags are correctly installed.
  • Check for kinks, clogs, or air bubbles in the tubing.
  • Rationale: Inadequate fluid flow can trigger temperature regulation errors.

Check for Machine Alarms and Logs

  • Review the error message details on the machine display.
  • Document any repeated heater or temperature-related alarms.
  • Rationale: Helps identify intermittent faults or patterns for repair.

Cycle the Machine

  • Power off the machine, wait 30 seconds, then power on.
  • Observe whether the error recurs.
  • Rationale: Resets internal sensors and controllers; may clear transient errors.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have been completed; heater or temperature regulation circuitry may be internally faulty.

  • Remove the machine from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation by qualified NxStage service personnel.
  • Rationale: Internal heater repairs require specialized equipment and knowledge.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt to troubleshoot the heater with a patient connected.
  • Always confirm dialysate temperature stability before resuming patient treatment.
  • Use a functioning machine for patient treatment while troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Machine shows heater error and will not reach target dialysate temperature.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Power outlet verified, heater cartridge seated, dialysate flow unobstructed, error persists. Likely internal heater or sensor fault.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Machine removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for NxStage repair evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and functional
  • Power cord and connector inspected
  • Heater cartridge seating checked
  • Dialysate flow verified, no kinks or air
  • Error codes and logs recorded
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures patient safety and prevents unnecessary internal repair attempts. Proper documentation and timely escalation maximize equipment uptime and reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
