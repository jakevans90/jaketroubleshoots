Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: Pacer Equip Malfunction

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: Pacer Equip Malfunction on the Philips HeartStart MRx. This error may indicate unreliable or unavailable external pacing and should be treated as a therapy-related safety concern.

The issue may involve multifunction electrode pads, the therapy cable, the therapy connector, power condition, setup, or an internal pacing/therapy fault.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the HeartStart MRx is connected to a patient or may be needed for pacing, notify clinical staff immediately.
  • Do not rely on the device for external pacing while this error is active.
  • Provide an alternate defibrillator/monitor with pacing capability if pacing support may be required.
  • Expected: Patient care is transferred to a reliable device before troubleshooting continues.
  • Why it matters: A pacing equipment malfunction can prevent therapy delivery or make pacing output unreliable.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on and confirm the exact message shown is Error: Pacer Equip Malfunction.
  • Observe whether the error appears during startup, when entering pacer mode, when pads or the therapy cable are connected, during an Operational Check, or intermittently during handling or movement.
  • Check for associated symptoms such as inability to enter pacer mode, failed Operational Check, therapy cable warnings, pad detection problems, or abnormal Ready For Use status.
  • Expected: The error is clearly observed or reproducible.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported pacing fault rather than a setup issue or user-reported misunderstanding.

Remove the Device from Pacing Use

  • If the error is present, label the device as not acceptable for pacing use until corrected.
  • If facility policy requires full removal from service for therapy-related errors, remove the HeartStart MRx from clinical use entirely.
  • Expected: The device is not available for patient pacing until the fault is resolved.
  • Why it matters: Pacing is a therapy function, and unresolved pacing errors should be treated as patient-safety concerns.

Inspect the Multifunction Electrode Pads

  • Check the pads being used with the device.
  • Look for expired pads, damaged packaging, dried gel, incorrect pad type, contaminated connectors, or pads that were previously opened or partially used.
  • Replace with known-good compatible multifunction electrode pads.
  • Expected: The device recognizes the pads correctly and the error clears if the issue was pad-related.
  • Why it matters: Faulty or incompatible pads can create detection or therapy path issues that appear as pacing problems.

Check the Therapy Cable Connection

  • Inspect the therapy cable from the MRx to the pads connection.
  • Confirm the cable is fully seated at the defibrillator and pad end.
  • Look for bent pins, cracked connector housing, loose locking tabs, cable cuts, strain damage near the connector, fluid contamination, or evidence of repeated pulling or twisting.
  • Reseat the cable securely.
  • Expected: Cable connection is firm, clean, and undamaged.
  • Why it matters: The pacer output path depends on the therapy cable. Poor cable integrity can trigger pacing-related malfunction messages.

Try a Known-Good Therapy Cable

  • Replace the installed therapy cable with a known-good compatible cable.
  • Reconnect known-good pads or a proper test load as required by your facility’s testing process.
  • Power cycle the device and check whether the error returns.
  • Expected: If the original cable was faulty, the error clears with a known-good cable.
  • Why it matters: Cable substitution is a quick way to separate accessory failure from internal device failure.

Check for Physical Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect the MRx exterior, especially the therapy connector area.
  • Look for cracked housing, loose connector, bent or recessed contacts, fluid intrusion, corrosion, burn marks, or signs of impact damage.
  • Do not open the device for internal inspection during basic troubleshooting.
  • Expected: No external damage or contamination is found.
  • Why it matters: Therapy connector damage can affect defibrillation, cardioversion, and pacing functions.

Verify Power Condition

  • Connect the MRx to known-good AC power.
  • Confirm the battery is installed, seated correctly, and not visibly damaged.
  • If available, try a known-good charged battery.
  • Observe whether the error appears only on battery power, only on AC power, or under both conditions.
  • Expected: The device has stable power and the error is not caused by a weak or unstable power source.
  • Why it matters: Therapy and pacing functions require stable internal power. Power issues can sometimes appear as therapy subsystem faults.

Run the Operational Check

  • Perform the Philips HeartStart MRx Operational Check according to facility procedure.
  • Confirm whether the pacer-related portion passes or fails.
  • Save or document the result if available.
  • Expected: Operational Check either passes after accessory correction or confirms a pacer/therapy failure.
  • Why it matters: The Operational Check helps verify whether the fault is active and whether it is isolated to therapy/pacing function.

Perform Pacer-Related Bench Testing

  • If your shop has the proper analyzer and test setup, test pacing output using approved clinical engineering test equipment.
  • Check whether the device can enter pacer mode and whether pacing output is detected correctly through the therapy cable.
  • Do not test pacing on a patient.
  • Expected: Pacer output is present and stable, or the device fails pacer output testing.
  • Why it matters: This separates a displayed error from actual pacing-output failure.

Review Status Logs or Service Records

  • If available, review the device status log, error history, or recent service history.
  • Look for repeated therapy, pacer, cable, power, or self-test failures.
  • Expected: The log supports whether this is a one-time accessory event or a recurring internal fault.
  • Why it matters: Repeated pacer equipment malfunction entries strongly suggest a device-side problem.

Determine Whether the Issue Is Accessory-Related or Device-Related

  • If the error clears after replacing pads or the therapy cable, the issue was likely accessory-related.
  • Replace the failed accessory, complete testing, document the results, and return the device to service if all required checks pass.
  • If the error remains with known-good pads, known-good therapy cable, stable power, and failed Operational Check or pacer testing, treat the fault as internal.
  • Expected: The problem is isolated to either external accessories or the MRx therapy/pacing circuitry.
  • Why it matters: Therapy-related internal faults should not be guessed at or cleared without proper verification.

Stop When Resolved

  • If the error clears and the MRx passes Operational Check and pacer-related testing, return the device to service.
  • Document the accessory replaced, test results, and final device status.
  • Expected: The device is safe and ready for clinical use, including pacing.

If the Problem Persists

If known-good multifunction pads, known-good therapy cable, stable power, and proper testing do not clear Error: Pacer Equip Malfunction, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent pacer equipment malfunction strongly indicates an internal therapy or pacing circuitry failure.

Remove the HeartStart MRx from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. Internal troubleshooting may require evaluation of the therapy/pacing circuitry and replacement of the failed therapy-related assembly as indicated by service findings.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot pacing problems on an active patient. If pacing may be needed, move the patient to a known-good defibrillator/monitor before evaluating pads, cables, or device function.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx displayed Error: Pacer Equip Malfunction. Staff reported the unit could not be trusted for external pacing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Known-good multifunction pads and therapy cable were tested. Error remained during Operational Check, indicating likely internal therapy/pacing circuitry failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from clinical use, labeled Out of Service, documented failed pacer-related testing, and sent unit for repair/bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Pacing errors on a defibrillator should always be treated as therapy-related safety issues. Start with pads, cables, power, and setup, but do not keep a device in service when the pacing fault remains. Clear documentation protects the patient, the clinical team, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
