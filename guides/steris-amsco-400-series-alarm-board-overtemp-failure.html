STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Board Overtemp Failure – main control circuit board temperature exceeds acceptable environmental limits

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Board Overtemp Failure alarm, which occurs when the main control circuit board exceeds safe operating temperature limits. This condition causes cycle aborts and prevents safe sterilizer operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service immediately if the alarm occurs
  • Do not use instruments from an aborted cycle
  • Transfer workload to a backup sterilizer

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows “Board Overtemp Failure”
  • Observe if the cycle aborts automatically
  • Check if alarm occurs at startup or during operation
  • Note if the control panel area feels unusually warm

3. Check Ambient Room Conditions

  • Verify room temperature is within acceptable range (typically ≤ 86°F / 30°C)
  • Check for recent HVAC issues or temperature fluctuations

4. Inspect Ventilation Around the Unit

  • Ensure adequate clearance around the sterilizer
  • Check that vents are not blocked by walls, equipment, or storage
  • Verify airflow pathways are unobstructed

5. Check Cooling Fans and Air Movement

  • Listen for internal cooling fan operation
  • Verify airflow near ventilation openings
  • Note any unusual noises or lack of airflow

6. Inspect for Dust, Debris, or Contamination

  • Check external vents for dust buildup
  • Clean accessible ventilation areas if clogged

7. Power Cycle the Unit After Cooling

  • Allow the unit to fully cool down
  • Power OFF for 1–2 minutes, then restart
  • Observe if the alarm clears or returns

8. Determine if the Issue Persists

  • If alarm returns despite proper environment and airflow:
  • External causes are ruled out
  • Likely internal overheating condition exists

If the Problem Persists

Persistent Board Overtemp Failure indicates likely internal issues such as a faulty temperature sensor, failed cooling components, or control board malfunction.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot overheating conditions on an active patient load. Always allow the unit to cool and move workload to a backup sterilizer before evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit alarmed 'Board Overtemp Failure' during cycle and aborted."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Excessive temperature detected at main control board; ventilation and environment verified. Likely internal overheating condition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Allowed unit to cool, verified airflow and environment. Alarm persisted after restart. Unit removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Overtemperature alarms protect critical system electronics and sterilization performance. Always rule out environmental causes first, then escalate when internal faults are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
