Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x1E06 SafeView Communication Hardware Error

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses Error 0x1E06 SafeView Communication Hardware Error, which indicates a failure in communication hardware within the SafeView system. This can result in loss of bed exit monitoring and SafeView functionality, increasing patient fall risk.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from active patient use if SafeView monitoring is required
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed if necessary
  • Implement alternate fall precautions

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error 0x1E06 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup vs during operation)
  • Check if SafeView features are unavailable or disabled
  • Note any additional alerts or abnormal behavior

Check External Cable Connections (Most Common Cause)

  • Inspect all visible SafeView-related cables and connectors
  • Reseat all connections firmly
  • Check for loose or partially connected plugs

Inspect Cable Assembly Condition

  • Trace the cable routing along the bed frame
  • Look for pinched, crushed, or cut sections
  • Inspect for frayed or exposed wiring
  • Check areas near moving parts for stress damage

Eliminate Environmental or Setup Causes

  • Check for recent bed movement or repositioning
  • Verify no cables were pulled or misrouted
  • Ensure accessories are not interfering with cable routing

Reseat or Replace Cable Assembly

  • Disconnect and reconnect cable assembly at both ends
  • If issue persists, replace the SafeView cable assembly
  • Confirm connections are secure after replacement

Stop if Resolved

  • If the error clears, perform a full SafeView functional test
  • Verify bed exit monitoring operates correctly
  • Return the bed to service once confirmed

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0x1E06 continues after cable inspection and replacement, external causes have been ruled out and an internal communication hardware failure is likely.

  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup bed before addressing SafeView-related faults to ensure continuous fall protection.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x1E06 SafeView Communication Hardware Error; SafeView system non-functional."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Fault traced to defective SafeView cable assembly interrupting communication."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced cable assembly, verified connections, tested SafeView functionality, returned bed to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Communication errors should always be approached from the outside in—starting with cables and connections. Proper troubleshooting and escalation ensure SafeView functionality is restored safely and efficiently.

That is successful troubleshooting.
