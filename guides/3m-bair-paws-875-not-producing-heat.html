3M Bair Paws 875 Series

Warming Unit Not Producing Heat

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Paws 875 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a 3M Bair Paws 875 patient warming unit that powers on but does not produce warm air. The guide focuses on verifying simple external causes first, such as power, hose connections, airflow restrictions, and blanket compatibility before assuming an internal heater or control failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the warming unit is not connected to a patient during troubleshooting.
  • If active warming therapy is required, move the patient to another functioning warming system.
  • Only begin troubleshooting once the unit is removed from patient care.

Verify the Unit Has Proper Power

  • Confirm the warming unit is properly connected to a working electrical outlet.
  • Check that the power cord is fully seated in the unit and outlet.
  • Test the outlet with another device if necessary.
  • Confirm the unit powers on and indicator lights illuminate.
  • If the unit is not receiving stable power, the heater element may not activate even if the display appears functional.

Confirm the Warming Mode is Properly Selected

  • Ensure the unit is actually set to a heating mode.
  • Check the control panel for the selected temperature setting.
  • Verify the device is not in standby or idle mode.
  • Cycle the power and reselect the temperature setting if needed.
  • The unit may appear operational but not initiate heating if the warming mode is not actively engaged.

Check the Hose Connection

  • Inspect the hose between the warming unit and the patient gown or blanket.
  • Confirm the hose is fully seated in the outlet port.
  • Look for cracks, damage, or loose fittings.
  • Ensure the hose is not kinked or blocked.
  • Poor airflow can prevent the system from circulating heated air properly.

Inspect the Blanket or Gown Connection

  • Confirm the Bair Paws gown or warming blanket is compatible and properly connected.
  • Verify the gown port connection is secure.
  • Ensure the gown or blanket air channels are not compressed or folded.
  • Confirm the correct disposable warming garment is being used.
  • Improper or obstructed airflow can cause the system to limit or disable heating.

Inspect the Air Intake Filter

  • Check the rear or side intake filter for blockage.
  • Remove and inspect the filter.
  • Clean or replace if visibly dirty.
  • Ensure the air intake vents are not blocked by walls, drapes, or equipment.
  • Restricted airflow can prevent the heater from functioning correctly or trigger internal safety protections.

Listen for the Blower Operation

  • Turn the unit on and listen for the blower fan.
  • Confirm airflow is coming from the hose outlet.
  • If airflow is present but not warm, the heater element may not be activating.
  • Distinguishing between airflow problems and heating problems helps narrow down the issue.

Observe for Alarm Indicators or Error Codes

  • Check the display and indicators for any alarms.
  • Look for overheating warnings.
  • Look for airflow restriction alerts.
  • Document any fault codes if displayed.
  • Some internal faults will disable heating but allow the unit to power on.

Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit off for approximately 30 seconds, then restart.
  • Re-select the temperature setting.
  • Confirm whether heat output returns.
  • This resets temporary electronic faults that can prevent the heater from activating.

If the Problem Persists

If the unit still fails to produce heat after verifying power, airflow, connections, and settings, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely due to an internal heater element failure, temperature sensor fault, or control board issue.

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop troubleshooting prevents unnecessary downtime and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot the warming unit while it is connected to a patient.

If warming therapy is required:

  • Move the patient to another functioning warming system first.
  • Perform troubleshooting only after the device is removed from patient care.

This ensures uninterrupted temperature management for the patient.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Staff reported the Bair Paws warming unit powered on but the gown was not warming the patient.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Unit produced airflow but no heat output. Intake filter was clean and hose connections were secure. Heater element suspected to be non-functional.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent to Clinical Engineering for bench evaluation and repair.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified functional
  • Hose connection inspected
  • Intake filter condition checked
  • Airflow present or absent
  • Alarm indicators or error messages
  • Unusual noises from blower
  • Any heat output detected
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting begins with verifying simple external causes before assuming internal failure. By systematically checking power, airflow, connections, and setup, Clinical Engineering can quickly determine whether the issue can be resolved immediately or requires escalation. Proper documentation and timely removal from service help maintain patient safety and reliable warming therapy.

That is successful troubleshooting.
