Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000015 Software Watchdog Timer Reset

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000015 indicating watchdog reset causing unexpected reboot, freezing, or system instability during operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use if it is rebooting, freezing, or unreliable.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring safely transferred to another device.
  • Why it matters: Unexpected resets can interrupt vital sign monitoring and delay care.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error #000000015 appears.
  • Observe when it occurs: during startup, during normal operation, or random/intermittent resets.
  • Watch for sudden reboot without warning, freezing before restart, or repeated cycling.
  • Expected: Error is reproducible or instability is clearly observed.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a watchdog-triggered reset rather than a one-time glitch.

3. Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Shut down the monitor completely.
  • Disconnect from AC power and remove the battery (if applicable).
  • Wait 60 seconds, then reconnect power and restart.
  • Expected: Device boots normally without immediate reset.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary software faults or memory lockups.

4. Check Power Source Stability

  • Verify the device is plugged into a known-good outlet.
  • Avoid power strips or shared circuits.
  • Test outlet with another device if needed.
  • Expected: Stable and continuous power supply.
  • Why it matters: Power interruptions can trigger watchdog resets or system instability.

5. Inspect Battery Condition and Connection

  • Ensure the battery is properly seated.
  • Check for swelling, damage, or loose fit.
  • If available, test with a known-good battery or run on AC only.
  • Expected: Device operates without resetting due to power fluctuation.
  • Why it matters: Intermittent battery connection can mimic system crashes.

6. Disconnect External Accessories

  • Remove all connected accessories and modules (NIBP hoses, SpO₂ probes, temperature probes, network cables, USB devices).
  • Restart the device and observe behavior.
  • Expected: Device stabilizes without resets.
  • Why it matters: Faulty accessories or communication faults can overload or crash the system.

7. Check Network Connectivity (If Connected)

  • Disconnect Ethernet or Wi-Fi (if applicable) and operate the device standalone.
  • Expected: Device runs without triggering resets.
  • Why it matters: Network communication errors or interface faults can trigger watchdog events.

8. Observe for Overheating or Environmental Factors

  • Check for blocked vents, dust buildup, or excessive heat.
  • Ensure the device is used in a properly ventilated area.
  • Expected: Device remains stable during operation.
  • Why it matters: Overheating can cause system instability and watchdog resets.

If the Problem Persists

If the watchdog reset continues after all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • The error indicates a system-level software or hardware fault

Remove the device from service, label as Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Common likely causes include corrupted firmware/software, main board instability, or internal communication failures.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a monitor that is rebooting or freezing during patient care. Always transfer monitoring before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device displaying Error #000000015 with intermittent resets during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"External causes ruled out; watchdog timer resets indicate likely internal system instability or main board/software fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed power cycle, verified power source, battery, and accessories. Issue persisted. Device removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • When the resets occur (startup, during use, intermittent)
  • Any accessories connected or removed during testing
  • Power source conditions and battery status
  • Environmental conditions (heat, ventilation, dust)
  • Any error codes or messages displayed
  • Final device status (removed from service, sent for repair)

Final Thought

Watchdog timer errors are critical indicators of system instability. Focus on ruling out power, accessory, and environmental causes, then escalate appropriately to protect patient safety and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
