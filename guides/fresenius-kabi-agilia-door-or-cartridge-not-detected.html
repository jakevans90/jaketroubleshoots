Fresenius Kabi Agilia Infusion Pump

Door or Cartridge Not Detected

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Fresenius Kabi

Model

Agilia

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a Fresenius Kabi Agilia infusion pump that fails to detect the syringe or cartridge, or reports a door/cover issue. Common symptoms include:

  • Pump displays “Close Door” or “Cartridge Not Detected”
  • Infusion does not start
  • Pump beeps repeatedly when the cartridge is installed

The guide focuses on external checks first before considering internal repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure the pump is powered on

  • Check battery or power connection. A low or dead battery can prevent the pump from detecting the cartridge.

This ensures the pump electronics are active.

Inspect the cartridge

  • Verify that the cartridge is the correct type and size for the Agilia pump.
  • Look for damage, leaks, or misalignment of the plunger. Incorrect cartridges may not engage sensors properly.

Check cartridge installation

  • Ensure the cartridge is fully seated in the pump holder.
  • Listen for a click or feel for proper engagement. Misalignment often triggers the detection error.

Verify door or cover closure

  • Ensure the pump’s cartridge cover or door is completely closed.
  • Sensors may not detect the cartridge if the door is partially open.

Inspect for debris or obstruction

  • Check the cartridge compartment for dust, fluids, or foreign objects.
  • Even small particles can block sensor engagement.

Clean sensor and contact areas

  • Use a soft, dry cloth or approved wipe to clean any visible sensors or contacts.
  • Dirt or residue can prevent the pump from recognizing the cartridge.

Reset the pump

  • Power cycle the device after verifying cartridge installation and door closure.
  • This can recalibrate sensors and clear false detection errors.

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks are complete and the pump still does not detect the cartridge:

  • Remove the pump from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for bench evaluation or repair

Knowing when to stop prevents further damage and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot the cartridge sensor while an active patient is connected.

Move the patient to a backup pump before investigating detection errors.

Confirm the cartridge type is compatible to prevent infusion errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Pump displays “Cartridge Not Detected” and will not start infusion.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Cartridge not fully seated, door partially closed, or sensor blocked by debris.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Verified cartridge type and seating, cleaned compartment, ensured door fully closed, and power-cycled the pump. Pump now detects cartridge and operates normally.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested or battery status checked
  • Cartridge type confirmed
  • Sensor area clean and unobstructed
  • Door fully closed
  • Pump reset
  • Final device status (functional or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Patient safety and methodical troubleshooting are the top priorities. Always check external and easily correctable issues first. Proper documentation and knowing when to escalate to repair maintain both safety and equipment longevity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
