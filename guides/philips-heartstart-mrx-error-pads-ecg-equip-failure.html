Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: Pads ECG Equip Failure

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Pads ECG Equip Failure on the Philips HeartStart MRx when pads ECG monitoring is unavailable or unreliable through the therapy pads signal path. This condition may affect rhythm monitoring through multifunction pads and should be treated as a patient safety concern until the pads ECG path is verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the HeartStart MRx is connected to a patient and the pads ECG signal is not reliable, do not rely on pads ECG monitoring from this device.
  • Transfer monitoring to another ECG source or replace the defibrillator if patient care requires active monitoring or therapy readiness.
  • Expected outcome: Patient monitoring and defibrillation readiness are maintained with a reliable device.
  • Why it matters: A pads ECG equipment failure may affect rhythm monitoring through multifunction pads and could delay safe clinical decision-making.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact message displayed is Pads ECG Equip Failure.
  • Observe whether the error occurs at startup, when pads are connected, during monitoring, when switching to pads ECG view, or intermittently with movement of the cable or connector.
  • Check whether the device still monitors through 3-lead or 5-lead ECG, if available.
  • Expected outcome: The error is confirmed and associated with the pads ECG monitoring path.
  • Why it matters: This separates a pads ECG monitoring failure from a general ECG lead issue, pads-off condition, or user setup problem.

Remove and Reconnect the Multifunction Pads

  • Disconnect the multifunction pads from the pads cable.
  • Inspect the pad connector for bent pins, contamination, fluid, gel residue, torn packaging, expired pads, or incomplete seating.
  • Reconnect the pads firmly.
  • Expected outcome: Pads connect securely with no visible damage or contamination.
  • Why it matters: Poor pad connection or damaged disposable pads can interrupt the pads ECG signal path.

Try Known-Good Multifunction Pads

  • Replace the installed pads with known-good, compatible Philips multifunction pads.
  • Do not reuse questionable, dried, expired, contaminated, or damaged pads.
  • Expected outcome: The error clears if the original pads were defective or degraded.
  • If resolved, remove the bad pads from use, document the finding, and return the MRx to service after operational verification.

Inspect the Pads Cable

  • Check the pads cable from end to end.
  • Look for cuts or crushed insulation, strain relief damage, bent or loose connectors, fluid intrusion, intermittent signal when the cable is flexed, or evidence of cleaning chemical damage.
  • Expected outcome: Cable is intact, clean, and seats firmly at both ends.
  • Why it matters: The pads cable carries the ECG signal from the multifunction pads to the defibrillator.

Try a Known-Good Pads Cable

  • Swap the pads cable with a known-good compatible cable.
  • Reconnect known-good pads and check whether the error clears.
  • Expected outcome: The MRx recognizes the pads ECG path without displaying the failure.
  • If resolved, replace the defective pads cable and return the device to service after confirming normal operation.

Inspect the Therapy Connector / Therapy Port

  • With the device removed from patient use, inspect the therapy connector on the MRx.
  • Look for bent contacts, broken plastic, loose connector fit, fluid residue, debris, corrosion, or damage from cable strain or impact.
  • Expected outcome: Therapy port is clean, undamaged, and holds the cable securely.
  • Why it matters: A damaged therapy connector can prevent the pads ECG signal from reaching the internal ECG or therapy interface.

Check for Contamination or Cleaning Residue

  • Inspect the therapy port and pads cable connector for dried cleaner, gel, fluids, or debris.
  • Clean only according to facility-approved and manufacturer-safe cleaning practices.
  • Allow the connector area to dry fully before retesting.
  • Expected outcome: No residue or moisture remains at the connector interface.
  • Why it matters: Conductive residue or moisture can cause signal path errors or false equipment failures.

Compare Pads ECG to Lead ECG Function

  • If available, connect a simulator or appropriate test setup and verify whether standard ECG monitoring works through the ECG lead input.
  • Then test pads ECG monitoring using known-good pads, cable, or simulator connection as appropriate for your shop process.
  • Expected outcome: Lead ECG and pads ECG behavior helps isolate the failure path.
  • Why it matters: Normal lead ECG with failed pads ECG points more strongly toward pads, pads cable, therapy connector, or therapy interface circuitry.

Power Cycle and Retest

  • Turn the MRx off, disconnect questionable accessories, reconnect known-good pads and cable, then restart the device.
  • Confirm whether the error returns.
  • Expected outcome: The error clears if it was related to a temporary connection or accessory recognition issue.
  • Do not return the device to service if the error returns or pads ECG remains unreliable.

Perform Functional Verification

  • After any correction, verify the MRx operates normally using approved department test procedures.
  • Confirm there is no Pads ECG Equip Failure message.
  • Confirm the pads cable connection is stable.
  • Confirm the pads ECG signal is present when selected.
  • Confirm the therapy connector remains secure.
  • Check for intermittent failure with gentle cable movement.
  • Confirm the device passes required operational checks.
  • Expected outcome: The device performs normally and the error does not return.

If the Problem Persists

If known-good pads and a known-good pads cable do not resolve the Pads ECG Equip Failure, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent failure strongly suggests an issue with the therapy port, pads ECG signal path, ECG/therapy interface circuitry, or internal device hardware.

Remove the HeartStart MRx from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue troubleshooting into board-level repair unless you are following approved service procedures and have the proper test equipment.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a pads ECG equipment failure on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup monitor or defibrillator first, and treat pads ECG as unreliable until the error is corrected and verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx displayed Pads ECG Equip Failure. Staff reported pads ECG monitoring was unavailable through multifunction pads."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Known-good pads and pads cable corrected the issue. Original pads cable caused intermittent pads ECG signal failure when moved."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed defective pads cable from service, installed known-good compatible pads cable, verified pads ECG monitoring operation, confirmed error did not return, and returned device to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Pads ECG equipment failures should be approached logically: protect the patient first, confirm the exact error, rule out pads and cable issues, inspect the therapy connector, and escalate when the signal path cannot be restored. Clear documentation helps show what was tested, what failed, and why the device was either returned to service or removed for repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
