Philips IntelliVue MP Series

Alarm: MSL Power High

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

IntelliVue MP Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the MSL Power High INOP on a Philips IntelliVue MP Series patient monitor. This condition may be caused by excessive MSL load, a defective MMS or connected MSL device, an MSL cable problem, or a monitor-side MSL power fault. If unresolved, the condition may affect connected measurement devices and could progress to MSL Power Off.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the Philips IntelliVue MP Series monitor is not the only active source of patient monitoring before disconnecting MMS devices, MSL cables, or modules.
  • If the patient requires continuous monitoring, have clinical staff transfer monitoring to another verified monitor before troubleshooting.
  • Expected outcome: Patient monitoring remains safe while the MSL power issue is evaluated.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact INOP displayed is MSL Power High.
  • Observe whether the alarm appears immediately at startup, after connecting the MMS, after connecting a specific MSL device, intermittently during use, or after the monitor has been in service for several minutes.
  • Check whether the condition progresses to MSL Power Off if the overload condition remains active.
  • Expected outcome: The MSL Power High INOP is confirmed and the timing is understood.

Remove the Monitor from Patient Use if the Alarm Is Active

  • If the alarm is active and monitoring function is affected, remove the monitor from patient use.
  • Label the monitor appropriately if the issue cannot be corrected at the bedside or bench.
  • Expected outcome: The patient is not monitored on a device with an unresolved MSL power INOP.

Check the MSL Cable Connection

  • Inspect the MSL cable between the monitor and MMS or attached MSL device.
  • Confirm the cable is fully seated at both ends.
  • Look for bent connector pins, loose connections, damaged cable jacket, strain at the connector, contamination, or fluid intrusion.
  • Disconnect and reconnect the MSL cable securely.
  • Expected outcome: The INOP clears after reseating the cable.

Power Cycle the Monitor

  • With the monitor removed from active patient use, power the monitor off, wait briefly, and power it back on.
  • Confirm whether MSL Power High returns with the same MMS and cable connected.
  • Expected outcome: The monitor restarts normally and the INOP does not return.

Disconnect Attached MSL Devices

  • Disconnect attached MSL devices or modules from the monitor.
  • Power cycle the monitor with no MSL load connected.
  • Expected outcome: The monitor powers up without the MSL Power High INOP.
  • If the alarm clears with devices disconnected, the issue is likely related to an attached device, MMS, or cable rather than the monitor itself.

Reconnect Devices One at a Time

  • Reconnect the MMS and any attached MSL devices one at a time.
  • After each connection, allow the monitor to stabilize and watch for MSL Power High.
  • Expected outcome: One device, MMS, or cable causes the INOP to return.

Swap the MSL Cable

  • Replace the MSL cable with a known-good compatible cable.
  • Reconnect the same MMS or MSL device and observe whether the INOP returns.
  • Expected outcome: The alarm clears with the known-good cable.

Test with a Known-Good MMS or Compatible MSL Device

  • Connect a known-good compatible MMS or MSL device using a known-good cable.
  • Confirm whether the monitor operates without the INOP.
  • Expected outcome: The monitor functions normally with known-good MSL equipment.

Identify Whether the Problem Follows the Accessory

  • Move the suspect MMS, module, or MSL cable to another compatible test monitor if appropriate.
  • Do not use active patient equipment for testing.
  • Expected outcome: The INOP follows the suspect MMS, MSL device, or cable.

Check for Overloaded MSL Configuration

  • Review what is connected to the MSL system.
  • Remove unnecessary attached MSL devices.
  • Confirm only compatible, approved accessories are being used.
  • Expected outcome: The monitor operates normally with a reduced or proper MSL load.

Stop When Resolved

  • If replacing the cable, removing a defective MSL device, or reducing the connected load clears the INOP, document the finding.
  • Perform functional verification before returning the monitor to service.
  • Confirm the monitor displays expected parameters and no MSL-related INOP remains.
  • Expected outcome: The monitor operates normally without MSL Power High or MSL Power Off.

If the Problem Persists

If MSL Power High remains after the MSL cable is replaced, attached devices are isolated, and known-good compatible equipment has been tested, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent MSL Power High INOP may indicate an internal MSL power or monitor-side hardware fault.

The monitor should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue swapping accessories indefinitely once the failure is proven to remain with the monitor.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot MSL power alarms on an active patient if monitoring could be interrupted. Move the patient to a backup monitor first, especially if ECG, SpO2, NIBP, or other MMS-based parameters are in use. Maintain therapy and monitoring continuity before disconnecting cables, modules, or attached MSL devices.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips IntelliVue MP Series monitor displayed MSL Power High INOP during use with MMS and attached MSL devices connected."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"MSL cable and attached devices were evaluated. INOP cleared after replacing defective MSL cable and reconnecting MSL devices one at a time. Excessive MSL load or cable fault identified."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced MSL cable, verified monitor operation with MMS connected, confirmed no recurrence of MSL Power High or MSL Power Off, and returned device to service after functional verification."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact alarm behavior, including whether MSL Power High occurred at startup, after MMS connection, after a specific MSL device was connected, or during use.
  • Accessories swapped, including MSL cable, MMS, modules, or attached MSL devices.
  • Power behavior, including whether a power cycle cleared the INOP and whether MSL Power Off appeared after continued alarm condition.
  • Environmental factors, including contamination, fluid intrusion, transport damage, or strain at the MSL connector.
  • Indicator lights or display behavior, including missing parameters, unavailable measurement devices, or communication-related INOPs.
  • Final device status, including returned to service, removed from service, labeled Out of Service, or sent for repair.

Final Thought

MSL Power High should be treated as a real power-load warning, not just a nuisance INOP. Start with patient safety, confirm the alarm, isolate the MSL cable and attached devices, and escalate when the fault remains with the monitor. Clear documentation helps show whether the issue was an external accessory problem or a likely internal monitor failure.

That is successful troubleshooting.
