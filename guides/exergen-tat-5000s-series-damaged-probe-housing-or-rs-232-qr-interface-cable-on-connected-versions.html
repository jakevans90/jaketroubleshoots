Exergen TAT-5000S Series

Damaged Probe, Housing, or RS-232/QR Interface Cable on Connected Versions

Asset Type

Thermometer

Manufacturer

Exergen

Model

TAT-5000S Series

What This Guide Helps With

Evaluating visible physical damage or damaged connected-version cable components that may affect safe handling, temperature measurement, or data transmission.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the thermometer from clinical use before inspecting any reported crack, broken probe area, damaged lens, exposed cable conductors, or loose connector.

Provide clinical staff with a known-working thermometer for patient temperature measurement.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues using a device without suspected physical or communication-related damage.

Why it matters: Exergen states that physical damage may require accuracy verification or return for service; a damaged device should not be trusted for clinical readings until evaluated.

2. Confirm the Reported Damage

Inspect the thermometer externally for:

  • Cracks, separation, or sharp edges in the housing
  • Damage to the probe head or sensing lens
  • Loose, missing, or damaged battery compartment cover
  • Fluid intrusion evidence, discoloration, or residue
  • Loose or unstable cable connection on connected RS-232/QR versions
  • Cuts, crushed areas, exposed conductors, bent connector pins, or damaged strain relief on the interface cable

Do not open the instrument beyond routine access permitted for the battery compartment or externally replaceable connected-version cable.

Expected outcome: The physical condition and exact location of damage are clearly identified and documented.

3. Evaluate Probe Head and Sensing Area Condition

Examine the probe head and lens carefully under good lighting.

If the probe head, sensing lens, or surrounding housing is cracked, chipped, separated, scratched in the sensing area, or contaminated in a way that cannot be safely cleaned, stop troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: A thermometer with an intact, clean sensing area may proceed to additional checks; a damaged sensing area is removed from service for evaluation.

Why it matters: The TAT-5000S uses infrared sensing through the probe area, and physical damage may compromise confidence in temperature accuracy.

4. Check for Safe Cleaning and Surface Integrity

If no structural damage is found, confirm that the reported issue is not dried residue, adhesive, or removable contamination on the exterior surface or cable.

Clean only according to approved facility and manufacturer-compatible cleaning practices. Do not submerge the thermometer in cleaning solution.

Expected outcome: Surface contamination is removed without revealing cracks, softening, separation, or other true physical damage.

Why it matters: Exergen specifically states that the thermometer must not be submerged in cleaning solution.

5. Inspect Battery Cover and Basic Operation

Confirm the battery cover closes securely and that the thermometer powers on normally.

Observe the display during startup and a test activation for:

  • Normal display response
  • bAtt, blank display, or Err
  • Intermittent operation when the instrument is handled normally

If the display is blank, shows persistent Err, or operation is intermittent after obvious external issues are ruled out, stop and escalate.

Expected outcome: The unit powers on normally without error indications or intermittent behavior.

Why it matters: The manufacturer’s diagnostic chart identifies persistent Err as requiring return for repair.

6. Inspect the RS-232/QR Interface Cable on Connected Versions

For TAT-5000S connected versions equipped with an RS-232/QR interface cable, inspect the full accessible cable length and both connection points.

Look for:

  • Torn or split insulation
  • Exposed conductors
  • Damaged connector housing
  • Broken retention features
  • Loose connection at the thermometer
  • Cable damage near strain-relief points
  • Intermittent data transmission when the cable is in its normal resting position

Do not tug, sharply bend, or manipulate the cable to force an intermittent failure.

Expected outcome: The connected-version cable is either confirmed intact or identified as visibly damaged or unreliable.

7. Replace Only an Approved Externally Replaceable Cable When Appropriate

If the thermometer housing and sensing area are intact, but the connected-version interface cable is visibly damaged or fails normal communication testing, replace the detachable cable only with the correct approved replacement part according to facility procedure and manufacturer guidance.

For the generic TAT-5000S-RS232 model, Exergen identifies a QR replacement cable as an externally replaceable component.

Expected outcome: A correctly installed replacement cable seats securely, has no visible damage, and restores normal connected operation.

Stop if resolved: If cable replacement restores reliable communication and the thermometer passes required functional verification, document the repair and return the unit to service per department policy.

8. Verify Standalone Thermometer Function

With the device removed from patient use, perform an operational check:

  • Confirm the thermometer powers on normally.
  • Confirm the display remains stable.
  • Confirm no persistent error message is present.
  • Confirm the probe button and display function consistently.
  • Inspect again for any looseness, cracking, or unsafe surface condition.

Do not use patient testing as the initial verification method for a damaged or recently repaired device.

Expected outcome: The thermometer operates consistently without visible damage or errors.

9. Verify Connected Operation When Applicable

For RS-232/QR connected versions, reconnect the device to its intended host equipment or approved test configuration.

Confirm:

  • The cable is fully seated.
  • Temperature data transmits appropriately.
  • No intermittent loss of connection occurs during normal positioning.
  • The host system receives the expected temperature result.

Expected outcome: Reliable communication is restored without manipulation of the cable or connector.

10. Consider Accuracy Verification Following Physical Damage

If the thermometer sustained an impact, probe damage concern, or any physical event that could affect sensing accuracy, perform appropriate accuracy verification according to department policy using approved verification equipment, or send the device for manufacturer evaluation.

Exergen indicates that recalibration is normally not required unless the thermometer is physically damaged or experiences component failure; verification may be performed using Exergen calibration verification equipment, while recalibration requires return to Exergen.

Expected outcome: The unit is returned to service only when physical condition, operation, communication where applicable, and required accuracy verification are acceptable.

If the Problem Persists

If the probe head, sensing lens, housing, cable receptacle, or non-replaceable portion of the device is damaged, or if communication remains unreliable after approved cable replacement, common external correction has been exhausted and the device may require repair or replacement.

The thermometer should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, manufacturer evaluation, or replacement assessment

A thermometer with unresolved physical damage should not be returned to clinical use simply because it powers on. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not evaluate a damaged thermometer while it is being used for patient care. Provide staff with a known-working thermometer first, then inspect and test the affected device in a safe, controlled setting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nursing reported the Exergen TAT-5000S connected thermometer had a cracked interface cable jacket near the thermometer connector and intermittently failed to transmit temperatures."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found the thermometer housing and probe intact, but the RS-232/QR interface cable had damaged strain relief and intermittent communication during normal positioning."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the thermometer from clinical use, replaced the approved external interface cable, verified stable display and reliable temperature transmission, and returned the unit to service after passing functional checks."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Location and description of physical damage
  • Probe head and sensing lens condition
  • Housing crack, separation, or sharp-edge findings
  • Battery cover condition
  • Display behavior and any error messages
  • Connected-version cable type and damage location
  • Interface communication test result
  • Cable replaced and replacement part information
  • Accuracy verification completed or repair referral made
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Physical damage to a thermometer may affect safe handling, sensing reliability, or connected data transmission. Start with patient safety and external inspection, replace only approved externally serviceable accessories when appropriate, and escalate damage that could compromise accuracy or device integrity. Clear CCR documentation supports safe return-to-service decisions.

That is successful troubleshooting.
