GE Healthcare LOGIQ E Series

Battery Not Charging or Short Runtime

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

LOGIQ E Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the GE LOGIQ E Series portable ultrasound system has battery issues, including:

  • Battery fails to charge when connected to AC power
  • Short runtime or unexpected shutdowns during battery operation
  • Battery indicator shows incorrect levels or erratic readings

These issues are often related to external factors such as connections, battery age, or charger function before assuming internal battery or system faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify AC Power Connection

  • Ensure the system is connected to a known working outlet.
  • Check for power strips or extension cords; remove them and connect directly to wall power.
  • Why: If the system isn’t receiving adequate power, the battery won’t charge.

Inspect the AC Adapter and Power Cord

  • Look for damaged cables, bent pins, or loose connections.
  • Swap with a known working adapter if available.
  • Why: Adapter failure is a common cause of no charging.

Check Battery Seating and Contacts

  • Remove the battery, inspect terminals for corrosion, debris, or damage.
  • Reinsert securely.
  • Why: Poor contact can prevent charging or proper battery operation.

Observe Charging Indicator Behavior

  • Plug in the system and note LED indicators or on-screen battery icons.
  • Erratic or absent indicator changes suggest battery or system detection issues.

Test with Alternate Battery

  • If available, insert a known working battery.
  • Check if charging resumes and runtime is normal.
  • Why: Isolates whether issue is with the battery or the system.

Power Cycle the System

  • Fully power off the system, disconnect AC, wait 1–2 minutes, then reconnect and power on.
  • Why: Some battery management circuits reset on power cycle.

Check System Logs (if accessible)

  • Some LOGIQ E systems log battery or power errors.
  • Document any error codes for repair tracking.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The battery or internal charging circuitry may be faulty.

  • Remove the system from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send battery or system to authorized GE repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting to prevent patient risk or further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not use the system on a patient if battery runtime is unreliable. Always move the patient to a system with confirmed power and battery reliability before performing imaging. Troubleshoot only when the system is safely disconnected from patient use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Battery fails to charge, system shuts down after short use.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed battery not seated properly, AC adapter tested, alternate battery showed same issue.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Removed battery for inspection, verified AC power and adapter, system labeled Out of Service and sent for bench evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • AC outlet tested
  • AC adapter and cable swapped
  • Battery terminals checked
  • Charging LED behavior
  • System shutdown timing and battery runtime
  • Any unusual sounds or heat from battery

Final Thought

Proper battery troubleshooting protects patient safety and prevents unexpected system downtime. By following a logical stepwise approach and escalating when external checks fail, Clinical Engineering ensures both accurate diagnosis and timely repair while maintaining documentation for traceability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
