Smiths Medical Medfusion 4000

Alarm: Primary Audible Alarm POST

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Medfusion 4000

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a Primary Audible Alarm POST failure on the Smiths Medical Medfusion 4000 infusion pump. This alarm may indicate that the pump cannot verify its primary audible alarm function during startup, which can affect the pump’s ability to alert staff to critical clinical conditions such as occlusion, battery, delivery, or completion alarms.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Medfusion 4000 from patient use if the Primary Audible Alarm POST failure is present.
  • Do not use the pump clinically if audible alarm function cannot be verified.
  • Transfer the infusion to another verified pump if therapy is active or needed.
  • Expected outcome: The patient is not placed on an infusion pump that may fail to sound critical alarms.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power the pump on and confirm the alarm shown is Primary Audible Alarm POST.
  • Note whether the alarm occurs at startup only, every power cycle, intermittently, after transport, after cleaning, or after battery or AC power changes.
  • Listen for any audible tone during startup or alarm testing.
  • Expected outcome: The POST failure is reproduced or clearly confirmed.

Confirm the Pump Is Not Muted or Misinterpreted

  • Check whether the display, visual alarm indicators, and keypad response appear normal.
  • Confirm with clinical staff whether the complaint is no sound, weak sound, distorted sound, intermittent speaker output, or a POST failure message at startup.
  • Verify the issue is related to audible alarm output and not general pump power, display, or user setup.
  • Expected outcome: The problem is narrowed to audible alarm function.

Check External Power and Startup Conditions

  • Connect the pump to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Confirm the AC power indicator is present and the pump powers on normally.
  • If available and appropriate, repeat startup using battery power only to compare behavior.
  • Expected outcome: The pump powers on consistently and the POST failure is not caused by unstable power during startup.

Inspect the Pump Exterior

  • Inspect the housing for signs of impact, cracked case sections, liquid intrusion, contamination, or damage near the speaker openings.
  • Check the speaker openings for tape, labels, residue, cleaning buildup, or foreign material.
  • Clean only according to facility and manufacturer-approved procedures.
  • Expected outcome: The speaker area is clean, unobstructed, and free of obvious external damage.

Run the Biomed Audio or Speaker Test

  • Place the pump in the appropriate Biomed test mode according to facility procedure.
  • Run the audio or speaker test.
  • Verify whether the primary speaker produces the expected alarm or alert tones.
  • Listen for no sound, very low volume, distorted tone, intermittent sound, clicking, partial tone, or tone that cuts in and out.
  • Expected outcome: Audible output is clear, loud, consistent, and the speaker test passes.

Power Cycle and Recheck the Alarm

  • After any external cleaning, obstruction removal, or test confirmation, power cycle the pump.
  • Confirm whether the Primary Audible Alarm POST failure returns.
  • Run the Biomed audio or speaker test again if the failure is intermittent.
  • Expected outcome: The pump completes startup without the Primary Audible Alarm POST failure and audible alarm output is verified.

Do Not Return the Pump Until Alarm Function Is Verified

  • If the speaker test passes and the POST failure does not return, complete normal operational checks before returning the pump to service.
  • If the speaker test fails, audible output is unreliable, or the POST failure returns, keep the device out of service.
  • Do not rely on visual alarms alone when the audible alarm system has not been verified.
  • Expected outcome: The pump is only returned to service after audible alarm function is confirmed.

If the Problem Persists

If the Primary Audible Alarm POST failure remains after verifying the alarm, checking power conditions, confirming the speaker openings are not blocked, and running the Biomed audio or speaker test, common external causes have been ruled out.

Remove the device from service. Label it Out of Service and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Persistent failure may involve the primary speaker, speaker wiring, speaker drive circuit, feedback path, connector, or control circuitry. These should be evaluated through approved service procedures before the pump is returned to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an audible alarm failure on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first, then test, restart, or evaluate the affected pump. A pump with unresolved audible alarm failure should not be used clinically, even if the display appears normal.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Medfusion 4000 failed power-on self-test with Primary Audible Alarm POST failure. Audible alarm function could not be verified during startup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Primary audible alarm POST failure was confirmed during startup. Biomed speaker test produced no audible output or intermittent audible output after external power and speaker opening checks were completed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump from service and labeled Out of Service. Verified external power, inspected speaker openings for blockage, ran Biomed audio/speaker test, and sent device for repair or bench evaluation due to failed audible alarm verification."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Primary Audible Alarm POST failure should be treated as a patient safety issue, not a nuisance alarm. Clinical Engineering should confirm the failure, rule out simple external causes, verify speaker function with the Biomed test, and escalate when audible alarm function cannot be restored. Clear CCR documentation supports safe service decisions and future repair history.

That is successful troubleshooting.
