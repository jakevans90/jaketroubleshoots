Berchtold Chromophare Surgical Light

Bulb or LED Module Failure

Asset Type

Surgical Light

Manufacturer

Berchtold

Model

Chromophare Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the light output of a Berchtold Chromophare surgical light is reduced, flickering, or completely out due to a failed bulb or LED module. The focus is on external and easily verifiable causes before escalating to module replacement or repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Supply

  • Check that the surgical light is properly connected to a live power source.
  • Confirm that the breaker or outlet is functioning using a multimeter or a known working device.

Why it matters: No light output may be due to lack of power rather than bulb failure.

Inspect Light Controls

  • Test the wall controller and light head controls for proper response.
  • Ensure intensity settings are at an appropriate level.

Why it matters: A failed control may mimic a bulb or LED failure.

Check Indicator Lights

  • Some Chromophare lights have LED indicators that show operational status.
  • Look for fault indicators or warning lights.

Why it matters: This helps distinguish control issues from actual light source failure.

Visual Inspection of the Light Head

  • Look for obvious signs of bulb burn-out (filament break, darkened glass) or LED module discoloration.
  • Inspect for physical damage, loose connectors, or overheating evidence.

Why it matters: Confirms whether the light source itself is damaged.

Swap or Test Bulb/LED Module (if possible)

  • If spare bulbs or LED modules are available, replace with a known working unit.
  • Test the light operation with the replacement.

Why it matters: Confirms whether the failure is isolated to the bulb/module.

Check for Loose or Damaged Connectors

  • Ensure all connectors between the power supply and the light head are fully seated.
  • Look for bent pins or corrosion.

Why it matters: Electrical connection issues can mimic light source failure.

If the Problem Persists

If power, controls, and connectors are verified, and the bulb or LED module still fails to operate:

  • Remove the light from service.
  • Label the light Out of Service.
  • Escalate to a qualified service technician or manufacturer for module replacement.

Remember: Stopping before internal board-level troubleshooting is proper Clinical Engineering practice.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt to troubleshoot or replace bulbs/LED modules while the light is over a patient.
  • Move the patient to another light if surgery is in progress.
  • Ensure hands are dry and the light is powered off before touching the module.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Light output is flickering or completely out on surgical light head.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Visual inspection shows burned-out bulb or faulty LED module; power and controls verified functional.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Removed failed bulb/module, labeled light Out of Service, escalated to manufacturer for replacement.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Power cable integrity
  • Control panel response
  • Indicator light status
  • Physical condition of the bulb/module
  • Any unusual heat or odor from the light head
  • Final device status: Out of Service or operational after module swap

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Always start troubleshooting with the simplest checks—power, control, and connections—before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation ensures traceability and reduces repeat failures. Knowing when to escalate preserves both safety and equipment integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
