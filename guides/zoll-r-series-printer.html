ZOLL R Series Defibrillator

Printer Not Printing / Paper Jam (Thermal Paper)

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

ZOLL

Model

R Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide provides a structured Clinical Engineering approach when a ZOLL R Series defibrillator printer will not print or displays a paper jam message.

The R Series uses thermal paper, which is sensitive to heat, pressure, and handling. Many printer issues are related to paper condition, loading, or feed alignment rather than internal printer failure.

This guide focuses on logical, external checks before assuming a failed printer mechanism.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot on an active code or patient.
  • If the device is in clinical use, move the patient to another defibrillator before proceeding.
  • Patient care always takes priority over equipment troubleshooting.

2. Check the Paper Stack

  • Open the printer compartment.
  • Confirm a paper stack is present.
  • Ensure the stack is properly seated and aligned in the tray.
  • Verify the top sheet’s leading edge is correctly positioned in the feed path.
  • Replace the stack if:
    • It is empty
    • Edges are curled or bent
    • Sheets are wrinkled or compressed
    • Paper appears old or heat-damaged
  • Thermal paper that is creased or compressed can misfeed and trigger jam errors.

3. Inspect for Paper Jam or Debris

  • With the cover open:
  • Remove any misfed or partially fed sheets.
  • Look for torn paper fragments inside the feed rollers.
  • Check for debris or adhesive residue.
  • Ensure rollers appear clean and unobstructed.
  • Do not use sharp tools that could damage the thermal print head.
  • Clear the path completely before closing the cover.

4. Verify Printer Cover Closure

  • The printer will not operate if the cover is not fully latched.
  • Close the printer cover firmly.
  • Listen and feel for the latch click.
  • Confirm the cover sits flush and does not spring open.
  • A partially latched cover can generate false jam or “Insert Paper” messages.

5. Try a Known-Good Thermal Paper Stack

  • If available:
  • Replace the current paper stack with a new, known-good thermal stack.
  • Ensure it is the correct type for the R Series.
  • Perform a test strip or print an event summary.
  • This step helps rule out paper quality issues.

6. Observe and Record the Exact Display Message

  • Document the exact error shown:
  • “Paper Jam”
  • “Insert Paper”
  • “Printer Error”
  • No error, but no print output
  • Precise wording is important for trending and repair diagnostics.

If the Problem Persists

If the printer still fails after:

  • Confirming proper paper loading
  • Clearing all jams
  • Verifying full cover closure
  • Trying known-good thermal paper

Then common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as:

  • Thermal print head failure
  • Feed motor malfunction
  • Sensor failure
  • Internal gear damage
  • Remove the defibrillator from service if printing capability is required per policy.
  • Apply an Out of Service label.
  • Send for bench evaluation or vendor repair.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary internal damage and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Printing during a code event is often required for documentation. If the printer is nonfunctional:

  • Immediately swap to another defibrillator if documentation printing is required.
  • Do not delay patient care while troubleshooting.
  • Always verify printer function during PMs and readiness checks to prevent in-service failures.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Printer will not print / displaying paper jam error.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Examples:
“Paper stack wrinkled and misaligned in tray.”
“Small torn paper fragment lodged in feed path.”
“Paper loaded correctly, cover secured, known-good paper used — printer still displays jam error. Suspected internal printer mechanism failure.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Cleared jam and replaced thermal paper stack; test print successful.”
“Re-seated paper and secured cover; printer operational.”
“Unit removed from service and sent for repair due to persistent printer error.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Paper stack loaded correctly: yes / no
  • Paper condition (wrinkled, old, damaged)
  • Jam cleared: yes / no
  • Feed path inspected: yes / no
  • Printer cover fully latched: yes / no
  • Known-good paper tested: yes / no
  • Exact display message
  • Test print performed: yes / no
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Thermal printers are highly sensitive to paper condition and alignment. Most printing issues are caused by simple loading or paper handling problems rather than true hardware failure.

A structured approach:

  • Protects patient safety
  • Rules out common external causes
  • Prevents unnecessary part replacement
  • Ensures strong documentation

That is successful troubleshooting.
