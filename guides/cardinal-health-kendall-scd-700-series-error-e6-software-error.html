Cardinal Health Kendall SCD 700 Series

Error: E6 Software Error

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error E6 indicating internal software failure causing device startup interruption or unreliable compression therapy.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use immediately if the error is present
  • Transition the patient to a functioning SCD unit
  • Ensure therapy continuity with alternate equipment

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm that Error E6 appears
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup vs during operation)
  • Check if therapy stops when the error appears
  • Determine if the error is persistent or intermittent

3. Perform a Power Cycle (Basic Reset Attempt)

  • Turn the device off
  • Unplug from AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart the device

4. Verify Power Source Stability

  • Plug the device into a known good outlet
  • Test the outlet with another device
  • Avoid using power strips or extension cords

5. Inspect External Connections and Accessories

  • Disconnect all tubing and sleeves
  • Inspect connectors for damage or contamination
  • Check for any signs of fluid ingress

6. Reattempt Startup Without Load

  • Power on the device with no accessories attached
  • Observe if the error appears during startup diagnostics

If the Problem Persists

If Error E6 continues after power cycling and external checks, internal software diagnostics have detected a failure that is not user-correctable.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send to manufacturer or qualified repair vendor for service

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or restart the device while it is actively being used on a patient. Always transfer therapy to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device displaying Error E6 Software Error during startup; compression therapy not initiated."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal software/processor diagnostic failure indicated by persistent E6 error after power cycle and external checks."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from service, labeled out of service, and sent to vendor for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Software-related errors like E6 represent internal system failures identified by built-in diagnostics. Proper troubleshooting involves confirming the condition, ruling out external factors, and escalating appropriately to protect patient safety and equipment integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
