Medivators Scope Buddy Plus

Pump Failure or Irregular Operation

Asset Type

Endoscope Flushing System

Manufacturer

Medivators

Model

Scope Buddy Plus

What This Guide Helps With

Pump not running, inconsistent flow, abnormal noise, or failure to maintain flushing pressure during scope channel flushing.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot while an endoscope is in clinical use
  • Remove the scope from service if flushing is incomplete or unreliable

Expected: No patient risk from improperly flushed or contaminated scopes
Why it matters: Inadequate flushing can compromise reprocessing and patient safety

Verify Power and System Status

  • Check power cord connection and outlet
  • Confirm power indicator lights are on
  • Restart the unit

Expected: Unit powers on normally and initializes
Why it matters: Pump operation depends on proper system startup

Confirm Pump Activation During Operation

  • Start a flushing cycle
  • Listen for pump motor sound
  • Observe flow through tubing
  • Check for intermittent start/stop behavior

Expected: Pump runs smoothly and continuously during the cycle
Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is total failure or inconsistent operation

Check Water Supply and Source Connections

  • Verify water source is connected and turned on
  • Inspect inlet tubing for kinks or restrictions
  • Confirm filters (if present) are not clogged

Expected: Unrestricted water supply to the pump
Why it matters: Insufficient supply can mimic pump failure

Inspect Tubing and Channel Connections

  • Check all tubing for kinks, cracks, or loose connections
  • Ensure proper channel hookup to the scope interface
  • Replace suspect tubing if needed

Expected: Secure, leak-free connections with no restrictions
Why it matters: Flow resistance affects pump performance and pressure

Check for Air in the System (Priming Issue)

  • Run a priming or flush cycle without a scope attached
  • Observe for sputtering or uneven flow
  • Ensure lines are fully filled with fluid

Expected: Smooth, consistent fluid flow
Why it matters: Air disrupts pressure and flow stability

Listen for Abnormal Pump Noise or Heat

  • Listen for grinding, clicking, or unusually loud operation
  • Check for excessive heat from the pump area

Expected: Quiet, steady operation with no overheating
Why it matters: Indicates potential internal pump wear or failure

Attempt Multiple Cycles to Confirm Consistency

  • Run multiple flush cycles
  • Observe if failure occurs consistently or randomly

Expected: Consistent performance across cycles
Why it matters: Intermittent issues may point to early component failure

If the Problem Persists

If power, supply, tubing, and external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal (pump motor, control board, or pressure regulation failure).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a flushing system with inconsistent pressure. Do not troubleshoot on an active patient; move the patient to a backup device first to maintain therapy continuity.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported Scope Buddy Plus pump not running consistently during flushing cycles."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal pump motor failure suspected after verifying power, tubing, and water supply were normal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and sent to biomed shop/vendor for pump evaluation and repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior during operation
  • Accessories or tubing swapped during troubleshooting
  • Power behavior and outlet verification
  • Environmental factors affecting water supply
  • Indicator lights or system status messages
  • Final device status (in service vs out of service)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple external checks before assuming failure. In flushing systems, flow, pressure, and supply issues often mimic pump problems. Once ruled out, timely escalation protects both equipment reliability and patient safety.

That is successful troubleshooting.
