HeartSine Samaritan PAD Series

PAD-Pak Not Recognized or Expired Alert

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

HeartSine

Model

Samaritan PAD Series

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses PAD-Pak not detected or expired alerts caused by installation issues, expired cartridges, or contact/connection problems.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the device is needed for patient care, immediately obtain another AED/defibrillator.
  • Do not rely on a unit with a PAD-Pak error.
  • Expected: Backup device is available for patient use
  • Why it matters: The AED may not deliver therapy if the PAD-Pak is not recognized

Verify the Alert Condition

  • Power on the AED and confirm the exact alert (not recognized vs expired).
  • Observe indicator lights and audible prompts.
  • Expected: Clear identification of the issue type
  • Why it matters: Determines whether the issue is installation-related or expiration-related

Check PAD-Pak Expiration Date (Most Common Cause)

  • Remove the PAD-Pak and inspect the expiration date printed on the label.
  • Expected: PAD-Pak is within valid date range
  • Why it matters: Expired PAD-Paks will trigger alerts and are not approved for use
  • If expired: Replace with a new PAD-Pak → issue resolved

Reseat the PAD-Pak Properly

  • Reinsert the PAD-Pak firmly until it is fully seated.
  • Ensure it clicks into place and is not loose.
  • Expected: AED recognizes the PAD-Pak and clears the alert
  • Why it matters: Improper seating prevents electrical and data connection
  • If resolved: Return device to service

Inspect PAD-Pak and Connector Area

  • Check for:
  • Bent or damaged contacts
  • Debris or contamination
  • Cracks or damage to the cartridge
  • Expected: Clean, intact contacts and housing
  • Why it matters: Physical damage can prevent recognition
  • If damage is found: Replace PAD-Pak → issue resolved

Try a Known-Good PAD-Pak

  • Install a new or verified working PAD-Pak.
  • Expected: AED recognizes the new PAD-Pak and shows ready status
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is with the cartridge or the device
  • If new PAD-Pak works: Original PAD-Pak is defective → discard
  • If new PAD-Pak fails: Possible device-side issue

Perform Device Self-Check

  • Allow the AED to complete its automatic self-test or initiate one if applicable.
  • Observe readiness indicator (green flashing status light).
  • Expected: Device passes self-test and shows ready status
  • Why it matters: Confirms overall system functionality

If the Problem Persists

If a known-good, in-date PAD-Pak is not recognized, common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (connector interface or control circuitry).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a defibrillator while it is assigned for active patient coverage. Always replace the unit first to ensure immediate emergency readiness.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"AED displaying PAD-Pak not recognized alert during routine check."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Expired PAD-Pak preventing device readiness."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced PAD-Pak with in-date cartridge and verified ready indicator."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • PAD-Pak expiration date checked
  • Cartridge reseated
  • Known-good PAD-Pak tested
  • Indicator light status (green flashing vs fault)
  • Audible prompts present or absent
  • Physical condition of contacts
  • Final device status (in service vs out of service)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple, external checks like expiration dates and proper installation. Patient safety always comes first, and escalation is appropriate when device readiness cannot be confirmed. Clear documentation ensures traceability and supports ongoing reliability of life-saving equipment.

That is successful troubleshooting.
