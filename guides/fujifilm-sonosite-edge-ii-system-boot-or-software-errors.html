Fujifilm Sonosite Edge II

System Boot or Software Errors

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

Fujifilm Sonosite

Model

Edge II

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Fujifilm Sonosite Edge II fails to boot properly, shows error messages during startup, or experiences software crashes. Common symptoms include:

  • System not completing boot sequence
  • Error messages on startup
  • Freezing or crashing during software initialization

The focus is on external and easily verifiable causes before assuming internal software or hardware failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the system is plugged into a known working outlet.
  • Check power cords and connections for damage or looseness.
  • Confirm that the AC power indicator is on.

Why it matters: Many “boot failures” are actually due to unstable or missing power.

Check Battery Condition

  • If running on battery, connect AC power and observe LED indicators.
  • Swap with a known good battery if available.

Why it matters: Weak or depleted batteries can prevent proper boot.

Inspect External Cables and Peripherals

  • Disconnect all probes, USB devices, and network cables.
  • Attempt boot with minimal configuration.

Why it matters: Faulty peripherals can halt software initialization.

Soft Reset / Safe Boot (if available)

  • Follow manufacturer’s procedure to perform a soft reset or safe boot mode.
  • Observe any error messages displayed.

Why it matters: Safe boot may allow the system to bypass minor software issues.

Check for Visible Damage or Overheating

  • Inspect for unusual heat, burning smell, or liquid damage.
  • Ensure vents are clear and fan is functional.

Why it matters: Overheating or physical damage can prevent proper startup.

Observe Error Codes

  • Document any messages or codes that appear on startup.
  • Refer to manufacturer support resources for error code meanings.

If the Problem Persists

After verifying power, battery, peripherals, and attempting soft reset:

  • Remove the system from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop and escalate is proper troubleshooting and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt troubleshooting on an active patient; move the patient to a backup system first.
  • Do not attempt internal software reinstallation or hardware disassembly unless trained and authorized.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Edge II will not boot and shows ‘Software Error 101’ on startup.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Power confirmed stable, battery verified, no external peripherals attached, soft reset failed; system still displays error.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
System removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent to the repair bench for evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and functional
  • AC power LED indicators
  • Battery swapped/checked
  • Probes and USB devices disconnected
  • Error codes captured
  • Fan/vent status

Final Thought

Thorough, logic-based troubleshooting prioritizes patient safety and efficient resolution. Documenting each step and knowing when to escalate ensures proper device management and reduces risk of repeated failures.

That is successful troubleshooting.
