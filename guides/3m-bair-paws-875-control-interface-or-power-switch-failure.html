3M Bair Paws 875 Series

Control Interface or Power Switch Failure

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Paws 875 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a 3M Bair Paws 875 patient warming unit that fails to respond to the control interface, touchscreen, or power switch. The focus is on external and easily verifiable issues before considering internal failure, ensuring patient safety and proper escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the unit is plugged into a verified working outlet.
  • Test outlet with a known working device or outlet tester.
  • Reason: A unit will appear non-responsive if no power reaches it.

Inspect Power Cable and Connections

  • Check for visible damage, kinks, or loose connections in the power cord.
  • Ensure the cable is fully seated in the device.
  • Reason: Damaged cables can prevent the system from powering on.

Check Power Switch Operation

  • Attempt to turn the unit on/off several times.
  • Listen for any audible clicks or see if indicator lights flash briefly.
  • Reason: Mechanical switches may stick or fail intermittently.

Inspect Control Panel / Interface

  • Examine the touchscreen or buttons for visible damage, debris, or moisture.
  • Wipe the panel with a dry, lint-free cloth if needed.
  • Reason: Contaminants or minor damage can prevent proper touch or button operation.

Cycle the Unit (Power Reset)

  • Unplug the device for 1–2 minutes, then plug it back in.
  • Attempt to power on again.
  • Reason: Some units require a full power cycle to reset internal electronics after minor faults.

Swap with a Known Working Unit (If Available)

  • Test the same blanket or hose on a different Bair Paws 875 unit to rule out interface misinterpretation.
  • Reason: Confirms whether the issue is with the unit itself or user/blanket interface.

If the Problem Persists

External power and interface issues have been ruled out. The power switch or control board may be faulty.

  • Remove the unit from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send for internal bench evaluation or manufacturer repair.

Note: Continuing to troubleshoot internally without proper tools can risk further damage or safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to warm a patient using a unit with an unresponsive interface or power switch. Move the patient to a verified working warming device before performing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
User reports the Bair Paws 875 unit will not power on or respond to the control panel.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Verified power source, outlet, cable, and blanket interface. Control panel remains unresponsive; power switch shows no activity.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Unit removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair/bench evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • Power cable inspected and reseated
  • Control panel visually inspected
  • Power cycling attempted
  • No indicator lights, sounds, or response observed

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting balances patient safety, efficient diagnosis, and timely escalation. Documenting each step ensures clarity and accountability while avoiding unnecessary internal repairs that could compromise device integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
