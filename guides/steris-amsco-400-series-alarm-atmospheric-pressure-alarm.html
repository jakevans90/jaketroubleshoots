STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Atmospheric Pressure Alarm – Atmospheric pressure differs by more than 1 PSI from calibrated value

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Atmospheric Pressure Alarm indicating incorrect pressure reference, causing inaccurate readings and preventing proper cycle operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service if the alarm is active
  • Do not process or release any loads while pressure accuracy is in question
  • Ensure no patient items rely on a potentially compromised cycle

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows Atmospheric Pressure Alarm
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup, idle, or during cycle)
  • Check if the unit prevents cycle start (expected behavior)
  • Confirm alarm is repeatable

3. Check for Recent Relocation or Environmental Changes

  • Determine if the sterilizer has been recently moved
  • Verify if installed at a different elevation or floor
  • If accessible, confirm altitude setting matches facility elevation

4. Check for Obvious Environmental Factors

  • Verify stable room conditions with no extreme pressure fluctuations
  • Ensure no HVAC or ventilation issues affecting ambient pressure
  • Confirm vents around the unit are not blocked

5. Inspect External Wiring and Connections (Non-Invasive)

  • Check visible cables related to pressure sensing
  • Look for loose, damaged, or corroded connections
  • Do not open panels or perform internal disassembly

6. Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the sterilizer OFF using proper procedure
  • Wait 30–60 seconds before powering back ON
  • Observe if the alarm clears after restart

7. Attempt Return to Service (If Alarm Clears)

  • Run a test cycle with an empty chamber
  • Monitor for alarm recurrence
  • If stable, return the unit to service

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after completing all external checks, internal causes are likely.

  • Pressure transducer failure
  • Calibration drift
  • Internal wiring fault
  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair and calibration

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot pressure-related alarms during an active sterilization load. Always move items to a backup device and verify a successful test cycle before returning to use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying Atmospheric Pressure Alarm; unable to start cycles."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Atmospheric pressure reading outside calibrated range; no environmental or external issues found."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified no relocation or environmental issues. Checked external connections and power cycled unit. Alarm persisted. Removed from service and sent for calibration and repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Accurate atmospheric pressure reference is essential for proper sterilizer operation. Persistent deviations indicate a system-level issue that should not be ignored. Escalating appropriately ensures safe and reliable sterilization processes.

That is successful troubleshooting.
