Mindray SynoVent E5

Touchscreen or Display Errors

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Mindray

Model

SynoVent E5

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists in troubleshooting touchscreen or display errors on a Mindray SynoVent E5 ventilator. Most issues are caused by external or easily verifiable factors such as power supply problems, loose connections, environmental interference, or improper cleaning.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the ventilator is connected to a verified live outlet.
  • Check that the power cord is fully seated at both ends.
  • Inspect for any visible damage to the power cable.

Why: Display glitches can appear if the device is underpowered or experiencing intermittent power.

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the ventilator off, wait 30–60 seconds, then power it on again.
  • Observe if the display initializes correctly.

Why: Temporary software glitches can be cleared with a full restart.

Check Touchscreen Responsiveness

  • Tap all corners and edges to see if the touch response is consistent.
  • Use a soft, dry cloth to clean the screen; avoid excessive moisture.

Why: Dirt, moisture, or residue can prevent proper touch detection.

Inspect the Display Connections

  • If accessible without disassembly, check that the display module and mainboard connectors are seated properly.
  • Ensure ribbon cables are not loose, bent, or damaged.

Why: Loose connectors can cause intermittent display errors without internal damage.

Verify Environmental Conditions

  • Confirm the ventilator is operating within the manufacturer’s temperature and humidity specifications.
  • Avoid direct sunlight or strong reflections on the screen.

Why: Extreme conditions can affect touchscreen calibration or visibility.

Check Software Version & Error Logs

  • Record any error codes displayed during startup or operation.
  • Compare with the device’s service documentation for known software-related display issues.

Why: Some touchscreen errors may be caused by outdated firmware or software faults.

Test with Minimal Accessories

  • Disconnect external modules, printers, or USB devices.
  • Reboot and check if the display issue persists.

Why: Peripheral devices can sometimes interfere with the display operation.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out; internal touchscreen or display electronics may be faulty. The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Stopping at this point prevents unsafe troubleshooting and preserves the integrity of the ventilator.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot the touchscreen while a patient is connected. Move the patient to another ventilator if alarms or monitoring are affected. Only perform tests when the device is safely offline.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

Ventilator touchscreen unresponsive; display shows intermittent errors during use.

Cause

No external power or connection issues observed; power cycling and peripheral removal did not resolve the issue.

Resolution

Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent to vendor for display repair.

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet verified and power cord inspected
  • Ventilator power cycled and rebooted
  • Screen cleaned and touchscreen response tested
  • External devices disconnected
  • Error codes documented
  • Environmental conditions confirmed

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting prioritizes patient safety and systematically rules out external causes before assuming internal failure. Proper CCR documentation ensures continuity of care and expedites repair. Knowing when to escalate is a key skill in Clinical Engineering.

That is successful troubleshooting.
