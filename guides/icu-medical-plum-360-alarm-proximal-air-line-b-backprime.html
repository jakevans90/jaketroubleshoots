ICU Medical Plum 360

Alarm: Proximal AIR Line B! Backprime.

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

Proximal AIR Line B alarm causing infusion to stop due to detected air in Line B near the pump cassette, preventing safe medication delivery.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the infusion immediately
  • Transition the patient to another infusion pump if therapy is required
  • Do not resume infusion until air is fully cleared

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm alarm message: “Proximal AIR Line B! Backprime.”
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup or during infusion)
  • Check if Line B is stopped while Line A may still function
  • Look for visible air bubbles near the cassette

Inspect Line B for Visible Air (Most Common Cause)

  • Trace tubing from fluid source to cassette
  • Look for air bubbles in tubing
  • Ensure drip chamber is at least half full
  • Check all connections for looseness

Check Fluid Source and Spike Connection

  • Ensure IV bag is not empty or near empty
  • Verify spike is fully inserted and secure
  • Check for collapsed bags or venting issues

Backprime Line B (Manufacturer-Directed Step)

  • Open cassette door
  • Clear program if required
  • Initiate backprime to remove air

Reinstall Cassette and Restart Infusion

  • Close cassette door securely
  • Reprogram infusion if needed
  • Restart Line B

Swap Administration Set if Issue Persists

  • Replace entire Line B administration set
  • Ensure proper priming before installation

If the Problem Persists

If air alarms continue after clearing, repriming, and replacing the set, internal air detection or cassette interface issues may be present.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or clear air alarms while connected to a patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before continuing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Proximal AIR Line B! Backprime alarm triggered and infusion stopped on Line B."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Air present in proximal Line B tubing due to incomplete priming and low fluid level in drip chamber."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Backprimed Line B, removed air, verified fluid source, and restarted infusion without further alarms."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Air-in-line alarms should always be treated as real hazards. Start with simple external causes like fluid source and priming, and escalate if necessary to protect patient safety.

That is successful troubleshooting.
