STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Over Sterilize Temperature - Sterilize temperature is above setpoint by more than prescribed amount

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the “Over Sterilize Temperature” alarm, which occurs when chamber temperature exceeds allowable limits. This condition can lead to improper sterilization control and potential damage to instruments or loads.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not use items from the affected cycle until sterilization is confirmed valid
  • If cycle is active, allow it to complete or abort per facility policy

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm message is displayed exactly
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (typically during sterilize phase)
  • Check if the cycle continues automatically (expected behavior)

3. Check Steam Supply Pressure

  • Verify facility steam pressure to the unit
  • Ensure pressure is not excessively high (greater than normal operating range)

4. Inspect Steam Supply Piping

  • Check for recent facility adjustments or valve changes
  • Ensure no bypass or regulator issues allowing excessive pressure

5. Check Chamber Steam Trap Function

  • Observe for improper condensate removal
  • Listen for abnormal steam trap behavior if accessible

6. Evaluate Solenoid Valve Operation

  • Listen for normal actuation sounds during cycle phases
  • Observe for signs of sticking or continuous flow

7. Consider Control Calibration Drift

  • If no external issues are found, suspect calibration drift
  • Look for consistent temperature overshoot across cycles

If the Problem Persists

If external causes have been ruled out and the alarm continues:

  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or calibration

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a sterilizer during active patient use. Always ensure instruments are processed on a verified working unit before clinical use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer alarmed ‘Over Sterilize Temperature’ during sterilize phase; cycle continued but exceeded setpoint."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Steam supply pressure elevated above normal range / no external cause identified indicating possible internal fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified steam supply and piping; unit removed from service and escalated for repair and calibration."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Over-temperature alarms indicate a loss of controlled sterilization conditions. Always rule out external causes first, but escalate promptly if proper temperature control cannot be maintained.
