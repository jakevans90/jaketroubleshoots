Shimadzu RADspeed Pro Series

Bucky table or vertical stand movement/tracking failure

Asset Type

Radiographic System

Manufacturer

Shimadzu

Model

RADspeed Pro Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Bucky table or vertical stand movement, positioning, alignment, or tracking problems before determining that repair is required.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the room from clinical use if the Bucky table or vertical stand cannot be positioned reliably, moves unexpectedly, binds, or does not maintain proper alignment with the X-ray tube.

Do not attempt positioning tests with a patient on the table or standing at the wall stand. Use another radiographic room or approved imaging alternative until the issue is resolved.

Expected outcome: No patient is exposed to movement hazards, positioning instability, repeat imaging risk, or possible misalignment.

2. Confirm the Installed Configuration and Exact Complaint

Determine whether the reported issue involves:

  • Bucky table vertical movement or tabletop movement
  • Vertical Bucky stand movement
  • Tube-to-Bucky vertical tracking
  • Table-study preset SID alignment
  • Bucky detector/tray position or longitudinal synchronization
  • Intermittent, noisy, stiff, drifting, or completely failed movement

The RADspeed Pro family may provide synchronized movement in which the tube focal point follows the vertical position of the Bucky stand or Bucky table; table-study configurations may also move the tube to a preset SID. Available functions vary by system configuration and installed options.

Expected outcome: The failed movement function and whether tracking is expected on this installed system are clearly identified.

3. Inspect the Area for Obstructions or Collision Conditions

With the room out of clinical use, inspect the table, vertical stand, detector tray area, wall clearance, floor area, cables, accessory holders, patient supports, and nearby equipment.

Check for:

  • Items beneath or against the table
  • Objects blocking vertical stand travel
  • Loose accessories interfering with motion
  • Cable loops, detector tethering, or covers catching during travel
  • Evidence that the table or stand recently struck an object

Remove any obstruction only when safe and then recheck movement.

Expected outcome: The table and vertical stand have a clear travel path. If removing an obstruction restores smooth movement and tracking, document the finding and stop.

4. Check for Visible Mechanical Damage or Unsafe Movement

Inspect accessible external surfaces and movement assemblies without removing covers.

Look for:

  • Bent panels, rails, handles, detector tray components, or accessory mounts
  • Loose hardware visible from the exterior
  • Cracked covers or impact marks
  • Grinding, scraping, clicking, or abnormal motor noise
  • Sudden dropping, drifting, jerking, or uneven motion

Do not continue movement testing if the table or stand appears structurally damaged, unstable, or capable of uncontrolled motion.

Expected outcome: The unit either appears externally intact for further testing or is immediately removed from service because of a mechanical safety concern.

5. Verify System Power and Normal Startup Status

Confirm that the radiographic system, table, stand, and associated control components are powered as expected.

Check for:

  • System power indications present
  • Normal startup completion
  • Fault messages, error codes, or abnormal status indicators
  • Emergency stop or room safety interlock conditions, where applicable and accessible
  • Movement control panels or hand controls responding normally

Record all displayed messages before cycling power or clearing a condition.

Expected outcome: The system is powered and ready for safe movement testing, or a recorded system fault indicates escalation is required.

6. Test Local Manual Movement Controls Without a Patient

Using only normal operator-accessible controls, attempt the affected Bucky table or vertical stand movement through a small, safe range of travel.

Evaluate whether:

  • Movement starts when commanded
  • Movement stops when the control is released
  • Travel is smooth and predictable
  • Locks release and re-engage normally
  • The problem affects one direction, one axis, or all movement
  • Movement works locally but not during synchronized tracking

Do not force the equipment past resistance or repeatedly command movement when abnormal noise or binding is present.

Expected outcome: Manual movement either operates normally or confirms a reproducible movement failure. If movement is unsafe or abnormal, stop and remove the device from service.

7. Verify Table Load, Detector Tray, and Accessory Conditions

Ensure the table is unloaded during troubleshooting and that no accessories, detector components, or positioning devices are interfering with operation.

Confirm that:

  • No patient or excessive equipment load is present
  • The detector tray or Bucky assembly is properly seated, if accessible during normal use
  • Removable accessories are not preventing travel
  • The table surface and stand are not being physically restricted

The RADspeed Pro Bucky table is described by Shimadzu as supporting up to 650 lb / 295 kg; troubleshooting should still be conducted unloaded to remove load-related movement variables and protect staff.

Expected outcome: External loading or accessory interference is ruled out. If correcting accessory placement restores movement, document the action and stop.

8. Test Tracking or Synchronization Only After Manual Motion Is Confirmed Safe

If the installed system is equipped for tracking, select the applicable Bucky table or vertical stand workflow and observe whether the related tube support follows appropriately during controlled positioning.

Check whether:

  • The correct Bucky table or vertical stand is selected
  • Manual movement works before tracking is attempted
  • Tube position follows the selected Bucky component as expected
  • Tracking stops, lags, moves in the wrong direction, or fails completely
  • Preset SID positioning is achieved during a table workflow, where equipped

Do not make exposures for troubleshooting if alignment or tracking is questionable.

Expected outcome: Tracking operates predictably and alignment can be confirmed, or the failure is isolated to synchronization/position sensing rather than basic movement.

9. Perform a Final Operational Safety Check Before Returning to Service

Only after the reported problem is resolved, confirm:

  • Smooth table or vertical stand movement
  • Predictable stopping and locking
  • No abnormal sounds or drift
  • Normal tracking behavior, when equipped
  • Correct tube-to-Bucky alignment through the tested workflow
  • No remaining system error messages

Follow local Clinical Engineering policy for functional verification before returning the room to clinical use.

Expected outcome: The system is safe, stable, and functioning as expected. If any concern remains, do not return it to service.

If the Problem Persists

If obstructions, external damage, accessory interference, load conditions, power status, configuration selection, and basic movement checks do not resolve the problem, the failure is likely related to an internal drive, brake, sensor, position feedback, synchronization, control, or communication issue.

The radiographic system should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or qualified bench/field-service evaluation

Do not continue commanding motion on a table or vertical stand that binds, drifts, moves unpredictably, or fails tracking verification. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting, especially when movement failure could affect patient safety or imaging accuracy.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot Bucky table or vertical stand movement with a patient positioned on or near the affected equipment. Move the patient to another imaging solution first, then evaluate movement and tracking only under safe, controlled conditions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the Shimadzu RADspeed Pro vertical Bucky stand moved manually but the overhead tube no longer followed during wall-stand positioning."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified normal stand movement with no external obstruction, but tube-to-stand vertical tracking failed repeatedly during the selected upright workflow."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the radiographic room from service, labeled it Out of Service, documented the tracking failure and error observations, and escalated for Shimadzu service evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Room or system asset number
  • Table versus vertical stand involved
  • Tracking feature installed and selected workflow
  • Whether manual movement operates normally
  • Whether failure affects one direction or all travel
  • Obstructions or accessories checked
  • Detector tray/Bucky seating verified
  • Displayed faults or status indicators
  • Abnormal sounds, drift, binding, or impact evidence
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed from service

Final Thought

Bucky table and vertical stand problems must be approached as both movement-safety and positioning-accuracy concerns. Begin with patient protection and simple external causes, confirm whether manual motion or tracking has failed, and escalate promptly when the system cannot be safely verified. Clear CCR documentation helps repair teams act efficiently and protects future patients from repeat failure.

That is successful troubleshooting.
