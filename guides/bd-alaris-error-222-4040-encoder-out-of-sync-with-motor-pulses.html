BD Alaris

Error: 222.4040 Encoder out of sync with motor pulses

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 222.4040 indicating motor/encoder mismatch causing inaccurate infusion delivery or pump stoppage.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use immediately if actively infusing
  • Transition infusion to a functioning pump

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error 222.4040 appears
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup or during infusion)
  • Listen for unusual motor sounds (grinding, skipping, hesitation)

Check IV Set Installation and Pump Module Seating

  • Open and reload the IV set per Alaris loading procedure
  • Inspect for misrouted or improperly seated tubing
  • Ensure door is fully latched
  • Confirm pump module is fully seated in the PC unit

Inspect Pump Channel for Obstruction or Contamination

  • Check for fluid intrusion, residue, or debris
  • Inspect for sticky or restricted movement
  • Clean externally per facility protocol if needed

Test with a Known Good IV Set

  • Load a new BD-approved IV set
  • Run a test infusion off patient

Observe Motor Operation During Test Run

  • Start a prime or low-rate infusion off patient
  • Verify smooth, continuous motor movement
  • Listen for irregular pulsing or abnormal noises

Power Cycle the Pump Module

  • Power down the system
  • Remove and reseat the module if applicable
  • Restart the pump

If the Problem Persists

If Error 222.4040 continues after completing all steps:

  • External and setup-related causes have been ruled out
  • The issue likely involves internal motor control circuitry, encoder feedback failure, or drive mechanism fault

Remove the pump from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before addressing the issue.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"BD Alaris pump displaying Error 222.4040 (encoder out of sync with motor pulses). Pump unable to maintain infusion."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified proper IV set loading, replaced tubing, inspected for obstruction, and reseated module. Error persisted, indicating likely internal motor/encoder failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair/biomed evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Encoder and motor synchronization is essential for precise infusion control. When a mismatch is detected, the system is protecting the patient from inaccurate delivery. Effective troubleshooting focuses on ruling out external causes before escalating appropriately.

That is successful troubleshooting.
