BD Alaris

Error: 120.4320 High voltage on 24V supply line

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error 120.4320 High voltage on 24V supply line, which indicates an overvoltage condition on the internal power rail. This can cause the pump to shut down, fail to power correctly, or create unsafe charging behavior.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the infusion and remove the pump from patient use
  • Transfer therapy to a functioning pump
  • Confirm patient care continues without interruption

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump in the module or PCU
  • Confirm Error 120.4320 is displayed
  • Determine when the error occurs (docking, AC connection, or during operation)
  • Observe whether the module powers at all
  • Check if the issue only occurs when connected to the PCU

Check PCU (Point-of-Care Unit) Power Source

  • Ensure the PCU is plugged into a known good outlet
  • Verify the PCU powers on normally
  • Check if other modules are experiencing similar power issues

Test Module in a Different PCU Slot or Another PCU

  • Move the affected module to a different slot in the same PCU
  • Test the module in a known working PCU
  • Observe whether the error follows the module or remains with the PCU/slot

Inspect Module and PCU Connectors

  • Check connectors for bent pins, debris, or contamination
  • Look for signs of arcing or discoloration
  • Reseat the module securely

Remove from Dock and Test Standalone (If Applicable)

  • Undock the module and observe behavior if standalone function is possible
  • Determine if the error only occurs when docked

Check for Environmental or Physical Damage

  • Inspect for liquid intrusion
  • Check for burning smells or excessive heat
  • Look for cracks or physical damage to the housing

If the Problem Persists

If Error 120.4320 continues after external troubleshooting and isolation:

  • Internal power regulation circuitry (module or PCU) is likely faulty
  • Overvoltage conditions cannot be safely corrected externally
  • Remove the affected equipment from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send the module and/or PCU for repair based on isolation results

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an Alaris pump on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup device before performing any troubleshooting steps.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Alaris module displaying Error 120.4320 and not operating when connected to PCU."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Overvoltage fault persisted across multiple PCUs and slots, indicating internal module power regulation failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Tested module in multiple PCUs, confirmed fault followed module, removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power-related errors in Alaris systems require careful isolation between module and PCU. Always verify external sources first, but escalate quickly when overvoltage conditions persist to ensure safety and reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
