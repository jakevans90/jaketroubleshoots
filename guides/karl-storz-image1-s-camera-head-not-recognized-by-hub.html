Karl Storz Image1 S

Camera Head Not Recognized by Hub

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Karl Storz

Model

Image1 S

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes when the camera head is not detected by the hub, preventing image display and delaying procedures.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the system is not in use on an active patient. Move the procedure to a backup camera system if needed.

Verify Camera Head Model Compatibility

  • Ensure the camera head is compatible with the Image1 S hub. Incompatible models may not be recognized. Expected outcome: hub should detect the correct camera head type.

Inspect and Secure Connections

  • Check that the camera cable is fully seated in the camera head and hub port. Look for bent pins or debris. Proper seating ensures the hub can communicate with the camera head.

Swap Cables or Ports

  • Replace the camera cable with a known-good cable or try a different hub port. If the hub recognizes the camera, the issue is likely a faulty cable or port.

Power Cycle the System

  • Turn off the hub and camera head, wait 10–15 seconds, then power back on. This can reset the communication handshake. Expected outcome: hub detects the camera head.

Check Firmware Versions

  • Confirm that both the camera head and hub are running compatible firmware. Mismatched firmware may prevent recognition.

Observe Hub Indicators

  • Look for hub LEDs or display messages indicating camera detection status. These can confirm whether the hub is actively attempting recognition.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. The camera head or hub may have internal faults.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents further damage and preserves patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt troubleshooting on an active patient. Always ensure an alternative camera system is available to maintain uninterrupted visualization during procedures.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"OR staff reported that the Image1 S camera head was not recognized by the hub."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Cable seating checked and swapped; hub and camera head firmware incompatible; hub LEDs indicate no handshake."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"System powered down and firmware verified; device labeled Out of Service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Cable condition checked/swapped
  • Hub port tested
  • LED indicators observed
  • Firmware versions documented
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Troubleshoot logically from external, verifiable causes before internal repair. Accurate documentation ensures smooth repair workflow and procedural readiness.

That is successful troubleshooting.
