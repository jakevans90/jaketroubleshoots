Stryker 1688 Camera System

Light Source Failure

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

1688 Camera System

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes when the camera light source fails, dimming or eliminating illumination and affecting procedural visualization.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm no patient is actively undergoing a procedure with the affected camera. Move the patient to a backup camera/light source to prevent procedural delays or hazards.

Verify Light Source Power

  • Ensure the light source unit is powered on. Check that the power switch is engaged and indicator lights are illuminated. Failure here may indicate a simple power issue.

Check Power Connection and Outlet

  • Confirm the light source is plugged into a functional outlet. Test the outlet with another device to ensure power availability.

Inspect Light Cable and Connector

  • Disconnect and reconnect the light cable from the camera and light source. Look for bent pins, debris, or visible damage that could interrupt illumination.

Swap Light Cable if Available

  • Replace the light cable with a known working cable to rule out cable failure. Observe if illumination is restored.

Verify Console/Camera Settings

  • Check the camera system settings to ensure light intensity is not set to minimum or muted. Reset to default if unsure.

Test Light Source with Alternative Camera

  • Connect the light source to a different camera head to determine if the problem is the light unit or the camera head.

Inspect for Overheating or Unusual Behavior

  • Look for excessive heat, smoke, or unusual sounds from the light source. If present, discontinue use immediately and escalate.

If the Problem Persists

After external checks, the issue is likely internal to the light source or console.

  • Remove the light source from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents patient risk and equipment damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt light source troubleshooting during active surgery. Move the patient to a functional system first. Only adjust connections and settings when safe to do so.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Light source not illuminating during procedure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Light cable connector worn, preventing proper power delivery."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced light cable; verified illumination restored."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and functional
  • Light cable swapped
  • Camera settings verified
  • Light source power indicator observed
  • Device status after testing

Final Thought

Patient safety and visualization are paramount. Troubleshoot logically from simple external causes to more complex issues. Proper escalation and documentation ensure continuity of care and regulatory compliance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
