Cardinal Health Kangaroo ePump

Error: 14 Timeout during downstream occlusion check (rotor signal failure)

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo ePump

What This Guide Helps With

Error 14 indicates a failed downstream occlusion check due to missing rotor feedback or motor-related sensing failure. This can cause the pump to stop operation and prevents safe enteral feeding delivery.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the pump immediately
  • Remove from patient use
  • Transfer feeding to a backup device

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and observe startup
  • Confirm Error 14 is displayed
  • Note when it occurs (startup, priming, or feeding)
  • Listen for motor activity or absence of movement

Power Cycle the Pump

  • Turn the pump OFF
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Turn the pump back ON
  • Observe if the error clears

Inspect Feeding Set Installation

  • Open the pump door
  • Remove and reinstall the feeding set
  • Ensure tubing is properly seated in the rotor path
  • Confirm the door fully closes and latches

Check for Mechanical Obstruction

  • Inspect rotor area for debris or dried formula
  • Check for visible damage
  • Gently rotate rotor if accessible
  • Ensure smooth movement without resistance

Swap Feeding Set

  • Install a new, known-good feeding set
  • Restart the pump
  • Observe if the error clears

Observe Motor Operation

  • Attempt to start a feed cycle
  • Listen for motor engagement
  • Check for normal rotation behavior

If the Problem Persists

If Error 14 continues after completing all external troubleshooting steps:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Indicates internal motor, rotor sensor, or control board failure
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or rely on a feeding pump displaying active errors while connected to a patient. Always transition to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 14 and unable to initiate feeding; no motor movement observed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Rotor signal failure during downstream occlusion check; no motor activity detected despite proper setup."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Power cycled unit, reloaded feeding set, inspected rotor area, and replaced tubing with no resolution; removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error 14 points to a failure in rotor detection or motor feedback. Always rule out setup and mechanical issues first, then escalate appropriately. Patient safety and timely removal from service are critical.

That is successful troubleshooting.
