Mindray BeneView T Series

Touchscreen / Display Problems

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Mindray

Model

BeneView T Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the touchscreen on a Mindray BeneView T Series monitor is unresponsive, erratic, or displays visual artifacts such as flickering, frozen screens, or distorted images. The focus is on external and easily verifiable causes first—like power, cables, environmental interference, and settings—before assuming internal hardware failure. The goal is to restore safe patient monitoring while ensuring patient safety and proper documentation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the monitor is plugged into a known working outlet or power strip.
  • Check the monitor’s power switch and confirm the display lights up.
  • Why: Touchscreen issues can mimic display failure if the monitor isn’t receiving proper power.

Check Battery and Charging Status

  • If the monitor is battery-powered, confirm battery is charged.
  • Swap in a known-good battery if available.
  • Why: Low battery can cause erratic touchscreen behavior.

Inspect Cables and Connections

  • Ensure all patient leads and accessory cables are properly seated.
  • Look for frayed or damaged cables that may affect system function.
  • Why: Loose or damaged connections can trigger screen errors or unresponsiveness.

Perform a Soft Reset / Reboot

  • Use the monitor’s power button to restart the system.
  • Wait 1–2 minutes for a full boot.
  • Why: Temporary software glitches can cause frozen or unresponsive touchscreens.

Clean the Touchscreen

  • Power off the monitor.
  • Use a soft, lint-free cloth slightly dampened with manufacturer-approved cleaner.
  • Avoid excessive moisture or harsh chemicals.
  • Why: Dirt, grease, or residue can prevent accurate touch detection.

Check Environmental Factors

  • Ensure the monitor is not exposed to extreme temperature, humidity, or direct sunlight.
  • Remove nearby electronic devices that may cause interference.
  • Why: Environmental conditions can impact touch sensitivity and display performance.

Test Touch Responsiveness

  • Tap multiple areas of the screen after reboot.
  • Confirm all functions respond (menu navigation, alarms, parameter selection).
  • Why: Helps determine if the issue is localized to a portion of the screen.

Check for Software Alerts or Updates

  • Look for any on-screen system messages or error codes.
  • Verify if the monitor requires a software update via hospital IT or Mindray service.
  • Why: Firmware/software issues can cause intermittent display or touchscreen failures.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out (power, cables, environment, battery, cleaning). The issue is likely internal (touch panel, LCD, or mainboard failure).

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label “Out of Service.”
  • Send to Mindray-authorized repair or in-house biomedical bench evaluation.
  • Do not attempt internal board or touchscreen replacement unless trained.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot while the patient is attached. Move the patient to another monitor to maintain continuous monitoring. Only attempt troubleshooting when it is safe for the patient.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Touchscreen is unresponsive, screen flickers, or buttons do not respond.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Power source and battery confirmed good.
Cables and connections checked.
Screen cleaned; soft reset performed.
Issue persists—likely internal touchscreen or display failure.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Device removed from service.
Labeled Out of Service.
Sent for repair/bench evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Power outlet verified
  • Battery level/status checked
  • Cables checked for integrity
  • Soft reset performed
  • Cleaning method documented
  • Environmental conditions noted
  • Touchscreen test results
  • Any error messages or codes

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting ensures patient safety, maintains logical workflow, and avoids unnecessary internal repairs. Documenting your steps using CCR ensures clarity for follow-up and repair. Knowing when to escalate is just as important as fixing the problem.

That is successful troubleshooting.
