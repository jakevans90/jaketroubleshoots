GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00] Thermostat Open During Run Time

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00], which indicates an open thermostat condition during run time. The failure can interrupt radiant heat operation and prevent the warmer from providing reliable controlled thermal support.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the Panda Warmer from patient use if Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00] occurs during warming or preparation for use.
  • Transfer the infant to another approved warming method or a known-working warmer according to clinical procedure before troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on the affected warmer for thermal support until the failure has been resolved and safe operation has been verified.

Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on a warmer with an unresolved heater safety shutdown.

Why it matters: This error indicates the warmer cannot reliably provide controlled radiant heat because a thermostat in the heater protection circuit is open.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, confirm that the display shows Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00].
  • Document whether the error occurs immediately at startup, after the warmer has been operating, after extended heating operation, or intermittently during use.
  • Observe for associated behavior such as loss of heat output, interruption of warming operation, or repeated shutdown after restart.

Expected outcome: The error is reproducible or clearly documented as occurring during run time.

Why it matters: This confirms a heater safety-related failure condition rather than an unrelated operating or setup issue.

3. Check Power Source and Basic External Condition

  • Confirm that the warmer is connected to an approved hospital-grade outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, and strain relief for damage, looseness, discoloration, or evidence of overheating.
  • Inspect the warmer externally for abnormal heat exposure, odor, discoloration, or visible damage near the heater head.

Expected outcome: The unit has a stable power source and no obvious external damage contributing to unsafe operation.

Why it matters: Basic electrical or physical abnormalities should be ruled out before repair escalation.

4. Inspect the Heater Duct and Heater Head Vents for Blockage

  • Allow the heater head to cool before inspection.
  • Inspect the heater duct and heater head ventilation openings for dust, lint, drapes, blankets, covers, stored items, tape, labels, debris, or other airflow restrictions.
  • Look for external contamination or visible physical damage that may interfere with airflow.
  • Remove external blockage or contamination according to facility cleaning and safety procedures.

Expected outcome: Heater airflow paths and cooling vents are clear and unobstructed.

Why it matters: Restricted airflow can cause overheating, opening the over-temperature thermostat and triggering the error.

5. Determine Whether Vent Blockage Caused an Over-Temperature Condition

  • If a blockage is found, document its location and condition.
  • After the heater head has cooled and the obstruction has been corrected, perform an operational check only while the warmer remains out of patient use.
  • Monitor for recurrence of Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00] during the test.
  • If the error does not recur and normal controlled heat output is verified, document the corrected airflow obstruction and return the warmer to service according to departmental policy.

Expected outcome: The warmer completes operational verification without the error returning.

Why it matters: Clearing a blocked airflow path may correct the condition when the thermostat opened due to heater head overheating.

6. Evaluate a Persistent Thermostat-Related Failure

  • If no external blockage is found, or the error returns after the vents are cleared and the heater head has cooled, do not continue clinical use.
  • Document that external airflow restrictions, visible overheating conditions, and basic power-source concerns were evaluated.
  • Route the warmer for qualified bench evaluation of the over-temperature thermostat circuit and heater-control system according to approved service procedures.

Expected outcome: External causes have been ruled out before repair evaluation begins.

Why it matters: A recurring thermostat-open error may indicate an internal heater-control or safety-circuit failure requiring repair.

7. Isolate a Persistent Device-Related Failure

  • If Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00] persists after external airflow paths are confirmed clear, the condition is no longer explained by an externally correctable vent obstruction.
  • Do not return the warmer to patient care based only on a temporary reset or restart.
  • Escalate for bench evaluation and repair, including Power PCA replacement when indicated by authorized service findings.

Expected outcome: The device is appropriately withheld from service until the heater safety failure is corrected.

Why it matters: Heater protection failures require verified correction before the warmer can be trusted for infant care.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00] remains after the power source, external condition, heater duct, and heater head vents have been checked and external airflow restrictions have been ruled out, the warmer should not be returned to clinical use.

The persistent error indicates a thermostat-related heater safety failure requiring repair or further bench evaluation. Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation according to departmental procedure.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or repeatedly restart an infant warmer while it is supporting an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first, because an unresolved thermostat-related heater failure can interrupt warming and create risk of inadequate thermal support.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00] during operation and stopped providing reliable controlled heat."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection identified blocked heater head ventilation consistent with an over-temperature thermostat opening condition. The obstruction was cleared and the heater head was allowed to cool."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from patient use, cleared obstruction from heater head vents, completed out-of-service functional verification without recurrence of Sys Fail 09 [0B 00 00 00], and returned the unit to service according to departmental policy."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A thermostat-open system failure on an infant warmer must be treated as a heater safety concern. Begin with patient protection, inspect external airflow and overheating causes, verify safe performance only while the warmer is out of patient use, and escalate persistent failures for repair. Clear CCR documentation supports safe decisions and efficient follow-up troubleshooting.

That is successful troubleshooting.
