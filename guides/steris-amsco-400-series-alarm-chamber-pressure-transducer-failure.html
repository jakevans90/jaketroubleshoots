STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Chamber Pressure Transducer Failure – Output voltage outside normal range

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: Chamber Pressure Transducer Failure causing cycle abort due to invalid pressure readings, preventing accurate chamber pressure monitoring and safe sterilization.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service immediately if this alarm occurs
  • Do not use any instruments from an aborted cycle

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows: Chamber Pressure Transducer Failure
  • Observe if the cycle aborts automatically
  • Check if the alarm occurs at startup or during a cycle
  • Look for missing, erratic, or out-of-range pressure readings

Inspect Accessible Transducer Wiring Connections

  • Check for loose, disconnected, or damaged wiring at accessible connection points
  • Ensure all connectors are secure

Check for Moisture or Contamination

  • Inspect for water intrusion or condensation near wiring or connectors
  • Verify the area is clean and dry

Perform a Power Reset

  • Power OFF the unit using the main disconnect
  • Wait 30–60 seconds, then power ON

Monitor Pressure Readings (If Accessible)

  • Observe chamber pressure values during startup
  • Check for stable readings within normal range
  • Identify erratic or out-of-range values indicating sensor or wiring issues

Determine Likely Cause

  • If wiring issues are found, address external connection problems
  • If wiring is intact but readings are invalid, suspect transducer failure
  • If readings are inconsistent across the system, consider calibration or control issues

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks are completed and the alarm remains, the issue is likely due to a failed chamber pressure transducer, control out of calibration, or main control board failure.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair and calibration

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move patient care to a backup device before addressing equipment issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Alarm: Chamber Pressure Transducer Failure displayed; unit aborts cycle and shows abnormal pressure readings"

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Pressure transducer output out of normal range; external wiring verified intact, likely transducer failure or calibration issue"

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Unit removed from service; labeled Out of Service and sent for repair and recalibration"

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Pressure sensing is fundamental to sterilization safety. Always verify simple causes first, but recognize that persistent sensor alarms typically indicate failure requiring escalation. Proper documentation ensures traceability and patient safety.

That is successful troubleshooting.
