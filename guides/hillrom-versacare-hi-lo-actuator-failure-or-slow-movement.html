Hillrom VersaCare

Hi-Lo Actuator Failure or Slow Movement

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

VersaCare

What This Guide Helps With

Bed not raising or lowering, slow hi-lo movement, intermittent function, or no response from height controls due to power, controls, or actuator-related issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Move the patient to another bed before troubleshooting.
  • Engage brakes and ensure the bed is stable.

Expected: No risk to patient during troubleshooting

Why it matters: Unexpected bed movement or failure can create fall risk or injury.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to raise and lower the bed using all available controls:
    • Side rail controls
    • Footboard controls
    • Pendant (if equipped)
  • Observe:
    • No movement
    • Slow or uneven movement
    • Clicking sounds or no sound

Expected: Consistent response from at least one control source

Why it matters: Helps determine if the issue is control-related or mechanical

Check Power Source and Bed Power Status

  • Confirm the bed is plugged into a known working outlet.
  • Check for:
    • Power indicator lights
    • Bed not running on battery only
  • Test outlet with another device if needed.

Expected: Bed has stable AC power

Why it matters: Hi-lo actuators require sufficient power; battery-only operation may limit function

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Check the power cord for:
    • Damage or cuts
    • Loose connection at bed base
  • Ensure the cord is fully seated.

Expected: Secure, undamaged power connection

Why it matters: Poor power delivery can cause slow or failed actuator movement

Check for Bed Lockout or Control Restrictions

  • Verify that no lockout functions are active:
    • Check caregiver control panel for disabled functions
    • Ensure hi-lo function is enabled

Expected: Hi-lo function is not locked out

Why it matters: Lockouts can mimic actuator failure

Inspect for Physical Obstructions

  • Look under the bed frame for:
    • Debris or foreign objects
    • Linens or equipment caught in the frame
    • Damage to linkage or frame

Expected: Clear movement path

Why it matters: Obstructions can slow or stop actuator movement and strain the motor

Listen for Actuator Operation

  • While pressing hi-lo controls, listen for:
    • Motor running but no movement
    • Clicking or grinding sounds
    • No sound at all

Expected: Smooth motor sound with corresponding movement

Why it matters: Motor sound without movement → possible mechanical or actuator failure; No sound → possible electrical or control issue

Check Bed Weight Load

  • Ensure the bed is not overloaded or unevenly loaded.
  • Remove excess equipment if present.

Expected: Bed operates within normal load range

Why it matters: Excess weight can slow or stall actuator movement

Inspect Pendant and Control Cables

  • If equipped, check pendant and control wiring for:
    • Damage
    • Loose connections
    • Pinched cables
  • Swap with a known working pendant if available.

Expected: Controls respond normally

Why it matters: Faulty controls can prevent actuator activation

Perform Power Reset

  • Unplug the bed from AC power.
  • Wait 30–60 seconds.
  • Plug back in and retest hi-lo function.

Expected: System resets and may restore normal operation

Why it matters: Clears temporary control or communication faults

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal, such as:

  • Hi-lo actuator failure
  • Control board or relay issue
  • Internal wiring fault

Remove the bed from service. Label it Out of Service and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot bed movement functions with a patient in the bed. Always transfer the patient first to avoid fall or crush hazards.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed will not raise or lower; hi-lo function unresponsive."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Hi-lo actuator not responding despite verified power and controls."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and sent to shop for actuator evaluation and repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Power indicator status
  • Control panels tested (rails, footboard, pendant)
  • Presence of actuator noise
  • Any obstruction found
  • Load condition of bed
  • Final device status (in service or removed)

Final Thought

Hi-lo issues are often caused by simple external factors like power, controls, or obstructions. Systematic troubleshooting ensures safe operation while avoiding unnecessary part replacement. When the issue points to internal failure, prompt removal from service protects both patients and staff. Proper documentation supports efficient repair and future trend tracking.

That is successful troubleshooting.
