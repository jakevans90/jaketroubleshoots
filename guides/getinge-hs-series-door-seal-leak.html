Getinge HS Series

Door Seal Leak

Asset Type

Washer-Disinfector

Manufacturer

Getinge

Model

HS Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies external causes of door seal leaks, including gasket wear, misalignment, and foreign debris, preventing cycle aborts and water spray hazards.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient and Staff Safety

Confirm the washer is empty and staff are clear of potential spray.

Why: Prevents injury and contamination during inspection.

Inspect Door Seal / Gasket

Open the door and visually check for cracks, tears, or hardening of the gasket.

Expected Outcome: Gasket should be intact, flexible, and free of visible damage.

Check for Foreign Objects or Debris

Wipe the seal and door frame to remove trapped debris, lint, or detergent residue.

Expected Outcome: Door closes evenly without obstruction.

Verify Door Latch Engagement

Close the door and ensure it fully engages and locks without excessive force.

Expected Outcome: Door remains closed securely; misalignment can cause leaks.

Check Alignment of Door and Chamber

Look for uneven gaps between seal and chamber edge. Adjust door hinge if minor misalignment is noted.

Expected Outcome: Uniform contact along the entire seal.

Perform a Test Fill Cycle

Run a short rinse or fill cycle without instruments to observe for leaks.

Expected Outcome: No water escaping from door area; if leaking persists, internal seal issues are suspected.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. The gasket may be degraded or the door mechanism damaged.

Action:

  • Remove the washer from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to escalate ensures safe and proper use.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not operate the washer with instruments if leakage is observed.

Move instruments to another washer for patient safety.

Inspect seals routinely to prevent cycle interruptions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported water leaking from washer door during cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Gasket showed signs of wear and minor misalignment of door frame."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned debris and tested door; leak persisted, unit labeled Out of Service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Seal condition (cracks, hardness)
  • Door alignment and latch engagement
  • Any unusual water spray pattern
  • Test cycle results
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety and proper washer function are paramount. Follow a logical troubleshooting path from simple inspection to escalation. Accurate documentation supports maintenance history and reduces repeat issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
