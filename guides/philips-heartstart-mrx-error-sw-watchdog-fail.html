Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: SW Watchdog Fail

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot SW Watchdog Fail errors on the Philips HeartStart MRx. This condition may indicate software instability, unexpected restart behavior, lockups, failed startup, failed operational checks, or unreliable defibrillator operation.

A software watchdog failure should be treated as a reliability concern because the device may not remain stable during monitoring, pacing, cardioversion, defibrillation readiness, or emergency response use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the HeartStart MRx from patient use if the error appears during operation, startup, charging, monitoring, pacing, defibrillation readiness checks, or operational testing.
  • Provide clinical staff with a known-good backup defibrillator before continuing troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on a defibrillator with recurring restart, lockup, or watchdog behavior for active patient care.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the HeartStart MRx on and confirm the exact displayed error is SW Watchdog Fail.
  • Note when the error appears, such as during startup, self-test, monitoring, charging, therapy mode selection, normal operation, or after a restart.
  • Ask staff whether the unit restarted unexpectedly, froze, became unresponsive, failed an operational check, or returned the error after a power cycle.
  • Document whether the error is reproducible or based on staff report.

Translate the Error Into the Real-World Failure

  • Understand the complaint as a software stability or system control failure.
  • Recognize that the error may not point to pads, paddles, ECG leads, sensors, or other normal accessories.
  • Look for real-world symptoms such as unexpected restart, frozen screen, failed startup, failed operational check, or repeated lockup.

Restart the Unit

  • Turn the HeartStart MRx off.
  • Wait briefly, then power the unit back on.
  • Allow the device to complete startup and normal self-check behavior.
  • If the error clears, continue with verification before considering return to service.
  • If the error returns, treat it as a recurring reliability issue.

Check Power Source and Battery Condition

  • Verify the unit is connected to a known-good AC power source.
  • Confirm the AC power indicator behaves normally.
  • Check that the battery is properly seated and charged.
  • If available, test with a known-good compatible battery.
  • Watch for flickering, shutdown, reset behavior, or error recurrence.

Inspect External Condition

  • Inspect the housing, display, controls, battery bay, AC input area, external ports, and therapy selector.
  • Look for signs of impact damage, cracked housing, loose connectors, contamination, fluid intrusion, sticky controls, or evidence of being dropped.
  • Document any visible damage before additional troubleshooting or escalation.

Remove Nonessential Accessories and Recheck

  • Disconnect nonessential accessories such as ECG cable, SpO2 sensor, NBP hose, CO2 accessories, therapy cable, printer-related accessories, data card, or external communication accessories if present.
  • Restart the unit again with only the required power source installed.
  • Observe whether the SW Watchdog Fail error returns.
  • This helps rule out an external connection or peripheral condition contributing to abnormal startup behavior.

Run Available Operational Checks

  • If the device powers on successfully, perform the standard operational check allowed by your facility process.
  • Verify power-on behavior, display response, control response, therapy selector response, battery indicators, AC indicators, and accessory recognition if applicable.
  • Confirm the error does not return during testing.
  • If the unit passes all required checks and the error does not recur, follow facility policy before returning it to service.

Determine Whether the Error Is Recurring

  • Treat the error as recurring if it returns after restart, appears during multiple power cycles, occurs during operational check, appears intermittently after clearing, or staff report repeated unexpected restarts or lockups.
  • Do not repeatedly clear and return a unit that continues to show watchdog behavior.
  • Recurring SW Watchdog Fail conditions require escalation through the approved service process.

Reload Software if Approved and Supported

  • If the SW Watchdog Fail error recurs, follow the approved Philips HeartStart MRx software support process used by your facility.
  • Only perform software reloads if your Clinical Engineering department is trained, authorized, and has the correct approved software tools and version control process.
  • Do not attempt unsupported software changes, unofficial reload methods, or unapproved configuration changes.

Verify After Software Reload

  • After an approved software reload, restart the device and complete all required operational checks.
  • Confirm the SW Watchdog Fail error does not return.
  • Verify startup is normal, controls respond normally, monitoring and therapy readiness checks pass, and no abnormal shutdowns, freezes, or restarts occur.
  • Confirm device configuration is correct for your facility before returning the unit to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the SW Watchdog Fail error returns after restart, external checks, accessory removal, power checks, and approved software reload, the likely external contributors have been ruled out.

Remove the HeartStart MRx from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. Escalate to Philips or the appropriate Response Center for further repair direction.

Persistent SW Watchdog Fail errors may indicate a deeper software, system control, or internal hardware-related fault that should not be handled through repeated field resets.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a defibrillator with a software watchdog error while it is assigned to an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first.

A defibrillator with unresolved restart, lockup, or watchdog behavior should not be relied on for monitoring, pacing, cardioversion, or defibrillation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx reported SW Watchdog Fail error during startup. Staff removed the unit from service due to concern for unreliable defibrillator operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"SW Watchdog Fail confirmed during troubleshooting. AC power, battery seating, visible damage, and accessory-related causes were checked. Error returned after restart, indicating recurring software watchdog failure requiring approved software reload or Philips escalation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Restarted unit, checked AC power and battery seating, inspected external condition, and removed nonessential accessories. Error recurred after restart. Unit labeled Out of Service and escalated for approved software reload or Philips Response Center support."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

SW Watchdog Fail should be treated as a reliability concern, not just a nuisance message. Start with patient safety, confirm the error, rule out simple external contributors, and escalate appropriately when the error recurs. Accurate CCR documentation shows that the device was evaluated logically and removed from service when continued use could not be justified.

That is successful troubleshooting.
