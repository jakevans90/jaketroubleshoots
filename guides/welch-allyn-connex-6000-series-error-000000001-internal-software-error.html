Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000001 Internal Software Error

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000001 indicating internal software failure causing system freeze, reboot, or inability to use the monitor.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use and transition monitoring to a backup device.
  • Expected: Patient care continues without interruption.
  • Why it matters: This error can cause loss of monitoring or unreliable readings.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error #000000001 appears.
  • Observe when it occurs (startup, during use, or intermittently).
  • Expected: Error is visible and behavior is consistent (freeze, reboot, or unresponsive screen).
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true system fault rather than a temporary glitch or user issue.

Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Shut down the monitor, unplug from AC power, and remove the battery (if accessible).
  • Wait 30–60 seconds, then reconnect and power on.
  • Expected: Device boots normally without error.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary software faults or memory corruption.
  • If resolved → Return device to service
  • If error persists → Continue troubleshooting

Check Power Source and Stability

  • Verify the monitor is connected to a known good outlet and/or a functioning battery.
  • Avoid power strips or unstable sources.
  • Expected: Stable power with no interruption during startup or operation.
  • Why it matters: Power instability can cause software faults and improper boot sequences.
  • If power issue found → Correct and retest
  • If power is stable → Continue

Inspect External Accessories and Modules

  • Disconnect all non-essential accessories (SpO₂ probe, NIBP hose, temperature probe, USB devices, network cables).
  • Power on with minimal configuration.
  • Expected: Device boots without error when accessories are removed.
  • Why it matters: Faulty accessories or communication errors can trigger system-level faults.
  • If error clears → Reconnect accessories one at a time to isolate cause
  • If error persists → Continue

Check for Environmental or Network Factors

  • Disconnect from network (Ethernet/Wi-Fi if applicable) and reboot the device.
  • Expected: Device operates normally when isolated.
  • Why it matters: Network communication issues or configuration conflicts can trigger software errors.

Attempt System Restart Multiple Times

  • Reboot the device 2–3 times to determine if the error is persistent or intermittent.
  • Expected: Consistent behavior across restarts.
  • Why it matters: Persistent errors indicate deeper system failure rather than transient faults.

If the Problem Persists

If the error continues after power cycling, accessory isolation, and power verification:

  • External and common causes have been ruled out
  • Error #000000001 strongly indicates an internal software or system board issue

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or manufacturer-level evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not rely on a monitor displaying internal software errors. Always transfer the patient to a fully functional monitor before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying Error #000000001 (internal software error). Device freezing during startup and unable to monitor patients."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal system/software fault. External power and accessories verified. Error persisted after multiple reboots and isolation steps."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair. Backup monitor provided for patient care."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • When the error occurs (startup, during monitoring, intermittent)
  • Any alarms, freezes, or reboot behavior observed
  • Accessories connected or removed during testing
  • Power source used (AC vs battery) and stability
  • Network connection status during testing
  • Indicator lights or screen behavior
  • Final device status (removed from service, sent for repair)

Final Thought

System-reported internal errors should be taken seriously. After ruling out power, accessories, and environment, escalation protects both patient safety and equipment integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
