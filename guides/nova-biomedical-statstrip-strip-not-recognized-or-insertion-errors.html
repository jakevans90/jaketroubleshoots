Nova Biomedical StatStrip

Strip Not Recognized or Insertion Errors

Asset Type

Blood Glucose Meter

Manufacturer

Nova Biomedical

Model

StatStrip

What This Guide Helps With

Meter fails to detect test strips, shows insertion errors, or prevents testing due to strip recognition issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot while actively testing a patient sample. Move testing to another meter if available.
  • Expected: No delay in patient care
  • Why it matters: Prevents incorrect results or delays in critical glucose readings

Verify the Reported Error Message

  • Power on the device and insert a test strip. Confirm the exact error (e.g., “Strip Not Recognized” or insertion error).
  • Expected: Error is consistent and repeatable
  • Why it matters: Confirms this is a true detection issue, not user technique

Check Test Strip Compatibility and Lot

  • Ensure the strips are correct for the StatStrip system and not mixed with another model or vendor.
  • Verify correct strip type
  • Confirm lot is approved for use
  • Expected: Correct strips are being used
  • Why it matters: Incorrect strips will not be recognized by the meter

Inspect Strip Expiration and Storage Conditions

  • Check expiration date and confirm strips were stored per manufacturer guidelines (sealed container, proper temperature/humidity).
  • Expected: Strips are within date and properly stored
  • Why it matters: Degraded strips can fail detection or produce errors

Inspect Strip Insertion Technique

  • Insert the strip fully and in the correct orientation.
  • Ensure contacts are facing the correct direction
  • Avoid partial insertion
  • Expected: Meter detects strip immediately
  • Why it matters: Improper insertion is a common cause of recognition errors

Check for Contamination or Debris in Strip Port

  • Visually inspect the strip port for:
  • Dirt or dried fluid
  • Bent or obstructed contacts
  • If needed, gently clean per approved methods (no liquid intrusion).
  • Expected: Clean, unobstructed port
  • Why it matters: Debris can block electrical contact with the strip

Try a New Test Strip from a Different Vial

  • Insert a strip from a new vial or different lot.
  • Expected: Meter recognizes the strip
  • Why it matters: Helps isolate whether the issue is strip-related or device-related

Check Meter Battery and Power Stability

  • Ensure the battery is adequately charged or properly seated.
  • Replace or reseat battery if needed
  • Expected: Stable power with no intermittent resets
  • Why it matters: Low or unstable power can affect strip detection circuitry

Restart the Device

  • Power cycle the meter and attempt strip insertion again.
  • Expected: Normal strip recognition resumes
  • Why it matters: Clears minor software or detection faults

Test with Known Good Conditions

  • Use a known good strip, proper insertion, and confirmed good battery.
  • Expected: Strip is recognized consistently
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is isolated to the device

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and the meter still does not recognize strips, the issue is likely internal (strip port contacts, internal connector, or detection circuitry).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Always verify strip recognition before applying a blood sample. Never attempt to troubleshoot during active patient testing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Meter not recognizing test strips during routine glucose testing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Contaminated strip port preventing proper contact with test strips."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned strip port and verified proper strip recognition with new vial of strips."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Multiple strip lots tested
  • Strip expiration verified
  • Strip port condition (clean/contaminated)
  • Battery status checked
  • Error message observed
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple, external checks like strips, insertion, and cleanliness before assuming device failure. Patient safety and accurate results must always come first. When basic causes are ruled out, proper escalation ensures reliability and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
