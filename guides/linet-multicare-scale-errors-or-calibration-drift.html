Linet Multicare

Scale Errors or Calibration Drift

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Linet

Model

Multicare

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses inaccurate or drifting weight readings due to mattress placement, patient movement, accessories, or environmental factors before assuming internal scale failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the patient is safely transferred to another bed or fully supported. Never troubleshoot scale functions with a patient on the bed.

Check Mattress Positioning

Ensure the mattress is correctly seated and aligned on the bed frame. Misalignment can trigger inaccurate readings. Expect proper mattress fit to stabilize weight display.

Remove Accessories or Bed Items

Detach sheets, pads, or monitoring equipment that may press unevenly on sensors. Expect readings to normalize without extra pressure on the scale.

Inspect Power and Connections

Verify the bed is connected to a reliable power source or fully charged battery. Inspect cables to the scale interface for secure connections. Loose or intermittent power can cause drift.

Observe Bed on Level Surface

Confirm the bed is on a flat, stable floor. Uneven floors can affect load cells. Expect consistent readings when the bed is level.

Reset Scale Function

If the bed provides a scale reset or zero function, perform this with the mattress removed or empty. Expect zeroed reading as confirmation of proper reset.

Check Indicator Lights or Alarms

Verify any scale-related error lights or alarms on the bed control panel. Persistent warnings may indicate a deeper issue.

If the Problem Persists

External factors have been ruled out. The issue may involve the load cells or internal electronics. The bed should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Always transfer patients to a safe location before testing bed scales. Do not rely on potentially inaccurate readings for clinical decisions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported inconsistent weight readings on the Multicare bed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Mattress misalignment and residual bedding caused scale drift."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Realigned mattress, removed excess bedding, and reset scale function; readings stabilized."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed stable
  • Cables inspected and secure
  • No unusual sounds or heat
  • Scale zeroed successfully
  • Bed level confirmed

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount when troubleshooting bed scales. Follow a stepwise approach, check all external factors first, and escalate appropriately. Document each action to support future maintenance and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
