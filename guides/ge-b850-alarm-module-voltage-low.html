GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B850

Alarm: Module Voltage Low

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B850

What This Guide Helps With

yAlarm: Module Voltage Low causing module failure, loss of monitoring parameters, or intermittent data acquisition due to unstable internal or supply voltage conditions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use if parameters are lost or unreliable
  • Transfer monitoring to a backup device
  • Ensure continuous patient monitoring is maintained

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the “Module Voltage Low” alarm is displayed
  • Power on the monitor and observe when the alarm occurs (startup vs during operation)
  • Note which modules are affected (ECG, SpO₂, IBP, etc.)

Check Power Source and External Supply

  • Verify the monitor is plugged into a known-good outlet
  • Test the outlet using another device
  • Remove from power strip or UPS and plug directly into wall outlet

Inspect Power Cord and AC Input Connection

  • Check for loose or damaged power cord
  • Ensure cord is fully seated in both the outlet and monitor
  • Gently move the cord to check for intermittent connection

Check Battery Condition and Operation

  • Disconnect AC power and confirm unit runs on battery
  • Check for battery alarms or failure indicators
  • Reconnect AC and observe charging behavior

Inspect Module Seating and Connections

  • Power off the monitor before inspection
  • Remove and reseat all parameter modules
  • Inspect for bent pins, debris, or poor contact

Remove Non-Essential Modules to Isolate Issue

  • Power on monitor with minimal modules installed
  • Add modules back one at a time
  • Identify if a specific module triggers the alarm

Perform Power Cycle

  • Shut down the monitor completely
  • Disconnect AC power and remove battery (if applicable)
  • Wait 30 seconds and restart the device

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after verifying power source, connections, battery, and module isolation, internal power supply or voltage regulation circuits are likely failing.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label “Out of Service”
  • Send for bench evaluation or repair

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup monitor before addressing voltage-related alarms to avoid loss of critical monitoring data.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor displaying 'Module Voltage Low' alarm with intermittent loss of patient parameters."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Voltage instability observed after verifying external power, likely due to faulty module or internal power regulation issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair and internal evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Voltage-related alarms are critical indicators of system instability. Effective troubleshooting requires ruling out external causes quickly and escalating appropriately when internal faults are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
