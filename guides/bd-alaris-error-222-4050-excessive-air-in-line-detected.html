BD Alaris

Error: 222.4050 Excessive air-in-line detected

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 222.4050 indicating excessive air-in-line, stopping infusion due to detected or falsely detected air in the IV tubing.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the infusion immediately
  • Clamp the IV line and assess the patient
  • Transfer infusion to another pump if therapy must continue

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error 222.4050 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup, priming, or mid-infusion)
  • Determine if infusion stops completely or intermittently

Inspect IV Fluid Source

  • Ensure IV bag is not empty or near empty
  • Verify proper venting if required
  • Check for collapsed bags that may introduce air

Inspect IV Tubing for Air Bubbles

  • Visually inspect tubing from bag to patient
  • Check for air bubbles or gaps in fluid
  • Inspect connections for looseness or damage
  • Re-prime or replace tubing if air is present

Verify Proper Tubing Installation in Pump

  • Ensure tubing is correctly seated in the air-in-line sensor channel
  • Check for kinks, twists, or pinching
  • Confirm use of BD-approved administration set

Check Air-in-Line Sensor Area

  • Inspect for fluid residue, moisture, or debris
  • Clean per approved procedure if contamination is present
  • Allow sensor area to fully dry before retesting

Test with Known Good Setup

  • Install new tubing and fluid source
  • Prime properly and run a test infusion
  • Monitor for recurrence of the error

Stop if Resolved

  • If error clears, return pump to service
  • Monitor briefly to confirm stable operation

If the Problem Persists

If Error 222.4050 continues after all external causes have been ruled out, this indicates a likely failure of the air-in-line detection system or safety circuitry.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before performing troubleshooting steps.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"BD Alaris pump displaying Error 222.4050, infusion stopping during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Air present in tubing or improperly seated set; possible air-in-line sensor issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed air, replaced administration set, retested. Removed from service and sent for repair when issue persisted."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Air-in-line alarms are critical safety protections. Always verify actual air presence first, but escalate quickly if the system falsely detects air to prevent delays in therapy.

That is successful troubleshooting.
