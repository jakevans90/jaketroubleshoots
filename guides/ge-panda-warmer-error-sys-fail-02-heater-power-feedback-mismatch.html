GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 02 Heater Power Feedback Mismatch

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 02 when heater power feedback does not match commanded output, creating unreliable warming operation. Because the warmer cannot be trusted to deliver the intended thermal support while this error is active, the device must be evaluated away from patient use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If Sys Fail 02 occurs while the warmer is supporting an infant, notify clinical staff immediately and transfer the infant to a known-working warming device according to clinical procedure.
  • Do not continue relying on the affected Panda Warmer for patient warming until the heater power fault is resolved and proper operation is verified.
  • Expected outcome: The infant is safely supported by alternate equipment before troubleshooting begins.
  • Why it matters: A heater power control mismatch can prevent reliable thermal support and may create risk of inadequate or unintended heating.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, confirm that Sys Fail 02 is displayed.
  • Observe and document whether the error occurs at startup, when heater output is commanded, during warm-up, or intermittently during operation.
  • Note associated behavior such as the heater not producing expected warmth, heater output stopping, the error returning after restart, unusual odor, visible damage, excessive heat, or abnormal sounds.
  • Expected outcome: The error is reproduced or clearly confirmed, with associated heater behavior documented.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a system-reported heater power mismatch rather than an operating or setup concern.

3. Inspect External Condition and Basic Setup

  • Disconnect the warmer from patient use and visually inspect the heater assembly, power cord, plug, and accessible exterior surfaces.
  • Check for a damaged power cord or plug, signs of liquid intrusion or contamination, cracked or visibly damaged heater components, burn marks, unusual odor, evidence of overheating, or obstructions around the heat engine area.
  • Connect the unit only to a verified appropriate power source before continuing evaluation.
  • Expected outcome: No external damage, contamination, or power-source issue is found that would make continued testing unsafe.
  • Why it matters: Visible damage or overheating evidence requires immediate removal from service regardless of further testing results.

4. Confirm the Error Is Not Cleared by a Safe Power Restart

  • After verifying the unit is not connected to a patient and no unsafe physical condition is present, perform a normal power restart.
  • Observe whether Sys Fail 02 clears and does not return during basic operation, or returns immediately or when heater output is requested.
  • Expected outcome: A persistent Sys Fail 02 remains present or reappears when the heater is commanded.
  • Why it matters: Recurrence indicates the Control PCA is still detecting a mismatch between commanded heater power and reported heater power.

5. Run the Heat Engine Health Check in Service Mode

  • Following approved Clinical Engineering access and service procedures, enter Service Mode and run the Heat Engine Health Check.
  • Document whether the test passes without recurrence of Sys Fail 02, fails, reports abnormal heater performance, or causes Sys Fail 02 to return.
  • Expected outcome: The Health Check either confirms normal heater control or supports an active heater power-control failure.
  • Why it matters: This is the manufacturer-directed functional check for isolating the reported heater power mismatch.

6. Check Accessible Connections Between the Control PCA and Power PCA

  • With the warmer removed from service and appropriately powered down before connection inspection, check the connections between the Control PCA and the Power PCA.
  • Inspect for loose or partially seated connectors, pin damage, discoloration or evidence of overheating, damaged wiring or harness strain, and contamination affecting connection integrity.
  • Reseat accessible connectors only according to approved service practices, then repeat the Heat Engine Health Check.
  • Expected outcome: Connections are secure and undamaged, and the error either clears after correcting a connection issue or remains present.
  • Why it matters: A poor connection can cause inaccurate communication or power feedback between the control and power circuitry.

7. Check Connections Between the Power PCA and the Heat Engine

  • Inspect the accessible connections from the Power PCA to the heat engine for looseness, damage, discoloration, contamination, or overheating evidence.
  • After correcting any safely addressable connection concern, run the Heat Engine Health Check again and confirm whether Sys Fail 02 returns.
  • Expected outcome: Heater-related connections are secure and intact; a continued error indicates the issue is not caused by an accessible connection problem.
  • Why it matters: The Power PCA must deliver and report heater output accurately through the heat engine connection path.

8. Determine Whether the Issue Has Been Resolved

  • If a loose or damaged connection was corrected, the Heat Engine Health Check passes, and Sys Fail 02 does not return during verification testing, document the correction and complete required return-to-service checks.
  • Return the warmer to clinical service only after normal heater operation and alarm/error-free function are verified according to facility procedures.
  • Expected outcome: The warmer operates normally without Sys Fail 02.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 02 remains after external inspection, safe restart evaluation, accessible connection checks, and the Heat Engine Health Check, common externally correctable causes have been ruled out.

A persistent error indicates a failure in the heater power-control path, with the Power PCA identified as the likely repair focus based on the reported fault and manufacturer troubleshooting direction.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the warmer for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Evaluate for Power PCA replacement according to approved service procedures.

Do not return the warmer to clinical use with an unresolved heater power mismatch.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the infant to a backup warming device first, because an unresolved Sys Fail 02 means heater output cannot be trusted.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 02 during operation. Reported heater power did not match commanded heater power, making warming performance unreliable."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Heat Engine Health Check failed and Sys Fail 02 persisted after verifying the power source and inspecting accessible connections between the Control PCA, Power PCA, and heat engine. Findings indicate likely Power PCA failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from clinical service, labeled unit Out of Service, documented failed heater power verification, and routed unit for bench repair and Power PCA evaluation/replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Sys Fail 02 should be treated as a heater reliability failure until proven otherwise. Protect the patient first, verify the reported condition, rule out accessible connection-related causes, perform the specified service check, and escalate appropriately when the error persists. Accurate CCR documentation supports safe repair decisions and a clear device history.

That is successful troubleshooting.
