GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 11 [XX 00 00 00] or [FF FF FF FF] Heat Control Processor Communication Failure

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 11 [XX 00 00 00] or [FF FF FF FF] when communication between the Heat Control Processor and Main Application Processor is lost, causing unreliable or unavailable infant warmer heat control.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the Panda Warmer is supporting an infant or is prepared for immediate clinical use, notify clinical staff and transfer care to a known-working warming device before troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on an infant warmer displaying Sys Fail 11 because communication required for dependable heat-control operation has been lost.
  • Remove the affected warmer from patient use until the failure is fully resolved and verified.

Expected outcome: The infant is safely supported using alternate equipment, and the affected warmer is available for evaluation without clinical risk.

Why it matters: An unresolved processor communication failure may prevent dependable heat-control operation or confirmation of safe warming performance.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, power the unit on and confirm the exact displayed message: Sys Fail 11 [XX 00 00 00] or Sys Fail 11 [FF FF FF FF].
  • Document whether the error appears immediately at startup, during normal operation, intermittently after warming begins, or following movement, cleaning, or power interruption.
  • For the [XX 00 00 00] format, document the displayed XX value when observed because it represents the number of seconds since the last communication between processors.
  • Observe for inability to enter normal warming operation, abnormal heater indications, repeated system failure messages, or loss of usable controls.

Expected outcome: The exact Sys Fail 11 condition is reproduced or clearly documented.

Why it matters: Confirming the displayed format and timing distinguishes a true processor communication fault from an incorrect complaint or unrelated power issue.

3. Check Power Source and Basic External Conditions

  • Confirm the warmer is connected to a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Verify the power cord is fully inserted and free of visible damage.
  • Inspect for a loose power connection, damaged plug or cord, signs of overheating, odor, discoloration, or fluid contamination.
  • Look for evidence of recent impact or improper cleaning-fluid intrusion around the control area.
  • Reapply power only after confirming there is no visible safety concern.

Expected outcome: The warmer receives stable external power with no obvious physical or environmental cause for the failure.

Why it matters: Power interruption, contamination, or damage may contribute to electronics faults and should be ruled out before internal service evaluation.

4. Confirm the Failure Is Not Cleared by an Approved Power Reset

  • With the warmer out of patient use, power the unit off, allow it to fully shut down, then restart it according to normal facility practice.
  • Observe whether Sys Fail 11 clears and normal operation returns, reappears immediately, or returns intermittently during operation.
  • Do not return the warmer to service solely because the message briefly clears.
  • Continue with operational verification before determining final device status.

Expected outcome: A transient condition is either cleared and verified, or the communication failure remains present.

Why it matters: A persistent or repeating Sys Fail 11 indicates more than a temporary startup interruption.

5. Inspect Accessible External Control-System Indicators and Condition

  • Without opening the device, inspect for blank, frozen, or abnormal display behavior.
  • Check for unresponsive controls or unexpected heater indicator behavior.
  • Inspect for loose external panels, visible damage, moisture, or contamination near the control area.
  • Document any abnormal observations that support the communication-failure complaint.

Expected outcome: External symptoms are identified or ruled out before bench-level isolation.

Why it matters: Visible control-panel abnormalities support escalation for internal processor, connection, or power-board evaluation.

6. Determine Whether the Error Is Persistent

  • If Sys Fail 11 remains after confirming proper external power, completing an approved restart, and ruling out obvious damage or contamination, classify the issue as a persistent device-related fault.
  • The reported condition indicates loss of communication between the Heat Control Processor and Main Application Processor.
  • Do not attempt to correct this condition through clinical setup changes or accessory replacement.

Expected outcome: The fault is classified as device-related rather than an external setup or power-source issue.

Why it matters: This error is associated with internal processor communication and requires controlled service evaluation when external checks do not resolve it.

7. Escalate for Controlled Bench Evaluation

  • Remove the warmer from service and proceed according to facility policy and manufacturer service documentation.
  • For qualified bench evaluation, inspect the seating and condition of the ribbon cable between the Power PCA and Control PCA.
  • The documented 20-pin ribbon cable is part number 6600-1411-601.
  • If the interconnect is correctly seated and the communication failure persists, evaluate the Control PCA as indicated by service documentation.
  • Evaluate the Power PCA when the required documented +5 V DC supply is not available.
  • Do not perform internal board-level evaluation while the warmer is available for patient use or outside authorized service procedures.

Expected outcome: The warmer is safely routed for authorized internal inspection, repair, and full post-repair verification.

Why it matters: Persistent Sys Fail 11 strongly indicates an internal cable, Control PCA, or Power PCA-related fault requiring bench service.

8. Stop When Resolved

  • If the error clears only after an identified corrective action and the warmer successfully completes required operational and safety verification, document the repair and return the device to service according to department policy.
  • Do not return the warmer to clinical use if Sys Fail 11 remains present or returns intermittently.
  • Do not return the warmer to service if heater-control operation cannot be verified or an internal connection or PCA concern remains unresolved.

Expected outcome: Only a verified, fully functional warmer is returned to patient care.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 11 [XX 00 00 00] or [FF FF FF FF] remains after external power, physical-condition, and approved restart checks have been completed, common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent error strongly indicates an internal communication failure involving the processor interconnect, Control PCA, or Power PCA supply path.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the device for authorized repair or bench evaluation.
  • Complete required functional and electrical verification before return to patient use.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. An infant warmer with unresolved heat-control processor communication errors should not be relied upon for patient warming.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an infant warmer while an infant is dependent on it for thermal support. Move the patient to a known-working backup warming device first, then evaluate the failed warmer away from active patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 11 [XX 00 00 00], indicating loss of communication between the Heat Control Processor and Main Application Processor. Warmer removed from patient use due to unresolved heat-control reliability concern."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed recurring Sys Fail 11 after testing with a known-good outlet and completing an approved restart. No external damage, power-cord defect, or contamination identified. Fault is consistent with an internal processor communication, ribbon-cable, Control PCA, or Power PCA supply-path failure requiring bench evaluation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed Panda Warmer from service, applied Out of Service labeling, and routed unit for authorized internal inspection and repair. Device will not be returned to clinical use until the error is corrected and required operational and safety verification is completed."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Sys Fail 11 indicates a failure in communication required for dependable warmer heat control. Patient safety requires removing the warmer from use, ruling out simple external concerns first, and escalating persistent faults for authorized internal repair. Clear CCR documentation preserves the displayed fault, supports efficient repair, and confirms why the device was not returned to service prematurely.

That is successful troubleshooting.
