GE Healthcare Giraffe OmniBed Carestation

Temperature / Humidity Control or Calibration Failures

Asset Type

Infant Incubator

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Giraffe OmniBed Carestation

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting temperature or humidity control failures and calibration errors on the Giraffe OmniBed Carestation. Symptoms may include incorrect incubator temperature, unstable humidity readings, or alarms indicating calibration issues. The focus is on external and easily verifiable factors before assuming internal sensor or system failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Supply

  • Confirm the incubator is plugged into a properly grounded outlet.
  • Test the outlet with a known working device or outlet tester.
  • Check that the power cord is intact and fully seated.

Why: Unstable or insufficient power can cause temperature and humidity fluctuations.

Inspect Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure incubator is not near air vents, open windows, or heating devices.
  • Confirm room temperature is within manufacturer-specified range.

Why: External drafts or temperature extremes can affect readings.

Check Canopy and Door Positioning

  • Ensure the canopy and porthole doors are fully closed and latched.
  • Look for obstructions preventing a proper seal.

Why: Gaps can cause heat or humidity loss, leading to control errors.

Review Settings and Alarm Indicators

  • Verify the target temperature and humidity settings are correct.
  • Check for active alarms related to sensors or calibration.

Why: Misconfigured setpoints or alarms may mimic sensor failure.

Inspect Humidity Reservoir / Water Level

  • Ensure water reservoir is filled to proper level with distilled water.
  • Check for blockages in water delivery lines or vapor paths.

Why: Insufficient or blocked water supply prevents proper humidity control.

Swap or Inspect External Sensors

  • If available, temporarily replace temperature/humidity probe with a verified working sensor.
  • Inspect sensor connections for loose or corroded contacts.

Why: External sensors can fail or read incorrectly without internal repair.

Run Built-in Calibration / Self-test

  • Follow the user interface instructions for temperature/humidity calibration.
  • Document any calibration errors or deviations.

Why: Built-in diagnostics help distinguish between sensor drift and mechanical failure.

If the Problem Persists

After performing all external checks and calibration attempts, if temperature or humidity control remains unstable:

  • Remove the incubator from patient use.
  • Label Out of Service clearly.
  • Escalate to manufacturer service or biomedical repair bench evaluation.

Note: Continuing to operate a device with unreliable thermal/humidity control risks patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot temperature or humidity issues with a patient in the incubator.

Transfer the infant to another verified incubator before performing tests or calibration.

Maintain safe environmental conditions during troubleshooting to avoid hypothermia or hyperthermia.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Nurse reports incubator temperature fluctuates and humidity alarms trigger intermittently.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed canopy not fully sealed; water reservoir partially empty; sensor wiring slightly loose.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Repositioned canopy, refilled water reservoir, re-seated sensor connections, ran calibration self-test; monitor readings normalized.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Water reservoir level confirmed
  • Canopy and porthole latches checked
  • Sensor connections inspected
  • Alarm history and any calibration error codes noted
  • Final device status: operating normally or removed from service

Final Thought

Patient safety and precise environmental control are critical in neonatal care. Systematic troubleshooting—from power and setup checks to calibration—ensures logical resolution and prevents unnecessary internal repair. Accurate documentation reinforces accountability and safe clinical practice.

That is successful troubleshooting.
