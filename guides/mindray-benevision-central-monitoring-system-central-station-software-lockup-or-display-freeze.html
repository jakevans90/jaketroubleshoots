Mindray BeneVision Central Monitoring System

Central station software lockup or display freeze

Asset Type

Central Monitoring Station

Manufacturer

Mindray

Model

BeneVision Central Monitoring System

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting BeneVision central station lockups, frozen displays, delayed response, or nonresponsive monitoring screens caused by external or workstation-level issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

A frozen central station may delay alarm recognition or prevent staff from viewing active patient data.

Action:

  • Confirm bedside monitors remain active and alarming locally.
  • Notify clinical staff that the central station display may not be reliable.
  • Have staff monitor affected patients directly at the bedside or from another functioning central station if available.
  • Do not assume patients are unmonitored just because the central station is frozen.

Expected outcome: Patient monitoring continues through bedside devices or another verified monitoring location before troubleshooting begins.

Why it matters: The central station is a secondary viewing and alarm management point. Patient safety depends on confirming the bedside monitors are still functioning.

Verify the Reported Freeze

Confirm whether the entire workstation is frozen or only the BeneVision application is locked.

Action:

  • Observe the screen.
  • Check whether patient waveforms are moving.
  • Check whether the mouse cursor moves.
  • Check whether keyboard input works.
  • Ask staff when the freeze began and whether alarms were still audible.

Expected outcome: You determine whether the issue is application-specific, display-related, network-related, or full workstation lockup.

If the screen begins responding normally, continue to observe briefly and document the reported issue.

Check for Obvious Display or Input Problems

A “frozen” central station may actually be a display, mouse, keyboard, or KVM issue.

Action:

  • Verify the monitor is powered on.
  • Check the video cable connection at the monitor and workstation.
  • Confirm the mouse and keyboard are connected.
  • If a KVM, extender, or wall-mounted display setup is used, check those connections.
  • Move the mouse and press a harmless key such as Shift or Ctrl.

Expected outcome: The display and input devices respond normally, or a loose connection is found and corrected.

If reconnecting a loose display, mouse, keyboard, or KVM cable resolves the issue, verify normal central station operation and stop.

Confirm the Workstation Has Power and Is Not in Sleep Mode

Some central station workstations may appear frozen if the display sleeps, the PC loses power, or the screen does not wake properly.

Action:

  • Check the workstation power indicator.
  • Check the monitor power indicator.
  • Verify the workstation is connected to a reliable outlet or UPS.
  • Confirm the UPS is not alarming, overloaded, or running on battery unexpectedly.

Expected outcome: The workstation and display are confirmed to have stable power.

If power was interrupted, restore power and allow the system to recover according to site procedure.

Check Whether Other Central Stations Are Affected

Determine whether this is a single workstation issue or a broader monitoring system issue.

Action:

  • Ask clinical staff if other BeneVision central stations are functioning.
  • Check another central station, if available.
  • Confirm whether the same patients are visible elsewhere.
  • Determine whether bedside monitors are still communicating normally.

Expected outcome: You identify whether the issue is isolated to one central station or may involve the monitoring network, server, or system communication path.

If multiple central stations are affected, escalate sooner to Clinical Engineering, IT, and vendor support according to site policy.

Check Network Indicators and Physical Connections

A frozen or non-updating central station can be caused by network interruption.

Action:

  • Check the network cable at the workstation.
  • Check for link lights at the network port if visible.
  • Verify the cable is not damaged, pulled, pinched, or disconnected.
  • Do not move network cables between wall ports unless your site allows it and the ports are verified for the monitoring network.

Expected outcome: The central station has a secure physical network connection.

If a disconnected cable is found, reconnect it and verify that patient data resumes normally.

Look for Signs of System Resource Problems

A workstation may lock up due to high CPU usage, memory problems, full storage, or an overloaded application state.

Action:

  • If the mouse and keyboard still work, check whether the operating system responds.
  • Look for delayed clicks, spinning cursor, application “not responding” messages, or abnormal slowness.
  • Check whether other windows or background prompts are blocking the BeneVision display.
  • Do not close monitoring software unless permitted by site procedure and clinical staff are aware.

Expected outcome: You determine whether the application is slow but responsive, locked, or blocked by another system prompt.

If a nonclinical pop-up or display issue is blocking the screen, clear it only if appropriate and verify monitoring view returns.

Restart the Application Only if Safe and Approved

If only the BeneVision application is locked and patients are being safely monitored elsewhere, a controlled application restart may restore operation.

Action:

  • Confirm clinical staff are aware.
  • Confirm bedside monitors or alternate central monitoring views are available.
  • Follow site-approved procedure for restarting the central station application.
  • Do not perform an uncontrolled restart during an active patient safety event.

Expected outcome: The BeneVision application restarts and patient sectors, waveforms, alarms, and communication status return normally.

If the application restarts successfully, monitor briefly for recurrence and document the action taken.

Reboot the Workstation Only if Needed

If the entire workstation is frozen and approved local procedure allows it, a controlled reboot may be required.

Action:

  • Confirm patient monitoring is covered at the bedside or another central station.
  • Notify the unit before rebooting.
  • Restart the workstation using the safest available method.
  • Allow the central station software to fully reload.
  • Verify patient sectors, waveforms, alarm status, time synchronization, and communication after startup.

Expected outcome: The workstation returns to normal function and displays live patient data.

If the workstation freezes again after reboot, remove it from service and escalate.

Verify Full Recovery Before Returning to Use

After any restart or recovery, confirm the central station is truly functioning.

Action:

  • Verify live waveforms are moving.
  • Confirm patient sectors are populated correctly.
  • Check that alarms display and annunciate as expected.
  • Confirm the system clock appears correct.
  • Ask clinical staff to confirm the display looks normal.

Expected outcome: The central station is functioning normally and staff can rely on it again.

If live data, alarms, or patient sectors do not return normally, continue escalation.

If the Problem Persists

If the BeneVision Central Monitoring System continues to freeze, lock up, fail to update, or require repeated restarts, common external causes have been ruled out.

The central station should be:

  • Removed from clinical reliance if alternate monitoring is available.
  • Labeled Out of Service if the workstation is dedicated equipment.
  • Sent for repair, IT evaluation, or vendor support review.
  • Escalated for possible workstation hardware, software corruption, database, network, server, or monitoring system communication issue.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A central station that repeatedly freezes should not be treated as reliable until the cause is identified and corrected.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a frozen central station as though it is only a computer problem. First verify patient monitoring and alarm coverage. If the central station is not updating, clinical staff should rely on bedside monitors or another working central station until the affected workstation is verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Mindray BeneVision Central Monitoring System display was frozen and patient waveforms were not updating."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the central station workstation unresponsive while bedside monitors remained active and communicating locally."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified patient monitoring coverage, checked power, display, input, and network connections, performed an approved workstation restart, and confirmed live waveforms and alarm display returned."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet or UPS status checked
  • Monitor power and video cable status
  • Mouse and keyboard response
  • Whether the full workstation or only the application was frozen
  • Whether bedside monitors remained active
  • Whether other central stations were affected
  • Network cable/link light status
  • Any alarm display or annunciation issues
  • Whether a restart was required
  • Whether the issue returned after reboot
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A frozen central station should always be handled with patient safety first. Confirm bedside monitoring, rule out simple power, display, input, and network issues, then escalate if the workstation or software remains unreliable. Clear CCR documentation helps show whether the issue was isolated, recurring, or part of a larger monitoring system problem.

That is successful troubleshooting.
