Cardinal Health Kendall SCD 700 Series

Error: E9 Temperature Error

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error E9 Temperature Error causing device shutdown or no therapy due to internal temperature being too high or too low.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use if compression therapy has stopped
  • Apply an alternate SCD unit if needed
  • Ensure patient continues receiving DVT prophylaxis

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the unit and confirm Error: E9 Temperature Error is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup vs during operation)
  • Check if the device feels unusually hot or cold
  • Confirm whether therapy has stopped

Check for Overheating Conditions

  • Ensure the unit is not covered by blankets, bedding, or linens
  • Verify the fan intake/exhaust near the power cord is not blocked
  • Allow proper airflow around the device

Inspect Fan Vent and Physical Condition

  • Check for dust or debris blocking the fan vent
  • Ensure all ventilation openings are clear
  • Listen for fan operation during startup

Evaluate Environmental Temperature

  • Confirm the device is being used in a normal room temperature environment
  • Avoid placing near heaters, vents, or direct sunlight

Check for Low Temperature Condition

  • If the unit feels cold, allow it to warm to room temperature
  • Wait 30–60 minutes before powering on

Perform Manual Reset

  • Power cycle the device after correcting environmental conditions
  • Confirm whether the error clears on restart

If the Problem Persists

If the E9 Temperature Error continues after airflow, environment, and reset checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Likely internal issue such as faulty temperature sensor, fan failure, or control board issue
  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot temperature-related errors on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before beginning troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying E9 Temperature Error and not providing compression therapy."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Vent blocked by bedding causing overheating condition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleared obstruction, allowed unit to cool, performed power cycle, verified normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Temperature errors are protective safeguards. Always check airflow and environment first, and escalate to service if the condition persists.

That is successful troubleshooting.
