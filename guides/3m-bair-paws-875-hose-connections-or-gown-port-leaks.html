3M Bair Paws 875 Series

Hose Connection or Gown Port Leaks

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Paws 875 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a 3M Bair Paws 875 patient warming unit when air leaks are suspected at hose connections, gown ports, or blanket interfaces. Leaks reduce the effectiveness of warming, may trigger alarms, and can cause patient discomfort. The guide emphasizes external checks first and avoids any internal board-level interventions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Power and Initial Setup

  • Verify the unit powers on correctly.
  • Ensure the unit has been set to an appropriate temperature and airflow mode.
    Why: Confirms the device is operational and delivering air.

Inspect Hose Connections

  • Disconnect and reconnect the hose to both the unit and patient warming blanket.
  • Check for loose fittings or cracked connectors.
  • Confirm that the hose locks into place properly.
    Why: Loose or damaged connections are the most common cause of leaks.

Examine Blanket/Gown Port Interfaces

  • Check where the hose enters the warming blanket or gown.
  • Ensure ports are fully seated and not torn, stretched, or blocked.
    Why: Air leaks often occur at these junctions, reducing patient warming effectiveness.

Check Hose Integrity

  • Inspect the hose for pinholes, kinks, or punctures along its length.
  • Flex the hose while the unit is running to see if airflow changes.
    Why: Damaged hoses can leak air intermittently.

Run an Airflow Test

  • Remove the blanket from the patient and connect to the unit.
  • Place your hand over ports and hose ends to feel for leaks.
  • Listen for hissing sounds indicating escaping air.
    Why: Confirms the leak location without exposing the patient.

Replace Suspect Components

  • Swap out damaged hoses or blankets if leaks are confirmed.
  • Use only manufacturer-approved replacements.
    Why: Ensures device integrity and patient safety.

If the Problem Persists

If leaks remain after inspecting and replacing hoses or blankets:

  • Remove the unit from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation by qualified personnel.
  • Avoid attempting internal repairs beyond hose or blanket replacement.
  • Knowing when to escalate is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot while the patient is actively using the unit.
  • Move the patient to an alternative warming device before testing.
  • Ensure patient safety by confirming airflow and warming effectiveness after any component replacement.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Hose connection at the blanket port leaks air, reducing warming efficiency.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed loose hose fittings and minor tears at the gown port interface during inspection.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Reconnected hose securely, replaced damaged blanket port, confirmed airflow restored and leak eliminated.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes, verified unit powers on.
  • Hose swapped: Yes, replaced with spare and retested.
  • Alarm behavior: None or minor airflow alerts.
  • Indicator lights: Normal operation.
  • Unusual sounds: Hissing detected at leak points.
  • Final device status: Fully operational after hose/blanket correction.

Final Thought

Patient safety is the priority: leaks reduce warming efficiency and can affect outcomes. Using logical troubleshooting steps—starting with external hoses and ports—prevents unnecessary internal repairs and ensures quick resolution. Proper CCR documentation captures actions and reinforces accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
