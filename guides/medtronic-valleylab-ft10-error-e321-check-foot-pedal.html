Medtronic Valleylab FT10

Error: E321 Check Foot Pedal

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Valleylab FT10

What This Guide Helps With

Error E321 Check Foot Pedal indicates that the Valleylab FT10 is detecting the Mono 1 foot pedal input as active. This may cause a medium-priority ESU alarm, activation lockout, or concern for unintended monopolar activation if the pedal, cable, connector, or foot pedal input circuit is faulty.

This guide helps Clinical Engineering and trained support personnel safely isolate external causes first, including a stuck pedal, damaged foot pedal cable, connector contamination, incorrect setup, or failed accessory, before escalating the ESU for internal repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Valleylab FT10 from patient use if Error E321 appears during setup, before activation, or during a procedure.
  • Stop use of the ESU until the activation input concern is resolved.
  • Confirm that no monopolar output is being activated unintentionally.
  • Use another properly functioning ESU if the procedure is active or urgent.
  • Do not continue using the unit if the foot pedal input appears stuck, unreliable, or falsely detected.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on and observe the display.
  • Confirm the exact message is Error: E321 Check Foot Pedal.
  • Identify whether the error appears at startup, when connecting the foot pedal, when selecting monopolar mode, during attempted activation, or intermittently during the case.
  • Confirm whether the issue is associated with the Mono 1 foot pedal input.
  • Ask clinical staff whether the pedal was being pressed, moved, stepped on accidentally, or exposed to fluid during use.

Release and Inspect the Foot Pedal

  • Make sure no one is pressing the foot pedal.
  • Remove anything resting on or near the pedal.
  • Press and release the pedal several times.
  • Check that the pedal returns normally and does not feel sticky, collapsed, jammed, or mechanically stuck.
  • Inspect for fluid, debris, tape, broken plastic, or signs of OR contamination.

Disconnect the Foot Pedal

  • Turn the ESU off.
  • Disconnect the Mono 1 foot pedal from the Valleylab FT10.
  • Power the unit back on without the foot pedal connected.
  • Observe whether Error E321 returns.
  • If the error clears with the pedal disconnected, suspect the foot pedal, pedal cable, or connector before suspecting the ESU.

Inspect the Foot Pedal Cable and Connector

  • Check the foot pedal cable for cuts, crushed sections, stretched areas, exposed conductors, or fluid contamination.
  • Inspect the connector pins and shell for bent pins, corrosion, debris, fluid residue, or damage.
  • Confirm the connector seats correctly and is not loose or partially inserted.
  • Gently flex the cable near strain relief points while watching for intermittent error behavior.
  • Remove the foot pedal from service if the cable, connector, or pedal housing is damaged.

Try a Known-Good Compatible Foot Pedal

  • Connect a known-good compatible Mono 1 foot pedal to the same foot pedal port.
  • Power the unit on and check whether Error E321 returns.
  • Confirm the unit does not detect activation unless the pedal is intentionally pressed.
  • If the known-good pedal works normally, replace the original foot pedal and document the failed accessory.

Check Whether the Error Follows the Foot Pedal

  • If allowed by shop procedure, test the suspect foot pedal on another compatible FT10 or approved test setup.
  • Do not use the suspect foot pedal clinically.
  • Observe whether the same stuck-pedal behavior follows the accessory.
  • If the error follows the foot pedal, remove the foot pedal from service and replace it.

Inspect the FT10 Foot Pedal Port

  • With the ESU powered off, inspect the Mono 1 foot pedal port.
  • Look for bent contacts, recessed pins, damaged connector housing, contamination, fluid residue, or impact damage.
  • Confirm the port does not feel loose or pushed into the chassis.
  • Do not continue using the ESU if the port is damaged or contaminated.

Reboot and Recheck After Accessory Isolation

  • After removing the suspect pedal or connecting a known-good pedal, power cycle the FT10.
  • Allow the unit to complete startup normally.
  • Confirm that Error E321 does not return.
  • Verify that the unit only detects foot pedal input when the pedal is intentionally pressed.
  • Do not return the unit to service if the error returns intermittently or appears without an external cause.

Determine Whether the Issue Is Accessory-Related or Device-Related

  • Accessory-related causes are likely if E321 clears when the foot pedal is disconnected, follows the suspect foot pedal, or resolves with a known-good compatible pedal.
  • Accessory-related causes are also likely if the pedal feels stuck, damaged, contaminated, or has a damaged cable or connector.
  • Device-related causes are likely if E321 remains with no foot pedal connected, occurs with multiple known-good foot pedals, appears at startup before any pedal is connected, or the Mono 1 foot pedal port is damaged.
  • Possible internal causes may involve the foot pedal input circuitry, including internal board-level faults that require bench evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If Error E321 continues after the foot pedal is released, disconnected, inspected, and replaced with a known-good compatible pedal, the common external causes have been ruled out.

Persistent E321 conditions may indicate an internal FT10 foot pedal input fault, damaged input connector, internal cable issue, Controller PCBA issue, Steering Relay PCBA issue, Foot Pedal PCBA issue, or related internal circuitry problem.

Remove the Valleylab FT10 from service.

Label it Out of Service.

Send the unit for bench evaluation, manufacturer repair, or vendor repair according to your facility process.

Do not return the ESU to patient use with an unresolved foot pedal input error.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a stuck or falsely detected foot pedal input while the ESU is connected to an active patient. Move the patient to a backup ESU or approved alternate device first. Any unresolved activation input error should be treated as a patient safety concern because electrosurgical output must only occur when intentionally commanded.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"User reported Medtronic Valleylab FT10 displaying Error E321 Check Foot Pedal during setup. Unit indicated Mono 1 foot pedal input was active and would not clear for normal ESU use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Mono 1 foot pedal input issue confirmed. Foot pedal was checked for stuck activation, cable damage, connector damage, contamination, and accessory failure. Error cleared with known-good compatible foot pedal, indicating defective original foot pedal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from patient use, inspected and disconnected suspect foot pedal, power cycled ESU, tested with known-good compatible foot pedal, and replaced defective foot pedal. Unit passed functional check and was returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

For Error E321 on the Medtronic Valleylab FT10, start with patient safety, then confirm whether the unit is detecting a Mono 1 foot pedal activation. Rule out a stuck pedal, damaged cable, bad connector, contamination, or failed foot pedal before suspecting an internal ESU fault. If the error persists with known-good accessories or with no foot pedal connected, remove the unit from service and escalate appropriately.

That is successful troubleshooting.
