GE Healthcare AMX Series

Unit will not power on or boots intermittently

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

AMX Series

What This Guide Helps With

Power-on failure, intermittent booting, charging issues, battery problems, outlet issues, or external power connection problems on GE AMX mobile X-ray units.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the mobile X-ray unit from clinical use if it will not power on reliably or shuts down during startup.

Action:

  • Move imaging needs to another available mobile X-ray system.
  • Do not attempt patient imaging with a unit that may lose power during positioning, exposure setup, or image acquisition.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues using a known-working imaging device.

Why it matters: A mobile X-ray unit that powers off unexpectedly may delay care, interrupt imaging, or create a positioning hazard near the patient.

2. Verify the Reported Power Issue

Confirm the actual symptom before assuming a battery or internal power failure.

Action:

  • Attempt to power the unit on in a safe area.
  • Observe whether the unit powers on normally.
  • Observe whether the unit does not respond at all.
  • Observe whether the unit starts booting and shuts down.
  • Observe whether the unit turns on only while plugged in.
  • Observe whether the unit displays battery or charging warnings.
  • Observe whether the unit has indicator lights but no screen activity.

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified and documented.

If the unit powers on normally and the issue cannot be duplicated, continue with charging and battery checks before returning it to service.

3. Check the Emergency Stop or Power Disable Condition

Some mobile X-ray systems may appear dead if an emergency stop, transport lock, or power disable condition is active.

Action:

  • Inspect the unit for any emergency stop switch, drive disable switch, key switch, or main power control in the wrong position.
  • Reset or return controls to the normal operating position if appropriate.

Expected outcome: If the unit was disabled by a switch or safety control, it should power on normally after reset.

If this resolves the issue, verify normal startup and document the cause.

4. Verify the AC Outlet

Rule out a bad wall outlet before troubleshooting the unit.

Action:

  • Plug a known-working device or outlet tester into the same outlet.
  • Confirm the outlet has proper power.
  • Avoid using damaged outlets, loose receptacles, extension cords, or power strips unless approved by facility policy.

Expected outcome: The outlet is confirmed functional and safe.

If the outlet is faulty, notify Facilities and move the unit to a verified outlet.

5. Inspect the Power Cord and Plug

A damaged power cord can prevent charging or cause intermittent booting.

Action:

  • Inspect the AC power cord for cuts, crushed insulation, bent prongs, loose strain relief, burn marks, or signs of overheating.
  • Check that the cord is fully seated at the unit and wall outlet.
  • Gently move the cord while observing charging or power indicators.

Expected outcome: The power cord is secure, undamaged, and not causing intermittent power loss.

If damage is found, remove the unit from service until the cord or cord assembly is repaired or replaced per facility procedure.

6. Confirm Charging Indicator Behavior

Determine whether the unit is receiving AC power and attempting to charge.

Action:

  • Plug the AMX unit into a verified outlet.
  • Observe charging lights, display messages, fans, relays, or other startup indicators.
  • Allow the unit to remain connected long enough to determine whether the battery begins accepting charge.

Expected outcome: The unit should show some indication that AC power is present or charging is active.

If there are no indicators at all with a known-good outlet and cord, suspect an internal power input, charger, battery, or power supply issue.

7. Allow Time for a Deeply Discharged Battery

If the unit has been unplugged for an extended period, the battery may be deeply discharged.

Action:

  • Leave the unit connected to a verified AC outlet.
  • After an appropriate charging period, attempt startup again.
  • Watch for changes in battery indicators, charger status, or display behavior.

Expected outcome: A deeply discharged unit may recover enough charge to boot after being connected to AC power.

If the unit still does not power on or repeatedly fails during startup, continue troubleshooting.

8. Check for Battery-Related Symptoms

Intermittent booting often points to low battery capacity, poor charging, or battery connection issues.

Action:

  • Determine whether the unit only boots while plugged in.
  • Determine whether the unit shuts down when unplugged.
  • Determine whether the unit shows low battery warnings.
  • Determine whether the unit dies during movement.
  • Determine whether the unit has reduced run time.
  • Determine whether the unit fails after sitting unplugged.
  • Determine whether the unit requires repeated startup attempts.

Expected outcome: Battery-related behavior is identified.

If the unit powers on only with AC power or cannot hold charge, remove it from service for battery and charging system evaluation.

9. Inspect External Battery Compartment Areas Without Disassembly

Do not perform deep internal disassembly during clinical troubleshooting, but inspect accessible areas for obvious issues.

Action:

  • Look for loose covers, missing screws, corrosion, liquid intrusion, impact damage, or signs that the unit was recently dropped or struck.
  • Check for unusual heat, smell, buzzing, clicking, or visible damage near the power entry or battery area.

Expected outcome: No obvious external damage, contamination, overheating, or electrical hazard is present.

If heat, burning smell, liquid damage, or arcing is suspected, stop troubleshooting and remove the unit from service immediately.

10. Perform a Controlled Restart

If the unit boots intermittently, perform a clean power cycle in a safe location.

Action:

  • Power the unit down fully if possible.
  • Disconnect from AC power briefly.
  • Reconnect to a verified outlet.
  • Attempt one normal startup.
  • Do not repeatedly force startup if the unit continues to fail.

Expected outcome: The unit either boots normally or the failure repeats clearly.

If the unit starts normally, verify charging status, battery condition, and basic function before considering return to service.

11. Check for Display Versus Power Failure

A blank screen does not always mean the entire unit has no power.

Action:

  • When attempting startup, observe for power indicator lights.
  • When attempting startup, observe for keypad lights.
  • When attempting startup, observe for fans.
  • When attempting startup, observe for drive or brake sounds.
  • When attempting startup, observe for computer boot sounds.
  • When attempting startup, observe for exposure console activity.
  • When attempting startup, observe for error tones or beeps.

Expected outcome: You can determine whether the unit is completely dead or powering internally with no display.

If indicators are active but the display remains blank, document as a display, computer, or boot/display path issue rather than a total power failure.

12. Review Recent History

Recent movement, battery replacement, charging complaints, or shutdowns may point to the likely cause.

Action:

  • Ask staff when the issue started.
  • Determine whether the unit was recently left unplugged.
  • Determine whether the unit was recently moved between departments.
  • Determine whether the unit was recently involved in a collision.
  • Determine whether the unit was recently cleaned heavily.
  • Determine whether the unit was recently exposed to liquid.
  • Determine whether the unit was recently reported for short battery life.
  • Determine whether the unit was recently serviced or transported.

Expected outcome: The work order includes useful context for repair evaluation.

13. Verify Basic Function Only If Startup Succeeds

If the unit powers on successfully, confirm safe basic operation before returning it to service.

Action:

  • Check that the system boots completely.
  • Confirm battery or charging status is acceptable.
  • Verify no active power, battery, drive, brake, generator, or system errors remain.
  • Confirm the unit remains powered on while idle and during basic movement checks.

Expected outcome: The unit demonstrates stable startup and no recurring power issue.

If the unit boots once but fails again, do not return it to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the AMX unit still will not power on, only boots intermittently, shuts down during startup, or shows abnormal charging behavior after external checks, common user-accessible causes have been ruled out.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Likely areas for repair evaluation may include the battery system, charger, power input circuit, internal power supply, control computer, wiring, display path, or main power distribution. Clinical Engineering should stop before deep disassembly unless trained, equipped, and authorized for GE AMX service.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A mobile X-ray system with unreliable power should not be returned to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot power instability near an active patient. Move imaging needs to another mobile X-ray unit first. A system that shuts down during positioning or imaging setup can delay care and create unnecessary risk in tight patient care spaces.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported that the GE AMX mobile X-ray unit would not power on and sometimes began booting before shutting off."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet and power cord were functional, but the unit showed intermittent startup and unreliable charging behavior."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for battery, charger, and internal power system evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result
  • Power cord condition
  • Whether charging indicators were present
  • Whether the unit powered on with AC connected
  • Whether the unit shut down when unplugged
  • Battery warning messages
  • Display behavior during startup
  • Indicator lights or fan activity
  • Any clicking, buzzing, heat, odor, or visible damage
  • Whether the issue was duplicated
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power problems on a mobile X-ray unit should be approached logically. Start with patient safety, then verify the outlet, power cord, charging status, battery behavior, and visible external condition. If the unit remains unreliable after these checks, escalation is the correct decision. Clear CCR documentation helps repair teams focus on the most likely failure area and prevents unsafe return to service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
