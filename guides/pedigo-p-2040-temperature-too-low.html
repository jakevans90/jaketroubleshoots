Pedigo P-2040

Temperature Too Low

Asset Type

Blanket Warmer

Manufacturer

Pedigo

Model

P-2040

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a Pedigo P-2040 blanket warmer that is not reaching set temperature or is warming blankets poorly.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not rely on blankets from a warmer that may not be maintaining the expected temperature.

Action:

  • Remove any questionable blankets from clinical use until temperature performance is verified.
  • Notify staff that the warmer may not be heating properly.
  • Use blankets from another approved warmer if patient warming is needed.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without relying on a blanket warmer with uncertain temperature performance.

Why it matters: Blankets that are too cool may not provide intended patient comfort or warming support, especially in perioperative, ED, or recovery areas.

Verify the Reported Temperature Issue

Confirm whether the unit is actually too cold or whether the complaint is based on user perception.

Action:

  • Check the displayed temperature, set temperature, and actual cabinet condition.
  • Ask staff when the issue was noticed:
  • After restocking?
  • After the door was left open?
  • After power loss?
  • After relocation?

Expected outcome: The issue is confirmed as either a true low-temperature condition or a normal recovery delay.

If the warmer is recovering normally after being loaded or opened frequently, allow time to stabilize and stop.

Check That the Unit Has Power

Verify the warmer is powered and not partially energized.

Action:

  • Confirm the power switch is on.
  • Check that the display or indicator lights are active.
  • Verify the power cord is fully seated and not damaged.
  • Test the outlet or plug the warmer into a known-good hospital-grade outlet if safe to do so.

Expected outcome: The unit powers normally and begins heating.

If power was the issue and the warmer heats normally afterward, return it to service after temperature verification and stop.

Check the Temperature Setting

Confirm the set temperature has not been lowered.

Action:

  • Review the current setpoint.
  • Compare it with the department’s approved blanket warming temperature range.
  • Do not raise the setpoint beyond facility policy or manufacturer recommendations.

Expected outcome: The warmer is set appropriately for clinical use.

If the setpoint was incorrect, correct it, allow the cabinet to stabilize, verify temperature, document the finding, and stop.

Check for Recent Loading or Overloading

A blanket warmer may read low or feel cool after being heavily restocked.

Action:

  • Ask whether cold blankets were recently added.
  • Check whether the cabinet is packed too tightly.
  • Make sure air can circulate around the contents.
  • Remove excess blankets if airflow is blocked.

Expected outcome: Air circulation improves and the unit begins recovering toward set temperature.

If overloading caused the issue, educate the department, allow recovery time, verify temperature, and stop.

Check Door Closure and Gasket Condition

A door that does not seal properly can prevent the cabinet from reaching temperature.

Action:

  • Confirm the door closes completely.
  • Inspect the gasket for gaps, tears, compression damage, or debris.
  • Check that blankets are not blocking the door from sealing.
  • Look for a loose handle, hinge issue, or misaligned door.

Expected outcome: The door seals fully and warm air stays inside the cabinet.

If the door was blocked or not fully closed, correct the issue, allow the warmer to recover, verify temperature, and stop.

Check Ambient Conditions and Placement

Poor placement can affect warming performance.

Action:

  • Confirm the warmer is not located directly under heavy airflow from HVAC vents.
  • Check that the unit is not against walls or objects that restrict ventilation.
  • Make sure the warmer is in a normal indoor clinical environment.

Expected outcome: The warmer has proper airflow and is not being cooled by the room environment.

If placement was the issue, relocate or correct the airflow concern, then verify temperature recovery.

Listen for Abnormal Operation

Check for signs that the heating system or internal air circulation may not be functioning normally.

Action:

  • Listen for unusual sounds, buzzing, clicking, or silence when the unit should be heating.
  • Check for unusual heat, burning smell, or electrical odor.
  • Do not continue troubleshooting if overheating, odor, smoke, or damaged wiring is present.

Expected outcome: No unsafe sounds, smells, or heat conditions are present.

If there is an electrical smell, unusual heat, smoke, or visible damage, remove the warmer from service immediately and send it for repair.

Verify Actual Cabinet Temperature

Do not rely only on touch.

Action:

  • Use a calibrated thermometer or approved temperature verification device.
  • Place it inside the cabinet according to your department’s normal temperature-check process.
  • Allow enough time for the reading to stabilize.
  • Compare the measured temperature to the display and setpoint.

Expected outcome: Actual cabinet temperature matches the expected range.

If the measured temperature is acceptable, explain findings to staff, document the verification, and return the warmer to service.

Check for Temperature Display Mismatch

The unit may be heating, but the display or sensing may be inaccurate.

Action:

  • Compare displayed temperature to the independent measured temperature.
  • Look for a large difference between the display and measured cabinet temperature.
  • Check whether the temperature continues rising, remains flat, or drops.

Expected outcome: Displayed and measured temperatures are reasonably consistent.

If the cabinet is warm but the display reads low, suspect a sensing, display, or control issue and escalate for bench evaluation.

Perform a Controlled Recovery Check

If no simple cause is found, observe whether the warmer can recover.

Action:

  • Close the door.
  • Leave the warmer powered on and undisturbed.
  • Allow reasonable stabilization time.
  • Recheck actual temperature.

Expected outcome: The warmer steadily rises toward set temperature and maintains temperature.

If the temperature does not rise, rises very slowly, or cannot maintain temperature, remove it from service.

If the Problem Persists

If power, outlet, settings, loading, door seal, placement, and basic temperature verification have all been checked, the issue is likely internal.

Possible internal causes may include:

  • Heating element failure
  • Temperature sensor issue
  • Control board problem
  • Thermostat or safety limit problem
  • Internal wiring or connection failure

At that point, the Pedigo P-2040 should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Do not continue adjusting settings or returning the warmer to clinical use without verified temperature performance. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a blanket warmer while clinical staff are actively depending on it for patient warming. Move staff to another approved source of warmed blankets first, then evaluate the warmer safely. Therapy continuity should be maintained before the warmer is tested.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported that the Pedigo P-2040 blanket warmer was not getting warm enough and blankets felt cool during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified the unit had power, but the cabinet temperature remained below the setpoint after door closure, loading, outlet, and placement checks."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the blanket warmer from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for bench evaluation for suspected heating or temperature control failure."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • Power cord condition
  • Displayed temperature
  • Set temperature
  • Independent measured cabinet temperature
  • Whether blankets were recently restocked
  • Whether the cabinet was overloaded
  • Door gasket condition
  • Door closure verified
  • Alarm or indicator behavior
  • Unusual sounds
  • Unusual heat or smell
  • Environmental factors such as HVAC airflow or restricted ventilation
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Temperature-too-low complaints should be handled logically. Start with safe patient care, then verify power, settings, loading, airflow, door seal, and actual temperature. If the warmer cannot recover or maintain temperature after external causes are ruled out, remove it from service and document the issue clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
