Medtronic Valleylab FT10

Touchscreen UI Freezing, Lag, or Calibration Issues

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Valleylab FT10

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a Valleylab FT10 ESU when the touchscreen is unresponsive, lags, or shows calibration problems. The focus is on verifying external and user-level issues first, before considering internal failure or service intervention.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Confirm the ESU is connected to a verified working outlet.
  • Check the power cord and plug for damage.
  • Why: Inconsistent power can cause UI lag or freezing.

Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Turn the ESU off, wait 30–60 seconds, and power it back on.
  • Why: This can clear temporary software or memory glitches causing touchscreen lag.

Check for Obstructions on the Touchscreen

  • Clean the screen with a soft, lint-free cloth and approved disinfectant.
  • Ensure no protective covers or moisture interfere with touch sensitivity.
  • Why: Dirt, liquids, or protective films can cause miscalibration or unresponsiveness.

Inspect User Settings

  • Check that touchscreen sensitivity settings are at recommended factory levels.
  • Ensure no locked functions or software restrictions are active.
  • Why: Misconfigured settings can mimic calibration issues.

Disconnect and Reconnect Peripherals

  • Remove instruments, footswitch, or other connected devices.
  • Reboot the system with only essential connections.
  • Why: External devices can interfere with UI performance.

Observe Software Behavior

  • Run a basic test procedure to see if UI freezing or lag occurs consistently.
  • Note if lag is during specific menu navigation or during energy delivery.
  • Why: Helps differentiate between software glitches and hardware failure.

Check for Software Updates or Error Logs

  • Verify if the ESU firmware is up to date according to Medtronic recommendations.
  • Review any system error logs for touchscreen or calibration faults.
  • Why: Known software bugs may cause freezing or calibration drift.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out.

Device may have internal touchscreen digitizer failure, mainboard issues, or software corruption.

Action:

  • Remove the ESU from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to authorized Medtronic service or internal bench evaluation.
  • Clinical Engineering must not attempt internal board-level repair.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt touchscreen troubleshooting while the device is in use on a patient.
  • Ensure patient procedures are moved to an alternative ESU before performing testing.
  • Confirm that staff are aware of the Out of Service status to prevent accidental use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Touchscreen freezes and lags during use; calibration seems off.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Cleaning and inspection performed; power cycle and peripheral check did not resolve issue; external connections verified.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Device labeled Out of Service and sent for Medtronic service evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet verified with tester
  • Power cord inspected
  • Peripheral devices disconnected
  • Screen cleaned and checked
  • Any error messages observed
  • Firmware version noted
  • Device left powered off in Out of Service status

Final Thought

Patient safety and logical troubleshooting are the top priorities. Address external factors first, escalate appropriately, and document all steps clearly. Proper CCR documentation ensures follow-up and compliance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
