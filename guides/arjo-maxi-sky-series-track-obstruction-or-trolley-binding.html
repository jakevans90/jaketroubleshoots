Arjo Maxi Sky Series

Track obstruction or trolley binding

Asset Type

Patient Lift

Manufacturer

Arjo

Model

Maxi Sky Series

What This Guide Helps With

Lift not moving smoothly along track due to obstruction, debris, misalignment, or trolley resistance affecting horizontal travel.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the lift from active patient use before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient safely transferred to another device.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Use the hand control to move the lift along the track. Observe movement.
  • Expected: Jerky movement, stopping, resistance, or complete lack of travel.
  • Why it matters: Confirms true mechanical restriction vs control or power issue.

Check Emergency Stop (E-Stop)

  • Ensure the E-stop is not engaged. Reset if necessary.
  • Expected: Full system power restored.
  • Why it matters: E-stop disables all powered movement, including track travel.

If movement returns → stop troubleshooting.

Inspect Track for Obstructions

  • Visually inspect the entire accessible track path. Look for debris, foreign objects, or buildup.
  • Expected: Track is clean and unobstructed.
  • Why it matters: Even small debris can prevent smooth trolley movement or cause binding.

If obstruction is found and removed → retest. If resolved, stop.

Check for Physical Damage or Misalignment

  • Inspect track sections for bends, dents, gaps at joints, or misalignment.
  • Expected: Track is straight, continuous, and properly aligned.
  • Why it matters: Misalignment can cause the trolley wheels to bind or stop.

If damage is found → remove from service and escalate.

Inspect Trolley Wheels and Movement

  • Observe the trolley as it moves (if accessible). Listen for grinding or resistance.
  • Expected: Smooth, quiet rolling movement.
  • Why it matters: Worn or seized wheels increase resistance and cause binding.

If abnormal noise or resistance is present → escalate.

Check for Load or Sling Interference

  • Ensure sling straps, patient attachments, or accessories are not dragging or catching on the track or motor unit.
  • Expected: Clear path with no interference.
  • Why it matters: External interference can mimic a track or trolley issue.

If interference is removed and movement returns → stop troubleshooting.

Verify Battery and Power Output

  • Check battery charge and seating. Swap with a known good battery if available.
  • Expected: Adequate power for smooth movement.
  • Why it matters: Low power can cause weak motor output, appearing as binding.

If movement improves → stop troubleshooting.

Check for Environmental Factors

  • Look for ceiling obstructions, rail-mounted accessories, or structural interference along the path.
  • Expected: Clear travel path across entire track.
  • Why it matters: External obstructions are often overlooked but common.

If cleared and movement returns → stop troubleshooting.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (trolley assembly, bearings, or drive system).

Remove the lift from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents further damage and ensures safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot while a patient is suspended. Always transfer the patient safely before evaluating lift movement issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Lift not moving along track and stopping intermittently during transport."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Debris found in ceiling track causing trolley binding."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed obstruction, cleaned track, and verified smooth operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Track inspected for debris or obstruction
  • Trolley movement observed
  • Abnormal noise (grinding, clicking) noted
  • Battery condition verified
  • Physical track condition (alignment/damage)
  • Final operational status of lift

Final Thought

Track-related issues are often caused by simple external factors like debris or obstruction. A structured approach ensures safe, efficient troubleshooting while avoiding unnecessary repairs. Always prioritize patient safety and proper escalation when mechanical resistance remains.

That is successful troubleshooting.
