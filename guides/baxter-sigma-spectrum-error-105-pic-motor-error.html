Baxter SIGMA Spectrum

Error: 105 PIC Motor Error

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Baxter

Model

SIGMA Spectrum

What This Guide Helps With

Error: 105 PIC Motor Error indicating a motor failure flag that can cause infusion stoppage, inability to start infusion, or inconsistent pumping performance.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use immediately.
  • Transfer infusion to a backup pump.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error: 105 PIC Motor Error is displayed.
  • Attempt to start an infusion if safe to test.
  • Observe for failure to start, immediate alarms, or intermittent stopping.

3. Check Power Source and Battery Condition

  • Ensure the pump is connected to AC power.
  • Verify battery charge level and charging indicators.

4. Inspect for Mechanical Obstruction

  • Open the pump door and inspect the pumping channel.
  • Check for kinked or improperly loaded tubing.
  • Look for debris or residue inside the channel.
  • Reload tubing using a known-good administration set.

5. Check Door Closure and Latch Integrity

  • Close the pump door firmly and ensure it latches completely.
  • Inspect for misalignment or damage to the latch.
  • Verify the door is fully seated with no gaps.

6. Power Cycle the Pump

  • Turn the pump off completely.
  • Disconnect from AC power briefly, then reconnect and power on.

7. Isolate Device vs Setup

  • Test with a different IV tubing set.
  • Try a different power outlet.
  • Compare with another SIGMA Spectrum pump if available.

If the Problem Persists

If Error: 105 PIC Motor Error continues after all checks, external causes have been ruled out and the motor failure flag indicates an internal fault.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a pump on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before evaluating motor-related errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error: 105 PIC Motor Error and unable to start infusion."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal motor failure flag present; external causes ruled out."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent for motor/control evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Motor-related errors directly impact infusion accuracy and patient safety. Always rule out external mechanical and setup causes first, then escalate promptly when internal faults are indicated.

That is successful troubleshooting.
