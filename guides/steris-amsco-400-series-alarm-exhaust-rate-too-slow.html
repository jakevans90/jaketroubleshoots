STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Exhaust Rate Too Slow – Liquid cycle exhaust occurring slower than expected cooling rate

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: Exhaust Rate Too Slow causing delayed chamber pressure release during liquid cycle and prolonged cooling and exhaust time, potentially impacting sterilization quality and load safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not use the sterilizer for processing loads until the alarm condition is resolved
  • If a cycle is in progress, allow it to complete safely if the unit is attempting recovery
  • Do not release loads from a questionable cycle

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm “Exhaust Rate Too Slow” is displayed
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (typically during liquid cycle exhaust phase)
  • Note extended exhaust time or slow pressure drop

Check Drain Line and Facility Drain Flow

  • Inspect drain line for kinks, restrictions, or blockage
  • Verify facility drain is functioning properly

Check for Condensate or Steam Backup

  • Observe for pooling condensate or slow drainage
  • Confirm normal steam discharge behavior

Confirm Correct Cycle Selection

  • Verify that a liquid cycle is being used for the load
  • Ensure settings match intended processing requirements

Evaluate Exhaust Solenoid Valves (S40 / S03)

  • Listen for proper actuation during exhaust phase
  • Check for delayed or inconsistent operation

Check Chamber Steam Trap Function

  • Look for signs of poor condensate removal
  • Identify inconsistent exhaust or pressure behavior

Assess Control and Calibration

  • Consider control drift if no mechanical issue is found
  • Verify system maintains proper exhaust rate automatically

If the Problem Persists

If external restrictions and simple causes have been ruled out:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Escalate for qualified repair and calibration

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or rely on a sterilizer during active patient use. Always move processing to a backup sterilizer before addressing alarm conditions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer displaying Exhaust Rate Too Slow alarm during liquid cycle with prolonged exhaust phase."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Restricted exhaust flow suspected due to partial blockage or malfunctioning solenoid valve."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified drain line, identified abnormal exhaust behavior, removed unit from service, and escalated for repair and calibration."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Exhaust-related alarms directly affect sterilization quality and safety. Always begin with external flow checks before moving to component evaluation. Escalate appropriately when system performance is compromised.

That is successful troubleshooting.
