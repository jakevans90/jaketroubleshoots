STERIS AMSCO 4085

Error: BATT DEF

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 4085

What This Guide Helps With

Error BATT DEF indicating battery voltage imbalance causing charging issues, reduced runtime, or failure to operate on battery power.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the surgical table from patient use if battery operation is required or unstable
  • Transfer patient to a safe surface if needed

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the table and confirm BATT DEF is displayed
  • Observe when the error appears (startup or during use)
  • Check if table works normally on AC power
  • Confirm battery operation is limited or unavailable

Confirm AC Power Operation

  • Plug into a known working outlet
  • Verify power indicator is illuminated
  • Operate table functions while connected

Inspect External Power and Charging Setup

  • Check power cord for damage or loose connections
  • Ensure plug is fully seated
  • Test with another outlet if needed

Check Battery Voltage Individually

  • Access battery compartment without deep disassembly
  • Measure voltage of each battery
  • Compare readings between batteries

Inspect Battery Condition and Connections

  • Check for corrosion, swelling, or leakage
  • Ensure terminals are clean and secure
  • Reseat loose connections

Isolate the Faulty Battery

  • Identify battery with lower or abnormal voltage
  • Replace defective battery (or both if aged)

Recheck System Operation

  • Power cycle the table
  • Confirm error is cleared
  • Test battery operation

If the Problem Persists

If external causes and battery replacement do not resolve the issue, this indicates a likely internal charging circuit or control board fault.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before addressing battery or power issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Table displays BATT DEF and will not operate on battery power."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Measured battery voltages showed imbalance greater than 0.6V between batteries."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced defective battery, verified balanced voltages, and confirmed normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery imbalance issues are often caused by aging components or poor connections. Always verify simple electrical conditions first and escalate only after external causes are ruled out.

That is successful troubleshooting.
