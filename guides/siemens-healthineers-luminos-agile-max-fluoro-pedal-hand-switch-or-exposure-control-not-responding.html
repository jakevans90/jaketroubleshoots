Siemens Healthineers Luminos Agile Max

Fluoro Pedal, Hand Switch, or Exposure Control Not Responding

Asset Type

Fluoroscopy System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

Luminos Agile Max

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting fluoro pedal, hand switch, exposure button, or X-ray control response issues using safe external checks first.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm whether a patient is positioned on the table or an exam is actively in progress.

If fluoro or exposure control is unreliable during an exam, stop troubleshooting and have clinical staff move the patient to another approved imaging workflow if needed.

Expected outcome: The patient is not dependent on a system with unreliable exposure control.

Confirm the Exact Control That Is Not Responding

Ask the technologist which control failed and when it failed.

Identify whether the issue involves:

  • Fluoro foot pedal not initiating fluoroscopy
  • Hand switch not initiating exposure
  • Exposure button not responding
  • Intermittent response from the control
  • Control works in one mode but not another
  • System shows X-ray inhibit, generator not ready, or exposure unavailable

Expected outcome: The affected control and failure condition are clearly identified before parts are suspected.

Check for System-Ready or X-ray Inhibit Messages

Review the operator display, acquisition workstation, and system status messages.

Look for messages related to:

  • X-ray inhibit
  • Door or room safety interlock
  • Tube or generator not ready
  • Detector not ready
  • Collimator or SID position
  • Table or tube stand position
  • Emergency stop active
  • Exposure mode not selected

Expected outcome: Any system condition preventing exposure is identified before assuming the pedal or hand switch is faulty.

If clearing the system-ready condition restores fluoro or exposure response, verify normal operation and stop.

Verify the Correct Exam Mode and Workflow State

Confirm the system is in a valid fluoro or radiographic acquisition workflow.

Make sure the exam is selected, patient/study information is active, and the system is not waiting for positioning, calibration, confirmation, or image processing.

Expected outcome: The control is being tested while the system is actually ready to accept an exposure command.

Inspect the Fluoro Pedal Externally

Visually inspect the foot pedal for damage, contamination, fluid intrusion, stuck buttons, cracked housing, loose cable strain relief, or crushed cable sections.

Press the pedal and confirm it returns normally.

Expected outcome: The pedal is physically intact, dry, unobstructed, and not mechanically stuck.

If the pedal is damaged, wet, or unsafe to use, remove it from service and escalate for replacement or repair.

Check the Foot Pedal Cable and Connection

Trace the fluoro pedal cable from the pedal to its connection point.

Confirm the connector is fully seated, not bent, not cross-threaded, and not under tension from table or room movement.

Expected outcome: The pedal connection is secure and the cable is not visibly damaged.

If reseating a loose external connector restores response, test several activations, document the finding, and stop.

Inspect the Hand Switch or Exposure Switch

Check the hand switch for cracked housing, damaged cord, sticky button action, bent connector pins, loose plug, or evidence of being dropped.

Confirm the switch is not trapped under accessories, table pads, drapes, or cable bundles.

Expected outcome: The hand switch is externally intact and able to actuate normally.

Confirm the Correct Control Is Assigned or Enabled

Check whether the system allows different exposure controls or room controls to be active depending on workflow, room setup, or selected mode.

Confirm staff are using the correct pedal, hand switch, or console control for the selected fluoro or radiographic function.

Expected outcome: The reported control matches the selected acquisition function.

Check Emergency Stop and Safety Interlocks

Inspect visible emergency stop buttons and room safety controls.

Confirm no emergency stop is pressed and that any door, table, tube stand, or collision safety condition has been resolved.

Expected outcome: Exposure is not being blocked by a safety interlock.

If an emergency stop or safety condition is found active, reset only according to site policy, verify system readiness, and retest.

Check Room Power and Control Accessories

Confirm the system, generator cabinet, operator console, monitor workstation, and room control components are powered normally.

Look for abnormal indicator lights, blank displays, startup errors, warning tones, or communication messages.

Expected outcome: The exposure control issue is not caused by a broader system power or communication problem.

Test With the Patient Area Clear

With no patient on the table and the room clear, ask qualified imaging staff to attempt a controlled system-ready check according to site policy.

Confirm whether the pedal, hand switch, or exposure control responds consistently.

Expected outcome: The control response is verified safely without patient risk.

If the control works normally after external checks, return the unit to service only after clinical staff confirm acceptable operation.

Compare With Another Approved Control If Available

If the system has another approved exposure control option, have qualified staff test whether fluoro or exposure can be initiated from that alternate control.

Do not bypass safety systems or use unapproved adapters.

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to one external control, one connection path, or a broader system inhibit.

If one control fails but another approved control works, remove the suspect control from service and escalate for evaluation or replacement.

Power Cycle Only If Safe and Appropriate

If the system is not in clinical use and site policy allows, perform a normal shutdown and restart of the fluoroscopy system and workstation.

Do not power cycle during an active patient procedure unless directed by the clinical team and site downtime procedure.

Expected outcome: Temporary software or control communication faults may clear after a controlled restart.

If control response returns after restart, verify repeated operation and document the event.

Do Not Continue Repeated Exposure Attempts

Avoid repeated fluoro or exposure attempts if the control is intermittent, delayed, or unreliable.

Intermittent exposure control is a patient safety concern because staff may not be able to predict when X-ray will initiate.

Expected outcome: The device is not used clinically until exposure control reliability is confirmed.

If the Problem Persists

If the fluoro pedal, hand switch, or exposure control still does not respond after external checks, common causes such as workflow state, loose connections, safety interlocks, accessory damage, and system-ready conditions have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal control circuit, exposure interface, generator enable path, room control communication fault, software fault, or service-level component failure.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, service evaluation, or bench-level control accessory testing

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not bypass exposure controls, defeat safety interlocks, or continue clinical use with unreliable X-ray initiation.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot fluoro or exposure response problems on an active patient unless the clinical team determines it is necessary for immediate patient care. Whenever possible, move the patient to another imaging system first and troubleshoot only when the room is safe and clear.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Technologist reported that the Luminos Agile Max fluoro pedal did not initiate fluoroscopy during exam setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"External inspection found the fluoro pedal connector partially seated at the room connection point, causing no response from the pedal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated the pedal connector, verified system-ready status, confirmed pedal response with staff, and returned the fluoroscopy system to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Which control failed: fluoro pedal, hand switch, console exposure button, or room control
  • Whether the issue was no response, delayed response, or intermittent response
  • Any system messages or X-ray inhibit warnings
  • Whether the system was in fluoro mode or radiographic exposure mode
  • Whether emergency stop or door interlock was active
  • Pedal and hand switch cable condition
  • Connector seating verified
  • Whether alternate approved control worked
  • Whether a restart was performed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Fluoro and exposure control issues should always be approached with patient safety first. Start with visible controls, cables, workflow state, system-ready messages, and interlocks before suspecting internal failure. If X-ray initiation remains unreliable, remove the system from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
