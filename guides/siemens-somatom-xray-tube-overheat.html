Siemens Healthineers SOMATOM Series

X-Ray Tube Overheat or Cooling System Fault

Asset Type

CT Scanner

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

SOMATOM Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where a Siemens Healthineers SOMATOM Series CT scanner reports an X-ray tube overheat warning or a cooling system fault. Symptoms may include system shutdown during scans, alarms for tube temperature, or warning messages indicating insufficient cooling. The guide focuses on external, easily verifiable causes before considering internal component failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check the Power and System Readiness

  • Ensure the CT scanner is properly powered on and all emergency stops are released.
  • Verify that the main power supply and UPS are functioning correctly.
  • Why: Inadequate power can prevent proper operation of tube cooling systems.

Inspect Cooling System Connections

  • Check that all external coolant lines and connectors are securely attached.
  • Verify that there are no visible leaks or kinks in the tubing.
  • Why: Loose connections or leaks can prevent proper circulation of the cooling medium.

Verify Coolant Levels (if applicable)

  • Confirm that the cooling reservoir or tank is at the correct level according to manufacturer guidelines.
  • Why: Low coolant levels can trigger overheat warnings.

Check Cooling Fans and Vents

  • Inspect the scanner’s external cooling fans for proper operation.
  • Ensure vents are unobstructed and clean from dust or debris.
  • Why: Blocked airflow reduces heat dissipation, increasing tube temperature.

Review Recent Scan Workload

  • Determine if multiple high-dose scans were performed consecutively.
  • Allow time for cooling between scans if necessary.
  • Why: Excessive consecutive scans can temporarily exceed tube cooling capacity.

Monitor System Alarms and Logs

  • Check the operator console or service logs for specific error codes.
  • Record the frequency and conditions of the overheat warnings.
  • Why: Helps determine if the issue is sporadic, workload-related, or persistent.

Attempt a Controlled Test Scan

  • With patient safety in mind, perform a low-dose test scan or phantom scan to monitor tube temperature and cooling response.
  • Observe whether the alarm triggers under controlled conditions.
  • Why: Confirms whether the cooling system is functional during normal operation.

If the Problem Persists

If external checks do not resolve the issue, the problem is likely internal (e.g., heat exchanger, internal pump, or X-ray tube degradation).

  • Remove the CT scanner from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send the system for manufacturer service or in-house bench evaluation.

Note: Stopping at this point is proper troubleshooting and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt to scan a patient when a tube overheat or cooling fault alarm is present.
  • Move the patient to another CT scanner if available.
  • Only perform troubleshooting on an idle system.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Operator reported a tube overheat warning during a routine scan.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
External inspection revealed no visible leaks, correct coolant levels, fans functioning, but alarms persisted after multiple low-dose test scans.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
CT scanner removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and referred to Siemens Healthineers service for evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and stable
  • Coolant level verified
  • Fans and vents unobstructed
  • Error codes logged
  • Number of scans performed prior to warning
  • Tube temperature readings during test scan

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Always approach tube overheat and cooling system faults methodically—start with external checks, document findings, and escalate appropriately. Proper CCR documentation ensures clear communication with service engineers and reduces unnecessary downtime.

That is successful troubleshooting.
