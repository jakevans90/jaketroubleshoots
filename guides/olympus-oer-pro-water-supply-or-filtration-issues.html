Olympus OER-Pro

Water Supply or Filtration Issues

Asset Type

Endoscope Reprocessor (AER)

Manufacturer

Olympus

Model

OER-Pro

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses low water flow, supply errors, filtration alarms, or cycle interruptions caused by water supply, pressure, or filter-related issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot while a scope is actively being processed for patient use
  • Remove the scope and use another AER if needed

Verify the Reported Issue and Alarm

  • Note exact alarm message (low water, supply error, filter warning)
  • Observe when the error occurs (start, rinse, or fill phase)

Check Facility Water Supply (Most Common Cause)

  • Ensure supply valve is fully open
  • Verify facility water is not shut off or under maintenance
  • Confirm no kinks in supply hose

Verify Water Pressure

  • Confirm adequate pressure from facility source
  • Compare with nearby equipment using same supply
  • Look for slow fill behavior

Inspect Inlet Water Filter (Very Common Cause)

  • Locate inlet filter screen
  • Inspect for debris, sediment, or discoloration
  • Clean or replace if accessible per facility protocol

Check External Water Lines and Connections

  • Inspect hoses for kinks or compression
  • Ensure all fittings are tight
  • Check for leaks or air intrusion

Evaluate Internal Filtration Indicators (If Accessible)

  • Check for filter replacement alerts
  • Review maintenance logs for overdue filter changes

Perform a Test Cycle (If Safe to Do So)

  • Monitor fill time and water flow
  • Watch for repeat alarms

If the Problem Persists

If water supply, pressure, and filtration have been verified and the issue continues, the problem is likely internal (flow sensors, valves, or internal plumbing). Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a questionable AER cycle for patient-ready scopes. If water flow or filtration is compromised, always reprocess the scope on a confirmed working unit.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"OER-Pro reporting water supply error and unable to start cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Clogged inlet water filter restricting flow into unit."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned inlet filter, verified water pressure, and confirmed normal cycle operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Water supply valve position verified
  • Filter condition (clean/dirty/replaced)
  • Hose condition (kinked/damaged/normal)
  • Alarm message text
  • Fill time behavior (slow/normal)
  • Leak presence or absence
  • Final operational status

Final Thought

Water-related issues in AERs are often external and preventable. Start with supply and filtration before assuming internal failure. Proper troubleshooting protects both equipment reliability and patient safety, while clear documentation supports long-term maintenance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
