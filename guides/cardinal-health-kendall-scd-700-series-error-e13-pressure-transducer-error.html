Cardinal Health Kendall SCD 700 Series

Error: E13 Pressure Transducer Error

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error E13 where the SCD cannot detect pressure increase, resulting in failed therapy cycles and inability to provide compression.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use immediately if therapy is not functioning
  • Apply an alternate DVT prevention method if needed

Expected: Patient continues receiving appropriate therapy
Why it matters: Loss of compression therapy increases DVT risk

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the unit and confirm Error E13 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup or inflation cycle)
  • Check if sleeves do not inflate or inflate minimally
  • Note if the unit stops therapy or alarms

Expected: Error is consistently present and reproducible
Why it matters: Confirms a true sensing failure, not intermittent use issue

Check Sleeve Connections and Tubing (Most Common External Cause)

  • Ensure tubing is fully connected to the device ports
  • Check for loose fittings
  • Look for kinks, occlusions, or disconnections

Expected: Tubing is secure with no airflow restriction
Why it matters: Blocked or disconnected tubing prevents pressure buildup, mimicking transducer failure

Inspect Sleeves for Leaks or Damage

  • Check for tears, holes, or worn areas
  • Listen for air leaks during inflation attempt
  • Swap with known good sleeves if available

Expected: Sleeves hold air and inflate normally
Why it matters: Air leaks prevent pressure rise, triggering E13

Verify Proper Sleeve Application and Fit

  • Confirm proper sizing and secure wrap
  • Ensure no excessive looseness allowing air escape

Expected: Sleeves inflate with resistance
Why it matters: Poor fit can reduce measurable pressure rise

Test Device Without Sleeves (Isolation Check)

  • Disconnect sleeves and briefly run the device
  • Observe if pump attempts to build pressure
  • Listen for pump operation

Expected: Pump runs but cannot build pressure without load
Why it matters: Helps determine if issue is external (sleeves/tubing) vs internal sensing

Power Cycle the Device

  • Turn the unit off, wait 10–15 seconds, and restart

Expected: Error clears only if transient
Why it matters: Rules out temporary startup fault

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. The device is unable to detect pressure rise internally. This strongly indicates a failed pressure transducer or internal pneumatic issue.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send to Cardinal Health or qualified repair vendor

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an SCD while actively connected to a patient. Always verify compression therapy on a working unit before relying on it for DVT prevention.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device displaying Error E13 Pressure Transducer Error. Sleeves not inflating during therapy."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Unable to detect pressure rise after verifying tubing and sleeves. Likely internal pressure sensing failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent to manufacturer for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • When error occurs (startup vs during inflation)
  • Tubing condition and connection status
  • Sleeves swapped with known good set
  • Pump sound during operation
  • Any observed air leaks
  • Final device disposition

Final Thought

Error E13 represents a failure to confirm pressure generation. Always rule out simple airflow issues first, but recognize persistent errors as internal failures requiring service. Patient safety and proper escalation are critical.

That is successful troubleshooting.
