Medtronic Valleylab FT10

Error: E324 Check Instrument

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Valleylab FT10

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error E324 Check Instrument on the Medtronic Valleylab FT10 Electrosurgical Unit. This error indicates the bipolar handswitch may be pressed, stuck, damaged, contaminated, connected incorrectly, or falsely detected by the generator.

This condition can prevent normal ESU readiness or activation. Because the issue involves an activation input, it should be treated as a patient safety concern until the bipolar instrument and ESU are verified safe for use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Valleylab FT10 from patient use if the error appears during setup, activation, or a procedure.
  • Stop use of the affected bipolar instrument.
  • Confirm the surgical team has an alternate ESU, alternate instrument, or safe clinical backup if energy delivery is needed.
  • Do not continue attempting activation with an unresolved handswitch-related error.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Check the FT10 display and confirm the message is Error: E324 Check Instrument.
  • Note whether the error appears at startup, when the bipolar instrument is connected, when the handswitch is touched, during activation, or intermittently during setup.
  • Ask clinical staff whether the bipolar switch felt stuck, was being pressed, or activated without a clear command.
  • Confirm the issue is specific to the bipolar instrument path and not a general ESU setup complaint.

Release the Bipolar Handswitch

  • Make sure the bipolar handswitch is not being held down.
  • Inspect the instrument handle or activation area for a stuck, jammed, wet, contaminated, or damaged switch.
  • Disconnect the instrument and reconnect it only after confirming the switch is released.
  • Check whether the E324 Check Instrument error clears after the handswitch is released.

Inspect the Bipolar Instrument

  • Inspect the bipolar instrument for cracked housing, bent connector pins, fluid contamination, damaged insulation, sticky switch movement, or signs of being dropped or crushed.
  • Confirm the accessory is compatible with the Valleylab FT10 setup.
  • Do not reuse an instrument that appears damaged, contaminated, or mechanically unreliable.

Replace the Bipolar Instrument

  • Disconnect the suspect bipolar instrument.
  • Connect a known-good compatible bipolar instrument.
  • Power cycle the ESU if needed after changing accessories.
  • Observe whether Error E324 returns.
  • If the error clears with a known-good instrument, remove the failed instrument from use and document it as the likely cause.

Check the Bipolar Instrument Connection

  • Confirm the bipolar instrument is fully seated in the correct connection point.
  • Check that the connector does not feel loose, forced, or misaligned.
  • Look for debris, moisture, bent pins, damaged connector surfaces, or signs of fluid intrusion.
  • Reconnect the known-good instrument and verify whether the error remains cleared.

Confirm the Issue Is Not Setup or User Technique

  • Ask whether the instrument was resting under tension, being squeezed by a clamp, or placed where the switch could be pressed.
  • Confirm staff are not connecting the wrong accessory type to the bipolar port.
  • Verify the error is not only occurring when a specific user handles the instrument.
  • Make sure accidental pressure on the handswitch is not being mistaken for a device failure.

Recheck the FT10 With No Instrument Connected

  • Disconnect the bipolar instrument from the FT10.
  • Power cycle the generator.
  • Observe whether Error E324 appears with no bipolar instrument connected.
  • If E324 appears with no instrument connected, the issue is less likely to be the external instrument and more likely related to the ESU detection or control path.

Isolate Accessory-Related vs Device-Related Failure

  • If E324 occurs only with one instrument, remove that instrument from service.
  • If E324 clears with a known-good instrument, verify normal standby behavior and return the FT10 to service if all checks pass.
  • If E324 occurs with multiple known-good instruments, remove the FT10 from service.
  • Do not perform internal disassembly during clinical troubleshooting.

If the Problem Persists

If Error E324 continues after the bipolar handswitch is released, the instrument is replaced, the connection is checked, and the ESU is tested with known-good accessories, external and common causes have been ruled out.

At that point, the persistent error may indicate an internal detection, control, or instrument interface issue.

Remove the Valleylab FT10 from service. Label it Out of Service. Send the unit for repair or bench evaluation by qualified service personnel.

Do not continue clinical use with an unresolved bipolar handswitch error.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an ESU activation error on an active patient. Move the patient or procedure to a backup ESU or known-working accessory path first, then troubleshoot the affected Valleylab FT10 after it is no longer needed for active clinical use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Medtronic Valleylab FT10 displayed Error E324 Check Instrument during bipolar setup or activation and would not operate normally with the connected bipolar instrument."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Bipolar handswitch signal was detected as pressed. Error cleared after replacing the suspect bipolar instrument with a known-good compatible instrument."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Released and inspected bipolar handswitch, checked instrument condition and connector, tested with known-good bipolar instrument, verified Error E324 cleared, and removed failed accessory from use."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error E324 Check Instrument should be treated as a bipolar handswitch detection problem until proven otherwise. Start with patient safety, release the switch, inspect and replace the instrument, check the connection, and only escalate after external causes are ruled out. Clear CCR documentation helps separate a failed accessory from an internal ESU repair issue.

That is successful troubleshooting.
