Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: VOLUME ALARMS OFF !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

VOLUME ALARMS OFF ! alarm indicating disabled volume monitoring, removing alerts for high/low ventilation volumes and increasing risk of unnoticed ventilation issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the device is in use, immediately verify adequate ventilation and monitoring
  • If volume alarms are off, enable alarms or transition to alternate monitoring before continuing
  • Confirm patient ventilation is stable and continuously monitored

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the “VOLUME ALARMS OFF !” message is displayed
  • Check if volume alarms are disabled in settings
  • Verify the device is still ventilating normally
  • Ask if the setting was recently changed by a user

Check Alarm Settings Configuration

  • Access the alarm settings menu
  • Navigate to volume alarm settings (tidal volume/minute volume limits)
  • Verify alarms are turned OFF

Reactivate Volume Alarms

  • Turn ON volume alarms
  • Set appropriate upper and lower limits based on clinical use
  • Confirm settings are saved and active

Verify Alarm Functionality

  • Simulate or observe ventilation conditions
  • Confirm alarms trigger appropriately when limits are exceeded
  • Ensure both visual and audible alerts are functioning

Check for Repeated Deactivation

  • Ask staff if alarms were intentionally disabled
  • Review workflow or training issues
  • Educate users on importance of active alarm monitoring

Stop When Resolved

  • If volume alarms are active and functioning, return the device to service
  • Ensure alarm limits are appropriate for clinical use

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm remains despite enabling settings:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before adjusting alarm settings or troubleshooting monitoring issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"VOLUME ALARMS OFF ! displayed; no volume alarms active during ventilation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Volume alarms found disabled in alarm settings due to user configuration."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Re-enabled volume alarms, adjusted limits appropriately, and verified proper alarm functionality."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

This alarm reflects a safety configuration issue rather than equipment failure. Recognizing this prevents unnecessary escalation while restoring critical patient safeguards. Always ensure alarms are active prior to patient use.

That is successful troubleshooting.
