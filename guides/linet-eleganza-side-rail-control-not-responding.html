Linet Eleganza

Side Rail Control Not Responding

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Linet

Model

Eleganza

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of unresponsive side rail controls, including power, lockout settings, cable connections, and panel faults before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

  • Confirm the patient is safely positioned or transferred. Do not attempt control tests with the patient reliant on the side rail.

Check Bed Lockouts

  • Verify that side rail lockout features are not engaged. If active, controls will not respond. Deactivating lockouts should restore functionality.

Inspect Power Supply

  • Ensure the bed is plugged in, battery is charged, and the main power switch is on. Unpowered controls cannot operate.

Examine Cables and Connectors

  • Check all cables connecting side rails to the main control box for secure connections, damage, or loose pins. Replace or reseat as needed.

Test Both Side Rails

  • Operate controls on both sides to determine if the issue is isolated to one rail or affects both, indicating panel versus wiring issues.

Observe Indicator Lights and Sounds

  • Look for fault LEDs or error beeps on the side rail or main control unit, which may indicate a detected malfunction.

Swap Side Rail Panels (If Available)

  • If another identical panel is available, swapping can isolate whether the panel itself is defective versus wiring or control electronics.

If the Problem Persists

External causes (lockouts, cables, power) have been ruled out.

Remove the bed from service and label Out of Service.

Send for repair or bench evaluation by qualified service personnel.

Recognizing when to stop protects patient safety and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Always troubleshoot side rail issues only when the patient is safely supported.

Never rely on malfunctioning rails to prevent patient falls.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nurse reports left side rail control does not respond."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Side rail panel cable was loose and lockout feature engaged."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Secured cable and disengaged lockout; controls function restored."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Power outlet tested
  • Battery status verified
  • Lockout settings checked
  • Cables inspected
  • Indicator lights noted
  • Panel swapped if available
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount when side rail controls fail. Follow logical troubleshooting from external causes to escalation. Accurate documentation ensures proper follow-up and reduces repeat incidents.

That is successful troubleshooting.
