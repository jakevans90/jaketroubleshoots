Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: SPEAKER FAIL !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the SPEAKER FAIL ! alarm, which indicates loss of audible alarm functionality. This creates a significant patient safety risk because critical alerts cannot be heard during operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from patient use if audible alarms are not functioning
  • Transition the patient to a device with working alarm systems
  • Confirm continuous monitoring is maintained

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the SPEAKER FAIL ! alarm is present on the display
  • Check if audible alarms are completely absent
  • Verify if visual alarms still function normally
  • Note when the alarm occurs (startup or during operation)

3. Check Alarm Volume and Mute Settings

  • Ensure alarm volume is set to an audible level
  • Confirm the system is not muted or silenced
  • Attempt to trigger a test alarm

4. Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the machine off
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power the machine back on and observe behavior

5. Inspect for External Damage or Obstruction

  • Check the speaker grill for physical damage
  • Ensure no debris, fluid, or coverings are blocking the speaker
  • Listen for faint or distorted sound output

6. Confirm Alarm Function Using System Self-Test

  • Run any available startup or alarm self-test
  • Observe whether audible tones are produced

7. Isolate the Issue

  • If sound returns after adjusting settings or rebooting → configuration or software issue
  • If no sound persists → likely internal speaker or audio circuit failure

If the Problem Persists

If the SPEAKER FAIL ! alarm remains after completing all external checks, internal causes are likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send the device for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or use an anesthesia machine with non-functional audible alarms on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before proceeding.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed SPEAKER FAIL ! alarm and no audible alarms were heard during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"No sound output during alarm testing despite correct volume settings and successful reboot. Indicates internal speaker or audio circuit failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair. Audible alarm functionality confirmed failed."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Audible alarms are a critical safety feature and must always be fully functional. Proper troubleshooting ensures patient safety while avoiding unnecessary internal work. Escalate when needed and document clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
