Drager Evita V500

Alarm: PMEAN high Mean airway pressure exceeds limit

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Evita V500

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the PMEAN high alarm, which indicates elevated mean airway pressure. This condition can affect ventilation effectiveness and may signal issues related to ventilator settings, the breathing circuit, or patient condition.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient’s respiratory status
  • If ventilation is compromised, remove from ventilator and provide alternate ventilation
  • Ensure oxygenation and ventilation are maintained at all times

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the PMEAN high alarm is active on the display
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup, during ventilation, intermittent)
  • Check for associated high pressure readings or stacked alarms

Check Ventilator Settings

  • Review PEEP setting
  • Check inspiratory time (I-time)
  • Assess pressure control or volume settings
  • Verify alarm limits are appropriately set

Inspect Breathing Circuit for Restrictions

  • Check for kinked or compressed tubing
  • Look for water accumulation in the circuit
  • Inspect filters for blockage
  • Ensure expiratory valve is functioning properly

Check Patient Interface

  • Inspect endotracheal tube for kinks or secretions
  • Verify proper placement
  • Check for biting or occlusion
  • Ensure mask fit if using non-invasive ventilation

Assess Patient Condition

  • Evaluate for decreased lung compliance
  • Check for bronchospasm, coughing, or agitation
  • Assess for fluid overload or ARDS-related changes

Isolate Equipment vs Patient Cause

  • Disconnect from patient when safe
  • Attach a test lung
  • Observe if mean airway pressure remains elevated

Run System Test (SST)

  • Perform system self-test
  • Verify test completes without errors

Run Extended System Test (EST)

  • Perform extended diagnostics if available
  • Check for any system failures

If the Problem Persists

If the PMEAN high alarm continues after external causes have been ruled out:

  • Remove the ventilator from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot high-pressure alarms on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup ventilator before continuing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator alarming PMEAN high during use with elevated airway pressure readings."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Water accumulation in breathing circuit causing increased resistance and elevated mean airway pressure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleared circuit, verified normal pressures, and confirmed alarm resolved."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

High mean airway pressure alarms require a structured approach. Always start with patient safety, verify settings and airflow, and escalate when needed to ensure safe operation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
