Philips PageWriter TC70

Error: Low battery warning: printing is disabled. Connect the cardiograph to AC power and recharge the batteries

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a Philips PageWriter TC70 low battery warning that disables ECG printing. The condition may be caused by depleted batteries, missing batteries, batteries that are not charging, poor battery seating, AC power problems, or battery capacity failure.

Because ECG printing may be needed for timely clinical review and documentation, the unit should not be relied on for patient testing until power, charging, and printing operation are verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the PageWriter TC70 is needed for active patient testing and printing is disabled, do not continue using it as the primary ECG device.
  • Use another available ECG machine or connect the unit to verified AC power before continuing.
  • Confirm patient ECG testing is not delayed by a cardiograph that cannot print.
  • A cardiograph that cannot print may prevent timely ECG documentation or clinical review.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the PageWriter TC70 and confirm the exact low battery warning is displayed.
  • Confirm whether printing is actually disabled.
  • Check whether the message appears during startup, when attempting to print, after running on battery power, or immediately after being removed from AC power.
  • Confirm the low battery warning and print-disabled condition are clearly observed before replacing parts.

Connect the Cardiograph to AC Power

  • Plug the PageWriter TC70 directly into a known-good AC outlet.
  • Avoid power strips, loose outlets, switched outlets, or damaged receptacles.
  • Confirm the AC power indicator or charging status appears on the device.
  • If the warning clears and printing works on AC power, allow the batteries to recharge before returning the unit for battery-dependent use.

Check the AC Power Cord and Connection

  • Inspect the power cord for cuts, bent prongs, loose strain relief, or signs of overheating.
  • Confirm the cord is fully seated at both the wall outlet and the cardiograph.
  • Try a known-good compatible power cord if the unit does not recognize AC power.
  • A dead battery complaint may actually be caused by the device not receiving AC power to operate or charge.

Check Battery Presence and Seating

  • Remove the device from patient care and inspect the battery compartment according to facility procedure.
  • Confirm the battery or batteries are installed and seated correctly.
  • Look for a missing battery, loose battery, battery not latched, damaged contacts, swollen battery, physically damaged battery, or contamination in the battery area.
  • If a battery is swollen, leaking, damaged, or unusually hot, remove the device from service and handle the battery per facility battery safety policy.

Allow the Batteries to Recharge

  • Leave the PageWriter TC70 connected to AC power long enough for the batteries to recharge.
  • Confirm the charging indicator or battery status improves.
  • Attempt printing again after the device has had time to charge.
  • A deeply discharged battery may need charging time before the cardiograph restores battery-supported printing.

Test Printing While on AC Power

  • With the unit connected to AC power, attempt a test print or ECG print from a non-patient test workflow.
  • Confirm printing works while connected to AC power.
  • If printing works on AC but not on battery, the issue is likely battery capacity, battery charge state, or battery connection.
  • If printing still does not work on AC power, investigate printer, paper, or system errors separately.

Check Battery Runtime

  • After charging, disconnect AC power and operate the PageWriter TC70 on battery.
  • Observe whether the battery level drops quickly or the low battery warning returns immediately.
  • Confirm the device operates normally on battery for a reasonable period.
  • If the battery rapidly depletes, cannot hold charge, or immediately triggers the warning, replace the battery.

Replace the Battery if Needed

  • Install a known-good or approved replacement battery if the existing battery is dead, missing, unable to charge, or unable to support printing.
  • Reconnect AC power and confirm the device recognizes the replacement battery.
  • Confirm the low battery warning clears and printing is restored.
  • Printing may be disabled when the battery condition is too low or unreliable.

Verify Final Operation

  • Confirm the PageWriter TC70 powers on normally.
  • Confirm the unit recognizes AC power.
  • Confirm acceptable battery status is shown.
  • Confirm the device prints successfully.
  • Confirm the low battery warning does not return.
  • Complete basic functional checks before returning the device to service.

If the Problem Persists

If AC power has been verified, the power cord has been checked, the battery is installed correctly, and a known-good battery does not clear the warning, external power and battery causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve the charging circuit, battery contacts, power management circuitry, or another internal device fault.

Remove the PageWriter TC70 from service.

Label it Out of Service and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Persistent battery or charging-related errors should not be ignored because the device may fail during patient testing, transport, or printing.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot battery or printing issues while the device is actively connected to or needed for a patient. Move the patient workflow to a backup ECG machine first, or use verified AC power and another available device so patient testing is not delayed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips PageWriter TC70 displayed 'Low battery warning: printing is disabled. Connect the cardiograph to AC power and recharge the batteries' and staff reported the unit would not print ECGs."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery was depleted and the cardiograph had not been charging from AC power. Power cord seating and battery status were checked before testing print operation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Connected cardiograph to known-good AC power, verified charging status, reseated battery, allowed battery to recharge, and completed successful ECG print test. Unit returned to service after low battery warning cleared."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery-related warnings on ECG machines should be handled logically and safely. Confirm the warning, verify AC power, inspect the battery condition, and test printing before returning the unit to service. If the warning persists after simple external causes are ruled out, escalation is appropriate. Clear CCR documentation helps show what was checked, what corrected the issue, and whether the device is safe for patient care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
