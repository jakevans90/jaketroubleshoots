Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x1F01 Inconsistent Software State

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0x1F01 indicating inconsistent software state causing abnormal bed behavior, control issues, or system instability during operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from active patient use if controls are unresponsive or unpredictable
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error 0x1F01 is displayed
  • Observe when the error appears (startup or during operation)
  • Check for abnormal behavior such as unresponsive controls or random movement

3. Perform a Full System Reboot

  • Unplug the bed from AC power
  • Remove battery or ensure complete power loss
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Restore power and restart the bed

4. Check Power Stability and Connections

  • Plug into a known-good outlet
  • Inspect power cord for damage
  • Ensure all power connections are secure

5. Inspect External Components and Modules

  • Check pendant and siderail controls
  • Inspect any accessory modules
  • Disconnect and reconnect external components

6. Reboot Again After Adjustments

  • Perform another reboot after verifying connections
  • Observe if error clears

7. Determine if Issue Persists

  • If error remains, external causes have been ruled out
  • Likely internal software corruption or MCB-related issue

If the Problem Persists

External and simple causes have been ruled out. Remove the bed from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Likely requires Main Control Board (MCB) replacement or software reconfiguration.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a bed with software errors while a patient is relying on it. Always transfer the patient to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x1F01 with intermittent control issues during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal software inconsistency not resolved by reboot; suspected MCB/software mismatch."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed full power cycle and verified power and connections. Error persisted. Removed bed from service and escalated for board-level repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Software-related errors may appear intermittent but can indicate deeper system faults when persistent. Proper isolation, logical troubleshooting, and timely escalation ensure both patient safety and efficient repair workflows.

That is successful troubleshooting.
