Ambu King Vision aBlade

No image after blade attachment

Asset Type

Video Laryngoscope

Manufacturer

Ambu

Model

King Vision aBlade

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting no video image after attaching a King Vision aBlade due to blade seating, contacts, battery, display, or accessory issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

If the device fails during airway preparation or active airway management, clinical staff should immediately use another ready video laryngoscope or alternate airway device.

Do not troubleshoot the Ambu King Vision aBlade on an active patient.

Expected outcome: Patient care is transferred to a reliable airway visualization device before troubleshooting continues.

Confirm the Reported Failure

Power on the display and attach the aBlade while observing the screen.

Confirm whether the issue is:

  • Completely black screen
  • Display powers on but shows no camera image
  • Image appears briefly, then drops out
  • Error or abnormal display behavior after blade attachment

Expected outcome: The failure mode is confirmed and documented.

Verify the Display Powers On Normally

Turn the King Vision display on without the blade attached.

Check for normal startup behavior, screen illumination, and battery indication if available.

Expected outcome: The display powers on normally before evaluating the blade connection.

If the display does not power on, stop this guide and troubleshoot the display power or battery condition first.

Check the Battery Condition

Replace the batteries with known-good, properly installed batteries if the display is dim, intermittent, or fails during blade attachment.

Confirm the battery contacts are clean and that the battery compartment is fully closed.

Expected outcome: The display remains powered on and stable during blade attachment.

If replacing the batteries restores video, return the unit to service after functional verification.

Remove and Reattach the aBlade

Remove the aBlade and inspect how it seats onto the display.

Reattach it firmly and evenly, making sure it is fully engaged and not angled, partially inserted, or loose.

Expected outcome: The display detects the blade and produces a live camera image.

If reseating the blade restores the image, verify stable operation with gentle handling and stop.

Inspect the Blade-to-Display Connection Area

Look at the blade interface and display connection area for:

  • Debris
  • Fluid residue
  • Bent or damaged contacts
  • Cracked plastic
  • Worn latch area
  • Signs of cleaning-fluid intrusion

Do not scrape contacts aggressively or use excessive liquid.

Expected outcome: Connection surfaces are clean, dry, and physically intact.

Try a Known-Good aBlade

Attach a new or known-good compatible Ambu King Vision aBlade.

Expected outcome: The display shows a clear image with the known-good blade.

If the known-good blade works, remove the original blade from use and document the disposable blade failure.

Check for Blade Damage or Packaging Issues

Inspect the suspect aBlade for cracked housing, damaged camera window, loose connection area, or contamination from packaging or storage.

Expected outcome: The blade is physically intact and suitable for use.

If the blade appears damaged, discard it according to facility policy and verify the display with another blade.

Verify the Display with Multiple Blades

If one blade does not work, test the display with at least one additional known-good blade when available.

Expected outcome: A properly functioning display should produce an image with known-good blades.

If multiple known-good blades fail to produce an image, suspect the display or blade connector interface.

Check for Intermittent Image During Handling

With a known-good blade attached, gently move the display and blade connection without stressing the device.

Watch for flickering, image dropout, or loss of video.

Expected outcome: Image remains stable during normal handling.

If the image cuts in and out, remove the display from service for repair evaluation.

Inspect for External Damage

Check the King Vision display for signs of impact, cracked housing, loose battery door, damaged screen, or contamination around the blade connection.

Expected outcome: No external damage is present that would explain the connection failure.

If damage is found, remove the display from service.

Perform a Final Functional Check

Attach a known-good blade and confirm:

  • Display powers on
  • Blade is recognized
  • Live image appears
  • Image remains stable
  • Camera view is clear
  • No flicker or dropout occurs during normal handling

Expected outcome: The device is ready for safe clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If the Ambu King Vision aBlade still shows no image after battery replacement, blade reseating, connector inspection, and testing with known-good blades, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

The likely issue may involve the display connector, internal video pathway, power delivery to the blade, or internal display electronics.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated blade swaps or return the device to service if video is unreliable.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a failed video laryngoscope during an active airway attempt. Provide clinical staff with another ready airway device first, then evaluate the Ambu King Vision only when patient care is no longer dependent on it.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Ambu King Vision aBlade produced no image after blade attachment."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Troubleshooting found that the display powered on, but no video image appeared with the reported blade; known-good blade testing confirmed the original blade connection failed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Defective aBlade was removed from use, display passed functional check with a known-good blade, and unit was returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the display powered on normally
  • Battery condition or battery replacement performed
  • Whether the issue occurred with one blade or multiple blades
  • Blade lot number if a disposable blade issue is suspected
  • Condition of blade-to-display contacts
  • Any flickering or intermittent image behavior
  • Signs of fluid residue, debris, impact, or cracked housing
  • Whether a known-good blade restored the image
  • Final device status

Final Thought

No image after blade attachment should be approached logically: protect the patient first, confirm the display powers on, check batteries, reseat the blade, inspect the connection, and test with known-good accessories. If the image remains absent or intermittent after external causes are ruled out, the safest action is removal from service and clear CCR documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
