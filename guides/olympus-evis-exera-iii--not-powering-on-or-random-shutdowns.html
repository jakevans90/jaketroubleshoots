Olympus EVIS EXERA III

System Not Powering On or Random Shutdowns

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Olympus

Model

EVIS EXERA III

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses no power or unexpected shutdowns caused by power supply issues, loose connections, overheating, or external accessory faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the system shuts down during a procedure, immediately transition to a backup imaging system.
  • Expected: Patient care continues without interruption
  • Why it matters: Loss of visualization during endoscopy is a critical safety risk

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to power on the system and observe behavior:
  • No response at all (no lights, no fan)
  • Powers on briefly, then shuts down
  • Intermittent shutdown during use
  • Expected: Clear identification of symptom type
  • Why it matters: Helps differentiate power input vs thermal or load-related issues

Check AC Power Source (Most Common Cause)

  • Verify the outlet is functional using a known-good device
  • Ensure the system is plugged into a proper hospital-grade outlet or power strip
  • Avoid extension cords if present
  • Expected: Stable, verified power source
  • Why it matters: Inconsistent or failed outlets are a common cause of power issues

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Check the AC power cord for damage, fraying, or loose ends
  • Ensure the cord is fully seated at both the wall outlet and system power inlet
  • Swap with a known-good compatible cord if available
  • Expected: Secure, undamaged power connection
  • Why it matters: Loose or faulty cords can cause intermittent shutdowns

Check System Power Switch and Indicators

  • Confirm the main power switch is functioning properly
  • Observe any indicator lights or front panel status LEDs
  • Listen for fan or internal startup sounds
  • Expected: System shows signs of startup (lights, fan noise)
  • Why it matters: Helps determine if power is reaching internal components

Inspect for Overheating Conditions

  • Check for blocked vents or dust buildup
  • Ensure adequate clearance around the unit (especially in OR towers)
  • Confirm cooling fans are operating
  • Expected: Proper airflow and normal operating temperature
  • Why it matters: Overheating can trigger automatic shutdown to protect internal components

Disconnect External Accessories

  • Remove all connected devices (scopes, light source cables, monitors, peripherals)
  • Attempt to power on the base unit alone
  • Expected: System powers on without accessories
  • Why it matters: Faulty peripherals or shorted cables can cause shutdowns

Check Power Distribution (If in Equipment Tower)

  • Verify the system is not connected to a failing power strip or isolation transformer
  • Try plugging directly into a wall outlet if possible
  • Expected: Stable operation on direct power
  • Why it matters: Tower power systems can introduce intermittent faults

Attempt Power Cycle Reset

  • Disconnect power completely for 60 seconds
  • Reconnect and attempt startup
  • Expected: System reinitializes normally
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary faults or internal protection states

If the Problem Persists

If all external power sources, cables, and environmental conditions have been verified, the issue is likely internal (power supply, internal board, or thermal sensor failure).

Remove the device from service

Label as Out of Service

Send for repair or further bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and is part of proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot power issues while the system is in active clinical use. Always move the procedure to a functioning backup system before beginning evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"EVIS EXERA III system would not power on and intermittently shut down during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose AC power cord connection at system inlet causing intermittent power loss."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Secured power connection and verified stable operation through power cycle testing."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified functional
  • Power cord condition and swap performed
  • Indicator light behavior observed
  • Fan operation confirmed
  • Accessories disconnected during testing
  • No unusual heat or odor detected
  • Final device status (operational or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Power issues should always be approached from the outside in—starting with simple, verifiable causes before assuming internal failure. Patient safety comes first, especially with visualization-dependent procedures. Proper escalation and clear documentation ensure reliability and accountability in Clinical Engineering workflows.

That is successful troubleshooting.
