Nihon Kohden ECG-1500 Series

Display or Keypad Failure

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Nihon Kohden

Model

ECG-1500 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Unresponsive screen, blank display, dim screen, or keypad not registering inputs due to power, connection, or user interface issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the device is in use, stop acquisition and move the patient to another EKG machine before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring continues on a functional device.
  • Why it matters: Loss of display or input prevents safe operation and data acquisition.

Verify Power to the Unit

  • Check if the unit is powered on.
  • Confirm power indicator lights are on
  • Press the power button to ensure proper startup
  • Observe for screen activity (logo, backlight, or sounds)
  • Expected: Device powers on with visible display activity.
  • Why it matters: A blank screen may be a power issue, not a display failure.

Check AC Power and Battery Status

  • Inspect external power sources.
  • Ensure AC cord is securely plugged into both the device and outlet
  • Test the outlet with another device
  • Check battery indicator status on screen (if visible)
  • Expected: Device operates on AC power or shows battery charge status.
  • Why it matters: Low or failed battery can cause screen dimming or failure to boot.

Adjust Display Brightness/Contrast Settings

  • If the screen is on but difficult to see:
  • Adjust brightness using available controls or menu
  • View screen from different angles
  • Look for faint images indicating backlight issues
  • Expected: Screen becomes visible and readable.
  • Why it matters: Apparent display failure may be due to low brightness or backlight settings.

Inspect for Physical Damage or Contamination

  • Check the screen and keypad surface.
  • Look for cracks, liquid intrusion, or residue
  • Check for stuck or worn keypad buttons
  • Clean surface if contamination is present
  • Expected: Buttons move freely and screen is intact.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage or debris can prevent proper input or visibility.

Test Keypad Functionality

  • Attempt to use multiple keys.
  • Press several buttons and observe for response
  • Listen for audible feedback (beeps)
  • Check if only certain keys are non-responsive
  • Expected: Keys respond consistently with input feedback.
  • Why it matters: Partial failure may indicate keypad wear or connection issue.

Perform a Device Restart

  • Reboot the unit to clear potential software issues.
  • Power off the device completely
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power back on and observe startup behavior
  • Expected: Device boots normally with responsive display and keypad.
  • Why it matters: Temporary software faults can affect interface responsiveness.

Check for External Accessories Interference

  • Disconnect any non-essential peripherals.
  • Remove USB devices, network cables, or accessories
  • Restart the device without accessories connected
  • Expected: Device functions normally without interference.
  • Why it matters: External devices can occasionally cause system instability.

Observe for Startup Behavior

  • During reboot, note:
  • Does the screen remain blank?
  • Does it partially display then freeze?
  • Are there any error tones or indicators?
  • Expected: Normal startup sequence with full display function.
  • Why it matters: Helps differentiate between display hardware vs system-level failure.

If the Problem Persists

After ruling out power, settings, and external causes, the issue is likely internal (display panel, keypad membrane, or main board).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not attempt internal disassembly. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot display or keypad issues while connected to a patient. Always transition to another EKG machine before addressing the problem.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"EKG machine screen blank and keypad not responding during patient setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed display/backlight or keypad interface after power and restart checks confirmed no external issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent to Biomed for repair and evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Power indicator status
  • Outlet tested
  • Battery condition
  • Screen behavior (blank, dim, partial image)
  • Keypad response (all vs partial failure)
  • Presence of physical damage or contamination
  • Startup behavior observations
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Display and keypad failures remove the user’s ability to safely operate the device. Always prioritize patient transfer, verify simple external causes, and escalate appropriately when needed. Clear documentation supports faster repair and better equipment tracking.

That is successful troubleshooting.
