Stryker S3

Error: 201 Head Left Load Cell

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Error 201 / Head Left Load Cell, which indicates an abnormal head-left load-cell reading and can make the bed's scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness functions unreliable or unavailable.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Stryker S3 bed from patient use if Error 201 Call Service is displayed or if the scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness functions cannot be trusted.
  • If the bed is occupied, coordinate with clinical staff to safely transfer the patient to another appropriate bed before beginning troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on weight readings, Bed Exit monitoring, or iBED Awareness status until the condition is corrected and verified.

Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on a bed with potentially unreliable weight, exit-monitoring, or awareness functions.

Why it matters: A failed or inaccurate load-cell signal may interfere with features used for patient monitoring and fall-risk precautions.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the bed removed from patient use, power the bed as appropriate and confirm that the display shows Error 201 Call Service.
  • Document whether the error appears immediately at startup, after the bed is loaded or repositioned, or intermittently.
  • Document any inaccurate weight readings, scale failure, Bed Exit malfunction, or iBED Awareness concerns reported by staff.

Expected outcome: Error 201 is reproduced or its occurrence is clearly confirmed from the bed display and clinical report.

Why it matters: Confirming the condition helps distinguish a system-reported load-cell fault from a setup or positioning concern alone.

3. Confirm the Bed Is Properly Positioned and Unloaded for Evaluation

  • Move the bed to a safe evaluation area.
  • Remove patient weight, stored equipment, excess linens, or accessories that could affect the weighing surface during testing.
  • Confirm the bed is on a stable, level surface.
  • Confirm the bed is not contacting walls, equipment, cords, or other objects that could apply abnormal force to the frame.

Expected outcome: The bed is free of external loading or environmental interference that could affect the load-cell reading.

Why it matters: External contact or uneven loading can contribute to abnormal scale behavior and may mimic or worsen a load-cell fault.

4. Inspect the Head-Left Load-Cell Area for Interference or Binding

  • Inspect the accessible head-left load-cell location and surrounding frame area for debris, contamination, pinched material, or foreign objects.
  • Check for bent, damaged, displaced, or visibly binding components.
  • Look for unintended frame contact or interference during bed positioning or movement.
  • Do not perform internal disassembly during initial troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: The head-left load-cell area is clean, unobstructed, and free from visible mechanical interference.

Why it matters: Error 201 indicates the head-left load-cell reading is under range or over range; mechanical binding or interference can create an abnormal reading without the load cell itself being failed.

5. Inspect Accessible Wiring and Connections Associated With the Head-Left Position

  • Inspect accessible cable routing and external connections associated with the head-left load-cell area.
  • Check for loose or disconnected connectors, pinched or stretched wiring, crushed insulation, visible cuts, abrasions, or contamination.
  • Check for harness damage caused by bed movement or contact with the frame.
  • Correct accessible connection or cable-routing issues according to approved repair practice.

Expected outcome: Accessible wiring and connections are properly seated, protected, and free of obvious damage.

Why it matters: A cable fault or poor connection can cause an out-of-range load-cell signal and produce the same logged error as a failed sensor.

6. Use the Maintenance Menu to Confirm Load-Cell Diagnostic Information

  • Enter the bed's Maintenance Menu according to approved service procedure.
  • View or run the available load-cell diagnostic information.
  • Confirm whether the diagnostic display identifies the affected position as Load Cell Error with HL shown beneath it.
  • Record the diagnostic result in the work order.

Expected outcome: Diagnostic information either identifies the head-left load-cell position as faulty or helps narrow the problem to another condition.

Why it matters: A confirmed HL diagnostic fault supports isolation of the failure to the head-left load-cell circuit or mechanical position.

7. Determine Whether the Fault Clears After External Corrections

  • If interference, binding, debris, or an accessible connection issue was found, correct it and repeat the diagnostic check.
  • If Error 201 clears after correction, proceed with approved recalibration and functional verification.
  • If the diagnostic continues to indicate HL after external checks are completed, remove the bed from service for repair-level evaluation.

Expected outcome: The failure is isolated as either an external or mechanical condition that was corrected, or a persistent device-related load-cell fault requiring repair.

Why it matters: A persistent error after external checks suggests the head-left load cell or associated internal circuit requires repair-level service.

8. Recalibrate and Function-Test After Correction or Repair

  • After the identified problem has been corrected or repair has been completed, perform the approved scale recalibration procedure.
  • Verify normal scale function.
  • Verify normal Bed Exit function.
  • Verify normal iBED Awareness function.
  • Confirm that Error 201 no longer appears on the display.

Expected outcome: The bed completes recalibration, provides expected scale behavior, and no longer reports the head-left load-cell error.

Why it matters: Clearing the error alone is not sufficient; the affected patient-monitoring features must operate reliably before the bed returns to service.

9. Stop When Resolved

  • If Error 201 clears, the HL diagnostic no longer shows a fault, recalibration is successful, and all affected functions pass verification, document the work performed.
  • Return the bed to service only according to department procedure and only after confirming the scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness functions are operational.

Resolved condition: The bed may be returned to clinical use only after all affected functions have been confirmed reliable.

If the Problem Persists

If Error 201 Call Service remains after checking bed position, external loading, mechanical interference, binding, accessible wiring and connections, and load-cell diagnostic information, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent HL / Head Left Load Cell fault indicates a likely failed head-left load cell or an associated internal circuit problem requiring repair-level evaluation.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the bed for repair or bench evaluation.
  • After repair, complete approved recalibration and functional verification before returning the bed to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this error on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first. Until the issue is corrected and verified, treat scale readings, Bed Exit monitoring, and iBED Awareness status as unreliable.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displayed Error 201 Call Service. Staff reported concern for unreliable scale and Bed Exit/iBED Awareness operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found mechanical interference at the head-left load-cell area causing abnormal HL load-cell diagnostic status. Error indicated the head-left load-cell reading was outside expected range."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed interference, verified accessible cable condition and connections, performed scale recalibration, and completed functional testing of scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness. Error 201 no longer displayed; bed returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A head-left load-cell error is more than a display message because it can affect patient-weight measurement and bed-based safety monitoring. Protect the patient first, inspect simple mechanical and connection-related causes, confirm the fault through diagnostics, and escalate when the condition remains unresolved. Accurate CCR documentation ensures the error, findings, corrective action, and final safety status are clearly recorded.

That is successful troubleshooting.
