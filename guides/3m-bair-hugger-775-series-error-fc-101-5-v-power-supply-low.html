3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-101 5 V POWER SUPPLY LOW

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-101 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series patient warming system. FC-101 indicates the 5 V power supply is under 4.667 V, which points toward a low-voltage control power issue and a likely defective control board.

This fault can affect control electronics, temperature regulation, alarms, display behavior, and safe operation. The device should not be used for patient warming until the fault is verified, repaired, calibrated, and tested.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the warming system from patient use if Error FC-101 is active.
  • Stop use of the Bair Hugger 775 if it is connected to a patient.
  • Provide an alternate warming device if patient warming is still needed.
  • Do not continue using the unit with an active power supply error.
  • Label the unit for Clinical Engineering evaluation.

Expected outcome: Patient warming is safely transferred to another device.

Why it matters: A low internal 5 V supply can affect control electronics, temperature regulation, alarms, and safe operation.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on using a known-good AC outlet.
  • Confirm Error FC-101 is displayed.
  • Note whether the error appears immediately at startup, during warmup, during active operation, or intermittently.
  • Listen for abnormal sounds.
  • Check for unusual odor, heat, flickering display, or unstable operation.

Expected outcome: Error FC-101 is reproducible or clearly observed.

Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported fault instead of a one-time power interruption or user report mismatch.

3. Check the Power Source First

  • Confirm the power cord is fully seated at the unit and wall outlet.
  • Plug the unit into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Avoid using extension cords, damaged power strips, or questionable outlets.
  • Verify the outlet has stable power if there is any doubt.

Expected outcome: The unit powers normally without external power problems.

Why it matters: Internal low-voltage errors can sometimes be reported after poor AC input, a loose power connection, or a power interruption.

4. Inspect the Power Cord and External Condition

  • Inspect the power cord for cuts, crushed sections, exposed conductors, or damaged plug blades.
  • Check the appliance inlet for looseness, damage, debris, or signs of overheating.
  • Inspect the housing for cracks, impact damage, fluid intrusion, or contamination.
  • Confirm vents are not blocked.

Expected outcome: No external damage or obvious safety concern is found.

Why it matters: A damaged power path or fluid-contaminated unit should not be returned to service without further evaluation.

5. Power Cycle and Recheck the Error

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Disconnect AC power briefly.
  • Reconnect to a known-good outlet.
  • Power the unit back on.
  • Confirm whether Error FC-101 returns.

Expected outcome: The error either clears or returns.

If Error FC-101 clears and does not return during testing, continue functional checks before returning the unit to service. If Error FC-101 returns, continue isolation.

6. Confirm the Fault Is Not Environmental or Accessory-Related

  • Confirm the unit is not exposed to fluid, excessive heat, blocked airflow, or obvious contamination.
  • Remove any questionable hose or blanket setup if attached.
  • Power the unit with only the required basic setup for testing.
  • Observe whether the error still appears.

Expected outcome: Error FC-101 persists even after external setup issues are removed.

Why it matters: This helps separate a true internal system fault from a setup or environmental issue.

7. Identify the Likely Failure

  • Interpret FC-101 as a low 5 V logic supply fault.
  • FC-101 means the system detected the 5 V power supply below 4.667 V.
  • The low-voltage logic supply is not within range.
  • Control electronics may not operate reliably.
  • The manufacturer troubleshooting path identifies the control board as defective.

Expected outcome: The failure is treated as an internal control board or power regulation issue.

8. Escalate to Repair

  • Label the unit Out of Service.
  • Do not return it to clinical use.
  • Send the unit for bench repair or qualified service.
  • Replace the control board according to approved service procedures.
  • After repair, perform required calibration and safety/functional verification before return to service.

Expected outcome: Control board is replaced, unit is calibrated, and the system passes post-repair testing.

Why it matters: A persistent low 5 V supply is not a user-correctable issue and can affect safe device operation.

9. Stop When Resolved

  • Perform calibration after repair.
  • Complete operational testing.
  • Verify proper temperature operation.
  • Confirm no active fault codes remain.
  • Inspect the unit for safe physical condition.
  • Return the unit to service only after all checks pass.

Expected outcome: The unit operates normally with no FC-101 error.

If the Problem Persists

If Error FC-101 remains after control board replacement, external and common causes have been ruled out. This includes the AC outlet, power cord, appliance inlet, visible external condition, basic setup, accessories, and environmental factors.

Remove the device from service. Keep it labeled Out of Service. Send the unit for additional bench evaluation or vendor repair. Do not continue repeated power cycling, and do not return the unit to clinical use.

Persistent FC-101 strongly indicates an unresolved internal power or control electronics failure.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient when an internal power supply error is present. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, then evaluate the failed Bair Hugger away from patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 Series removed from service for Error FC-101. Unit reported 5 V power supply under 4.667 V and was not cleared for patient use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified FC-101 error during power-on testing. External power source, power cord, appliance inlet, and visible external condition checked. Error persisted, indicating likely defective control board per fault description."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and labeled Out of Service. Control board replacement required. Unit must be calibrated and pass operational/safety verification before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error FC-101 should be treated as a significant internal power or control fault after basic external checks are complete. Patient safety comes first, and a Bair Hugger with an unresolved low 5 V supply error should not be trusted for clinical warming. Good troubleshooting means verifying the error, ruling out simple causes, escalating appropriately, and documenting the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
