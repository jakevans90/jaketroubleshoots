Karl Storz C-MAC Pocket Monitor

Rechargeable Battery Will Not Charge or Rapidly Depletes

Asset Type

Video Laryngoscope

Manufacturer

Karl Storz

Model

C-MAC Pocket Monitor

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting charging failure, short runtime, power adapter issues, connection problems, or degraded rechargeable battery performance.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the C-MAC Pocket Monitor is not needed for an active airway procedure before troubleshooting.

If the monitor has low battery, will not power on, or powers off unexpectedly, provide clinical staff with another ready video laryngoscope or monitor.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not dependent on a device with unreliable battery power.

Verify the Reported Battery Issue

Ask clinical staff what was observed:

  • Will not charge at all
  • Charging indicator does not appear
  • Battery drains rapidly
  • Monitor powers off shortly after startup
  • Device only works while connected to power
  • Issue occurs with one charger or all chargers

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified before parts or device replacement is considered.

Inspect the Power Adapter and Charging Cable

Check the charger, cable, strain relief, plug, and connector for:

  • Bent or damaged pins
  • Cracked housing
  • Loose cable ends
  • Frayed insulation
  • Signs of fluid exposure
  • Missing or incorrect power supply

Use only the appropriate charger approved for the C-MAC Pocket Monitor.

Expected outcome: The charger and cable are physically intact and correct for the device.

If a damaged or incorrect charger is found, replace it with the proper charger and retest. If charging resumes normally, stop troubleshooting.

Confirm the Wall Outlet Is Working

Plug the charger into a known-good outlet.

Avoid using an outlet controlled by a wall switch, overloaded power strip, or questionable extension cord.

Expected outcome: The charger receives reliable facility power.

If the monitor charges normally from another outlet, document the failed outlet condition and stop troubleshooting the device.

Check the Charging Connection at the Monitor

Connect the charger firmly to the Pocket Monitor.

Inspect the monitor’s charging port externally for:

  • Debris
  • Bent contacts
  • Loose fit
  • Cracked port housing
  • Fluid residue
  • Intermittent charging when the connector is moved

Do not force the connector into the port.

Expected outcome: The charger seats securely and the charging indicator appears consistently.

If charging is intermittent when the connector is moved, remove the monitor from service for port or internal connection evaluation.

Observe the Charging Indicator or Display Behavior

With the charger connected, observe whether the monitor shows normal charging status, battery icon activity, or any visible indication of charging.

Also note whether the monitor becomes unusually warm, emits odor, or shows display flicker during charging.

Expected outcome: The monitor shows normal charging behavior without heat, smell, or unstable operation.

If there is unusual heat, odor, swelling, or visible damage, disconnect power, remove the device from service, and escalate immediately.

Allow Adequate Charge Time

If the battery was fully depleted, allow the monitor to remain connected to the correct charger for a reasonable charging period before declaring failure.

Recheck whether the unit powers on and whether the battery level increases.

Expected outcome: A deeply discharged battery begins accepting charge and the battery indicator improves.

If the battery level does not increase after adequate charge time, continue troubleshooting.

Compare With a Known-Good Charger

Test the Pocket Monitor using a known-good compatible charger.

Also test the suspected charger with another compatible monitor if available and allowed by facility procedure.

Expected outcome: The issue follows either the charger or the monitor.

If the known-good charger resolves the issue, replace the faulty charger and return the monitor to service after verifying normal charging.

Check for Rapid Battery Depletion After Charging

Fully charge the monitor, then operate it off battery under normal conditions.

Observe whether the monitor:

  • Powers off quickly
  • Shows sudden battery percentage drops
  • Runs normally but for a shortened time
  • Only functions while plugged in

Expected outcome: Battery runtime is stable and appropriate for clinical use.

If runtime is consistently short after full charge, the rechargeable battery is likely degraded or the internal charging circuit may be failing.

Inspect for Environmental or Use-Related Causes

Ask whether the device was recently:

  • Stored off charger for an extended period
  • Exposed to fluid during cleaning
  • Dropped
  • Stored in extreme heat or cold
  • Used with a questionable charger
  • Left continuously connected for long periods

Expected outcome: Any external condition that could explain charging or battery damage is identified and documented.

Perform a Final Operational Check

If charging appears restored, verify:

  • Monitor powers on normally
  • Battery indicator behaves normally
  • Image displays correctly with a compatible video laryngoscope connected
  • Charger connection remains stable
  • No abnormal heat, smell, or shutdown occurs

Expected outcome: The monitor is safe and reliable for return to service.

If all checks pass, clean the device per facility procedure, document findings, and return it to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the C-MAC Pocket Monitor still will not charge, rapidly depletes after a full charge, only works while plugged in, or has an unstable charging port, common external causes have been ruled out.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. The likely causes may include a degraded rechargeable battery, damaged charging port, internal charging circuit issue, or fluid-related internal damage.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue clinical use with unreliable battery performance.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot charging or battery failure during an active airway procedure. If battery reliability is uncertain, move clinical staff to another ready video laryngoscope system before testing. Airway equipment must be ready before it is needed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Karl Storz C-MAC Pocket Monitor would not hold a charge and powered off shortly after removal from the charger."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified correct charger and known-good outlet; monitor accepted power but battery depleted rapidly after full charge attempt, indicating likely battery degradation or internal charging fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed monitor from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation/repair due to unreliable rechargeable battery performance."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result
  • Charger inspected or swapped
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Battery level before and after charging
  • Runtime after full charge attempt
  • Whether the device works while plugged in
  • Charging port condition
  • Any loose connector behavior
  • Any unusual heat, odor, swelling, or fluid residue
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery problems on a video laryngoscope monitor should be handled carefully because failure may not appear until the device is needed urgently. Start with power source, charger, cable, and port checks before suspecting internal failure. If the monitor cannot reliably charge or maintain runtime, remove it from service and document the findings clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
