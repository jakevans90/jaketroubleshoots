GE Carescape B450

Won’t Power On / Random Shutdowns

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

B450

Verify Power Source

  • Confirm the outlet is live using a known-working device.
  • If connected via a power strip or extension cord, plug the monitor directly into the wall outlet.
  • Why: Many power issues are caused by outlets or strips not supplying consistent voltage.

Check Power Cord and Connections

  • Inspect the power cord for cuts, fraying, or bent prongs.
  • Ensure the cord is fully seated in both the monitor and the wall outlet.
  • Why: Loose or damaged cords are a common cause of power failures and intermittent shutdowns.

Inspect Battery and Charging

  • Confirm the monitor battery is installed correctly and seated properly.
  • If the battery is removable, check for swelling, leakage, or corrosion.
  • Try powering the monitor with AC only (battery removed) to isolate battery vs. AC issues.
  • Why: A failing battery can prevent startup or cause random shutdowns even when AC power is applied.

Observe Power Indicators

  • Note LED behavior when attempting to power on: steady, blinking, or no light.
  • Compare with monitor documentation to interpret indicator patterns.
  • Why: LED status can indicate battery issues, AC failure, or internal faults.

Attempt Soft Reset (If Supported)

  • Some B450 units have a reset procedure—usually holding the power button for a set duration.
  • Only perform this if safe and per manufacturer guidance.
  • Why: Temporary software glitches can sometimes prevent proper startup.

Check Environmental Factors

  • Ensure monitor is not exposed to extreme temperatures, moisture, or liquids.
  • Confirm ventilation is clear—overheating can cause shutdowns.

If the Problem Persists

  • After completing the above, if the monitor still won’t power on or shuts down randomly:
  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for vendor repair or bench evaluation.
  • Why: Continuing to troubleshoot internally without proper tools or schematics risks patient safety and further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot a monitor while it is connected to a patient.
  • If a patient requires monitoring, move them to another verified working monitor first.
  • Only perform troubleshooting in a safe, non-patient environment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

  • Complaint: “Monitor will not power on / randomly shuts down during use.”
  • Cause: “Power cord inspected and seated properly. Battery removed and tested separately; LED indicator remains off. AC outlet verified as live. Environmental conditions normal. Issue persists.”
  • Resolution: “Monitor removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for vendor repair. Patient transferred to another monitor.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested with multimeter or known device
  • Power cord swapped or inspected
  • Battery removed/tested
  • LED behavior recorded
  • Monitor environment checked for temperature, moisture, and ventilation
  • Final status: Out of Service

That is successful troubleshooting.
