ZOLL R Series

Error: CHECK RECORDER

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

ZOLL

Model

R Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a ZOLL R Series CHECK RECORDER error. This condition may be caused by missing paper, incorrectly loaded paper, paper jams, recorder door problems, paper path obstruction, latch issues, or recorder hardware faults.

A recorder problem may not always stop the defibrillator from powering on, but it can affect rhythm strip printing, event documentation, code records, and confidence in device readiness.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the ZOLL R Series is being used for active patient care, do not troubleshoot the recorder while the device is needed for monitoring, pacing, cardioversion, or defibrillation.
  • Remove the unit from patient use if the recorder error affects documentation, event printing, or confidence in device readiness.
  • Use another defibrillator if patient care is active or expected.
  • Do not interrupt patient care to investigate a recorder fault.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on in a safe, non-patient-care area.
  • Confirm that the exact message displayed is CHECK RECORDER.
  • Observe whether the error appears at startup, during printing, after pressing the recorder button, or intermittently.
  • Check whether the recorder does not print, feeds paper poorly, makes motor noise without moving paper, stops partway through printing, or shows no response.

Check the Recorder Paper Supply

  • Open the recorder compartment and confirm that recorder paper is installed.
  • Check that the roll is not empty, loose, crushed, wet, contaminated, or installed backward.
  • Install a known-good ZOLL-compatible recorder paper roll if needed.
  • Close the recorder door and attempt a test print.

Check for Paper Jam or Obstruction

  • Open the recorder door and inspect the paper path.
  • Remove torn paper, folded paper, adhesive residue, labels, debris, or paper fragments.
  • Verify that the paper feeds smoothly and is not pinched.
  • Close the recorder door and run a test print.

Confirm the Recorder Door Is Fully Closed

  • Open and close the recorder door firmly.
  • Listen and feel for the latch engaging.
  • Inspect the door for cracks, broken hinges, loose latch points, or anything preventing full closure.
  • If reseating the door clears the error, complete a successful test strip before returning the device to service.

Verify Correct Paper Orientation

  • Confirm the thermal paper is loaded in the correct direction.
  • Make sure the thermal print side is positioned correctly for the recorder head.
  • Feed the paper through the recorder path as intended.
  • Run a test print and confirm that the recorder prints clearly and advances normally.

Check for Physical Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect the recorder door, latch, hinges, paper roller, and visible paper path.
  • Look for fluid contamination, heavy dust, debris, cracks, broken latch points, burnt smell, grinding, or clicking noises.
  • If damage or contamination is found, remove the unit from service.
  • Do not attempt internal disassembly during front-line troubleshooting.

Power Cycle and Recheck

  • After external checks are complete, turn the unit off.
  • Wait briefly, then power the unit back on.
  • Check whether CHECK RECORDER returns.
  • If the message clears, perform a test print and verify normal recorder function before returning the device to service.

Isolate User-Correctable vs Device-Related Failure

  • Likely external causes include an empty paper tray, incorrectly loaded paper, paper jam, recorder door not latched, damaged paper roll, or minor debris in the paper path.
  • Likely device-related causes include recorder motor failure, recorder sensor failure, door switch or latch detection issue, thermal print mechanism failure, internal recorder connection issue, or recorder control fault.
  • If all external checks pass and the error remains, treat the device as requiring service evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If CHECK RECORDER remains after paper is replaced, jams are cleared, the recorder door is confirmed closed, correct paper orientation is verified, and the unit has been power cycled, external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent recorder error may indicate a recorder assembly, sensor, latch detection, motor, thermal print mechanism, or internal control issue.

  • Remove the defibrillator from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Do not continue returning the unit to clinical use with an unresolved recorder error.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first.

Do not troubleshoot the recorder during an active code or while the defibrillator is needed for patient care. If rhythm strip documentation is required and the recorder is not reliable, use another defibrillator or alternate documentation method according to facility policy.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"ZOLL R Series displayed CHECK RECORDER error and staff reported the recorder would not print a rhythm strip during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Recorder paper path inspected. Paper roll was empty and recorder could not feed paper. No physical damage was found."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Installed new compatible recorder paper roll, verified correct orientation, closed recorder door, power cycled unit, and completed successful test print. Device returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

CHECK RECORDER is usually a practical front-line failure caused by paper, jams, door closure, or loading issues. Clinical Engineering should check those simple causes first, verify print function, and escalate when the recorder fault remains after external causes are ruled out.

Clear troubleshooting and CCR documentation protect patient care, service history, and device readiness.

That is successful troubleshooting.
