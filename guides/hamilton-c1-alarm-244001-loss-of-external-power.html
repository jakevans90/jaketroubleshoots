Hamilton C1

Alarm: 244001 Loss of external power

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Hamilton

Model

C1

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Alarm: 244001 Loss of external power on the Hamilton C1 ventilator. This alarm indicates the ventilator has lost external AC power and may be running on battery power. If external power is not restored, battery runtime may become limited and patient ventilation could be affected.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the patient is being ventilated properly.
  • Check the displayed battery status and estimated remaining battery time.
  • Notify clinical staff that the ventilator has lost external power.
  • Prepare a backup ventilator or alternate ventilation method if battery time is limited or power cannot be restored quickly.
  • Do not assume the ventilator is safe just because it continues running on battery.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the ventilator displays Alarm: 244001 Loss of external power.
  • Confirm whether the ventilator is running on internal battery.
  • Observe whether the alarm occurs immediately after plugging in, after moving the ventilator, intermittently when the cord is touched, only in one outlet, or during facility power issues.
  • Determine whether the alarm is reproducible or clearly associated with loss of AC input.

Check the AC Power Cord

  • Confirm the AC power cord is fully seated at the ventilator or external power supply connection.
  • Confirm the wall plug is fully inserted into the outlet.
  • Inspect the cord for cuts, bent prongs, loose strain relief, crushed areas, or signs of overheating.
  • Gently move the cord while observing whether external power returns or drops out.
  • If the alarm clears after reseating or replacing the cord, verify stable operation before returning the device to service.

Check the Wall Outlet

  • Plug the ventilator into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Avoid using a questionable power strip, extension cord, or loose outlet.
  • If available, test the original outlet with an approved outlet tester or another known-good device.
  • Check whether other equipment in the same area also lost power.
  • If the issue follows the outlet, notify Facilities and document the outlet location.

Check the External Power Supply or Adapter

  • Confirm the adapter or power supply is the correct type for the Hamilton C1.
  • Check that all inline power connections are fully seated.
  • Inspect the adapter for damage, loose connectors, excessive heat, smell, or cracked housing.
  • If an approved spare power supply is available, test with the spare.
  • If replacing the external power supply resolves the issue, remove the failed power supply from service.

Check for Movement-Related Power Loss

  • Ask staff whether the alarm started after moving the ventilator, transferring the patient, or switching outlets.
  • Inspect the cord routing for tension, pulling, or pinching.
  • Confirm the power cord is not being pulled loose by the ventilator stand, bed movement, or cable management.
  • Verify that power remains stable during normal positioning and transport setup.

Check Battery Status

  • Check battery charge level and any battery-related messages.
  • Confirm whether the battery is charging after external power is restored.
  • Look for signs that the battery charge is low, not recognized, or not charging.
  • If battery status is poor or charging does not resume, remove the ventilator from service after patient support is transferred.

Check Facility Power Conditions

  • Ask staff whether the room, outlet bank, transport cart, or area recently lost power.
  • Check whether the outlet is connected to emergency power if required for the clinical location.
  • Confirm whether any facility power work, generator transfer, or circuit issue occurred.
  • Document any facility-side issue that may have caused repeated external power alarms.

Confirm Alarm Clears and External Power Is Stable

  • Reconnect the ventilator to a known-good power source.
  • Confirm the Loss of external power alarm clears.
  • Confirm the battery charging indicator or external power indicator behaves normally.
  • Allow the ventilator to remain powered long enough to confirm the alarm does not return.
  • If this resolves the issue, document the cause and return the device to service if all checks pass.

If the Problem Persists

If Alarm: 244001 Loss of external power continues after checking the power cord, wall outlet, external power supply or adapter, facility power, and battery status, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent loss of external power may indicate an internal power input, charging, battery interface, or power management failure.

Remove the ventilator from service.

Label it Out of Service.

Send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a power alarm for an extended period while the ventilator is supporting an active patient. Restore external power immediately, monitor battery status closely, and move the patient to a backup ventilator or approved alternate ventilation method before continuing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Hamilton C1 ventilator reported Alarm: 244001 Loss of external power. Staff stated ventilator was running on battery while connected to wall power."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"AC power cord was loose at the external power connection. Wall outlet tested good, and battery status was acceptable after external power was restored."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated AC power cord, verified external power indicator returned, confirmed battery charging, and monitored ventilator with no recurrence of alarm. Device returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Alarm: 244001 Loss of external power should be treated as a patient safety issue because the ventilator may only be supported by battery power. Start with the simple external causes first: power cord, outlet, adapter, facility power, and battery status. If the alarm persists after those checks, remove the ventilator from service and escalate for repair. Clear CCR documentation shows what was tested, what was ruled out, and why the final decision was made.

That is successful troubleshooting.
