Teleflex AC3 Optimus

Battery Charging Or Runtime Failure

Asset Type

Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump

Manufacturer

Teleflex

Model

AC3 Optimus

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting AC3 Optimus battery charging, low runtime, or power transition issues caused by external power, battery, or charging problems.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the AC3 Optimus is not supporting an active patient before troubleshooting the battery or power system.

If the pump is in clinical use, have clinical staff transfer support to another verified intra-aortic balloon pump before Clinical Engineering evaluates the device.

Expected outcome: The patient is supported safely and troubleshooting is performed only on a non-patient-use device.

Verify the Reported Battery Problem

Ask clinical staff what was observed:

  • Battery not charging
  • Short battery runtime
  • Low battery alarm
  • Device shuts down when unplugged
  • Battery icon does not change while connected to AC power
  • Pump does not transition smoothly from AC to battery power

Expected outcome: The complaint is clearly identified before testing.

Connect the Pump to a Known Good AC Outlet

Plug the AC3 Optimus into a verified hospital-grade outlet.

Confirm the outlet has power and is not controlled by a wall switch, power strip, or loose receptacle.

Expected outcome: The pump powers normally from AC power and the charging indicator appears.

If the device begins charging normally, the issue may have been outlet-related. Document findings and stop.

Inspect the Power Cord and AC Connection

Check the power cord for cuts, bent blades, loose strain relief, damaged insulation, or evidence of liquid exposure.

Confirm the cord is fully seated at the pump and outlet.

Expected outcome: The cord is intact and secure.

If a damaged or loose cord is found, remove the cord from use, replace it with an approved compatible cord, retest charging, document the correction, and stop if resolved.

Check for External Power Indicators

With the pump connected to AC power, verify that the device shows normal AC power or charging status.

Look for abnormal behavior such as:

  • No AC power indication
  • No battery charging symbol
  • Repeated low battery messages while plugged in
  • Flickering power status
  • Alarm when cord is moved

Expected outcome: AC power is recognized consistently.

If AC status drops out when the cord or connector is moved, suspect a power cord, inlet, or connector issue and remove the device from service.

Allow Adequate Charge Time

If the battery was fully depleted, leave the AC3 Optimus connected to known good AC power long enough for the battery to recover and charge.

Recheck the battery status after charging.

Expected outcome: Battery charge level increases and low battery messages clear.

If the battery begins charging and runtime is acceptable after charging, return the device to service after normal safety and functional checks.

Perform a Controlled Battery Runtime Check

Only when the device is off patient use, disconnect AC power and observe whether the pump remains powered on battery.

Monitor the battery level and any low battery or shutdown behavior.

Expected outcome: The device remains powered on battery without immediate shutdown or rapid battery drop.

If the device shuts down quickly, gives an immediate low battery alarm, or runtime is far below expected clinical use needs, continue troubleshooting.

Check Battery Age and Maintenance History

Review the equipment record for battery age, prior battery replacements, repeated low-runtime complaints, or missed battery maintenance.

Expected outcome: Battery condition is evaluated against service history.

If the battery is old, repeatedly failing runtime checks, or has a history of charge complaints, battery replacement or vendor/bench evaluation is appropriate.

Inspect for Heat, Odor, Swelling, or Physical Damage

Check the pump exterior and battery area without opening internal compartments beyond normal facility-approved access.

Look for:

  • Unusual heat
  • Burning smell
  • Battery swelling
  • Cracked housing
  • Liquid intrusion
  • Corrosion near accessible contacts or connectors

Expected outcome: No signs of unsafe battery or charging system damage are present.

If heat, odor, swelling, corrosion, or liquid damage is found, stop troubleshooting immediately, remove the pump from service, label it Out of Service, and escalate for repair.

Confirm Charging Behavior After Reseating External Connections

Reconnect the AC cord firmly and verify the pump recognizes AC power and resumes charging.

Avoid repeated power cycling or unnecessary stress testing.

Expected outcome: Charging status remains stable and battery level improves.

If charging remains intermittent or absent with known good AC power and cord, suspect an internal charging circuit, battery pack, power inlet, or internal connection issue.

Check for Related Alarms or System Messages

Record any battery, charger, power supply, or service-related messages displayed by the AC3 Optimus.

Expected outcome: Alarm behavior is documented accurately for repair escalation.

If the device displays repeated battery or charger errors, remove it from service for bench evaluation.

Do Not Return the Pump Based on AC Operation Alone

Confirm both AC operation and battery backup operation before returning the pump to service.

A pump that runs on AC but fails immediately on battery should not be used clinically.

Expected outcome: The device is only returned to service if both AC and battery performance are acceptable.

If the Problem Persists

If known good AC power, power cord condition, charge time, and external checks do not resolve the issue, common external causes have been ruled out.

The problem is likely related to the internal battery pack, charging circuit, power inlet, internal wiring, or power management system.

The AC3 Optimus should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, battery replacement, or bench evaluation according to facility procedure

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. An intra-aortic balloon pump with unreliable battery backup should not be returned to patient care.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot battery runtime or charging issues while the pump is supporting a patient. Battery backup is a critical safety function during transport, power interruption, and emergency movement. Move the patient to another verified pump first when support is required.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Teleflex AC3 Optimus displayed a low battery condition and had very short battery runtime when unplugged from AC power."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified AC outlet and power cord were functional, but the pump would not maintain acceptable battery operation after charging."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the AC3 Optimus from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for battery/charging system bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested with known good power
  • Power cord inspected or swapped
  • AC power indicator status
  • Battery charge indicator behavior
  • Runtime observed during controlled battery check
  • Low battery or charger alarms displayed
  • Whether device shut down when unplugged
  • Battery age or replacement history
  • Unusual heat, odor, swelling, or corrosion
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery reliability is a patient safety issue on an intra-aortic balloon pump. Troubleshooting should begin with simple external checks, confirm safe AC operation, then verify battery backup performance. If runtime or charging remains unreliable, escalation is the correct action. Clear CCR documentation helps repair teams identify whether the failure followed the battery, charger, power cord, or device.

That is successful troubleshooting.
