Ambu King Vision aBlade

Low Battery / Flashing Battery Indicator

Asset Type

Video Laryngoscope

Manufacturer

Ambu

Model

King Vision aBlade

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting low battery warnings, flashing battery indicators, weak display power, or intermittent shutdown during King Vision aBlade use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the King Vision aBlade is not being relied on for an active airway attempt before troubleshooting.

If the device is being used during a procedure and the battery indicator is flashing or the display becomes unstable, clinical staff should switch to another ready airway device immediately.

Expected outcome: Patient care is moved to a dependable device before battery-related troubleshooting continues.

Verify the Reported Battery Indicator

Power on the display and observe the battery icon or flashing battery indicator.

Confirm whether the issue is:

  • Flashing battery icon
  • Low battery message or symbol
  • Display powers on briefly then shuts off
  • Display dims or flickers
  • Unit fails to power on after a low battery warning

Expected outcome: The battery-related symptom is confirmed and documented.

Replace the Batteries With New Approved Batteries

Remove the battery compartment cover and replace the batteries with new, correct-type batteries as specified for the device.

Do not reuse partially depleted batteries from another device.

Expected outcome: The display powers on normally and the low battery indicator clears.

If this resolves the issue, return the device to service after functional verification and stop troubleshooting.

Check Battery Orientation

Verify each battery is installed in the correct polarity direction.

Incorrect battery orientation may allow no power, intermittent power, or a continued low battery indication.

Expected outcome: Batteries are seated correctly and the display powers on without a flashing battery warning.

Inspect the Battery Compartment

Remove the batteries and inspect the battery compartment for:

  • Corrosion
  • Bent or recessed contacts
  • Fluid residue
  • Loose springs
  • Cracked plastic
  • Evidence of cleaning solution intrusion

Expected outcome: Battery contacts are clean, dry, intact, and making proper contact.

If corrosion, fluid intrusion, or damaged contacts are found, remove the device from service.

Verify the Battery Cover Secures Properly

Reinstall the battery cover and confirm it closes fully without looseness or movement.

A loose cover can allow momentary battery contact loss during handling.

Expected outcome: Battery compartment remains secure during normal handling.

Power Cycle the Device

Turn the display off, wait briefly, and power it back on after installing fresh batteries.

Observe whether the low battery indicator returns immediately.

Expected outcome: The unit starts normally and the battery indicator remains stable.

Check for Intermittent Power During Gentle Handling

With fresh batteries installed, gently move the display as it would be handled during setup.

Do not drop, strike, or stress the device.

Watch for flickering, shutdown, or battery indicator changes.

Expected outcome: Display remains powered and stable during normal handling.

If the display flickers or shuts off, suspect battery contact damage or an internal power issue.

Inspect the Display and Blade Connection Area

Confirm the aBlade connection area is clean, dry, and free of visible contamination.

A connection issue is not usually the primary cause of a low battery indicator, but poor connection or moisture can create confusing power or display behavior.

Expected outcome: Device connection points appear clean and undamaged.

Perform a Basic Functional Check

Attach a known-good compatible aBlade and power on the device.

Confirm:

  • Display turns on normally
  • Image appears
  • Battery indicator is stable
  • No flickering occurs
  • Device remains powered during normal movement

Expected outcome: The device passes a practical operational check before being returned to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the low battery or flashing battery indicator continues after installing new batteries, checking polarity, inspecting contacts, and verifying the battery cover, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, replacement evaluation, or manufacturer-supported service review

Do not continue troubleshooting into internal circuitry during clinical-area response. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a low battery video laryngoscope during an active airway attempt. Have clinical staff move to another ready video laryngoscope or backup airway device first, then evaluate the reported unit only when patient care is safe.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Ambu King Vision aBlade display showed a flashing low battery indicator during airway setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Batteries were depleted, and the low battery indicator cleared after new batteries were installed with correct polarity."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced batteries, inspected battery contacts and cover, verified stable power and video image with a compatible aBlade, and returned the device to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery indicator behavior observed
  • Whether batteries were replaced
  • Battery type used
  • Battery polarity verified
  • Battery compartment condition
  • Corrosion or fluid intrusion noted
  • Battery cover condition
  • Display flicker or shutdown observed
  • Blade/display connection checked
  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Low battery complaints should be handled logically and safely. Start with patient safety, then verify the indicator, replace batteries, inspect contacts, and check for stable operation. If fresh batteries do not resolve the issue, remove the device from service and document the findings clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
