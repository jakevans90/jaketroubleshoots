Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: FLOW SENSOR CAL DUE !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the alarm FLOW SENSOR CAL DUE ! indicating that flow sensor calibration is overdue or was reset. This condition can lead to inaccurate flow and volume readings, potentially impacting ventilation reliability and patient safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the machine from active patient use or transition to another anesthesia machine
  • Ensure patient ventilation is maintained on a safe, alternate device
  • Do not perform calibration while connected to a patient

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the unit and confirm the FLOW SENSOR CAL DUE ! alarm is present
  • Check if the alarm appears at startup
  • Determine if the alarm followed a cable reconnection
  • Observe if flow/volume readings are present but potentially inaccurate

Check Flow Sensor Cable Connection

  • Inspect the flow sensor cable at both ends
  • Ensure connectors are fully seated
  • Look for bent pins or physical damage
  • Reseat the cable securely

Inspect Flow Sensor and Breathing Circuit

  • Check for moisture or condensation in the flow sensor
  • Inspect for debris or contamination
  • Verify proper installation of the sensor
  • Confirm breathing circuit integrity

Perform Flow Sensor Calibration

  • Place the machine in standby mode
  • Ensure no patient is connected
  • Follow on-screen prompts to complete calibration
  • Allow the process to finish without interruption

Confirm Alarm Clears and Function Restores

  • Verify the alarm is no longer displayed
  • Confirm normal flow and volume readings
  • Observe stable operation during testing

If Alarm Persists After Calibration

  • Repeat the calibration procedure
  • Try a known-good flow sensor if available
  • Recheck cable connections and condition
  • Assess for possible internal sensor or circuit issue

If the Problem Persists

If external causes have been ruled out and calibration cannot be completed or the alarm continues:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or calibrate the flow sensor on an active patient. Always transition the patient to a backup device and perform calibration in standby mode to ensure accuracy and safety.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed FLOW SENSOR CAL DUE ! alarm."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Flow sensor required calibration due to elapsed interval or cable reconnection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified connections, performed flow sensor calibration successfully, alarm cleared, and unit returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Calibration-related alarms are not failures, but they must be addressed promptly. Accurate flow measurements are critical for safe ventilation. Completing calibration and escalating when necessary ensures reliable operation and patient safety.

That is successful troubleshooting.
