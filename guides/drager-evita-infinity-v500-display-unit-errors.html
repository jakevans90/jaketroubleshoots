Drager Evita Infinity V500

Display Unit or Control Panel Communication Errors

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Evita Infinity V500

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the Drager Evita Infinity V500 ventilator:

  • Shows errors indicating the display unit or control panel is not communicating properly with the main system.
  • Has frozen or unresponsive touch controls.
  • Displays intermittent or delayed parameter updates.

The focus is on external, logical troubleshooting steps first, before assuming internal board-level failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Proper Power and Battery Status

  • Verify the ventilator is plugged into a working wall outlet.
  • Check battery charge level; a low battery can sometimes trigger communication warnings.
  • Confirm the device powers on normally.
  • Why: Power instability can mimic control panel faults.

Inspect Physical Connections

  • Confirm the display unit cable (if removable) is fully seated at both ends.
  • Check for bent pins, loose connectors, or visible damage.
  • Why: Loose or damaged connectors are a common external cause of communication errors.

Check for Environmental Interference

  • Ensure no strong electromagnetic interference (EMI) sources are near the ventilator (large motors, cordless phones, wireless routers).
  • Move the device slightly and observe if errors persist.
  • Why: EMI can temporarily disrupt communication between modules.

Restart the Device Safely

  • Perform a controlled shutdown following the ventilator’s standard procedure.
  • Wait ~30 seconds, then power it back on.
  • Observe if the display unit initializes correctly and errors clear.
  • Why: Some communication faults are temporary and reset after power cycling.

Verify Software Status

  • Confirm the device firmware is up to date (per hospital policy).
  • Look for any logs or error codes related to display module communication.
  • Why: Firmware inconsistencies can cause intermittent communication failures.

Swap Display Unit (If Available)

  • If a spare display unit is available, connect it to the ventilator.
  • Confirm if errors persist.
  • Why: Helps isolate whether the fault is in the display or main system.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (display board or main control board fault).

Action:

  • Remove the ventilator from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send for manufacturer repair or bench evaluation.

Reminder: Knowing when to escalate prevents further device damage or patient risk.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not troubleshoot on an active patient.
  • Move the patient to another ventilator before any in-depth evaluation.
  • Keep patient safety the highest priority while troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Display panel shows communication error and is unresponsive.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed that display cable was seated but errors persisted after power cycling; no visible external damage; errors repeated intermittently.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent to Biomedical Engineering / vendor for bench evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet voltage verified
  • Display cable and connectors inspected
  • Device power cycled
  • Error codes logged
  • Firmware version noted
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures patient safety and preserves device integrity. External checks often resolve or isolate communication errors. Proper documentation and timely escalation protect both patients and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
