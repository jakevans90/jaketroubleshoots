GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B850 Patient Monitor

PDM Connection Loss

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B850

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the CARESCAPE B850 loses connection to its Parameter Display Module (PDM). Symptoms may include missing patient parameters on the PDM, intermittent data display, or a PDM offline message. Many connection issues are caused by external factors such as cabling, network communication, or module placement, before considering internal hardware or software faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Device Power

  • Ensure both the B850 monitor and the PDM are powered on.
  • Check indicator LEDs on both devices for normal operation.

Why: A powered-off module will appear disconnected.

Check Connection Cables

  • Inspect PDM connection cables for damage or loose connections.
  • Reseat the cable at both the monitor and PDM ends.

Why: Physical connection issues are the most common cause of communication loss.

Confirm Network/Hub Connectivity

  • If the PDM communicates via network or bus, ensure it is connected to the correct port or hub.
  • Swap to a known good cable or port if needed.

Why: Network interruptions or bad ports can simulate PDM failure.

Check for Module Placement Issues

  • Ensure the PDM is properly seated or docked if applicable.
  • Verify no obstructions or mechanical interference with the docking mechanism.

Why: Poor seating can interrupt data transmission without triggering an alarm.

Restart the PDM

  • Power cycle the PDM and allow it to reconnect to the B850.
  • Monitor connection status messages.

Why: Software glitches can often be cleared by a simple restart.

Review Monitor Logs

  • Access the monitor’s event or system log to check for repeated communication errors.
  • Note any timestamps or error codes.

Why: Logs help determine if the issue is intermittent or persistent.

If the Problem Persists

External connections, power, and placement have been verified. The issue is likely internal (PDM hardware or B850 communication board).

  • Remove the PDM or monitor from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents patient risk and avoids further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to another monitor or module if needed before attempting reconnection. Only reconnect or replace modules when it is safe to do so.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“User reports that the PDM is not displaying patient parameters and shows “offline.””

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed that the PDM cable was partially loose and the module was not properly seated. Connection remained unstable after reseating.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Verified cable integrity and port function. Restarted the PDM; issue persisted. Device labeled Out of Service and sent for repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and functioning
  • Cables swapped or reseated
  • Indicator LEDs status
  • PDM docking alignment
  • Error messages or log codes

Final Thought

Patient safety must always come first. Use a systematic, logic-based approach when troubleshooting, verifying external connections and configurations before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation ensures continuity and supports cost-effective maintenance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
