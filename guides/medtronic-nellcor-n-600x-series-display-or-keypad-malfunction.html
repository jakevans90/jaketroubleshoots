Medtronic Nellcor N-600x Series

Display or Keypad Malfunction

Asset Type

Pulse Oximeter

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Nellcor N-600x Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting unresponsive buttons, frozen screen, dim display, or incorrect input response caused by power, settings, or external component issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use if the display or controls are not functioning properly.
  • Transfer monitoring to a working unit.

Expected: Patient monitoring continues safely on alternate equipment
Why it matters: Loss of display or controls prevents reliable patient assessment

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the unit and confirm the malfunction.
  • Observe blank or dim display.
  • Check for frozen or lagging screen.
  • Test if buttons are not responding or responding incorrectly.

Expected: Issue is clearly identified and reproducible
Why it matters: Confirms a true device issue vs user error or temporary glitch

Check Power Source and Battery Status

  • Plug into a known good outlet.
  • Verify power indicator lights.
  • Check battery icon or status.

Expected: Device powers on with stable display brightness
Why it matters: Low voltage or unstable power can cause display and keypad issues

Perform a Power Cycle (Most Common Fix)

  • Turn off the unit.
  • Disconnect from AC power.
  • Wait 30–60 seconds.
  • Reconnect and power back on.

Expected: Display and keypad return to normal operation
Why it matters: Clears temporary software or interface lockups

Inspect Keypad for Physical Obstruction or Damage

  • Look for stuck or damaged buttons.
  • Check for fluid intrusion, debris, or worn overlay.
  • Gently press each button to assess tactile response.

Expected: Buttons move freely and respond consistently
Why it matters: Physical wear or contamination is a common failure point

Check Display Visibility and Settings

  • Adjust brightness (if available).
  • View screen from different angles.
  • Check for partial display or fading segments.

Expected: Display is clearly visible and stable
Why it matters: Backlight or contrast issues can mimic full display failure

Disconnect Accessories and External Connections

  • Remove SpO₂ sensor and cables.
  • Disconnect any external modules or communication cables.

Expected: No change in display or keypad behavior
Why it matters: Faulty accessories can sometimes affect system responsiveness

Inspect for Signs of Liquid Damage or Environmental Exposure

  • Look for discoloration, residue, or corrosion.
  • Check for unusual odor or sticky buttons.

Expected: No signs of contamination or damage
Why it matters: Fluid intrusion commonly affects keypads and display circuits

If the Problem Persists

If all external and basic functional checks have been completed and the issue remains, the problem is likely internal (display module, keypad membrane, or control board failure).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and is part of proper Clinical Engineering troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot display or keypad issues while the device is actively monitoring a patient. Always transfer the patient first to ensure continuous monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nursing reported pulse oximeter screen blank and buttons not responding during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Keypad unresponsive and display remained blank after power cycle; suspected internal display or keypad failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service, labeled out of service, and sent to Biomed shop for further evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Power outlet tested and verified
  • Battery status and charging behavior
  • Display condition (blank, dim, partial)
  • Keypad response consistency
  • Any visible damage or contamination
  • Accessories disconnected during testing
  • Final device status (removed from service or returned to use)

Final Thought

Display and keypad issues can often appear severe but are frequently caused by simple power or external factors. Always start with basic checks, rule out environmental causes, and escalate appropriately. Protecting patient safety and documenting clearly are just as important as resolving the issue.

That is successful troubleshooting.
