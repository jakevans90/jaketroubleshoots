Baxter SIGMA Spectrum

Error: 347 Check Battery

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Baxter

Model

SIGMA Spectrum

What This Guide Helps With

Error 347 Check Battery indicating defective battery causing power instability, failure to hold charge, or unexpected shutdown during operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use immediately if the error is active
  • Transfer infusion to a known working pump
  • Confirm therapy continues without interruption

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error 347 Check Battery is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup, during operation, or when unplugged)
  • Check for rapid battery drain, inability to run on battery, or unexpected shutdowns

Check AC Power Source and Power Adapter

  • Plug the pump into a known working outlet
  • Ensure the power supply is securely connected
  • Verify indicator lights and normal AC operation

Inspect Battery Installation

  • Ensure the battery is properly seated (if accessible without disassembly)
  • Check for loose fit, corrosion, swelling, or physical damage
  • Confirm secure connections

Check Charging Behavior

  • Leave the pump connected to AC power for a full charging period
  • Observe charge indicators and behavior
  • Check if the error persists after charging

Attempt Operation on Battery Only

  • Disconnect AC power after charging
  • Operate the pump using battery power only
  • Monitor for immediate shutdown or instability

Swap with Known Good Battery

  • Install a known working battery if available
  • Observe whether the error clears
  • Confirm normal operation if replaced

If the Problem Persists

If Error 347 remains after battery replacement and external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • This indicates a possible internal charging or power management failure
  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before evaluating battery or power-related issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 347 Check Battery. Unit shuts down when unplugged."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery failed to hold charge and error persisted until replaced. Verified with known good battery."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced defective battery and verified proper charging and operation. Returned unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery-related errors directly impact patient safety due to the risk of therapy interruption. Always verify reliable power operation and escalate promptly when internal faults are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
