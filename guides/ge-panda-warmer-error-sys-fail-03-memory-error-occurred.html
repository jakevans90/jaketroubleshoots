GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 03 Memory Error Occurred

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 03 when a memory error prevents reliable warmer control, startup, settings retention, or safe clinical operation.

A persistent memory-related system failure may affect heater control, stored settings, alarm reliability, or the warmer's ability to safely support infant thermal care.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the Panda Warmer from patient use if Sys Fail 03 is displayed during setup or while supporting an infant.
  • Transfer the patient to a known-working warming device according to clinical procedure.
  • Do not rely on a warmer displaying an unresolved system failure for patient thermal support.

Expected outcome: The infant is supported by alternate warming equipment before troubleshooting begins.

Why it matters: A memory-related system failure may affect control operation, stored settings, or the warmer's ability to function reliably.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the unit removed from patient use, confirm that the display shows Sys Fail 03.
  • Document whether the error occurs during initial power-up, after self-test, after settings are entered, intermittently during operation, or after the unit has been moved or power-cycled.
  • Check for associated symptoms such as failure to complete startup, unavailable controls, settings not being retained, repeated system-failure messages, alarms, or interrupted heater operation.

Expected outcome: The error is reproduced or its occurrence is clearly documented.

Why it matters: This confirms an actual system-reported memory condition rather than an isolated user, power, or setup issue.

3. Check Power Source and External Conditions

  • Verify the warmer is connected to a known-good, properly grounded outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, strain relief, and receptacle for damage, looseness, contamination, or evidence of overheating.
  • After confirming safe power, power-cycle the unit once using the approved startup procedure while it remains off patient use.

Expected outcome: The unit receives stable power and either starts normally or continues to display Sys Fail 03.

Why it matters: Unstable power or damaged external power components can cause abnormal startup behavior and should be ruled out before internal failure is suspected.

4. Inspect the Warmer for External Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect the Panda Warmer for signs of impact, fluid intrusion, heavy contamination, loose external panels, unusual odor, or evidence of overheating.
  • Ask clinical staff whether the unit was recently cleaned, moved, dropped, exposed to fluid, or experienced a power interruption before the error occurred.
  • Do not continue testing a device with visible damage, contamination, fluid intrusion, or evidence of overheating.

Expected outcome: No external condition is found that could reasonably explain unsafe or unreliable operation, or the unit is appropriately escalated due to external damage.

Why it matters: Physical damage or fluid exposure may contribute to control-system failures and supports removal from service.

5. Determine Whether the Error Clears or Returns

  • After external checks are complete, restart the warmer while it remains off patient use.
  • Confirm whether Sys Fail 03 clears and the unit completes startup normally.
  • Confirm whether the error immediately returns, recurs intermittently, or prevents normal operation.
  • If the error does not return, perform all required operational, electrical safety, and performance verification before considering return to service.

Expected outcome: The condition is either resolved and verified or confirmed as persistent.

6. Isolate the Condition as Device-Related

  • If stable power, external condition, and normal startup procedures have been verified but Sys Fail 03 remains present, treat the issue as an internal memory or control-system failure.
  • The reported condition is consistent with a memory problem associated with the control system, including possible NVRAM retention, application flash, stored-default data, or Control PCA failure.
  • Do not attempt further internal repair activities unless qualified and authorized under approved service procedures.

Expected outcome: The warmer is correctly identified as requiring qualified bench evaluation rather than continued external troubleshooting.

Why it matters: Persistent memory errors can prevent confidence in heater control and alarm reliability.

7. Stop When Resolved

  • If the error clears and the warmer successfully passes required operational verification, electrical safety checks, and performance testing according to facility procedure, document the findings and return the unit to service.
  • Do not return the warmer to clinical use based only on disappearance of the message without confirming proper function.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 03 remains after external inspection and power verification, common external causes have been ruled out and the condition strongly indicates an internal memory or Control PCA-related failure.

Remove the infant warmer from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for qualified bench evaluation or repair.

Authorized service personnel may need to evaluate memory-retention components, restore or reprogram application memory, reset system defaults in Service Mode, or replace the Control PCA in accordance with approved service documentation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a Sys Fail 03 condition while an infant is relying on the Panda Warmer for thermal support. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, because unresolved system-memory errors may affect safe control operation or alarm reliability.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 03 during startup. Unit could not be confirmed reliable for infant warming and was removed from clinical use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified known-good outlet and inspected external power components and warmer condition with no external issue identified. Sys Fail 03 returned after restart, indicating likely internal memory, NVRAM retention, application flash, or Control PCA-related failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from service and labeled Out of Service for qualified bench evaluation and repair. Unit not returned to clinical use pending correction of the memory fault and successful post-repair operational and safety verification."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Sys Fail 03 message on a Panda Warmer should be treated as a reliability and patient-safety concern, not simply a nuisance error. Confirm the reported condition, rule out external causes, and escalate persistent memory errors appropriately. Clear CCR documentation supports safe equipment decisions and effective repair follow-up.

That is successful troubleshooting.
