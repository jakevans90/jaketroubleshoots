GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B650 Patient Monitor

NIBP Pump Failure

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B650

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting Non-Invasive Blood Pressure (NIBP) pump failures on the CARESCAPE B650. Symptoms may include NIBP measurement failures, pump error alarms, or an inability to inflate the cuff. Most issues are caused by external connections, cuff problems, or simple device settings rather than internal pump failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power and Device Status

  • Ensure the monitor is plugged in and powered on.
  • Check for low battery warnings if running on internal battery.
  • Why: Insufficient power can prevent the NIBP pump from operating properly.

Inspect NIBP Cuff and Tubing

  • Examine cuff for tears, leaks, or improper attachment.
  • Check tubing for kinks, blockages, or disconnections.
  • Swap with a known-good cuff and tubing if available.
  • Why: Cuff or tubing issues are the most common cause of pump failure.

Check Connector Ports

  • Ensure the NIBP hose is securely connected to the monitor’s NIBP port.
  • Inspect connector pins for damage or debris.
  • Why: Loose or dirty connections can prevent pump activation.

Run Self-Test / NIBP Calibration Check

  • Access the monitor’s built-in self-test function for NIBP.
  • Observe error codes or messages displayed.
  • Why: Confirms whether the issue is detected by the system or external.

Verify Settings and Alarms

  • Confirm the NIBP measurement interval is appropriate and not in “manual only” mode.
  • Check if alarms or restrictions are preventing automatic measurements.
  • Why: Incorrect settings can appear as pump failure.

Inspect Environmental Factors

  • Ensure the monitor is not in a high-interference environment.
  • Avoid situations where the cuff is placed over clothing that restricts inflation.
  • Why: External factors can impede proper cuff inflation.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out, suggesting internal NIBP pump or board failure.

Action:

  • Remove monitor from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to escalate is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt NIBP measurements on patients with a suspected pump failure.
  • Use an alternative monitor for patient safety until resolved.
  • Always ensure cuff placement is correct and patient is relaxed for accurate readings.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Monitor displays NIBP pump failure and will not inflate cuff.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed cuff tubing intact, connected, but NIBP pump does not activate; self-test confirms pump error.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Removed monitor from service, labeled Out of Service, sent to Biomedical Repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested?
  • Cables swapped?
  • NIBP cuff integrity?
  • Error codes displayed?
  • Self-test results?
  • Final device status?

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Troubleshooting should follow logical steps from external checks to escalation. Proper documentation ensures repeatability and accountability, and knowing when to stop prevents harm.

That is successful troubleshooting.
