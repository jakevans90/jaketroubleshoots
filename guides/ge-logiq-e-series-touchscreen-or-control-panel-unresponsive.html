GE Healthcare LOGIQ E Series

Touchscreen or Control Panel Unresponsive

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

LOGIQ E Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the GE LOGIQ E Series ultrasound system touchscreen or control panel becomes unresponsive. Symptoms may include:

  • Touchscreen does not respond to input
  • Buttons or soft keys do not activate
  • Trackball or knobs do not respond
  • Partial control panel response or intermittent freezing

Many control interface issues are caused by software lockups, connection issues, or environmental factors rather than internal hardware failure. This guide focuses on logical external checks first.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify the system is powered on and fully booted.

  • Confirm the ultrasound system has completed its startup sequence.
  • During boot or system initialization, the touchscreen and controls may temporarily be unresponsive.

Check for system freeze or software lockup.

  • Observe whether the screen image is frozen or if menus cannot be accessed.
  • Attempt to move the trackball or press several control panel buttons to determine if the issue affects the entire interface or only the touchscreen.

Perform a soft restart of the system.

  • If the system appears frozen but still powered, perform a normal system shutdown using the power button or shutdown menu if accessible.
  • Wait approximately 30 seconds and power the unit back on.
  • Temporary software faults often resolve after a restart.

Inspect the touchscreen surface.

  • Ensure the touchscreen is clean and free of gel, fluid, or debris.
  • Ultrasound gel or moisture can interfere with touch detection.
  • Clean the surface using an approved hospital disinfectant wipe and dry with a lint-free cloth.

Check for external obstructions.

  • Verify that no protective covers, plastic films, or accessories are interfering with the touchscreen or control panel.

Test additional input controls.

  • Operate the trackball, rotary knobs, and physical buttons.
  • If these inputs work but the touchscreen does not respond, the issue may be isolated to the touchscreen component.

Verify USB peripherals if present.

  • Some LOGIQ systems use USB-connected keyboards or input devices.
  • Disconnect and reconnect any external USB accessories that could interfere with system input devices.

Power cycle the system completely.

  • Shut the system down fully.
  • Disconnect AC power from the wall outlet for approximately 60 seconds.
  • Reconnect power and restart the system to clear possible hardware interface faults.

Observe for error messages or abnormal behavior.

  • Watch for warning messages, unusual sounds, or repeated freezing during reboot.
  • These observations may indicate deeper hardware or operating system issues.

If the Problem Persists

If the touchscreen or control panel remains unresponsive after performing the above steps, common external causes have likely been ruled out.

The issue may involve:

  • Touchscreen hardware failure
  • Internal control panel interface fault
  • System motherboard or I/O board issue
  • Software corruption

At this point the device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or scheduled for bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop and escalate is part of proper troubleshooting and protects both the equipment and patient care operations.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an ultrasound system while it is actively being used on a patient.

Ensure the patient exam is safely transferred to another ultrasound unit before performing restarts or power cycling.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“User reported that the ultrasound touchscreen and control panel were not responding during an exam. System appeared frozen.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“System software appeared locked up. Touchscreen unresponsive while other system functions were frozen.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Performed full system restart and power cycle. Touchscreen functionality restored. Unit tested with basic imaging functions and returned to service.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • System boot status verified
  • Touchscreen surface inspected and cleaned
  • Trackball and buttons tested
  • USB peripherals checked
  • System power cycled
  • Error messages observed (if any)
  • Final device status documented

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting begins with simple, external checks before assuming internal hardware failure. Restarting the system, checking for environmental interference, and verifying user inputs can resolve many interface problems. When these steps do not correct the issue, removing the device from service and escalating for repair ensures patient safety and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
