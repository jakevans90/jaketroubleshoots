STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: ROM Failure – Main control board read-only memory failure

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Alarm: ROM Failure – Main control board read-only memory failure, which causes the sterilizer to abort cycles and prevents proper system operation due to failure of critical firmware storage.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service immediately if this alarm occurs
  • Do not use instruments from an aborted cycle
  • Ensure no questionable loads are released for use

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows ROM Failure
  • Observe if the cycle automatically aborts
  • Note if the alarm occurs at startup or during operation
  • Check if the unit fails to initialize or stops unexpectedly

Check Power Stability and Supply

  • Verify the unit is connected to a stable power source
  • Check for recent power interruptions or fluctuations
  • Confirm outlet voltage is within normal operating range if possible

Perform a Controlled Power Cycle

  • Power the unit OFF completely
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Restore power and allow the unit to reboot

Inspect External Connections to Control System

  • Check for loose wiring at accessible panels
  • Look for signs of damage, moisture, or contamination near control electronics
  • Ensure all visible connections are secure

Determine if Alarm Persists

  • If alarm returns after reset, external causes are unlikely
  • Recognize that ROM failure indicates internal control board fault
  • Confirm consistent and repeatable alarm condition

If the Problem Persists

Once power and external causes are ruled out, a persistent ROM Failure alarm indicates internal failure of the main control circuit board.

  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the control board

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or attempt to run cycles on a sterilizer with control system errors while in clinical use. Transfer workload to a functioning sterilizer first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer displayed ROM Failure alarm and aborted cycle during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Persistent ROM Failure alarm after power cycle indicating internal control board memory failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified alarm, checked power stability, performed reset. Alarm persisted. Removed unit from service and scheduled control board repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

ROM Failure alarms indicate a critical failure within the sterilizer’s control system. Proper troubleshooting means verifying the condition, ruling out external causes, and escalating appropriately to ensure patient safety and device reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
