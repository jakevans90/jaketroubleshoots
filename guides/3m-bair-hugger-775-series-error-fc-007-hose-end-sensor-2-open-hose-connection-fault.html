3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-007 Hose End Sensor 2 Open / Hose Connection Fault

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-007 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. FC-007 indicates hose end sensor 2 is open and may be caused by a disconnected hose, loose hose connection, faulty hose, damaged connector, elbow sensor issue, or related sensing circuit fault.

This error can affect hose temperature sensing and may prevent reliable warming operation. The unit should not be used for patient warming until the fault is understood, corrected, and verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Bair Hugger 775 from patient use if Error FC-007 is active, recurring, or preventing normal warming operation.
  • If the patient still requires warming therapy, have clinical staff transfer the patient to another approved warming unit.
  • Do not continue using the unit if hose temperature sensing is unreliable.
  • Do not troubleshoot the device while it is actively warming a patient unless clinical staff confirm it is safe.

Expected outcome: Patient warming therapy is maintained safely using reliable equipment.

Why it matters: A hose sensor fault may affect temperature monitoring or system control. Patient safety comes before equipment troubleshooting.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on and observe the display.
  • Confirm that Error FC-007 is present.
  • Ask staff whether the error occurred at startup, after connecting the hose, during use, or intermittently when the hose was moved.
  • Check whether the unit will run, alarm, shut down, or prevent warming operation.

Expected outcome: Error FC-007 is confirmed or can be reproduced.

Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported hose sensor fault instead of a vague “not working” complaint.

3. Check Whether the Hose Is Disconnected

  • Inspect the hose connection at the warming unit.
  • Confirm the hose is fully seated into the unit.
  • Check that the hose is not loose, partially disconnected, crooked, or pulled away from the unit.
  • Remove and reattach the hose securely.
  • Power cycle the unit if needed and check whether FC-007 clears.

Expected outcome: The hose is securely connected and the error clears.

Why it matters: The troubleshooting note specifically points to the hose being disconnected from the unit. A loose hose can appear as an open sensor condition.

4. Inspect the Hose and Connector Area

  • Inspect the hose connector for damage.
  • Look for bent, loose, contaminated, or damaged contacts.
  • Check the elbow area for cracks, poor fit, or obvious damage.
  • Inspect the hose for pinching, twisting, liquid intrusion, debris, or rough handling damage.
  • Clean obvious external debris from the connection area if appropriate for your facility workflow.

Expected outcome: Hose and connection area are clean, intact, and properly seated.

Why it matters: FC-007 means hose end sensor 2 is open. A damaged hose, connector, or elbow sensor area can interrupt the sensor circuit.

5. Try a Known-Good Hose, If Available

  • Remove the suspect hose.
  • Install a known-good compatible hose.
  • Power on the warming unit.
  • Check whether Error FC-007 clears.
  • If the error clears with the known-good hose, remove the original hose from service.

Expected outcome: The error clears with a known-good hose.

Why it matters: If the fault clears with another hose, the original hose or hose sensor assembly is the likely cause.

6. Check for Elbow Sensor Issue

  • Inspect the elbow sensor area for visible damage, poor seating, loose fit, or signs of failure.
  • Do not perform deep internal disassembly during routine floor troubleshooting.
  • If the elbow sensor is a replaceable service part in your facility workflow, replace it as appropriate.
  • After replacement, complete required calibration and functional testing before return to service.

Expected outcome: Sensor-related fault clears after correcting the elbow sensor or hose sensor issue.

Why it matters: The system is reporting an open sensor condition, meaning the unit is not seeing a valid sensor circuit.

7. Calibrate and Verify Before Return to Service

  • Perform required calibration after replacing the hose or elbow sensor as applicable.
  • Run the unit through normal operating modes.
  • Confirm Error FC-007 does not return.
  • Confirm the hose is secure and airflow is present.
  • Verify temperature operation appears normal.
  • Confirm alarms and indicators behave normally.
  • Check for unusual heat, smell, noise, or shutdown behavior.

Expected outcome: Unit passes calibration and functional verification.

Why it matters: Replacing sensing components without verifying operation can leave an unsafe or unreliable warming unit in service.

If the Problem Persists

If Error FC-007 remains after the hose is confirmed connected, the connector is inspected, a known-good hose is tested, and hose or elbow sensor issues are addressed, external and common causes have been ruled out.

At that point, the fault may involve the internal sensing circuit, connector interface, wiring path, or control electronics.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not keep cycling the unit back into use with an unresolved hose sensor fault.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a forced-air warming fault on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device first if warming therapy is still needed, then continue troubleshooting the affected unit away from patient use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 displayed Error FC-007 during startup or use. Staff reported hose end sensor 2 open message and possible hose connection issue."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Hose connection was found loose at the warming unit, causing an open hose sensor condition. Error cleared after the hose was reseated."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reattached hose securely to warming unit. Powered unit on and verified Error FC-007 cleared. Ran functional check, confirmed normal operation, and returned unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error FC-007 should be approached as a hose sensing and connection problem first. Start with patient safety, verify the exact fault, check the hose connection, then isolate the hose and sensor path before suspecting deeper internal failure. Good documentation should clearly show what was checked, what corrected the issue, and whether the unit was safe to return to service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
