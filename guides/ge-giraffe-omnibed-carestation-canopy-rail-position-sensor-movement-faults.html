GE Healthcare Giraffe OmniBed Carestation

Canopy / Rail Position Sensor or Movement Faults

Asset Type

Infant Incubator

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Giraffe OmniBed Carestation

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting canopy or rail position sensor faults and movement issues on the Giraffe OmniBed Carestation. Symptoms may include alarms related to canopy or rail positions, sensors indicating incorrect placement, or rails/canopy failing to move or lock correctly. Most problems can be traced to external obstructions, misalignment, or connection issues rather than internal electronics.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Device Is Safe to Access

  • Confirm no patient is in the incubator.
  • Move the infant to another incubator if necessary.
  • Safety first—never troubleshoot sensors while the canopy/rail is moving near a patient.

Inspect Physical Movement

  • Check the canopy and rails for smooth movement along tracks.
  • Look for obstructions such as medical tubing, wires, or linens that may impede motion.
  • Verify rails are fully seated in their guides.

Check Sensor Connections

  • Inspect wiring and connectors for loose or disconnected plugs at rail and canopy sensors.
  • Re-seat connectors to ensure good contact.
  • Look for visible signs of wear, bent pins, or corrosion.

Verify Alignment

  • Confirm canopy and rails are aligned properly with their mechanical stops.
  • Some sensors trigger alarms if the canopy or rails are not fully seated.
  • Manually move the canopy/rails through full range to check for smooth operation.

Power Cycle the Incubator

  • Turn the incubator off, wait 30 seconds, then power on.
  • Observe if position sensors reset and alarms clear.
  • Power cycling can clear temporary electronic faults.

Test Sensor Function

  • Use the incubator’s display panel to monitor position sensors while moving canopy or rails.
  • If sensor readings do not change appropriately, note which sensor is failing.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have likely been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Do not attempt internal sensor replacement without manufacturer guidance.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Always relocate infants before troubleshooting movement or sensor faults.
  • Ensure hands, tubing, and other items are clear of rails and canopy before operating manually.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Canopy and side rails alarm frequently and do not indicate correct positions.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Inspection revealed misaligned rails with connectors partially seated; no obstruction present.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Reseated all sensor connectors, aligned rails and canopy, verified full range of motion, power cycled the incubator. Sensors now register correctly, alarms cleared.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Obstructions observed
  • Connector and wiring condition
  • Indicator lights or display messages
  • Manual movement resistance or abnormal sounds
  • Final status of alarms and sensor readings

Final Thought

Patient safety and proper device function are paramount. Logical, stepwise troubleshooting helps isolate issues without risking damage to the incubator or harm to the patient. Always escalate when external checks fail and document all observations carefully.

That is successful troubleshooting.
