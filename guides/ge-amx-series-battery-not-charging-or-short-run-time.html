GE Healthcare AMX Series

Battery Not Charging or Short Run Time

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

AMX Series

What This Guide Helps With

Battery charging problems, reduced drive/run time, weak charge retention, charger connection issues, or battery-related mobile x-ray readiness concerns.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the mobile x-ray unit from clinical use if battery performance may affect imaging, transport, braking, or positioning.

Action:

  • Move the patient exam to another available mobile x-ray system if imaging is needed.
  • Do not continue using the AMX unit if it may lose power during transport, positioning, exposure setup, or return to storage.

Expected outcome: Patient imaging is not dependent on a mobile x-ray system with unreliable battery operation.

Why it matters: A mobile x-ray system with weak or unstable batteries may stop during transport, fail to position correctly, or become unavailable during patient care.

Verify the Reported Battery Problem

Confirm whether the issue is no charging, slow charging, short run time, or sudden power loss.

Action:

  • Ask the user when the problem occurs: while plugged into AC power, after being unplugged overnight, during driving, during setup before exposure, after only a few exams, or after sitting unused.
  • Check the battery charge indicator, charging symbol, warning messages, and any audible alarms.

Expected outcome: The complaint is narrowed to charging failure, battery capacity loss, user workflow issue, or intermittent power problem.

If the issue was caused by misunderstanding the charge indicator or normal low-battery behavior, educate the user, document the finding, and stop.

Confirm the Unit Is Connected to a Known-Good AC Outlet

Check the simplest external charging source first.

Action:

  • Verify the AMX power cord is fully plugged into the wall outlet and the unit.
  • Test the outlet with an outlet tester or another approved load.
  • Confirm the outlet is not controlled by a wall switch, emergency circuit behavior, loose receptacle, or failed power strip.

Expected outcome: The mobile x-ray system is connected to verified AC power.

If the unit begins charging normally on a known-good outlet, return it to service after confirming charge behavior and stop.

Inspect the Power Cord and Plug

Look for damage that could prevent charging or create a safety hazard.

Action:

  • Inspect the AC cord, plug blades, strain relief, and cord jacket.
  • Look for cuts, exposed conductors, loose plug fit, burn marks, crushed areas, or signs of overheating.
  • Confirm the cord is seated firmly and not pulling out under light movement.

Expected outcome: The power cord is safe and able to supply AC power.

If the cord is damaged, remove the unit from service until the cord or cord assembly is repaired or replaced.

Check for AC Input or Charging Indicators

Confirm whether the unit recognizes incoming AC power.

Action:

  • Plug the AMX into a verified outlet.
  • Observe the charging indicator, display message, battery icon, AC indicator, or charger status light if present.
  • Listen for abnormal clicking, repeated cycling, fan noise changes, or alarms.

Expected outcome: The unit should show some indication that AC power is present and charging is active.

If there is no AC recognition on a known-good outlet with a good cord, the issue may involve the charger input path, power supply, charger assembly, or internal connection. Remove from service and send for bench evaluation.

Allow Adequate Charging Time

Rule out incomplete charging before assuming battery failure.

Action:

  • Leave the unit plugged into verified AC power for an appropriate charge period based on local practice and device condition.
  • Confirm staff are not repeatedly unplugging the unit before it reaches full charge.
  • Check whether the unit is stored plugged in when not in use.

Expected outcome: The battery should recover enough charge for normal operation if the battery and charger are healthy.

If the unit charges normally after being left connected, educate the department on charging workflow, document the finding, and stop.

Check for High-Load Use Patterns

Short run time can be caused by heavy use rather than failure.

Action:

  • Ask how many exams are performed between charges.
  • Determine whether the unit is used heavily for long transports, repeated positioning, drive motor use, or back-to-back portable exams.
  • Compare run time to another similar AMX unit in the same department if available.

Expected outcome: The complaint is separated between abnormal battery performance and expected reduced run time under heavy workload.

If run time is similar to other units under the same use pattern, document that no failure was confirmed and recommend normal charging between uses.

Inspect Battery Status After Charging

Check whether the battery reaches and holds charge.

Action:

  • After charging, unplug the unit and observe the battery indicator.
  • Drive and position the unit in a safe area away from patients.
  • Watch for rapid charge drop, low-battery warnings, sudden shutdown, weak drive response, or system reboot.

Expected outcome: A healthy battery set should hold charge and support normal drive and positioning operation.

If the charge drops quickly or the unit shuts down soon after charging, battery capacity loss or battery circuit issues are likely.

Check for Environmental or Storage Issues

Battery life can be affected by storage habits and temperature.

Action:

  • Verify the unit is not stored in an unusually hot, cold, or poorly ventilated area.
  • Check whether the unit sits unplugged for extended periods.
  • Ask whether the unit was recently unused, relocated, or stored during construction or room changes.

Expected outcome: Storage conditions should support normal battery charging and capacity.

If storage or charging workflow caused the issue, correct the workflow, recharge fully, retest, document, and stop.

Inspect for Signs of Battery or Charging Hazard

Do not continue troubleshooting if there are safety warning signs.

Action:

  • Check externally for unusual heat, odor, smoke, battery compartment swelling, leaking, corrosion, or charging area discoloration.
  • Do not open battery compartments beyond what is allowed by local Clinical Engineering procedures.

Expected outcome: No signs of battery overheating, leakage, or electrical hazard are present.

If heat, odor, leakage, swelling, smoke, or burning smell is present, remove the unit from service immediately, label it Out of Service, follow facility battery safety procedures, and escalate for repair.

Review Recent Service or Battery Replacement History

Determine whether the issue follows a recent repair or aging battery set.

Action:

  • Check the service history for recent battery replacement, charger repair, drive complaints, blown fuses, charging faults, or repeated low-battery calls.
  • Look for repeated short-run-time complaints over several months.

Expected outcome: History helps identify whether this is a new charging issue, aging battery problem, or recurring internal fault.

If the battery set is aged, repeatedly failing, or cannot support normal use after full charging, remove from service for battery evaluation or replacement according to facility procedure.

Perform a Safe Functional Check After Charging

Confirm normal operation before returning the unit to service.

Action:

  • After charging, power the unit on.
  • Verify normal startup, battery indicator behavior, drive operation, brake release, column/arm movement, and readiness for imaging workflow.
  • Do not perform unnecessary radiation exposure unless required by local test procedure and done safely.

Expected outcome: The unit operates normally without low-battery warnings, charging faults, shutdowns, or weak drive behavior.

If the AMX passes functional checks and holds charge appropriately, return it to service and document the results.

If the Problem Persists

If the GE Healthcare AMX Series still will not charge, loses charge quickly, shuts down, or shows battery/charger warnings after outlet, cord, charging time, workflow, and external safety checks are complete, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as battery pack degradation, charger failure, power supply fault, battery wiring issue, battery management problem, or internal control fault.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair, battery evaluation, or bench testing.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A mobile x-ray system with unreliable battery performance should not remain in clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a battery or charging problem while the mobile x-ray unit is being used for patient imaging. Move the exam to another unit first. A weak battery can affect transport, positioning, and readiness even if the system still powers on. Maintain therapy continuity by making sure imaging needs are supported by another available mobile x-ray system before troubleshooting continues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the GE AMX mobile x-ray unit would not hold a charge and had very short run time after being unplugged."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified AC outlet and power cord were good, but the battery indicator dropped rapidly during functional testing after charging."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the AMX unit from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for battery and charger bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result
  • Power cord condition
  • Whether AC/charging indicator was present
  • Battery indicator level before and after charging
  • Charging time allowed
  • Run time or drive time observed
  • Any low-battery warnings or alarms
  • Any sudden shutdown or reboot
  • Any unusual heat, odor, sound, or visible damage
  • Environmental factors such as hot, cold, or poorly ventilated storage areas
  • Recent battery or charger service history
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery complaints on mobile x-ray systems should be handled logically and safely. Start with the outlet, power cord, charging behavior, storage habits, and user workflow before assuming internal failure. If the unit cannot reliably charge or support normal operation, remove it from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
