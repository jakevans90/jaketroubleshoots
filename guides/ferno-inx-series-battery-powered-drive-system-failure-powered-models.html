Ferno iNX Series

Battery or powered drive system failure (powered models)

Asset Type

Stretcher

Manufacturer

Ferno

Model

iNX Series

What This Guide Helps With

Stretcher will not power on, drive system not engaging, or battery not charging due to external power, connection, or control issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the stretcher from active patient transport before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient safely transferred to another device.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to power on the stretcher and engage the powered drive system.
  • Expected: Confirm no power, no drive engagement, or intermittent operation.
  • Why it matters: Confirms true failure vs user operation error.

Check Battery Charge Level

  • Observe battery indicator or display status (if equipped).
  • Expected: Battery shows adequate charge.
  • If low or depleted → charge fully and retest.
  • Why it matters: Low voltage is the most common cause of drive failure.
  • If resolved → stop troubleshooting.

Verify Charging Method and Connection

  • Ensure the charger or dock is properly connected and fully seated.
  • Expected: Charging indicator activates when connected.
  • Why it matters: Misalignment or incomplete connection prevents charging.
  • If corrected and charging begins → retest after charge.

Check Power Source (Outlet or Dock)

  • Test the outlet with another device or verify dock power indicators.
  • Expected: Confirmed working power source.
  • If no power → move to known-good outlet or powered dock.
  • Why it matters: Eliminates facility power issues.

Inspect Charging Cable and Contacts

  • Check for frayed cables, bent prongs or pins, and dirty or corroded contacts.
  • Expected: Clean, intact connections with proper alignment.
  • Clean contacts if dirty.
  • If damaged → remove from service.
  • Why it matters: Poor electrical contact prevents charging and operation.

Verify Battery Seating and Connection

  • Inspect battery compartment to ensure battery is fully seated and secured.
  • Expected: Battery properly installed with no looseness.
  • Why it matters: Loose batteries can cause intermittent or no power.
  • If reseated and power returns → stop troubleshooting.

Perform a Power Reset (If Applicable)

  • Turn the stretcher off, wait 10–15 seconds, then power back on.
  • Expected: System reinitializes and may restore function.
  • Why it matters: Clears minor electronic faults.

Attempt Drive Engagement Under Proper Conditions

  • Ensure brakes are disengaged, correct drive control is used, and no overload condition exists.
  • Expected: Drive motor engages normally.
  • Why it matters: Safety interlocks can prevent drive activation.

Observe for Indicators, Sounds, or Fault Behavior

  • Check for error lights or codes, clicking without movement, or no response at all.
  • Expected: Normal startup and drive response.
  • Why it matters: Helps distinguish electrical vs mechanical vs control issue.

If the Problem Persists

If all external power sources, connections, battery condition, and controls have been verified, the issue is likely internal (battery failure, motor controller, or wiring fault).

Remove the stretcher from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and is part of proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to troubleshoot a powered drive system with a patient on the stretcher. Always transfer the patient first to ensure safety.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stretcher powered drive not engaging and unit not powering on consistently."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery found depleted and not charging due to poor dock connection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Corrected docking alignment, verified charging, and confirmed normal drive operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet or dock tested
  • Battery indicator status
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Cable and contact condition
  • Drive response (none, intermittent, clicking)
  • Any error lights or codes
  • Final operational status

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple power and connection checks before assuming internal failure. Prioritizing patient safety, following a logical process, and documenting clearly ensures reliable outcomes and reduces unnecessary repairs.

That is successful troubleshooting.
