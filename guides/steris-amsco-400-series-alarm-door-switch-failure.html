STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Door Switch Failure – Door switch open while seal switch indicates sealed condition

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Door Switch Failure alarm, which occurs when the door switch indicates open while the seal pressure switch indicates a sealed condition. This mismatch causes cycle abort and prevents safe sterilizer operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service immediately
  • Do not use any load from an aborted cycle
  • Ensure no instruments are released for patient use

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows “Door Switch Failure”
  • Observe cycle abort and chamber exhaust to atmospheric pressure
  • Check if alarm persists after reset

Check Door Position and Closure

  • Open and firmly re-close the door
  • Ensure proper latch engagement
  • Confirm consistent full closure

Inspect for Mechanical Obstruction

  • Check door gasket area for debris
  • Inspect for misaligned racks or trays
  • Ensure nothing interferes with door closure

Check Door Switch (LS1/LS2) Connections

  • Inspect accessible wiring to door switch
  • Look for loose or damaged connections
  • Reseat connectors if accessible

Check Seal Operation Behavior

  • Start a cycle and observe seal inflation
  • Confirm seal activates only after door is closed
  • Watch for premature seal engagement

Evaluate Seal Pressure Switch Function

  • Observe if alarm occurs when seal pressurizes
  • Check for false or premature seal indication
  • Verify proper sequence of door then seal confirmation

Perform a Power Reset

  • Turn unit OFF
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Power unit back ON

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after all external checks, external causes have been ruled out. The issue likely involves door switch (LS1/LS2), seal pressure switch (PS1/PS2), or control system interpretation.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot door interlock issues on an active patient load. Always move instruments to a backup sterilizer and reprocess if a cycle is interrupted.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit alarming 'Door Switch Failure' during cycle; cycle aborts and chamber exhausts."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Door/seal interlock mismatch observed; door switch or seal pressure switch malfunction suspected after external checks."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified door closure and cleared obstruction; checked wiring and performed reset; issue persisted—unit removed from service for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Door and seal interlocks are critical safety systems. When conflicting signals occur, the unit is protecting against unsafe operation. Always follow external troubleshooting steps and escalate appropriately when mismatch conditions persist.

That is successful troubleshooting.
