GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B850

Alarm: Speaker Failure

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B850

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the “Speaker Failure” alarm, which results in no audible alarms, tones, or alerts. This condition prevents proper notification of patient events and compromises alarm audibility, which is critical for patient safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use if alarms are not audible
  • Transfer monitoring to a functioning unit with verified alarm output
  • Confirm alarms are active and audible on the replacement device

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the “Speaker Failure” alarm is displayed
  • Power on the monitor and observe startup behavior
  • Trigger a test alarm if available
  • Listen for any sound output or partial tones

3. Check Alarm Volume and Settings (Most Common Cause)

  • Navigate to alarm settings menu
  • Increase alarm volume to maximum
  • Ensure alarm is not muted or silenced
  • Disable any temporary silence functions

4. Inspect External Speaker Cable (If Applicable)

  • Check for external speaker or audio connections
  • Inspect cable for damage or loose connections
  • Reseat all audio-related connectors securely

5. Perform Power Cycle

  • Turn off the monitor
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power the unit back on

6. Test Audio Output Again

  • Trigger an alarm or test tone
  • Listen for proper and consistent audio output
  • Verify tones are clear and audible

7. Isolate Likely Cause

  • Determine if issue resolves after cable reseating (connection issue)
  • If no sound persists with correct settings, suspect internal speaker failure
  • If alarm remains with no output, consider processor or audio amplifier failure

If the Problem Persists

If external causes have been ruled out and the alarm persists, the issue is likely due to internal hardware failure such as the speaker, audio amplifier, or system board.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device with verified audible alarms before continuing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor displaying Speaker Failure alarm with no audible alarm tones."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"No audio output despite correct volume settings, cable checks, and power cycle. Internal speaker failure suspected."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed monitor from service and sent to biomed for repair. Audio function not restorable through external troubleshooting."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Audible alarms are a critical safety feature. Always rule out simple causes like settings or connections first, but escalate quickly when a system-reported speaker failure persists.

That is successful troubleshooting.
