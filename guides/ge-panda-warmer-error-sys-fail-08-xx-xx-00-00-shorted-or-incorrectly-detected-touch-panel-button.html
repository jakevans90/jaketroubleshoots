GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 08 [XX XX 00 00] Shorted or Incorrectly Detected Touch-Panel Button

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 08 [XX XX 00 00] when a shorted or incorrectly detected touch-panel button prevents reliable warmer control operation. This condition may occur during an incorrect Service Mode entry attempt or may indicate a true touch-panel or control-input failure that affects safe use of the warmer.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

If the Panda Warmer is supporting an infant or prepared for immediate clinical use, notify clinical staff and transfer the patient to a known-working warming device before troubleshooting.

Do not rely on an infant warmer with an unresolved system failure affecting the touch panel or control interface.

  • Expected outcome: The infant is safely supported by alternate equipment, and the affected warmer is available for evaluation without clinical risk.
  • Why it matters: A button-input or control-panel failure can interfere with reliable operation, settings verification, or access to required warmer functions.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

With the warmer removed from patient use, power the unit on and confirm the exact displayed error:

Sys Fail 08 [XX XX 00 00]

Observe and document when it occurs:

  • During normal startup
  • While attempting to enter Service Mode
  • Intermittently after startup
  • After a particular button is touched
  • Whether buttons appear stuck or unresponsive
  • Whether unexpected menu selections occur
  • Whether the warmer is unable to complete startup

Expected outcome: The error is reproducible or clearly observed during a defined startup or button-input condition.

Why it matters: This confirms whether the warmer is identifying a true touch-panel input fault or reacting to an incorrect Service Mode entry attempt.

3. Rule Out Incorrect Service Mode Entry

If the error occurred while attempting to enter Service Mode, repeat the entry procedure only after confirming that both required Service Mode buttons are depressed during power-on, as specified by the manufacturer procedure.

A Sys Fail 08 message may occur when only one required button is detected during the Service Mode power-on sequence.

  • Expected outcome: Correct Service Mode entry proceeds without Sys Fail 08 if the previous error was caused by incomplete button entry.
  • Why it matters: This prevents replacing components for an error caused only by incorrect button timing or incomplete key depression.

If this resolves the error, document the incorrect Service Mode entry condition, verify normal warmer operation, and return the device to service according to facility policy.

4. Inspect the Touch Panel Externally

Inspect the touch panel and control area for:

  • A physically stuck or continuously depressed button
  • Torn, cracked, lifted, or damaged overlay material
  • Residue, liquid intrusion, cleaning-product buildup, or contamination around buttons
  • Evidence that an object or accessory is pressing against the panel

Clean or correct only according to approved facility and manufacturer guidance.

Expected outcome: No button remains mechanically depressed, contaminated, or externally obstructed.

Why it matters: Sys Fail 08 indicates a button is being detected as pushed; physical pressure or contamination may mimic an electrical short.

5. Use the Button-Function Diagnostic Test

If Service Mode can be properly entered, access the approved diagnostic button-function test and identify which button or buttons are being detected as pushed.

Do not perform this testing while the warmer is in patient use.

  • Expected outcome: The diagnostic test identifies either normal button operation or one or more buttons remaining detected as active without intentional pressing.
  • Why it matters: A continuously detected button supports isolation of the failure to the touch-panel input path rather than a general startup issue.

6. Check the Touch-Panel Connection

With the warmer removed from service and following approved electrical safety precautions, inspect the touch-panel connection to the Control PCA for:

  • Loose connection
  • Partially seated connector
  • Visible damage to the cable or connector
  • Contamination or evidence of liquid exposure

Reseat the accessible connection only when permitted by facility procedure and manufacturer service guidance, then repower and check for the error again.

Expected outcome: A secure, undamaged connection is confirmed, and the error either clears or remains reproducible.

Why it matters: An unstable or compromised touch-panel connection can create false or continuous button-input detection.

7. Isolate the Touch Panel From the Control Path

If the error persists, qualified service personnel should follow the approved service procedure to disconnect the touch-panel connector from the Control PCA, then repower the unit and observe whether Sys Fail 08 returns.

  • If Sys Fail 08 clears with the touch panel disconnected, the Touch Panel is the likely failed component.
  • If Sys Fail 08 remains with the touch panel disconnected, the Control PCA is the likely failed component.

Expected outcome: The isolation test identifies whether the detected button fault originates from the touch panel or the control-input circuitry.

Why it matters: This separates a shorted touch-panel input from a Control PCA input-detection failure without unnecessary component replacement.

8. Stop When Resolved and Verify Function

If the issue is corrected through proper Service Mode entry, removal of external interference, connector correction, or authorized component replacement, complete operational verification before returning the warmer to service.

Confirm:

  • Sys Fail 08 no longer appears during normal startup
  • Touch-panel buttons respond appropriately
  • No buttons remain falsely detected as pushed
  • The warmer completes required operational and safety checks according to facility procedure

Expected outcome: The warmer starts normally, accepts valid button input, and is safe for clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 08 continues after correct Service Mode entry has been confirmed, external button interference has been ruled out, the touch-panel connection has been checked, and the approved isolation test has been completed, the condition strongly indicates failure of the Touch Panel or Control PCA.

Remove the Panda Warmer from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation according to facility policy and manufacturer service guidance.

  • If the error clears when the touch panel is disconnected, proceed with Touch Panel replacement through the authorized repair process.
  • If the error remains with the touch panel disconnected, proceed with Control PCA evaluation or replacement through the authorized repair process.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. An infant warmer with an unresolved control-input system failure should not be returned to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a touch-panel system failure while an infant depends on the warmer for thermal support. Move the patient to a backup device first, then evaluate the warmer away from patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 08 [XX XX 00 00] during startup. Touch-panel button input appeared continuously detected, preventing confidence in normal control-panel operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified correct Service Mode button entry. External obstruction and contamination were not identified. Button-function diagnostics identified a continuously detected touch-panel input. Sys Fail 08 cleared when the touch-panel connector was disconnected from the Control PCA, indicating a failed Touch Panel."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from clinical service, labeled unit Out of Service, and routed for Touch Panel replacement and post-repair operational verification before return to patient use."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Sys Fail 08 condition requires careful interpretation because it may represent either an incorrect Service Mode entry attempt or a true touch-panel or control-input failure. Protect the patient first, confirm the exact behavior, rule out external and connection-related causes, and escalate appropriately when component failure is indicated. Accurate CCR documentation supports safe repair decisions and reliable device history.

That is successful troubleshooting.
