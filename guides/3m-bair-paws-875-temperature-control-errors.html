3M Bair Paws 875 Series

Temperature Control Errors or Inconsistent Warming

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Paws 875 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a 3M Bair Paws 875 patient warming unit that powers on but shows inconsistent temperatures, fluctuates unexpectedly, or displays temperature control errors. The guide prioritizes verifying external and simple causes first before assuming internal electronic or sensor faults. Patient safety and proper escalation are emphasized throughout.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the device is connected to a properly functioning outlet.
  • Confirm that other devices operate correctly on the same outlet.
  • Why: Fluctuating voltage or poor connections can affect temperature regulation.

Inspect Power Cord and Plug

  • Look for visible damage, frays, or bent prongs.
  • Swap with a known working cord if available.
  • Why: Damaged cords can cause intermittent heating.

Check Hose Connections

  • Ensure the warming hose is fully seated on the unit and the blanket.
  • Inspect for kinks, blockages, or tears in the hose.
  • Why: Obstructed airflow can lead to inconsistent heating.

Examine the Blanket or Warming Pad

  • Inspect for visible damage, pinholes, or moisture.
  • Ensure it matches the unit model specifications.
  • Why: Faulty blankets may cause uneven heating or trigger sensor errors.

Observe Device Indicators and Alarms

  • Record any error codes, warning lights, or audible alarms.
  • Note whether the temperature sensor icon is flashing or alerting.
  • Why: Codes can help differentiate between external and internal issues.

Perform a Controlled Test Run

  • Set the unit to a mid-range temperature and monitor stability.
  • Measure the air temperature at the hose outlet using a thermometer if available.
  • Why: Confirms whether the system maintains the set temperature consistently.

Swap Components if Available

  • If possible, test with a different, compatible hose or blanket.
  • Why: Helps isolate whether the issue is with the unit or accessories.

Reset Device

  • Power cycle the unit and check for recurring errors.
  • Why: Temporary faults or sensor misreads may clear after reset.

If the Problem Persists

External factors have been ruled out. Likely causes include internal temperature sensor failure, control board faults, or heater assembly issues.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send to the manufacturer or authorized service bench for repair.

Note: Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and maintains patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not use the device on a patient if temperature control errors occur. Move the patient to a verified functioning warming system first. Troubleshoot only when the patient is safely relocated.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Staff reports the Bair Paws 875 unit shows fluctuating temperatures or errors during warming.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Hose connections, blanket integrity, and power were checked; error persists. Likely internal sensor or control board issue.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair/bench evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and confirmed
  • Power cord inspected/swapped
  • Hose and blanket condition noted
  • Error codes and indicator lights recorded
  • Temperature measured at outlet
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Consistent, safe patient warming depends on methodical troubleshooting and knowing when to escalate. Proper documentation, careful observation, and following these checks protect patient safety and reduce unnecessary downtime or equipment damage.

That is successful troubleshooting.
