3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Hose Connection Damage or Poor Airflow to Blanket

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series patient warming system when airflow to the warming blanket is reduced or interrupted. Symptoms may include:

  • Patient not warming properly
  • Low or no airflow felt at the blanket
  • Alarms related to pressure or airflow

Focus is on external checks and avoiding internal component repair unless necessary.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Inspect the Hose for Visible Damage

  • Check the entire hose length for cracks, kinks, or holes.
  • Ensure hose connectors are not broken or cracked.

Why: Damaged hoses restrict airflow and can trigger alarms.

Confirm Proper Hose Attachment

  • Ensure the hose is fully seated on both the blower outlet and the blanket inlet.
  • Listen for any air leaks at the connections.

Why: Loose connections are a common cause of airflow loss.

Check the Blanket for Obstructions

  • Remove the blanket and inspect the internal channels for folds, twists, or blockages.
  • Ensure the blanket is not damaged or punctured.

Why: Blocked channels prevent proper airflow, reducing warming efficiency.

Inspect the Blower Filter and Air Intake

  • Ensure the blower intake is free of dust and debris.
  • Replace or clean the air filter if dirty.

Why: Restricted intake reduces airflow to the hose and blanket.

Test with a Known-Good Hose and Blanket

  • Swap with a spare hose or blanket to determine if the problem persists.

Why: Isolates whether the issue is with the hose, blanket, or blower unit.

Verify System Settings and Airflow Indicator

  • Ensure the blower is set to the correct temperature and flow setting.
  • Observe the airflow indicator (if present) to confirm proper operation.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out.

The device may have internal blower or pressure sensor issues.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for manufacturer repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to escalate is an important part of proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not use the system on a patient if airflow is compromised.

Always move the patient to another functioning warming system first.

Troubleshoot only when the patient is safely away from the device.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
User reported poor airflow to the warming blanket; patient not warming adequately.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Hose connection partially disconnected and minor kinking noted along the hose; filter slightly clogged.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Reconnected hose securely, straightened kinks, replaced/cleaned filter, verified airflow with test blanket.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet and hose connection inspected
  • Blanket channel checked
  • Air filter condition
  • Any audible leaks or hissing
  • Flow indicator readings
  • Final device status: airflow restored or device sent for repair

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting prioritizes patient safety, starts with simple external checks, and documents all observations clearly. Escalation should be prompt when external fixes do not resolve the issue.

That is successful troubleshooting.
