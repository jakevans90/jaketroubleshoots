Drager Vamos 2

Display Or Power Startup Fault

Asset Type

Anesthesia Monitor

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Vamos 2

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a Vamos 2 that will not power on, has no display, freezes, or fails during startup.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the monitor while it is needed for active anesthesia monitoring.

Action:

  • Remove the Vamos 2 from patient use if it fails to power on, has a blank display, freezes during startup, or cannot display gas values reliably.
  • Use another verified anesthesia gas monitor or anesthesia machine monitor before continuing.

Expected outcome: The patient is monitored safely on known-good equipment.

Why it matters: A startup or display fault can prevent oxygen, CO₂, nitrous oxide, or anesthetic agent monitoring from being available during a case.

Verify the Reported Problem

Confirm exactly what the user is reporting.

Action:

  • Check whether the issue is no power at all.
  • Check whether the issue is a blank display with power indicator present.
  • Check whether the issue is display flicker or dim screen.
  • Check whether the issue is startup freeze.
  • Check whether the issue is repeated rebooting.
  • Check whether the issue is error during startup.
  • Check whether the unit powers on but does not complete warm-up.

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified before troubleshooting continues.

Why it matters: A dead unit, a display fault, and a startup software fault may look similar at first but lead to different troubleshooting paths.

Check the AC Outlet

Verify that the wall outlet or power strip is supplying power.

Action:

  • Plug a known-good device or outlet tester into the same receptacle.
  • Confirm the outlet is not controlled by a switched circuit.
  • Avoid using damaged power strips, loose receptacles, or questionable OR boom outlets.

Expected outcome: The outlet provides proper AC power.

If the outlet is dead or unreliable, move the monitor to a known-good outlet and retest. If the monitor starts normally, the issue is external to the Vamos 2. Stop troubleshooting and document the outlet issue.

Inspect the Power Cord

Check the external power cord before suspecting the monitor.

Action:

  • Inspect the cord for cuts, crushed insulation, bent blades, loose connectors, or signs of heat damage.
  • Confirm the cord is fully seated at the monitor and wall outlet.
  • Swap with a known-good compatible hospital-grade power cord if available.

Expected outcome: The monitor powers on normally with a verified cord.

If the cord is damaged or the problem follows the cord, replace the cord, verify operation, and return the monitor to service if all checks pass.

Check for Obvious Physical Damage

Inspect the monitor for signs of drop damage or liquid exposure.

Action:

  • Look for cracked housing, damaged display glass, broken knobs or buttons, loose connectors, fluid residue, unusual smell, or rattling sounds.
  • Check around the power entry area and display bezel.

Expected outcome: No visible damage is found.

If there is evidence of fluid intrusion, burning smell, unusual heat, or impact damage, remove the device from service and send it for bench evaluation.

Confirm the Power Button or Switch Operation

Verify that the power control is physically working.

Action:

  • Press the power button or operate the power switch as normally required.
  • Observe whether any indicator lights, fan noise, relay clicks, display backlight, or startup sounds occur.

Expected outcome: The monitor shows a normal startup response.

If the switch feels stuck, loose, damaged, or does not produce any response, remove the monitor from service for repair evaluation.

Observe Startup Indicators

Look for signs that the unit is receiving power even if the display appears blank.

Action:

  • Check for power LED activity.
  • Check for backlight glow.
  • Check for startup screen.
  • Check for audible beeps.
  • Check for internal fan or airflow sound.
  • Check for display flicker.
  • Check for repeated restart behavior.

Expected outcome: The startup behavior helps separate a total power failure from a display or software startup issue.

If the unit appears powered but the screen stays blank, suspect display, backlight, internal power, or startup electronics after external checks are complete.

Disconnect External Accessories and Retest

External connections can occasionally interfere with startup or make a fault appear worse.

Action:

  • Power the unit off.
  • Disconnect nonessential accessories, network connections, printer connections, data cables, sample line, scavenging/exhaust tubing if applicable, and external peripherals.
  • Reconnect only AC power and attempt startup again.

Expected outcome: The Vamos 2 starts normally without external accessories connected.

If the monitor starts normally, reconnect accessories one at a time to identify the item causing the fault. Replace the faulty accessory or cable if confirmed.

Check Sampling System Load Only After Power Is Stable

A sampling system issue is not usually the first cause of a dead display, but a blocked or fluid-contaminated system can contribute to startup or readiness problems.

Action:

  • Inspect the water trap, sample line, and gas inlet path.
  • Remove obvious kinks, blockage, fluid, or contamination.
  • Install a clean, correct water trap and sample line if needed.

Expected outcome: The monitor completes startup and sampling readiness without obstruction-related warnings.

If the display or power fault remains unchanged, continue troubleshooting as a power/display/startup issue.

Try a Controlled Power Cycle

Perform a simple reset only if the unit is not connected to a patient and there are no signs of electrical damage.

Action:

  • Turn the monitor off.
  • Disconnect AC power.
  • Wait briefly to allow the unit to fully shut down.
  • Reconnect AC power.
  • Power the unit back on.

Expected outcome: The monitor boots normally and the display becomes usable.

If the problem clears, allow the unit to complete startup and verify normal display, gas measurement readiness, alarms, and controls before returning it to service.

Check for Overheating or Environmental Issues

Startup faults may occur if the monitor is overheated or ventilation is blocked.

Action:

  • Verify the unit is not covered by drapes, supplies, or stacked equipment.
  • Check that ventilation openings are not blocked.
  • Feel for unusual heat at the housing or power entry area.
  • Listen for abnormal fan noise if applicable.

Expected outcome: The monitor operates in a normal environment without excessive heat.

If the unit is unusually hot, smells burnt, or shuts down again after cooling, remove it from service for repair evaluation.

Verify Normal Operation Before Return to Service

If the monitor starts normally after external troubleshooting, confirm it is safe to use.

Action:

  • Verify display is bright and stable.
  • Verify controls respond normally.
  • Verify startup completes without freezing.
  • Verify no repeated rebooting occurs.
  • Verify no active power or system alarms remain.
  • Verify sampling system is clear.
  • Verify gas readings respond appropriately during functional check.

Expected outcome: The monitor passes a basic operational check and is ready for clinical use.

If any abnormal behavior remains, do not return the monitor to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the Vamos 2 still has no power, a blank display, startup freeze, repeated rebooting, abnormal heat, burning smell, or intermittent power after outlet, cord, accessory, and external checks have been completed, the likely cause is internal.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Possible internal causes may include display assembly failure, internal power supply failure, power entry issue, processor/startup fault, internal cabling issue, or board-level failure.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Clinical Engineering should not continue troubleshooting at the bedside once external causes have been ruled out and the monitor cannot be trusted for patient monitoring.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a failed anesthesia monitor during an active case unless patient monitoring has already been transferred to another verified device. Loss of gas monitoring during anesthesia can create serious patient safety risk, especially if oxygen, CO₂, or anesthetic agent values are unavailable.

If the monitor is needed for therapy continuity or active anesthesia monitoring, move the patient to a backup device first before troubleshooting continues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"OR staff reported that the Drager Vamos 2 anesthesia monitor would not complete startup and the display stayed blank after power-on."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified wall outlet and power cord were functional, external accessories were disconnected, and the monitor still failed to display a normal startup screen."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the Vamos 2 from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for bench evaluation due to suspected internal display or power startup fault."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior or startup behavior observed
  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Power cord inspected or swapped
  • Whether any power indicator lights were present
  • Whether display was blank, dim, flickering, or frozen
  • Whether startup screen appeared
  • Any beeps, fan noise, relay clicks, or reboot loops
  • Whether accessories were disconnected during testing
  • Water trap and sample line condition
  • Environmental factors such as blocked vents, overheating, fluid residue, unusual odor, or physical damage
  • Indicator lights observed during startup
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A display or power startup fault on a Drager Vamos 2 should be handled carefully because the device supports anesthesia gas monitoring. Start with external power, cord, connection, accessory, and environment checks before suspecting internal failure. If the monitor cannot complete startup reliably, remove it from service and document the troubleshooting clearly using the CCR method.

That is successful troubleshooting.
