STERIS AMSCO Century V Series

Door seal or gasket leak causing cycle abort

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO Century V Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of door seal leaks leading to cycle aborts, loss of chamber pressure, and failed sterilization cycles.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the sterilizer is not pressurized and has cooled before opening the door.
  • Never troubleshoot during an active or recently aborted hot cycle.

Review Alarm or Error Messages

  • Check the display for door seal, pressure loss, or leak-related alarms.
  • This helps confirm the issue is seal-related and not steam supply.

Inspect Door Gasket Visually

  • Open the door and examine the gasket for cracks, flattening, debris, or deformation.
  • A compromised gasket cannot maintain chamber pressure.
  • If damaged → Replace gasket and retest.

Check for Debris or Obstructions

  • Inspect gasket and door mating surface for lint, tape, packaging material, or residue.
  • Clean using approved methods and retest.

Verify Door Closure and Latching

  • Ensure the door fully seats and latches evenly.
  • Improper closure can mimic a gasket failure.
  • If door does not close properly → Adjust or escalate.

Inspect Gasket Seating and Alignment

  • Ensure gasket is properly seated and not twisted or dislodged.
  • Reseat if necessary and retest.

Check for Steam Leaks During Cycle Start

  • Run a test cycle and observe from a safe distance.
  • No visible steam should escape.
  • Visible steam indicates seal failure or alignment issue.

Verify Chamber and Door Surface Condition

  • Inspect for residue buildup, warping, or surface damage.
  • Clean surfaces if needed and retest.

Check Door Alignment and Hinges

  • Look for uneven gaps when the door is closed.
  • Misalignment can cause uneven gasket compression.
  • If suspected → Escalate for repair.

If the Problem Persists

If gasket condition, cleanliness, and door closure have been verified, the issue is likely internal (door mechanism, pressure system, or structural alignment).

Remove the sterilizer from service, label as Out of Service, and send for repair or further bench evaluation.

Recognizing when external causes are ruled out is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot a leaking sterilizer on instruments intended for patient use. Move instruments to a known working unit to maintain sterilization integrity.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer cycle aborts with door leak alarm during pressurization phase."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Worn and flattened door gasket preventing proper chamber seal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced door gasket, cleaned sealing surface, and verified successful cycle completion without leaks."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Gasket condition (cracked, flat, intact)
  • Presence of debris on seal surface
  • Door alignment observations
  • Steam leakage observed during test
  • Alarm codes or messages
  • Final operational status after testing

Final Thought

Door seal issues are one of the most common and preventable causes of sterilizer failure. Start with simple visual and physical checks before assuming complex faults. Proper escalation and clear documentation protect both patient safety and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
