Hillrom VersaCare

Error: Err0 Scale Display Error

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

VersaCare

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Err0 on the Hillrom VersaCare scale display when the bed scale system reports a scale control or scale board fault. This error may not stop normal bed movement, but it can affect patient weight accuracy and scale reliability.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use if the scale is needed for clinical decisions, medication dosing, fluid monitoring, or daily weights.
  • If the patient is already in the bed, do not rely on the displayed weight until the issue is corrected.
  • Use another approved method to obtain patient weight if needed.
  • Expected outcome: Patient care is not based on an unreliable bed scale reading.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact error shown on the scale display is Err0.
  • Check whether the error appears during power-up, after the bed is plugged into AC power, when attempting to use the scale, or intermittently during normal use.
  • Check whether the scale display responds to normal controls or remains locked in the error condition.
  • Expected outcome: Err0 is confirmed and the behavior is clearly observed.

Remove the Bed From Active Scale Use

  • Notify clinical staff that the bed scale is not reliable while Err0 is present.
  • Do not document patient weight from the bed scale until the error is cleared and function checks pass.
  • Expected outcome: The bed scale is not used for clinical weight decisions while the error remains active.

Check External Power First

  • Verify the bed is connected to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord for damage, loose connection, or signs of stress.
  • Confirm the plug is fully seated.
  • Expected outcome: The bed has stable AC power and no obvious power cord issue is present.

Cycle Bed Power

  • Unplug the bed from AC power.
  • Wait at least 10 seconds.
  • Plug the bed back into AC power.
  • Allow the bed electronics to restart normally.
  • Expected outcome: The bed completes its restart and the scale display can be checked again.

Press the Enable Key

  • After reconnecting AC power, press the Enable key on the siderail controls.
  • Observe the scale display.
  • Expected outcome: Err0 clears and the scale controls respond normally.

If Err0 Clears, Perform Function Checks

  • If Err0 clears, perform the appropriate bed function checks.
  • Check basic bed movement.
  • Check siderail control response.
  • Check scale display response.
  • Perform a scale function check per facility process.
  • Inspect for damage or loose components.
  • Expected outcome: The bed operates normally and the scale display no longer shows Err0.

Inspect for Obvious External or Physical Issues

  • If Err0 remains, inspect the bed for obvious issues before assuming board failure.
  • Check for damage around the scale display area.
  • Check for liquid intrusion or contamination near controls.
  • Check for damaged siderail controls.
  • Check for loose or damaged accessible cables.
  • Look for signs of impact or frame damage.
  • Check for anything interfering with normal bed operation.
  • Expected outcome: No simple external issue is missed.

Determine Whether the Issue Is Device-Related

  • If the bed has stable power, the power cycle was performed correctly, the Enable key was pressed, and Err0 remains, treat the problem as a likely scale electronics fault.
  • Err0 that remains after the basic reset path points toward a scale P.C. board or related internal scale system problem.
  • Expected outcome: External and simple causes have been ruled out.

Escalate for Scale P.C. Board Repair

  • Remove the bed from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Replace the scale P.C. board if qualified and authorized by facility procedure.
  • After replacement, repeat function checks.
  • If replacing the scale P.C. board does not resolve Err0, contact Hill-Rom Technical Support.
  • Expected outcome: The bed is repaired, tested, and returned to service only after Err0 is cleared.

If the Problem Persists

If Err0 remains after cycling power, waiting 10 seconds, reconnecting AC power, pressing Enable, and checking for obvious external issues, common user-level and external causes have been ruled out.

At that point, the condition strongly indicates an internal scale system failure, most likely involving the scale P.C. board or related scale electronics.

  • Remove the bed from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Escalate to Hill-Rom Technical Support if scale P.C. board replacement does not resolve the issue.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first if troubleshooting requires movement, repair handling, or any action that could affect patient safety.

Do not rely on the VersaCare bed scale for active patient weight decisions while Err0 is present. Use another approved weighing method until the bed scale passes function checks.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Hillrom VersaCare bed reported with Err0 on the scale display. Scale function could not be verified for patient weight use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed Err0 on scale display. AC power connection checked. Bed power cycled with 10-second wait before reconnecting to AC. Enable key pressed on siderail controls. Error remained, indicating likely internal scale electronics or scale P.C. board fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for repair/bench evaluation. Scale P.C. board replacement recommended per troubleshooting path. If error remains after board replacement, escalate to Hill-Rom Technical Support."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Err0 on a VersaCare scale display should be handled as a scale reliability issue until proven otherwise. Start with patient safety, verify the exact error, check power and controls first, then stop when the fault points beyond external troubleshooting. Clear documentation helps show what was checked, what was ruled out, and why the bed was returned to service or escalated for repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
