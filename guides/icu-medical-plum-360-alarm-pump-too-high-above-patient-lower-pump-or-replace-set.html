ICU Medical Plum 360

Alarm: Pump too high above patient. Lower pump or replace set.

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the alarm indicating the pump is positioned too high relative to the patient, causing increased backpressure and potential distal occlusion. This condition can stop infusion or prevent proper fluid delivery.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If infusion is interrupted or unreliable, pause therapy
  • Transition to a backup infusion device if needed
  • Confirm patient continues receiving ordered therapy

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm message is displayed
  • Check if alarm occurs during infusion or at start
  • Observe if infusion is stopped or paused
  • Look for additional occlusion alarms

Check Pump Height Relative to Patient (Most Common Cause)

  • Inspect pump mounting position
  • Ensure pump is not significantly above the patient
  • Compare pump height to patient’s heart level

Lower the Pump to Correct Position

  • Adjust IV pole or mounting location
  • Position pump closer to heart level
  • Press START to resume infusion

Inspect IV Tubing and Setup

  • Check for kinks or compression in distal line
  • Ensure tubing is not routed upward excessively
  • Verify proper cassette and tubing installation

Evaluate IV Set Condition

  • Replace IV set if no visible issues found
  • Re-prime line per procedure
  • Restart infusion

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after correcting height, tubing, and IV set:

  • External and common causes have been ruled out
  • Persistent alarm suggests internal pressure sensing or flow-related failure
  • Remove the pump from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot positioning-related alarms on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup infusion device before making adjustments.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump alarming 'Pump too high above patient. Lower pump or replace set,' infusion not running."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Pump mounted above patient level causing backpressure and distal occlusion condition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Lowered pump to heart level, restarted infusion, and verified normal operation without recurring alarms."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Position-related alarms are often simple but critical. Correct physical setup ensures accurate infusion delivery and prevents unnecessary escalation. Always verify external setup before assuming internal failure.

That is successful troubleshooting.
