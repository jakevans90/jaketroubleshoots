Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: APNOEA PRESSURE !!

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

APNOEA PRESSURE !! alarm indicating airway pressure is not reaching the required threshold, suggesting absent ventilation, circuit disconnection, or a major leak preventing pressure buildup.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient and ventilation status
  • If ventilation is compromised, remove the machine from use
  • Begin manual ventilation using a bag-valve device

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm APNOEA PRESSURE !! is displayed
  • Observe if airway pressure remains low or absent
  • Check if bellows are not moving or not filling
  • Determine when the alarm occurs (mode or phase of ventilation)

Check Breathing Circuit Connections

  • Ensure inspiratory and expiratory hoses are fully connected
  • Verify connection at the patient Y-piece
  • Confirm CO₂ absorber assembly is properly seated

Check for Circuit Leaks

  • Inspect hoses for cracks, holes, or loose fittings
  • Check reservoir bag, APL valve, and seals
  • Ensure vaporizers and absorber canister are seated correctly

Verify Patient Interface

  • Ensure endotracheal tube or mask is properly connected
  • Check cuff inflation if intubated
  • Look for dislodgement or disconnection at the patient

Confirm Ventilator Settings and Mode

  • Verify correct ventilation mode is active (not standby)
  • Check apnea ventilation settings
  • Confirm pressure thresholds and trigger sensitivity

Check Gas Supply and Flow

  • Verify oxygen and air supplies are connected and within range
  • Confirm fresh gas flow is appropriately set

Observe Ventilator Mechanics

  • Watch for bellows movement
  • Listen for leaks or absence of gas flow

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes such as disconnections, leaks, and incorrect settings have been ruled out and airway pressure still does not rise:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot ventilation alarms on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before continuing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit alarming APNOEA PRESSURE !! with no airway pressure during ventilation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Breathing circuit leak or disconnection identified, preventing pressure buildup."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reconnected circuit and verified normal ventilation, or removed from service after issue persisted."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Apnea pressure alarms indicate a critical loss of ventilation pressure. Always prioritize patient safety, check external components first, and escalate quickly if normal pressure cannot be restored.

That is successful troubleshooting.
