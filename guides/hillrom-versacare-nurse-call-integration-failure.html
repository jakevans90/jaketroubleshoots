Hillrom VersaCare

Nurse Call Integration Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

VersaCare

What This Guide Helps With

Bed not triggering nurse call system, no signal on bed exit, or failed communication between bed and facility nurse call system.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not rely on the bed’s nurse call integration for patient monitoring during troubleshooting.
  • Ensure alternate monitoring (manual checks or working call device) is in place.

Why it matters: Loss of nurse call functionality can delay response to patient needs or alarms.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Activate the nurse call from the bed (call button or bed exit alarm if applicable).
  • Confirm whether:
    • No signal is received at the nurse station
    • Signal is intermittent
    • Only certain features (bed exit, side rail buttons) fail

Expected: Clear understanding of what functions are not working

Why it matters: Helps isolate whether this is a full integration failure or partial feature issue

Check Nurse Call Cable Connection at Bed

  • Inspect the nurse call cable connected to the bed interface port.
  • Ensure:
    • Cable is fully seated
    • No bent pins or loose connection
    • Correct port is being used
  • Reseat the cable firmly.

Expected: Secure, stable connection

Why it matters: Loose or damaged connections are the most common cause of failure

Inspect Nurse Call Cable and Facility Jack

  • Trace the cable from the bed to the wall outlet.
  • Check for:
    • Frayed or damaged cable
    • Loose wall jack connection
    • Proper engagement in wall port
  • If possible, test with a known-good cable.

Expected: Cable integrity confirmed or issue identified

Why it matters: External cabling issues frequently interrupt communication

Test Wall Jack / Nurse Call System

  • Plug a known working device (or another bed) into the same wall jack.

Expected:

  • If the second device works → issue is with the bed or cable
  • If the second device fails → issue is with the wall jack or nurse call system

Why it matters: Isolates the problem between bed and facility infrastructure

Check Bed Settings and Configuration

  • Access the bed’s control interface (if applicable) and verify:
    • Nurse call integration is enabled
    • Correct communication mode (normally open/closed depending on facility)
    • Bed exit alarm settings tied to nurse call

Expected: Settings match facility requirements

Why it matters: Incorrect configuration can prevent signal transmission even with good hardware

Test Bed Exit Alarm Integration (If Applicable)

  • Activate the bed exit alarm and observe if it triggers the nurse call system.

Expected: Alarm should generate a nurse call signal

Why it matters: Confirms whether integration failure is limited to certain functions

Inspect for Physical Damage to Bed Interface Port

  • Examine the nurse call interface port on the bed for:
    • Bent or recessed pins
    • Signs of wear or fluid intrusion
    • Loose internal connection

Expected: Port is intact and undamaged

Why it matters: Physical damage at the interface will prevent communication

Power Cycle the Bed

  • Turn the bed off (if applicable), disconnect from power briefly, then restore power.

Expected: System resets and may restore communication

Why it matters: Clears temporary communication faults or software glitches

If the Problem Persists

If all external connections, cables, wall jacks, and settings have been verified, the issue is likely internal (interface board or wiring).

Remove the bed from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary downtime and ensures safe escalation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot nurse call functionality on an occupied bed without ensuring the patient has another reliable way to call for assistance.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed nurse call not triggering at nurse station when activated."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Damaged nurse call cable preventing signal transmission."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced nurse call cable and verified proper operation at wall jack and nurse station."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Nurse call cable condition verified
  • Wall jack tested with known working device
  • Bed exit alarm behavior observed
  • Interface port condition checked
  • Cables swapped
  • Final device status (functional or removed from service)

Final Thought

Nurse call integration failures are most often caused by external cabling or connection issues. Always start with simple checks and verify the system path before suspecting internal faults. Patient safety depends on reliable communication, and proper escalation ensures issues are resolved efficiently and safely.

That is successful troubleshooting.
