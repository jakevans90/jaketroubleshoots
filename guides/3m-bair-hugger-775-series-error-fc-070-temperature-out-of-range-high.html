3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-070 Temperature out of range high.

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-070 Temperature out of range high on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. This error indicates the unit detected a high-temperature condition and may no longer be safe for patient warming until the cause is verified and corrected.

A high-temperature fault can be caused by restricted airflow, blocked intake, hose issues, environmental setup problems, temperature sensing issues, heater control problems, blower performance issues, or control system failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the warming unit from patient use immediately if Error FC-070 is active or overheating is suspected.
  • Stop using the unit for active patient warming.
  • Disconnect the hose from the warming blanket if it is safe to do so.
  • Provide an alternate warming method if warming is clinically needed.
  • Do not continue warming therapy with a unit reporting a high-temperature fault.
  • Label the unit for Clinical Engineering evaluation.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without relying on a warming unit reporting a possible overheating condition.

Why it matters: A high-temperature fault can indicate unsafe delivered air temperature, heater control failure, airflow restriction, or temperature feedback failure.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact error displayed is Error FC-070 Temperature out of range high.
  • Power the unit on only in a safe bench or non-patient-care setting.
  • Note whether the error appears immediately at startup, after warm-up, at higher temperature settings, or intermittently during operation.
  • Check for excessive heat, weak airflow, burning smell, hose restriction, abnormal fan noise, shutdown behavior, or repeated alarming.

Expected outcome: Error FC-070 is confirmed as a true system-reported high-temperature condition.

Why it matters: Verifying the exact error prevents unnecessary repairs based on a misunderstood complaint.

3. Disconnect Power and Allow the Unit to Cool

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Disconnect the unit from AC power.
  • Allow the warming unit to cool completely before retesting.
  • Inspect for obvious overheating signs, odor, damaged plastic, or abnormal physical condition before powering the unit again.

Expected outcome: The unit cools down and can be evaluated safely.

Why it matters: A temporary over-temperature condition may clear after cooling, but the unit should not be returned to service until the cause is understood.

4. Check the Hose and Airflow Path

  • Inspect the hose for kinks, blockage, collapse, cracking, contamination, or improper connection.
  • Confirm the hose is properly seated at the warming unit.
  • Check that the outlet is not obstructed.
  • Verify the hose is not covered, pinched, or restricted during operation.
  • Replace the hose if it is damaged, questionable, or suspected of restricting airflow.

Expected outcome: Hose and airflow path are clear, properly connected, and not restricting airflow.

Why it matters: Reduced airflow can allow heat to build up inside the warming unit and trigger a high-temperature fault.

5. Check the Filter and External Air Intake

  • Inspect the air filter area.
  • Check for dust buildup, lint, blockage, or missing filter components.
  • Clean or replace the filter according to site policy and manufacturer guidance.
  • Confirm the air intake is not blocked by a wall, blanket, drape, cart, linen, or other obstruction.

Expected outcome: Air intake is clean and unrestricted.

Why it matters: Poor intake airflow can cause overheating even when the heater and electronics are functioning normally.

6. Check the Environment and Setup

  • Ask staff where the unit was located when the error occurred.
  • Confirm the unit was not covered by blankets, drapes, linen, or warming materials.
  • Confirm the hose was not routed in a way that restricted airflow.
  • Check whether the error occurred during long operation at a high temperature setting.
  • Look for anything around the unit that could trap heat or limit airflow.

Expected outcome: No external setup issue is found that would trap heat or restrict airflow.

Why it matters: Environmental heat buildup can mimic a device failure and cause repeated warming faults.

7. Retest the Unit in a Safe Bench Setting

  • Only retest after the unit has cooled and external airflow issues have been checked.
  • Connect the unit to a known-good outlet.
  • Power the unit on in a controlled non-patient-use test setup.
  • Observe whether Error FC-070 returns.
  • Check airflow strength and temperature behavior.
  • Do not leave the unit unattended during retest.

Expected outcome: The unit operates without Error FC-070, abnormal heat, odor, weak airflow, or shutdown.

Why it matters: Controlled retesting helps confirm whether the issue was external or still present inside the warming unit.

8. Isolate Accessory-Related Causes

  • If the error occurred with a specific hose or setup, compare operation with known-good compatible accessories.
  • Test with a known-good compatible hose.
  • Check whether the original hose caused reduced airflow.
  • Confirm whether the fault follows the accessory or remains with the warming unit.

Expected outcome: The fault either clears with a known-good hose or remains with the warming unit.

Why it matters: This separates a hose or airflow restriction problem from a device-level temperature fault.

9. Suspect Temperature Control or Internal Heating Fault

  • If Error FC-070 persists after cooling, hose checks, filter inspection, airflow checks, environmental checks, and accessory checks, treat the unit as a likely internal failure.
  • Possible causes include temperature sensing issues, heater control failure, blower performance problems, internal airflow restriction, control system fault, or over-temperature protection circuit issues.
  • Do not continue repeated warming tests if the error persists.
  • Remove the device from service for repair or formal bench evaluation.

Expected outcome: Persistent Error FC-070 is escalated instead of cleared casually.

Why it matters: A repeated high-temperature fault can create a patient safety risk.

If the Problem Persists

If Error FC-070 returns after the unit cools and external causes have been ruled out, remove the Bair Hugger 775 Series from service.

At that point, hose restriction, blocked intake, filter condition, environmental obstruction, accessory issues, and basic power/setup causes have been checked. The device should be labeled Out of Service and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Further evaluation should focus on temperature sensing, heater control, hose and airflow performance, fan or blower operation, and control system issues.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a high-temperature warming fault on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device or alternate warming method before testing, cooling, power cycling, or retesting the Bair Hugger.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 Series reported Error FC-070 Temperature out of range high. Unit removed from patient use due to possible overheating/high-temperature fault."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"High-temperature fault confirmed/reported. External checks performed for hose restriction, blocked airflow, filter condition, environmental obstruction, and overheating after use. Persistent fault indicates likely temperature sensing, heater, airflow, blower, or control system issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service. Disconnected power and allowed unit to cool. Inspected hose, air intake, filter area, airflow path, and physical condition. Retested in non-patient-use setting. Error FC-070 persisted, so unit was labeled Out of Service and sent for repair/bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error FC-070 should be treated as a patient safety concern because it points to a high-temperature condition. Start with safe external checks, verify airflow and accessories, allow the unit to cool, and escalate if the fault persists. Clear documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
