Hillrom CareAssist

Scale Drift or Inaccuracy

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

CareAssist

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses inaccurate or drifting weight readings caused by mattress position, accessories, setup, or environmental factors before assuming internal scale failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the patient is safely transferred to another bed or fully supported. Do not troubleshoot or zero the scale with a patient on the bed.

Verify Bed Is on a Level Surface

  • Ensure all four wheels are on a flat, even floor and brakes are engaged. Uneven surfaces can cause incorrect readings.

Check for External Contact or Obstructions

  • Ensure nothing is touching the bed (walls, IV poles, equipment, linens dragging on the floor). Even light contact can affect load cell readings.

Remove All Extra Items from Bed

  • Remove pumps, bags, extra linens, or equipment not normally part of the bed. Only the standard mattress and required accessories should remain.

Inspect Mattress and Deck Position

  • Ensure the mattress is properly seated and not bunched or overhanging. Verify the bed is in a neutral, flat position (not articulated).

Check for Locked-Out or Improper Scale Settings

  • Verify the scale is enabled and not locked out via control panel settings. Confirm units (lb/kg) are correct to avoid perceived inaccuracies.

Perform a Scale Zero (Tare) Procedure

  • With the bed empty and properly set up, zero the scale per normal operation. The scale should read zero consistently without drifting.

Test with Known Weight

  • Place a known test weight (e.g., 25 lb or 50 lb) evenly on the bed. Verify the reading is accurate and stable.
  • If accurate → issue resolved
  • If drifting or incorrect → continue troubleshooting

Inspect Cables and Connections (External Only)

  • Check visible scale-related cables under the bed for damage, pinching, or disconnection. Do not disassemble internal components.

Power Cycle the Bed

  • Unplug the bed, wait 30 seconds, and reconnect power. This can reset scale electronics and clear minor faults.

If the Problem Persists

If the scale continues to drift or provide inaccurate readings after these checks, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal (load cell failure, control board issue, or calibration fault).

  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and ensures accurate patient care.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a questionable weight for medication dosing or clinical decisions. Always move the patient to a verified scale if accuracy is in doubt.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nursing reported bed scale showing inconsistent and drifting weight readings."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Bed was in contact with wall and had additional equipment affecting load distribution."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed obstructions, cleared extra items, re-zeroed scale, and verified accuracy with test weight."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Bed on level surface and brakes engaged
  • Zeroing performed successfully or not
  • Test weight results
  • Any external contact with bed
  • Cables visually inspected
  • Power cycle completed
  • Final device status (in service or removed)

Final Thought

Scale issues are often caused by environmental or setup factors rather than true failure. A structured approach ensures accuracy while protecting patient safety. Escalate only after external causes are fully ruled out, and always document clearly for traceability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
