Hillrom TotalCare

Nurse Call Integration Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

TotalCare

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses inability of the bed to communicate with nurse call systems, including wiring, connectors, configuration, and power issues before assuming internal electronics failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the patient is safely in bed and alert staff to potential communication delay. Do not perform troubleshooting that could disrupt patient care.

Verify Bed Power

  • Check that the bed is plugged in and powered on. Confirm indicator lights on the bed control panel are normal. Without proper power, nurse call signals cannot transmit.

Check Nurse Call Cable Connections

  • Inspect the nurse call interface cable for secure connections at both the bed and wall jack. Look for bent pins, damaged plugs, or loose wiring. Proper connection ensures signal transmission.

Inspect Bed Side Rails and Modules

  • Remove and reseat any removable side rail modules that may carry nurse call wiring. Misaligned modules can interrupt signals to the nurse call system.

Test with Known Good Wall Jack / System

  • If possible, connect the bed to a verified functional nurse call jack or system. If the nurse call activates, the issue is likely the original wall or system connection.

Check Bed Configuration Settings

  • Review the bed’s integrated settings for nurse call functionality. Ensure the bed is configured to send alerts to the correct nurse call line. Incorrect settings can prevent alerts from triggering.

Observe Alarm and Indicator Behavior

  • Trigger a test nurse call from the bed. Confirm the system receives the alert. Note any error messages or LED behavior for documentation.

If the Problem Persists

All external connections, power, and configuration settings have been verified. The issue may be internal to the bed’s control board or nurse call interface module.

  • Remove bed from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for bench evaluation or manufacturer repair

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot nurse call wiring while the bed is actively supporting a patient requiring immediate care. Move patient to another bed if necessary.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed nurse call system not notifying staff when patient initiates call."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose connection at bed nurse call interface module discovered during inspection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated module and verified communication with nurse call system; bed returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Wall jack functionality tested
  • Cables inspected and reseated
  • Side rail modules checked
  • Test nurse call signal successfully received
  • Indicator LEDs normal

Final Thought

Maintaining patient safety and system integrity is critical. Troubleshoot logically, starting with external checks before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation ensures accountability and efficient repair workflow.

That is successful troubleshooting.
