Physio-Control LIFEPAK 12

Defibrillator Not Charging to Energy Level

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Physio-Control

Model

LIFEPAK 12

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the LIFEPAK 12:

  • Powers on normally but fails to charge to the selected defibrillation energy level
  • Displays error messages related to charge readiness or “Charge Failed”
  • Experiences slow or incomplete capacitor charge despite a fully charged battery

The focus is on external, logical troubleshooting steps before assuming internal capacitor or board failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Battery Condition

  • Check that the battery is fully seated and properly locked in place.
  • Confirm battery charge using the device’s battery indicator.
  • Swap with a known good battery to rule out weak or defective battery.
    Reason: A weak battery may not supply enough current for full energy charge.

Inspect Power Connections

  • If on AC power, ensure the power cable is securely connected and outlet is live.
  • Test outlet with a multimeter or another device.
    Reason: Insufficient power input can prevent full capacitor charge.

Check Defibrillator Pads and Cables

  • Ensure pads are correctly connected and not expired or damaged.
  • Inspect ECG/defibrillation cables for visible breaks or wear.
    Reason: Open or shorted circuits may prevent safe charge completion.

Attempt Charge with Minimal Configuration

  • Remove patient cables and pads, leaving only the device and battery.
  • Attempt to charge to the desired energy level.
    Reason: Isolates potential patient circuit issues.

Observe Device Indicators and Error Messages

  • Note any “Charge Failed,” “Low Energy,” or “Check Battery” messages.
  • Record the behavior for work order documentation.
    Reason: Provides clues whether the issue is battery, capacitor, or internal electronics.

Test with Another Device (if available)

  • Attempt same charging procedure on a second LIFEPAK 12 using the same battery.
  • Compare results to determine if the issue is with the battery or the device.

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks pass and the device still cannot charge to the selected energy level:

  • Remove the LIFEPAK 12 from service
  • Label it Out of Service
  • Send to Physio-Control service or internal bench evaluation for capacitor, charging circuit, or board inspection

Knowing when to stop and escalate is part of proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt defibrillation on a patient with a device showing charge failure.

Move the patient to another functioning defibrillator before continuing troubleshooting.

Only troubleshoot when patient safety is not at risk.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Device will not charge to selected energy level; displays ‘Charge Failed’.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Battery tested good; cables and pads confirmed intact; issue persists with device only.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent to Physio-Control for repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Battery condition and voltage
  • Any indicator lights during attempted charge
  • Error messages displayed
  • Condition and expiration of pads and cables
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety and systematic troubleshooting are the priority. External checks often reveal the cause, but persistent charging issues indicate internal component failure. Accurate documentation ensures proper follow-up and reduces risk to patients.

That is successful troubleshooting.
