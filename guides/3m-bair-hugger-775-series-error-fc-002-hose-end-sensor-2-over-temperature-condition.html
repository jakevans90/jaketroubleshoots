3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-002 Hose end sensor 2 over-temperature condition

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-002 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. FC-002 indicates a hose end sensor 2 over-temperature condition. This may be caused by high heat at the patient end, hose positioning, restricted airflow, moisture, contamination, a faulty hose-end sensor, a faulty elbow sensor, intermittent sensor behavior, or an internal device fault.

Because this fault involves temperature sensing near the hose end, the unit should not remain in patient use while the condition is active or recurring.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Bair Hugger 775 from patient use if FC-002 appears during active warming.
  • Disconnect the warming hose from the patient warming blanket if safe to do so.
  • Stop therapy on this unit.
  • Provide alternate warming if clinically needed.
  • Do not continue using the warmer while FC-002 is active or recurring.

Expected outcome: Patient warming continues safely using another device if needed.

Why it matters: FC-002 is related to a hose-end temperature sensing condition. Continued use could risk unsafe warming or unreliable temperature monitoring.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact fault displayed is Error: FC-002.
  • Ask staff when the fault occurred, such as at startup, shortly after warming began, during patient use, after repositioning the hose or blanket, or intermittently during movement.
  • Observe whether the unit shuts down, alarms, or prevents normal warming operation.
  • Do not repeatedly reset and return the device to patient use if the error keeps coming back.

Expected outcome: FC-002 is reproducible or clearly confirmed.

Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported hose-end sensor or over-temperature condition instead of a general warming complaint.

Translate the Error Into the Real-World Problem

  • FC-002 indicates a hose end sensor 2 over-temperature condition.
  • In practical terms, the unit believes the patient-end hose temperature is too high or the hose-end sensing circuit is not reporting correctly.
  • Possible causes include high heat at the hose end, faulty hose-end sensor, faulty elbow sensor, moisture, intermittent sensor failure, hose or sensor connection problems, or an internal control issue.

Expected outcome: Troubleshooting focuses on the hose, elbow sensor area, moisture, cooling, airflow, and recurrence before moving toward repair.

Disconnect Power and Allow the Unit to Cool

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Disconnect the power cord from the outlet.
  • Allow the unit to cool.
  • Wait at least 30–60 seconds before reconnecting power.

Expected outcome: The fault clears after cooling and power reset.

Why it matters: FC-002 can occur from an actual over-temperature condition or temporary sensor condition. Cooling and a controlled reset help determine whether the issue is temporary or persistent.

Inspect the Hose and Patient-End Area

  • Inspect the warming hose for kinks, occlusion, crushed sections, improper positioning, blocked hose end, blanket connection issues, or damage to the hose end or elbow area.
  • Verify the hose is properly seated and positioned as intended.
  • Correct any obvious airflow restriction or poor hose positioning before retesting.

Expected outcome: Hose airflow path is clear and the patient-end connection is not blocked or damaged.

Why it matters: Restricted airflow or poor hose positioning can create heat buildup at the patient end and contribute to over-temperature faults.

Check for Moisture or Contamination

  • Inspect the hose end, elbow sensor area, and connection points for moisture, fluid contamination, cleaning residue, condensation, or debris.
  • Do not return the device to service if moisture or contamination is present at the sensor area.
  • Remove the device from service if the hose, elbow sensor area, or connection points appear compromised.

Expected outcome: Hose and sensor areas are clean, dry, and free of contamination.

Why it matters: Moisture can cause incorrect sensor readings, intermittent faults, or sensor failure indications.

Reconnect Power and Retest

  • Reconnect the unit to a known-good power outlet.
  • Power the unit on.
  • Allow it to complete startup.
  • Run the unit under normal test conditions with the hose positioned correctly.
  • Confirm that FC-002 does not return.

Expected outcome: FC-002 does not return during test operation.

Why it matters: If the fault does not return, the issue may have been temporary overheating, hose positioning, moisture, or airflow restriction.

Test With a Known-Good Hose if Available

  • If FC-002 returns, remove the suspect hose and test with a known-good compatible hose.
  • Inspect the suspect hose for visible damage before setting it aside.
  • If FC-002 clears with a known-good hose, suspect the original hose or hose-end sensor condition.
  • If FC-002 remains with a known-good hose, suspect the elbow sensor path, sensor circuit, or internal device fault.

Expected outcome: Testing helps separate a hose-related problem from a device-related problem.

Why it matters: Accessory swaps should be performed before assuming the unit requires internal repair.

Check for Intermittent Behavior

  • With the unit running in a safe test setup, gently reposition the hose and elbow area.
  • Do not pull, twist, or stress the hose connection.
  • Watch for FC-002 returning during movement.

Expected outcome: The unit continues operating without FC-002.

Why it matters: A fault that appears during movement may indicate an intermittent hose-end sensor, damaged hose, faulty elbow sensor, or connection issue.

Determine Whether the Issue Is Resolved

  • If FC-002 clears after cooling, hose inspection, moisture correction, and proper hose positioning, verify normal operation.
  • Confirm no fault returns during test operation.
  • Return the device to service only if all checks pass.
  • If the fault returns repeatedly, do not continue resetting and returning the device to use.

Expected outcome: The device is either verified safe for return to service or removed for further evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If FC-002 remains after cooling, power reset, hose inspection, moisture check, proper hose positioning, and known-good hose testing, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent FC-002 strongly indicates a hose-end sensor, elbow sensor, intermittent sensor failure, or internal device fault.

Remove the device from service. Label it Out of Service. Send it for repair or bench evaluation.

If repair requires hose replacement, elbow sensor replacement, or internal service, perform required calibration and operational verification before return to service.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot FC-002 on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device first if patient temperature management is still needed. Do not rely on a warming unit with recurring hose-end over-temperature or sensor faults.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported 3M Bair Hugger 775 displayed Error: FC-002 during use. Unit alarmed and warming operation was interrupted."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"FC-002 confirmed. Hose-end sensor 2 over-temperature condition indicated. Hose and patient-end connection inspected for occlusion, moisture, damage, and improper positioning. Unit allowed to cool and retested."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Disconnected unit from power, allowed cooling period, inspected hose and elbow sensor area, verified hose positioning, and retested. Error did not return during functional test. Unit returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

FC-002 should be treated as a patient-end temperature sensing problem until proven otherwise. Start with patient safety, cooling, hose positioning, moisture, and accessory checks before assuming internal failure. If the fault is permanent or repeatable after external causes are ruled out, remove the device from service and escalate for repair. Clear documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the device history.

That is successful troubleshooting.
