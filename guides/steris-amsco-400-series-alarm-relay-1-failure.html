STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Relay #1 Failure – switched neutral relay for door seals and chamber float switch failure

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Relay #1 Failure alarm, which causes the sterilizer to abort a cycle due to failure of the switched neutral relay controlling door seals and chamber float switch functions. This impacts proper sealing, water level detection, and safe operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service immediately if the alarm occurs during a cycle
  • Do not use any instruments from the aborted cycle
  • Transfer workload to another sterilizer

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows “Relay #1 Failure”
  • Observe if the alarm occurs at startup or during a cycle
  • Check if the cycle aborts automatically
  • Look for abnormal door seal or chamber behavior

3. Check Power Supply Stability

  • Verify the unit is connected to a stable power source
  • Check for recent power interruptions or fluctuations
  • Confirm outlet voltage is within expected range if possible

4. Inspect Door Seal Operation (External Check)

  • Observe door seal A and B during operation
  • Check if seals inflate properly
  • Listen for air or steam leaks

5. Check Chamber Float Switch Behavior

  • Observe for abnormal water level readings
  • Check for unexpected alarms related to chamber level
  • Verify normal fill and drain behavior

6. Inspect External Connections and Wiring (Non-Invasive)

  • Power down the unit before inspection
  • Check accessible control cabinet connections
  • Inspect visible harness connections
  • Look for loose connectors or heat damage

7. Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit OFF and wait 30–60 seconds
  • Restore power and restart the sterilizer
  • Attempt a test cycle if safe to do so

8. Stop if Resolved

  • If the alarm does not return and a cycle completes successfully, return the unit to service
  • Continue to monitor for recurrence

If the Problem Persists

If the Relay #1 Failure alarm continues after external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • The issue likely involves a failed relay, control board failure, or internal wiring fault
  • Remove the unit from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot relay or control-related alarms on an active patient load. Always move the workload to a backup sterilizer before continuing evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed ‘Relay #1 Failure’ alarm and aborted cycle during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Suspected internal relay or control circuit failure after external power and connection checks were verified normal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed sterilizer from service, labeled Out of Service, and referred for further repair and diagnostic evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Relay-related alarms indicate loss of critical control functions tied to sealing and safety systems. Focus on external verification first, but escalate quickly when control faults persist.

That is successful troubleshooting.
