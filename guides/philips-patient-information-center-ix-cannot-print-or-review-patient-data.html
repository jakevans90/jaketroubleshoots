Philips Patient Information Center iX

Cannot Print or Review Patient Data

Asset Type

Central Monitoring Station

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

Patient Information Center iX

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting missing print, review, or stored patient data functions from the Philips Patient Information Center iX central station.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the central station in a way that delays active patient monitoring.

Action:

  • Confirm bedside monitors are still displaying patient waveforms, numerics, and alarms.
  • Verify staff can monitor the patient locally at the bedside if central review or printing is unavailable.
  • If central monitoring display is also affected, escalate immediately and use unit downtime procedures.

Expected outcome: Patient monitoring remains active even if printing or review functions are unavailable.

Why it matters: Printing and review issues are usually secondary to live monitoring, but they can affect documentation, rhythm review, and clinical workflow.

Verify the Reported Problem

Confirm exactly what function is failing.

Action:

  • Ask staff whether the issue is cannot print strips or reports.
  • Ask staff whether the issue is cannot open patient review.
  • Ask staff whether the issue is cannot view stored waveforms, alarms, or trends.
  • Ask staff whether the issue is cannot print for one patient only.
  • Ask staff whether the issue is cannot print or review any patient data.

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to printing, review access, patient-specific data, or a wider central station problem.

If the issue is only one patient sector, focus on that patient’s admission, connection, and data availability before assuming a printer or system failure.

Confirm Live Patient Data Is Present

Check whether the patient is actively connected and sending data to the central station.

Action:

  • Verify the patient sector shows current waveforms, heart rate, SpO2, NBP, or other active parameters.
  • Confirm the bedside monitor is powered on, assigned to the correct patient, and communicating with the central station.
  • Check for messages such as no data, disconnected, not admitted, equipment offline, or communication loss.

Expected outcome: Live patient data is visible before attempting review or printing.

If live data is missing, troubleshoot the bed connection or bedside monitor communication first. Stop this guide and follow the no-data or bed-not-connected workflow.

Check Patient Admission and Location Assignment

Confirm the patient is admitted or assigned correctly in the system.

Action:

  • Verify the patient name, room, bed label, and MRN are correct if available.
  • Check whether the patient was recently transferred, discharged, readmitted, or moved to another bed.
  • Confirm staff are selecting the correct patient sector when attempting review or print.

Expected outcome: The central station is looking at the correct patient record and bed assignment.

Why it matters: Review and print functions may appear unavailable if the wrong patient, discharged patient, or inactive bed is selected.

Confirm There Is Data Available to Review

Verify that enough monitoring time has passed for reviewable data to exist.

Action:

  • Ask when the patient was connected or admitted.
  • Check whether the event, alarm, trend, or waveform review window contains stored data.
  • Confirm staff are not trying to review a time period before the patient was connected.

Expected outcome: The selected time range contains stored patient data.

If the patient was just admitted or reconnected, allow time for new data to populate before assuming a failure.

Check Review Filters and Time Range

Incorrect review settings can make stored data appear missing.

Action:

  • Check the selected time range, patient, alarm/event category, and waveform/trend view.
  • Reset the review window to a recent time period where the patient was known to be monitored.
  • Try viewing a different review category, such as alarms, trends, or waveforms.

Expected outcome: Previously hidden data becomes visible when the correct review range or category is selected.

If changing filters restores the information, educate staff and stop troubleshooting.

Check User Access or Workflow Restrictions

Some functions may depend on login status, permissions, or hospital configuration.

Action:

  • Confirm whether the user is logged in, if applicable.
  • Have an authorized staff member attempt the same print or review function.
  • Check whether the issue occurs for one user or all users.

Expected outcome: The issue is identified as either user-specific or system-wide.

If only one user is affected, escalate through the normal IT or application support process for access review.

Check Printer Power and Local Printer Status

If review works but printing fails, check the printer first.

Action:

  • Verify the printer is powered on.
  • Check for paper, toner, paper jam, open tray, warning lights, or offline status.
  • Confirm the printer display does not show an error condition.
  • Print a test page if allowed by site procedure.

Expected outcome: The printer is ready and able to print.

If the printer has a paper, toner, jam, or offline issue, correct it or route to the responsible printer support team. Stop if printing is restored.

Check Printer Connection

Verify the printer is connected to the central station or network as expected.

Action:

  • Check USB, Ethernet, or network connections depending on the local setup.
  • Confirm cables are fully seated and not damaged.
  • If using a network printer, verify the printer has link lights at the network port.
  • Do not move network cables between ports unless approved by site procedure.

Expected outcome: The printer has a valid physical connection.

Why it matters: A loose cable or disconnected network drop can cause printing failure while the central station itself continues to function normally.

Try Printing From Another Patient or Central Station Function

Determine whether the problem is patient-specific or printer/system-wide.

Action:

  • Attempt to print a strip, report, or review page from another monitored patient if clinically appropriate.
  • Try printing a different type of report if available.
  • Ask whether other central stations can print normally.

Expected outcome: The failure is narrowed to one patient record, one function, one central station, or the shared printer path.

If only one patient is affected, continue checking that patient’s data and assignment. If all patients are affected, focus on printer, network, or central station configuration.

Check for Central Station Application Freezing or Delays

Review and printing may fail if the application is slow, frozen, or partially unresponsive.

Action:

  • Confirm whether the mouse, keyboard, touchscreen, or menu selections respond normally.
  • Check whether other patient sectors update in real time.
  • Look for application alerts, communication warnings, or system messages.
  • Do not restart the central station without following site procedure and confirming patient monitoring coverage.

Expected outcome: The central station application is responsive and updating.

If the central station is frozen or not updating patient data, remove it from active reliance and escalate immediately.

Confirm Network and Server-Dependent Functions

Patient review and print functions may depend on hospital network services, central monitoring servers, or data storage.

Action:

  • Check whether multiple Philips central stations are having the same review or printing issue.
  • Ask whether the issue started after network work, server maintenance, power interruption, or software downtime.
  • Verify other network-dependent functions, such as patient lookup or central data access, if available.

Expected outcome: A wider infrastructure issue is identified or ruled out.

If multiple central stations are affected, escalate to Clinical Engineering, IT, and Philips support according to site procedure.

Perform a Controlled Restart Only If Safe and Allowed

A restart may clear a local application issue, but only when patient monitoring coverage is maintained.

Action:

  • Confirm no active patient safety risk will be created by restarting the central station.
  • Ensure bedside monitoring remains available.
  • Notify clinical staff before restarting.
  • Follow site-approved shutdown and startup procedures.
  • After restart, verify live data, review access, and printing.

Expected outcome: The central station returns to normal operation and review/print functions are restored.

If the restart resolves the issue, document the behavior and final verification.

Verify Final Operation

Before returning the system to normal use, confirm both review and printing if applicable.

Action:

  • Open patient review for an active patient.
  • Confirm stored data is visible for an appropriate time range.
  • Send a test print or clinically appropriate print job.
  • Verify the printed output is complete, legible, and associated with the correct patient.

Expected outcome: Patient data can be reviewed and printed correctly.

If successful, return the device to service and document the work order.

If the Problem Persists

If live data is present, the patient is correctly admitted, the printer is ready, cables are connected, and review/print functions still fail, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve the central station application, printer configuration, network path, server connection, database access, or Philips system software.

The central station should be removed from service if it cannot safely support required monitoring workflow, labeled Out of Service if local policy requires, and escalated for bench evaluation, IT review, Philips service support, or central monitoring system repair.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not perform deep software changes, database changes, or internal repair steps without the appropriate Philips support pathway and site approval.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot central monitoring review or printing problems while staff are depending on that station as the only active view of the patient. Confirm bedside monitoring is active first, and move monitoring responsibility to another safe display or workflow before restarting or removing the central station.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Philips Patient Information Center iX central station could not print strips or review stored patient data."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified live patient monitoring was present, but review and print functions failed after checking patient assignment, printer status, and network connection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the central station from active reliance, labeled it Out of Service, and escalated for Philips/IT evaluation after external causes were ruled out."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior or any alarm-related messages displayed
  • Accessories swapped, including printer, USB cable, Ethernet cable, mouse, keyboard, or connected peripherals
  • Power behavior, including outlet status, UPS status, restart performed or not performed, and whether the central station was responsive or frozen
  • Environmental factors, including recent network work, server maintenance, power interruption, software downtime, or unit workflow changes
  • Indicator lights, including printer warning lights, network link lights, and any central station status indicators
  • Final device status, including returned to service, removed from service, labeled Out of Service, or escalated for Philips/IT evaluation

Final Thought

For a Philips Patient Information Center iX central station, printing and review problems should be approached logically. Confirm patient safety first, then verify live data, patient assignment, stored data availability, printer condition, connections, and network behavior. If the problem remains after external checks, escalate appropriately and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
