Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: INSP O2 HIGH !!

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

INSP O2 HIGH !! alarm indicating inspiratory oxygen concentration above the configured upper limit, which may lead to unintended hyperoxia due to incorrect gas settings, sensor inaccuracies, or gas delivery issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Assess current ventilation and oxygen delivery
  • If oxygen levels are uncontrolled, remove device from patient use
  • Transition patient to alternate ventilation support

2. Verify the Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm INSP O2 HIGH !! alarm is displayed
  • Observe FiO₂ reading compared to alarm limit
  • Note when alarm occurs (startup or during ventilation)
  • Check if oxygen level remains consistently elevated

3. Check Fresh Gas Flow Settings

  • Inspect O₂ flow control setting
  • Verify ratio of O₂ to air or N₂O
  • Ensure total fresh gas flow matches intended clinical settings

4. Check Vaporizer and Gas Configuration

  • Ensure proper gas mix is selected
  • Verify air or N₂O is available for dilution if required
  • Confirm vaporizers are properly seated

5. Check O₂ Alarm Limit Settings

  • Review upper O₂ alarm limit configuration
  • Ensure limit is appropriate for intended FiO₂
  • Adjust if necessary to align with clinical parameters

6. Inspect Breathing Circuit

  • Check for conditions causing O₂ buildup
  • Inspect absorber and circuit for rebreathing
  • Ensure proper scavenging and gas exchange

7. Check O₂ Sensor Function

  • Verify sensor is connected properly
  • Check calibration status
  • Perform calibration if needed

8. Stop When Resolved

  • Confirm FiO₂ returns to expected range
  • Ensure alarm no longer activates
  • Return device to service if stable

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after verifying gas settings, alarm limits, circuit integrity, and sensor calibration, internal causes such as sensor failure or gas mixing malfunction should be suspected.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot oxygen delivery issues on an active patient. Always transition the patient to a backup device before making adjustments.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"INSP O2 HIGH !! alarm observed during case; oxygen concentration reading above expected range."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Oxygen flow set significantly higher than air flow, resulting in elevated FiO₂."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Adjusted gas flow ratio, verified FiO₂ normalized, and alarm cleared. Device returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

High oxygen concentration alarms should always be taken seriously. Start with simple configuration checks, confirm accurate measurement, and escalate appropriately if the issue persists.

That is successful troubleshooting.
