3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-071 Temperature out of range low

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-071 Temperature out of range low on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. This error may occur when the unit detects low temperature output, cannot reach the selected warming temperature, or cannot maintain proper warming performance during use.

Because this condition can affect patient warming therapy, it should be treated as a clinical performance concern rather than just a nuisance error.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the unit from patient use if warming performance is questionable.

  • Stop using the warmer on the patient.
  • Transfer warming support to another approved warming device if needed.
  • Do not continue patient warming with a unit showing a temperature control error.
  • Allow the unit to cool before handling the hose or air outlet if it was recently operating.

Expected outcome: Patient warming is maintained safely without relying on a device with unresolved temperature control concerns.

Why it matters: Low temperature output may result in ineffective patient warming and can delay proper temperature management.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

Confirm the exact error before resetting or removing the unit.

  • Confirm the displayed error is FC-071.
  • Ask staff when the error occurred.
  • Check whether it appeared at startup, during warming, after running for several minutes, or intermittently.
  • Confirm whether the unit produces airflow.
  • Confirm whether the air feels cool, weak, inconsistent, or never warms properly.
  • Check whether the selected temperature setting was 32°C, 38°C, or 43°C.

Expected outcome: Error FC-071 is reproducible or clearly reported with low warming output.

Why it matters: This confirms the issue is a true temperature regulation fault and not a misunderstanding of settings or operation.

3. Disconnect Power, Reset, and Retest

Perform the basic reset first.

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Disconnect the power cord from the wall outlet.
  • Wait at least 30 seconds.
  • Reconnect power to a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Power the unit back on.
  • Select a normal operating temperature and allow the unit to run.

Expected outcome: The unit powers up normally, warms properly, and FC-071 does not return.

Why it matters: Some temperature faults may clear after a reset, but recurring FC-071 indicates a real operating problem.

If resolved, continue functional testing before returning the unit to service.

4. Check Power Source and Power Cord

Verify the unit is receiving proper power.

  • Inspect the power cord for cuts, looseness, bent blades, or damage.
  • Confirm the outlet is working.
  • Try another known-good outlet.
  • Check that the plug fits securely and does not wiggle.
  • Verify the unit does not shut off, flicker, or restart during operation.

Expected outcome: Unit remains powered and stable during warming.

Why it matters: Unstable power can affect heater operation and create misleading temperature faults.

5. Check Hose Connection and Airflow Path

Inspect the external airflow path before suspecting internal failure.

  • Confirm the hose is fully connected.
  • Inspect the hose for kinks, crushing, cracks, loose fit, or blockage.
  • Make sure the hose is not obstructed by blankets, bedding, carts, or equipment.
  • Verify airflow exits the hose normally.
  • Replace the hose if damaged or questionable.

Expected outcome: Airflow is strong, unrestricted, and consistent.

Why it matters: Restricted airflow can prevent normal heat delivery and may cause the control system to detect temperature problems.

6. Check Blanket and Setup

Confirm the warming setup is not restricting output.

  • Make sure the correct compatible warming blanket is being used.
  • Check that the blanket is not folded, blocked, or incorrectly positioned.
  • Confirm the hose is properly inserted into the blanket port.
  • Remove excessive bedding or pressure over the blanket air channels.
  • Retest using a known-good blanket if available.

Expected outcome: Warm air flows through the blanket normally.

Why it matters: A setup problem can make the unit appear to have poor warming performance even when the warmer itself is operating.

7. Inspect Filter and Air Intake Area

Check for blocked intake or poor ventilation.

  • Inspect the air intake and filter area.
  • Look for dust buildup, lint, tape, labels, fluid contamination, or blockage.
  • Clean or replace the filter according to facility procedure and manufacturer guidance.
  • Make sure the unit has proper clearance around the intake.

Expected outcome: Intake area is clean and airflow is not restricted.

Why it matters: Reduced intake airflow can affect heater performance and temperature regulation.

8. Retest Temperature Performance

After external issues are checked, retest the unit.

  • Run the unit at a selected temperature setting.
  • Observe whether the unit reaches operating temperature.
  • Confirm whether the error returns.
  • Check for weak heat, unstable heat, unusual smells, abnormal sounds, or intermittent operation.
  • If available, compare performance to another known-good Bair Hugger unit.

Expected outcome: Unit reaches and maintains selected warming temperature without FC-071.

Why it matters: This step helps separate an external setup issue from an internal heating or sensing fault.

9. Isolate the Likely Failure Area

If FC-071 returns after external checks, narrow the likely cause.

  • If airflow is weak, suspect hose, filter, intake restriction, blower, or airflow path issue.
  • If airflow is normal but air stays cool, suspect heater or heater control issue.
  • If heat is present but unstable or the error returns intermittently, suspect temperature sensor or control board issue.
  • If the unit fails immediately at startup, suspect internal sensing, heater control, or control board fault.

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to accessory/setup, airflow, heater, sensor, or internal control function.

Why it matters: FC-071 points to low temperature output or low detected temperature, but the real cause may be airflow, heating, sensing, or control related.

10. Stop When Resolved

If the unit resets, warms normally, maintains temperature, and the error does not return, complete final checks before returning it to clinical use.

  • Complete functional testing.
  • Inspect the unit for safe physical condition.
  • Document the findings.
  • Return the device to service only if performance is normal and reliable.

Expected outcome: Device is safe, functional, and documented before return to clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If Error FC-071 returns after power reset, outlet check, hose inspection, filter/intake inspection, setup check, and retesting, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent FC-071 strongly suggests an internal failure involving the heater, temperature sensor, airflow/heating control, or control board function.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Do not continue troubleshooting on an active patient. Do not return the unit to service if it cannot reach or maintain operating temperature.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a patient warming system on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device first if warming support is needed.

Do not use a patient warming system with an unresolved temperature error. Ineffective warming can create a false sense of patient temperature protection while the device is not actually delivering proper therapy.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bair Hugger 775 reported Error FC-071 Temperature out of range low. Staff reported unit would not reach or maintain selected warming temperature during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified FC-071 temperature low error. Checked power source, power cord, hose connection, airflow path, blanket setup, intake area, and filter condition. Error persisted during retest, indicating likely internal heater, temperature sensor, or control board related fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for bench evaluation/repair due to recurring FC-071 and inability to confirm reliable warming temperature performance."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status
  • Exact error displayed: FC-071
  • When the error occurs: startup, during use, intermittent, or after warm-up
  • Selected temperature setting
  • Outlet tested
  • Power cord condition
  • Hose condition
  • Blanket type/setup checked
  • Filter/intake condition
  • Airflow strength
  • Whether air was cool, warm, or inconsistent
  • Any unusual sounds, heat, smell, or shutdown behavior
  • Whether reset cleared the error temporarily
  • Final device status

Final Thought

FC-071 should be treated as a patient warming performance concern, not just a nuisance error. Start with safety, confirm the error, check simple external causes, and escalate when the unit cannot reliably reach or maintain temperature. Clear documentation helps the next technician understand what was ruled out and why the unit was removed from service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
