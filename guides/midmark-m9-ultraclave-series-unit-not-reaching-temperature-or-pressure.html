Midmark M9 UltraClave Series

Unit Not Reaching Temperature or Pressure

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

Midmark

Model

M9 UltraClave Series

What This Guide Helps With

Cycle runs but fails to reach proper temperature or pressure due to water, sealing, loading, or external setup issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from active use.
  • Expected: Backup sterilization method available.
  • Why it matters: Incomplete sterilization directly risks patient safety.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Run a cycle and observe temperature/pressure readings and any alarms.
  • Expected: Unit fails to reach setpoint or alarms out.
  • Why it matters: Confirms the issue and identifies when failure occurs (heat-up vs. pressure phase).

Check Water Reservoir Level and Quality

  • Inspect the reservoir and confirm it is filled with distilled water to the correct level.
  • Expected: Proper level of clean distilled water.
  • Why it matters: Steam generation depends on adequate, clean water.
  • If low/empty → refill and retest
  • If contaminated → drain, refill with distilled water, and retest

Inspect Chamber Loading

  • Ensure instruments are not overloaded and are arranged to allow steam circulation.
  • Expected: Proper spacing between items, no overpacking.
  • Why it matters: Overloading prevents proper steam penetration and temperature rise.
  • If overloaded → reduce load and retest

Inspect Door Closure and Gasket Condition

  • Check that the door is fully closed and the gasket is clean, intact, and properly seated.
  • Expected: Tight seal with no visible gaps or steam leakage.
  • Why it matters: Loss of pressure is commonly caused by poor sealing.
  • If debris present → clean gasket and sealing surface
  • If damaged (cracked, flattened, brittle) → replace gasket

Check for Steam Leaks During Operation

  • Run a cycle and observe around the door and fittings for escaping steam.
  • Expected: No visible steam leaks.
  • Why it matters: Any leak prevents pressure buildup.
  • If leak observed → address gasket or sealing issue

Verify Unit is Level and Properly Installed

  • Check that the sterilizer is on a level surface with proper clearance.
  • Expected: Unit sits level with no tilt.
  • Why it matters: Improper leveling can affect water distribution and sensor readings.
  • If not level → adjust and retest

Inspect Power Source Stability

  • Ensure the unit is plugged directly into a dedicated, functioning outlet. Avoid power strips.
  • Expected: Stable, uninterrupted power.
  • Why it matters: Inconsistent voltage can affect heating performance.
  • If unstable → correct power source and retest

Observe Heating Behavior

  • During cycle start, listen for normal heating sounds and monitor if temperature rises at all.
  • Expected: Gradual increase in temperature and pressure.
  • Why it matters: No temperature rise suggests heater or control-related issue.
  • If no heating → likely internal component issue

If the Problem Persists

If water level, loading, gasket, leveling, and power have all been verified, the issue is likely internal (heating element, pressure sensor, or control system).

Remove the unit from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and wasted time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a sterilizer needed for immediate patient care. Always ensure instruments can be sterilized using an alternate method first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer not reaching temperature/pressure during cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Door gasket worn causing pressure leak."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced gasket and verified normal cycle operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Water level verified
  • Distilled water used
  • Load configuration checked
  • Steam leaks observed/not observed
  • Gasket condition
  • Unit level confirmed
  • Power source tested
  • Alarm codes or behavior
  • Final operational status

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting focuses on the most common external causes first—water, sealing, loading, and power. Patient safety depends on recognizing when sterilization cannot be guaranteed and escalating appropriately. Proper documentation ensures continuity and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
