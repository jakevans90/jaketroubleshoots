GE Healthcare MAC 5

Battery Runtime, Charging, Power-On, or AC Adapter Issue

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

MAC 5

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting MAC 5 battery runtime, charging, power-on, AC adapter, and external power issues before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the MAC 5 is not being used for an active patient ECG.

If the unit powers off, fails to boot, or shows low battery during patient use, have clinical staff move to another available ECG machine.

Expected outcome: Patient testing is not delayed or performed on an unreliable power source.

Confirm the Exact Power Complaint

Ask the operator what happened and when it occurred.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Unit will not power on
  • Unit powers on only with AC connected
  • Unit shuts off on battery
  • Battery runtime is short
  • Battery does not charge
  • Charging indicator does not appear
  • AC adapter not recognized
  • Intermittent power when the cord is moved
  • Unit powers on but shuts down during printing

Expected outcome: The issue is separated into battery, AC adapter, charging, power button, or internal power path behavior.

Check for Active Physical Damage or Safety Concerns

Inspect the MAC 5, AC adapter, power cord, battery area, and connector points for damage.

Look for:

  • Cracked adapter housing
  • Bent or loose plug
  • Damaged cord insulation
  • Burn marks
  • Liquid intrusion
  • Swollen or leaking battery
  • Unusual heat or odor

Expected outcome: Any unsafe power accessory or device condition is identified before further use.

If damage, heat, odor, or battery swelling is found, stop troubleshooting, remove the unit from service, and escalate for repair.

Verify the Wall Outlet

Plug the AC adapter into a known working hospital-grade outlet.

Avoid using a power strip during troubleshooting unless approved by site policy.

Expected outcome: The MAC 5 receives power from a verified outlet.

If the unit powers on normally from a different outlet, document the failed outlet location and notify Facilities.

Confirm the Correct AC Adapter Is Being Used

Verify the AC adapter matches the MAC 5 and is not borrowed from another device.

Check that the adapter rating and connector type match the approved accessory for the unit.

Expected outcome: The device is not being powered by an incorrect, under-rated, or incompatible adapter.

If the adapter is incorrect, replace it with the correct approved adapter and retest.

Inspect the AC Adapter Connection

Confirm the adapter is fully seated at the wall outlet, power brick, and MAC 5 power input.

Gently check for looseness without forcing the connector.

Expected outcome: Charging or AC power status appears consistently when the adapter is connected.

If the power status changes when the connector is moved, suspect a damaged adapter cable, plug, or device power input and escalate.

Test With a Known-Good AC Adapter If Available

Use a known-good approved MAC 5 AC adapter.

Expected outcome: The unit powers on and indicates AC power or charging normally.

If the known-good adapter resolves the issue, replace the faulty adapter and return the MAC 5 to service after functional verification.

Check Battery Seating

Power the device down safely if possible.

Confirm the battery is properly installed and seated according to normal operator-accessible battery handling procedures.

Expected outcome: The battery is fully seated and recognized by the device.

If reseating restores battery operation, charge the battery and verify normal power-on behavior before returning the unit to service.

Allow Adequate Charging Time

Connect the MAC 5 to AC power and allow the battery to charge.

The MAC 5 is designed for AC mains or battery operation, and published product information lists battery operation as supporting a minimum of 180 minutes under a defined ECG acquisition and printing use pattern.

Expected outcome: Battery charge status improves and the unit remains powered on after charging.

If the battery does not accept charge or runtime remains very short, continue troubleshooting.

Check Battery Age and Use History

Review equipment records, battery replacement history, and staff reports.

Battery concerns are more likely if:

  • Runtime has gradually decreased
  • The unit is frequently left unplugged
  • The device shuts off during printing
  • The battery has not been replaced in several years
  • Multiple low-battery complaints are documented

Expected outcome: A weak or aged battery is identified as a likely external replaceable cause.

Test Battery-Only Operation

After charging, disconnect AC power and power on the MAC 5 using battery power.

Observe whether the unit:

  • Powers on normally
  • Immediately shuts off
  • Shows low battery
  • Runs briefly then powers down
  • Fails during printing

Expected outcome: Battery performance is confirmed under realistic use.

If the unit operates normally on AC but fails on battery, suspect a depleted or failed battery.

Test AC-Only Operation

Connect the MAC 5 to AC power and power it on.

Confirm the unit boots normally, remains powered, and can perform basic ECG workflow checks without relying on battery power.

Expected outcome: AC operation is stable.

If the unit does not power on with a known-good adapter and known-good outlet, suspect an internal power input, charging, or power supply fault.

Check for Charging Indicator or Battery Status Message

Review the device screen or power indicator for battery and charging status.

Expected outcome: The device clearly indicates AC connection, battery presence, or charging state.

If the battery is installed but not recognized, or charging status never appears with a known-good adapter, remove the device from service for bench evaluation.

Perform a Functional Power Check

Once the suspected issue is corrected, confirm the MAC 5 can:

  • Power on with AC connected
  • Show expected charging or AC power status
  • Power on using battery
  • Remain powered during basic operation
  • Print or complete a test ECG workflow if site policy allows

Expected outcome: Power and battery behavior are stable before returning the unit to service.

If the device passes these checks, document the result and return it to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the MAC 5 still will not power on, will not charge, does not recognize the battery, shuts down unexpectedly, or fails with a known-good outlet and AC adapter, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Likely internal concerns may include the battery interface, charging circuit, power input jack, internal power supply path, or power control circuitry.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue cycling power or attempting patient use when power reliability is uncertain.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot power, battery, or charging issues while the MAC 5 is connected to a patient. If the unit loses power during ECG acquisition, move the patient to another ECG machine first, then troubleshoot the failed device only when it is safe.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the GE MAC 5 would not stay powered on when unplugged from AC power."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery-only testing confirmed the unit powered off shortly after startup, while AC operation was stable with a known-good adapter."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced the failed battery, verified charging status, confirmed battery operation, and returned the MAC 5 to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • AC adapter inspected
  • Known-good AC adapter used
  • Battery seated or reseated
  • Battery age or replacement date
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Battery runtime observed
  • Whether failure occurs during printing
  • Power-on behavior on AC power
  • Power-on behavior on battery power
  • Unusual heat, odor, or damage
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power issues on an ECG machine should be handled carefully because sudden shutdown can interrupt patient testing and delay care. Start with the outlet, adapter, cable, battery seating, and charge status before assuming internal failure. If the MAC 5 remains unreliable after basic external checks, remove it from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
