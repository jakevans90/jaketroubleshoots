Defibtech Lifeline AED Series

Self-Test Failure on Power-On

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Defibtech

Model

Lifeline AED Series

What This Guide Helps With

Self-test failure during power-on preventing AED readiness, often related to battery, pads, or environmental/storage issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If this AED is needed for a patient, immediately obtain a backup AED
  • Do not rely on a unit that has failed self-test

Expected: Patient care is not delayed
Why it matters: A failed self-test means the device may not deliver therapy safely

Verify the Reported Failure

  • Power on the AED and observe indicators
  • Audible prompts
  • Status LED (flashing vs solid red)
  • Any error tones or voice alerts

Expected: Confirm consistent self-test failure
Why it matters: Confirms a true fault vs transient condition

Check Battery Installation and Condition (Most Common Cause)

  • Remove and reseat the battery pack
  • Ensure proper orientation and full engagement
  • Inspect contacts for corrosion or debris
  • If available, install a known-good battery

Expected: Device powers on and passes self-test
Why it matters: Low or improperly seated batteries are the most frequent cause of self-test failures

Check Battery Expiration Date

  • Inspect the battery label for expiration

Expected: Battery is within valid date
Why it matters: Expired batteries may not support internal diagnostics even if they power the unit

Inspect Electrode Pads Connection and Expiration

  • Verify pads are properly connected to the AED
  • Confirm pads are within expiration date
  • Ensure packaging is intact (not dried out or damaged)
  • Replace with a known-good pad set if needed

Expected: Device completes self-test successfully
Why it matters: AEDs perform readiness checks on pad presence and condition

Check for Environmental Factors

  • Assess storage conditions
  • Check for excessive heat or cold
  • Check for moisture exposure
  • Inspect for physical damage
  • Allow the device to return to normal room temperature if needed

Expected: Device stabilizes and passes self-test
Why it matters: Environmental stress can affect internal diagnostics and battery performance

Perform a Power Cycle Reset

  • Remove battery for 30–60 seconds
  • Reinstall battery and power on again

Expected: Self-test completes successfully
Why it matters: Clears temporary faults or startup errors

Observe Status Indicator After Troubleshooting

  • Confirm green flashing indicator (ready state)
  • Ensure no audible fault tones

Expected: AED indicates ready for use
Why it matters: Confirms device readiness for emergency use

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (self-test circuitry, internal memory, or system fault).

Remove the AED from service immediately. Label the device Out of Service. Send for manufacturer-approved repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an AED during an active emergency. Always move to a backup unit first, then evaluate the failed device afterward.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"AED failed self-test on power-on with audible alert and red status indicator."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Expired battery and electrode pads failed readiness check."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced battery and pads; AED passed self-test and returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Status indicator color before and after troubleshooting
  • Battery expiration date
  • Pads expiration date
  • Audible tones or voice prompts
  • Environmental storage conditions
  • Physical condition of device
  • Final device status (in service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

AED readiness is critical and non-negotiable. Always start with simple external checks like battery and pads, but escalate quickly when self-tests fail. Proper documentation and decisive action ensure patient safety and system reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
