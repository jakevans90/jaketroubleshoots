Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: Printer Malfunction

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: Printer Malfunction on the Philips HeartStart MRx. This error may cause failed strip printing, printer self-test failure, blank or incomplete printouts, paper feed issues, or loss of printer communication.

The printer may not directly affect shock delivery, but failed printing can affect rhythm strip documentation, event review, code records, and clinical workflow.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the HeartStart MRx from patient use if printing is required for patient care, code documentation, rhythm review, or event recording.
  • If the device is actively needed for defibrillation, monitoring, pacing, or critical use, provide a backup defibrillator before troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on a device with an unresolved printer failure when printed documentation is required by clinical workflow or facility policy.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact displayed message is Error: Printer Malfunction.
  • Check whether the error appears during startup, operational check, print attempt, event summary printing, or intermittent use.
  • Confirm the associated behavior, such as no printing, no paper feed, blank printout, incomplete printout, failed self-test, printer door issue, or printer communication failure.
  • Determine whether the issue is reproducible or clearly documented by staff.

3. Check Printer Paper First

  • Open the printer door and verify that approved thermal printer paper is installed correctly.
  • Confirm the paper roll is not empty, jammed, backwards, wrinkled, wet, contaminated, or binding inside the printer compartment.
  • Reload the paper and attempt a print test.
  • If printing resumes normally, complete functional verification before returning the device to service.

4. Check the Printer Door and Latch

  • Inspect the printer door for proper closure.
  • Verify the latch is not cracked, loose, obstructed, or prevented from fully closing by paper, debris, or damage.
  • Open and close the printer door firmly, then retry printing.
  • If the error clears, document the door or loading correction and complete an operational check.

5. Inspect the Printer Mechanism Externally

  • With the device removed from active use, inspect the visible printer area for torn paper, paper dust buildup, label adhesive, contamination, obstructed paper path, damaged roller, loose parts, impact damage, or fluid intrusion.
  • Clear only visible paper debris or obstruction that can be removed safely without internal disassembly.
  • Do not perform deep disassembly at the clinical area.

6. Power Cycle and Recheck

  • Turn the HeartStart MRx off, wait briefly, then power it back on using a known-good battery or AC power source.
  • Observe whether the Printer Malfunction error returns during startup, operational check, or print attempt.
  • If the error clears, continue with printer verification and a complete operational check.
  • If the error returns, continue troubleshooting because recurring errors indicate a likely printer or communication fault.

7. Run or Review the Printer Self-Test / Operational Check

  • Perform the appropriate operational check or printer-related test available through the device workflow.
  • Confirm whether the printer passes or fails.
  • Verify that the paper feeds correctly and the printout is legible.
  • If the printer fails self-test after paper, door, and external checks are corrected, suspect the printer assembly or related communication path.

8. Check for Accessory or Configuration Confusion

  • Confirm staff are not confusing printer failure with other documentation or workflow issues.
  • Check whether event summary printing was selected properly.
  • Confirm paper output is not missing due to incorrect paper loading or thermal paper orientation.
  • Verify that the complaint is a hardware or communication printer fault, not a user workflow issue.

9. Isolate Printer Assembly vs Communication Path

  • If the printer fails self-test consistently, suspect the printer assembly first.
  • If the printer appears mechanically functional but the device continues to report Printer Malfunction or communication failure, remove the device from service for bench evaluation.
  • During bench evaluation, inspect service-accessible printer connections, including the printer data cable.
  • If printer replacement and cable checks do not correct the issue, escalate evaluation to printer communication or processor-related control electronics.

10. Stop When Resolved

  • After correcting paper loading, door closure, obstruction, or an accessible connection during bench evaluation, perform a complete operational check.
  • Confirm no Printer Malfunction error returns.
  • Confirm the printer feeds correctly, the printout is legible, and the operational check passes.
  • Return the HeartStart MRx to service only after successful verification.

If the Problem Persists

If the Printer Malfunction error remains after paper, door, external printer mechanism, power, and basic operational checks have been completed, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Printer Malfunction error may indicate a failed printer assembly, printer data cable issue, or printer communication/control electronics fault.

Remove the defibrillator from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Recommended repair path is to evaluate or replace the printer assembly first if the printer self-test fails, check the printer data cable if communication failure remains, and evaluate processor-related communication circuitry if printer and cable correction do not resolve the problem.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot printer faults on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first if the printer failure affects documentation, rhythm strips, code record review, or operational readiness.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx reported Error: Printer Malfunction. Staff stated the printer would not print rhythm strips and the error appeared during operational check."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Printer paper and door were checked and reloaded. No paper jam or external obstruction found. Printer self-test continued to fail, indicating likely printer assembly failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for bench repair. Printer replacement recommended; printer data cable and processor communication path to be evaluated if communication failure remains after printer replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Printer errors on a defibrillator should be handled logically. Start with paper, door, loading, and visible mechanism issues before suspecting internal failure. If Printer Malfunction persists after basic checks, escalation is appropriate because the fault may involve the printer assembly, printer data cable, or printer communication electronics. Clear documentation supports patient safety, repair accuracy, and device readiness.

That is successful troubleshooting.
