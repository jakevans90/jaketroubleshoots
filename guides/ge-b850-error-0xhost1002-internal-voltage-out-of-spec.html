GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B850

Error: 0xHOST1002 Internal voltage out of spec

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B850

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0xHOST1002 indicating internal voltage out of specification, potentially causing instability, unexpected shutdowns, or unreliable monitoring performance.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use if actively alarming or behaving unpredictably
  • Transfer monitoring to a backup device

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the monitor and observe for Error 0xHOST1002
  • Note when it occurs (startup or during operation)
  • Check for symptoms like rebooting, display flicker, or shutdowns

3. Check External Power Source (Most Common Cause)

  • Verify connection to a known good hospital-grade outlet
  • Test outlet with another device
  • If on a cart, confirm AC power is stable

4. Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Check for damage, loose fit, or bent pins
  • Reseat connections at both ends
  • Test with a known-good power cord

5. Check Battery Status and Seating

  • Remove and reseat the battery
  • Inspect for swelling or damage
  • Test operation with and without the battery installed

6. Review Internal Voltage via Webmin (If Accessible)

  • Access Webmin and navigate to Hardware Stats
  • Check voltage rail values
  • Look for out-of-range readings

7. Power Cycle the Monitor

  • Shut down completely
  • Disconnect from AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect and power on

8. Isolate Environmental Factors

  • Move device to a different outlet or room
  • Avoid circuits with heavy equipment load

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0xHOST1002 continues after all external checks, internal causes have been ruled out. This indicates a likely internal power regulation or power supply issue.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup monitor before addressing voltage-related errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor displaying Error 0xHOST1002 with intermittent rebooting during use"

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal voltage out of specification; external power, cord, and battery verified good"

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair evaluation"

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Voltage-related errors should always be taken seriously. Start with external checks, but recognize that persistent faults indicate internal failure requiring escalation and repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
