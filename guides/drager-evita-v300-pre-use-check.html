Drager Evita V300 Ventilator

Pre-Use Check Failure

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Evita V300

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the Drager Evita V300 ventilator fails its pre-use check. Symptoms may include:

  • Ventilator displays errors during startup self-test
  • Alarms trigger before patient connection
  • Device does not proceed to ready-to-use mode

Focus is on verifying external and easily accessible components before considering internal failure or service intervention.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the ventilator is connected to a functioning outlet or a fully charged battery.
  • Check the power cord for visible damage.

Reason: A low or interrupted power supply can cause the pre-use check to fail.

Inspect Breathing Circuit and Sensors

  • Confirm that the inspiratory and expiratory circuits are properly connected.
  • Make sure flow sensors, O₂ sensors, and CO₂ sensors are seated and connected.

Reason: Misconnected or missing sensors often trigger pre-use errors.

Check Humidifier and Filters

  • Remove and reseat the humidifier chamber if installed.
  • Inspect bacterial/viral filters for blockage or incorrect installation.

Reason: Obstructions or improperly seated accessories can prevent the self-test from completing.

Perform Circuit Leak Test Manually

  • Use the ventilator’s leak test function to verify integrity of the patient circuit.
  • Look for error messages or pressure inconsistencies.

Reason: Leaks in the circuit will cause pre-use checks to fail.

Inspect Software Status

  • Ensure the device firmware is up-to-date and not in a locked or error state.
  • Reboot the ventilator if it has been idle for a long period.

Reason: Software errors or incomplete startups can cause self-test failures.

Reconnect All Modules

  • Detach and reconnect detachable modules (O₂ module, expiratory module, etc.).
  • Verify that all connectors click into place securely.

Reason: Loose modules may prevent the ventilator from passing self-tests.

If the Problem Persists

If all external and accessible checks have been completed and the pre-use check still fails:

  • Remove the ventilator from service
  • Label it Out of Service
  • Contact biomedical engineering service or Drager-authorized repair for bench evaluation

Do not attempt internal board-level repair. Knowing when to escalate is proper troubleshooting and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not use on a patient until the pre-use check passes
  • Move the patient to a backup ventilator before troubleshooting
  • Always ensure a functioning ventilator is immediately available in case of failure

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Ventilator fails pre-use check, will not enter ready mode.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Pre-use check error persists after confirming power, sensors, circuits, and modules are properly connected.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Ventilator removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for service evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Battery status checked
  • Circuit connections inspected
  • Filters and humidifier chamber verified
  • Alarm messages captured
  • Device status at removal from service

Final Thought

Patient safety and logical troubleshooting come first. Always start with external checks before assuming internal failure. Document each step clearly and escalate when necessary to ensure the ventilator is safe for patient care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
