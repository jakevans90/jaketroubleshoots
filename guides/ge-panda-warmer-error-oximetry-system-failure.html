GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Oximetry System Failure

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Oximetry System Failure when optional SpO2 monitoring is unavailable or unreliable because the oximetry communication path is not functioning normally.

This condition may prevent reliable oxygen saturation monitoring through the warmer and requires alternate monitoring before evaluation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the warmer is supporting an infant and SpO2 monitoring is required, notify clinical staff immediately.
  • Provide an alternate approved pulse oximetry monitoring method before troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on the Panda Warmer optional SpO2 function while Oximetry System Failure remains active.

Expected outcome: The patient is safely monitored by reliable alternate equipment while the warmer is evaluated.

Why it matters: A failed or intermittent SpO2 pathway can result in missing, unreliable, or unavailable oxygen saturation monitoring.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use as appropriate, confirm that Oximetry System Failure is displayed.
  • Observe whether the error occurs immediately at startup, when an SpO2 sensor or cable is connected, during attempted monitoring, or intermittently during operation.
  • Document associated symptoms, including absent SpO2 readings, intermittent operation, failure to recognize an accessory, or repeated system failure messages.

Expected outcome: The error is reproducible or clearly associated with an SpO2 malfunction.

Why it matters: This helps separate a true oximetry system problem from incorrect setup or an isolated accessory concern.

3. Inspect External SpO2 Accessories and Connections

  • Examine the SpO2 sensor, patient cable, extension cable, and accessible external connector area.
  • Check for bent or recessed contacts, cracked connector housings, frayed or pinched cables, contamination, fluid intrusion, or loose connections.
  • Reconnect external SpO2 accessories securely.
  • Replace a visibly damaged or suspected defective external sensor or cable with a compatible known-good accessory when available.

Expected outcome: External accessories and accessible connections are intact, clean, and firmly seated.

Why it matters: Damaged or loose external oximetry accessories can create symptoms similar to a communication-path failure.

4. Confirm Whether the Failure Is Isolated to SpO2

  • Operate the warmer only in a safe, non-patient test condition.
  • Determine whether basic warmer functions remain normal while the SpO2 option is unavailable.
  • Document whether the error affects only SpO2 monitoring or whether other warmer functions or displayed values are also abnormal.
  • Note whether the failure changes when an external accessory is connected, disconnected, or gently repositioned.

Expected outcome: The problem is identified as isolated to the optional oximetry pathway or recognized as part of a broader device concern.

Why it matters: Proper isolation helps guide repair escalation and prevents unnecessary parts replacement.

5. Inspect Accessible External Connector Areas and Cable Routing

  • Inspect visible SpO2 cable routing and accessible connector areas for pinching, strain, chafing, contamination, or intermittent contact.
  • Confirm that external accessories remain seated without recreating the failure during normal cable positioning.
  • Do not open the warmer or perform internal disassembly during bedside troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: No external connection, cable-routing, or accessory condition explains the failure.

Why it matters: External causes should be ruled out before sending the device for internal evaluation.

6. Confirm Resolution Before Return to Service

  • After correcting an external accessory or connection issue, power the warmer on and confirm that Oximetry System Failure no longer appears.
  • Using approved functional verification methods, confirm that the SpO2 option initializes and operates normally.
  • Observe for intermittent recurrence during testing.
  • Document the corrective action and final device status.

Expected outcome: The error clears, the SpO2 function is available, and the failure does not return during verification.

If the Problem Persists

If external sensors, patient cables, extension cables, accessible connector areas, and visible cable-routing concerns have been ruled out and Oximetry System Failure remains present, the failure may involve an internal SpO2 interface, harness, flex circuit, communication connection, or control-related problem.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Qualified service evaluation may require inspection of the SpO2 communication path and assessment of the SpO2 Interface PCA or Control PCA as indicated.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an unresolved SpO2 system failure while an infant depends on the warmer for oximetry monitoring. Move the patient to a backup monitoring device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Oximetry System Failure. Optional SpO2 monitoring was unavailable or intermittent during startup and functional verification."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection identified an external SpO2 cable connection that was not fully seated, resulting in loss of reliable optional SpO2 operation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from patient use, secured the external SpO2 connection, inspected associated accessories and visible cabling, completed functional verification, and confirmed Oximetry System Failure no longer returned. Device returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

An Oximetry System Failure on the GE Healthcare Panda Warmer should be treated as a reliability concern for the optional SpO2 monitoring pathway. Protect the patient with alternate monitoring first, rule out external accessories and accessible connection issues logically, and escalate the device for qualified repair when the condition remains unresolved. Clear CCR documentation records what was reported, what was found, and how the device was safely managed.

That is successful troubleshooting.
