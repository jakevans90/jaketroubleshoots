Stryker S3

Error: 207 Zero Fail

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Error 207 Zero Fail, where the Stryker S3 cannot establish a stable scale zero value. This condition affects the reliability of the Scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness systems and may prevent safe use of monitoring or weighing functions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use if Error 207 Call Service is present and Scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness functions are required for patient care.
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, known-working bed before performing scale troubleshooting or attempting to zero the bed.
  • Do not rely on the affected bed's weight, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness status while this fault is active.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without dependence on unreliable monitoring or weighing functions.

Why it matters: This error indicates that the bed cannot establish a stable scale zero point, which may make safety-related monitoring functions unreliable.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the bed removed from patient-dependent use, power the bed as appropriate and confirm that the display reports Error 207 Call Service.
  • Determine whether the error occurs during a scale zeroing attempt, after accessories were added or removed, after the bed was moved, or intermittently while stationary.
  • Confirm associated behavior such as inability to zero the scale, questionable weight readings, or unavailable or unreliable Bed Exit and iBED Awareness functions.

Expected outcome: Error 207 is reproduced during the zeroing process or clearly confirmed in the error history.

Why it matters: This helps distinguish a true scale-system fault from an incorrect setup, loading condition, or procedure issue.

3. Confirm the Bed Is Properly Unloaded for Zeroing

  • Remove the patient before attempting scale troubleshooting.
  • Remove or account for equipment and accessories as required by facility procedure and the applicable bed setup instructions.
  • Check for linens, bags, pumps, personal items, drainage bags, tubing under tension, cords, hoses, or cables affecting the weighing structure.
  • Confirm that no equipment is contacting the floor, wall, or another device while transferring force to the bed.
  • Retry zeroing only when the bed is properly prepared and free of unintended loading.

Expected outcome: The bed is unloaded and able to complete a stable zero procedure without Error 207.

Why it matters: Shifting weight, cable tension, or external contact can prevent the scale system from obtaining a repeatable zero value.

4. Inspect for External Contact or Mechanical Interference

  • Inspect the bed from all sides and underneath without performing internal disassembly.
  • Verify that the bed frame is not contacting a wall, furniture, equipment, or floor obstruction.
  • Check that siderails are not binding against attached equipment or accessories.
  • Ensure caster areas are free of debris, cords, tubing, or other objects.
  • Confirm that cables, nurse-call cords, and accessory lines are not supporting weight or pulling on the bed.
  • Look for visible damage, bent components, contamination, or lodged objects that may affect the weighing structure.

Expected outcome: The bed rests freely without external contact, binding, or load transfer.

Why it matters: A scale system may appear defective when an object or cable is creating unstable force on the weighing structure.

5. Verify Stable Bed Position and Basic Operating Conditions

  • Place the bed on a firm, level surface.
  • Verify the bed is not rocking, shifting, or positioned on an uneven threshold or obstruction.
  • Confirm reliable power is available and note whether any additional system errors are present.
  • Allow the bed to remain stationary before retrying the zero procedure.

Expected outcome: The bed remains stable and stationary during the scale procedure.

Why it matters: Movement or uneven support during zeroing can produce unstable scale data and repeated zero failures.

6. Review Scale Information and Retry the Applicable Procedure

  • Use the appropriate Maintenance Menu function to review available scale-system information and confirm whether the zero failure remains active.
  • With the bed unloaded, stable, and free of interference, retry the applicable scale zeroing procedure.
  • Document whether the bed zeros successfully, the zero value remains unstable, another scale or load-cell-related error appears, or affected monitoring functions remain unavailable.

Expected outcome: The bed obtains a stable zero value and Error 207 no longer returns.

Why it matters: Once external causes are eliminated, continued failure during the proper procedure supports isolation to the scale system.

7. Check Accessible Scale-System Connections

  • If Error 207 remains after setup and interference checks, inspect accessible scale-system wiring and connections for obvious damage, pinching, contamination, looseness, or disconnection.
  • Review whether the error is accompanied by another load-cell error that identifies a specific bed location.
  • Do not proceed into board-level diagnosis or internal component repair unless authorized and supported by the appropriate service procedure.

Expected outcome: An accessible connection issue is corrected, or the failure is identified as requiring further bench evaluation.

Why it matters: Persistent unstable zeroing after environmental and setup checks may indicate a load-cell, wiring, or scale-electronics failure.

8. Verify Function Before Return to Service

  • If removing interference, correcting bed position, adjusting accessories, or correcting an accessible connection allows the bed to complete a stable zero procedure, confirm Error 207 does not return.
  • Verify scale function using the applicable functional check.
  • Verify Bed Exit and iBED Awareness functions as appropriate before returning the bed to service.
  • Document the condition found and corrective action completed.

Expected outcome: The bed completes zeroing and all affected functions perform normally.

Why it matters: The bed should not be returned to clinical use until the functions affected by the error have been verified.

If the Problem Persists

If Error 207 Zero Fail remains after the bed is unloaded, stable, free of external interference, and the applicable scale procedure has been retried, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent zero failure may indicate a problem involving the load cells, scale-system wiring, or scale electronics.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation according to facility and manufacturer service procedures.

Do not rely on the Scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness systems while the fault remains active.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first, and never depend on unresolved Scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness functions for patient care or fall-risk monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displays Error 207 Call Service / Zero Fail during scale zeroing. Scale will not obtain a stable zero value; Scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness functions are considered unreliable."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Bed inspected unloaded and stationary. No external frame contact, cable tension, caster obstruction, accessory interference, or unstable positioning found. Error remained after applicable zero procedure was retried, indicating a likely scale-system, load-cell, wiring, or electronics failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from clinical service, labeled Out of Service, and routed for further scale-system bench evaluation or repair. Staff advised not to rely on Scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness functions while fault is present."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error 207 indicates more than a failed weighing attempt: the bed cannot establish a reliable scale baseline, affecting functions tied to patient safety. Logical troubleshooting begins with patient safety, removal of external interference, confirmation of stable setup, and appropriate escalation when the fault persists. Clear CCR documentation preserves what was observed, ruled out, and corrected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
