Baxter PrismaFlex

Alarm: Scale Zero Test

Asset Type

Continuous Renal Replacement Therapy (CRRT)

Manufacturer

Baxter

Model

PrismaFlex

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a Baxter PrismaFlex Scale Zero Test alarm. This alarm occurs when one or more scales cannot complete the zero test because of a setup issue, obstruction, missing carrying bar, unexpected weight on the scale, or a failed scale zero detection condition.

Scale accuracy is critical during CRRT because fluid balance monitoring depends on reliable scale readings. A scale zero failure should be corrected before setup or treatment preparation continues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the PrismaFlex is connected to an active patient and the alarm affects safe operation, follow clinical protocol and make sure the patient is supported before troubleshooting.
  • Do not ignore a scale-related alarm during CRRT setup or treatment preparation.
  • Do not continue therapy preparation if scale accuracy cannot be trusted.
  • Expected outcome: Patient care is not dependent on a CRRT machine with unresolved scale zero failure.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the displayed alarm is Scale Zero Test.
  • Identify whether the alarm occurs during startup, setup, prime, scale zero testing, or after bags or carrying bars were installed.
  • Ask whether the alarm began after the machine was moved, cleaned, or repositioned.
  • Observe whether one scale or multiple scales appear affected.
  • Expected outcome: The alarm is confirmed and the condition is reproducible or clearly observed.

Remove Bags From the Scale

  • Check each scale and confirm no bag is unexpectedly present during the zero test.
  • Remove any dialysate, replacement, effluent, or accessory bags from scales that should be empty for the zero test.
  • Close the scale and press RETEST.
  • Expected outcome: The alarm clears if the scale was detecting weight when it expected zero.

Check for Missing Carrying Bars

  • Inspect the carrying bars for each scale location.
  • Confirm all required carrying bars are installed correctly and seated properly.
  • Reinstall any missing or incorrectly positioned carrying bar.
  • Close the scale and press RETEST.
  • Expected outcome: The zero test passes once the expected scale hardware is present.

Check for Foreign Objects or Physical Interference

  • Inspect around each scale and carrying bar for anything touching, hanging, resting, or pulling on the scale.
  • Look for tubing caught on a carrying bar.
  • Look for bag hooks, straps, cleaning residue, debris, or objects hanging from the carrying bars.
  • Check for items leaning against the scale door or scale area.
  • Check for cables or tubing applying tension to the scale.
  • Remove the obstruction, confirm the scale moves freely, close the scale, and press RETEST.
  • Expected outcome: The alarm clears if the scale was being loaded or restricted by an external object.

Confirm Proper Scale Closure and Machine Position

  • Verify that the scale door or scale area is fully closed.
  • Confirm the PrismaFlex is stable and not tilted, bumped, or positioned where tubing or bags are pulling sideways on the scale.
  • Make sure the scale is in a stable, unloaded, normal zero-test condition.
  • Expected outcome: The machine can complete scale zero testing with a clean unloaded baseline.

Repeat the Zero Test

  • After removing bags, reinstalling carrying bars, and clearing interference, close the scale and press RETEST.
  • Confirm whether the Scale Zero Test alarm clears.
  • If the alarm clears, continue setup according to clinical workflow and verify no additional scale alarms return.
  • Document the specific external cause, such as bag left on scale, missing carrying bar, or tubing contacting the scale.

If the Problem Persists

If the Scale Zero Test alarm does not clear after bags, carrying bars, foreign objects, scale closure, machine position, and physical interference have been checked, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent scale zero test failure may indicate a scale calibration problem, scale sensor issue, or failed scale assembly.

Remove the PrismaFlex from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

For service or bench evaluation, evaluate the scales in Diagnose Screen – Scale Diagnose, calibrate scales if required, perform a new prime after service action, and replace any scale that continues to fail zero testing.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first.

Do not continue CRRT setup or treatment preparation with an unresolved Scale Zero Test alarm. Fluid balance accuracy depends on reliable scale function.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Baxter PrismaFlex reported Scale Zero Test alarm during setup. Zero test failed and machine would not proceed normally."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found external scale interference from tubing contacting the carrying bar. Scale could not complete zero test until obstruction was removed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed tubing from scale area, verified carrying bars installed correctly, closed scale, pressed RETEST, and confirmed Scale Zero Test alarm cleared. Device returned to service after successful setup verification."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Scale alarms on a CRRT system must be treated carefully because they affect fluid accuracy and therapy safety. Start with visible setup issues first, confirm the scale is unloaded and unobstructed, then escalate when the alarm persists after common causes are ruled out. Clear CCR documentation helps show whether the issue was corrected externally or required service-level repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
