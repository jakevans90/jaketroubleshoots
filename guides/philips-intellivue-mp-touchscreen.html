Philips IntelliVue MP Series

Touchscreen / Display Not Responding

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

IntelliVue MP Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the touchscreen on a Philips IntelliVue MP Series patient monitor is unresponsive. The focus is on simple, external, and easily verifiable causes first—such as power, connections, environmental factors, and user interaction—before assuming internal hardware failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check Power Source

  • Ensure the monitor is plugged into a live AC outlet or has sufficient battery charge.
  • Observe power indicator lights. No lights may indicate power issue.

Why: Touchscreen unresponsiveness can occur if the device has insufficient or unstable power.

Inspect Cables and Connections

  • Verify all cables (power, patient leads, network) are properly seated and undamaged.
  • Remove and reseat cables if necessary.

Why: Loose or damaged connections can cause the touchscreen to appear unresponsive.

Perform a Soft Reboot

  • Use the monitor’s power button to turn it off, wait 10–15 seconds, and turn it back on.

Why: A software freeze can often cause the touchscreen to stop responding.

Clean the Touchscreen Surface

  • Wipe the screen with a soft, lint-free cloth slightly dampened with approved cleaning solution.
  • Ensure no liquids enter edges or ports.

Why: Residue, moisture, or debris can interfere with capacitive touch detection.

Check for Environmental Interference

  • Ensure there are no strong electromagnetic sources near the monitor (e.g., unshielded motors, large phones, MRI, etc.).

Why: Strong EM interference can cause touchscreens to behave erratically.

Test Touchscreen Responsiveness

  • After reboot and cleaning, touch multiple points on the screen to confirm responsiveness.
  • Document any areas of non-response.

Why: Confirms whether the issue is intermittent, localized, or total failure.

If the Problem Persists

If the above steps do not restore touchscreen functionality:

  • The monitor may have an internal touchscreen controller or display failure.
  • Remove from service and label as Out of Service.
  • Send for Philips-authorized repair or internal bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt touchscreen troubleshooting while the patient is connected.
  • Move the patient to another monitor before working on the unresponsive device.
  • Ensure any patient care is uninterrupted and safe.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
User reported that the IntelliVue MP touchscreen was not responding to input.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed that monitor powered on, but touch input produced no response; cables intact; screen surface clean; soft reboot did not restore function.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Monitor removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes
  • Power indicator lights: Observed
  • Reboot attempted: Yes
  • Touchscreen cleaning performed: Yes
  • Any unusual sounds, heat, or smell: None observed
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Always start with external checks—power, cables, and software reset—before assuming internal failure. Documentation and proper escalation maintain patient safety and help track recurring device issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
