Drager Evita V500

Alarm: Accessory ID detection failed accessory not recognized by ventilator

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Evita V500

What This Guide Helps With

Accessory ID detection failed alarm where the ventilator cannot recognize connected components, which may impact monitoring accuracy and accessory-dependent ventilation functions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Assess the patient and confirm adequate ventilation
  • Transfer the patient to a backup ventilator if reliability is in question
  • Do not troubleshoot while actively ventilating a patient

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the “Accessory ID detection failed” alarm is displayed
  • Determine when the alarm occurs (startup or during operation)
  • Identify which accessory is connected (flow sensor, expiratory valve, circuit)
  • Observe any loss of monitoring or functionality

Check Accessory Connections

  • Disconnect and reconnect all accessories firmly
  • Ensure proper alignment and full seating
  • Verify any locking mechanisms are engaged

Inspect Accessory Condition

  • Check connectors for bent pins, debris, or moisture
  • Inspect cables and housings for visible damage
  • Clean contacts per facility protocol if needed

Verify Correct Accessory Type

  • Confirm accessory is approved for Evita V500 use
  • Replace with a known-good compatible accessory

Power Cycle the Ventilator

  • Place ventilator in standby
  • Power off, wait 30 seconds, and restart
  • Reconnect accessory and observe alarm status

Isolate Accessory vs Device Issue

  • Test accessory on another compatible ventilator if available
  • Test a different accessory on this ventilator
  • Determine if issue follows accessory or remains with device

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and the alarm continues:

  • Remove the ventilator from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup ventilator before attempting troubleshooting steps.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator displaying 'Accessory ID detection failed' alarm; accessory not recognized during setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose connection or damaged accessory prevented proper identification by the ventilator."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated accessory connections and replaced with known-good accessory; verified proper detection and cleared alarm."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Accessory detection alarms are often caused by simple connection or accessory issues. Always start with external checks and confirm compatibility before escalating. When unresolved, remove the device from service to ensure patient safety and proper repair handling.

That is successful troubleshooting.
