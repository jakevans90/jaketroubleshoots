Berchtold Chromophare Surgical Light

Arm Balance or Suspension Drift Issues

Asset Type

Surgical Light

Manufacturer

Berchtold

Model

Chromophare Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where a Berchtold Chromophare surgical light arm drifts or does not stay in the intended position. Symptoms may include the light slowly dropping or rising on its own, difficulty in positioning, or uneven support when moving the arm. The focus is on external, verifiable causes before considering internal adjustments or repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power and Control Settings

  • Ensure the light is plugged into a stable power source.
  • Some models have electronically assisted suspension; verify the control panel or wall controller shows normal operation.

Why: Power fluctuations or inactive motorized assists can affect suspension tension.

Inspect Mechanical Locks and Brakes

  • Check that all arm rotation locks and brake knobs are fully engaged and not loose.
  • Tighten or adjust according to manufacturer specifications without disassembly.

Why: Loose brakes can cause the arm to drift even if internal balance is correct.

Check Arm Joints and Pivots

  • Visually inspect joints for wear, corrosion, or debris.
  • Move the arm through its full range and listen for unusual resistance or grinding.

Why: Dirt or wear at pivot points can create uneven tension and drift.

Assess Counterbalance Mechanisms

  • Identify the spring, gas, or hydraulic balance mechanism (visible externally if possible).
  • Ensure cables or springs are properly seated and not visibly stretched, kinked, or detached.

Why: Improperly seated counterbalance components are a common cause of drift.

Test Movement Under Load

  • Gently move the light head up and down while observing for drift.
  • If the light drifts under normal load, note the direction and speed.

Why: Confirms whether the issue is balance-related or external obstruction.

Check for Environmental Factors

  • Ensure the mounting surface is secure and level.
  • Verify that no external equipment or cables are pulling on the arm.

Why: External forces can mimic suspension failure.

If the Problem Persists

External adjustments have been ruled out, and drift continues:

  • Remove the surgical light from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Escalate to manufacturer service or a qualified repair bench evaluation.

Clinical Engineering principle: knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Avoid internal spring or motor repairs unless certified.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not adjust the light while a patient is on the table.
  • Move the patient or schedule a room with a functional light before further troubleshooting.
  • Only perform checks when the environment is safe and there is no risk to ongoing procedures.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"The surgical light arm drifts downward when positioned; it does not stay in place."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed loose brake knobs and slight misalignment of pivot points causing uneven suspension tension.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Tightened all brake knobs and inspected pivot points. Noted that arm drift persists; light removed from service and labeled Out of Service for manufacturer evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Check which arm(s) are affected.
  • Note any unusual noise, roughness, or resistance when moving the arm.
  • Document whether drift occurs unloaded or under typical surgical load.
  • Record final device status (Out of Service, pending repair).

Final Thought

Patient safety comes first: never attempt internal spring or motor repairs unless trained and certified. Logic-based troubleshooting and accurate documentation help maintain safe surgical environments and avoid unnecessary downtime. Escalating appropriately prevents further equipment damage and ensures consistent operating room functionality.

That is successful troubleshooting.
