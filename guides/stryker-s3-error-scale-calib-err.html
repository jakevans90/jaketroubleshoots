Stryker S3

Error: Scale Calib. Err

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Scale Calib. Err when the installed scale is unavailable or unreliable because calibration has not been completed. An uncalibrated scale may provide inaccurate patient weights and can affect scale-based functions such as Bed Exit or related monitoring features.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use for any care that depends on accurate patient weight, Bed Exit, or related scale-based monitoring until the scale system is verified.
  • Transfer the patient to a known-good bed or use an approved alternate weighing method when an accurate weight is clinically required.
  • Do not rely on the bed scale while the calibration condition remains unresolved.

Expected outcome: The patient is not dependent on a bed with an unverified scale system.

Why it matters: An uncalibrated scale may provide inaccurate weights or affect functions that depend on scale data.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Bed Behavior

  • With the bed removed from use, power the bed normally and confirm that Scale Calib. Err is displayed in the lower status screen during normal operating mode.
  • Confirm that the bed is equipped with the scale system.
  • Note whether the message appears at startup or during normal operation.
  • Check for any additional scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness error messages.

Expected outcome: Scale Calib. Err is clearly observed and associated with an installed scale system requiring calibration.

Why it matters: This confirms a calibration-related condition rather than an incorrect report or unrelated scale failure.

3. Check Power, Bed Condition, and External Interference

  • Confirm the bed is connected to a known-good power source and operates normally aside from the scale calibration message.
  • Inspect the power cord and visible cables for damage.
  • Check for objects touching the bed frame, base, siderails, or casters.
  • Remove or reposition cables, hoses, or linens that may pull against the bed.
  • Remove excess equipment or accessories not intended to remain on the bed during calibration.
  • Look for visible damage, contamination, binding, instability, or interference with the weighing structure.
  • Position the bed on a firm, level surface with stable casters and no external contact affecting the bed.

Expected outcome: The bed is stable, powered correctly, and free from external conditions that could affect calibration.

Why it matters: Outside forces or unstable positioning may prevent accurate scale calibration.

4. Confirm the Bed Is Prepared for Scale Calibration

  • Remove the patient before calibration.
  • Prepare the mattress, linens, and permanently installed accessories according to facility practice and applicable service information.
  • Confirm the bed configuration is appropriate for the calibration procedure.
  • Have the specified calibration weight available and confirm it is suitable for use.

Expected outcome: The bed is unloaded, properly configured, and ready for the calibration sequence.

Why it matters: Calibration performed with an incorrect setup may produce inaccurate patient weight readings after the error is cleared.

5. Perform the Scale Calibration Procedure

  • Enter the Maintenance Menu and select Calib. Scale.
  • Follow the displayed prompts exactly, including prompts such as Do Not Touch Bed, Place Weight in Center, the Trendelenburg positioning step, and the reverse Trendelenburg positioning step.
  • Do not lean on, move, or contact the bed unless directed by the procedure.
  • Place the calibration weight centered on the support surface when instructed.
  • Allow each step to complete before moving to the next prompt.
  • Do not interrupt power during calibration.

Expected outcome: The bed completes the scale calibration sequence and the Scale Calib. Err message no longer appears.

Why it matters: This error indicates that calibration has not been completed, making completion of the prompted sequence the primary corrective action.

6. Verify Scale Performance After Calibration

  • Return the bed to normal operating mode and confirm that Scale Calib. Err is no longer displayed.
  • Verify scale performance using a known test weight or approved functional verification method.
  • Confirm that the displayed weight is stable and reasonably matches the applied known weight.
  • Check for any related messages involving the scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness functions.

Expected outcome: The scale displays a stable, accurate test weight and no calibration or related scale error is present.

Why it matters: Clearing the message alone does not prove that the scale is safe for clinical use.

7. Stop When Resolved

  • If calibration completes successfully, the error message clears, and scale performance verifies correctly, document the service action and return the bed to service.
  • Return the bed to patient use only after the scale system operates normally and accurately.

If the Problem Persists

If Scale Calib. Err remains displayed, the calibration cannot be completed, or the scale fails verification after calibration, external and common calibration-preparation causes have been ruled out.

A persistent calibration condition may indicate an issue involving load-cell performance, wiring or connection integrity, weighing structure interference, or associated bed electronics.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not return a hospital bed to clinical use when the scale remains uncalibrated or cannot be verified as accurate.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or calibrate the scale on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first, and never rely on patient weight, Bed Exit, or related scale-based monitoring until calibration has been completed and verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displayed Scale Calib. Err in normal operating mode. Installed scale system was not available for reliable patient weighing until calibration was completed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Scale system calibration had not been completed. Bed was inspected for external interference and prepared for calibration with no abnormal contact or instability identified."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from patient use, entered the Maintenance Menu, selected Calib. Scale, and completed the displayed calibration sequence using the required centered test weight and prompted bed-position steps. Verified stable scale performance with a known weight and confirmed the Scale Calib. Err message cleared. Bed returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Scale Calib. Err message means the Stryker S3 scale cannot yet be trusted for patient care. Protect the patient first, rule out simple external interference, complete the prompted calibration correctly, verify scale performance, and escalate the bed when calibration cannot be successfully completed. Accurate CCR documentation confirms why the bed was safe to return or why it remained out of service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
