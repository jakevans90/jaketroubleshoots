GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Side-Rail Latch or Movement Faults

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting side-rail movement or latch faults on the GE Panda Warmer. Symptoms may include difficulty raising or lowering side rails, rails not locking securely, unusual resistance or looseness, or alarms related to rail position. The focus is on external checks and mechanical verification before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Device is Powered Off and Safe

  • Verify the warmer is not in use with a patient.
  • Unplug from AC power if possible.

Why: Prevents accidental movement that could harm staff or patients.

Inspect the Side Rails Visually

  • Check for visible damage, bent components, or obstructions.
  • Confirm rails are properly seated in their mounting points.

Why: Physical damage or misalignment is a common cause of latch faults.

Test Rail Movement Manually

  • Slowly raise and lower the side rails.
  • Listen for unusual resistance, clicking, or grinding.
  • Check that the latch engages fully when raised.

Why: Confirms mechanical operation and identifies points of friction or obstruction.

Check for Obstructions in the Latch Mechanism

  • Look for debris, bedding, or cables interfering with latches.
  • Remove any foreign objects carefully.

Why: Even small obstructions can prevent secure locking.

Inspect Latch Springs and Pins

  • Observe whether the latch spring returns to position when released.
  • Ensure pins are aligned and not worn down.

Why: Worn or misaligned latch components can prevent engagement.

Tighten Loose Fasteners

  • Check screws and mounting hardware around the rails and latches.
  • Tighten if necessary, but do not overtighten to avoid damaging plastic components.

Why: Loose fasteners can create play and prevent secure locking.

Test Alarm Function (If Applicable)

  • Some Panda Warmers trigger alarms if side rails are not locked.
  • After adjustments, power on the unit and verify the alarm clears when rails are properly engaged.

If the Problem Persists

Common external issues (obstruction, misalignment, loose hardware) have been ruled out. The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot or manipulate the side rails while a patient is in the warmer. Move the infant to a safe alternative warmer if needed before performing any mechanical checks.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Side rails difficult to move; latch does not engage properly.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed misalignment and debris in the latch mechanism; manual movement indicated resistance.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Cleared debris, adjusted alignment, tightened hardware; rails now move smoothly and latch securely.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Rails tested on both sides for full range of motion.
  • No unusual sounds after adjustment.
  • Latch alarm verified and cleared post-maintenance.
  • Final device status: Functional, ready for clinical use.

Final Thought

Patient safety is the top priority. Start troubleshooting with simple visual and mechanical checks before considering internal repair. Proper escalation and documentation preserve both safety and operational efficiency.

That is successful troubleshooting.
