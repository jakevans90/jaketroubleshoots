ICU Medical Colleague

Door or Cassette Not Recognized

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Colleague

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting ICU Medical Colleague infusion pumps that fail to recognize the door or cassette. These issues can prevent proper pump operation and may trigger alarms or prevent infusion. The guide focuses on external checks such as door alignment, sensor cleanliness, cassette placement, and mechanical obstructions before considering internal component failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Pump Power

  • Ensure the pump is connected to a functioning AC outlet or fully charged battery.
  • Reason: Some recognition errors occur when the pump is not receiving adequate power.

Inspect Door and Cassette for Damage

  • Check for cracks, warping, or broken components on both the door and the cassette.
  • Reason: Physical damage can prevent proper engagement with sensors.

Check Cassette Placement

  • Remove and reinsert the cassette, ensuring it seats fully and aligns with guides.
  • Reason: Misaligned or partially inserted cassettes may not be recognized.

Inspect and Clean Sensor Area

  • Look for dust, liquid residue, or tubing interference near the recognition sensors.
  • Clean gently with a lint-free cloth or recommended cleaning wipe.
  • Reason: Contaminants can block the sensor and trigger “not recognized” errors.

Examine Door Latch and Hinges

  • Confirm the door closes securely and latches correctly.
  • Reason: The pump may not detect the cassette if the door is not fully engaged.

Swap with Known Good Cassette or Door

  • If available, try a different cassette or door to determine if the issue is with the consumable or pump.
  • Reason: This helps isolate whether the problem is mechanical on the pump or the cassette itself.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out; recognition failure may be due to internal sensor or circuit issues.

  • Remove the pump from service
  • Label it “Out of Service”
  • Send it for vendor repair or bench evaluation

Note: Avoid attempting internal sensor repair to maintain patient safety and warranty compliance.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt troubleshooting while the pump is connected to a patient.
  • Move the infusion to another functional pump before investigating the recognition issue.
  • Ensure proper hand hygiene and clean environment when handling cassettes to prevent contamination.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Pump fails to recognize inserted cassette; door appears closed but error persists.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Cassette misaligned; sensor area contaminated with residue preventing proper detection.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Cleaned sensor area, reseated cassette, verified recognition. If issue persisted, pump removed from service and sent for vendor evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Pump power status (AC or battery)
  • Door and cassette inspection findings
  • Sensor cleaning actions
  • Attempts with alternate cassettes or doors
  • Alarm codes or error messages observed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount—ensure infusion continuity before troubleshooting hardware issues. Logical, stepwise evaluation and knowing when to escalate to repair prevent unnecessary downtime and reduce risk. Documentation of each step preserves a clear history for Clinical Engineering and supports cost avoidance through proper maintenance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
