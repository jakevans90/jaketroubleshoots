GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00] Heater Safety Relay Open During Operation

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00] when an open Heater Safety Relay interrupts radiant warmer heat control during operation. This error may result in unavailable or unreliable warming and requires the device to be removed from patient use until safe function is verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the Panda Warmer from patient use if Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00] occurs during warming or preparation for use.
  • Notify clinical staff and transfer the infant to another approved warming device or clinical environment according to facility procedure.
  • Do not rely on the warmer for thermal support while a heater safety relay or heater-control failure remains unresolved.

Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on a warmer with unreliable heat-control operation.

Why it matters: This error indicates the warmer detected an open Heater Safety Relay during run time, which may prevent safe and dependable heater operation.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, confirm that the display reports Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00].
  • Document whether the error occurs immediately at power-up, after the warmer begins operating, when heat is requested, or intermittently during operation.
  • Observe for loss of radiant heat output, inability to enter normal warming operation, repeated system failure messages, audible alarms, or interrupted operation.

Expected outcome: The reported error is reproduced or clearly confirmed from the device error history and observed behavior.

Why it matters: Confirming the exact error helps distinguish a heater-control safety failure from an unrelated complaint or operational concern.

3. Inspect the Warmer for Obvious External Concerns

  • Visually inspect the heater head, control area, power cord, plug, and accessible cabling for physical damage.
  • Check for evidence of fluid intrusion, contamination, discoloration, unusual odor, overheating, loose external components, or strained cabling near moving portions of the warmer.
  • Do not continue powered troubleshooting if there is evidence of burning, heat damage, liquid intrusion, or another unsafe physical condition.

Expected outcome: No visible external condition explains the failure or creates an additional safety concern.

Why it matters: External damage or contamination may contribute to control failures and requires escalation before further testing.

4. Confirm Power Source and Basic Operation

  • Verify the warmer is connected to a known-good, properly grounded outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord and plug for damage, loose connections, or signs of overheating.
  • With the unit out of patient use, power it on and determine whether the same system failure returns during startup or when heater operation is initiated.

Expected outcome: The warmer receives stable power, but the reported system failure either clears or remains reproducible.

Why it matters: A reliable power source should be confirmed before escalating the failure as an internal heater-control issue.

5. Review Accessible Connection Concerns

  • Inspect externally accessible cabling and connections for looseness, damage, contamination, or strain.
  • Document whether the device was moved, impacted, serviced, or exposed to contamination before the error occurred.
  • Do not perform internal disassembly while completing frontline troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: External connection concerns are corrected if identified, or the failure remains without an accessible cause.

Why it matters: Accessible cable damage or connection issues should be ruled out before the warmer is routed for bench-level evaluation.

6. Retest After Correcting Any External Issue

  • After correcting any external power, cord, or accessible connection concern, power the warmer on while it remains out of patient use.
  • Observe whether Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00] returns when the unit enters operation or heat is requested.
  • If the error clears, complete required functional verification and electrical safety testing according to department procedure before considering return to service.

Expected outcome: Normal warming function is restored without recurrence of the error, or the failure remains present and requires escalation.

7. Escalate Persistent Heater-Control Failure

  • If the error remains after external inspection and power verification, remove the warmer from service for bench evaluation.
  • Provide the exact displayed error, observed behavior, and completed external checks to the repair technician or manufacturer-supported service pathway.
  • Service information indicates evaluation of the heater-control pathway, including the Power PCA-to-Control PCA connection and indicated PCA replacement if the failure is confirmed during bench repair.

Expected outcome: The warmer is routed for appropriate repair and post-repair verification rather than returned to patient use with an unresolved heater-control failure.

8. Stop When Resolved

  • Confirm the error no longer recurs.
  • Verify normal warming function according to department procedure.
  • Complete required safety and performance checks.
  • Document findings and corrective action.
  • Return the warmer to service only after it passes all required checks.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00] continues after external inspection, power verification, and accessible connection checks, common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent system failure indicates an unresolved heater-control or safety-relay circuit condition requiring repair evaluation.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the warmer for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not return a warmer with an unresolved heater-control failure to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an infant warmer while it is providing thermal support to an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, because an unresolved heater-control error can result in unavailable or unreliable warming even if the device temporarily appears operational.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00] during operation. Radiant heat function was interrupted and the unit was removed from patient use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00]. Power source and external device condition were acceptable. No accessible external damage or connection issue was identified. Persistent error indicates heater safety-relay or heater-control pathway failure requiring bench evaluation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from clinical use and labeled Out of Service. Routed unit for bench repair evaluation of the heater-control pathway and required post-repair functional and electrical safety testing before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Panda Warmer reporting Sys Fail 09 [0A XX 00 00] is indicating a heater safety-control condition that can compromise dependable warming. Protect the patient first, confirm the error, inspect external power and physical concerns, and escalate safely when the failure remains device-related. Accurate CCR documentation preserves the troubleshooting path and supports safe return-to-service decisions.

That is successful troubleshooting.
