Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000009 An Audio System Error

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000009 indicating audio system failure affecting alarms, tones, or audible alerts critical for patient monitoring.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use if alarm audibility is compromised.
  • Transfer monitoring to a functioning device.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring continues safely on alternate equipment.
  • Why it matters: Loss of audible alarms creates a serious patient safety risk.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error #000000009 is displayed.
  • Test for any audible output (startup tones, alarm sounds, button tones).
  • Expected: No sound or distorted/intermittent audio present.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true audio system fault versus user settings or muted alarms.

Check Volume and Alarm Settings

  • Access device settings and verify alarm volume is turned up and not muted.
  • Ensure alarm silence is not active.
  • Expected: Volume is adjustable and not set to zero or muted.
  • Why it matters: Misconfigured settings can mimic audio failure.
  • If muted/low → adjust settings and retest
  • If normal settings → continue

Inspect for External Audio Indicators or Accessories

  • Check for any connected accessories (e.g., nurse call integration, external speakers if applicable).
  • Ensure connections are secure and not interfering.
  • Expected: No external device is suppressing or redirecting audio.
  • Why it matters: External integrations can override internal audio output.
  • Disconnect accessories and retest if applicable

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the monitor off, unplug from AC power, remove battery (if accessible), wait 30–60 seconds, then restart.
  • Expected: Device reboots and audio returns if issue was temporary.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary software or initialization faults affecting audio hardware.
  • If resolved → return device to service

Check for Physical Damage or Obstruction

  • Inspect speaker openings for debris, fluid ingress, or physical damage.
  • Expected: Speaker area is clean, unobstructed, and undamaged.
  • Why it matters: Blocked or damaged speakers can prevent sound output.
  • Clean debris if present and retest

Evaluate Environmental Factors

  • Confirm the device has not been exposed to fluid spills, cleaning solution intrusion, or excessive humidity.
  • Expected: No signs of moisture or contamination.
  • Why it matters: Audio components are sensitive to fluid damage.

Functional Isolation

  • Determine if the issue is configuration-related, accessory-related, or device-related.
  • Expected: If still no audio, issue is internal to the device.

If the Problem Persists

If Error #000000009 continues after all external checks:

  • External and configuration causes have been ruled out
  • The condition strongly indicates internal audio hardware or system board failure

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a monitor with compromised audio alarms. Visual alarms alone are not sufficient for safe patient monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying Error #000000009 with no audible alarms or tones during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal audio system failure suspected after verifying volume settings, performing power cycle, and ruling out external causes."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Volume settings verified
  • Power cycle performed
  • No audio during startup or alarms
  • Speaker area inspected
  • No external accessories affecting audio
  • Device removed from service

Final Thought

Audio system errors directly impact patient safety. Always verify simple causes first, but escalate quickly when alarm functionality is compromised.

That is successful troubleshooting.
