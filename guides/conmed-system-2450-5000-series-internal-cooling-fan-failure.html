CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series

Internal Cooling Fan Failure Causing Thermal Shutdown

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

CONMED

Model

System 2450 / 5000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a CONMED System 2450 / 5000 ESU when the device shuts down due to internal thermal protection, likely caused by fan failure or airflow obstruction. The guide focuses on verifying external and simple checks first before escalating to internal repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure the device is powered off and unplugged

  • Safety first: thermal shutdown may indicate internal overheating.
  • Prevents accidental burns or electrical hazards.

Inspect the external vents and fan intake areas

  • Check for dust, lint, or surgical drape debris obstructing airflow.
  • Blocked vents can mimic fan failure by causing overheating.

Verify ambient operating conditions

  • Ensure the ESU is not in a confined space near heat sources.
  • High ambient temperature can trigger thermal shutdown.

Check for error codes on the display

  • CONMED may show a specific fan or thermal fault code.
  • Document codes for CCR entry and potential service escalation.

Inspect external fan assembly (if accessible)

  • Some models allow removal of external fan covers.
  • Check for loose connectors, broken blades, or abnormal resistance when spun by hand.

Power on device in a controlled environment

  • With vents clear, monitor for fan start-up and normal sound.
  • If fan fails to spin or overheating recurs within minutes, internal fan likely failed.

If the Problem Persists

All external causes ruled out: blocked vents, ambient heat, and power issues.

Device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for manufacturer repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Engineering should avoid further internal disassembly; overheating components may be hazardous.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not use the ESU on patients if thermal shutdown occurs.
  • Move the patient to an alternative unit while the affected ESU is evaluated.
  • Avoid troubleshooting during active procedures.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“ESU shuts down unexpectedly with thermal error during operation.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed that internal cooling fan did not activate; vents clear, power stable.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for manufacturer repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Error codes displayed (if any)
  • Ambient temperature at time of shutdown
  • Condition of vents and fan housing
  • Any unusual sounds or smells observed
  • Duration of operation before shutdown

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting balances patient safety, logical stepwise evaluation, and knowing when to escalate. Documenting all observations ensures future repairs are efficient and traceable. Avoid attempting internal fan replacement unless authorized and trained—overheating hazards can cause injury.

That is successful troubleshooting.
