Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: Paddles Cable Failure

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the Paddles Cable Failure error on a Philips HeartStart MRx defibrillator. This condition may appear when external paddles are not detected correctly, which can prevent reliable manual defibrillation readiness, paddle-based testing, or therapy delivery.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Philips HeartStart MRx from patient use if the error appears during setup, testing, or clinical readiness checks.
  • Do not rely on external paddles for patient therapy while this error is unresolved.
  • Provide staff with a backup defibrillator or alternate therapy method according to facility protocol.
  • Expected result: Patient care is supported by a reliable defibrillator.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the MRx on and confirm the exact error displayed is Paddles Cable Failure.
  • Observe whether the error appears at startup, when paddles are connected, when paddles are reseated, during an operational check, or during paddle test setup.
  • Check whether other therapy accessories are detected normally, if available and appropriate.
  • Expected result: The error is reproducible or clearly associated with the external paddles connection.

Remove the Device From Clinical Readiness Until Checked

  • If the MRx is assigned to a crash cart, treatment area, or transport role, notify clinical staff that the unit should not remain in service until the paddles issue is corrected.
  • Do not leave a defibrillator with an unresolved therapy accessory error available for emergency response.
  • Expected result: The device is not available for emergency use with an unresolved paddles failure.

Inspect the External Paddles

  • Examine both paddles for cracked housings, broken controls, stuck buttons, contamination, or fluid intrusion.
  • Check the paddle contact surfaces and make sure they are clean and intact.
  • Confirm the paddles are physically suitable for testing.
  • Expected result: Paddles are physically intact with no obvious damage or contamination.

Inspect the Paddles Cable and Strain Relief

  • Check the full length of the paddles cable for cuts, kinks, pinching, crushed areas, exposed conductors, or stretched sections.
  • Pay close attention to strain relief areas near the paddles and near the therapy connector.
  • Gently flex the cable while observing whether the error appears, clears, or changes.
  • Expected result: Cable condition is intact and the error does not change with movement.

Inspect the Therapy Connector

  • Disconnect and reconnect the paddles cable at the MRx therapy connector.
  • Inspect the connector for bent pins, recessed pins, damaged sockets, debris, fluid residue, poor seating, or loose fit.
  • Reconnect the paddles firmly and verify they latch or seat correctly.
  • Expected result: Connector is clean, undamaged, and fully seated.

Check the Paddle Tray and Test Load Path

  • Place the paddles correctly in the paddle tray or test position as applicable for the unit configuration.
  • Verify that the paddles are seated properly.
  • Inspect the tray or test load contact area for damage, contamination, or obstruction.
  • Run the appropriate operational check or user test available to Clinical Engineering.
  • Expected result: The MRx recognizes the paddles and completes testing without the paddles cable error.

Clean External Contact Areas if Needed

  • If contamination is found on paddle contacts, tray contacts, or connector exterior, clean according to facility-approved procedures and manufacturer-compatible cleaning practices.
  • Allow all cleaned areas to dry fully before retesting.
  • Expected result: No residue, moisture, or debris remains on external contact areas.

Try Known-Good External Paddles if Available

  • Connect a known-good compatible external paddles set to the MRx.
  • Power cycle the device and repeat the operational check.
  • Expected result: The error clears with known-good paddles if the original paddles or cable assembly is the cause.

Compare With Another Compatible MRx if Appropriate

  • If another compatible test unit is available and facility policy allows, connect the suspect paddles to that unit for confirmation.
  • Expected result: The same error follows the suspect paddles if the paddles cable assembly has failed.
  • If the error stays with the original MRx when known-good paddles are used, suspect a device-side connector or therapy pathway issue.

Check for Device-Side Connector Damage

  • If known-good paddles also fail on the original MRx, inspect the MRx therapy connector area for mechanical damage, looseness, broken latch points, or signs of impact.
  • Do not disassemble beyond normal Clinical Engineering access unless authorized and trained.
  • Expected result: Device-side connector is mechanically secure and undamaged.

Run Required Operational and Safety Checks After Any Correction

  • If the error clears after reseating, cleaning, or replacing paddles, perform the required operational check for the MRx.
  • Confirm that the unit passes defibrillator readiness testing before returning it to service.
  • Expected result: The device passes testing with no recurring Paddles Cable Failure error.

If the Problem Persists

If the Paddles Cable Failure error remains after inspecting the paddles, cable, strain relief, therapy connector, connector pins, paddle tray, test load path, and trying known-good paddles, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent failure may indicate a device-side issue such as a damaged therapy connector, internal therapy pathway fault, or therapy circuitry failure.

Remove the defibrillator from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot paddle detection problems on an active patient. Move the patient to a reliable backup defibrillator first, then remove the affected MRx from clinical use until the error is corrected and the device passes required testing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx displayed Paddles Cable Failure during readiness check. External paddles were not detected properly, preventing reliable paddle-based therapy readiness."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"External paddles cable connection issue found at the therapy connector. Cable was reseated and connector area inspected. No device-side damage observed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Inspected paddles, cable strain relief, connector pins, and paddle tray/test load path. Reseated paddles cable and performed operational check. Error cleared and device passed testing. Returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Paddles cable errors on a defibrillator must be treated seriously because they may affect therapy readiness. Start with visible external causes such as paddles, cable condition, strain relief, connector seating, pins, and test load path. If known-good paddles do not clear the error, stop and escalate the device for repair. Clear documentation supports safe service decisions and protects patient care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
