Siemens Healthineers Multix Impact

Tube Stand, Wall Stand, Table, or Bucky Positioning Error

Asset Type

Radiographic System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

Multix Impact

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting positioning, movement, alignment, table, wall stand, tube stand, or Bucky readiness errors before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the Multix Impact is not actively positioning around a patient before troubleshooting movement or alignment issues.

If the issue occurs during patient setup, stop all movement and have clinical staff safely move the patient away from the table, wall stand, tube stand, and detector area if needed.

Expected outcome: No patient is exposed to unintended system movement, collision risk, or delayed imaging on an unreliable system.

Confirm the Exact Positioning Complaint

Ask the radiologic technologist what failed and when it happened.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Tube stand movement
  • Tube angulation
  • Wall stand positioning
  • Table movement
  • Table lock or release
  • Bucky movement
  • Detector tray seating
  • Alignment or centering error
  • System not recognizing the selected position
  • Exposure inhibited due to positioning status

Expected outcome: The fault is narrowed to the tube stand, wall stand, table, Bucky, detector tray, or positioning interlock.

Check for Patient, Staff, or Object Interference

Inspect the table, wall stand, tube stand, floor path, and Bucky area for anything blocking travel.

Look for stools, step platforms, cables, linen, patient belongings, foot pedals, detector cables, or accessories interfering with movement.

Expected outcome: Movement path is clear. If obstruction removal resolves the error, verify normal operation with staff and stop troubleshooting.

Verify All Mechanical Locks and Releases Are Fully Engaged or Released

Check whether the tube stand, wall stand, table, and Bucky locks are in the correct state for the requested movement.

Confirm manual release handles, brake releases, detents, and locking controls are not stuck halfway between positions.

Expected outcome: The system recognizes a valid lock or release state. If the error clears after correcting a lock position, document and return to service if function is normal.

Check Tube Stand Position and Alignment

Confirm the tube stand is not at a travel limit, angled unexpectedly, or positioned outside the valid range for the selected exam.

Return the tube stand to a normal centered or detented position if appropriate and safe.

Expected outcome: The tube stand position is recognized and the system allows normal positioning or exposure readiness.

Check Wall Stand Position and Detector/Bucky Seating

Inspect the wall stand and Bucky assembly for proper seating, full travel engagement, and obvious misalignment.

Confirm the wall stand Bucky is not between detents or partially positioned.

Expected outcome: The wall stand and Bucky report a valid position. If reseating or repositioning resolves the issue, perform a basic functional check before returning to use.

Check Table Position and Tabletop Travel

Confirm the table is not at a travel limit or blocked by accessories.

Check that the tabletop is not binding against rails, patient supports, compression accessories, cassette holders, or nearby equipment.

Expected outcome: Table movement is smooth, controlled, and recognized by the system.

Verify Detector Tray or Bucky Is Fully Inserted

If the error involves the Bucky or detector area, confirm the tray is fully closed and properly latched.

A partially seated detector tray or Bucky can prevent the system from confirming readiness.

Expected outcome: The Bucky or detector tray reaches its proper seated position and the positioning error clears.

Check the Selected Exam and Positioning Requirements

Review the selected exam protocol with the technologist.

Some positioning errors may occur if the selected protocol expects a specific detector, wall stand, table Bucky, tube angle, SID, or orientation.

Expected outcome: The selected exam setup matches the physical system setup. If the wrong protocol or detector selection caused the issue, correct the workflow and stop troubleshooting.

Check System Messages and Indicator Status

Review the operator console, workstation, or generator display for specific positioning, lock, collision, table, wall stand, or tube stand messages.

Record the exact wording or error code before clearing it.

Expected outcome: The displayed message helps identify whether the issue is operator setup, positioning mismatch, safety interlock, or likely equipment fault.

Power Cycle Only When Safe

If no patient is present and no movement is occurring, coordinate with radiology staff and perform a controlled system restart according to site practice.

Do not power cycle during an active exam, image transfer, exposure sequence, or while a patient is positioned.

Expected outcome: Temporary communication or position recognition faults may clear. If the fault returns, continue escalation.

Perform a Basic Functional Check

With clinical approval and no patient present, verify safe movement of the affected area only.

Check the reported function, such as tube stand travel, wall stand Bucky movement, table movement, tabletop locks, or positioning recognition.

Expected outcome: The system moves normally, stops appropriately, and no positioning error returns.

Do Not Force Movement

If the tube stand, wall stand, table, or Bucky feels stuck, binds, makes abnormal noise, or reports repeated position errors, do not force it.

Forcing movement can worsen mechanical damage, create alignment issues, or compromise patient safety.

Expected outcome: Troubleshooting stops before causing further damage or unsafe movement.

If the Problem Persists

If the positioning error continues after clearing obstructions, checking locks, confirming detector/Bucky seating, verifying exam setup, reviewing messages, and performing a safe restart, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal position sensor, brake assembly, encoder, motion control component, interlock circuit, table mechanism, wall stand mechanism, tube stand mechanism, or system communication fault.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench/system evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated movement attempts on a radiographic system with unresolved positioning or motion faults.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot tube stand, wall stand, table, or Bucky movement while a patient is positioned in the system. Move the patient to another radiographic room or mobile X-ray unit first if imaging is clinically urgent.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the Siemens Multix Impact displayed a positioning error and would not recognize the wall stand Bucky position for chest exams."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the wall stand Bucky not fully seated in its detent, preventing the system from confirming the selected position."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated the Bucky, verified wall stand position recognition, confirmed tube alignment readiness, and returned the system to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact positioning error or message displayed
  • Whether issue involved tube stand, wall stand, table, or Bucky
  • Whether a patient was present when the fault occurred
  • Exam type being attempted
  • Detector or Bucky selected
  • Tube angle, SID, or positioning status
  • Whether movement was blocked
  • Whether locks or brakes engaged normally
  • Any unusual noise, binding, vibration, or delayed movement
  • Whether power cycle cleared the issue
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Positioning errors on a radiographic system should be approached carefully and logically. Start with patient safety, then check external obstructions, locks, positioning setup, detector/Bucky seating, and workflow selection before assuming an internal fault. If movement is abnormal or the error repeats, remove the system from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
