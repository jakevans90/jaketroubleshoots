Stryker S3

Error: 303 Foot Left Siderail Switch

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Error 303 indicating a foot-left siderail switch fault that can make iBED Awareness siderail status unreliable. This condition may prevent the bed from accurately recognizing whether the foot-left siderail is raised or lowered, affecting reliable safety-awareness monitoring.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the bed from patient use if the iBED Awareness system is required for patient safety, fall-risk monitoring, or siderail status monitoring.

If a patient is currently occupying the bed, inform clinical staff that the foot-left siderail position may not be reliably recognized by the bed. Move the patient to a known-working bed before troubleshooting when dependable monitoring is required.

Until repaired and verified, do not rely on iBED Awareness to confirm the foot-left siderail position.

  • Expected outcome: The patient is not dependent on a bed with unreliable siderail-status monitoring.
  • Why it matters: A failed siderail switch can cause the system to incorrectly recognize whether the siderail is raised or lowered.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

With the bed removed from patient-dependent use, confirm that the display reports:

Error 303 Call Service

Document whether the error appears:

  • At startup
  • When iBED Awareness is enabled
  • When the foot-left siderail is raised or lowered
  • Intermittently during normal bed operation

Physically inspect the foot-left siderail and confirm whether it raises, lowers, and locks mechanically.

  • Expected outcome: Error 303 is observed or supported by a reproducible abnormal foot-left siderail status indication.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a true switch or switch-circuit issue rather than a misunderstanding of siderail position.

3. Confirm the Foot-Left Siderail Is Mechanically Secure

Raise and lower the foot-left siderail through its normal travel while the bed is not occupied.

Confirm that the siderail:

  • Moves without binding or excessive looseness
  • Fully locks in the raised position
  • Fully releases and lowers normally
  • Is not physically damaged or misaligned

If the siderail will not reliably lock or appears damaged, remove the bed from service regardless of whether the error clears.

  • Expected outcome: The siderail is mechanically secure and moves through its full range normally.
  • Why it matters: A switch cannot provide reliable status if the siderail is damaged, misaligned, or not reaching its normal actuation position.

4. Check Whether iBED Awareness Responds to Siderail Position

With the bed removed from patient use, observe the iBED Awareness indication while changing the foot-left siderail position.

Raise and lower the siderail several times and compare the indicated siderail status to the actual physical position.

  • Expected outcome: The iBED Awareness system correctly recognizes each foot-left siderail position change.
  • If the physical siderail changes position but the iBED Awareness status does not follow correctly, the fault is consistent with the foot-left siderail switch or its associated wiring.

5. Inspect for Visible Switch Assembly or Wiring Problems

Inspect the accessible foot-left siderail switch area and related cabling for:

  • Broken or damaged switch components
  • Loose or disconnected accessible connectors
  • Pinched, pulled, cut, or abraded wiring
  • Contamination or debris interfering with movement
  • Poor switch actuation caused by looseness or misalignment

Do not perform unnecessary internal disassembly while attempting basic fault isolation.

  • Expected outcome: No visible damage, loose connection, or mechanical obstruction is present.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage or poor actuation can prevent the bed from correctly determining siderail position.

6. Correct Simple External or Accessible Findings

Correct only simple visible or accessible findings permitted by facility repair practices.

  • If an accessible connector is visibly loose, reseat it securely and repeat the siderail-position functional check.
  • If debris or an obvious obstruction prevents normal siderail or switch movement, remove the obstruction and retest.
  • If wiring is visibly damaged, pinched, or unsafe, stop troubleshooting and proceed with repair or replacement evaluation.

Expected outcome: Error 303 clears and iBED Awareness correctly recognizes foot-left siderail position changes after repeated operation.

7. Verify Resolution Before Returning the Bed to Service

If the error clears after correcting an accessible issue, perform a functional check of:

  • Foot-left siderail raise and lower movement
  • Foot-left siderail mechanical locking
  • iBED Awareness recognition of the siderail position
  • Absence of Error 303 after repeated operation
  • Any required facility safety or operational checks before release

Expected outcome: The bed consistently recognizes the foot-left siderail position and no longer displays Error 303.

If all checks pass, document the repair and return the bed to service.

If the Problem Persists

If Error 303 remains after confirming normal siderail movement and checking accessible switch wiring and connections, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Error 303 Call Service condition indicates a likely failed foot-left siderail switch assembly, damaged switch wiring, or another device-level fault affecting iBED Awareness input recognition.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. Functionally verify proper iBED Awareness operation before the bed is returned to patient use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first. While Error 303 is present, physically confirm siderail position and do not rely on iBED Awareness to verify the foot-left siderail status.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displays Error 303 Call Service. iBED Awareness foot-left siderail status may not reliably match the physical siderail position."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Foot-left siderail moved and locked mechanically, but iBED Awareness status did not consistently respond to siderail position changes. Accessible wiring and connections were inspected; fault remained, indicating likely failed switch assembly or associated wiring."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and labeled Out of Service due to unreliable iBED Awareness siderail monitoring. Sent for repair or bench evaluation of the foot-left siderail switch assembly and related wiring, with functional verification required before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A siderail switch error is more than a logged fault when it affects an awareness system used to support patient safety. Confirm the physical siderail condition, isolate accessible causes first, escalate persistent faults appropriately, and document findings clearly before the bed returns to clinical use.

That is successful troubleshooting.
