Baxter SIGMA Spectrum

Error: 320 Latch Switch Error

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Baxter

Model

SIGMA Spectrum

What This Guide Helps With

Error 320 Latch Switch Error indicating door/latch switches disagree, which can prevent the pump from operating or stop infusion due to unsafe door status detection.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use immediately if actively infusing
  • Transfer infusion to a backup device
  • Do not continue using a pump with a latch error

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error: 320 Latch Switch Error is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup, door movement, or during operation)
  • Open and close the door slowly while watching for changes

3. Inspect Door and Latch Mechanism

  • Check latch hook for bending or damage
  • Inspect door alignment and closure
  • Ensure nothing is blocking full latch engagement

4. Check for Debris or Contamination

  • Inspect latch and switch contact areas for residue or debris
  • Look for dried fluids, tape adhesive, or dirt
  • Clean using approved cleaning methods

5. Test Door Closure Consistency

  • Open and close the door multiple times
  • Apply light pressure during closure
  • Check if the error clears intermittently

6. Check for Physical Damage

  • Inspect for cracked housing or loose hinges
  • Check for worn latch components
  • Move the door gently to identify excessive play

7. Power Cycle the Pump

  • Turn the pump off
  • Wait 10 seconds
  • Restart with the door fully closed

If the Problem Persists

If Error 320 continues after all external checks, latch switches may be failing or misaligned internally.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a functioning infusion pump before addressing latch or safety-related errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 320 Latch Switch Error and would not operate when door closed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Latch switches not agreeing due to door misalignment and debris in latch mechanism."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned latch area, verified door alignment, and confirmed normal operation. Alternatively, removed from service for repair when issue persisted."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Latch switch errors directly impact safe infusion operation. Always prioritize mechanical inspection and consistency of door closure before escalating to repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
