Philips PageWriter TC70

Error: Timed ECG number exceeds available number in Main Archive

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the Philips PageWriter TC70 error Timed ECG number exceeds available number in Main Archive. This condition can prevent additional timed ECG records from being saved to the Main Archive because the available archive capacity or allowed timed ECG storage count has been exceeded.

This issue matters because ECG acquisition may appear successful, but the record may not save properly. That can create documentation gaps, missing ECG records, or delayed clinical workflow if the cardiograph is still used without confirming archive function.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the PageWriter TC70 is being used for active patient testing and ECGs cannot be saved reliably, stop using that unit for new patient ECG acquisition.
  • Move patient testing to another verified ECG machine if ECG capture, storage, or documentation is clinically needed.
  • Do not continue relying on the affected unit until archive function has been confirmed.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the Philips PageWriter TC70 and confirm the exact message displayed is Timed ECG number exceeds available number in Main Archive.
  • Determine whether the error appears during timed ECG acquisition, while saving a timed ECG, when reviewing stored ECGs, or after multiple ECGs have been performed.
  • Confirm whether standard ECG acquisition still works, whether only timed ECG saving fails, or whether general archive storage is also affected.
  • If possible, ask staff whether the error began after a high number of ECGs, repeated test ECGs, or failed archive cleanup.

Check Whether the Main Archive Is Full or Near Capacity

  • Access the Main Archive and review the number of stored ECG records.
  • Look for old, duplicate, test, training, or unwanted ECG files that may be using archive space.
  • Confirm whether stored ECGs have accumulated because records were not transmitted, exported, cleared, or archived through the normal workflow.
  • The expected finding is a buildup of stored ECG records that explains why additional timed ECGs cannot be saved.

Delete Unwanted Files From the Main Archive

  • Delete unneeded ECG files from the Main Archive according to facility policy.
  • Do not delete patient ECGs unless they are confirmed to be unwanted, duplicates, test records, or already properly stored in the approved clinical record system.
  • Use care when removing records because archive cleanup can affect patient documentation and record retention.
  • The goal is to free enough archive capacity for additional timed ECG records to save normally.

Verify Records Are Properly Transmitted or Backed Up Before Deletion

  • Before clearing patient ECGs, confirm whether the records have been transmitted to the ECG management system, network archive, EMR, or another approved storage location.
  • Check with clinical staff, cardiology, or the ECG system administrator if record retention status is unclear.
  • Only remove records that are appropriate to delete under facility procedure.
  • This prevents Clinical Engineering from unintentionally contributing to patient record loss while correcting a storage issue.

Restart the PageWriter TC70

  • After deleting unwanted archive files, restart the cardiograph.
  • Allow the unit to boot normally and check for any startup, storage, or archive-related warnings.
  • Confirm that the touchscreen, archive menu, and general system operation respond normally after restart.
  • Restarting helps confirm the archive cleanup is recognized by the system.

Perform a Controlled Timed ECG Save Test

  • In a Biomed or approved test setting, perform a test timed ECG workflow if allowed by facility procedure.
  • Confirm the ECG can be acquired and saved without the error returning.
  • Review the Main Archive to verify the test record saved correctly.
  • Delete the test record afterward if appropriate.

Check for Related Storage or Workflow Issues

  • If the error returns quickly after files are deleted, check whether users are saving excessive duplicate ECGs, test ECGs, or repeated timed ECG records.
  • Confirm the intended workflow for transmitting, archiving, and clearing stored ECGs.
  • Look for signs that ECGs are not being sent to the ECG management system or that staff are repeatedly storing records locally.
  • Repeated archive capacity errors may indicate a workflow issue, failed transfer process, or lack of routine archive maintenance.

Inspect Basic External Conditions

  • Confirm the TC70 has stable AC power or a charged battery during archive review, deletion, restart, and testing.
  • Check for abnormal shutdowns, frozen screens, failed touch response, or repeated restarts.
  • Do not perform archive cleanup while the device is unstable or powering off unexpectedly.
  • Interrupted power or system instability during file handling can complicate archive behavior.

Stop When Resolved

  • If unwanted files are deleted, the unit restarts normally, and a timed ECG saves successfully, return the PageWriter TC70 to service.
  • Document the archive cleanup, records removed if applicable, restart result, and final timed ECG save test in the work order.
  • Make sure the final device status is clear for clinical staff.

If the Problem Persists

If the Main Archive has available space but the error continues, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent archive capacity errors may indicate software, database, internal storage, configuration, or system file management problems.

Remove the PageWriter TC70 from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue repeated troubleshooting on a unit that cannot reliably save ECG records.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this issue on an active patient. If the ECG machine has an unresolved save or archive error, move the patient to a backup ECG machine first, then troubleshoot the affected unit in a controlled setting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips PageWriter TC70 displays error 'Timed ECG number exceeds available number in Main Archive' while saving a timed ECG. Staff reported timed ECGs could not be saved."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Main Archive was full or near capacity due to stored ECG files, preventing additional timed ECG records from being saved."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from active use during evaluation. Reviewed Main Archive, confirmed stored ECG file buildup, deleted unwanted files per facility policy, restarted unit, and verified timed ECG save function passed. Unit returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Archive errors should be handled carefully because they affect patient record retention and ECG documentation. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact message, verify storage capacity, remove only appropriate unwanted records, and escalate if the device still cannot save ECGs reliably.

That is successful troubleshooting.
