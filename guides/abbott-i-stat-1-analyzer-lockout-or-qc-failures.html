Abbott i-STAT 1

Analyzer Lockout or QC Failures

Asset Type

Blood Gas Analyzer

Manufacturer

Abbott

Model

i-STAT 1

What This Guide Helps With

Analyzer lockout, failed QC checks, or inability to run tests due to quality control, cartridge, or configuration-related issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot while the analyzer is needed for active patient testing.
  • Move testing to a backup analyzer if available.
  • Expected: No interruption to patient care
  • Why it matters: Analyzer lockout prevents timely diagnostic results

Verify the Reported Lockout or QC Failure

  • Power on the analyzer and review the display.
  • Confirm lockout message or QC failure notification
  • Note any specific error messages or codes
  • Attempt to access testing mode
  • Expected: Issue is reproducible and clearly displayed
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true lockout vs user workflow issue

Check Operator ID and Authorization Settings (Common Cause)

  • Verify operator ID is entered correctly
  • Confirm operator certification status (if required by facility settings)
  • Try a known authorized operator ID
  • Expected: Access restored with valid credentials
  • Why it matters: Many lockouts are tied to user access controls, not hardware failure

Verify Quality Control (QC) Requirements

  • Check QC status in analyzer menu
  • Confirm QC has been performed within required timeframe
  • Review last QC result (pass/fail)
  • Expected: QC status is current and passing
  • Why it matters: The system enforces QC compliance before allowing patient testing

Perform Required QC Test

  • Use appropriate QC cartridge/material
  • Ensure cartridge is within expiration date and stored correctly
  • Run QC per facility protocol
  • Expected: QC passes and clears lockout
  • Why it matters: Failed or missing QC is the most common cause of analyzer lockout
  • If QC passes, resume normal operation.

Inspect Test Cartridges (Frequent Contributor to QC Failure)

  • Check expiration date on cartridges
  • Confirm proper storage (temperature/humidity)
  • Allow cartridge to reach room temperature before use
  • Try a new cartridge from a different lot
  • Expected: QC/test passes with a good cartridge
  • Why it matters: Cartridge integrity directly affects analyzer performance

Check Analyzer Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure analyzer is within operating temperature range
  • Avoid drafts, direct sunlight, or cold storage areas
  • Allow device to acclimate if recently moved
  • Expected: Stable environment improves QC reliability
  • Why it matters: Blood gas analyzers are sensitive to temperature and environment

Inspect Analyzer Contacts and Cartridge Port

  • Inspect cartridge port for debris or contamination
  • Ensure contacts are clean and dry
  • Do not insert damaged cartridges
  • Expected: Proper contact allows accurate readings
  • Why it matters: Signal issues can mimic QC failure

Perform Analyzer Reset

  • Power off the analyzer
  • Remove batteries briefly if applicable
  • Restart and reattempt QC
  • Expected: System resets and clears transient faults
  • Why it matters: Resolves minor software or memory issues

If the Problem Persists

If QC continues to fail or the analyzer remains locked out after verifying credentials, QC compliance, cartridges, and environment, the issue is likely internal. Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and prevents unreliable patient testing.

Clinical Use Tip

Never bypass QC requirements to run patient tests. Always confirm QC passes before returning the analyzer to clinical use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Analyzer locked out and unable to run patient tests due to QC failure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Expired QC requirement and failed QC due to outdated cartridge lot."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed QC with new cartridge, passed QC, and restored analyzer to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • QC status checked and documented
  • Cartridge lot and expiration verified
  • Operator ID tested
  • Environmental conditions assessed
  • Analyzer reset performed
  • Final device status (returned to service or removed)

Final Thought

Analyzer lockouts are most often related to QC compliance, cartridges, or user authorization—not internal failure. A structured approach ensures safe, compliant operation while avoiding unnecessary repairs. Proper documentation supports both regulatory compliance and future troubleshooting.

That is successful troubleshooting.
