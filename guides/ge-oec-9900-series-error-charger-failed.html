GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: CHARGER FAILED

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm CHARGER FAILED error. This condition may appear during bootup, may alternate with technique information, and may indicate that the battery charger, AC input path, interconnect cable, charger indicator circuit, fuse path, battery charging circuit, or related power-support hardware is not operating correctly.

A charger fault should be treated seriously because it can affect battery readiness, charging status, and confidence that the C-Arm will remain powered during clinical use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot charging or power-related faults while the C-Arm is needed for active patient care.
  • Remove the OEC 9900 Series C-Arm from patient use if battery status is uncertain, the charger fault repeats, or the unit may lose power during use.
  • Use another imaging system if the procedure requires fluoroscopy.
  • Do not continue using the device for patient care until power stability and charger status have been verified.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the system on and observe the display during bootup.
  • Confirm whether the display shows CHARGER FAILED.
  • Check whether the message alternates with technique information.
  • Look for abnormal battery warnings, charging indicators, startup behavior, or unexpected shutdowns.
  • Document when the error appears and whether it clears or returns after restart.

Check AC Power First

  • Confirm the workstation power cord is fully seated.
  • Verify the unit is plugged into a known-good hospital-grade AC outlet.
  • Test the outlet or move to a verified working outlet if needed.
  • Do not assume the charger has failed until AC power has been confirmed.

Inspect the Power Cord and Plug

  • Inspect the AC cord, plug, strain relief, and cord path.
  • Look for cuts, crushed insulation, loose plug fit, bent prongs, heat marks, or arcing damage.
  • If power cord damage is found, remove the device from service and correct the power cord issue before further testing.

Check the Workstation/C-Arm Interconnect Cable

  • Verify that the workstation/C-Arm interconnect cable is fully connected at both ends.
  • Inspect connector pins, locking hardware, cable jacket, and strain relief.
  • Reseat the cable if allowed by facility procedure.
  • A loose or damaged interconnect cable can interrupt charger-related power or signal paths between system sections.

Observe the Battery Charger Light

  • Locate and observe the battery charger light during AC connection and startup.
  • Note whether the light is on normally, off, flashing unexpectedly, intermittent, or different from known normal behavior.
  • Record the charger light status in the work order.
  • An abnormal charger light may help narrow the issue toward the charger circuit, charger input, battery connection, or internal charging hardware.

Power Cycle and Confirm Whether the Error Returns

  • After confirming AC power and cable connections, power cycle the system using normal shutdown and startup procedure.
  • Observe whether CHARGER FAILED returns during bootup.
  • If the error clears and charging indicators appear normal, complete normal operational checks before returning the device to service.
  • If the error returns, continue troubleshooting and keep the device out of clinical use.

Check for Related Power or Battery Symptoms

  • Check for poor battery runtime, failure to charge, battery-related warnings, shutdown when unplugged, unusual charger smell, heat, sound, or intermittent boot behavior.
  • Document whether the problem appears isolated to the charger message or is part of a broader power-support issue.
  • Related symptoms may indicate an internal charger PCB, battery, fuse, wiring, or power-distribution problem.

Inspect Accessible Fuses and External Power Path per Facility Procedure

  • If your facility allows Clinical Engineering to check accessible fuses, inspect the charger-related fuse path according to approved procedure.
  • Do not bypass fuses.
  • Do not substitute incorrect fuse ratings.
  • If a fuse is open, replace only with the correct type and rating if permitted, then retest.
  • If a replacement fuse opens again, remove the device from service and escalate for repair.

Determine Whether External Causes Have Been Ruled Out

  • Confirm that a known-good AC outlet was tested.
  • Confirm that the power cord was inspected.
  • Confirm that the workstation/C-Arm interconnect cable was seated and inspected.
  • Confirm that the charger light behavior was checked.
  • Confirm that related battery or power symptoms were documented.
  • If the error remains after these checks, suspect an internal charger circuit, charger PCB, power/signal interface path, charger wiring, fuse path, battery charging circuit, or associated power circuitry.

If the Problem Persists

If CHARGER FAILED continues after AC power, power cord condition, interconnect cable seating, charger indicator behavior, and accessible power checks have been verified, external causes have been ruled out.

Remove the C-Arm from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Persistent charger failure may involve the battery charger PCB, power/signal interface path, charger wiring, related fuses, battery charging circuit, or internal power distribution hardware.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first if imaging is needed.

Do not rely on a C-Arm with an active charger failure for a procedure, especially if battery support may be required.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"User reported GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm displayed CHARGER FAILED during bootup. Battery charging status was uncertain and charger fault returned after restart."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified AC outlet and power cord. Workstation/C-Arm interconnect cable was checked and reseated. Charger indicator behavior was abnormal and CHARGER FAILED returned after restart, indicating likely internal charger or power-path failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed C-Arm from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation of charger PCB, charger wiring, related fuses, battery charging circuit, and power/signal interface path."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A CHARGER FAILED error should be treated as a power-support issue, not just a nuisance message. Start with patient safety, confirm the reported error, check AC power and external cabling, then escalate when the fault remains. Clear documentation helps show that external causes were ruled out before repair was requested.

That is successful troubleshooting.
