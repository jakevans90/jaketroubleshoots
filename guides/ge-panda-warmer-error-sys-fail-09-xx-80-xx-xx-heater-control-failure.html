GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX] Heater Control Failure

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX] when heater control fails during operation, interrupting reliable infant warming capability. This error means radiant heat regulation cannot be trusted for patient care until the warmer has been evaluated and repaired.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the Panda Warmer from patient use if Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX] occurs during warming operation.
  • Transfer the infant to an approved alternate warming method or another functioning warmer according to clinical procedure.
  • Do not rely on a warmer with an unresolved heater control failure.

Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on equipment with unreliable heat control.

Why it matters: This error indicates the warmer may not safely or reliably regulate radiant heat.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, confirm that the display reports Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX].
  • Observe whether the error occurs during startup, shortly after power-on, during active warming operation, or intermittently after the unit has operated for a period of time.
  • Document associated behavior, such as interrupted heating, a system failure indication, loss of normal operation, or inability to maintain warming readiness.

Expected outcome: The Heater Control Failure is reproducible or clearly confirmed from the reported event.

Why it matters: This confirms a true device-reported heater control problem rather than an operational or setup concern.

3. Remove the Unit From Clinical Service Before Further Evaluation

  • Disconnect the warmer from patient-care use.
  • Attach an appropriate Out of Service label while troubleshooting is performed.
  • Do not attempt functional warming checks with an infant under the heater.

Expected outcome: The unit is controlled for evaluation and cannot be inadvertently returned to patient use before resolution.

4. Inspect the External Condition of the Warmer

  • Check for signs of liquid intrusion or contamination.
  • Inspect for cracked, loose, or damaged heater-head components.
  • Check for burn odor, discoloration, unusual heat, or other evidence of overheating.
  • Inspect ventilation openings for blockage or contamination.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, and strain relief for damage.

Expected outcome: No obvious external damage, contamination, or overheating condition is found.

Why it matters: External damage or overheating evidence confirms the unit should remain out of service and may affect repair priority.

5. Verify Power Source and Basic Power Condition

  • Confirm the warmer is connected to an approved hospital-grade outlet.
  • Confirm the power cord and plug are intact and securely connected.
  • When appropriate, test the outlet using approved Clinical Engineering procedures or connect the warmer to a known-good approved power source for observation.

Expected outcome: The unit receives stable facility power, yet the documented Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX] condition remains present or has been confirmed.

Why it matters: Documenting basic power checks rules out an obvious external source concern before escalation.

6. Confirm the Failure Is Not Due to an Obvious Setup or Environmental Condition

  • Ensure there are no external obstructions, contamination concerns, or unusual environmental conditions affecting the warmer during evaluation.
  • Verify that the issue is not simply an operator-selected setting, interrupted use condition, or obvious external physical problem.

Expected outcome: No accessory, setup, environmental, or external condition explains the Heater Control Failure.

Why it matters: The system-reported error should be treated as an internal equipment failure after easily verifiable external issues are excluded.

7. Isolate the Condition as a Device-Related Heater Control Failure

  • If the error remains confirmed after external inspection and basic power verification, classify the failure as device-related.
  • For Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX], the reported service correction is replacement of the Power PCA.
  • Do not continue operational testing as though the warmer is safe for clinical use.

Expected outcome: The warmer remains removed from service and is routed for qualified repair or bench evaluation involving Power PCA replacement.

Why it matters: The error identifies a failure in heater control during run time, making continued clinical reliance inappropriate.

8. Stop When Resolved

  • If the warmer has been repaired according to approved service procedures, complete all required functional, safety, and performance verification before returning it to service.
  • If the error was confirmed and repair has not been completed, the warmer must remain out of service.

Resolved condition: The warmer powers on normally, completes required verification, provides reliable warming operation, and no longer reports Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX].

If the Problem Persists

Once power source, external condition, ventilation, contamination, and obvious setup concerns have been ruled out, a persistent Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX] Heater Control Failure indicates a device-related heater control failure requiring qualified repair or bench evaluation.

The service direction for this error is replacement of the Power PCA.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the device for qualified repair or bench evaluation.
  • Complete required functional, electrical safety, and performance verification before return to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first. An unresolved heater control error may result in inadequate or unsafe thermal support.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX] Heater Control Failure during operation, with reliable radiant warming function no longer assured."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed Sys Fail 09 [XX 80 XX XX] heater control failure indication during run time. External inspection, ventilation review, and basic power-source checks did not identify an external cause. Error condition indicates internal Power PCA failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from patient use, labeled unit Out of Service, documented confirmed error condition, and routed device for qualified repair or bench evaluation with Power PCA replacement required before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A heater control failure on an infant warmer is a patient-safety concern because reliable thermal support cannot be assumed. Clinical Engineering troubleshooting should confirm the error, rule out simple external contributors, remove the device from use, and escalate appropriately for Power PCA repair. Clear CCR documentation preserves the failure history and supports safe return-to-service decisions.

That is successful troubleshooting.
