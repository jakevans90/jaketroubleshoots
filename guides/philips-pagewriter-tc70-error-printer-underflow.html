Philips PageWriter TC70

Error: Printer underflow

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Printer underflow errors on the Philips PageWriter TC70. This condition may appear during rhythm printing and can be associated with intermittent Patient Interface Module communication, signal acquisition interruptions, or patient cable connection problems. The issue may interrupt rhythm strip printing, create blank or delayed print sections, or occur alongside ECG signal instability.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the PageWriter TC70 is connected to a patient and the error occurs during rhythm printing or ECG acquisition, stop using the affected device for that patient.
  • Move ECG acquisition to another verified cardiograph if a patient ECG is clinically needed.
  • Do not continue testing cables, PIM connections, or rhythm printing behavior while the device is actively being used on a patient.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact displayed error is: Printer underflow.
  • Observe when the error appears, such as during rhythm printing, during ECG acquisition, after movement of the PIM, or after movement of the patient cable.
  • Ask staff whether the issue occurs only with a specific PIM, patient data cable, lead set, or cardiograph.
  • Check whether the issue is repeatable in a controlled Biomed setting.

Check Whether Printing Stops, Pauses, or Produces Poor Output

  • Attempt a rhythm print in a controlled setting after the patient has been moved to another device.
  • Look for printing that stops unexpectedly, blank sections on the rhythm strip, delayed waveform printing, interrupted waveform printing, or ECG signal dropout during the printout.
  • Check whether lead-off messages or acquisition-related messages appear with the printer error.
  • If printing symptoms occur with signal dropout, treat the issue as acquisition or PIM communication related rather than only a paper feed problem.

Check AC Power and Battery Status

  • Connect the cardiograph directly to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Confirm the unit powers normally and does not display low battery, battery missing, or power-related warnings.
  • Allow the unit to remain stable on AC power while repeating rhythm print testing.
  • Rule out unstable power before focusing on PIM communication or accessory faults.

Inspect the Printer Area for Simple Issues

  • Open the printer drawer or paper compartment.
  • Confirm paper is loaded correctly and is not binding, torn, wrinkled, or misrouted.
  • Check for debris in the paper path.
  • Confirm the printer door or drawer closes fully and latches properly.
  • Restart the cardiograph and repeat the rhythm print test.

Check the PIM Connection

  • Inspect the Patient Interface Module connection to the cardiograph.
  • Confirm the connector is fully seated and not loose, damaged, contaminated, or strained.
  • Gently manipulate the cable near the connector while observing for signal dropout or error recurrence.
  • If the error appears when the PIM connection is moved, remove the affected PIM, cable, or device from service as appropriate.

Inspect the Patient Data Cable

  • Examine the patient data cable for cuts, pinching, bent connector pins, loose strain relief, intermittent connection, fluid contamination, twisting damage, or pulling damage.
  • Replace the patient data cable with a known-good compatible cable if available.
  • Repeat rhythm printing and ECG acquisition checks with the known-good cable.
  • If the error clears after replacing the cable, remove the original cable from service.

Inspect Lead Wires and Patient Cable Accessories

  • Check lead wires and electrode connections for damage, contamination, looseness, or poor fit.
  • Confirm lead wires are connected correctly and are not pulling on the PIM or patient data cable.
  • Replace questionable lead wires or accessories with known-good compatible parts.
  • Repeat rhythm printing and confirm the ECG signal remains stable.

Run or Review PIM Test Results

  • Perform the available PIM-related diagnostic or functional test according to facility procedure.
  • Check whether the PIM passes communication and acquisition checks.
  • If the PIM fails testing, remove it from use and replace it or send it for repair evaluation.
  • Document the PIM test result in the work order.

Try a Known-Good PIM

  • If available, connect a known-good compatible PIM.
  • Repeat rhythm printing and ECG acquisition checks.
  • If the error clears with a known-good PIM, the original PIM is the likely cause.
  • Remove the original PIM from service and document the replacement or repair path.

Check for a Device-Specific Pattern

  • If the same patient data cable and PIM work correctly on another PageWriter TC70, reconnect them to the original cardiograph and repeat testing.
  • If the issue follows the PIM or patient data cable, remove that accessory from service.
  • If the issue remains with the original cardiograph, suspect a device-side communication or internal interface issue.
  • Do not open the cardiograph or perform internal repair during frontline troubleshooting.

Restart and Recheck

  • Power the cardiograph off.
  • Disconnect and reconnect the PIM and patient data cable.
  • Restart the unit and repeat rhythm printing and acquisition testing.
  • If the error does not return and the unit passes functional testing, return the device to service according to facility procedure.

Stop When Resolved

  • If reseating or replacing the PIM, patient data cable, lead wires, or accessory connections resolves the issue, document the corrective action.
  • Perform successful rhythm printing and ECG acquisition checks before returning the cardiograph to service.
  • Include the final device status in the work order.

If the Problem Persists

If the Printer underflow error continues after checking AC power, battery status, printer paper path, PIM connection, patient data cable, lead wires, known-good accessories, and PIM test results, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent Printer underflow errors may indicate an unresolved PIM communication fault, accessory interface issue, or internal cardiograph communication problem.

Remove the Philips PageWriter TC70 from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot intermittent PIM communication errors on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first, then test cables, PIM connections, accessories, and rhythm printing behavior in a controlled setting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips PageWriter TC70 displayed Printer underflow during rhythm printing. Staff reported interrupted rhythm strip printing and intermittent ECG signal acquisition behavior."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Printer underflow condition confirmed during rhythm printing. PIM communication path inspected. Patient data cable and PIM connection checked due to intermittent acquisition communication behavior."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from patient use. Checked AC power, printer paper path, PIM connection, patient data cable, and lead wires. Reseated connections and repeated rhythm print testing. Device passed functional testing and was returned to service, or labeled Out of Service for bench evaluation if unresolved."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Printer underflow on the Philips PageWriter TC70 should be approached as a communication-related acquisition problem first, not just a printer problem. Patient safety comes first, followed by logical external checks of power, printer condition, PIM connection, patient data cable, lead wires, and PIM test results. If the error persists after those checks, escalation for repair is appropriate and should be clearly documented.

That is successful troubleshooting.
