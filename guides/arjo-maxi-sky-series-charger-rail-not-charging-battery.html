Arjo Maxi Sky Series

Charger Rail Not Charging Battery

Asset Type

Patient Lift

Manufacturer

Arjo

Model

Maxi Sky Series

What This Guide Helps With

Battery not charging while lift is on rail due to power loss, poor rail contact, or charging system connection issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the lift from active patient use before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient safely transferred to another device.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Place the lift on the charging section of the rail and observe charging indicators (LEDs or display).
  • Expected: No charging indication or intermittent charging.
  • Why it matters: Confirms true charging failure vs normal positioning delay.

Confirm Rail Power Supply

  • Verify the rail system is powered (check wall connection, breaker, or power supply unit if accessible).
  • Expected: Rail system has active power.
  • Why it matters: The charger rail requires facility power to function.
  • If no power → restore power and retest.

Check Charging Zone Positioning

  • Ensure the lift is positioned exactly at the designated charging section of the rail. Slowly move it back and forth to find contact.
  • Expected: Charging indicator activates when correctly positioned.
  • Why it matters: Charging only occurs at specific rail contact points.
  • If charging starts → stop troubleshooting.

Inspect Charging Contacts (Lift and Rail)

  • Visually inspect contact points on both the lift and rail for dirt, corrosion, or misalignment.
  • Clean gently if needed.
  • Expected: Clean, aligned contacts with solid connection.
  • Why it matters: Poor contact prevents current transfer to the battery.
  • If charging resumes → stop troubleshooting.

Check for Obstructions or Damage on Rail

  • Look for debris, bent rails, or physical damage at the charging section.
  • Expected: Rail is smooth, clean, and undamaged.
  • Why it matters: Physical issues can prevent proper electrical contact.

Test with Known Good Lift or Battery (If Available)

  • Place another lift on the same rail or install a known good battery in the unit.
  • Expected: Determine if issue follows the lift or stays with the rail.
  • Why it matters: Helps isolate rail charging system vs lift-side issue.

Check Charger Indicator Behavior (If Equipped)

  • Observe any charger LEDs or indicators on the lift or rail system.
  • Expected: No lights or abnormal indication may point to charger failure.
  • Why it matters: Indicator status can help differentiate power vs internal fault.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal to the rail charging system, lift charging circuitry, or battery interface.

Remove the lift from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or further bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot while a patient is suspended. Always transfer the patient to a safe surface or alternate lift before evaluating charging issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Lift battery not charging when parked on ceiling rail charger."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Poor electrical contact at rail charging point due to contaminated contacts."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned charging contacts and verified proper charging function on rail."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Rail power confirmed
  • Charging indicator status
  • Contact condition (clean/dirty/damaged)
  • Battery swapped/tested
  • Alternate lift tested on same rail
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Charging issues on ceiling lifts are often external and related to positioning, power, or contact integrity. Systematically ruling out these factors ensures safe operation and prevents unnecessary repairs. Knowing when to escalate protects both the patient and equipment.

That is successful troubleshooting.
