Cardinal Health Kangaroo ePump

Error: 1 Flash chip missing or incompatible version

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo ePump

What This Guide Helps With

Error: 1 Flash chip missing or incompatible version indicates a flash memory or firmware issue that prevents the pump from starting or operating normally. This results in complete loss of function and inability to deliver enteral feeding.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the pump from patient use
  • Transfer feeding to a backup pump or approved alternative method
  • Verify feeding continues without interruption

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and observe the display
  • Confirm “Error: 1” is present
  • Note if the unit fails to boot, freezes, or is unresponsive

3. Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the pump OFF
  • Disconnect AC power
  • Remove battery if accessible without disassembly
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart

4. Check Power Source Stability

  • Plug into a known-good outlet
  • Test with AC power only
  • Observe startup behavior for consistency

5. Attempt Startup Without Accessories

  • Remove all feeding sets and external attachments
  • Restart the pump in a clean state
  • Observe if the error persists

If the Problem Persists

If Error: 1 continues after external checks and power cycling, internal causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely due to flash memory failure, missing firmware, or incompatible firmware.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair, board replacement, or firmware reflash

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or attempt to use a feeding pump with system-level startup errors on an active patient. Always transition the patient to a verified working device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displays Error: 1 on startup and will not run."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Device consistently shows Error: 1 indicating flash memory or firmware failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Power cycled unit with no resolution; removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error: 1 indicates a system-level fault that cannot be corrected through normal external troubleshooting. Recognizing internal failures early and escalating appropriately protects both patient safety and equipment integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
