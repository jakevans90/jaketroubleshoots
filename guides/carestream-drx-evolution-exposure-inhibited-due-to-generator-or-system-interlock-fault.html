Carestream DRX-Evolution

Exposure Inhibited Due to Generator or System Interlock Fault

Asset Type

Radiographic System

Manufacturer

Carestream

Model

DRX-Evolution

What This Guide Helps With

Exposure inhibited conditions caused by room interlocks, generator readiness issues, tube stand position, detector status, or safety-related system faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot exposure problems while the patient is positioned for imaging unless it is safe to continue.

Action:

  • Stop the attempted exposure.
  • Move the patient away from the table, wall stand, or tube area if troubleshooting requires repositioning equipment.
  • Notify radiology staff that the system is not ready for exposure.
  • Use another radiographic room or portable X-ray system if imaging is urgent.

Expected outcome: The patient is protected from delayed imaging, repeated positioning, or attempted exposure on an inhibited system.

Why it matters: An exposure inhibit is a safety stop. The system is preventing radiation output because one or more required conditions are not satisfied.

Verify the Reported Exposure Inhibit

Confirm the exact behavior before assuming a generator failure.

Action:

  • Ask the technologist when the exposure inhibit occurs.
  • Confirm whether the system prevents exposure at prep, rotor, or exposure.
  • Check the operator console for the displayed warning, fault, or interlock message.
  • Note whether the issue occurs on table exams, wall stand exams, or all exams.

Expected outcome: The failure pattern is identified and documented.

If the issue only occurs on one receptor or one exam type, focus troubleshooting on that specific setup.

Check Room Door and Radiation Safety Interlocks

Verify that all room safety interlocks are satisfied.

Action:

  • Confirm the X-ray room door is fully closed.
  • Check for any door interlock, exposure hold, or room-ready indicator issue.
  • Inspect the door switch area for obvious damage, looseness, or misalignment.
  • Ask staff if the issue began after door work, wall repair, or room maintenance.

Expected outcome: The system recognizes the room as safe for exposure.

If the door or room interlock clears the issue, test system readiness and return the room to service if operation is normal.

Confirm Emergency Stop and Safety Switch Status

Check for an engaged emergency stop or safety stop condition.

Action:

  • Inspect visible emergency stop buttons in the room and control area.
  • Confirm no E-stop is pressed or latched.
  • Reset only if it is safe and appropriate to do so.
  • Verify that the system returns to normal ready status after reset.

Expected outcome: The system is no longer held in a safety-stop condition.

If resetting an E-stop resolves the issue, document the finding and verify normal operation before returning the system to use.

Verify System Power and Startup Status

Confirm the radiographic system completed startup normally.

Action:

  • Check the operator workstation, generator cabinet indicators, tube stand indicators, and wall stand or table status.
  • Confirm there are no startup errors still active.
  • Verify the generator appears powered and ready.
  • Perform a controlled restart only if approved by site procedure and patient care is not affected.

Expected outcome: All major system components are powered, communicating, and ready.

If the system fails to complete startup or generator status remains abnormal, remove the room from service.

Check Tube Stand, Wall Stand, and Table Positioning

Exposure may be inhibited if the system does not recognize a valid imaging position.

Action:

  • Confirm the tube is properly aligned to the selected receptor.
  • Verify the tube stand, wall stand, and table are not in an unsafe or invalid position.
  • Check whether detents, locks, or positioning indicators show ready status.
  • Move the system out of and back into the expected exam position if safe.

Expected outcome: The selected receptor and tube position are recognized as valid for exposure.

If repositioning clears the inhibit, perform a test workflow and document the positioning-related cause.

Confirm Detector or Receptor Selection

Make sure the selected detector matches the intended exam setup.

Action:

  • Verify the correct detector, wall stand, or table bucky is selected at the console.
  • Confirm the detector is inserted, seated, charged, and available if using a DR detector.
  • Check for detector communication or registration messages.
  • Try a known-good detector or alternate receptor if available and appropriate.

Expected outcome: The system recognizes a valid image receptor and allows exposure readiness.

If the issue follows one detector, remove that detector from service and continue with a known-good detector if clinically acceptable.

Check Collimator and Tube-Related Readiness

Confirm that the tube and collimator are not causing a readiness inhibit.

Action:

  • Verify the collimator is powered and responding.
  • Check for visible collimator display, light field, or control issues.
  • Confirm the tube is not reporting a thermal, rotation, filament, or housing-related warning.
  • Ask staff whether the issue occurred after repeated high technique exposures.

Expected outcome: Tube and collimator readiness conditions are normal.

If a tube thermal or generator readiness message is present, allow appropriate cooldown if indicated by the system and retest only when safe.

Verify Technique and Exam Selection

Incorrect or unsupported technique settings can prevent exposure.

Action:

  • Confirm the exam protocol is valid.
  • Check kVp, mA, mAs, exposure mode, AEC selection, and detector selection.
  • Make sure AEC is not selected for an exam setup that does not support it.
  • Have the technologist select a simple known-good exam protocol and attempt prep readiness without exposing a patient.

Expected outcome: The system accepts the selected technique and prepares normally.

If changing to a valid protocol clears the inhibit, document the setup issue and return the system to service after functional verification.

Check Hand Switch or Exposure Control

A faulty or partially pressed exposure switch can cause prep or exposure problems.

Action:

  • Inspect the hand switch or exposure control for obvious damage.
  • Confirm the switch is not stuck, loose, or physically damaged.
  • Ask staff whether prep occurs but exposure does not, or whether neither stage responds.
  • Try an alternate approved exposure control if available.

Expected outcome: Exposure control input is reliable and recognized by the system.

If the issue follows the exposure switch, remove the switch from service and replace or escalate according to site process.

Check for Active System Faults or Error History

Review available system messages without entering deep service-level repair.

Action:

  • Record the exact displayed fault, error code, or inhibit message.
  • Note whether the fault references generator, tube, detector, interlock, door, table, wall stand, or system communication.
  • Check whether the fault returns immediately after restart.
  • Do not clear recurring safety faults without identifying the cause.

Expected outcome: The fault is narrowed to an external condition, accessory issue, configuration problem, or likely internal system failure.

If the fault repeatedly returns and no external cause is found, escalate for repair.

Perform a Safe Functional Check

Only test the system when the room is clear and site policy allows.

Action:

  • Confirm no patient is present.
  • Confirm the room is secured.
  • Use the appropriate test workflow or phantom process per department policy.
  • Verify prep, exposure readiness, exposure completion, and image acquisition.

Expected outcome: The system exposes normally, captures the image, and no inhibit returns.

If the exposure inhibit is resolved and the system passes functional checks, return the room to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the exposure inhibit remains after checking patient safety, room interlocks, E-stops, power status, positioning, detector selection, technique settings, and exposure controls, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal generator fault, tube circuit problem, safety interlock circuit fault, motion/position sensing issue, or system communication failure.

The DRX-Evolution should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for vendor repair, service evaluation, or bench-level troubleshooting by qualified personnel

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not bypass exposure inhibits or attempt internal generator repair during routine Clinical Engineering troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot exposure inhibit faults on an active patient. Move the patient to another radiographic room or imaging device first if the exam is urgent. Exposure inhibits are safety-related and should be treated as protective system responses until proven otherwise.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the Carestream DRX-Evolution would prep but would not expose due to an exposure inhibit message at the operator console."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the wall stand was not fully locked into the selected receptor position, preventing the system from reaching exposure-ready status."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Repositioned and locked the wall stand, verified tube-to-receptor alignment, performed functional test exposure with no patient present, and returned the room to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact displayed inhibit message or error code
  • Whether prep occurs before the exposure is inhibited
  • Whether the issue affects table, wall stand, or all exams
  • Door interlock status
  • E-stop status
  • Tube stand and wall stand position
  • Selected receptor or detector
  • Detector battery and communication status
  • Technique selected
  • AEC selection status
  • Generator ready indicators
  • Alarm behavior or warning tones
  • Indicator lights on console, generator, table, or wall stand
  • Unusual sounds from generator, tube, or exposure control
  • Unusual heat or smell
  • Whether restart temporarily cleared the issue
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Exposure inhibit troubleshooting should always begin with safety and simple external checks before assuming generator failure. Room interlocks, positioning, detector selection, exam setup, and exposure controls can all prevent exposure by design. If those causes are ruled out and the fault remains, removing the DRX-Evolution from service and escalating for repair protects patients, staff, and the equipment record.

That is successful troubleshooting.
