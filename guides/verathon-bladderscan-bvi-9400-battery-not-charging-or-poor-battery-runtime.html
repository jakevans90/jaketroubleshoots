Verathon BladderScan BVI 9400

Battery not charging or poor battery runtime

Asset Type

Bladder Scanner

Manufacturer

Verathon

Model

BladderScan BVI 9400

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting charging failure, short battery runtime, docking issues, power adapter problems, or battery age concerns on the BladderScan BVI 9400.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the bladder scanner during active patient assessment if the device is unreliable.

Action:

  • Remove the BladderScan BVI 9400 from clinical use if it cannot power on, hold charge, or complete scans reliably.
  • Use another bladder scanner if patient assessment is needed.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues using a reliable device.

Why it matters: A scanner with poor battery runtime may shut down during use, delay care, or cause repeat scanning.

2. Verify the Reported Power Problem

Confirm the exact behavior before assuming battery failure.

Action:

Ask clinical staff what was observed:

  • Device will not power on
  • Device powers on only while docked or plugged in
  • Battery does not charge
  • Battery drains quickly
  • Charging indicator does not appear
  • Device shuts off during scanning
  • Runtime is shorter than expected

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified.

If the issue is confirmed, continue troubleshooting.

3. Check the Charging Dock or Power Connection

Verify that the charging station is receiving power.

Action:

  • Confirm the dock or charging base is plugged into a known working outlet.
  • Check that the power supply is firmly connected to the wall outlet and to the charging base.
  • Look for damaged power cords, bent connectors, loose strain reliefs, or missing power indicators.

Expected outcome: The charger has stable power and no obvious external damage.

If the outlet or power connection was the issue, correct it, verify charging begins, document the repair, and stop.

4. Test the Wall Outlet

Rule out a simple facility power issue.

Action:

  • Plug another known-working device or outlet tester into the same receptacle.
  • If the outlet is controlled by a wall switch, confirm it is switched on.
  • Avoid using damaged outlets, loose receptacles, or questionable power strips.

Expected outcome: The outlet provides reliable power.

If the outlet is failed, move the charger to a working outlet or notify Facilities, document findings, and stop.

5. Inspect How the Scanner Sits in the Charging Base

A poor physical connection can prevent charging.

Action:

  • Place the BVI 9400 properly into the dock or charging base.
  • Confirm it seats fully and does not rock, sit crooked, or lose contact.
  • Inspect the dock area for debris, tape, labels, gel residue, or anything blocking proper contact.

Expected outcome: The scanner sits securely and charging status appears normally.

If reseating the scanner resolves the charging issue, clean the dock area as appropriate, verify charging, document, and stop.

6. Inspect External Charging Contacts

Dirty or damaged contacts can cause intermittent charging.

Action:

  • Inspect the scanner and dock charging contacts for contamination, corrosion, bent contacts, residue, or physical damage.
  • Clean external contact areas only according to approved facility cleaning practices and manufacturer-safe methods.
  • Do not scrape, bend, or aggressively clean contacts.

Expected outcome: Contacts are clean, aligned, and free of visible damage.

If cleaning resolves the issue, verify charging and runtime before returning the device to service.

7. Check the Battery Indicator Behavior

Observe how the device reports battery status.

Action:

  • Power on the BladderScan BVI 9400 and check the battery icon or charge indication.
  • Place the device on the charger and observe whether the battery status changes.
  • Allow the unit to remain on charge long enough to determine whether charge level increases.

Expected outcome: Battery status should show charging and gradually improve.

If the charge level never changes despite verified charger power and proper docking, suspect battery, dock, charging circuit, or internal power issue.

8. Compare With a Known-Good Charger or Dock if Available

This helps separate scanner failure from charging accessory failure.

Action:

  • Place the scanner on a known-good compatible charger or dock if one is available.
  • Alternatively, test a known-good BVI 9400 on the suspect charger if your facility has another compatible unit.

Expected outcome:

  • If the problem follows the scanner, suspect battery or internal scanner issue.
  • If the problem stays with the charger or dock, suspect charger, dock, or power supply issue.

If a faulty charger is confirmed, remove the charger from service and replace or repair it according to facility process.

9. Check for Battery Age or Usage Pattern

Poor runtime may be caused by an aging battery rather than an immediate charging failure.

Action:

Review service history, battery replacement history, and staff reports.

Look for patterns such as:

  • Device charges fully but dies quickly
  • Runtime is poor after normal charging
  • Battery percentage drops suddenly
  • Scanner powers off during scan activity
  • Battery has not been replaced in several years

Expected outcome: Battery condition is assessed based on age, symptoms, and history.

If the battery is aged or runtime remains poor after normal charging, remove the scanner from service for battery evaluation or replacement.

10. Perform a Controlled Runtime Check if Safe

Do this only off-patient and only if it does not delay clinical care.

Action:

  • Fully charge the device using a verified charger.
  • Power the device on and monitor whether the battery drains unusually fast during normal standby or test use.
  • Do not use the device clinically during this evaluation if reliability is questionable.

Expected outcome: Runtime should be reasonable and stable.

If runtime drops quickly or the device shuts off unexpectedly, remove the device from service.

11. Inspect for Heat, Swelling, Odor, or Damage

Battery-related problems can become safety issues.

Action:

Check the scanner and charging base for:

  • Unusual heat
  • Burning smell
  • Cracked housing
  • Swollen battery area
  • Discoloration near charging contacts
  • Intermittent power when moved
  • Signs of liquid damage

Expected outcome: No unsafe physical condition is present.

If heat, odor, swelling, liquid damage, or physical damage is found, stop troubleshooting immediately, remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and escalate for repair.

12. Verify Final Device Status

Before returning the scanner to service, confirm reliable operation.

Action:

  • Confirm the scanner powers on normally.
  • Confirm charging indicator behavior is normal.
  • Confirm the device holds charge long enough for expected clinical use.
  • Confirm no power interruptions occur during handling or scanning setup.

Expected outcome: The BladderScan BVI 9400 charges properly and operates reliably.

If confirmed, return the device to service and document the work order.

If the Problem Persists

If the charger, outlet, docking position, contacts, and usage pattern have been checked and the BladderScan BVI 9400 still will not charge or has poor runtime, the likely cause is internal battery failure, charger/dock failure, charging circuit failure, or internal power management issue.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, battery evaluation, or bench testing

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue cycling or using a bladder scanner with unreliable battery performance.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot battery or charging problems while staff are waiting to scan a patient. Provide another bladder scanner first, then evaluate the failed unit off-patient. A scanner that shuts down during use can delay care, interrupt therapy or assessment continuity, and create unnecessary repeat scanning.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Verathon BladderScan BVI 9400 would not hold a charge and powered off shortly after being removed from the charging dock."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet and charger power were good, charging contacts were clean, and the scanner still showed poor runtime after a full charge."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the BladderScan BVI 9400 from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for battery and charging system evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed working
  • Charging dock or power supply tested
  • Scanner seated correctly in dock
  • Charging contacts inspected or cleaned
  • Battery indicator behavior noted
  • Approximate runtime after full charge
  • Any shutdowns during use
  • Any unusual heat, odor, swelling, or damage
  • Whether issue followed scanner or charger
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed from service

Final Thought

Battery and charging issues should be approached logically. Start with outlet power, dock seating, charger condition, contacts, and runtime behavior before assuming internal failure. If the device cannot reliably charge or maintain power, remove it from service and escalate appropriately. Clear documentation protects patient care, supports repair decisions, and helps identify repeat battery problems.

That is successful troubleshooting.
