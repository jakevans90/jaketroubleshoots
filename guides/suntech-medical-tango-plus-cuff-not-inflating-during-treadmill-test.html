SunTech Medical Tango+

Cuff Not Inflating During Treadmill Test

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

SunTech Medical

Model

Tango+

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses failure of the BP cuff to inflate during stress testing due to connection, cuff, hose, or system setup issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the stress test if the cuff is not functioning properly.
  • Ensure the patient is stable and monitored by alternate means if needed.

Expected: Patient remains safe without reliance on faulty BP readings
Why it matters: Inaccurate or absent BP readings during stress testing can lead to unsafe clinical decisions

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to initiate a manual BP cycle during the test or in standby mode.

Expected: Confirm cuff does not inflate at all or inflates inconsistently
Why it matters: Confirms true inflation failure vs timing or trigger issue

Check Cuff and Hose Connections

  • Inspect the cuff tubing from cuff to monitor
  • Ensure hose is fully seated at the Tango+ port
  • Check for loose, cracked, or disconnected fittings

Expected: Secure, airtight connection
Why it matters: Any air leak will prevent proper inflation

Inspect the Cuff for Damage or Incorrect Size

  • Check for tears, worn Velcro, or internal bladder leaks
  • Verify proper cuff size for patient
  • Swap with a known good cuff if available

Expected: Cuff holds pressure and is appropriate size
Why it matters: Damaged cuffs or improper sizing prevent inflation or pressure buildup

Check Hose for Kinks or Obstructions

  • Trace the full length of tubing
  • Look for sharp bends, pinching, or internal blockage
  • Ensure tubing is not caught under equipment or treadmill components

Expected: Unobstructed airflow path
Why it matters: Restricted airflow prevents cuff pressurization

Verify Mode and Trigger Settings

  • Confirm correct mode (exercise/stress mode) is selected
  • Verify trigger source (ECG gating or manual) is functioning

Expected: System initiates inflation when triggered
Why it matters: Incorrect settings may prevent inflation command from being sent

Check for Audible Pump Operation

  • Initiate a BP cycle and listen for pump activity
  • Note any unusual noise or complete silence

Expected: Audible pump sound during inflation attempt
Why it matters: No sound suggests pump or internal pneumatic failure

Test with Known Good Accessories

  • Replace cuff
  • Replace hose
  • Retry inflation

Expected: Inflation works with known good components
Why it matters: Confirms issue is accessory-related vs device-related

Power Cycle the Device

  • Turn the Tango+ off
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power back on and retry BP measurement

Expected: System resets and attempts normal operation
Why it matters: Clears minor software or control faults

If the Problem Persists

If cuff, hose, connections, and settings have been verified and the cuff still does not inflate, the issue is likely internal (pump, valve, or pneumatic system failure).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot BP measurement issues during an active stress test. Always stabilize the patient and use alternate monitoring before proceeding.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Cuff would not inflate during treadmill stress test on Tango+ monitor."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed internal pump—no audible operation during inflation attempt after ruling out cuff and hose."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from service and sent for repair; verified function with known good accessories prior to escalation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior or absence of alarms
  • Accessories swapped (cuff, hose)
  • Power behavior and outlet verification
  • Environmental factors (treadmill interference, tubing routing)
  • Indicator lights or system response
  • Final device status (removed from service or returned to use)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple external checks like cuffs, hoses, and settings before assuming internal failure. Patient safety always comes first, and clear documentation ensures continuity and accountability in Clinical Engineering workflows.

That is successful troubleshooting.
