Hamilton G5

Error: TF 9704 A/D converter offset or status is out of specification at VUP startup

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Hamilton

Model

G5

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting TF 9704, which occurs when the A/D converter offset value or converter status is out of specification at VUP startup. This technical fault causes the Hamilton G5 ventilator to sound a high-priority alarm, display the fault number, record the fault, and enter ambient state, preventing dependable mechanical ventilation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately provide alternative ventilation using an approved backup ventilator or manual ventilation method according to clinical protocol.
  • Do not rely on the Hamilton G5 while TF 9704 is displayed or while the ventilator is in ambient state.
  • Do not troubleshoot the affected ventilator while a patient depends on it for ventilation.

Expected outcome: The patient is safely ventilated without dependence on the faulted ventilator.

Why it matters: TF 9704 causes the ventilator to enter ambient state and prevents dependable mechanical ventilation.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact displayed technical fault is TF 9704.
  • Determine whether the error occurs during startup, immediately after power-up, after an attempted restart, or intermittently.
  • Observe whether the ventilator sounds a high-priority alarm, displays the technical fault number, enters ambient state, or fails to provide normal ventilation.

Expected outcome: TF 9704 is clearly confirmed, along with the associated loss of normal ventilation.

Why it matters: This separates an internal technical fault from a patient circuit, accessory, or setup-related issue.

Remove the Ventilator From Clinical Use

  • After alternate ventilation is established, disconnect the ventilator from patient use.
  • Apply an Out of Service label identifying TF 9704 and ambient state entry.
  • Prevent the ventilator from being returned to clinical use until approved evaluation and verification are completed.

Expected outcome: The affected ventilator is isolated from patient care until the technical fault is evaluated.

Why it matters: A ventilator entering ambient state during startup should not be cleared and returned to use without service evaluation.

Check External Power and Physical Condition

  • Connect the ventilator to a known-good AC power source for evaluation.
  • Inspect the exterior for visible damage, fluid intrusion, contamination, signs of overheating, unusual odor, or a damaged power cord.
  • Check for loose or damaged external connections.

Expected outcome: No external power or visible physical issue is identified as a contributing factor.

Why it matters: TF 9704 indicates an internal monitoring fault, but obvious external conditions should still be documented and ruled out.

Review the Event Log

  • Access the event log according to approved service procedures.
  • Confirm that TF 9704 was recorded.
  • Document the event date and time, whether it occurred once or repeatedly, and whether related technical faults were recorded before or after TF 9704.
  • Note whether the fault occurs at startup each time.

Expected outcome: The event log confirms the technical fault and identifies any related fault history.

Why it matters: Event log findings support accurate repair escalation and technical support review.

Perform the A/D Converter Check in Test Mode

  • With the ventilator removed from patient use, perform the A/D Converter Check in Test Mode using the approved Hamilton service procedure.
  • Record the test result and any repeated TF 9704 occurrence.
  • Do not return the unit to service based only on a successful restart or a temporarily cleared display.

Expected outcome: The A/D Converter Check either passes completely or confirms an out-of-specification condition requiring further repair evaluation.

Why it matters: TF 9704 specifically indicates that the A/D converter offset value or status was outside specification at VUP startup.

Complete Return-to-Service Verification Only if the Fault Is Cleared

  • If the A/D Converter Check passes and TF 9704 does not recur, complete required functional verification according to hospital policy and the approved Hamilton service procedure.
  • If TF 9704 reappears, the A/D Converter Check fails, or the ventilator continues to enter ambient state, stop at the approved evaluation level and escalate for repair.

Expected outcome: The ventilator is returned to service only after the technical fault is absent and required verification testing is completed.

Why it matters: A life-support device must not be returned to clinical use with an unresolved startup technical fault.

If the Problem Persists

External and common observable causes have been ruled out. A persistent TF 9704 indicates that the Hamilton G5 requires qualified bench evaluation for an internal A/D converter monitoring path or associated control electronics issue.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the ventilator Out of Service — TF 9704 / Ambient State.
  • Send the unit for repair or bench evaluation by qualified service personnel.
  • Escalate to Hamilton technical support when required, with event log findings and A/D Converter Check results available.

Do not continue repeated restarts in an attempt to make the ventilator clinically usable.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a Hamilton G5 displaying TF 9704 while it is supporting an active patient. Move the patient to a backup ventilation method first, then remove the affected ventilator from service for controlled evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Hamilton G5 ventilator displayed TF 9704 during startup, sounded a high-priority alarm, and entered ambient state, preventing normal ventilation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed TF 9704 indicating A/D converter offset or status out of specification at VUP startup. External inspection and known-good AC power check found no obvious contributing issue. Event log was reviewed and A/D Converter Check was performed in Test Mode."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Alternative ventilation was provided before troubleshooting. Ventilator was removed from service, labeled Out of Service — TF 9704 / Ambient State, event log findings were documented, and unit was routed for qualified bench repair or Hamilton technical support evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

TF 9704 is a startup technical fault that can place the Hamilton G5 ventilator in ambient state and prevent dependable ventilation. Safe troubleshooting means providing backup ventilation immediately, verifying the fault through approved checks, removing the device from service when the issue persists, and documenting the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
