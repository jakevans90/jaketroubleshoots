Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: PRESSURE SENSOR FAIL !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

PRESSURE SENSOR FAIL ! alarm indicating loss or inaccuracy of airway pressure measurement, affecting safe ventilation monitoring and pressure control.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from active patient use if pressure readings are unreliable or absent
  • Transition to alternate ventilation
  • Confirm patient is safely ventilated and monitored

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the PRESSURE SENSOR FAIL ! alarm is displayed
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup vs during ventilation)
  • Check if airway pressure readings are missing, fixed, or inaccurate
  • Look for additional alarms or ventilation issues

Check Breathing Circuit Connections

  • Ensure all hoses are securely connected
  • Inspect for disconnections or loose fittings
  • Verify correct assembly of inspiratory and expiratory limbs

Inspect for Obstructions or Kinks

  • Check for kinked or compressed tubing
  • Look for blockages such as moisture, debris, or clogged filters
  • Ensure unobstructed gas flow path

Check Sensor-Related Connections (External)

  • Verify any accessible pressure sensing lines are properly connected
  • Inspect for damaged or pinched sensing tubing
  • Ensure connectors are secure without disassembly

Power Cycle the Device

  • Turn the machine off
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power the unit back on and observe behavior

Attempt Calibration (If Available)

  • Perform a pressure or system calibration using normal user procedures
  • Verify calibration completes successfully
  • Check if the alarm clears

Isolate Device vs External Cause

  • If all external checks are normal and alarm persists
  • If pressure readings remain absent or incorrect
  • Suspect internal pressure sensor failure or calibration fault

If the Problem Persists

If the PRESSURE SENSOR FAIL ! alarm continues after all checks:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before addressing pressure monitoring issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying PRESSURE SENSOR FAIL ! alarm with no airway pressure readings during setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Breathing circuit and external connections verified normal. Alarm persisted after power cycle and calibration attempt, indicating internal pressure sensor failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair and bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Pressure monitoring is a critical safety function. When a sensor failure alarm is present, verify all external factors and escalate appropriately to ensure patient safety and device reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
