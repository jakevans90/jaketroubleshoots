GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 05 ADC Configuration Error or ADC Failure

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 05 when an ADC configuration or reference-channel failure prevents confidence in accurate warmer sensing and reliable control operation. This system failure indicates that the reference channel is outside the specified range of 1597 to 1775 and may prevent safe clinical use of the warmer.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the Panda Warmer from patient use if Sys Fail 05 occurs while supporting an infant or during preparation for care.
  • Notify clinical staff and transfer the infant to a known-working warming device according to clinical procedure.
  • Do not rely on the warmer for thermal support while an unresolved system failure is present.
  • Expected outcome: The infant is supported by alternate equipment before troubleshooting begins.
  • Why it matters: An ADC or reference-channel failure may prevent the warmer from correctly interpreting critical sensing signals used for safe operation.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, power the unit on and confirm whether Sys Fail 05 is displayed.
  • Observe whether the error occurs immediately at startup, during self-test, after warming operation begins, or intermittently during operation.
  • Document associated behavior, such as failure to enter normal operation, heater output unavailable or interrupted, audible system-failure indication, or display and control response abnormalities.
  • Expected outcome: The Sys Fail 05 condition is reproduced or clearly documented.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a system-reported ADC configuration or reference-channel fault rather than a misunderstood operating condition.

3. Check Power Source and Basic External Conditions

  • Confirm that the warmer is connected to a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, strain relief, and visible power-entry area for damage, looseness, overheating, or contamination.
  • Disconnect the warmer from any questionable outlet or damaged power source and retest only on a verified outlet.
  • Expected outcome: The warmer receives stable power and the error does not result from an obvious external power issue.
  • Why it matters: Power instability or damaged supply connections should be ruled out before concluding that an internal control failure is present.

4. Inspect the Unit for Visible Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect the control panel, display, warmer head, column, connectors, and accessible external surfaces for evidence of fluid intrusion, impact damage, burn marks, unusual odor, loose connectors, or overheating.
  • Do not continue powering the warmer if there is evidence of fluid entry, burning, overheating, or significant physical damage.
  • Expected outcome: No external condition is found that could explain or worsen the failure.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage or contamination can affect electronics and make further operational testing unsafe.

5. Confirm That External Accessories Are Not Contributing

  • If removable external accessories are connected, inspect and disconnect only those not required to observe the startup fault, following facility procedure.
  • Check accessory connectors and cables for bent pins, contamination, damage, or improper connection.
  • Restart the warmer and observe whether Sys Fail 05 remains.
  • Expected outcome: The error either clears after correcting an external accessory concern or remains present independent of removable accessories.
  • Why it matters: This helps distinguish an external connection problem from an internal control or signal-processing failure.

6. Determine Whether the Error Persists After Basic Checks

  • After confirming safe power, visible condition, and external accessory status, power the warmer on again while it remains out of patient use.
  • If Sys Fail 05 clears and the warmer successfully completes required functional and safety verification, document the correction and return it to service according to department policy.
  • If Sys Fail 05 returns or remains active, stop operational troubleshooting.
  • Expected outcome: Persistent Sys Fail 05 is identified as a repeatable system-level failure requiring bench evaluation.
  • Why it matters: This error indicates an ADC configuration error or ADC failure because the reference channel is outside the allowed range of 1597 to 1775.

7. Escalate for Bench Evaluation or Repair

  • A persistent Sys Fail 05 after external checks strongly indicates an internal control electronics problem rather than a correctable user or accessory issue.
  • The manufacturer-directed repair path includes resetting NVRAM defaults and, if the failure remains, replacing the Control PCA.
  • These actions should be performed only by qualified service personnel using approved service procedures and required post-repair verification.
  • Expected outcome: The warmer is routed for appropriate repair without unnecessary continued troubleshooting in the clinical environment.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 05 remains after verifying power, inspecting the warmer for external damage or contamination, and ruling out removable external accessory concerns, external causes have been ruled out and the likely cause is an internal ADC/reference-channel or Control PCA failure.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the device for qualified bench evaluation or manufacturer-authorized repair.
  • Complete required functional and electrical safety verification before returning the warmer to patient use.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. An infant warmer with an unresolved system failure should not be used for patient warming.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an infant warmer while it is supporting an active patient. Move the infant to a known-working backup warming device first. A system-failure error involving sensing or control electronics requires patient safety to be addressed before evaluation begins.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 05 during startup and could not be considered reliable for patient warming."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified stable outlet power and inspected external condition and removable accessory connections. Sys Fail 05 remained reproducible, indicating likely internal ADC/reference-channel or Control PCA failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from service and labeled Out of Service. Routed unit for qualified bench evaluation, including manufacturer-directed NVRAM default reset and Control PCA replacement if the error persists. Return to service pending successful repair verification and safety testing."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Sys Fail 05 indicates that the Panda Warmer cannot confirm a valid ADC reference-channel reading, making dependable control operation uncertain. Protecting the patient, ruling out basic external causes, recognizing when the condition indicates internal failure, escalating for qualified repair, and documenting the findings clearly are essential Clinical Engineering actions.

That is successful troubleshooting.
