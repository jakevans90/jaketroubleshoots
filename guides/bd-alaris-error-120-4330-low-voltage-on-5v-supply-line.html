BD Alaris

Error: 120.4330 Low voltage on 5V supply line

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 120.4330 indicating low 5V supply, causing pump startup failure, rebooting, or loss of module/system functionality.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use immediately if actively infusing
  • Transfer infusion to a functioning pump or channel

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the Alaris PC unit and confirm Error 120.4330 appears
  • Observe if the issue occurs at startup or during operation
  • Determine if it affects the entire PC unit or specific modules
  • Look for rebooting, blank screen, or failed initialization

Check AC Power Source and Power Cord

  • Plug the PC unit into a known good outlet
  • Ensure the power cord is fully seated at the rear inlet
  • Inspect for damage, fraying, or loose connections
  • Try a known-good hospital-grade cord

Check Battery Status

  • Review battery indicators or system status if accessible
  • Power off and reseat the battery (biomed access only)
  • Inspect for swelling, overheating, or damage
  • Swap with a known-good battery if available

Power Cycle the System

  • Shut down the PC unit
  • Disconnect AC power for 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart the unit

Remove All Modules and Retest

  • Detach all infusion modules from the PC unit
  • Power on the PC unit alone
  • Reattach modules one at a time
  • Identify if a specific module triggers the error

Inspect for Physical or Environmental Damage

  • Check for liquid intrusion
  • Inspect module connectors for bent pins or damage
  • Look for signs of overheating or impact

Isolate the Issue

  • If resolved with power cord replacement, suspect external power issue
  • If resolved after removing a module, identify faulty module
  • If persistent with no modules and known-good power, suspect internal PC unit failure

If the Problem Persists

If Error 120.4330 continues after all external checks:

  • Remove the PC unit from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Persistent low 5V supply indicates internal power supply or regulation failure within the PC unit.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot power-related errors on an active patient. Always transfer infusion to a backup device first to prevent therapy interruption.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"BD Alaris PC unit displaying Error 120.4330 and intermittently rebooting."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Error persisted after verifying AC power and removing all modules, indicating internal 5V supply fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed PC unit from service and sent for internal power supply repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Stable low-voltage rails are critical for Alaris system operation. Always rule out external power and module-related causes first, but persistent voltage errors typically require escalation for internal repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
