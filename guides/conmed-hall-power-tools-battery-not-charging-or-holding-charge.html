CONMED Hall Power Tools

Battery Not Charging or Holding Charge

Asset Type

Surgical Power Tool System

Manufacturer

CONMED

Model

Hall Power Tools

What This Guide Helps With

Battery not charging, short runtime, or failure to power handpieces due to charger, battery, or connection issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the system from active use before troubleshooting
  • Ensure an alternate power tool system is available if needed

Expected: No patient dependence on the affected device
Why it matters: Power tool failure during a procedure can cause delays or safety risks

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to charge the battery and observe behavior
  • Check charging indicator lights
  • Confirm battery seating in charger
  • Evaluate runtime when installed in handpiece

Expected: Issue is reproducible (no charge, intermittent charge, or rapid discharge)
Why it matters: Confirms true failure vs user error or isolated event

Check Charger Power Source (Most Common External Cause)

  • Confirm charger is plugged into a known-good outlet
  • Test outlet with another device
  • Check for power indicator lights on charger

Expected: Charger powers on normally
Why it matters: No charger power = no battery charging

Inspect Battery Seating and Contacts

  • Remove and reinstall the battery into the charger
  • Ensure battery is fully seated and locked in place
  • Inspect battery contacts for debris, corrosion, or damage
  • Clean contacts if needed using approved methods

Expected: Battery seats securely and aligns properly
Why it matters: Poor contact prevents charging even if charger is functional

Check Charger Indicator Behavior

  • Insert battery and monitor LED status (charging, full, fault)
  • Compare behavior with known-good battery if available

Expected: Normal charge indication (not fault or no response)
Why it matters: LED patterns help isolate charger vs battery failure

Swap with Known-Good Components

  • Place suspect battery in a known-good charger
  • Place known-good battery in suspect charger

Expected:
If battery fails in multiple chargers → battery issue
If multiple batteries fail in one charger → charger issue
Why it matters: Fastest way to isolate the failed component

Check for Battery Age or Usage Degradation

  • Check battery age or usage cycles if tracked
  • Inspect for swelling, overheating, or physical damage

Expected: Older or damaged batteries may show reduced runtime or no charge
Why it matters: Rechargeable batteries degrade over time and lose capacity

Inspect for Environmental or Handling Issues

  • Ensure charger is used within appropriate temperature range
  • Check for fluid exposure or OR contamination
  • Verify proper cleaning practices are being followed

Expected: No environmental interference with charging
Why it matters: Extreme conditions or contamination can damage batteries or chargers

If the Problem Persists

If power source, charger function, battery seating, and component swaps have been verified, the issue is likely internal.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and ensures proper equipment reliability.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot or swap batteries during an active surgical case. Always transition to a backup system first to avoid procedural interruption.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Battery not charging and losing power quickly during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed battery pack confirmed by testing in known-good charger."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced defective battery and verified proper charging and runtime."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Charger power indicator status
  • Battery LED behavior during charge
  • Results of battery/charger swap test
  • Condition of battery contacts
  • Battery age or visible damage
  • Final device status after testing

Final Thought

Battery issues are most often caused by simple external factors like charger power, contact alignment, or normal battery wear. Systematic isolation of components ensures accurate diagnosis without unnecessary escalation. Always prioritize patient safety and document clearly for future reference.

That is successful troubleshooting.
