STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Door Unsealed - Steam pressure in door seal below required level

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: Door Unsealed indicating loss of steam pressure in the door seal, causing cycle abort and preventing proper chamber sealing required for sterilization.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from use immediately if the alarm occurs during a cycle
  • Do not use instruments from a failed or aborted cycle
  • Ensure alternative sterilization methods are available

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the “Door Unsealed” alarm is displayed
  • Observe if the cycle automatically aborted
  • Check if the chamber vented to atmospheric pressure
  • Note when the alarm occurs during operation

Check Door Position and Seal Condition

  • Ensure the door is fully closed and latched
  • Inspect the gasket for damage, debris, or misalignment
  • Verify proper seating of the door

Check Steam Supply to Door Seal

  • Verify facility steam supply is available
  • Confirm supply valves are fully open
  • Listen for steam flow during seal activation

Check Seal Activation Behavior

  • Start a cycle and observe seal inflation
  • Listen for air or steam leaks around the door
  • Confirm seal holds pressure once activated

Inspect for External Leaks

  • Check around the door for audible hissing
  • Look for visible steam escaping
  • Inspect surrounding components for leakage signs

Verify External Connections

  • Inspect accessible wiring to seal pressure switches (PS1/PS2)
  • Ensure connectors are secure and undamaged
  • Avoid internal disassembly

Isolate the Likely Cause

  • No steam supply suggests facility issue
  • No seal inflation indicates activation issue
  • Seal inflates but leaks suggests gasket failure
  • Normal behavior suggests control or sensor fault

Stop When Resolved

  • Correct any simple issues such as door alignment or steam supply
  • Clear the alarm
  • Run a test cycle to confirm proper operation

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Remove the unit from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or continue using a sterilizer with a door seal alarm on an active patient load. Always transfer workload to a backup sterilizer first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Door Unsealed alarm occurred during cycle and sterilizer aborted."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Door seal pressure dropped below required level; no external leaks found; suspected seal pressure switch or solenoid issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and sent for repair of seal pressure system components."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Door seal pressure is critical to sterilization integrity. Always verify simple mechanical and supply causes first, and escalate when pressure-related issues persist.

That is successful troubleshooting.
