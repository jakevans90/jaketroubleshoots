Masimo Rad-5

SpO2 Readings Inaccurate or Unstable

Asset Type

Pulse Oximeter

Manufacturer

Masimo

Model

Rad-5

What This Guide Helps With

Inaccurate, fluctuating, or unreliable SpO2 readings caused by sensor issues, poor perfusion, motion artifact, or environmental interference.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If readings are unreliable, do not depend on this device for clinical decisions.
  • Move the patient to a known working monitor.

Expected: Patient monitoring continues safely on alternate equipment
Why it matters: Inaccurate SpO2 can lead to improper treatment decisions

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Observe the device while in use.
  • Check if readings fluctuate rapidly
  • Compare SpO2 with another device (if available)
  • Note any alarms or low perfusion indicators

Expected: Issue is confirmed and reproducible
Why it matters: Confirms a true device or setup issue, not a one-time event

Check Sensor Placement and Condition (Most Common Cause)

  • Inspect and reposition the sensor.
  • Ensure proper placement on finger, toe, or approved site
  • Remove nail polish or artificial nails
  • Check for proper alignment of emitter and detector
  • Inspect sensor for damage (cracks, frayed cable)

Expected: Stable waveform and consistent readings
Why it matters: Misalignment or damage is the most common cause of inaccurate readings

Assess Patient Conditions (Perfusion and Motion)

  • Evaluate external patient-related factors.
  • Check for cold extremities or poor circulation
  • Look for excessive patient movement or tremors
  • Try a different site (earlobe, alternate finger)

Expected: Improved signal stability at a better site
Why it matters: Low perfusion and motion artifact directly affect SpO2 accuracy

Check Sensor Cable and Connection

  • Inspect the connection between the sensor and device.
  • Ensure cable is fully seated in the Rad-5 port
  • Check for bent pins or loose connection
  • Swap with a known working sensor if available

Expected: Device detects sensor and readings stabilize
Why it matters: Intermittent connections can cause unstable or incorrect readings

Evaluate Ambient Light Interference

  • Check for environmental factors.
  • Shield the sensor from bright surgical lights or sunlight
  • Ensure sensor is fully closed and properly fitted

Expected: Reduced signal noise and improved accuracy
Why it matters: Excess light can interfere with optical measurements

Check Device Settings and Mode

  • Verify configuration.
  • Confirm correct patient mode (adult/pediatric if applicable)
  • Ensure no incorrect averaging or sensitivity settings

Expected: Settings match clinical use and readings stabilize
Why it matters: Incorrect settings can affect displayed values and responsiveness

Perform a Power Reset

  • Cycle the device.
  • Turn off the unit
  • Remove and reinsert batteries
  • Power back on

Expected: Device resets and readings normalize if caused by a temporary fault
Why it matters: Clears minor software or processing errors

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes (sensor, patient condition, environment, and connections) have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and is part of proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot inaccurate SpO2 readings on an actively monitored patient. Always transition the patient to a reliable device before testing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nursing reported unstable and fluctuating SpO2 readings on Masimo Rad-5."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Damaged and misaligned finger sensor causing poor signal detection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced sensor with known good unit; verified stable and accurate readings."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Sensor swapped and tested
  • Alternate site attempted
  • Presence of motion artifact
  • Perfusion status observed
  • Ambient light conditions checked
  • Alarm behavior noted
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

SpO2 inaccuracies are most often caused by external factors like sensor placement, patient condition, or environmental interference. Always start simple and verify these before assuming device failure. Protecting patient safety and documenting clearly ensures effective troubleshooting and reliable equipment performance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
