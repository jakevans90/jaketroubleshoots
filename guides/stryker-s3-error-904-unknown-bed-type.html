Stryker S3

Error: 904 Unknown Bed Type

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Error 904 Unknown Bed Type, which causes the Stryker S3 hospital bed to stop all functions when the software detects an unrecognized bed-type configuration value. This condition can prevent safe positioning, transport, and use of installed patient-support features.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the Stryker S3 bed from patient use if Unknown Bed Type - Call Service is displayed or bed functions are unavailable.
  • If a patient is on the bed, coordinate a safe transfer to a known-working bed before troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on a bed with disabled or unreliable functions for positioning, transport, or patient care.

Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on a bed with disabled or unreliable functions.

Why it matters: This condition may stop all bed functions and create an immediate patient-care risk.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the bed removed from patient use, confirm that the display shows Unknown Bed Type - Call Service.
  • Document whether the message occurs immediately at startup, remains continuously displayed, or appears intermittently.
  • Determine whether all bed functions are unavailable while the message is active.
  • Document any recent service, configuration change, option installation, or electronics replacement.

Expected outcome: Error 904 is reproduced or clearly confirmed, with the associated loss of bed function documented.

Why it matters: This helps distinguish a configuration-recognition failure from a normal lockout, isolated control issue, or accessory complaint.

3. Check Power Source and External Condition

  • Verify the bed is connected to a known-working power source.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, and visible external wiring for damage.
  • Inspect for signs of impact, fluid intrusion, loose covers, damaged control panels, or evidence of recent repair activity.

Expected outcome: The bed has reliable incoming power and no obvious external damage contributing to the fault.

Why it matters: Although Error 904 indicates a configuration issue, visible damage or recent service activity may help identify the cause.

4. Confirm the Failure Is Not a Simple Control or Lockout Condition

  • Attempt only basic, safe functional checks with the bed removed from patient use.
  • Confirm whether the bed stops all functions while the Unknown Bed Type message is active.
  • Verify that the complaint is not limited to a control lockout, pendant disconnection, or one failed movement control.

Expected outcome: Bed functions remain unavailable specifically while the configuration-related error is active.

Why it matters: This isolates the condition as a bed-level failure rather than an individual external control problem.

5. Review Bed Type and Installed Configuration Information

  • Where accessible through the approved service information screen, review the identified bed type and installed options.
  • Compare the displayed information with the actual Stryker S3 bed and its installed features.
  • Look for an incorrect bed type, abnormal configuration information, missing option identification, or a configuration change following recent service.

Expected outcome: The bed type and installed option configuration either appear correct or an abnormal configuration value is identified.

Why it matters: Error 904 occurs when the software receives a bed-type configuration value that it does not recognize.

6. Inspect Accessible Configuration-Related Connections

  • Following facility safety procedures, inspect accessible connectors and harnesses associated with bed identification or recently serviced electronics.
  • Check for loose or partially seated connectors, pinched wiring, damaged harnesses, contamination, or evidence of improper reassembly.
  • Do not proceed into board-level troubleshooting or unauthorized internal repair.

Expected outcome: Accessible connections are secure and no obvious configuration-related damage is present.

Why it matters: A loose, damaged, incorrectly configured, or failed component may cause the bed to report an invalid bed type.

7. Correct an Identified Authorized Configuration or Connection Issue

  • If a loose accessible connection or authorized configuration issue is identified within Clinical Engineering scope, correct it according to facility procedure.
  • Cycle power as appropriate and verify whether Unknown Bed Type - Call Service clears.
  • Do not return the bed to service solely because the message disappears; complete functional verification first.

Expected outcome: The error message clears after correction of an identified issue.

Why it matters: The bed cannot be safely used unless its configuration is recognized and normal operation is verified.

8. Perform Functional Verification Before Returning to Service

  • If the error is cleared, verify applicable bed movement controls, head and foot articulation, and hi-low operation.
  • Verify siderail controls and indicators, brake and steering status, scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness functions when installed and applicable.
  • Confirm the error does not recur during testing.
  • Document the final functional status before returning the bed to service.

Expected outcome: All installed and applicable bed functions operate normally without recurrence of Error 904.

Why it matters: A configuration-related failure can affect multiple systems and requires complete verification before clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If Error 904 Unknown Bed Type remains after the power source, visible external condition, basic controls, accessible connections, and authorized configuration information have been checked, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent error indicates an invalid configuration condition or failed controlling electronic component requiring further repair evaluation.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the bed Out of Service.
  • Send the bed for repair or bench evaluation according to facility procedure.
  • Do not return the bed to patient use while the error remains active or functions are unavailable.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this condition on an active patient. Because Error 904 may disable all bed functions, move the patient to a backup bed first and do not rely on the affected bed until full function has been verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displayed Error 904 Unknown Bed Type - Call Service at startup. Bed functions were unavailable while the error message was active."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Bed software detected an unrecognized configuration value. Power source, visible condition, basic controls, and accessible configuration-related connections were checked; error remained active, indicating a configuration or controlling electronics fault requiring further evaluation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from patient use, labeled unit Out of Service, verified external checks and accessible connections, and referred bed for bench evaluation/repair due to persistent Error 904 and unavailable functions."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error 904 indicates that the Stryker S3 cannot recognize its configured bed type and may disable all functions. Safe troubleshooting requires immediate removal from patient use, logical external checks, proper escalation when the fault persists, and complete CCR documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
