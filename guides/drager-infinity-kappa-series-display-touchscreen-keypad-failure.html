Drager Infinity Kappa Series

Display or Touchscreen / Keypad Failure

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Infinity Kappa Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Infinity Kappa monitor display, touchscreen, or keypad is unresponsive, partially responsive, or behaves abnormally (e.g., flickering, frozen screen, or incorrect inputs). The focus is on external checks, system settings, and safe escalation rather than internal electronics repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Confirm the monitor is plugged into a working outlet or UPS.
  • Check battery status if on battery operation.
  • Why: A weak power supply can cause display or touch issues.

Inspect Physical Connections

  • Ensure all cables and connectors to the display module are secure.
  • Check for visible damage, loose connectors, or bent pins.
  • Why: Loose or damaged cables can prevent proper touchscreen or keypad function.

Check for External Interference

  • Remove any nearby strong electromagnetic devices.
  • Ensure monitor isn’t exposed to liquids or extreme temperatures.
  • Why: Environmental factors can temporarily affect touchscreen response.

Perform a Soft Reboot

  • Power cycle the monitor according to manufacturer guidelines.
  • Observe if the display initializes normally.
  • Why: Temporary software glitches can cause unresponsiveness.

Inspect On-Screen Indicators and Error Messages

  • Look for any error codes on startup or during operation.
  • Record messages for reference.
  • Why: Error codes can indicate module or software issues requiring escalation.

Test Touchscreen and Keypad Inputs

  • Attempt standard navigation and input across all areas of the screen and keypad.
  • Note any unresponsive regions or inconsistent behavior.
  • Why: Pinpoints whether the problem is localized (screen area) or generalized.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out (power, connections, environment, software reset).

Likely internal display, touchscreen, or keypad module failure.

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to manufacturer-approved repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Engineering best practice: Knowing when to escalate prevents patient risk and equipment damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt touchscreen or keypad troubleshooting while the patient is actively connected.

Move the patient to another monitor first.

Only perform checks when it is safe and will not interrupt patient monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Monitor touchscreen and keypad unresponsive; display flickers intermittently.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Power confirmed, cables secure, environmental factors normal, soft reboot ineffective; inputs fail across entire screen and keypad.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for bench evaluation/repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified.
  • Battery level and UPS status.
  • Any visible damage to cables or connectors.
  • Error codes or abnormal indicators.
  • Description of touchscreen/keypad responsiveness.
  • Final device status before removal.

Final Thought

Patient safety, logical troubleshooting, and proper escalation are paramount. Document all steps clearly using CCR to maintain compliance and ensure continuity of care. External checks eliminate simple causes before committing to repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
