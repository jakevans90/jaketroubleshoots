Stryker 1688 Camera System

Coupler or Scope Connection Errors

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

1688 Camera System

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes when the camera coupler or endoscope connection fails, preventing proper video signal and affecting surgical visualization.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm no patient is actively undergoing a procedure with the affected camera. Move the patient to a backup camera or scope to maintain continuity of care.

2. Inspect Physical Connections

  • Check the camera coupler and endoscope connectors for visible debris, scratches, or bent pins.
  • Action: Clean connectors with approved wipes; ensure proper alignment before reconnecting.
  • Expected outcome: Secure connection may restore video signal.

3. Confirm Proper Coupler Engagement

  • Verify that the coupler is fully seated and clicks into place on the camera head.
  • Action: Re-seat coupler carefully without forcing it.
  • Expected outcome: Camera recognizes scope; image returns.

4. Swap Cables or Couplers

  • Use a known working coupler and endoscope to isolate the faulty component.
  • Action: Replace one component at a time.
  • Expected outcome: Identify if issue is with coupler, scope, or camera head.

5. Check Camera Head Recognition

  • Ensure the camera console detects the camera head after reconnection.
  • Action: Observe for “camera connected” indicator or image display.
  • Expected outcome: Recognition confirms external connection integrity.

6. Verify Console Settings

  • Ensure the console input mode matches the connected scope type.
  • Action: Adjust input selection or reset camera settings if needed.
  • Expected outcome: Correct input restores normal image.

If the Problem Persists

External checks (connectors, coupler, scope, cables) have been ruled out.

Device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Proper escalation prevents patient risk and further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot coupler or scope connections during an active procedure.

Always have a backup scope and camera ready for patient safety.

Avoid forcing connectors, which can damage pins or optical surfaces.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Camera not recognizing endoscope; video output absent."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Coupler alignment loose and connectors partially obstructed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned connectors, reseated coupler; camera now recognizes scope."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Verified coupler click engagement
  • Checked for debris or bent pins
  • Confirmed camera head recognition
  • Scope swap test performed
  • Device status: operational after troubleshooting

Final Thought

Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures patient safety while isolating external connection issues. Documenting each action prevents repeat failures and informs proper repair escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
