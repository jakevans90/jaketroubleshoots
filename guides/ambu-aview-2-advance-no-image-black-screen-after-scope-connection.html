Ambu aView 2 Advance

No Image / Black Screen After Scope Connection

Asset Type

Endoscopy Monitor

Manufacturer

Ambu

Model

aView 2 Advance

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting black screen or no image after connecting a compatible Ambu endoscope to the aView 2 Advance monitor.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the aView 2 Advance while it is being relied on for an active procedure.

If the monitor or scope is needed for patient care, provide a known-good monitor and compatible scope before continuing.

Expected outcome: Patient care is supported by a functioning visualization system.

Why it matters: A black screen during endoscopy can delay visualization, airway assessment, or procedural decision-making.

Confirm the Reported Problem

Power on the aView 2 Advance and connect the reported scope.

Confirm whether the monitor has:

  • A completely black screen
  • A powered display with no camera image
  • A frozen image
  • A “no scope detected” or similar connection message
  • Normal menus but no live video

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified.

If the image appears normally, document unable to duplicate and return the device only after functional verification.

Verify Monitor Power and Startup

Confirm the monitor powers on fully and reaches the normal operating screen.

Check for low battery, charging status, startup delays, or abnormal indicator behavior.

Connect the monitor to approved power if battery level is low.

Expected outcome: The monitor powers on normally and is not failing due to low power.

If charging or power behavior is abnormal, stop troubleshooting the image complaint and evaluate the monitor for a power issue.

Check Display Brightness and Screen Visibility

Verify that the screen brightness is not set too low.

Inspect the display under normal room lighting and reduce glare if needed.

Expected outcome: Menus and on-screen indicators are visible.

If the display itself remains black with no visible menu or status information, remove the monitor from service for bench evaluation.

Confirm Scope Compatibility

Verify that the connected Ambu scope is compatible with the aView 2 Advance system.

Do not assume all Ambu scopes use the same connection or software support.

Expected outcome: The connected scope is appropriate for use with the monitor.

If the scope is not compatible, use the correct scope/monitor combination and document the finding. Stop troubleshooting.

Inspect the Scope Connector

Disconnect the scope and inspect the connector for:

  • Bent or damaged contacts
  • Fluid contamination
  • Debris
  • Cracked housing
  • Loose strain relief
  • Evidence of rough handling

Expected outcome: The scope connector is clean, dry, and physically intact.

If connector damage or contamination is found, remove the scope from use and test the monitor with a known-good compatible scope.

Inspect the Monitor Scope Port

Visually inspect the aView 2 Advance scope input port.

Look for:

  • Obstruction
  • Bent pins or damaged contacts
  • Foreign material
  • Fluid intrusion
  • Cracked or loose port housing

Expected outcome: The monitor input port appears clean and undamaged.

If the port is damaged or contaminated, remove the monitor from service and send it for repair evaluation.

Reconnect the Scope Correctly

Reconnect the scope fully and securely.

Confirm that the connector seats completely without forcing it.

Avoid twisting, angling, or partially inserting the connector.

Expected outcome: The monitor detects the scope and displays a live image.

If the image returns, the issue was likely incomplete seating. Document the correction and verify operation before return to service.

Power Cycle With Scope Connected

Turn the monitor off, leave the compatible scope connected, then power the monitor back on.

Observe whether the monitor detects the scope during startup.

Expected outcome: The monitor completes startup and displays the scope image.

If the image returns after reboot, perform several disconnect/reconnect checks before returning the device to service.

Test With a Known-Good Compatible Scope

Connect a known-good compatible Ambu scope to the same aView 2 Advance monitor.

Expected outcome: The monitor displays a live image from the known-good scope.

If the known-good scope works, the original scope is the likely cause. Remove the original scope from use and document the failed accessory.

Test the Reported Scope on a Known-Good Monitor

When available, connect the reported scope to another compatible known-good Ambu monitor.

Expected outcome: The scope displays an image on the alternate monitor.

If the scope works on another monitor, the original aView 2 Advance monitor is likely the issue and should be removed from service.

Check for Intermittent Connection Behavior

With a test scope connected and not in patient use, gently move the connector and cable near the scope plug.

Do not sharply bend or stress the cable.

Watch for:

  • Image loss
  • Flicker
  • Scope disconnect messages
  • Freezing
  • Black screen returning

Expected outcome: The image remains stable during light handling.

If movement causes image loss, suspect connector, cable, or input port damage. Remove the affected item from service.

Inspect for Scope Lens or Distal Tip Obstruction

If the monitor is powered and the image area appears dark rather than truly disconnected, inspect the scope’s distal tip.

Check for a covered lens, packaging material, debris, fluid, or damage.

Expected outcome: The camera lens is unobstructed and produces a visible image.

If clearing the obstruction restores the image, document the cause and verify image quality before return to use.

Check for Error Messages or System Prompts

Review the monitor screen for any connection, software, compatibility, or accessory-related message.

Note the exact wording in the work order.

Expected outcome: Any system message helps identify whether the issue is scope detection, video signal, compatibility, or monitor hardware.

If the monitor displays a recurring error with multiple known-good scopes, remove it from service for repair evaluation.

Verify Final Operation

Before returning the device, verify:

  • Monitor powers on normally
  • Compatible scope is detected
  • Live image displays
  • Image remains stable during normal handling
  • No abnormal error messages appear
  • Battery or power status is acceptable

Expected outcome: The aView 2 Advance and scope pass basic functional verification.

If all checks pass, return the device to service and document the results.

If the Problem Persists

If the aView 2 Advance still shows no image or a black screen after power, compatibility, connector, scope, and known-good accessory checks, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely related to the monitor input port, internal video processing, software detection, display subsystem, or scope-side electronic failure.

The device or failed accessory should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue using an endoscopy monitor that cannot reliably display a live image.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a black screen during an active procedure unless clinical staff have already moved to a working visualization system. A monitor that intermittently loses image should not be returned to clinical use without repeated connection and image stability checks.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Ambu aView 2 Advance displayed a black screen after connecting an Ambu scope."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found the reported scope failed to produce an image on the aView 2 Advance, while a known-good compatible scope displayed normally."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the failed scope from service, verified the aView 2 Advance with a known-good compatible scope, and returned the monitor to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Monitor powered on normally
  • Battery or AC power status checked
  • Scope model and compatibility verified
  • Original scope tested
  • Known-good scope tested
  • Reported scope tested on another monitor, if available
  • Scope connector condition
  • Monitor input port condition
  • Error messages or prompts observed
  • Image behavior during light cable movement
  • Environmental factors such as lighting, glare, fluid exposure, or room conditions
  • Final device status

Final Thought

No-image complaints on endoscopy monitors should be approached logically: protect the patient first, confirm the failure, rule out power and connection issues, then isolate the scope from the monitor using known-good testing. Clear CCR documentation helps show whether the problem followed the scope, stayed with the monitor, or could not be duplicated.

That is successful troubleshooting.
