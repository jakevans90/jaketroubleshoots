Roche Accu-Chek Inform II

Battery Not Charging or Docking Issues

Asset Type

Blood Glucose Meter

Manufacturer

Roche

Model

Accu-Chek Inform II

What This Guide Helps With

Meter not charging in base, low battery warnings, or failure to power when docked due to external power or docking issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the meter from clinical use if battery is critically low or unreliable. Use another meter for patient testing.
  • Expected: No interruption to patient care
  • Why it matters: A dead meter during testing can delay care and compromise workflow

Verify Docking Behavior

  • Place the meter firmly into the docking station. Observe for charging indicators (LEDs or on-screen battery icon).
  • Expected: Charging indicator activates within a few seconds
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is with charging initiation or power delivery

Check Power to Docking Station

  • Ensure the dock is plugged into a known working outlet. Verify power LED (if present) is illuminated.
  • Test outlet with another device
  • Check power adapter connection
  • Expected: Dock has confirmed power
  • Why it matters: No dock power = no charging, even if meter is functional

Inspect Docking Contacts (Meter and Base)

  • Visually inspect metal charging contacts on both the meter and dock.
  • Look for dirt, corrosion, or damage
  • Clean gently with approved wipe if needed
  • Expected: Contacts are clean and intact
  • Why it matters: Poor contact prevents charging even with good power

Reseat the Meter in Dock

  • Remove and reinsert the meter slowly and firmly. Ensure proper alignment.
  • Expected: Meter seats securely and begins charging
  • Why it matters: Misalignment is a common cause of intermittent charging

Try a Known Working Dock

  • Place the meter into another verified working docking station.
  • Expected: Meter charges normally in alternate dock
  • Why it matters: Helps isolate whether the issue is with the meter or the dock
  • If it charges → Original dock is faulty
  • If it does not charge → Meter or battery issue likely

Check Battery Condition

  • If the meter powers on briefly but does not hold charge:
  • Observe for rapid battery drain
  • Check battery age if known
  • Expected: Battery holds charge after docking
  • Why it matters: Worn batteries may not accept or retain charge

Inspect Dock Cable and Connections

  • Check for:
  • Loose or damaged cables
  • Frayed power cords
  • Poor connection at power brick
  • Expected: All cables secure and intact
  • Why it matters: Intermittent power can mimic charging failure

Power Cycle the Meter

  • Remove the meter from the dock and restart it if possible, then reseat it in the dock.
  • Expected: Meter resumes normal charging behavior
  • Why it matters: Clears minor software-related charging issues

If the Problem Persists

If all external power sources, docks, and connections have been verified and the meter still does not charge, the issue is likely internal (battery failure or charging circuitry).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and wasted troubleshooting time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot or rely on a meter with low battery during active patient testing. Always switch to a fully functional device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Meter not charging in docking station and showing low battery warning."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Faulty docking station power supply preventing charging."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced docking station and verified proper charging function."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed working
  • Alternate dock tested
  • Charging indicator behavior observed
  • Contacts cleaned or inspected
  • Battery runtime noted
  • Cable condition verified
  • Final device status (working / removed from service)

Final Thought

Charging issues are most often caused by simple external problems like power loss, dirty contacts, or faulty docks. Always rule these out first before assuming internal failure. Clear documentation and proper escalation protect both patient safety and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
