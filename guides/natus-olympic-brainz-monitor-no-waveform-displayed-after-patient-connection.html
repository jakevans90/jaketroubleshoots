Natus Olympic Brainz Monitor

No waveform displayed after patient connection

Asset Type

EEG System

Manufacturer

Natus

Model

Olympic Brainz Monitor

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting missing EEG waveform display after patient connection due to lead, electrode, cable, setup, or device input issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the patient is clinically stable and that troubleshooting will not delay needed monitoring or treatment.

If EEG monitoring is clinically required, notify clinical staff and arrange an alternate monitoring method or backup EEG system if available.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not dependent on a monitor that is not displaying waveforms.

Confirm the Reported Issue

Verify whether the Olympic Brainz Monitor powers on normally and whether the display is functioning.

Check if the issue is truly no waveform, or if the screen is blank, frozen, dimmed, paused, or displaying an error message.

Expected outcome: The failure is confirmed as no waveform after patient connection, not a general power or display issue.

Check That Monitoring Has Been Started

Confirm the system is in an active monitoring or recording mode and has not been left in standby, paused, review, demo, or setup mode.

Look for obvious screen indicators showing that acquisition is active.

Expected outcome: The monitor is actively attempting to acquire EEG data.

If starting or resuming monitoring restores the waveform, stop troubleshooting.

Verify Patient Cable Connection at the Monitor

Inspect the patient cable connection to the Olympic Brainz Monitor.

Make sure the connector is fully seated, correctly oriented, and not partially inserted.

Gently check for looseness at the input connection without stressing the cable.

Expected outcome: The patient cable is securely connected with no loose or intermittent connection.

If reseating the cable restores the waveform, stop troubleshooting and document the loose connection.

Inspect the Patient Cable and Lead Set

Examine the patient cable, electrode leads, and connectors for:

  • Bent pins
  • Broken strain relief
  • Crushed or kinked cable sections
  • Torn insulation
  • Fluid contamination
  • Corrosion or residue inside connectors

Expected outcome: Cable and lead set appear intact and safe to use.

If damage is found, remove the cable or lead set from service and replace it.

Confirm Correct Electrode / Sensor Application

Verify with clinical staff that the electrodes or sensors are applied correctly for the intended EEG monitoring setup.

Check that electrodes are placed securely and have not lifted from the skin.

Expected outcome: Electrodes are attached correctly and have good patient contact.

Poor electrode contact can prevent usable EEG signal from appearing.

Check Electrode Skin Contact and Prep

Confirm that the skin was prepared according to clinical procedure and that electrode gel, adhesive, or sensor contact is adequate.

Look for dry electrodes, poor adhesion, hair interference, sweat, lotion, or skin oils affecting contact.

Expected outcome: Electrode contact is acceptable and not causing loss of signal.

If improving electrode contact restores the waveform, stop troubleshooting.

Check for Lead-Off or Impedance Warnings

Look for any lead-off, high impedance, disconnected electrode, or poor contact messages on the monitor.

If available, review the impedance or signal quality indicators.

Expected outcome: The monitor identifies whether the issue is related to poor electrode contact or disconnected leads.

If high impedance or lead-off is present, correct the electrode or lead issue before suspecting the monitor.

Verify All Required Leads Are Connected

Confirm that the required electrodes, reference, ground, or common leads are connected as needed for the monitoring configuration.

A missing reference or ground connection may prevent normal waveform display even if other electrodes are attached.

Expected outcome: The complete patient connection path is present.

Swap Known-Good Disposable Electrodes or Sensors

If the issue persists, replace the electrodes or sensors with a known-good set.

Avoid reusing questionable disposables if adhesion, gel quality, or packaging integrity is uncertain.

Expected outcome: A defective or poorly contacting disposable is ruled out.

If the waveform appears with new electrodes or sensors, stop troubleshooting.

Swap the Patient Cable or Lead Set

Test with a known-good compatible patient cable or lead set if available.

Do not continue using a cable that causes intermittent signal loss when moved or flexed.

Expected outcome: A cable or lead set fault is either confirmed or ruled out.

If the waveform appears with a replacement cable, remove the failed cable from service.

Check Display Scale, Filters, and Channel Visibility

Confirm the waveform is not hidden due to screen layout, channel selection, gain, sensitivity, filter, or display configuration.

Reset the view to a normal acquisition screen if appropriate.

Expected outcome: Waveform channels are visible and scaled appropriately for clinical review.

Restart the Monitor Safely

If patient care allows and clinical staff approves, disconnect from active use and restart the Olympic Brainz Monitor.

Reconnect the known-good cable and electrodes after restart.

Expected outcome: Temporary software or acquisition lockup is cleared.

If waveform display returns after restart, monitor for recurrence and document the behavior.

Check for Environmental or Interference Issues

Look for strong electrical interference sources near the patient or monitor, including damaged power cords, nearby equipment, poorly grounded accessories, or excessive cable movement.

Separate EEG leads from power cords when possible.

Expected outcome: Signal acquisition is not being masked by avoidable interference.

Verify the Issue Across More Than One Setup

If available, test the monitor with a known-good patient simulator, test fixture, or known-good electrode/cable setup according to facility procedure.

Expected outcome: The issue is isolated to either patient setup/accessories or the monitor itself.

If no waveform appears with known-good accessories, suspect an internal acquisition or input issue.

If the Problem Persists

If the Olympic Brainz Monitor still does not display waveforms after verifying patient safety, active monitoring mode, electrode contact, lead placement, patient cable condition, accessory substitution, and restart, common external causes have been ruled out.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue repeated bedside troubleshooting if the monitor cannot reliably acquire EEG signal. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot EEG acquisition problems in a way that delays needed patient monitoring. If the patient requires continuous EEG monitoring and the waveform is absent, move to a backup monitor or alternate clinical plan first, then troubleshoot the removed device safely.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Natus Olympic Brainz Monitor powered on but displayed no EEG waveform after patient connection."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Troubleshooting found poor electrode contact and high impedance at the patient connection, preventing usable waveform acquisition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Electrodes were reapplied with proper skin contact, patient cable was reseated, waveform display returned, and monitor was left in service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the monitor powered on normally
  • Whether the screen was blank, frozen, or active with no waveform
  • Patient cable connection checked
  • Lead set inspected for damage
  • Electrodes or sensors replaced
  • Lead-off or impedance messages observed
  • Waveform returned after reseating cable
  • Waveform returned after replacing electrodes
  • Known-good cable tested
  • Any unusual alarms or messages
  • Any signs of fluid contamination
  • Power behavior during troubleshooting
  • Environmental factors or interference sources noted
  • Indicator lights or acquisition status shown
  • Final device status

Final Thought

No waveform after patient connection should be approached logically from the outside in. Confirm patient safety first, then verify monitoring mode, electrodes, lead contact, patient cable condition, and accessory integrity before suspecting an internal acquisition fault. Clear documentation helps separate setup-related issues from true device failures.

That is successful troubleshooting.
