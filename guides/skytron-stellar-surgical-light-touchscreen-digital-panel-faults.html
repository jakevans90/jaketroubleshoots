Skytron Stellar Surgical Light

Touchscreen or Digital Control Panel Faults

Asset Type

Surgical Light

Manufacturer

Skytron

Model

Stellar

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Skytron Stellar surgical light’s touchscreen or digital control panel is unresponsive, erratic, or displays error messages. Such faults can impact light positioning, intensity adjustment, or other programmable functions. The focus is on external, easily verifiable checks before assuming internal electronics failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Power and Connections

  • Verify the light is properly connected to a live electrical outlet.
  • Check that all main power switches are on.
  • Confirm no tripped breakers or blown fuses are present.

Why: A completely unpowered system can mimic touchscreen or panel failure.

Inspect Control Panel for Physical Damage

  • Look for cracks, water intrusion, or obvious debris on the touchscreen.
  • Check that mounting connectors are secure.

Why: Physical damage or loose connectors can prevent proper panel response.

Clean Touchscreen or Panel

  • Use manufacturer-approved cleaning solution and lint-free cloth.
  • Remove any residue, fingerprints, or liquids that may interfere with capacitive touch.

Why: Contamination can cause erratic or non-responsive behavior.

Check for Panel Initialization or Boot Issues

  • Power cycle the surgical light (turn off, wait 30 seconds, turn on).
  • Observe if the panel completes boot-up correctly and responds to input.

Why: Digital panels may fail temporarily due to software hang or startup glitch.

Inspect Cable Connections to Control Module

  • If accessible, ensure ribbon cables or data connectors between the panel and light controller are fully seated.
  • Look for visible pin damage or corrosion.

Why: Poor connections can result in partial or total panel failure.

Test Basic Functionality with Backup Controls

  • If the light has a secondary control method (wall panel, footswitch, or manual knob), verify the light responds through that interface.

Why: Confirms whether the issue is isolated to the touchscreen/panel.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have been completed.

The control panel or internal electronics are likely faulty.

Action:

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label “Out of Service.”
  • Send for manufacturer repair or internal bench evaluation.

Note: Knowing when to stop prevents further damage and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot the touchscreen while the device is in use over an active patient.

If urgent use is required, switch the patient to an alternate light or operating room until the issue is resolved.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
OR staff reported that the Stellar touchscreen does not respond or shows error messages when adjusting light intensity and position.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Upon inspection, the touchscreen was unresponsive after multiple power cycles; no physical damage was evident, and secondary controls worked intermittently.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled “Out of Service,” and scheduled for manufacturer repair of the digital control panel.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes/No
  • Power cycle attempted: Yes/No
  • Touchscreen cleaning attempted: Yes/No
  • Secondary controls functional: Yes/No
  • Error codes displayed: List codes
  • Device temperature or unusual sounds: Note if observed
  • Final status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures issues are identified without risking further damage or patient exposure. Proper documentation of complaints, causes, and resolutions preserves departmental knowledge and supports timely repair. Always escalate to manufacturer support when internal electronics are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
