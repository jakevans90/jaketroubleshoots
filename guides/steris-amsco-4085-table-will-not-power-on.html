STERIS AMSCO 4085

Table Will Not Power On

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 4085

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the STERIS AMSCO 4085 surgical table will not power on. Common symptoms include:

  • No indicator lights when attempting to turn on
  • Table does not respond to foot or hand controls
  • System completely unresponsive

Focus is on external checks and patient-safe troubleshooting.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify power source

  • Confirm the table is plugged into a known working outlet.
  • Use a voltage tester or another device in the same outlet to ensure power is present.
  • Why: A dead outlet is the most common cause of “no power” issues.

Check the main power switch

  • Ensure the table’s main power switch is in the ON position.
  • Some tables require the pendant to be docked or the foot switch activated first.

Inspect power cord and connections

  • Look for visible damage, kinks, or loose connections on the power cord.
  • Ensure connectors are fully seated into both the table and wall outlet.

Examine circuit breakers or fuses

  • Check any external wall breakers or table-mounted fuses.
  • Replace blown fuses with the correct rating if safe to do so.

Check battery (if applicable)

  • Some AMSCO 4085 tables have a backup battery for movement.
  • Ensure the battery is installed and charged; a completely dead battery may prevent power-on.

Attempt power-on from alternate controls

  • If the table has both a foot and hand control, try both.
  • A failed pendant can sometimes prevent the system from powering on.

If the Problem Persists

All common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (main power supply, control board, or internal wiring).

  • Remove the table from service.
  • Label it “Out of Service.”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop avoids patient safety risks and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt troubleshooting while a patient is on the table.
  • Move the patient to a different table before starting any checks.
  • Always ensure the table is stable and isolated from patient contact before testing power.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Table will not power on; no lights or response from controls.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Outlet confirmed live, power cord and switch verified; problem persisted. Likely internal power supply failure.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Table removed from service, labeled “Out of Service,” sent for repair/bench evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and confirmed live
  • Power cord inspected, no visible damage
  • Fuse/circuit breaker status
  • Battery presence and charge
  • Attempted power-on via foot and hand controls
  • Table status after troubleshooting

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting prioritizes patient safety and systematic logic. Start with simple, external checks before escalating. Document clearly using CCR to ensure repair workflow is efficient and safe.

That is successful troubleshooting.
