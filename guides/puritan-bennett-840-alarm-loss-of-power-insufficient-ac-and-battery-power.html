Puritan Bennett 840

Alarm: LOSS OF POWER insufficient AC and battery power

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Puritan Bennett

Model

840

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the LOSS OF POWER alarm indicating insufficient AC and battery power, resulting in ventilator shutdown or inability to operate, placing the patient at risk of loss of ventilatory support.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the ventilator from patient use if ventilation is interrupted
  • Transfer the patient to a backup ventilator or provide manual ventilation (BVM)
  • Confirm adequate ventilation is maintained

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the ventilator and confirm LOSS OF POWER alarm
  • Observe for no power, rapid shutdown, or no system response
  • Determine when the issue occurs (startup, during operation, or after unplugging AC)

Check AC Power Source (Most Common Cause)

  • Plug ventilator into a known-good hospital-grade outlet
  • Test outlet using another device
  • Avoid power strips or extension cords

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Ensure power cord is fully seated at both ends
  • Inspect cord for damage such as cuts or frays
  • Replace with a known-good cord if needed

Verify Battery Condition

  • Disconnect AC briefly and observe if unit stays powered
  • Check for immediate shutdown or minimal runtime
  • Confirm battery provides temporary backup power

Perform Power Reset

  • Turn the ventilator OFF
  • Ensure AC power is properly connected
  • Turn the ventilator back ON

Isolate the Cause

  • Outlet issue → facility power problem
  • Power cord issue → accessory failure
  • Battery failure → no backup during AC loss
  • All external sources verified → internal power supply failure

If the Problem Persists

If the LOSS OF POWER alarm continues after verifying external power sources, cord integrity, and battery function, internal failure is likely.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a ventilator power issue while connected to a patient. Always transition the patient to a backup ventilation method first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator displaying LOSS OF POWER alarm and not powering on during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery failed to support unit and internal power system suspected after verifying AC source and cord were functional."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified outlet and replaced power cord with no change. Removed unit from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Total power loss alarms must be treated as critical failures. Always verify external power sources first, but escalate quickly when both AC and battery backup fail.

That is successful troubleshooting.
