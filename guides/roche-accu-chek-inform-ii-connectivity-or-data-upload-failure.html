Roche Accu-Chek Inform II

Connectivity or Data Upload Failure

Asset Type

Blood Glucose Meter

Manufacturer

Roche

Model

Accu-Chek Inform II

What This Guide Helps With

Failure to upload patient results, docking station communication issues, or wireless connectivity problems preventing data transfer to the hospital system.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not delay patient care while troubleshooting connectivity. Use another validated meter if results cannot be uploaded.
  • Expected: Patient testing continues safely
  • Why it matters: Data delays should not impact clinical decision-making

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Confirm symptoms with staff:
    • Results not uploading
    • Dock not syncing
    • Wireless icon missing or error displayed
  • Expected: Clear understanding of failure mode
  • Why it matters: Helps narrow whether issue is network, dock, or device-related

Check Docking Station Connection (Most Common Issue)

  • Ensure meter is fully seated in dock
  • Verify dock has power (LED indicator on)
  • Check Ethernet cable is securely connected
  • Expected: Dock shows active power/network status
  • Why it matters: Most upload failures are due to dock or network disconnection

Test Known-Good Dock

  • Place the meter in a different working dock
  • Attempt upload
  • Expected: Successful upload on alternate dock
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether issue is dock vs meter
  • If resolved: Remove faulty dock from service

Check Network Availability

  • Verify network port is active (link light on switch if accessible)
  • Confirm other devices on same network are functioning
  • Check for known network outages
  • Expected: Active network connectivity
  • Why it matters: Meter relies on hospital network to communicate with middleware/LIS

Inspect Meter Contacts and Dock Pins

  • Check for debris, bent pins, or contamination on contacts
  • Clean contacts if needed per facility protocol
  • Expected: Clean, undamaged contacts
  • Why it matters: Poor contact prevents proper docking communication

Reboot the Meter

  • Power the meter off and back on
  • Re-dock and attempt upload
  • Expected: Temporary software issues cleared
  • Why it matters: Resets communication processes

Verify User Login and Device Status

  • Ensure operator is properly logged in
  • Check for QC lockout or device restrictions
  • Expected: Meter is in normal operating mode
  • Why it matters: Certain lockouts can block data transmission

Check Wireless Function (If Applicable)

  • Confirm wireless is enabled on device
  • Look for signal indicator
  • Move to known coverage area
  • Expected: Strong signal and connection
  • Why it matters: Weak or disabled wireless prevents uploads

Attempt Manual Sync or Data Transfer

  • Trigger manual upload if available
  • Observe for error messages
  • Expected: Data transmits without error
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether issue is persistent or intermittent

If the Problem Persists

If docking stations, network, contacts, and settings have been verified, the issue is likely internal (wireless module, communication board, or firmware fault).

Remove the device from service. Label it Out of Service and send for repair or further bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never delay patient testing due to upload issues. Perform testing on another meter and ensure results are documented per downtime procedures.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Accu-Chek Inform II not uploading patient results when docked."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Faulty docking station with no network connectivity."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced dock and verified successful data upload."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Dock power LED status
  • Ethernet link light status
  • Alternate dock test results
  • Wireless signal strength
  • Error messages displayed
  • Contact condition (clean/damaged)
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Connectivity issues are often external—dock, network, or contact-related—not internal failures. A structured approach prevents unnecessary repairs and keeps devices available. Always prioritize patient care, verify simple causes first, and document clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
