Puritan Bennett 840

Alarm: SCREEN BLOCK Blocked beam or touchscreen fault

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Puritan Bennett

Model

840

What This Guide Helps With

SCREEN BLOCK alarm causing loss of touchscreen control due to obstruction or interface failure, limiting ventilator operation and user interaction.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the patient is being ventilated properly
  • Prepare manual ventilation (BVM) or backup ventilator if control is limited

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm SCREEN BLOCK alarm is displayed
  • Check if touchscreen is unresponsive or partially responsive
  • Determine if issue is continuous or intermittent

Check for Physical Obstruction

  • Remove any objects touching the screen (tubing, drapes, gloves)
  • Ensure nothing is resting against the display

Clean the Touchscreen Surface

  • Wipe screen using approved cleaning solution
  • Dry thoroughly
  • Ensure no residue or moisture remains

Check for Environmental or Setup Issues

  • Inspect for condensation or excessive moisture
  • Ensure cables or accessories are not pressing on the display
  • Check for glare or unusual environmental conditions

Perform a System Reset

  • Power down the ventilator
  • Disconnect AC power for 30–60 seconds
  • Restart the unit

Evaluate Touchscreen Functionality

  • Test responsiveness across entire screen
  • Check for dead zones or constant input areas
  • Navigate menus to confirm proper operation

If the Problem Persists

If the SCREEN BLOCK alarm continues after all external causes are ruled out, this indicates a likely touchscreen or internal interface failure.

  • Remove the ventilator from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a touchscreen issue on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup ventilator before addressing interface problems.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying SCREEN BLOCK alarm with unresponsive touchscreen during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"No obstruction found; touchscreen remained unresponsive after cleaning and power cycle."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed ventilator from service and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Interface alarms like SCREEN BLOCK may appear minor but represent a critical loss of control. Always rule out simple external causes first, then escalate appropriately to ensure safe operation and proper documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
