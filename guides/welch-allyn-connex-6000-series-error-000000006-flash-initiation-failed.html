Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000006 FLASH Initiation Failed

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000006 indicating flash memory initialization failure causing boot failure, startup freezing, or inability to load system software.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use.
  • Expected: Patient is transitioned to another monitoring device.
  • Why it matters: A device that cannot complete startup cannot provide reliable monitoring.

2. Verify the Reported Error/Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the device and observe the startup sequence. Confirm Error #000000006 appears.
  • Note if the unit fails to boot, freezes, or enters a restart loop.
  • Expected: Error is reproducible during startup.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true flash initialization failure, not a transient glitch or user-related issue.

3. Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Disconnect AC power and remove the battery (if accessible).
  • Wait 60 seconds, then reconnect power and restart.
  • Expected: Device attempts a normal boot sequence.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary faults that may interrupt flash initialization.
  • If device boots normally → Return to service
  • If error persists → Continue

4. Check Power Source Stability

  • Plug the unit into a known good, grounded outlet.
  • Avoid power strips or shared circuits.
  • Expected: Stable power during startup.
  • Why it matters: Voltage instability can interfere with initialization of onboard memory.
  • If power issue found → Correct and retest
  • If power is stable → Continue

5. Inspect Battery and Power System

  • Check for:
  • Swollen or damaged battery
  • Loose or intermittent battery connection
  • Attempt startup with and without the battery installed.
  • Expected: No change in error behavior.
  • Why it matters: Power irregularities during boot can mimic or trigger memory-related faults.
  • If behavior changes → Replace battery
  • If no change → Continue

6. Remove External Accessories

  • Disconnect all peripherals:
  • NIBP hose
  • SpO₂ probe
  • Temperature probes
  • USB devices
  • Network/Ethernet cables
  • Restart the unit.
  • Expected: Error persists regardless of accessories.
  • Why it matters: Confirms the issue is internal and not caused by external modules or communication faults.
  • If error clears → Reconnect accessories one at a time to isolate cause
  • If error remains → Continue

7. Repeat Startup Attempt

  • Perform 2–3 additional restart cycles.
  • Expected: Error consistently reappears.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a persistent failure rather than a one-time boot interruption.

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and Error #000000006 continues:

This strongly indicates internal flash memory or mainboard failure.

The device cannot properly initialize or access its system storage.

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Important: This is not a field-correctable issue. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot or attempt repeated startups on an active patient. Always transfer monitoring before addressing startup or memory-related errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displays Error #000000006 (FLASH initiation failed) during startup and fails to boot."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Performed power cycle, verified power source, checked battery, and removed all accessories. Error persisted, indicating likely internal flash memory or mainboard failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent to vendor/biomed repair for further evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm or error behavior during startup
  • Accessories removed or tested
  • Power source verification results
  • Battery condition and testing results
  • Environmental or power stability factors
  • Indicator lights or startup sequence behavior
  • Final device status (removed from service, sent for repair)

Final Thought

Flash initialization errors directly impact the device’s ability to load its operating system. Once basic power and external causes are ruled out, prompt escalation protects both patient safety and equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
