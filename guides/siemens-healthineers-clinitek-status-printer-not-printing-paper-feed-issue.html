Siemens Healthineers CLINITEK Status+

Printer Not Printing / Paper Feed Issue

Asset Type

Urine Analyzer

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

CLINITEK Status+

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting no printout, blank printout, paper feed problems, paper loading errors, or failed result printing on the CLINITEK Status+.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the analyzer is not actively being used for patient testing.

If results are needed immediately, have staff record available displayed results per facility policy or use another analyzer.

Expected outcome: Troubleshooting is performed without interrupting active patient testing.

Verify the Reported Printer Problem

Ask staff what is happening:

  • No paper movement
  • Paper feeds but does not print
  • Paper jams
  • Printout is faint or blank
  • Analyzer reports no paper
  • Results only print when manually selected

Siemens documentation notes that results may print automatically only if that option is configured; otherwise the operator may need to select Print.

Expected outcome: The issue is confirmed as a printer, paper, configuration, or operator workflow problem.

Check Analyzer Power and Startup Status

Verify the CLINITEK Status+ powers on normally and reaches the ready screen.

If the analyzer is unstable, restarting, dim, or not completing startup, troubleshoot power first before focusing on the printer.

Expected outcome: The analyzer is operating normally aside from the printer complaint.

Inspect Printer Paper Supply

Open the printer cover and confirm paper is present.

Replace the roll if empty, nearly empty, crushed, wet, discolored, or incorrectly sized.

The CLINITEK Status documentation identifies a “No printer paper” condition as requiring printer paper replacement, and manufacturer instructions describe loading the roll through the printer paper compartment.

Expected outcome: A proper paper roll is installed.

If this resolves the issue, stop troubleshooting.

Confirm Paper Is Loaded in the Correct Direction

Verify the paper feeds from the correct side of the roll and routes cleanly through the printer path.

Thermal paper loaded backward may feed normally but produce a blank printout.

Expected outcome: Paper advances in the correct direction and printed text appears when a test page or result is printed.

If this resolves the issue, stop troubleshooting.

Check That the Printer Cover Is Fully Closed

Close the printer cover firmly and verify it sits flush.

Look for paper trapped under the cover, a damaged cover hinge, or a latch that does not fully seat.

Expected outcome: The printer cover is closed without obstruction and the analyzer no longer reports a paper or printer condition.

Remove Obvious Paper Jams or Debris

With the analyzer off, inspect the paper path for torn paper, label backing, adhesive residue, dust, or small fragments.

Remove only accessible debris. Do not force tools into the printer mechanism.

Expected outcome: The paper path is clear and the paper feeds smoothly after restart.

Check for Incorrect Paper or Label Roll Use

Confirm the installed media matches what the site intends to use.

If staff recently switched between standard thermal paper and labels, verify the correct roll type is installed and not binding in the paper compartment.

Expected outcome: The roll rotates freely and does not drag, bind, or skew during feeding.

Attempt a Manual Print

From a completed or recalled result, select the available print option and observe printer behavior.

Watch for:

  • Motor noise with no paper movement
  • Paper feed without printing
  • Partial printout
  • Repeated paper error
  • No response from printer

Expected outcome: The analyzer either prints successfully or the failure mode is clearly identified.

Review Print Configuration

Confirm whether automatic printing is enabled if the complaint is “results are not printing automatically.”

A manual print may still work even when automatic printing is disabled.

Expected outcome: The reported issue is not caused by a print setting or workflow expectation.

If enabling or correcting the setting resolves the issue, document the configuration change and stop.

Power Cycle the Analyzer

Turn the analyzer off, wait briefly, and power it back on.

Confirm it returns to the ready screen, then try printing again.

Expected outcome: A temporary printer or software state clears and printing resumes.

If this resolves the issue, stop troubleshooting.

Check for Signs of Mechanical Printer Failure

Listen for abnormal clicking, grinding, no motor activity, or repeated feed attempts.

Inspect for unusual heat, odor, cracked plastic, loose printer cover components, or damaged paper compartment parts.

Expected outcome: Any evidence of printer mechanism failure is identified before returning the analyzer to service.

Verify Result Handling Before Removing From Service

If the analyzer can still test but cannot print, confirm whether results are visible on screen, stored, or transferred by the facility’s approved workflow.

Do not leave the analyzer in service if the clinical area requires printed reports for documentation or patient care.

Expected outcome: The final device status matches the site’s result documentation requirements.

If the Problem Persists

If correct paper is installed, the cover is closed, the paper path is clear, settings are correct, and the analyzer still will not print or feed paper, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal to the printer mechanism, printer sensor, feed motor, printer board, cabling, or related control circuitry.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not disassemble the printer assembly at the point of care or return the analyzer to service if results cannot be reliably documented.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot printer feeding or paper jams while the analyzer is being used for active patient testing. Move testing to another analyzer first if printed or documented results are required for patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the CLINITEK Status+ would not print patient urinalysis results and paper was not advancing from the printer."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the printer paper roll loaded incorrectly, causing the thermal side to face the wrong direction and producing blank feed output."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reloaded the correct thermal paper roll, verified the printer cover latched fully, performed a manual result print, and returned the analyzer to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Analyzer powered on normally
  • Paper roll present or empty
  • Paper type verified
  • Paper loaded in correct direction
  • Printer cover latched
  • Manual print attempted
  • Automatic print setting checked
  • Paper feed behavior observed
  • Blank, faint, partial, or no printout noted
  • Any paper jam or debris removed
  • Any abnormal sound, odor, heat, or damaged cover noted
  • Final device status documented

Final Thought

Printer problems on the CLINITEK Status+ should be handled logically: protect patient testing first, verify paper and settings, clear simple feed issues, and escalate when the printer no longer behaves mechanically or electronically as expected. Clear CCR documentation helps show whether the issue was user-correctable, configuration-related, or a true repair need.

That is successful troubleshooting.
