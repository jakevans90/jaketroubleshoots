Karl Storz C-MAC HD Monitor

No Display / Monitor Does Not Power On

Asset Type

Video Laryngoscope

Manufacturer

Karl Storz

Model

C-MAC HD Monitor

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a C-MAC HD Monitor with a blank screen or no power caused by battery, external power, connection, or physical damage.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the C-MAC HD Monitor from clinical use if it does not power on or the display cannot be confirmed functional.

If it is needed for an active or anticipated airway procedure, provide clinical staff with a known-working video laryngoscope or appropriate backup airway visualization device before troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: The patient is not dependent on unavailable or unverified airway visualization equipment.

Why it matters: A monitor that does not power on cannot provide dependable visualization during airway management.

2. Confirm the Reported Condition

With the device away from patient care, press the monitor power control normally and observe for:

  • Display illumination or startup screen
  • Battery or charging indication
  • Indicator light activity
  • Audible startup response
  • Intermittent power when the unit is repositioned

Document whether the monitor is completely unresponsive or whether it briefly starts and then shuts down.

Expected outcome: The failure is verified as a true no-power or no-display condition rather than an image-source problem.

If the monitor powers on but displays no laryngoscope image, stop this workflow and evaluate the connected video laryngoscope, cable, and video input path instead.

3. Inspect the Monitor for External Damage or Contamination

Examine the monitor housing, screen, power control area, charging/power connection, and battery area for:

  • Cracks, impact damage, or fluid intrusion evidence
  • Bent, contaminated, recessed, or loose connector contacts
  • Damaged power button or controls
  • Swelling, heat, odor, or damage involving the battery area

Do not attempt to energize a device with suspected fluid intrusion, battery swelling, unusual heat, or burning odor.

Expected outcome: The monitor is either physically safe to continue evaluating or immediately removed from service due to visible damage.

4. Check Battery Charge Status and External Power Operation

The C-MAC HD Monitor uses a rechargeable lithium-ion battery and has a 12 V input specification. Verify whether the unit powers on from its approved external power supply or charging setup.

Actions:

  • Connect the approved Karl Storz power supply to the monitor.
  • Connect the supply to a known-working receptacle.
  • Observe for any charging, battery, or power indication.
  • Allow sufficient charging time per department practice or approved manufacturer guidance before retesting power-on.

Expected outcome:

  • If the monitor powers on with external power, suspect a depleted, failed, or poorly charging battery condition.
  • If the monitor remains completely unresponsive, continue troubleshooting.

If restoring approved external power resolves the problem and the battery subsequently charges and supports normal operation, document the resolution and stop.

5. Verify the Wall Outlet and External Power Supply Path

Check the simplest external power causes before suspecting monitor failure.

Actions:

  • Test the receptacle with approved equipment or a receptacle tester according to facility practice.
  • Inspect the power supply cord and output cable for cuts, strain damage, bent pins, loose connectors, or evidence of overheating.
  • Reseat all accessible power connections firmly.
  • When available, substitute a known-good compatible C-MAC monitor power supply approved for use with this equipment.

Expected outcome: The monitor receives confirmed external power through an intact power path.

If a known-good approved power supply restores operation, remove the defective power supply from service, verify normal monitor function, document the repair, and stop.

6. Isolate Accessories and Connected Components

Disconnect nonessential attached items, such as connected video laryngoscopes, external display cables, USB devices, or SD media, as applicable to the configuration.

Attempt to power on the monitor using only its approved power source.

Expected outcome: The monitor starts without an attached accessory contributing to the failure.

If the monitor powers on only after an accessory is removed, inspect and isolate the suspect accessory or cable before returning the system to service.

7. Evaluate for Intermittent Power Behavior

While the monitor is safely positioned on the bench and not in clinical use, observe whether power indication changes when:

  • The external power connector is gently checked for secure seating
  • The approved power supply cable is placed in a neutral, unstressed position
  • The monitor is allowed to remain powered on without movement

Do not flex connectors excessively or attempt to reproduce a fault in a way that could further damage the unit.

Expected outcome: Power remains stable without flickering, unexpected shutdown, or loss of charging indication.

If power is intermittent at the monitor connector, battery area, or control surface, remove the monitor from service for repair evaluation.

8. Perform a Safe Functional Verification if Power Is Restored

If the monitor powers on after external checks:

  • Confirm the display illuminates normally and remains stable.
  • Confirm the battery or external-power indication behaves normally.
  • Connect a known-good compatible video laryngoscope, when available.
  • Verify that a stable image is displayed.
  • Confirm the unit remains powered during a brief unoccupied functional check.

Expected outcome: The monitor powers on reliably and displays a stable video image without interruption.

If all checks pass, the monitor may be returned to service according to department policy.

If the Problem Persists

If the monitor remains blank or does not power on after verifying the outlet, approved external power supply, battery charging attempt, accessory isolation, and visible condition, common external causes have been ruled out. The fault is likely within the monitor battery system, power input circuitry, display assembly, power control, or another internal component.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation according to departmental and Karl Storz service procedures

Knowing when to stop after safe external verification is proper troubleshooting. Do not proceed into internal disassembly or unauthorized battery or circuit-level repair.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a nonfunctional C-MAC HD Monitor during active airway management. Move clinical staff to a known-working visualization device or approved backup method first, then evaluate the failed monitor only when patient safety is protected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Karl Storz C-MAC HD Monitor screen remained blank and the unit would not power on when needed for airway equipment setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Monitor remained unresponsive with a verified working outlet and known-good approved external power supply, with no recovery after accessory isolation and charging attempt."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the C-MAC HD Monitor from service, labeled it Out of Service, provided a replacement monitor for clinical use, and routed the failed unit for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result
  • Approved power supply inspected or swapped
  • Charging or battery indicator behavior
  • Screen, control, and connector condition
  • Any fluid intrusion, heat, swelling, or unusual odor
  • Connected accessories removed during testing
  • Whether the monitor powered on under external power
  • Whether a known-good laryngoscope produced an image after recovery
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed for repair

Final Thought

A C-MAC monitor that does not power on is an airway visualization reliability concern. Safe troubleshooting begins with patient protection, proceeds through logical external checks, and ends with appropriate escalation when dependable operation cannot be confirmed. Clear CCR documentation preserves the findings and supports safe replacement or repair decisions.

That is successful troubleshooting.
