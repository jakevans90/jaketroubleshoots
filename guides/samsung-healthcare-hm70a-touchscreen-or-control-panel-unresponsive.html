Samsung Healthcare HM70A

Touchscreen or Control Panel Unresponsive

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

Samsung Healthcare

Model

HM70A

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting an unresponsive touchscreen or control panel, including power, lock settings, peripheral issues, and external causes affecting input response.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the system from active patient use before troubleshooting.
  • Move the patient to another ultrasound system if needed.

Why it matters: Prevents delays in care and avoids relying on a system that cannot be controlled safely.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the system and confirm the touchscreen or control panel is unresponsive.
  • Check if the entire screen is unresponsive or only certain areas.
  • Check if buttons, trackball, or keyboard are also affected.

Expected: Clear understanding of whether the issue is full or partial input failure.

Why it matters: Helps determine if this is a system-wide issue or localized hardware problem.

Check for Screen Lock or Freeze Condition

  • Verify the system is not in a locked or frozen state.
  • Look for lock icons or inactive UI indicators.
  • Attempt to access any physical “freeze” or unlock controls.

Expected: System responds or unlocks.

Why it matters: Some systems disable input when frozen or locked.

Perform a System Reboot

  • Power down the system completely.
  • Wait 15–30 seconds, then restart.

Expected: Touchscreen and controls respond normally after reboot.

Why it matters: Clears temporary software or UI freezes.

Inspect for External Obstructions or Contamination

  • Check the touchscreen and control panel for gel residue, dirt or debris, and moisture or fluid intrusion.
  • Clean using approved disinfectant wipes.

Expected: Improved or restored responsiveness.

Why it matters: Contamination can interfere with capacitive touch response or button function.

Check Connected Accessories and Peripherals

  • Disconnect any external devices such as USB devices, external keyboards or mice, and network cables if recently connected and suspected.

Expected: System regains responsiveness after removing interfering device.

Why it matters: Faulty peripherals can interfere with system input processing.

Test Alternate Input Methods

  • Attempt to use trackball, physical buttons, or keyboard.

Expected: Some input methods may still function.

Why it matters: Helps isolate touchscreen failure vs full control board issue.

Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Check for cracked screen, loose or damaged control panel, and signs of impact or wear.

Expected: No visible damage.

Why it matters: Physical damage often indicates hardware failure requiring repair.

Check for System Performance Issues

  • Observe if the system is extremely slow, freezing intermittently, or lagging before becoming unresponsive.

Expected: Normal system speed.

Why it matters: Performance issues may indicate software or OS instability.

If the Problem Persists

If the touchscreen and control panel remain unresponsive after these steps, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal (touchscreen digitizer, control board, or system electronics).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and ensures proper resolution.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot input issues while scanning a patient. Always transition to a working system first to avoid delays in diagnosis.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Touchscreen and control panel not responding during ultrasound exam."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Touchscreen remained unresponsive after reboot and cleaning; likely internal digitizer or control interface failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service, labeled out of service, and sent for vendor repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • System powered on normally
  • Reboot performed with no change
  • Screen cleaned and inspected
  • Accessories disconnected
  • Alternate inputs tested
  • No visible external damage
  • Final device status: Removed from service

Final Thought

Input failures on ultrasound systems directly impact usability and patient care. Always start with simple external checks, but recognize when the issue points to internal failure. Safe escalation and clear documentation are key to effective Clinical Engineering support.

That is successful troubleshooting.
