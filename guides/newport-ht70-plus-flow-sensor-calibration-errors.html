Newport HT70 Plus

Flow Sensor Calibration Errors

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Newport

Model

HT70 Plus

What This Guide Helps With

Flow sensor calibration failures, inaccurate readings, or alarms caused by external factors such as contamination, setup issues, or environmental conditions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the ventilator from active patient use if calibration is required
  • Transition the patient to a backup ventilator

Expected: Patient ventilation continues safely
Why it matters: Calibration errors can lead to inaccurate tidal volume delivery

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to run the flow sensor calibration per normal startup or service menu process
  • Observe for failure to complete or error messages
  • Check for inconsistent or drifting readings

Expected: Error is reproducible
Why it matters: Confirms a true calibration issue vs user workflow error

Check Circuit and Patient Setup (Most Common Cause)

  • Disconnect and inspect the entire breathing circuit
  • Remove patient circuit from ventilator
  • Ensure no tubing is kinked or occluded
  • Remove inline accessories (filters, HMEs, water traps)

Expected: Open, unobstructed circuit
Why it matters: Calibration requires an open system with no resistance

Inspect Flow Sensor and Connections

  • Locate the flow sensor and inspect for proper seating and connection
  • Check for visible damage or cracks
  • Check for moisture or condensation
  • Reseat the sensor securely

Expected: Sensor is clean, dry, and properly connected
Why it matters: Poor connection or contamination directly affects calibration

Check for Moisture or Contamination (Very Common)

  • Inspect for condensation inside tubing or sensor
  • Check for secretions or debris
  • Replace disposable components if contaminated
  • Allow reusable sensor to dry if needed

Expected: Dry, clean airflow path
Why it matters: Moisture alters airflow measurement and causes calibration failure

Ensure Proper Calibration Conditions

  • Ensure ventilator is not connected to a patient
  • Ensure circuit is disconnected or properly configured for calibration
  • Place ventilator in a stable environment without airflow drafts

Expected: Calibration runs without interference
Why it matters: External airflow or resistance will cause failure

Power Cycle the Ventilator

  • Turn the unit off
  • Wait 30 seconds
  • Power back on and retry calibration

Expected: Calibration completes successfully
Why it matters: Clears temporary software or sensor communication issues

Verify Use of Correct Accessories

  • Ensure only compatible circuits and sensors are used for the HT70 Plus

Expected: Compatible components installed
Why it matters: Non-approved accessories can cause inaccurate flow readings

Attempt Calibration with Known Good Setup

  • Swap in a known good flow sensor if available
  • Use a known good circuit

Expected: Calibration passes with known good components
Why it matters: Helps isolate whether issue is accessory vs device

If the Problem Persists

If calibration continues to fail after verifying circuit, sensor condition, and setup, the issue is likely internal (flow sensor module, internal pneumatics, or control board).

Remove the ventilator from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt flow calibration while connected to a patient. Always transition the patient to another ventilator before troubleshooting to ensure therapy continuity.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator displaying flow sensor calibration error and failing calibration."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Moisture found in flow sensor and circuit causing inaccurate readings."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced circuit and dried sensor; calibration completed successfully."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Calibration error message displayed
  • Circuit condition (wet/dry, obstructed)
  • Sensor condition and connection
  • Accessories removed during testing
  • Calibration success or failure after steps
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Flow calibration issues are most often caused by external factors like moisture, circuit resistance, or setup errors. Always rule these out before assuming internal failure. Patient safety and proper escalation are critical, and clear documentation ensures accurate follow-up and trend tracking.

That is successful troubleshooting.
