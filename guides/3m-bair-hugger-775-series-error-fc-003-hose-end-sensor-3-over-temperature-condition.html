3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-003 Hose end sensor 3 over-temperature condition

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-003 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. FC-003 indicates a hose end sensor 3 over-temperature condition, which may be caused by high heat, moisture, hose restriction, elbow sensor problems, hose end sensor failure, or intermittent sensor behavior.

This fault can interrupt patient warming and should be treated as a temperature sensing and patient safety-related issue until the unit is verified safe for use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Bair Hugger from patient use if FC-003 appears during clinical use.
  • Do not continue warming a patient with a device reporting an unresolved over-temperature or hose end sensor fault.
  • Transfer warming support to another approved warming device if patient warming is still clinically needed.
  • Allow clinical staff to assess whether patient temperature management was interrupted.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues safely without relying on a warming unit reporting an over-temperature condition.

Why it matters: FC-003 is related to hose end temperature sensing. Continued patient use with an unresolved over-temperature or sensor fault may create a safety risk.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the displayed error is FC-003.
  • Ask staff when the fault occurred.
  • Determine whether it happened during startup, active warming, extended use, hose repositioning, or intermittently during patient warming.
  • Observe whether the fault clears after power removal and cooldown or returns immediately.

Expected outcome: FC-003 is confirmed and the timing of the fault is understood.

Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported hose end temperature fault instead of a general warming complaint or user workflow issue.

3. Translate the Error Into the Real-World Problem

  • Recognize that FC-003 indicates a hose end sensor 3 over-temperature condition.
  • Treat the fault as a possible high-temperature condition or a problem with hose end temperature sensing.
  • Consider high heat, moisture, faulty hose end sensor, faulty elbow sensor, intermittent sensor failure, or hose and airflow-related causes.

Expected outcome: The fault is treated as a temperature sensing or over-temperature safety issue.

Why it matters: This is not just a nuisance alarm. The device is reporting a condition tied to temperature monitoring at the patient end of the hose.

4. Disconnect Power and Allow the Unit to Cool

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Disconnect the unit from AC power.
  • Allow the unit to cool.
  • Wait at least 30–60 seconds before reconnecting power.

Expected outcome: Temporary over-temperature or transient sensor conditions may clear after cooldown and power reset.

Why it matters: FC-003 may occur from a temporary high-heat or intermittent hose end sensor condition. Cooling and power removal is the first safe reset step.

5. Inspect the Hose and Hose End

  • Inspect the warming hose for kinks, crushing, obstruction, damage, loose connection, moisture, contamination, or incorrect positioning.
  • Confirm the hose end is not blocked.
  • Confirm airflow is not restricted.
  • Do not use a hose that appears wet, damaged, contaminated, or unreliable.

Expected outcome: Hose is dry, intact, properly connected, and free of restriction.

Why it matters: A restricted or damaged hose can cause abnormal heat buildup or trigger hose end sensor faults.

6. Inspect the Elbow Sensor Area

  • Check the elbow sensor area for visible damage.
  • Look for moisture, contamination, loose fit, or signs of unreliable connection.
  • Do not continue using a hose or elbow assembly that appears wet, damaged, or unreliable.

Expected outcome: Elbow sensor area appears clean, dry, secure, and undamaged.

Why it matters: FC-003 troubleshooting is commonly associated with hose end sensor failure, faulty elbow sensor, moisture, intermittent sensor failure, or high heat.

7. Reconnect Power and Retest

  • Reconnect the unit to AC power after the cooldown period.
  • Power the unit on.
  • Observe whether FC-003 returns.
  • Run the unit long enough to verify stable operation.

Expected outcome: The unit powers on without FC-003 returning.

Why it matters: If the fault clears and does not return, the issue may have been a temporary over-temperature or transient sensor condition.

8. Verify Normal Warming Operation

  • Confirm the unit operates normally in available temperature modes.
  • Listen for normal blower operation.
  • Confirm airflow is present.
  • Watch for recurring faults, abnormal heat, unusual smells, or abnormal sounds.

Expected outcome: Unit operates normally without FC-003 or related fault codes.

Why it matters: A cleared code alone is not enough. The unit should demonstrate stable operation before being returned to service.

9. Stop When Resolved

  • If FC-003 clears after cooldown and the unit operates normally, document the fault.
  • Document the cooldown and retest.
  • Confirm normal operation.
  • Return the unit to service according to department policy.

Resolution statement: FC-003 cleared after power removal/cooldown and did not return during functional check.

If the Problem Persists

If FC-003 remains, returns immediately, or comes back during testing, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent FC-003 fault may indicate a hose end sensor issue, elbow sensor issue, moisture-related sensor problem, intermittent sensor failure, or device-related temperature sensing problem.

  • Remove the unit from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Replace the hose or elbow sensor as indicated by service findings.
  • Perform required calibration and safety/functional checks before returning the unit to service.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not keep resetting and returning a patient warming device that continues to report an over-temperature-related fault.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot FC-003 on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, then evaluate the Bair Hugger in a safe non-patient-care setting. Never rely on a patient warming system with an unresolved over-temperature or hose end sensor fault.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 reported Error FC-003 during use. Staff reported warming unit faulted and could not be reliably used for patient warming."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed FC-003 hose end sensor 3 over-temperature condition. Inspected hose and elbow sensor area for obstruction, moisture, damage, and connection issues. Fault cleared after power removal/cooldown, or if recurring, hose end sensor or elbow sensor issue suspected."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from patient use. Disconnected power and allowed unit to cool. Inspected hose and elbow sensor area. Reconnected power after 30–60 seconds and verified operation. If FC-003 returned, unit was labeled Out of Service and sent for bench evaluation/repair. Calibration required before return to service if hose or elbow sensor repair is performed."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

FC-003 should be treated as a patient safety-related temperature sensing fault. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact error, check the hose and elbow sensor area, allow the unit to cool, and retest logically. If the fault persists or returns, remove the unit from service and escalate for repair. Clear CCR documentation helps show what was checked, what was found, and why the final decision was appropriate.

That is successful troubleshooting.
