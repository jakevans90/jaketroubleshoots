Siemens Healthineers Ysio Max

System Startup Failure or Acquisition Workstation Freeze

Asset Type

Radiographic System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

Ysio Max

What This Guide Helps With

Startup failure, frozen acquisition workstation, boot delays, display lockups, or system readiness issues on a Siemens Ysio Max radiographic system.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the Ysio Max during an active patient exam if the system is frozen, partially booted, or not responding.

Action:

  • Stop the exam workflow.
  • Move the patient away from the table or wall stand if positioning or exposure status is uncertain.
  • Use another radiographic room or portable X-ray system if imaging is clinically urgent.
  • Do not attempt exposures from a system that has not completed startup or workstation readiness.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues safely without relying on an unstable imaging system.

Why it matters: A frozen workstation or incomplete startup can prevent image review, exposure control, safety interlock confirmation, or proper exam documentation.

2. Verify the Reported Failure

Confirm whether the issue is with the entire radiographic system or only the acquisition workstation.

Action:

  • Check if the system powers on at all.
  • Note whether the generator, table, wall stand, tube stand, or workstation display responds.
  • Confirm whether the freeze occurs:
  • During initial boot
  • At user login
  • When opening the acquisition software
  • When selecting a patient
  • After exposure
  • During image processing or transfer
  • Record any displayed error messages or frozen screen text.

Expected outcome: The failure is narrowed to system power/startup, acquisition computer/software, or workflow-related lockup.

Why it matters: “System will not start” and “workstation frozen” can have different causes and different escalation paths.

3. Check Room Power and Emergency-Off Status

Verify that the room has proper power and that no emergency stop or room shutdown condition is active.

Action:

  • Confirm the room power indicator is normal, if present.
  • Check the main wall power switch or room control power switch.
  • Verify that any emergency-off button is not pressed.
  • Inspect nearby breaker panels only if they are accessible and permitted by facility policy.
  • Ask staff whether there was a recent power outage, shutdown, or room reset.

Expected outcome: The system has normal facility power and is not disabled by an external emergency-off condition.

If the system starts normally after restoring room power or releasing an emergency-off condition, document the finding and stop.

4. Inspect the Workstation Power and Display

Check whether the acquisition workstation itself is powered and displaying correctly.

Action:

  • Verify the workstation monitor is powered on.
  • Check for power indicator lights on the workstation computer.
  • Confirm the display cable is secure at the monitor and computer.
  • If there are multiple monitors, verify whether all displays are frozen or only one is blank.
  • Check the keyboard and mouse connections.

Expected outcome: The workstation has power, video output, and input devices connected.

Why it matters: A blank or frozen display may be mistaken for a full system failure when the issue is limited to the monitor, workstation, or input device.

5. Look for Simple Peripheral or Accessory Problems

Check for external devices that may interfere with startup or workstation operation.

Action:

  • Inspect USB devices, barcode scanners, keyboard, mouse, and any external media devices.
  • Remove nonessential USB drives or accessories if allowed by site policy.
  • Check for damaged cables, loose connectors, or recently added peripherals.
  • Confirm that no keyboard key or mouse button appears stuck.

Expected outcome: External peripherals are ruled out as a cause of boot delay, input lockup, or workstation freeze.

If removing a nonessential external device allows normal startup, document the accessory issue and stop.

6. Perform a Controlled System Restart

If the system is frozen and no patient is connected or actively being imaged, perform a controlled restart according to site policy.

Action:

  • Use the normal shutdown or restart process if the workstation responds.
  • If the workstation is fully locked, follow the facility-approved power cycle process.
  • Allow the system enough time to shut down and restart completely.
  • Do not repeatedly power cycle the system in rapid succession.
  • Observe the startup sequence and note where the system stops or freezes.

Expected outcome: The Ysio Max restarts, acquisition software loads, and the system reaches ready status.

If the restart clears the issue and the system passes normal readiness checks, return it to service after confirming proper operation.

7. Confirm Login and Network Access

If the workstation boots but freezes or fails during login or patient/worklist access, check user and network-related causes.

Action:

  • Confirm whether the issue occurs with one user account or all users.
  • Check for a network connection indicator if available.
  • Verify the Ethernet cable is connected and undamaged.
  • Ask staff whether PACS, RIS, worklist, or hospital network issues are affecting other imaging rooms.
  • Test whether local functions respond even if worklist access does not.

Expected outcome: The issue is separated into workstation freeze, login problem, or network/worklist dependency.

Why it matters: A worklist or network failure can make the workstation appear unusable even when the X-ray system itself is operational.

8. Check for Acquisition Software Freeze

If the operating system responds but the acquisition application is locked, focus on the software state.

Action:

  • Note whether the mouse and keyboard still respond.
  • Check whether only the acquisition software is frozen.
  • Look for pending dialog boxes, error popups, or windows hidden behind the main application.
  • Restart the acquisition application only if permitted by local procedure.
  • If the software reopens, verify patient selection, detector status, and image acquisition readiness.

Expected outcome: The acquisition software either recovers normally or the fault is confirmed as a recurring workstation/software issue.

If the application freezes again during the same workflow, remove the room from service and escalate.

9. Verify Detector and System Communication Status

If the workstation freezes during detector selection, image acquisition, or image processing, check external communication points.

Action:

  • Confirm the detector is powered or docked as expected.
  • Check detector battery status if applicable.
  • Verify detector docking contacts or connection points are clean and undamaged.
  • Confirm the selected detector matches the exam room setup.
  • Check whether the freeze happens only with one detector or all detectors.

Expected outcome: Detector-related communication problems are identified or ruled out.

Why it matters: Acquisition workstations may hang or fail to proceed if they cannot communicate properly with the selected detector or imaging chain.

10. Check for Environmental or Overheating Concerns

Inspect the workstation and equipment area for obvious environmental problems.

Action:

  • Check that workstation vents are not blocked.
  • Listen for unusual fan noise from the workstation or system cabinet.
  • Look for unusual heat, burning smell, or visible dust buildup around vents.
  • Confirm the room is not excessively hot.
  • Do not continue operating equipment that smells hot or appears overheated.

Expected outcome: Obvious overheating or ventilation concerns are identified before continued use.

If overheating, smoke, odor, or abnormal noise is present, remove the system from service immediately.

11. Test Basic System Readiness After Recovery

If the system starts successfully after troubleshooting, verify basic readiness before returning it to clinical use.

Action:

  • Confirm the acquisition workstation is responsive.
  • Confirm the system reaches normal ready status.
  • Verify detector recognition.
  • Confirm table, wall stand, and tube stand status indicators appear normal.
  • Confirm no startup errors remain.
  • Perform any required site-approved operational check before releasing the room.

Expected outcome: The Ysio Max is stable, responsive, and ready for clinical imaging.

If the system freezes again, does not complete startup, or produces recurring errors, do not return it to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the Ysio Max continues to fail startup, freezes during acquisition workstation boot, locks at login, or repeatedly becomes unresponsive after restart, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench/vendor evaluation

Likely internal or service-level causes may include workstation hardware failure, corrupted acquisition software, failed storage media, operating system issues, generator/system controller communication failure, detector communication faults, or network configuration problems requiring advanced support.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Repeated resets without identifying the cause can create downtime risk and may make the failure harder to diagnose.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a frozen radiographic system with a patient positioned for exposure. Move the patient to a safe location or another imaging room first. A system that has not fully completed startup should not be trusted for patient imaging, detector communication, exposure control, or image verification.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Siemens Ysio Max acquisition workstation froze during startup and the room could not be used for patient imaging."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified the workstation powered on but repeatedly locked before the acquisition software reached ready status; room power, emergency-off status, monitor connections, keyboard, mouse, and external cabling were checked with no external fault found."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the Ysio Max room from service, labeled the system Out of Service, documented the startup freeze behavior, and escalated for workstation/software evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the full system failed or only the acquisition workstation froze
  • Exact screen, message, or boot stage where the freeze occurred
  • Whether the issue happened before or after user login
  • Whether the acquisition software opened
  • Whether keyboard and mouse responded
  • Whether the monitor had power and signal
  • Room power and emergency-off status
  • Network/worklist behavior
  • Detector recognition status
  • Alarm behavior or displayed startup messages
  • Accessories or peripherals removed, swapped, or tested
  • Power behavior during startup, shutdown, or restart
  • Environmental factors such as heat, blocked vents, dust, odor, or fan noise
  • Indicator lights on the workstation, monitor, system cabinet, detector, or room power controls
  • Whether a controlled restart restored operation
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed from service

Final Thought

Startup and workstation freeze issues should be handled logically from the outside in. Confirm patient safety first, then check room power, emergency-off status, display connections, input devices, peripherals, network behavior, and detector communication before assuming an internal failure. If the Ysio Max continues to freeze or cannot reach ready status, remove it from service and escalate with clear CCR documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
