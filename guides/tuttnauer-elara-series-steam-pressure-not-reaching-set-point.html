Tuttnauer Elara Series

Steam Pressure Not Reaching Set Point

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

Tuttnauer

Model

Elara Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of insufficient steam pressure to ensure sterilization cycles complete fully, protecting instruments and patient safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Staff Safety

  • Confirm the sterilizer chamber is not pressurized and no personnel are near the door. Avoid burns or injury during inspection.

2. Verify Water Supply

  • Check that the water reservoir is filled to the recommended level. Low water prevents proper steam generation. Expected outcome: water level adequate.

3. Inspect Inlet Valve and Filters

  • Ensure the water inlet valve is open and filters are clear. Restricted flow can reduce steam pressure. Expected outcome: unobstructed water supply.

4. Confirm Correct Cycle Selection

  • Verify the selected cycle matches the load type and required pressure. Incorrect settings can prevent the system from reaching set point. Expected outcome: correct cycle selected.

5. Check Chamber Drain and Safety Valves

  • Inspect the drain line and safety valve for blockage or leaks. Blockages can lower chamber pressure. Expected outcome: drains and valves clear; no leaks detected.

6. Observe Steam Generation

  • Start a test cycle with an empty chamber. Monitor pressure gauge. Expected outcome: pressure rises toward set point steadily.

7. Inspect Door Seal and Gasket

  • Check the chamber door gasket for cracks, wear, or misalignment. Leaks at the door can prevent pressure build-up. Expected outcome: door seal intact and properly seated.

8. Verify Steam Line Connections

  • Check external steam lines and fittings for tightness and leaks. Expected outcome: no external steam leaks detected.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. Likely internal issues include heater, pressure sensor, or control board faults.

  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop avoids unsafe troubleshooting and prevents damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt pressure tests or open the chamber during an active cycle. Always move patient instruments to a verified backup sterilizer before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer did not reach steam pressure set point during standard cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Water supply, inlet valve, and door seal verified; pressure remained low."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Sterilizer removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for internal repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested for adequate power
  • Water level confirmed
  • Door gasket and chamber seals inspected
  • Safety and drain valves checked
  • Pressure gauge behavior noted
  • Sterilizer status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting prioritizes patient safety, external checks, and logical escalation. Document all findings and actions to support safe operations and cost-effective maintenance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
