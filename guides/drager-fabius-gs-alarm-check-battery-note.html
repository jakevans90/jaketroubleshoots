Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: CHECK BATTERY !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm CHECK BATTERY ! indicating 0% backup battery charge, resulting in no battery backup during power loss and risk of ventilation interruption.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from patient use if backup power is required or reliability is in question
  • Transition the patient to alternate anesthesia or ventilation support
  • Confirm patient ventilation is stable on backup equipment

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the unit and confirm CHECK BATTERY ! alarm is present
  • Observe if the alarm appears at startup or continuously
  • Check if battery indicator shows 0%
  • Disconnect AC power briefly (off patient) and observe if unit shuts down immediately

Check AC Power Source

  • Ensure the machine is connected to a known working outlet
  • Test outlet with another device
  • Verify AC power indicator on the machine is active

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Check for loose or damaged power cord
  • Ensure proper seating at the rear of the unit
  • Inspect for bent prongs or visible wear

Allow Battery Charging Time

  • Leave unit plugged into AC power for at least 30–60 minutes
  • Monitor battery percentage for increase
  • Check if alarm clears after charging period

Check for Immediate Power Loss on Unplug

  • While off patient, disconnect AC power
  • Observe if unit powers off immediately
  • Confirm whether battery provides any backup duration

Inspect External Indicators and Physical Condition

  • Look for battery warning indicators
  • Check for signs of overheating, swelling, or odor near battery compartment
  • Confirm no visible external damage

Check External Fuse (If Accessible)

  • Inspect user-accessible fuse in power entry module
  • Verify fuse is intact and properly seated
  • Do not open internal panels

If the Problem Persists

If the battery remains at 0% and the alarm continues after verifying external power and connections, external causes have been ruled out. This indicates a failed battery or internal charging circuit issue.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or battery replacement

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or rely on a device with a failed battery while connected to a patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before continuing evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying CHECK BATTERY ! alarm. Battery indicated 0% and no backup power available."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery failed to charge despite verified AC power; likely depleted battery or internal charging circuit failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for battery replacement. Verified immediate shutdown when unplugged."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery-related alarms directly impact patient safety and device reliability. Always verify power sources first, confirm charging behavior, and escalate promptly when backup capacity is lost.

That is successful troubleshooting.
