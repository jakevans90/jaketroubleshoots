Nova Biomedical StatStrip

QC Lockout or Control Failures

Asset Type

Blood Glucose Meter

Manufacturer

Nova Biomedical

Model

StatStrip

What This Guide Helps With

QC lockout conditions preventing testing due to failed, expired, or incomplete quality control checks on the meter.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not attempt patient testing while the meter is in QC lockout.
  • Obtain another functioning meter for patient care.

Expected: Patient testing continues on a different device
Why it matters: QC lockout indicates the meter cannot verify accuracy

Confirm QC Lockout Message

  • Power on the meter and verify the displayed message (e.g., QC required, QC failed, or lockout warning).

Expected: Clear indication of QC requirement or failure
Why it matters: Confirms this is a QC enforcement issue, not a hardware fault

Check QC Due Status and Timing

  • Navigate to QC status and verify if daily/shift QC is overdue.
  • Check last successful QC date/time
  • Compare with facility QC policy

Expected: QC is overdue or missing
Why it matters: Most lockouts are triggered by missed scheduled QC

Verify QC Materials (Most Common Cause)

  • Inspect QC solution and test strips
  • Check expiration dates
  • Ensure proper storage conditions (room temp, sealed)
  • Confirm correct QC level (Level 1 or Level 2 as required)

Expected: Valid, in-date QC materials
Why it matters: Expired or degraded QC materials cause failures and lockout

Perform QC Test Properly

  • Insert strip fully and correctly
  • Apply QC solution properly (not too much or too little)
  • Avoid contamination of strip or solution

Expected: QC passes within acceptable range
Why it matters: Improper technique is a frequent cause of repeated QC failures

Check Strip Lot and Coding (If Applicable)

  • Ensure test strips match the expected lot and are recognized by the meter
  • Verify strip vial matches configured lot
  • Confirm no damage to strips

Expected: Meter accepts strip and runs QC
Why it matters: Mismatched or unrecognized strip lots can trigger failures

Inspect Meter Contacts and Strip Port

  • Check for contamination or debris in the strip port
  • Visually inspect port
  • Clean per approved procedure if needed

Expected: Clean, unobstructed strip insertion
Why it matters: Poor contact can cause invalid readings and QC failure

Check Docking/Connectivity (If QC Data Sync Required)

  • If QC appears completed but lockout persists
  • Ensure meter is docked properly
  • Confirm communication with data management system

Expected: QC results upload successfully and lockout clears
Why it matters: Some systems require successful QC upload to release lockout

Restart the Meter

  • Power cycle the device after successful QC

Expected: Lockout clears and meter returns to ready state
Why it matters: Resets temporary software or communication issues

If the Problem Persists

If QC continues to fail or lockout does not clear after verified successful QC and proper materials:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

This indicates a likely internal issue such as sensor drift, calibration failure, or internal electronics fault. Proper troubleshooting includes recognizing when escalation is required.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to bypass QC lockout. Always move the patient to another verified meter before troubleshooting to ensure accurate and safe glucose readings.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Meter in QC lockout and unable to perform patient testing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Expired QC solution causing repeated QC failures and lockout condition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced QC materials, performed successful QC, and restored meter to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • QC solution expiration verified
  • Test strip lot checked
  • QC pass/fail results observed
  • Meter docking and upload status confirmed
  • Strip port condition inspected
  • Final device status (returned to service or removed)

Final Thought

QC lockouts are safety features designed to prevent inaccurate patient results. Focus on simple external causes like materials, technique, and timing before suspecting device failure. Knowing when to escalate protects both the patient and the integrity of testing.

That is successful troubleshooting.
