Philips Avalon FM20 Fetal Monitor

Paper Recorder or Printout Failure

Asset Type

Fetal Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

Avalon FM20

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Avalon FM20 fetal monitor fails to print reports or produces incomplete/blank paper recordings. These issues can prevent proper documentation of fetal and maternal monitoring during labor. Most causes are external or setup-related, such as paper loading errors, sensor issues, or communication problems with the recorder.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check Paper Supply

  • Confirm the correct type and size of paper is loaded.
  • Ensure paper is not jammed or folded.
  • Why: The recorder cannot print without properly loaded paper.

Inspect Printer Door and Paper Path

  • Verify the printer door is fully closed and the paper path is clear of obstructions.
  • Remove any debris or small torn pieces of paper.
  • Why: Mechanical sensors may prevent printing if the door isn’t closed or path is blocked.

Verify Recorder Settings

  • Check the monitor’s menu settings for print mode and interval.
  • Ensure the recorder is enabled and the correct channel(s) are selected.
  • Why: Incorrect configuration can prevent prints even if the paper is loaded.

Inspect Printer Connections

  • Ensure cables (if external) between monitor and printer are secure.
  • If the printer is built-in, confirm internal connectors have not loosened.
  • Why: Loose connections can prevent data from reaching the recorder.

Test with a Known Good Paper Roll

  • Swap the paper with a new roll to rule out defects or moisture damage.
  • Why: Faulty paper can cause jams or blank printouts.

Power Cycle the Monitor

  • Turn the monitor off, wait 30 seconds, then power back on.
  • Attempt a test print.
  • Why: A software glitch can sometimes prevent printing; restarting can reset the printer subsystem.

If the Problem Persists

After all external checks, if printing still fails:

  • The recorder mechanism or internal electronics may be malfunctioning.
  • Action: Remove the device from service, label Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Note: Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting—do not attempt internal repairs beyond trained service limits.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt to troubleshoot the recorder during active patient monitoring.
  • Move the patient to another functional monitor first.
  • Ensure documentation is maintained manually if necessary until the monitor is fully operational.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“User reports that the Avalon FM20 monitor fails to print paper records.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Paper not loaded correctly, printer door not closed, or communication issue with recorder.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Cleared jams, verified paper, confirmed settings, and tested print function. Escalated to repair if issue persists.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Printer indicator lights (on/off, error)
  • Paper type and manufacturer
  • Any unusual sounds (grinding, clicking)
  • Device power cycle status
  • Last successful printout time

Final Thought

Ensuring proper fetal monitoring documentation is critical for patient safety. A logical, stepwise approach—starting with external checks and moving to escalation when necessary—prevents unnecessary downtime and maintains accurate records. Always document actions clearly in CCR format.

That is successful troubleshooting.
