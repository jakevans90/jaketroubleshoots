Hillrom Progressa Hospital Bed

Side Rail Controls Not Responding

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Progressa

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the side rail control buttons on a Hillrom Progressa bed do not respond. It focuses on simple, external, and easily verifiable causes first—such as power availability, control cable connections, and user settings—before assuming internal hardware failure. The goal is safe troubleshooting while the bed is not in active patient use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure the Bed Is Powered

  • Verify the bed is plugged into a live outlet.
  • Check that the main power switch on the bed (usually near the footboard) is in the ON position.
  • Why: Side rail controls rely on the bed’s main power; a disconnected or switched-off power source will render them inactive.

Check the Battery Backup (if equipped)

  • If the bed uses a battery backup, confirm the battery is charged.
  • A completely drained battery can prevent the side rails from responding.

Inspect the Control Cable Connection

  • Side rail controls typically connect via a cable to the bed frame.
  • Ensure the connector is fully seated and not damaged.
  • Look for visible wear, bent pins, or frayed wires.
  • Why: Loose or damaged connections are a common cause of unresponsive controls.

Test the Nurse/Bedside Control Panel

  • Attempt to operate the bed using the central control or nurse handset.
  • If the bed responds here but not the side rail, the issue is isolated to the side rail control.

Clean the Side Rail Buttons

  • Dust or debris can interfere with button contact.
  • Wipe the controls gently with a damp cloth (no solvents).
  • Why: Physical contamination can prevent electrical contact inside the control buttons.

Check for Lockout Mode

  • Some Progressa beds have a side rail lockout feature.
  • Verify lockout is not engaged; consult bed labeling or quick guide indicators.

Restart the Bed Electronics

  • If safe, power the bed down for 30 seconds, then power back on.
  • Observe if side rail controls respond after reboot.

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks fail, the issue is likely internal to the side rail control board or wiring.

  • Remove the bed from service.
  • Label the bed Out of Service.
  • Escalate to Hillrom-authorized service or internal biomedical repair bench evaluation.
  • Why: Continuing to operate a bed with unresponsive controls can risk patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt troubleshooting while a patient is in the bed.

Move the patient to a safe alternative bed before testing electrical or control functions.

Only operate the bed when it is safe for staff and patient.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Side rail controls do not respond when pressed; patient cannot adjust bed.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Control cable fully seated but side rail buttons remain unresponsive; central control responds normally.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Bed powered down and re-checked; if still unresponsive, removed from service and labeled Out of Service; service request submitted.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet and power verified
  • Battery status checked
  • Central control function tested
  • Side rail buttons cleaned
  • Lockout mode checked
  • Bed status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Troubleshooting the Progressa bed requires a systematic, safety-first approach. External and easily verified causes should always be ruled out before considering internal repairs. Proper documentation ensures patient safety and efficient repair escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
