Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x11A1 Right Pendant Button Stuck (Critical)

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0x11A1 indicates a stuck right pendant button, which can cause unintended bed movement or continuous activation of a function. This creates a significant safety risk due to loss of controlled operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the bed from active patient use if unintended movement is occurring
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed
  • Ensure no controls are actively moving the bed

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm Error 0x11A1 is displayed on the system
  • Observe if the bed moves without input
  • Check if a specific function is continuously active
  • Press buttons to confirm abnormal response

Inspect the Right Pendant Buttons

  • Press each button individually
  • Check for sticking, binding, or failure to return
  • Look for visible damage or wear

Check for Contamination or Fluid Intrusion

  • Inspect for dried fluids or residue
  • Look for cracks or compromised seals
  • Clean the exterior per facility guidelines
  • Do not open the pendant

Inspect Pendant Cable and Connection

  • Ensure the cable is fully seated at the connection point
  • Trace the cable for pinching, cuts, or strain
  • Verify no exposed wiring or damage

Test Bed Function Without the Pendant

  • Disconnect the right pendant if possible
  • Operate the bed using side rail or alternate controls
  • Observe for normal operation without unintended movement

Stop if Resolved

  • If cleaning or reseating resolves the issue, clear the error
  • Verify all controls operate normally
  • Return the bed to service

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0x11A1 continues after all external checks, the pendant likely has an internal mechanical or electrical failure.

  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Replace the right pendant

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or continue using a bed with a stuck control while a patient is present. Always transfer the patient to a backup device before proceeding.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x11A1 with continuous activation from right pendant; unintended movement observed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Right pendant button stuck causing continuous input; issue persisted after cleaning and cable inspection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and replaced right pendant; verified proper operation and cleared error."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Critical control faults like a stuck pendant button must be addressed immediately. Always rule out external causes, but prioritize safety and escalate to replacement when necessary.

That is successful troubleshooting.
