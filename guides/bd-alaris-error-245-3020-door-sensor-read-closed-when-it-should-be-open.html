BD Alaris

Error: 245.3020 Door sensor read CLOSED when it should be OPEN

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 245.3020 indicates the pump is falsely detecting the door as CLOSED when it is physically OPEN. This prevents proper administration set loading and safe infusion startup, as the system cannot verify door position.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use if infusion setup cannot be completed or verified
  • Transfer therapy to another infusion device if needed

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and open the door
  • Confirm Error 245.3020 is displayed
  • Observe if the pump indicates CLOSED while the door is open
  • Attempt to proceed with loading or priming

3. Inspect Door Operation and Alignment

  • Open and close the door fully multiple times
  • Check for incomplete travel or uneven movement
  • Ensure the door sits properly when opened and closed

4. Check for Obstructions in Door and Latch Area

  • Inspect latch, hinge, and internal channel areas
  • Look for debris, residue, or dried fluids
  • Check for cracked or damaged plastic components

5. Verify Proper Set Removal and Loading Area

  • Remove any administration set from the channel
  • Ensure no tubing is caught or partially seated
  • Inspect for pinched or misrouted tubing

6. Inspect Door Sensor Activation Point

  • Check where the door engages the sensor
  • Look for worn or broken actuator tabs
  • Verify the sensor is not stuck in the CLOSED position
  • Confirm proper alignment between door and sensor

7. Power Cycle the Pump

  • Turn the pump off and wait a few seconds
  • Power back on and reopen the door
  • Observe if the sensor state updates correctly

8. Attempt Normal Operation

  • Close and reopen the door
  • Attempt to reload the administration set
  • Confirm the pump detects OPEN and CLOSED states correctly

If the Problem Persists

If Error 245.3020 continues after completing all checks, external and mechanical causes have been ruled out. The door sensor or internal switch circuitry is likely faulty.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup infusion pump before investigating door or sensor-related issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"BD Alaris pump displaying Error 245.3020 indicating door reads CLOSED while open; unable to load set."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Door sensor remained engaged in CLOSED state despite proper door movement and no obstructions; suspected sensor failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump from service, labeled out of service, and sent to biomed for repair and evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Door sensor errors affect how the pump verifies safe set loading and infusion readiness. Always rule out external and mechanical causes before escalating to internal repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
