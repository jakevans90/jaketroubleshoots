Spacelabs Healthcare 90207

Battery Not Charging

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Spacelabs Healthcare

Model

90207

What This Guide Helps With

Device not charging, not powering on, or shutting down due to battery, charger, or connection issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from the patient before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Monitoring is stopped safely or transferred to another device.
  • Why it matters: Prevents interruption of patient data collection and ensures safe handling.

Verify Charging Method and Equipment

  • Confirm the correct charging dock or AC adapter is being used.
  • Ensure the correct Spacelabs charger is being used.
  • Confirm compatibility (no third-party chargers).
  • Expected: Proper charger is connected to the device.
  • Why it matters: Incorrect chargers will not deliver proper voltage/current.

Check Power Source (Outlet)

  • Verify the wall outlet is functional.
  • Test outlet with another device.
  • Try a different known working outlet.
  • Expected: Outlet provides consistent power.
  • Why it matters: A dead outlet is a common and easily missed issue.

Inspect Charger and Power Cord

  • Examine for visible damage or loose connections.
  • Check for frayed cables or bent prongs.
  • Ensure connections are fully seated.
  • Swap with a known good charger if available.
  • Expected: Charger is intact and securely connected.
  • Why it matters: Damaged cords prevent proper charging.

Inspect Device Charging Contacts

  • Check the contact points between the monitor and charger/dock.
  • Inspect for dirt, corrosion, or bent contacts.
  • Clean contacts gently with approved wipes if needed.
  • Expected: Clean, undamaged contacts.
  • Why it matters: Poor contact prevents charging current flow.

Confirm Charging Indicators

  • Place the device on the charger and observe indicators.
  • Look for charging LED or screen indicator.
  • Note if there is no response, intermittent response, or error.
  • Expected: Charging indicator activates.
  • Why it matters: Helps determine if power is reaching the device.

Allow Charging Time and Recheck

  • Leave the device on charge for an adequate period.
  • Allow at least 30–60 minutes.
  • Attempt to power on after charging.
  • Expected: Device powers on or shows increased battery level.
  • Why it matters: Deeply discharged batteries may take time to respond.

Perform Power Cycle

  • Attempt a restart after initial charge.
  • Remove from charger.
  • Power off (if possible), then power back on.
  • Reconnect to charger.
  • Expected: Device begins normal charging behavior.
  • Why it matters: Clears minor firmware or charging state issues.

Swap with Known Good Battery (If Applicable)

  • If the model supports battery replacement.
  • Install a known good battery.
  • Reattempt charging.
  • Expected: Charging resumes with known good battery.
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is battery vs device circuitry.

If the Problem Persists

If all external power sources, chargers, and connections have been verified, the issue is likely internal (battery failure or charging circuit).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and saves time.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot charging issues while the device is assigned to a patient study. Replace the monitor to avoid incomplete or lost BP data.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device not charging and would not power on during setup for patient use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed internal battery after confirming good charger, outlet, and connections."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent for battery replacement and evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Charger swapped with known good
  • Charging indicator status (on/off/intermittent)
  • Condition of charging contacts
  • Battery behavior (no charge vs short runtime)
  • Final device status (removed from service)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple external checks before assuming internal failure. Always prioritize patient safety and continuity of monitoring. When external causes are ruled out, escalation is the correct and professional decision. Clear documentation ensures continuity and supports repair efficiency.

That is successful troubleshooting.
