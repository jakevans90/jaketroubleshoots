Hamilton C6 Ventilator

Touchscreen Lag / Freeze

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Hamilton

Model

C6

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the Hamilton C6 ventilator:

  • Exhibits delayed response to touchscreen inputs
  • Freezes intermittently or becomes unresponsive
  • Displays incorrect or stuck menu selections

Focus is on external, logical checks first—power, connections, and environmental factors—before assuming internal hardware or software failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check Power Source

  • Verify AC mains connection or battery level.
  • Low voltage or unstable power can cause the touchscreen to lag.

Inspect Cables and Connectors

  • Ensure AC power cord, battery contacts, and any USB/data ports are fully seated.
  • Look for bent pins, corrosion, or frayed cables that could affect system stability.

Confirm Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure the ventilator is not exposed to excessive heat, direct sunlight, or high humidity, which can slow touch response.
  • Remove any dust, liquid, or debris from the touchscreen surface.

Restart the Device

  • Power down the ventilator completely.
  • Wait 30–60 seconds and power back on.
  • Observe if touchscreen responsiveness improves.

Check for Software Updates

  • If safe to do so, verify the device firmware version.
  • Outdated firmware can cause UI lag; note version for service records.

Reduce System Load

  • Close non-essential menus or monitoring windows if the device allows.
  • Excessive simultaneous monitoring can temporarily slow touchscreen response.

Observe for Patterned Behavior

  • Note if freezing occurs with certain menus, alarms, or settings.
  • Document times, duration, and any error messages for service escalation.

If the Problem Persists

Common external and user-side factors have been ruled out. Device may have internal touchscreen or processor issues.

  • Remove the ventilator from service.
  • Label it “Out of Service.”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Do not attempt internal board-level troubleshooting; this is a service-level repair.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot on an active patient.
  • If touchscreen becomes unresponsive during patient care, switch the patient to a backup ventilator immediately.
  • Always ensure patient safety before attempting any device troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Ventilator touchscreen is slow and sometimes freezes during normal operation.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed lag and unresponsive touch panels even after verifying power, connections, and environmental conditions.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Device powered down, restarted, monitored; issue persists. Device labeled out of service and sent for repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • AC outlet tested and confirmed stable
  • Battery contacts inspected
  • No visible damage to touchscreen
  • Software version documented
  • Final status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety and systematic troubleshooting are critical. Start with external checks, document all observations, and escalate when the device cannot be reliably operated. Proper CCR documentation ensures the service team has accurate information to resolve the issue quickly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
