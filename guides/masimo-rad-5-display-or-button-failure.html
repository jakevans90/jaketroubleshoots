Masimo Rad-5

Display or Button Failure

Asset Type

Pulse Oximeter

Manufacturer

Masimo

Model

Rad-5

What This Guide Helps With

Unresponsive buttons, blank or dim display, or intermittent screen operation caused by power issues, external damage, or user interface faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use if the display or controls are not functioning reliably.
  • Use an alternate monitoring device.

Expected: Patient monitoring continues safely on another device
Why it matters: Inability to view readings or control the unit compromises patient care

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the device and confirm the failure.
  • Observe for:
    • Completely blank screen
    • Dim or flickering display
    • Buttons not responding or delayed response

Expected: Issue is confirmed and reproducible
Why it matters: Confirms true device failure vs user misunderstanding

Check Battery Status and Power Source

  • Replace batteries with new, known-good batteries
  • Ensure correct battery orientation
  • Power cycle the device

Expected: Device powers on with normal display and button response
Why it matters: Insufficient power is a common cause of UI failure

Inspect for Physical Damage or Contamination

  • Examine the screen and keypad area closely
  • Check for:
    • Cracked display
    • Worn or stuck buttons
    • Fluid ingress or residue
    • Dirt or debris around buttons

Expected: No visible damage or obstruction
Why it matters: Physical damage or contamination can prevent proper input or display function

Perform a Hard Reset (Power Cycle)

  • Remove batteries
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reinstall batteries and power on

Expected: Display and buttons return to normal function
Why it matters: Clears minor internal glitches affecting UI

Check Display Visibility Settings and Environment

  • Adjust viewing angle
  • Move device to better lighting
  • Look for faint display indicating backlight failure

Expected: Display is clearly visible
Why it matters: Backlight or contrast issues may appear as display failure

Test Button Functionality Individually

  • Press each button and observe response
  • Check for:
    • No response
    • Intermittent response
    • Only certain buttons failing

Expected: All buttons respond consistently
Why it matters: Helps determine if issue is isolated to keypad vs full device failure

If the Problem Persists

If display or button issues remain after verifying power, cleaning, and resetting, external causes have been ruled out and internal failure (keypad membrane, display module, or control board) is likely.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot a display or control issue while the device is in active patient use. Always switch to a functioning monitor first to maintain therapy continuity.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nursing reported Rad-5 display blank and buttons not responding during spot check."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Depleted batteries caused device to power intermittently, affecting display and keypad function."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced batteries, verified normal display and button operation, returned device to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery condition verified
  • Power cycle performed
  • Display behavior (blank, dim, flicker)
  • Button response consistency
  • Presence of physical damage or contamination
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Start with simple, external causes like power and physical condition before assuming internal failure. Prioritize patient safety and avoid over-troubleshooting. Clear documentation supports future reliability and repair decisions.

That is successful troubleshooting.
