Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: PRESSURE LIMITING !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

PRESSURE LIMITING ! alarm indicating airway pressure reaching or exceeding PMAX, causing limited ventilation and potential risk of inadequate or unsafe pressure delivery.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If airway pressures are elevated or ventilation is compromised, remove the machine from patient use
  • Provide alternate ventilation (bagging or backup machine)
  • Confirm patient is stable and ventilated

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the PRESSURE LIMITING ! alarm is displayed
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup vs during ventilation)
  • Compare airway pressure readings to PMAX setting
  • Check if delivered breaths appear limited or truncated

Check Ventilator Pressure Settings (PMAX)

  • Review the PMAX (maximum pressure limit) setting
  • Verify PMAX is appropriate for patient and ventilation mode
  • Compare with typical clinical values

Check for Breathing Circuit Obstruction or Restriction

  • Inspect for kinked or compressed hoses
  • Check filters, water traps, and connectors for blockage
  • Ensure no obstruction at the patient interface

Check APL Valve and Fresh Gas Flow (if in Manual/Spont Mode)

  • Ensure APL valve is not overly closed
  • Verify fresh gas flow is appropriate
  • Confirm pressure can be relieved through the circuit

Evaluate Patient or Test Lung Conditions

  • Assess patient lung compliance and resistance
  • Use a test lung to isolate device vs patient condition
  • Compare pressure behavior with test lung

Check Ventilation Mode and Settings

  • Review tidal volume, inspiratory pressure, and flow settings
  • Ensure settings are appropriate for patient condition
  • Adjust overly aggressive settings if necessary

Stop When Resolved

  • Confirm airway pressures return to normal
  • Ensure alarm no longer appears
  • Return device to service only if stable

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. Persistent pressure limiting may indicate internal pressure sensing or control issues.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always transfer ventilation to a backup device before evaluating pressure-related alarms.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying PRESSURE LIMITING ! alarm during ventilation with elevated airway pressures and limited breath delivery."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Breathing circuit obstruction or PMAX set too low causing pressure to reach limit; no internal faults if resolved externally."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Adjusted PMAX and cleared circuit obstruction restoring normal operation, or removed unit from service for further evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Pressure-related alarms must be addressed immediately. Always start with settings and external restrictions before escalating to internal issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
