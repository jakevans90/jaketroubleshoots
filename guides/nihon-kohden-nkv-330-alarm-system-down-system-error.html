Nihon Kohden NKV-330

Alarm: SYSTEM DOWN — **** SYSTEM ERROR ****

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Nihon Kohden

Model

NKV-330

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the Nihon Kohden NKV-330 ventilator alarm SYSTEM DOWN — **** SYSTEM ERROR ****. This condition indicates that a system error occurred and was recorded in error history. Because this is a system-level alarm on a ventilator, the device should not be trusted for patient ventilation until the condition is evaluated and the ventilator passes appropriate testing.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot this alarm while the ventilator is actively supporting a patient.
  • Move the patient to another approved ventilator or provide manual ventilation per clinical protocol.
  • Notify respiratory therapy or the clinical care team immediately.
  • Do not return the ventilator to patient use just because the alarm clears after a restart.

Verify the Exact Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the displayed alarm is SYSTEM DOWN — **** SYSTEM ERROR ****.
  • Check whether the alarm occurred during startup, active ventilation, after restart, or intermittently.
  • Observe whether the monitoring window returns after restart.
  • Note whether ventilation stopped, controls became unresponsive, the screen froze, or the unit restarted unexpectedly.

Remove the Ventilator From Clinical Use

  • Tag the ventilator as Out of Service.
  • Move the device to a safe bench evaluation area if available.
  • Do not repeatedly restart the ventilator at the bedside to force it back into service.
  • Treat the alarm as a serious device condition, not a routine clinical alarm.

Check External Power

  • Confirm the ventilator is connected to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord for damage, looseness, or signs of overheating.
  • Verify AC power indication or charging status if available.
  • Move the ventilator to another known-good power source if power instability is suspected.

Inspect for Obvious External Conditions

  • Check for liquid intrusion, physical damage, dropped-unit damage, or blocked ventilation openings.
  • Look for signs of overheating, burning smell, unusual fan noise, or loose external accessories.
  • Confirm the ventilator is not being operated in an extreme environment.
  • Correct any obvious external issue before continuing evaluation.

Restart Under Controlled Conditions

  • With the ventilator removed from patient use, power the device off.
  • Wait briefly, then power the ventilator back on using a known-good AC power source.
  • Observe whether the monitoring window returns.
  • If the ventilator will not restart or the monitoring window does not return, stop troubleshooting and send the device for repair.

Perform a Test Run If the Monitoring Window Returns

  • Use appropriate test equipment, a simulator, or a test lung according to facility procedure.
  • Confirm startup completes normally.
  • Confirm the SYSTEM DOWN alarm does not return.
  • Verify display response, control response, alarm function, and stable ventilation output.
  • Watch for abnormal resets, heat, odor, fan noise, or intermittent failures.

Review Error History If Available

  • Check error history according to approved service or facility procedures.
  • Document the SYSTEM DOWN / SYSTEM ERROR event.
  • Record whether the alarm is a single event or a repeat condition.
  • Do not perform internal board-level repair unless trained and authorized.

Escalate for Repair if the Alarm Repeats

  • If the ventilator will not restart, the monitoring window does not return, or the alarm repeats, keep the ventilator Out of Service.
  • Based on the provided troubleshooting information, the DIGITAL Board UR-4362 may be faulty.
  • If replacement of the DIGITAL Board UR-4362 does not resolve the issue, escalate to Nihon Kohden.
  • Do not return the ventilator to patient care until repair and required testing are completed.

If the Problem Persists

If external power, visible damage, environmental concerns, restart behavior, and test-run checks do not resolve the issue, the common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent SYSTEM DOWN — **** SYSTEM ERROR **** alarm indicates a likely internal system fault.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Evaluate for possible DIGITAL Board UR-4362 failure.
  • Escalate to Nihon Kohden if the issue remains after internal repair.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a SYSTEM DOWN alarm on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup ventilator or approved alternate ventilation method first, then evaluate the device under controlled conditions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nihon Kohden NKV-330 ventilator displayed SYSTEM DOWN — **** SYSTEM ERROR **** and was removed from patient use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed SYSTEM DOWN system error condition. External power and visible external conditions checked. Alarm repeated during restart/test-run, indicating likely internal system fault recorded in error history."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Ventilator removed from service and labeled Out of Service. Restart and controlled test-run attempted. Unit referred for bench evaluation with DIGITAL Board UR-4362 suspected per troubleshooting guidance. Escalate to Nihon Kohden if unresolved."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A SYSTEM DOWN alarm on a ventilator should be handled as a serious safety condition. Protect the patient first, confirm the exact alarm, check simple external causes, and only continue testing if the ventilator restarts normally and remains stable. If the alarm repeats or the ventilator will not restart, escalation is the correct action.

That is successful troubleshooting.
