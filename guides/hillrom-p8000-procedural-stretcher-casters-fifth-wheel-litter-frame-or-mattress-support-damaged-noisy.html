Hillrom P8000 Procedural Stretcher

Casters, fifth wheel, litter frame, or mattress support damaged/noisy

Asset Type

Stretcher

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

P8000 Procedural Stretcher

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting noisy, damaged, loose, binding, or unstable caster, fifth wheel, litter frame, or mattress support concerns on a procedural stretcher.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the stretcher from patient use before troubleshooting if there is any concern with stability, rolling control, frame damage, or mattress support integrity.

Expected outcome: The patient is transferred to another safe stretcher or bed before inspection continues.

If this resolves the immediate safety risk, stop and document the action.

Verify the Reported Problem

Ask staff what was noticed: squeaking, grinding, wobbling, hard steering, uneven rolling, frame flexing, mattress sagging, or visible damage. Roll the stretcher in a clear area and listen for the reported noise.

Expected outcome: The issue is confirmed and narrowed to casters, fifth wheel, frame, or mattress support area.

Perform a Visual Safety Inspection

Inspect the stretcher from all sides. Look for bent metal, cracked welds, loose hardware, missing fasteners, damaged covers, broken supports, or signs that the stretcher struck a wall, elevator, doorway, or equipment.

Expected outcome: Obvious damage is identified before functional testing continues.

If structural damage is found, remove from service and stop.

Check Each Caster for Damage or Debris

Inspect all caster wheels for flat spots, cracks, missing chunks, embedded debris, wrapped tape, hair, string, or buildup around the axle. Rotate each wheel by hand if safe.

Expected outcome: Each caster rotates smoothly without grinding, rubbing, or excessive side play.

If debris is removed and the caster rolls normally, return to service only after a full function check.

Check Caster Swivel Movement

With the stretcher unloaded, move it forward, backward, and side to side. Watch whether each caster swivels freely or binds.

Expected outcome: Casters pivot smoothly without hopping, dragging, or locking unexpectedly.

If a caster binds, wobbles, or has excessive play, remove from service for caster repair or replacement.

Check Brake and Steer Interaction

Cycle the brake/steer pedals through brake, neutral, and steer positions. Confirm the reported caster or fifth wheel issue is not actually caused by a partially engaged brake or steer mechanism.

Expected outcome: The stretcher rolls freely in neutral, locks in brake, and tracks properly in steer.

If brake/steer adjustment or linkage concern is suspected, remove from service for bench evaluation.

Inspect the Fifth Wheel Area

Look underneath the center area of the stretcher for a damaged fifth wheel, loose hardware, debris, worn tread, dragging components, or anything contacting the floor incorrectly.

Expected outcome: The fifth wheel engages and disengages cleanly without dragging, clunking, scraping, or causing unstable tracking.

If the fifth wheel is damaged, loose, or not tracking correctly, remove from service.

Check for Unusual Noise While Rolling

Roll the stretcher slowly over a smooth floor, then over a doorway threshold if safe. Listen for squeaking, clicking, grinding, clunking, or rattling.

Expected outcome: Noise is repeatable and traceable to a caster, fifth wheel, frame area, side rail area, or mattress support area.

If the noise is caused by loose external hardware that can be safely secured, correct it and retest.

Inspect the Litter Frame

Raise and lower the litter if the stretcher is equipped for height adjustment. Watch for twisting, uneven movement, rubbing, loose frame components, cracked welds, or abnormal flexing.

Expected outcome: The litter frame remains stable and level without excessive movement or noise.

If the frame appears bent, cracked, unstable, or misaligned, remove from service immediately.

Inspect the Mattress Support Surface

Remove or lift the mattress if needed and inspect the deck/support surface. Look for broken panels, bent supports, sharp edges, loose screws, missing hardware, sagging sections, or areas that could damage the mattress or create pressure points.

Expected outcome: The mattress support is secure, smooth, and able to support the patient safely.

If the mattress support is damaged or unstable, remove from service.

Inspect the Mattress Condition

Check the mattress for tears, bottom-side damage, compression, fluid intrusion, or damage caused by the support surface underneath.

Expected outcome: The mattress is intact and properly supported.

If the mattress is damaged or contaminated, replace or route according to facility policy.

Check Accessory Mounts and Attachments

Inspect IV pole sockets, oxygen tank holders, side rail mounts, push handles, and any attached accessories near the noisy or damaged area. Loose accessories can sound like frame or caster problems.

Expected outcome: Accessories are secure and not creating false noise or instability.

If an accessory is loose or damaged, correct or remove it before returning the stretcher to service.

Perform a Final Function and Safety Check

After any simple correction, roll the stretcher, steer it, brake it, inspect the frame, and confirm the mattress support is secure.

Expected outcome: The stretcher rolls smoothly, tracks correctly, brakes securely, and has no unsafe structural or support concerns.

If the issue is resolved, document the work and return the stretcher to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the stretcher still has caster noise, fifth wheel issues, frame damage, loose mattress support, unstable movement, grinding, wobbling, or structural concerns after external checks, the likely cause is mechanical wear, impact damage, failed caster hardware, fifth wheel assembly wear, or frame/support damage.

The stretcher should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, parts replacement, or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A stretcher with questionable rolling control, brake/steer function, frame integrity, or patient support should not remain in service.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot caster, fifth wheel, frame, or mattress support problems with a patient on the stretcher unless the situation is urgent and movement would create a greater risk. Transfer the patient to another safe device first whenever possible. A stretcher that rolls poorly, does not track correctly, or has support damage can become a patient fall or transport hazard.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported the Hillrom P8000 procedural stretcher made a grinding noise while rolling and appeared difficult to steer during transport."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found debris wrapped around one caster and excessive play/noise from the fifth wheel area during steer testing."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed debris from caster, verified continued fifth wheel noise, labeled stretcher Out of Service, and routed for bench repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the stretcher was occupied or removed from patient use
  • Which caster or wheel position was affected
  • Whether the fifth wheel engaged or dragged
  • Brake/steer pedal behavior
  • Noise type: squeak, grind, clunk, rattle, scrape
  • Whether noise occurred in neutral, brake, or steer mode
  • Floor condition where issue was reported
  • Any visible impact damage
  • Loose or missing hardware
  • Mattress support condition
  • Mattress condition
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Caster, fifth wheel, litter frame, and mattress support problems should be treated as patient safety concerns, not just comfort or convenience issues. Start with visible damage, debris, loose parts, brake/steer position, and external causes before assuming internal mechanical failure. If rolling control, frame stability, or patient support is questionable, remove the stretcher from service and document clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
