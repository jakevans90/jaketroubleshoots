3M Versaflo TR-300 / TR-600 Series

Control Panel or Indicator Lights Not Working

Asset Type

Powered Air-Purifying Respirator (PAPR)

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Versaflo TR-300 / TR-600 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the 3M Versaflo TR-300 or TR-600 PAPR control panel or indicator lights are non-functional. Often, these issues are caused by simple external factors such as battery charge, loose connections, or contamination, rather than internal electronic failure. This guide focuses on safe, external troubleshooting steps.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure the device is powered off

  • Turn the PAPR off before inspecting. This prevents false readings or accidental short circuits.

Check the battery

  • Verify the battery is fully charged.
  • Swap with a known good battery if available.
  • A weak or depleted battery may prevent lights or the control panel from functioning.

Inspect battery and device connections

  • Ensure the battery is seated correctly.
  • Check for corrosion, dirt, or debris on contacts. Clean gently with a dry cloth if needed.

Examine the control panel and indicators

  • Look for visible damage, cracks, or water ingress.
  • Ensure the panel is free from moisture or contamination that could impede electrical contact.

Test device with minimal configuration

  • Remove any hoses or accessories and turn the device on with just the battery connected.
  • If lights still do not illuminate, external causes are less likely.

Check for software reset

  • Some models allow a power reset or firmware refresh by disconnecting the battery for 5–10 minutes.
  • Reinstall the battery and power on to see if lights return.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may be an internal control board failure or damaged indicator LEDs.

  • Remove the PAPR from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to 3M service or qualified repair bench for evaluation.

Proper troubleshooting includes recognizing when to escalate rather than continuing unsafe attempts.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt to use a PAPR with non-functional indicators on a patient.
  • Ensure an alternate PAPR is available for safe respiratory protection before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Control panel and indicator lights do not illuminate when powered on.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Battery verified good, connections clean and secure, panel appears intact; no external cause identified.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“PAPR removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for repair evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Battery voltage checked
  • Connections inspected and cleaned
  • Device powered on with minimal configuration
  • Control panel and lights visually inspected
  • Device labeled Out of Service

Final Thought

Clinical troubleshooting is about patient safety, methodical checks, and knowing when to escalate. Recording the CCR ensures clear communication and future reference, reducing unnecessary service delays. Non-functional indicators are a safety concern and must be addressed before device use.

That is successful troubleshooting.
