Tuttnauer Elara Series

Temperature Sensor Calibration Errors

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

Tuttnauer

Model

Elara Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of inaccurate temperature readings, preventing failed sterilization cycles and ensuring instrument and patient safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Staff Safety

Confirm the sterilizer chamber is not pressurized and no personnel are near the door. Avoid burns or injury during inspection.

Check Display and Error Messages

Review the control panel for temperature error codes or warnings. Note any unusual readings. This indicates if calibration drift or sensor failure is suspected.

Verify Power Stability

Ensure the sterilizer is plugged into a reliable outlet with stable voltage. Fluctuations can cause false temperature readings.

Inspect Sensor Connections

Check the wiring and connectors to the temperature probe. Loose or corroded connections can cause inaccurate readings. Expected outcome: secure connections without damage.

Compare Against External Thermometer

If safe, run a short test cycle using a calibrated external thermometer or temperature probe. Confirm the sterilizer’s reading matches the external measurement within tolerance.

Reset Calibration (if supported)

Some Elara models allow a software recalibration or reset of the temperature sensor. Follow manufacturer prompts and verify that readings stabilize afterward.

Observe Next Cycle

Run a monitored cycle to ensure temperature reaches and maintains the set point. If stable, the issue is resolved.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. The temperature sensor or internal electronics may be faulty. The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop avoids risk and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt patient sterilization if temperature readings are inconsistent. Move instruments to another functioning sterilizer until the Elara unit is verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer displayed fluctuating temperature readings during cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose sensor connection and minor calibration drift identified."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Secured sensor, performed recalibration, verified proper temperature readings during test cycle."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and stable
  • Sensor wiring inspected
  • Control panel error codes recorded
  • External thermometer validation
  • Device status after recalibration

Final Thought

Accurate temperature measurement is critical for effective sterilization. Follow logical troubleshooting from simple external checks to controlled calibration, escalating appropriately, and document thoroughly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
