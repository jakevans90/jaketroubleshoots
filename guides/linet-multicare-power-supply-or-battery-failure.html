Linet Multicare

Power Supply or Battery Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Linet

Model

Multicare

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses loss of bed power or battery backup failure caused by outlet issues, cords, fuses, or battery connections before assuming internal electronics failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the patient is safely transferred to another bed or fully supported. Never troubleshoot power issues with a patient on the bed.

Check External Power Source

Verify the wall outlet is live using a known working device or outlet tester. Expected outcome: device should receive power when plugged in.

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

Examine the bed’s power cord for damage, kinks, or loose connections. Ensure the plug is fully seated. Expected outcome: no visible damage, secure connections.

Check Bed Fuses and Circuit Breakers

Locate any external fuses or breakers on the bed and inspect for tripped circuits or blown fuses. Replace or reset if needed. Expected outcome: fuse intact or replaced, bed powered on.

Inspect Battery Status

If the bed has a removable or internal battery, ensure it is properly seated and charged. Expected outcome: battery connected, charging indicator active.

Test Alternate Power Source

If possible, plug the bed into a different, known working outlet. Expected outcome: bed powers on, indicating original outlet may be faulty.

Observe Bed Indicators

Power lights, alarms, or status LEDs should illuminate. Lack of indicators may suggest battery or internal power module failure.

If the Problem Persists

After confirming outlet, cord, fuse, and battery are functional, the issue is likely internal. Remove the bed from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Proper escalation prevents patient risk and equipment damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Always move the patient to another bed first. Troubleshoot power issues only when the bed is empty to prevent patient harm.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed would not power on; patient unable to use controls."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Bed was plugged into a non-functional outlet; battery seated correctly."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Outlet tested and verified; cord checked; bed powered on successfully."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet verified live
  • Power cord inspected
  • Fuses/circuit breakers checked
  • Battery seated and charged
  • Power indicator lights functioning

Final Thought

Patient safety comes first. Logical, stepwise evaluation of power sources, connections, and batteries avoids unnecessary internal repairs. Escalation should occur only after external causes are ruled out. Proper CCR documentation preserves clinical and engineering clarity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
