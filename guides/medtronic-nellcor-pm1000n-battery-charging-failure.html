Medtronic Nellcor PM1000N

Battery Charging Failure or Backup Battery Degradation

Asset Type

Pulse Oximeter

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Nellcor PM1000N

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting issues where the Nellcor PM1000N fails to charge its battery properly or shows reduced backup battery runtime. Such problems are often caused by external factors such as AC power, charging connections, battery age, or environmental conditions before considering internal failures.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Always move the patient to a working device before troubleshooting a battery issue. Never rely on a device with an unverified or degraded backup battery during patient monitoring.

Verify AC Power Source

  • Check that the wall outlet is live using a verified device or outlet tester.
  • Ensure the power cord is properly seated at both the outlet and device.

Why: A faulty outlet or loose connection is the most common cause of battery charging issues.

Inspect Power Cord and Adapter

  • Look for frays, cracks, or bent prongs on cords and adapter.
  • Swap with a known working cord/adapter if available.

Why: Damaged cords can prevent proper charging or trigger intermittent charging behavior.

Check Battery Installation

  • Ensure the battery is seated correctly in the compartment.
  • Inspect battery contacts for corrosion, dirt, or debris. Clean gently if needed.

Why: Poor contact can mimic battery failure.

Observe Charging Indicators

  • Plug the device in and monitor the battery LED or screen indicator.
  • Confirm the device shows charging status.

Why: Confirms whether the device is attempting to charge or the battery is non-responsive.

Test with Backup Battery

  • If available, replace the internal battery with a known good battery.
  • Observe device behavior and charge time.

Why: Helps determine if the original battery has degraded or failed.

Environmental Checks

  • Ensure the device is in a temperature-controlled environment. Extreme cold or heat can reduce battery performance.
  • Avoid charging in direct sunlight or near heat sources.

If the Problem Persists

External factors have been ruled out.

Likely causes include internal battery failure or charging circuit issues.

Action: Remove from service, label Out of Service, and send to authorized repair or bench evaluation.

Note: Do not attempt internal board repair or battery disassembly; this is a safety risk.

Clinical Use Tip

Always move the patient to a working device before troubleshooting a battery issue.

Never rely on a device with an unverified or degraded backup battery during patient monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Device does not charge and backup battery lasts only 10 minutes.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed that the battery fails to hold charge; AC outlet and power cord confirmed functional; battery contacts clean.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for repair; temporary replacement unit provided for patient monitoring.

Helpful Details to Include

  • AC outlet tested
  • Power cord swapped
  • Charging LED behavior
  • Backup battery runtime
  • Device ambient temperature

Final Thought

Ensuring patient safety and proper device readiness is paramount. Logical, stepwise checks prevent unnecessary device disassembly and avoid patient risk. Clear documentation ensures that repair, replacement, and patient care are properly tracked.

That is successful troubleshooting.
