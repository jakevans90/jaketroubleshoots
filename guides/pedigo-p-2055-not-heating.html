Pedigo P-2055

Not Heating

Asset Type

Blanket Warmer

Manufacturer

Pedigo

Model

P-2055

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a Pedigo P-2055 blanket warmer that powers on but does not warm, heats slowly, or fails to maintain temperature.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not rely on a blanket warmer that is not heating correctly for patient warming needs.

Action:

  • Remove the blanket warmer from clinical use if staff report cold blankets, inconsistent warming, overheating, burning smell, or abnormal operation.
  • Check whether warmed blankets are needed for active patient care.
  • Provide another approved blanket warmer if needed.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without relying on equipment that may not maintain safe warming temperatures.

Why it matters: Blanket warmers are not life-support devices, but incorrect warming can affect patient comfort, infection control workflows, and burn-prevention expectations.

Verify the Reported Problem

Confirm what “not heating” means before assuming a heater failure.

Action:

  • Ask staff whether the unit is not powering on.
  • Ask staff whether the unit is powered on but staying cold.
  • Ask staff whether the unit is heating slowly.
  • Ask staff whether the unit is reaching temperature and then cooling down.
  • Ask staff whether the unit is displaying an alarm or abnormal temperature reading.

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified.

If the warmer has no power at all, troubleshoot it as a power issue instead of a heating issue.

Check That the Unit Is Plugged Into a Working Outlet

Verify basic power before checking the warmer.

Action:

  • Confirm the power cord is fully inserted into the wall outlet.
  • Check the wall outlet with an outlet tester or another known-working device.
  • Avoid using extension cords or non-approved power strips.

Expected outcome: The outlet supplies proper power and the warmer remains powered.

If the outlet is dead, relocate the warmer to a working outlet or notify Facilities.

If this resolves the issue, return the warmer to service after confirming normal heating.

Inspect the Power Cord and Plug

A damaged cord can allow the unit to appear powered but still cause unreliable operation.

Action:

  • Inspect the power cord for cuts, exposed conductors, flattened sections, loose strain relief, or heat damage.
  • Check the plug blades for looseness, discoloration, or signs of overheating.

Expected outcome: The cord and plug are intact with no visible damage.

If damage is found, remove the unit from service and send it for repair.

Confirm the Power Switch and Controls Are Set Correctly

Incorrect settings can look like a heating failure.

Action:

  • Confirm the warmer is turned on.
  • Check the temperature setpoint.
  • Make sure the control is not set unusually low.
  • Verify staff did not recently adjust the temperature or leave the unit off after cleaning.

Expected outcome: The unit is powered on and set to an appropriate warming temperature.

If the setpoint was incorrect and the unit begins heating normally, document the finding and return the unit to service.

Allow Adequate Warm-Up Time

Blanket warmers may take time to recover temperature, especially when recently loaded or opened repeatedly.

Action:

  • Start with an empty or lightly loaded warmer.
  • Close the door fully.
  • Allow the unit time to warm without repeated door openings.
  • Observe whether the temperature rises gradually.

Expected outcome: The displayed or measured temperature should increase over time.

If the unit heats normally after adequate warm-up, no repair may be needed.

Check Door Closure and Door Gasket Condition

A door that does not seal can prevent the cabinet from reaching temperature.

Action:

  • Confirm the door closes fully.
  • Inspect the gasket for tears, gaps, deformation, or debris.
  • Check for blankets blocking the door from sealing.

Expected outcome: The door seals evenly and does not leak excessive heat.

If the door was blocked or the gasket was dirty, correct the issue and retest.

If the gasket is damaged or the door does not close properly, remove the warmer from service for repair.

Check for Overloading or Poor Air Circulation

Too many blankets can block heat circulation and make the warmer appear weak.

Action:

  • Look inside the cabinet.
  • Confirm blankets are not packed tightly against vents, shelves, or internal airflow areas.
  • Reduce the load if the warmer is overfilled.

Expected outcome: Air can circulate around the blankets and through the cabinet.

If reducing the load restores normal heating, educate staff and return the unit to service.

Verify the Temperature With an Independent Thermometer

Do not rely only on staff perception of blanket warmth.

Action:

  • Place a calibrated thermometer or temperature logger inside the warmer.
  • Allow the temperature to stabilize.
  • Compare the independent reading to the warmer’s display or setpoint.

Expected outcome: The cabinet temperature should reasonably match the setpoint and displayed temperature.

If the display shows heating but the independent thermometer shows low temperature, suspect a heating, sensor, control, or calibration issue.

Listen and Observe for Abnormal Operation

External signs can help determine whether the issue is simple or repair-related.

Action:

  • Observe the unit during a heating attempt.
  • Check for no heat output.
  • Check for clicking relays without heating.
  • Check for unusual fan noise, if equipped.
  • Check for burning smell.
  • Check for excessive heat at the plug or cord.
  • Check for temperature display that does not change.

Expected outcome: The unit operates normally with no unusual smell, heat, or sounds.

If there is a burning smell, abnormal heat, or electrical concern, unplug the unit and remove it from service immediately.

Perform a Basic Functional Check

Confirm whether the unit can heat and hold temperature safely.

Action:

  • With the unit empty or lightly loaded, power it on.
  • Set it to the normal facility-approved blanket warming range.
  • Allow the cabinet to warm.
  • Confirm the temperature rises and stabilizes.

Expected outcome: The warmer reaches and maintains temperature without alarms, abnormal odor, or erratic control behavior.

If it passes, return to service per department policy.

If it fails, escalate for repair.

If the Problem Persists

If the Pedigo P-2055 still does not heat after confirming outlet power, cord condition, settings, door seal, load condition, and temperature verification, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as a failed heater element, temperature sensor, thermostat, thermal cutoff, relay, control board, wiring issue, or internal safety device.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue troubleshooting into internal heated components unless you are following approved service procedures and have the proper training. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot the warmer while clinical staff are actively depending on it for patient care. Move the need to another approved blanket warmer first, then test the Pedigo P-2055 only when it is safe and available.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported the Pedigo P-2055 blanket warmer was powered on but blankets remained cold."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet power and correct temperature setting; unit failed to raise cabinet temperature during functional testing."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed blanket warmer from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation for suspected internal heating circuit failure."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Outlet tested
  • Power behavior
  • Power cord and plug inspected
  • Power switch status
  • Temperature setpoint
  • Displayed temperature
  • Independent thermometer reading
  • Door gasket condition
  • Cabinet load condition
  • Whether vents or airflow paths were blocked
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Unusual smell, sound, heat, or indicator behavior
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A blanket warmer that is not heating should be approached logically: confirm safety, verify power and settings, check the door and loading condition, then independently confirm cabinet temperature. If the unit still will not heat, remove it from service and document the failure clearly so repair can begin with useful information.

That is successful troubleshooting.
