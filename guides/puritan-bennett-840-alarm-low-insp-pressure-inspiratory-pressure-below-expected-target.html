Puritan Bennett 840

Alarm: LOW INSP PRESSURE Inspiratory pressure below expected target

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Puritan Bennett

Model

840

What This Guide Helps With

LOW INSP PRESSURE alarm indicating insufficient inspiratory pressure, resulting in inadequate ventilation or failure to reach set tidal volume.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Assess the patient immediately
  • Provide alternate ventilation if needed (BVM or backup ventilator)
  • Confirm adequate oxygenation and ventilation

2. Verify the Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm LOW INSP PRESSURE is displayed
  • Observe reduced tidal volume delivery
  • Check if alarm occurs consistently during inspiration

3. Check Patient Condition

  • Assess airway patency
  • Confirm proper airway placement
  • Evaluate for changes in lung compliance

4. Check Circuit Connections

  • Inspect full circuit from ventilator to patient
  • Ensure all connections are tight
  • Look for loose or partially disconnected fittings

5. Check for Leaks

  • Inspect tubing, humidifier, and filters
  • Listen for audible air leaks
  • Check cuff inflation if applicable

6. Verify Ventilator Settings

  • Confirm target tidal volume or pressure settings
  • Check inspiratory time and flow settings
  • Ensure correct ventilation mode is selected

7. Inspect Sensors and Circuit Components

  • Check flow sensor placement and condition
  • Inspect for moisture or condensation
  • Drain any water traps

8. Run Extended Self-Test (EST)

  • Remove device from patient use
  • Run EST per facility protocol
  • Review results for system faults

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and the alarm continues, internal pressure regulation, sensors, or control systems may be failing. Remove the ventilator from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a ventilator while actively supporting a patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before performing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator alarming LOW INSP PRESSURE with reduced tidal volume during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Leak identified in patient circuit preventing adequate inspiratory pressure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated circuit connections, eliminated leak, verified normal pressure delivery and alarm cleared."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

LOW INSP PRESSURE alarms are most commonly caused by leaks, patient condition changes, or incorrect settings. Always rule out external causes first and escalate only after confirming system integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
