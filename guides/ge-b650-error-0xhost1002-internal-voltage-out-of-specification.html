GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B650

Error: 0xHOST1002 Internal voltage out of specification

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B650

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0xHOST1002 indicating internal voltage instability causing monitor shutdowns, alarms, or unreliable patient monitoring performance.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use if unstable, alarming, or shutting down
  • Transfer monitoring to a known working device
  • Confirm continuous patient monitoring on alternate equipment

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the monitor and confirm Error: 0xHOST1002 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup vs during operation)
  • Note symptoms such as reboots, display flicker, alarms, or shutdowns

3. Check External Power Source

  • Verify connection to a known-good hospital-grade outlet
  • Test the outlet with another device
  • Plug directly into the wall (avoid power strips or extension cords)

4. Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Check power cord for damage such as cuts or frays
  • Ensure firm connection at both the outlet and monitor
  • Swap with a known-good power cord if available

5. Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Power OFF the monitor
  • Disconnect AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart

6. Check Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure vents are not blocked
  • Verify adequate airflow around the device
  • Confirm normal room temperature conditions

7. Review Hardware Voltage Status via Webmin

  • Log into Webmin
  • Navigate to Diagnostics > Hardware Stats
  • Check for voltage rails outside specification

8. Isolate the Source of the Issue

  • If power source or cabling issues are found, address external cause
  • If voltage faults persist in Webmin, suspect internal failure

9. Stop When Resolved

  • If the error clears after corrections, return device to service
  • Verify stable operation with no recurring faults

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0xHOST1002 remains after all external checks, external causes have been ruled out and the issue is likely internal.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before addressing voltage-related issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor displaying Error 0xHOST1002 with intermittent rebooting and alarm activity during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal voltage out of specification confirmed via Webmin Hardware Stats; external power and cabling verified good."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for further evaluation and repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Voltage-related errors should be taken seriously. Start with simple external checks, but recognize that persistent faults indicate internal failure requiring escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
