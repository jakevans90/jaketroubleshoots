Arjo Maxi Sky Series

Lift not moving along track

Asset Type

Patient Lift

Manufacturer

Arjo

Model

Maxi Sky Series

What This Guide Helps With

Lift not traveling along ceiling track due to power, control, obstruction, or track-related issues affecting horizontal movement.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the lift from active patient use before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient safely transferred to another device or surface.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to move the lift along the track using the hand control.
  • Expected: No movement, intermittent movement, or abnormal stopping.
  • Why it matters: Confirms true travel issue vs user misunderstanding.

Check Emergency Stop (E-Stop)

  • Ensure the E-stop button is not engaged. Reset if needed.
  • Expected: Full system power restored and controls responsive.
  • Why it matters: E-stop disables all powered functions, including track movement.

If lift moves → stop troubleshooting.

Verify Battery Charge and Seating

  • Check battery level and ensure it is fully seated. Swap with a known good battery if available.
  • Expected: Adequate charge and stable power.
  • Why it matters: Track drive motor requires sufficient power; low voltage can prevent movement.

If movement returns → stop troubleshooting.

Test Hand Control Functions

  • Confirm other functions (lift up/down) work properly using the same control.
  • Expected: Vertical lift functions operate normally.
  • Why it matters: Helps isolate whether the issue is specific to track drive vs full control failure.

If no functions work → troubleshoot hand control/power instead.

Inspect Track for Obstructions

  • Visually inspect the entire track path for debris, foreign objects, or physical blockage.
  • Expected: Clear, unobstructed track.
  • Why it matters: Even small obstructions can prevent trolley movement.

If obstruction found and removed → retest and stop if resolved.

Check for Track Misalignment or Damage

  • Look for bends, gaps, or misaligned sections in the ceiling track.
  • Expected: Smooth, continuous track with no visible damage.
  • Why it matters: Misalignment can bind the trolley and stop movement.

If damage is found → remove from service.

Inspect Trolley Wheels and Movement

  • Manually attempt slight movement (if safe and unloaded) to feel for binding or resistance.
  • Expected: Smooth, free movement along track.
  • Why it matters: Binding may indicate wheel or internal trolley issue.

If binding is present → likely mechanical issue.

Check Track Charging/Power Rail (if applicable)

  • Ensure charging rails or power contacts are clean and properly aligned.
  • Expected: Clean contacts with no debris or corrosion.
  • Why it matters: Some Maxi Sky units rely on track power for operation.

If dirty → clean and retest.

Cycle Power to the Unit

  • Turn the unit OFF, wait 10 seconds, then power ON.
  • Expected: System resets and resumes normal operation.
  • Why it matters: Clears minor control or communication faults.

If movement returns → stop troubleshooting.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (track motor, drive assembly, or control system failure).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a ceiling lift while a patient is suspended. Always transfer the patient safely before evaluating movement issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Lift would not travel along ceiling track when using hand control."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"No external obstruction found; suspected internal track drive motor failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed lift from service and sent to vendor for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery level verified or swapped
  • Hand control functionality confirmed
  • Track inspected for obstruction or damage
  • Trolley movement (free/binding) noted
  • Indicator lights or alarms observed
  • Charging rail/contact condition checked
  • Final device status (in service vs out of service)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple, visible checks before assuming internal failure. Ceiling lift movement issues are often caused by power or track conditions, but recognizing when escalation is needed is just as important. Proper documentation supports both safety and long-term reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
