GE Healthcare MAC VU360

No Battery Backup

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

MAC VU360

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a MAC VU360 that will not operate on battery power or loses power when unplugged from AC.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the MAC VU360 is not being used for an active patient ECG.

If the device loses power when unplugged, do not attempt patient testing until battery backup is confirmed reliable.

Expected outcome: Patient testing is not interrupted by sudden device shutdown.

Confirm the Reported Battery Backup Failure

Ask staff what occurred and when it occurred.

Determine whether the unit:

  • Shuts off immediately when unplugged
  • Runs only briefly on battery
  • Shows a low battery warning
  • Does not appear to charge
  • Shows abnormal battery LED behavior
  • Fails battery diagnostics or checkout testing

Expected outcome: The failure mode is clearly identified before parts are replaced.

Verify AC Power First

Plug the MAC VU360 into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.

Confirm the power cord is fully seated at the device and wall outlet.

Check that the unit powers normally on AC.

Expected outcome: The device operates normally on AC power before battery backup is evaluated.

If the unit does not power on with AC, troubleshoot AC power or power supply concerns first.

Check Battery Charge Status

Leave the device connected to AC power long enough to allow charging.

Observe the keyboard battery LED.

Useful LED indications include:

  • Flashing amber every two seconds: battery is charging
  • Flashing amber every one second: battery is critically low
  • Flashing amber every half-second: battery communication failure

Expected outcome: The battery LED provides a clear indication of charge, low battery, or communication status.

Inspect Battery Seating and Battery Door

Power the unit down safely.

Remove AC power only after confirming it is safe to do so.

Check that the battery is properly seated in its compartment.

Verify the battery door is installed correctly and fully latched.

Expected outcome: A loose battery, poor seating, or improperly installed battery door is ruled out.

If reseating restores battery function, document the correction and stop.

Reseat the Battery

Remove and reinstall the battery according to facility-approved procedures.

Reconnect AC power and observe whether the battery LED resumes normal charging behavior.

Expected outcome: The device recognizes the battery and begins charging normally.

If the LED continues to indicate communication failure, continue troubleshooting.

Check for Battery Communication Failure

If the keyboard battery LED flashes amber every half-second, treat this as a battery communication failure.

Reseat the battery again and inspect for obvious issues such as:

  • Battery not fully seated
  • Damaged battery contacts
  • Debris in the battery area
  • Loose or damaged battery holder
  • Battery door interference

Expected outcome: Any simple seating or contact issue is corrected before replacing parts.

Run Battery Metrics

Access the battery metrics available through the device service or diagnostic workflow.

Review the reported battery condition, charge level, and any battery-related fault information.

Expected outcome: Battery condition is verified using device-reported data rather than guesswork.

If the battery metrics show poor battery condition, failed communication, or abnormal battery status, replace or escalate as appropriate.

Perform the Quick Battery Charge and Discharge Test

Run the quick battery charge and discharge functional checkout test.

Confirm the battery can charge, support operation, and discharge as expected.

Expected outcome: The battery passes the functional checkout and supports short battery operation.

If the device fails the quick charge/discharge test, do not return it to service.

Test Battery Operation After Charging

After charging, power the device on while connected to AC.

Disconnect AC power briefly in a controlled test area.

Confirm the unit remains powered on without rebooting, shutting down, or displaying a critical battery condition.

Expected outcome: The MAC VU360 continues operating on battery backup when AC is removed.

If the device remains powered on and battery status is acceptable, return it to service after documenting the results.

Replace the Battery if Diagnostics Fail

If the battery fails metrics, fails the quick charge/discharge test, will not hold charge, or continues to show communication failure after reseating, replace the battery per facility process.

After replacement, repeat the battery metrics and quick battery charge/discharge test.

Expected outcome: The replacement battery is recognized, charges correctly, and supports battery operation.

Inspect Battery Holder and External Power Path

If a known-good or replacement battery still does not work, inspect the battery holder area and accessible power path for obvious damage.

Look for damaged contacts, loose holder components, unusual heat, smell, or signs of liquid intrusion.

Expected outcome: External and easily visible battery connection issues are identified before deeper repair escalation.

Do not perform internal board-level troubleshooting during basic Clinical Engineering response unless authorized and trained.

If the Problem Persists

If AC power, battery seating, battery door installation, charge status, LED behavior, battery metrics, and the quick battery charge/discharge test have been checked and the MAC VU360 still has no battery backup, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve the battery, battery communication path, battery holder, charging circuit, or internal power management.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A MAC VU360 that cannot maintain battery backup should not be returned to clinical use until battery operation is verified.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot battery backup during active patient testing. If the MAC VU360 is needed immediately, move the patient workflow to another ECG machine first. Battery backup is a patient care reliability issue because loss of AC power can interrupt acquisition, printing, or transmission.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported the GE MAC VU360 shut off immediately when unplugged from AC power and would not operate on battery backup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery LED showed abnormal battery status and the unit failed the quick battery charge/discharge test after battery reseating."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the MAC VU360 from service, labeled it Out of Service, replaced the battery for evaluation, and repeated battery diagnostics before return-to-service decision."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • AC adapter or power cord condition
  • Battery LED flash pattern observed
  • Battery charging indication
  • Battery critically low indication
  • Battery communication failure indication
  • Battery reseated
  • Battery door verified closed
  • Battery metrics results
  • Quick battery charge/discharge test result
  • Battery replaced, if applicable
  • Any unusual heat, smell, or liquid intrusion
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery backup problems should be handled logically and safely. Confirm AC power first, then verify battery seating, battery door condition, LED behavior, diagnostics, and functional checkout results. If the battery or charging path cannot be verified, remove the MAC VU360 from service and document the CCR clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
