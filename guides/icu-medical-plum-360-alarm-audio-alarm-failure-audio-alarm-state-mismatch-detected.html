ICU Medical Plum 360

Alarm: Audio alarm failure. Audio alarm state mismatch detected

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Audio alarm failure where the pump detects a mismatch in alarm state, resulting in loss or improper function of audible alarms. This creates a significant safety risk due to missed critical alerts.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use immediately if audible alarms are not functioning
  • Transfer infusion to a verified working pump

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm the alarm message is displayed
  • Check for lack of audible alarms during alarm conditions
  • Observe if visual indicators are active without sound
  • Determine if issue occurs at startup or during infusion

Check Alarm Volume and Mute Settings

  • Ensure alarm volume is set above minimum
  • Confirm alarm is not muted or silenced
  • Check for active silence timers

Perform a Functional Alarm Test

  • Trigger a known alarm condition (e.g., open door or simulate occlusion off-patient)
  • Verify whether an audible alarm sounds

Power Cycle the Pump

  • Turn the pump off completely
  • Wait several seconds
  • Power the pump back on

Re-evaluate Alarm Function After Restart

  • Repeat alarm test
  • Confirm whether audible alarm function is restored
  • Check if the alarm message returns

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after power cycling and external checks:

  • External and configuration causes have been ruled out
  • The issue indicates an internal audio system or control failure

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replacement

Important: Audible alarm failures are safety-critical and must not be used clinically.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or test alarm functionality on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before testing alarms.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Audio alarm failure displayed; pump alarms visually but no audible sound."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Alarm state mismatch persisted after power cycle; likely internal audio circuit or control fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair/replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Audible alarms are a critical safety feature in infusion devices. If an alarm system reports a failure, it should be treated as a high-priority issue and escalated after basic external checks are completed.

That is successful troubleshooting.
