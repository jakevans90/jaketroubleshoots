Stryker S3

Error: 204 Foot Right Load Cell

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Error 204 Foot Right Load Cell, indicating an unreliable foot-right load-cell signal affecting Scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness functions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the bed from patient use if the Scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness function is needed for safe patient care.

Do not rely on patient weight readings, Bed Exit monitoring, or iBED Awareness status while this error is present. Transfer the patient to a known-working bed or provide alternate fall-prevention and monitoring measures before troubleshooting.

  • Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on affected bed safety functions.
  • Why it matters: Error 204 indicates the bed cannot reliably interpret the foot-right load-cell signal, which may compromise weight measurement and monitoring functions.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

Confirm that the bed displays Error 204 Call Service and document whether the condition occurs at startup, during scale use, when activating Bed Exit, or while iBED Awareness is enabled.

Enter the appropriate service or Maintenance Menu area and review the error information and load-cell diagnostics, when available. A foot-right load-cell fault may be identified as Load Cell Error with FR displayed beneath it.

Observe whether the Scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness functions are disabled, inaccurate, or unavailable.

  • Expected outcome: Error 204 is confirmed, with the fault associated with the foot-right load-cell position.
  • Why it matters: Confirming the reported error prevents replacing parts based only on a general Call Service message.

3. Inspect the Bed for External Mechanical Interference

With the bed safely removed from patient use, inspect the foot-right corner of the bed and the weighing structure for anything that could affect load-cell movement or loading.

  • Linens, straps, cables, or accessories caught between moving or weighing surfaces.
  • Contact between the bed frame and nearby equipment, walls, cords, or floor obstructions.
  • Debris, contamination, bent components, or visible binding near the foot-right load-cell area.
  • An uneven or unstable bed position that could affect scale readings.

Correct any interference found, then recheck the error and scale diagnostic status.

  • Expected outcome: The foot-right weighing area is free of contact, binding, contamination, or external load interference.
  • Why it matters: A load cell can appear failed when the bed structure is mechanically restricted or improperly loaded.

4. Inspect Accessible Foot-Right Load-Cell Wiring and Connections

Inspect accessible wiring and connectors associated with the foot-right load-cell path.

  • Loose or partially seated connectors.
  • Pinched, stretched, cut, or damaged wiring.
  • Evidence of fluid intrusion or corrosion.
  • Harness routing that allows the cable to bind during bed movement.
  • Visible damage near the foot-right load-cell assembly.

Reseat accessible connections as appropriate and secure any incorrectly routed wiring.

  • Expected outcome: Wiring and accessible connections are intact, properly seated, and free of damage or contamination.
  • Why it matters: An open, damaged, or unstable connection can produce an under-range or over-range load-cell condition.

5. Recheck Load-Cell Diagnostics

After correcting any external interference or connection issue, run or view the load-cell diagnostic information again.

Confirm whether the FR fault remains present and compare the foot-right reading or status with the other load-cell positions, when the diagnostic display allows.

  • Expected outcome: The foot-right load-cell indication returns to normal if the issue was caused by an external or connection-related problem.
  • Why it matters: This step isolates whether the condition was corrected or remains specific to the foot-right load-cell circuit.

6. Verify Scale System Calibration and Function if the Error Clears

If the Error 204 condition clears after correcting an external issue or after repair of the indicated component, perform the applicable scale calibration procedure according to approved service instructions.

After calibration:

  • Verify the bed scale produces stable and reasonable readings using an appropriate known test weight.
  • Verify Bed Exit operation as applicable.
  • Verify iBED Awareness operation as applicable.
  • Confirm no related error returns during functional testing.
  • Expected outcome: The scale system calibrates successfully, associated monitoring functions operate normally, and Error 204 does not return.
  • Why it matters: A repaired load-cell fault must be followed by verification of all affected patient-monitoring functions.

7. Stop When Resolved

If interference correction, connection repair, or repair of the identified foot-right load-cell component resolves the error and all required functional checks pass, document the repair and return the bed to service.

Resolved condition: No active Error 204, stable scale operation, and verified Bed Exit and iBED Awareness function as equipped.

If the Problem Persists

If Error 204 Call Service remains after external interference, physical condition, accessible wiring, and connector checks have been completed, external causes have been ruled out and the fault may indicate a failed foot-right load cell or an internal scale-system electronics issue.

Remove the bed from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. The affected load cell may require replacement, followed by scale calibration and functional verification of the Scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness systems.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A bed with an unresolved load-cell fault should not be returned to clinical use when its weight or patient-monitoring functions may be relied upon.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first, or establish approved alternate monitoring and fall-prevention measures before service activity involving the Scale, Bed Exit, or iBED Awareness systems.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displayed Error 204 Call Service. Load-cell diagnostics identified the foot-right position as FR Load Cell Error. Scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness functions were considered unreliable while the fault was active."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Foot-right load-cell fault remained after checking for frame interference, binding, contamination, and accessible wiring or connector issues, indicating a likely failed foot-right load cell or associated scale-system component."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and labeled Out of Service for repair of the foot-right load-cell system. Scale calibration and verification of Scale, Bed Exit, and iBED Awareness operation are required before return to clinical use."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error 204 should be treated as more than a scale-reading problem because it can affect patient-monitoring functions used for safety. Begin with patient protection, verify the fault, rule out external interference and connection issues, and escalate appropriately when the foot-right load-cell error remains. Complete documentation ensures the clinical impact and repair status are clear.

That is successful troubleshooting.
