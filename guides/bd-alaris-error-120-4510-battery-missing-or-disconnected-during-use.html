BD Alaris

Error: 120.4510 Battery missing or disconnected during use

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 120.4510 indicating battery missing or disconnected, causing loss of backup power and risk of pump shutdown when unplugged.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the pump is in use, immediately connect to AC power or transfer infusion to another pump
  • Ensure therapy continues without interruption

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm Error 120.4510 is displayed
  • Check if error appears at startup or during infusion
  • Observe if pump alarms or shuts down when unplugged
  • Verify operation while on AC power

3. Confirm AC Power Function

  • Plug into a known working outlet
  • Verify AC indicator light is on
  • Confirm pump powers normally on AC

4. Check Battery Presence and Seating

  • Remove battery from rear compartment
  • Inspect proper orientation and full insertion
  • Ensure latch or door is fully secured
  • Reinstall firmly and power cycle

5. Inspect Battery Contacts and Compartment

  • Check for clean, intact contacts
  • Look for corrosion or debris
  • Ensure pins are not bent or recessed

6. Test with a Known Good Battery

  • Install a verified working battery
  • Power on the pump
  • Disconnect AC power briefly

7. Check for Battery Recognition Behavior

  • Observe battery icon and level on display
  • Confirm charging indicator is active when plugged in

8. Evaluate for Internal Failure

  • If battery is not recognized after replacement
  • If error persists
  • If pump shuts off when unplugged
  • Suspect internal battery interface or charging circuit failure

If the Problem Persists

After confirming battery and external conditions:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or PC unit evaluation

Persistent battery detection errors strongly indicate internal failure.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Alaris pump displaying Error 120.4510 and powers off when unplugged."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery not recognized after reseating and replacement with known good battery, indicating internal power or battery interface failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified AC power, reseated battery, installed known good battery. Error persisted. Removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery-related errors directly impact infusion continuity and patient safety. Always verify simple external causes first, but escalate quickly when backup power is unreliable.

That is successful troubleshooting.
