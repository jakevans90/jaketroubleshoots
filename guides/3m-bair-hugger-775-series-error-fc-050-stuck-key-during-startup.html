3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-050 Stuck key during startup

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-050 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series patient warming system. FC-050 indicates a stuck key during startup, which can prevent normal startup or reliable operation of the user interface.

A stuck key or defective user interface may affect temperature selection, mode control, alarm response, startup behavior, or safe operation of the warmer.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the warmer from patient use if Error FC-050 appears during startup or before use.
  • Do not use the warming unit on a patient with an active startup error.
  • Provide another warming unit if patient warming is needed.
  • Allow the unit to cool if it was recently in use.
  • Keep the hose and blanket setup away from the patient until the unit is confirmed safe.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on and observe the startup sequence.
  • Confirm the displayed error is FC-050.
  • Check whether the error occurs every startup or only intermittently.
  • Look for associated symptoms, such as buttons not responding, a button appearing permanently pressed, the unit failing to complete startup, temperature settings not changing, or erratic control panel behavior.
  • Document when the error appears and whether it can be reproduced.

Check for Obvious External Causes

  • Inspect the control panel before assuming internal failure.
  • Look for visible damage to the user interface.
  • Check for cracked plastic, punctures, dents, or separated overlay material.
  • Inspect for fluid residue, cleaning chemical buildup, tape residue, or contamination around the buttons.
  • Make sure no object is pressing against the keypad during startup.
  • Confirm the unit is sitting normally and is not wedged against a bed, cart, wall, or accessory.

Clean and Recheck the User Interface Surface

  • Power the unit off and unplug it.
  • Clean the control panel surface according to facility policy and manufacturer-safe cleaning practices.
  • Do not soak the panel or force liquid into the keypad edges.
  • Allow the surface to dry completely.
  • Restart the unit and check whether Error FC-050 returns.

Check for Physical Keypad Response

  • With the unit powered off, gently press each user interface button.
  • Check whether any key feels stuck, collapsed, unusually firm, unusually soft, or does not rebound.
  • Compare the feel of all buttons across the panel.
  • Do not pry, puncture, or disassemble the user interface during basic troubleshooting.

Power Cycle and Recheck

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Unplug the power cord.
  • Wait briefly.
  • Reconnect the unit to a known good outlet.
  • Power the unit on and observe the startup sequence.
  • If the error clears, complete functional verification before returning the unit to service.

Isolate the Fault

  • If Error FC-050 returns after cleaning, inspection, and restart, treat the issue as device-related.
  • Confirm the error is not caused by an external object pressing the keypad.
  • Confirm the control panel is clean and dry.
  • Confirm the error repeats on startup.
  • Do not continue cycling the unit repeatedly.
  • Remove the unit from service for repair.

Repair Action

  • Replace the defective user interface per approved service procedure.
  • Perform required calibration after repair.
  • Perform operational verification before returning the unit to service.
  • Confirm Error FC-050 clears and keypad function is normal.

If the Problem Persists

If Error FC-050 remains after the user interface is replaced and calibration is completed, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Keep the unit removed from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for further bench evaluation or vendor repair.

Do not return the warmer to patient use with unresolved startup errors. Persistent FC-050 after user interface replacement may indicate a deeper control, connection, or internal electronic issue.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first.

A patient warmer with an active startup error should not be used for patient temperature management until the fault has been resolved, calibration has been completed if required, and operational verification has passed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 Series patient warming unit reported with Error FC-050 during startup. Unit would not reliably complete startup due to stuck key/user interface fault."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified Error FC-050 on startup. External causes checked, including control panel contamination, physical obstruction, keypad damage, and power source. Error persisted, indicating defective user interface."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and labeled Out of Service. Replaced user interface, performed calibration, and completed operational verification. Unit returned to service after FC-050 cleared and keypad function passed."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error FC-050 should be treated as a startup control issue that can affect safe operation. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact error, check for simple external causes, and avoid assuming internal failure until the keypad surface and environment are ruled out.

If the error persists, user interface replacement and calibration are appropriate. Clear documentation helps show that the device was evaluated logically and safely.

That is successful troubleshooting.
