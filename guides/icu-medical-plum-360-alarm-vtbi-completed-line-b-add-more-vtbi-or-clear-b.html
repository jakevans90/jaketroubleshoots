ICU Medical Plum 360

Alarm: VTBI Completed Line B! Add more VTBI or Clear B.

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

VTBI Completed Line B alarm indicating programmed volume finished, stopping infusion on Line B and requiring user action to continue or clear.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Assess the patient and confirm therapy status
  • Transition to a backup infusion method if continuous therapy is required
  • Verify ordered therapy is not interrupted

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm alarm message: “VTBI Completed Line B! Add more VTBI or Clear B.”
  • Check if Line B infusion has stopped
  • Determine if the alarm occurred at expected completion
  • Check for any additional alarms

Confirm VTBI Settings and Infusion Completion

  • Review programmed VTBI for Line B
  • Verify VTBI has reached zero
  • Check delivered volume against programmed amount
  • Confirm therapy order matches delivered volume

Determine Next Clinical Action

  • If continuing therapy: enter new VTBI and restart infusion
  • If therapy is complete: stop Line B and clear the program
  • Open cassette door if needed to reset programming

Check for Unintended Early Completion

  • Verify correct VTBI was originally programmed
  • Check infusion rate settings
  • Ensure no incorrect configuration caused rapid delivery

Return Device to Service

  • Confirm normal pump operation after action is taken
  • Ensure no recurring alarms are present
  • Verify infusion resumes correctly if restarted

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm occurs repeatedly without VTBI being reached or behaves inconsistently:

  • External and programming causes have been ruled out
  • This may indicate a sensing or software issue
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before adjusting VTBI or restarting infusion.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump alarming 'VTBI Completed Line B! Add more VTBI or Clear B,' stopping infusion."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"VTBI for Line B reached programmed limit, indicating normal infusion completion."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified VTBI completion, entered new VTBI per clinical instruction, and confirmed normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Not all alarms indicate failure—some reflect normal therapy completion. Accurate interpretation prevents unnecessary troubleshooting while ensuring safe continuation of care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
