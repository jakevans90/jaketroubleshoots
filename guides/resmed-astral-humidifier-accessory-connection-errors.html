ResMed Astral Ventilator

Humidifier or Accessory Connection Errors

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

ResMed

Model

Astral

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting humidifier or accessory connection errors on a ResMed Astral ventilator. Symptoms may include alarms or messages indicating “humidifier not detected,” “accessory connection error,” or inconsistent device operation related to attached accessories. The focus is on external and verifiable checks before assuming internal device failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify device power and readiness

  • Ensure the ventilator is powered on and battery or mains supply is stable.
  • A low battery or intermittent power can trigger accessory errors.

Inspect accessory connection points

  • Check that the humidifier and any connected tubing are fully seated in their ports.
  • Look for misalignment, loose fittings, or obstruction at connectors.

Examine accessories for damage

  • Inspect the humidifier chamber, tubing, and water chamber for cracks, leaks, or residue.
  • Verify that no water or condensation has entered electrical connection points.

Check for correct accessory type

  • Confirm that the connected humidifier is compatible with the Astral model.
  • Incompatible devices may cause detection errors.

Clean connection contacts

  • Power off the ventilator.
  • Wipe electrical contacts on both the ventilator and accessory with a lint-free cloth.
  • Ensure no residue or corrosion is present.

Reattach accessory and power cycle

  • Reconnect the humidifier or accessory.
  • Power off, wait 10 seconds, and power on to allow device detection routines to run.

Swap with known good accessory if available

  • If the error persists, connect a different verified humidifier or accessory.
  • This isolates whether the problem is with the device or the accessory.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out.

The ventilator should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation by qualified personnel

Clinical Engineering should not attempt internal board-level repair.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot accessories while a patient is actively on the ventilator.
  • Move the patient to an alternate ventilator if needed before continuing checks.
  • Always confirm the accessory is fully compatible to prevent patient safety risks.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Ventilator displays humidifier connection error; alarm triggered intermittently.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed humidifier not fully seated; minor residue on connection contacts.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Cleaned contacts, reattached humidifier, powered device off/on; error cleared.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet and power supply verified
  • Device and accessory contacts cleaned
  • Accessories swapped or tested
  • Alarm behavior noted
  • No unusual heat, smell, or sounds detected
  • Final device status: Operating normally with accessory connected

Final Thought

Patient safety and proper documentation are paramount. Logical troubleshooting from external checks to accessory verification minimizes unnecessary repairs and ensures reliable device operation. Escalation to service should occur only when external causes are eliminated.

That is successful troubleshooting.
