CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series

Power Output Faults or Generator Error Codes

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

CONMED

Model

System 2450 / 5000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a CONMED System 2450 / 5000 ESU when the generator displays error codes, shows power output faults, or fails to deliver energy consistently. The guide focuses first on external and easily verifiable issues before assuming internal generator or circuit board failure, ensuring patient safety and proper escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the ESU is connected to a properly rated electrical outlet.
  • Check for tripped circuit breakers or blown fuses.
  • Use a known-good outlet or power meter to confirm adequate voltage.

Why: Inconsistent power can trigger generator fault codes.

Check Power Cord and Connections

  • Inspect the main power cord for cuts, frays, or bent pins.
  • Confirm secure connections at both the wall outlet and ESU.

Why: Loose or damaged cords can cause intermittent faults.

Examine Footswitch and Handpiece Connections

  • Disconnect and reconnect the footswitch and handpiece cables.
  • Swap with known-good accessories if available.

Why: Faulty accessories can trigger output errors or prevent energy delivery.

Inspect Electrodes and Patient Return Electrode (Pad)

  • Ensure the correct electrode type is installed and compatible.
  • Check the patient return electrode for proper placement and intact connection.
  • Replace pads if adhesive or conductive gel is compromised.

Why: Poor contact increases impedance, leading to generator error codes.

Review Settings and Mode Selection

  • Confirm the ESU is in the correct mode (cut, coag, blend, etc.) for the procedure.
  • Reset any custom presets or safety lockouts that may prevent output.

Why: Incorrect mode selection can simulate a power fault.

Check Display for Specific Error Codes

  • Note the exact error code(s) displayed.
  • Reference the manufacturer’s external troubleshooting chart (do not attempt internal repair).

Why: Error codes help determine if escalation is needed.

Perform Basic Functional Test (if safe)

  • With the ESU powered on and no patient connected, verify that the unit passes self-test routines or outputs low-level energy to a dummy load (per manufacturer safe procedure).

Why: Confirms if the issue is isolated to the generator or external connections.

If the Problem Persists

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label it “Out of Service.”
  • Escalate to authorized CONMED repair or internal service bench evaluation.
  • Avoid any internal board-level inspection or component replacement.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and ensures safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt troubleshooting on an active patient.
  • Move the patient to a known-working ESU before further investigation.
  • Only perform safe functional checks or accessory swaps on the device while it is unconnected from a patient.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“OR staff reported that the ESU displayed “Output Fault” and would not deliver energy during surgery.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Inspected power source, cords, footswitch, handpiece, electrodes, and settings; all appeared normal. Generator error code persisted.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Unit removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for authorized repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Verified outlet voltage and power cord integrity.
  • Footswitch and handpiece functionality tested/swapped.
  • Patient return electrode checked/replaced.
  • Error codes noted exactly as displayed.
  • Self-test and dummy load checks performed (if safe).

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Use logical, stepwise troubleshooting and avoid internal disassembly. Proper documentation ensures traceability and helps reduce repeat service calls. Escalate promptly when external causes have been ruled out.

That is successful troubleshooting.
