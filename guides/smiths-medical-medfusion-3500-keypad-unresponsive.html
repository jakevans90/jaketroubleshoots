Smiths Medical Medfusion 3500

Keypad Unresponsive

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Medfusion 3500

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a Medfusion 3500 syringe infusion pump when the physical keypad does not respond to user input.

Common symptoms may include:

  • Buttons not responding when pressed
  • Delayed or intermittent response to input
  • Certain keys functioning while others do not

The goal is to rule out simple causes such as lockout modes, contamination, or system freezes before assuming internal keypad failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Confirm the Pump Is Powered and Fully Booted

  • Verify the pump is powered on and the display is active.
  • Check that the screen shows the normal interface.
  • If the pump appears frozen, the keypad may seem unresponsive.
  • If frozen, proceed to the power cycle step.

2. Check for Keypad Lockout Mode

  • The pump may be placed in a locked state during clinical use.
  • Look for a lock icon or message indicating controls are locked.
  • Attempt the standard unlock sequence used by your facility.
  • A locked keypad will appear nonfunctional even though hardware is fine.

3. Inspect the Keypad Surface

  • Visually inspect the keypad for residue from cleaning wipes or medications.
  • Check for sticky keys or debris between buttons.
  • Inspect for physical damage to the keys.
  • Clean any contamination with an approved hospital disinfectant wipe and dry thoroughly.

4. Check for Physical Obstruction

  • Verify that no external items interfere with button operation (misaligned labels, tape, debris).
  • Remove any obstruction and retest.

5. Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Turn the pump off.
  • Disconnect AC power.
  • Wait ~30 seconds.
  • Reconnect power and restart the pump.
  • This can reset the internal control system and resolve temporary freezes.

6. Test All Keys

  • After restarting, press each key individually.
  • Note if certain keys respond while others do not.
  • Partial response may indicate failing keypad contacts.

7. Verify No Active Alarm Blocks Input

  • Some alarms or prompts require acknowledgment before other keys function.
  • Check for alarm messages or confirmation prompts.
  • Clear any pending prompts and retest.

8. Check for Signs of Internal Damage

  • If keys remain unresponsive, look for signs of wear, damage, or repeated malfunction.
  • This may indicate an internal keypad interface failure.

If the Problem Persists

If the keypad remains unresponsive after cleaning, power cycling, and confirming no lockout or alarm blocking input:

  • External causes are ruled out
  • Likely internal keypad failure
  • Action:
    • Remove the pump from service
    • Label Out of Service
    • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop and escalate is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot keypad issues while the pump is actively infusing a patient. Transfer the patient to a functioning pump first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Nursing reported the Medfusion 3500 keypad would not respond during programming.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
During testing, keypad buttons did not register input after cleaning and power cycling.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Pump removed from service and sent to Clinical Engineering for evaluation and repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Power outlet tested
  • AC adapter verified
  • Pump successfully powered on
  • Keypad cleaned and inspected
  • Power cycle performed
  • Response tested for all keys
  • Any visible keypad damage noted
  • Final device status (in service or removed)

Final Thought

Keypad failures are often external initially (lockout, debris, sticky keys), but persistent unresponsiveness usually indicates internal hardware issues. Following a logical, stepwise approach and escalating when appropriate ensures patient safety and proper documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
