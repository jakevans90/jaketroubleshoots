Spacelabs Healthcare Qube

NIBP Measurement Errors or Failures

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Spacelabs Healthcare

Model

Qube

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a Spacelabs Qube patient monitor experiencing non-invasive blood pressure (NIBP) measurement errors or failures. Common signs include:

  • NIBP readings failing to display
  • Repeated “Cuff Error,” “Measurement Failed,” or similar alarms
  • Inconsistent or physiologically implausible readings

The guide emphasizes external and easily verifiable checks before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check the Power and Display

  • Confirm the monitor powers on and the display functions correctly.

Why: A low battery or intermittent power can prevent NIBP cycles from completing.

Verify Cuff Connection

  • Inspect the NIBP hose connection to the monitor and cuff. Ensure it is fully seated and not loose.
  • Check for kinks or sharp bends in the tubing.

Why: Poor connections or blocked lines can prevent proper inflation/deflation.

Inspect the Cuff

  • Examine the cuff for tears, worn Velcro, or leaks.
  • Confirm correct cuff size for the patient’s limb.

Why: Damaged cuffs or improper sizing can trigger measurement failures or inaccurate readings.

Check for Air Leaks

  • Inflate the cuff manually (if possible) and listen for escaping air.
  • Use a known good cuff on the same monitor to test functionality.

Why: Air leaks reduce pressure buildup, causing failed readings.

Monitor Placement and Patient Condition

  • Ensure the cuff is applied to the correct limb and at heart level.
  • Minimize patient movement during measurement.

Why: Motion and improper positioning can interfere with NIBP oscillometric measurements.

Run a Self-Test / NIBP Calibration (if available)

  • Some Qube monitors allow an internal NIBP self-test via the menu.

Why: Confirms whether the monitor can complete a cycle independently of patient factors.

If the Problem Persists

  • Remove the monitor from service
  • Label it Out of Service
  • Send it for bench evaluation or repair

Knowing when to stop and escalate is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not rely on a failing NIBP reading for patient care.

Use an alternative monitor or manual BP measurement until resolved.

Ensure patient safety by confirming accurate vital signs before making clinical decisions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Monitor shows ‘Cuff Error’ and fails to measure blood pressure.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Cuff tubing found kinked; self-test failed with original cuff. Alternative cuff worked normally.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Replaced damaged cuff. NIBP measurement successful with new cuff.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested
  • Battery level
  • Hose and cuff integrity
  • Alarm messages displayed
  • Self-test results
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Troubleshooting should prioritize patient safety, starting with simple, external checks. Documentation of each step provides a clear record for future reference, and escalation to repair ensures equipment reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
