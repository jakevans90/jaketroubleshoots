Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI

Alarm: Low Battery Caution

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo OMNI

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Low Battery Caution alarms on the Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI feeding pump when battery life is critically low but feeding or flushing may continue. This alarm should be treated as a time-sensitive power warning because therapy could stop if the battery fully discharges.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the pump is actively feeding or flushing a patient, do not ignore the alarm.
  • Confirm clinical staff is aware that approximately 15 minutes of battery life remains.
  • If continued therapy is needed, connect the pump to AC power immediately.
  • If AC power is not available or cannot be trusted, replace the pump with a known-good feeding pump.
  • Expected outcome: Feeding or flushing is maintained without relying on a nearly depleted battery.
  • Why it matters: The pump may continue operating during the caution alarm, but therapy could stop if the battery fully discharges.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Check the pump display and confirm the alarm is specifically Low Battery Caution.
  • Ask staff when the alarm appeared.
  • Check whether it occurred during active feeding, during flushing, while disconnected from wall power, shortly after being removed from charge, or while already plugged in.
  • Confirm whether the pump is still delivering fluid.
  • Expected outcome: The Low Battery Caution alarm is confirmed and the pump continues delivery if the alarm occurred during feeding or flushing.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a true low-battery condition instead of a different power, charging, or pump failure.

Connect the Pump to Wall Power

  • Plug the Kangaroo OMNI into a known-working wall outlet using the approved power adapter.
  • Confirm the adapter is connected to the pump’s power adapter port.
  • Allow the pump to begin charging.
  • Expected outcome: The pump recognizes external power and battery charging begins.
  • Why it matters: Low Battery Caution indicates the battery is nearly depleted and needs immediate charging.

Confirm the Outlet Is Working

  • If the pump does not appear to recognize AC power, test the wall outlet with another approved device or move the pump to a known-good outlet.
  • Avoid using loose outlets, damaged power strips, or questionable extension cords.
  • Expected outcome: The pump receives power from a confirmed working outlet.
  • Why it matters: A failed outlet can make the pump appear to have a charging or battery problem.

Inspect the Power Adapter Connection

  • Confirm the power adapter plug is fully inserted into the pump’s power adapter port.
  • Check for a loose connection, partially inserted plug, bent or damaged adapter tip, damaged power cord, strain at the connector, or contamination in the power port.
  • Reconnect the adapter firmly.
  • Expected outcome: The pump detects external power once the adapter is seated correctly.
  • Why it matters: A loose or partially inserted adapter can cause a Low Battery Caution alarm even when the pump appears to be plugged in.

Check the Power Adapter

  • Inspect the power adapter for visible damage, overheating, frayed cable sections, cracked housing, or an intermittent connection.
  • If available, try a known-good compatible power adapter.
  • Expected outcome: The pump charges normally with a verified power adapter.
  • Why it matters: A failed adapter can prevent charging and allow the battery to continue draining.

Charge the Pump Fully

  • Leave the pump connected to wall power for at least 12 hours to fully charge the battery.
  • Do not assume the battery is restored after only a short charging period.
  • Expected outcome: The battery charges sufficiently and the Low Battery Caution alarm clears.
  • Why it matters: This alarm appears when approximately 15 minutes of battery life remains, so a full recharge is needed before reliable battery use.

Verify Battery Operation After Charging

  • After the charge period, confirm the pump powers normally and operates without immediately returning to Low Battery Caution.
  • If facility procedure allows, briefly verify battery operation off AC power after the full charge.
  • Expected outcome: The pump operates normally and does not quickly return to Low Battery Caution.
  • Why it matters: A battery that rapidly returns to caution may no longer be holding charge properly.

Stop When Resolved

  • If the alarm clears after connecting to AC power, confirming the adapter connection, and completing the charge cycle, return the pump to service after basic functional verification.
  • Document the issue as a low-battery condition corrected by charging or power connection correction.

If the Problem Persists

If the Low Battery Caution alarm remains while connected to a known-good outlet and known-good power adapter, or if the alarm returns shortly after a full 12-hour charge, external and common charging causes have been ruled out.

Persistent Low Battery Caution may indicate a failed battery, charging circuit issue, power adapter port problem, or internal power management failure.

Remove the pump from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a Low Battery Caution alarm on an active patient while the pump is relying on battery power. Connect to AC power immediately or transfer therapy to another pump before the battery fully depletes.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI feeding pump displayed Low Battery Caution alarm during feeding. Staff reported pump was still delivering fluid but battery warning indicated low remaining runtime."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Pump battery was critically low. Power adapter connection and wall outlet were checked. Adapter plug was confirmed fully seated in the pump power adapter port. No external power damage noted."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Pump was connected to a known-good wall outlet and charged for at least 12 hours. Alarm cleared after charging. Pump passed basic power and operational verification and was returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

The Low Battery Caution alarm is a time-sensitive power warning, not an immediate pump-stop condition. Clinical Engineering should confirm the alarm, protect patient therapy, check the outlet and adapter connection, fully charge the pump, and escalate if the battery or charging system cannot be trusted. Clear CCR documentation supports safe return to service or appropriate repair escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
