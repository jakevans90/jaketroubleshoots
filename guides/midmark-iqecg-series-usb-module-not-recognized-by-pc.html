Midmark IQecg Series

USB Module Not Recognized by PC

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Midmark

Model

IQecg Series

What This Guide Helps With

EKG USB acquisition module not detected by PC due to connection, driver, power, or port-related external issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Disconnect the patient from the EKG system before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: No active patient connected.
  • Why it matters: Prevents inaccurate readings or electrical risk during troubleshooting.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Connect the USB module and open the IQecg software. Observe if the device is detected.
  • Expected: Module not listed or software shows no device.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true recognition issue versus user workflow error.

Check USB Connection at Both Ends

  • Inspect and reseat the USB cable at the module and PC. Ensure it is fully inserted.
  • Expected: Secure, firm connection.
  • Why it matters: Loose or partially connected USB cables are a common cause of detection failure.
  • If loose → reseat and retest.

Try a Different USB Port on the PC

  • Move the USB cable to another known working USB port.
  • Expected: Module is recognized on a different port.
  • Why it matters: USB ports can fail or be disabled.
  • If recognized → original port is faulty.

Test with a Known Good USB Cable

  • Replace the USB cable with a verified working cable of the same type.
  • Expected: Device becomes recognized.
  • Why it matters: Internal wire breaks in USB cables are common and not always visible.
  • If resolved → replace faulty cable.

Check PC Device Manager

  • Open Device Manager and look for:
  • Unknown USB device
  • Missing or unrecognized hardware
  • Expected: Device appears (even if flagged).
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the PC detects the hardware at a system level.
  • If not present → possible cable, port, or module failure.

Verify Driver Installation

  • Ensure Midmark IQecg drivers/software are properly installed and up to date.
  • Expected: No driver errors in Device Manager.
  • Why it matters: Missing or corrupted drivers prevent proper device recognition.
  • If drivers missing/corrupt → reinstall IQecg software and retest.

Restart the PC and Software

  • Restart the computer and relaunch IQecg software with the module connected.
  • Expected: Device recognized after reboot.
  • Why it matters: Clears USB communication faults and software conflicts.

Test Module on Another PC

  • Connect the USB module to a different PC with IQecg software installed.
  • Expected: Module is recognized on alternate system.
  • Why it matters: Isolates whether the issue is PC-related or module-related.
  • If works on another PC → original PC configuration issue.
  • If not → likely module failure.

Inspect Module for Physical Damage

  • Check the USB module and connector for bent pins, cracks, or damage.
  • Expected: No visible damage.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage can prevent proper communication.

If the Problem Persists

All common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal to the USB acquisition module or PC hardware interface.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or further bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and wasted troubleshooting time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an EKG system while connected to a patient. Always move the patient to a functioning device before beginning diagnostics.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"EKG USB module not recognized by PC; unable to acquire ECG."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed USB acquisition module not detected on multiple PCs and ports."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent for repair; replaced with working module."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • USB ports tested (multiple)
  • Cable swapped
  • Device Manager status
  • Driver reinstall performed
  • Tested on alternate PC
  • Physical condition of module
  • Final device status (in service / out of service)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple connection and configuration checks before assuming failure. Patient safety and proper escalation are just as important as fixing the issue. Clear documentation ensures continuity and supports repair decisions.

That is successful troubleshooting.
