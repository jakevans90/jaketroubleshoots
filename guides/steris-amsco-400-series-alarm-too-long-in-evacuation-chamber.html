STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Too Long In Evacuation - Chamber did not reach required vacuum level within allotted time

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: Too Long In Evacuation indicating failure to reach required vacuum level, impacting sterilization cycle completion and proper air removal.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove sterilizer from active use if alarm persists during a cycle
  • Do not rely on incomplete sterilization loads

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm “Too Long In Evacuation” alarm is displayed
  • Observe when it occurs during evacuation phase
  • Check if cycle pauses, extends, or attempts to continue

Check Water Supply

  • Verify water supply valve is open
  • Confirm facility water pressure is at least 30 PSIG

Inspect Chamber Drain Strainer

  • Locate and inspect the chamber drain strainer
  • Remove debris and clean thoroughly if clogged

Check Door Seal Function

  • Inspect door gasket for proper inflation and activation
  • Check for damage or wear
  • Verify seal steam and exhaust connections are intact

Inspect for External Leaks

  • Check chamber plumbing, hoses, and fittings for leaks
  • Inspect door interface sealing
  • Run leak test if available

Evaluate Valves (Non-Invasive Checks)

  • Listen for proper actuation of check valves and solenoid valves
  • Look for sticking, delayed response, or no actuation sound

Attempt Cycle Again

  • Restart sterilization cycle after corrections
  • Verify evacuation completes without alarm

If the Problem Persists

If alarm continues after external causes have been ruled out (water supply, drain, seal, leaks):

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move instruments and workflow to a properly functioning sterilizer before continuing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer displaying 'Too Long In Evacuation' alarm and unable to complete cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Chamber drain strainer partially clogged restricting evacuation flow."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned drain strainer, verified water pressure, and confirmed normal operation on test cycle."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Evacuation alarms directly impact sterilization effectiveness. Always verify water supply, drainage, and sealing before suspecting internal failures. Escalate appropriately when external causes are ruled out to maintain safety and reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
