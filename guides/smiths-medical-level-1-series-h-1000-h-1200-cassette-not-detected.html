Smiths Medical Level 1 Series H-1000 / H-1200

Cassette Not Detected

Asset Type

Fluid & Blood Warmer

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Level 1 Series H-1000 / H-1200

What This Guide Helps With

Cassette not recognized or detected errors, often caused by improper installation, damaged disposables, or sensor/interface issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use.
  • Transition to an alternate warming method before troubleshooting.

Verify Alarm / Message

  • Confirm the unit is displaying a “cassette not detected” or similar error.
  • Expected: Unit will not allow heating without cassette recognition.

Inspect Cassette Installation

  • Open and reseat the cassette. Ensure it is fully inserted and latched.
  • Expected: Cassette sits flush with no gaps.
  • If properly seated and detected → issue resolved.

Check for Obstructions or Debris

  • Inspect the cassette slot and sensor area for fluid, debris, or packaging remnants.
  • Why it matters: Optical/mechanical sensors can be blocked.
  • Clean if needed and reinsert cassette.

Verify Correct Disposable Cassette Type

  • Confirm the cassette is a compatible Level 1 set for the H-1000 / H-1200.
  • Why it matters: Incorrect or off-brand disposables may not align with sensors.

Inspect Cassette for Damage

  • Check for cracks, warped plastic, or damaged alignment features.
  • Expected: Cassette structure intact and rigid.
  • If damaged → replace cassette and retest.

Try a New Cassette Set

  • Install a new, unopened cassette.
  • Why it matters: Eliminates defective disposable as root cause.
  • If new cassette is detected → original cassette was faulty.

Check Cassette Latch / Door Mechanism

  • Ensure the latch fully closes and engages.
  • Expected: Firm closure with no looseness.
  • If latch does not fully engage → sensor may not trigger.

Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit off, wait 10–15 seconds, then power back on with cassette installed.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary detection faults.
  • If cassette is now recognized → issue resolved.

Observe for Intermittent Detection

  • Gently press or slightly adjust cassette while inserted (no force).
  • Why it matters: Intermittent detection may indicate worn latch or sensor alignment issue.

If the Problem Persists

If multiple known-good cassettes are not detected and proper installation has been verified, internal cassette detection sensors or latch mechanisms are likely faulty.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot cassette detection issues while connected to a patient. Always transition to a backup warming method first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying “cassette not detected” and unable to initiate warming."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed cassette recognition due to defective disposable cassette (or internal sensor failure if unresolved)."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced cassette and verified proper detection (or removed from service for repair if issue persisted)."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm message wording
  • Cassette type verified
  • Multiple cassettes tested
  • Cassette seating and latch function checked
  • Presence of debris or fluid in slot
  • Intermittent vs constant failure
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Cassette detection issues are most commonly caused by setup or disposable problems, not internal failure. A structured approach ensures patient safety, avoids unnecessary repairs, and supports clear documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
