STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Water In Chamber – Excess water detected in chamber (burn hazard from steam condensate)

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the “Water In Chamber” alarm, which indicates excess condensate accumulation inside the chamber. This condition creates a serious burn hazard and prevents safe door operation or cycle continuation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • DO NOT open the sterilizer door
  • Treat the chamber as potentially filled with hot water and steam
  • Remove the unit from service immediately

Expected: No exposure to hot condensate or steam

Why it matters: Opening the chamber under this condition can cause severe burns

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm display shows “Water In Chamber”
  • Observe when alarm occurs (during or after cycle)
  • Check if door remains locked
  • Look for visible water (if viewing window is present)

Expected: Alarm is present and unit remains in safe lockout condition

Why it matters: Confirms a true condensate accumulation issue

3. Allow Chamber to Cool and Depressurize

  • Wait for full cooldown and pressure equalization
  • Follow facility safety procedures

Expected: Chamber reaches safe temperature and pressure

Why it matters: Prevents injury during further evaluation

4. Check External Steam Supply Conditions

  • Verify steam supply quality
  • Check for excessive moisture in steam lines
  • Ask if other equipment is experiencing similar issues

Expected: Dry, stable steam supply

Why it matters: Wet steam can introduce water into the chamber

5. Inspect Drain and Strainer Condition (Externally Accessible)

  • Check visible drain paths
  • Inspect strainers for blockage or debris
  • Look for restricted flow

Expected: Clear drainage with no restriction

Why it matters: Blocked drains prevent condensate removal

6. Evaluate for Drain/Trap Function Symptoms

  • Observe for slow or no drainage
  • Check for repeated water accumulation
  • Listen for abnormal drain noises

Expected: Normal drainage after cycles

Why it matters: Failed traps prevent proper condensate evacuation

7. Consider Sensor-Related False Alarm

  • If no water is present, suspect float sensor malfunction
  • Compare alarm condition with actual chamber state

Expected: Sensor accurately reflects chamber condition

Why it matters: Faulty sensors can falsely trigger hazardous alarms

8. Stop if the Issue is Resolved

  • If blockage or external issue is corrected, return to service
  • Run a test cycle to confirm proper drainage

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm remains or water accumulation continues:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Likely internal issues include trap failure, strainer blockage, steam intrusion, or sensor failure
  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or attempt to access a sterilizer with a water-in-chamber alarm while in use. Always move to a backup device and ensure safe conditions before inspection.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed Water In Chamber alarm after cycle. Door remained closed with possible water hazard."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Suspected condensate drainage issue or trap malfunction. External checks did not resolve condition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, allowed cooldown, and sent for repair to evaluate traps, strainers, and sensor."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Water in the chamber represents both a safety hazard and a system failure. Always prioritize safety, verify external causes first, and escalate when internal components are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
