Jaeger Medical Vmax Encore

Software Freeze / PC Connection Lost

Asset Type

Pulmonary Function Tester

Manufacturer

Jaeger Medical

Model

Vmax Encore

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where a Jaeger Medical Vmax Encore pulmonary function testing system experiences software freezes, becomes unresponsive, or loses connection to the PC. These issues may be related to network, USB/serial connections, power instability, or temporary software conflicts. External and easily verifiable causes should be checked before assuming internal hardware failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm no patient is actively connected to the device.
  • If a patient is using the device, move them to an alternative device before troubleshooting.

Check Power and Cables

  • Verify the Vmax Encore is properly powered and turned on.
  • Inspect the power cable for damage and ensure it is fully seated.
  • Confirm the PC is on and functioning normally.

Inspect PC Connection

  • Check USB or serial cable connection between Vmax Encore and PC.
  • Reseat the cable in both device and PC ports.
  • Swap with a known good cable if available.

Check Device Status Lights / Indicators

  • Observe any LED or display indicators for abnormal patterns.
  • A frozen display or unlit power indicator may point to device power or internal fault.

Restart Device and Software

  • Close the Vmax Encore software on the PC.
  • Power cycle the Vmax Encore (turn off, wait 30 seconds, turn on).
  • Relaunch the software and attempt to reconnect.

Check PC Environment

  • Ensure no other software is interfering with Vmax Encore (antivirus, firewall, or USB hub conflicts).
  • If possible, try connecting to a different PC to rule out PC-specific issues.

Verify Software Version / Updates

  • Confirm the Vmax Encore software is up-to-date and compatible with the connected PC OS.
  • Update if necessary, following manufacturer recommendations.

Log Software Errors

  • Note any error messages, codes, or abnormal behaviors.
  • Capture screenshots if needed for vendor support or work order documentation.

If the Problem Persists

After checking all external connections, PC compatibility, and software issues, persistent software freezes or connection loss may indicate internal device or interface board failure.

  • Remove the Vmax Encore from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to manufacturer or authorized service for bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents further patient risk and unnecessary troubleshooting attempts.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt internal repair while the device is connected to a patient.
  • Always confirm patient testing is paused and an alternative system is available.
  • Keep patient data safe by avoiding abrupt disconnections that may corrupt test files.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Vmax Encore software froze and lost connection to PC during patient testing.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Device and PC checked; cables and power verified. Issue persisted after software and device restart.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent to manufacturer for evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes / No
  • USB/serial cables checked or swapped
  • Device power lights normal
  • PC software restarted
  • Any error codes or freeze behavior observed

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting focuses on safety, logical stepwise checks, and appropriate escalation. Document all observations and interventions clearly using the CCR method. Recognizing when to escalate protects patients and preserves the integrity of the device.

That is successful troubleshooting.
