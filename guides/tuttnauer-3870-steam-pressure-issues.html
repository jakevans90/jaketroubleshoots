Tuttnauer 3870 Autoclave

Steam Generation or Pressure Buildup Issues

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

Tuttnauer

Model

3870

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Tuttnauer 3870 sterilizer fails to generate adequate steam or build proper chamber pressure. Such issues can cause incomplete sterilization cycles, cycle aborts, or error codes. Most causes are external, such as water supply problems, valve issues, or simple operator/setup errors, before assuming internal component failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Water Supply

  • Check that the reservoir is filled to the correct level with distilled water.
  • Ensure the water line (if plumbed) is open and supplying water without obstruction.

Why: Steam generation requires adequate water; low levels prevent proper pressure buildup.

Inspect Water Quality and Condition

  • Ensure water is clean and free of debris or scale.
  • Remove and flush the reservoir if necessary.

Why: Contaminated or mineral-heavy water can clog steam pathways.

Check Power and Heating Elements

  • Confirm the sterilizer is properly plugged in and receiving power.
  • Observe if the heating element indicator illuminates during cycle start.

Why: Without power to the heater, steam cannot be generated.

Examine Steam and Pressure Valves

  • Verify the chamber drain and pressure relief valves are closed before starting a cycle.
  • Inspect for visible blockages or leaks around valves and seals.

Why: Open or leaking valves prevent pressure buildup even if steam is generated.

Inspect Door Seal and Gasket

  • Check that the door gasket is intact, properly seated, and free from debris.
  • Ensure the door closes fully and locks.

Why: A compromised seal allows steam to escape, preventing pressure from reaching cycle requirements.

Run a Test Cycle Empty

  • Start a cycle without load and observe pressure gauge and temperature readings.
  • Listen for unusual sounds from the steam generator or valves.

Why: Confirms whether the issue is with the unit itself or related to load and setup.

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks pass and the sterilizer still fails to generate steam or build pressure:

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send the sterilizer for repair or bench evaluation by qualified service personnel.

Note: Continuing to operate a sterilizer that cannot build proper pressure is unsafe and may compromise patient care. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot a sterilizer while it is running a patient load.
  • Move instruments to a backup sterilizer or delay non-urgent sterilization until the unit is verified safe.
  • Always ensure the chamber is cool before inspecting valves or gaskets.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Sterilizer fails to generate steam or reach target pressure; cycle aborts.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Water supply confirmed; valves and door gasket inspected; no steam generation observed.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for bench evaluation/repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Water reservoir level confirmed
  • Distilled water used
  • Power and heating element indicators observed
  • Valve positions verified
  • Door seal inspected
  • Test cycle behavior logged
  • Any unusual sounds or leaks noted

Final Thought

Patient safety and proper sterilization are paramount. Troubleshooting should follow a logical, stepwise approach that rules out simple external issues first. Escalation and thorough documentation prevent equipment misuse and maintain infection control standards.

That is successful troubleshooting.
