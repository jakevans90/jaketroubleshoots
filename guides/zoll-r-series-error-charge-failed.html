ZOLL R Series

Error: CHARGE FAILED

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

ZOLL

Model

R Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: CHARGE FAILED on a ZOLL R Series defibrillator. This error means the device cannot complete a defibrillator charge attempt, which prevents normal shock readiness and creates a serious patient safety concern.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the ZOLL R Series from patient use immediately if CHARGE FAILED occurs during clinical use or readiness testing.
  • Transfer the patient to another functional defibrillator or monitor/defibrillator if needed.
  • Do not rely on a defibrillator that cannot charge successfully.
  • Do not repeatedly attempt charging on a patient-connected device unless directed by emergency clinical protocol.
  • Expected outcome: Patient care is transferred to a working defibrillator and the failed unit is isolated.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm that the exact displayed error is CHARGE FAILED.
  • Power on the unit and observe startup behavior.
  • Attempt a charge only in a safe test condition using approved test equipment or facility procedure.
  • Confirm whether the error appears at startup, when CHARGE is pressed, during charge ramp-up, after reaching partial charge, or during daily testing.
  • Listen for charge tone behavior and note whether the unit starts charging, stops immediately, or times out.
  • Expected outcome: The CHARGE FAILED message is confirmed and the failure behavior is documented.

Remove the Device from Clinical Service

  • Label the unit Out of Service.
  • Keep the defibrillator out of emergency use areas until evaluated.
  • Document that the failure occurred during a charge attempt.
  • Expected outcome: The device is not available for patient use until repaired or cleared.

Check AC Power and Battery Status

  • Connect the R Series to a known-good hospital-grade AC outlet.
  • Verify AC power indicators are present.
  • Check battery condition, charge level, and seating.
  • Reseat the battery if appropriate.
  • Try a known-good compatible battery if available.
  • If battery replacement or reseating resolves the error, perform full operational and defibrillator output testing before returning the unit to service.
  • Expected outcome: The unit has adequate battery and AC power support for high-demand charging.

Check the Power Cord, Outlet, and Environment

  • Inspect the power cord for damage, loose fit, or strain.
  • Test the outlet or move the unit to a known-good outlet.
  • Avoid questionable power strips or damaged wall outlets.
  • Confirm the unit is not exposed to moisture, excessive heat, or obvious contamination.
  • Expected outcome: External power and environmental causes are ruled out.

Check Pads, Paddles, and Therapy Cable Setup

  • Inspect the therapy cable connection at the device.
  • Check for bent pins, looseness, contamination, or cable damage.
  • Try known-good pads, paddles, or therapy cable if available.
  • Confirm the selected therapy path matches the connected accessory.
  • If a known-good cable or accessory resolves the issue, remove the defective accessory from service and test the defibrillator fully before return.
  • Expected outcome: The failure is isolated to an accessory or remains present with known-good accessories.

Perform a Safe Defibrillator Test

  • Connect the R Series to a defibrillator analyzer or approved test load.
  • Select a standard test energy level according to facility procedure.
  • Attempt to charge the unit.
  • Observe whether the unit fails immediately, partially charges then fails, charges but will not hold charge, or charges but will not deliver correctly.
  • Document whether CHARGE FAILED appears repeatedly under controlled test conditions.
  • Expected outcome: The failure pattern is confirmed without involving an active patient.

Check for Physical Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect the case, therapy connector, paddles, battery contacts, AC inlet, and front panel controls.
  • Look for signs of drops, fluid ingress, corrosion, damaged buttons, or burned smell.
  • Verify that the CHARGE button is not physically stuck or damaged.
  • Expected outcome: Any external damage is identified and documented before repair escalation.

Check Whether the Error Follows a Specific Control

  • If equipped, test charging from the front panel and paddles in a safe test setup.
  • Do not test charge controls on a patient.
  • Note whether only one control fails or all charge attempts produce CHARGE FAILED.
  • Expected outcome: The failure is narrowed to a control/input issue or the defibrillator charging subsystem.

Determine Whether the Failure Is Accessory-Related or Device-Related

  • Consider the issue accessory-related if the error clears with a known-good therapy cable, paddles, or pads.
  • Consider the issue power-related if the problem changes with AC power, battery replacement, or battery seating.
  • Consider the issue device-related if CHARGE FAILED occurs with known-good power, battery, therapy cable, and test load.
  • Do not continue repeated charge attempts once the failure is confirmed.
  • Expected outcome: The failure path is narrowed before repair or bench evaluation.

Stop When Resolved

  • If the issue is corrected by a battery, power, cable, accessory, or setup correction, perform a complete operational check.
  • Run defibrillator output testing using approved test equipment.
  • Confirm charging, energy delivery, alarms, and documentation functions pass.
  • Return the device to service only after it passes required safety and performance checks.
  • Document the corrected cause clearly in the work order.

If the Problem Persists

If CHARGE FAILED continues after power, battery, cable, accessory, setup, and external condition checks have been ruled out, the failure strongly indicates an internal defibrillator charging or high-voltage subsystem problem.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation according to facility procedure and manufacturer service requirements.

Possible internal failure areas may include the charging circuit, high-voltage module, capacitor system, control circuitry, or related internal connections. Do not continue repeated charge attempts once the failure is confirmed.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot CHARGE FAILED on an active patient during routine Clinical Engineering response. Move the patient to a backup defibrillator or monitor/defibrillator first, then troubleshoot the failed unit only after patient care is protected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"ZOLL R Series displays Error: CHARGE FAILED during defibrillator charge attempt. Unit will not complete charge cycle and is not ready for shock delivery."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified CHARGE FAILED during controlled test. AC power, battery seating, therapy cable, and test load were checked. Failure remained present, indicating likely internal defibrillator charging or high-voltage subsystem fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation. Device was not returned to clinical use."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

CHARGE FAILED on a ZOLL R Series should be treated as a serious defibrillation-readiness failure. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact behavior, rule out power and accessory issues, then escalate when the charging failure remains. Clear documentation protects patients, staff, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
