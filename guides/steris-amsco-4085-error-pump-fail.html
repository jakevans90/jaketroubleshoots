STERIS AMSCO 4085

Error: PUMP FAIL

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 4085

What This Guide Helps With

Error: PUMP FAIL where the hydraulic pump runs continuously, causing unintended table movement or potential system overheating.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the table from patient use immediately
  • Ensure the table is stable and not actively moving
  • Transfer the patient to a safe surface if needed

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the table and confirm “PUMP FAIL” is displayed
  • Observe if the hydraulic pump runs continuously without input
  • Note when the issue occurs (startup vs during operation)

3. Check Hand Control and Foot Pedal for Stuck Inputs

  • Inspect all buttons for sticking or damage
  • Press and release each button to verify proper function
  • Disconnect hand control (if safe) and observe behavior

4. Inspect Control Cables and Connections

  • Check for pinched, damaged, or worn cables
  • Verify connectors are fully seated
  • Ensure no contamination in connectors

5. Power Cycle the Table

  • Turn the table OFF
  • Wait 30 seconds
  • Turn the table back ON
  • Observe if the pump starts immediately

6. Verify Power Supply Behavior

  • Connect the table to mains power
  • Listen for pump activation during startup
  • Confirm pump only runs when commanded

If the Problem Persists

If all external controls, cables, and power conditions have been ruled out and the pump continues running:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send the unit for repair or bench evaluation
  • Power supply or control board replacement is typically required

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a surgical table on an active patient. Uncontrolled hydraulic movement can create serious safety risks. Always transfer the patient to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Table displaying Error: PUMP FAIL with hydraulic pump running continuously without input."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Pump continued running after verifying controls and cables, indicating internal control circuit failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent for repair. Power supply replaced to resolve issue."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • When the pump begins running (startup vs during use)
  • Results of control isolation testing
  • Power cycle behavior
  • Cable condition and connector status
  • Any abnormal sounds or overheating
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Continuous pump operation is a high-risk condition. Always rule out external inputs first, but recognize when persistent behavior indicates internal failure requiring escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
