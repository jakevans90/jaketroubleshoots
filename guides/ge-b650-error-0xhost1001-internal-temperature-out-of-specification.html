GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B650

Error: 0xHOST1001 Internal temperature out of specification

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B650

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0xHOST1001 indicating internal temperature out of range, which can lead to overheating, unexpected shutdowns, or unstable monitoring performance.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use if overheating or shutting down
  • Transfer monitoring to a backup device
  • Ensure continuous patient monitoring is maintained

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the monitor
  • Confirm Error: 0xHOST1001 is displayed
  • Observe when the error appears (startup, during operation, intermittent)
  • Note symptoms such as fan noise, excessive heat, shutdowns, or sluggish response

3. Allow the Monitor to Stabilize

  • Power OFF the monitor
  • Disconnect AC power
  • Let the unit sit for 20–30 minutes in a normal room environment
  • Restart and observe if the error clears

4. Check Ventilation and Airflow

  • Inspect all ventilation openings and cooling vents
  • Remove dust, debris, or obstructions
  • Ensure the monitor is not placed against walls or other equipment

5. Verify Environmental Conditions

  • Confirm room temperature is within normal clinical range (~20–25°C / 68–77°F)
  • Ensure the monitor is not near heat sources (warming devices, sunlight, HVAC vents)
  • Avoid use in enclosed or poorly ventilated carts

6. Check for Signs of Internal Cooling Issues

  • Listen for internal fan operation during startup
  • Check for abnormal heat buildup
  • Observe for repeated shutdowns or recurring errors

7. Use Diagnostics if Available (Webmin / Hardware Stats)

  • Access diagnostic interface if configured
  • Navigate to Hardware Statistics
  • Review temperature readings across sensors

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0xHOST1001 continues after external and environmental checks, internal causes such as fan failure, sensor fault, or CPU/power issues are likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot overheating issues on an active patient. Always transition the patient to a backup monitor before evaluating temperature-related faults.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor displaying Error 0xHOST1001 with intermittent shutdown and excessive heat during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal temperature out of specification. Airflow and environmental conditions verified, indicating likely internal cooling or sensor issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, cleaned vents, verified environment, allowed stabilization. Error persisted and unit sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Temperature-related errors should always be taken seriously. Begin with airflow and environment, but escalate quickly when conditions persist to prevent unexpected shutdowns.

That is successful troubleshooting.
