Valleylab Force FX Electrosurgical Unit

Error Codes / Self-Test Failure at Power-Up

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

Valleylab

Model

Force FX

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the Valleylab Force FX ESU fails its self-test during power-up, displays an error code, or will not proceed to ready mode.

The focus is on logical, external checks first—such as power supply, accessories, REM connections, and environmental factors—before assuming an internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure the Unit Is Not Connected to a Patient

  • If connected to an active case, immediately transfer the patient to a backup ESU before troubleshooting.
  • Do not power-cycle repeatedly during an active surgical procedure.

2. Perform a Full Power Reset

  • Turn the unit OFF.
  • Disconnect the power cord from the wall outlet.
  • Wait at least 60 seconds.
  • Reconnect directly to a known-good wall outlet (not a power strip).
  • Power ON and observe the startup sequence.

Why this matters: Temporary logic faults or voltage instability can trigger self-test failures.

3. Verify the Wall Outlet and Line Power

  • Confirm the outlet is hospital-grade and functioning.
  • Test outlet with an outlet tester or plug in another known-good device.
  • Avoid extension cords or daisy-chained power strips.

Power irregularities commonly trigger startup errors.

4. Disconnect All Accessories

  • Remove active electrode handpiece.
  • Disconnect return electrode (REM) cable.
  • Remove footswitch.
  • Remove any adapters or extension cables.
  • Power the unit ON with nothing connected.

Why this matters: Faulty accessories can cause impedance or detection errors during self-test.

5. Inspect the Return Electrode (REM) Port

  • Check for bent pins.
  • Check for debris or fluid contamination.
  • Ensure no foreign objects are inside the port.

REM detection failures are a common cause of startup errors.

6. Check for Environmental or Fluid Exposure Issues

  • Inspect for signs of fluid ingress.
  • Look for corrosion around connectors.
  • Note any unusual odor (burning smell).
  • Check for excessive heat from the chassis.

If fluid intrusion is suspected, stop troubleshooting and escalate.

7. Document the Exact Error Code Displayed

  • Record the exact code and message.
  • Note whether the unit fails immediately or midway through self-test.
  • Note any audible tones or alarm patterns.

Precise documentation helps determine whether the issue is power supply–related, memory-related, or internal board-related.

8. Attempt Startup on a Different Circuit (If Available)

  • Move the unit to a separate, known-good circuit in another room if feasible.
  • If the error clears, building power instability may be contributing.

If the Problem Persists

If the unit continues to fail self-test after:

  • Verified power source
  • Accessory removal
  • Full reset
  • Port inspection

The issue is likely internal (power supply module, control board, memory fault, or internal calibration failure).

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label it Out of Service
  • Send for qualified bench evaluation or depot repair

Repeated power cycling is not appropriate once external causes are ruled out.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an ESU that is connected to a patient or sterile field.

Always move the patient to a backup unit first.

ESU failures during surgery present significant patient safety risk.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Unit displays error code during startup and will not enter ready mode.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Unit failed internal self-test at power-up. Error persisted after full power reset and accessory removal. No external power issues identified.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Removed unit from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent to Biomed shop for further evaluation and repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Exact error code displayed
  • Outlet tested (yes/no)
  • Accessories disconnected (yes/no)
  • Any unusual odor, sound, or heat
  • Final operational status

Final Thought

Power-up self-test failures on ESUs are often caused by power instability or accessory-related detection faults. Logical external checks protect patient safety and prevent unnecessary internal disassembly. Once external causes are ruled out, timely escalation prevents further damage and ensures reliable surgical performance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
