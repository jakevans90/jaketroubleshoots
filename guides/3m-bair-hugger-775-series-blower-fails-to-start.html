3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Blower Fails to Start or Shuts Down During Operation

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series patient warming system when the blower fails to start, stops unexpectedly, or shuts down during operation. Common external causes, such as power supply issues or obstructed air pathways, are addressed before considering internal repair. Early identification prevents patient safety risks and workflow interruptions in the OR.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check Power Source

  • Verify the system is connected to a live outlet.
  • Test the outlet with another device to confirm power availability.
  • Confirm that any extension cords or power strips are rated for medical equipment use.

Why: No power or inadequate power prevents blower operation.

Inspect Power Cord and Plug

  • Look for frayed wires, cracks, or bent prongs.
  • Ensure the plug is fully seated in the outlet.

Why: Damaged cords can cause intermittent power loss.

Check System Fuse or Circuit Breaker

  • Some Bair Hugger units have internal fuses accessible externally.
  • Replace blown fuses or reset breakers if accessible and safe.

Why: Electrical protection may trip and stop blower function.

Examine Air Path and Filters

  • Remove and inspect intake filters for dust or debris.
  • Confirm the hose and blanket connection are not blocked or kinked.

Why: Overheating due to blocked airflow can trigger automatic shutdown.

Observe Error Indicators

  • Check the control panel for any fault codes or warning lights.
  • Record displayed codes for documentation and possible escalation.

Why: Codes indicate internal protection mechanisms or specific faults.

Perform a Controlled Restart

  • Power off the unit, wait 30 seconds, and power back on.
  • Ensure the system warms up without obstruction.

Why: Some units require a reset after thermal protection trips.

Swap Blankets (if available)

  • Use a known-good warming blanket to eliminate potential short circuits or faults in the blanket.

Why: Faulty blankets can trigger blower shutdown.

If the Problem Persists

If all above checks pass and the blower still fails to operate consistently:

  • Remove the unit from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send for bench evaluation or manufacturer repair.

Knowing when to stop prevents further damage and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt patient warming if the blower is unreliable.

Move the patient to another functioning Bair Hugger unit.

Only troubleshoot when the patient is safely relocated.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Blower fails to start or stops during operation. Indicator lights flicker, unit shuts down unexpectedly.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Power confirmed at outlet, cord and plug intact, intake filter blocked with debris, airflow restricted, thermal protection triggered.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Cleaned filters and hose, verified unobstructed airflow, unit restarted successfully. If unresolved, labeled Out of Service and sent for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes
  • Cords and plugs inspected: Yes
  • Filters and hoses inspected: Yes
  • Error codes observed: [List if applicable]
  • Unusual sounds or smells: None noted
  • Final device status: Functional or Out of Service

Final Thought

Always prioritize patient safety by confirming another warming source is available before troubleshooting. Follow a logical, stepwise approach from external checks to escalation. Accurate CCR documentation ensures clear communication and regulatory compliance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
