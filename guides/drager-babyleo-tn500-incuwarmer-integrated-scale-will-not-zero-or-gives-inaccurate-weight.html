Drager Babyleo TN500 IncuWarmer

Integrated Scale Will Not Zero Or Gives Inaccurate Weight

Asset Type

Infant Incubator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Babyleo TN500 IncuWarmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting integrated scale zeroing or inaccurate weight issues caused by setup, mattress load, accessories, patient contact, or scale obstruction.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

If the Babyleo TN500 is in active patient use, do not remove supports, reposition accessories, or manipulate the mattress area without clinical staff involvement.

Have clinical staff determine whether the infant can be safely moved, held, or temporarily supported during scale troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Patient safety and thermal support are maintained before any scale checks begin.

Confirm the Reported Scale Issue

Verify whether the issue is:

  • Scale will not zero
  • Weight reading is unstable
  • Weight is clearly inaccurate
  • Weight changes when nothing was added or removed
  • Scale alarms, prompts, or errors are present

Expected outcome: The exact scale complaint is confirmed before parts or device failure are assumed.

Check That the Device Is Stable and Not Being Moved

Confirm the Babyleo is on a level surface with wheels locked and no one leaning on the incubator, mattress tray, side panels, or accessory rails.

Expected outcome: The weight reading should stabilize when the unit is stationary and not being physically disturbed.

If this resolves the issue, stop and document the cause.

Verify Nothing Is Touching or Binding the Weighing Surface

Inspect around the mattress platform for anything contacting the bed frame, side panels, incubator wall, tubing, blankets, positioning aids, or cables.

Look for items that may be resting partly on the mattress and partly on the incubator frame, because this can unload or add force to the scale.

Expected outcome: The mattress area should be free-floating without external contact affecting the scale reading.

If this resolves the issue, stop and document the obstruction or contact point.

Remove Nonessential Items Before Zeroing

With clinical approval, remove extra blankets, gel pads, positioning rolls, supplies, toys, paperwork, or loose equipment from the mattress area.

Only items intended to be included in the tare/zero process should remain.

Expected outcome: The scale should zero more reliably when unnecessary added weight is removed.

Check Mattress and Pad Position

Confirm the mattress, mattress cover, and any approved pad are seated correctly and not folded, wedged, or caught under an edge.

A shifted or pinched mattress can interfere with weight transfer to the scale system.

Expected outcome: The mattress should sit flat and centered without rubbing or binding.

If correcting the mattress position restores normal scale function, stop and document.

Verify Tubing, Cables, and Sensors Are Not Pulling on the Patient or Mattress

Check IV lines, respiratory tubing, temperature probes, SpO₂ cables, ECG leads, and other patient connections.

Make sure they have enough slack and are not pulling upward, downward, or sideways on the infant or mattress.

Expected outcome: The scale reading should not be affected by external tension from patient connections.

Reattempt the Zero/Tare Process Under Proper Conditions

Follow the facility-approved operating procedure for zeroing the integrated scale.

Confirm the correct items are present during zeroing and that the mattress area is not touched during the process.

Expected outcome: The scale should complete zeroing and provide a stable baseline.

If zeroing completes successfully, stop and document the setup correction.

Compare With a Known-Good Reference Method

If the weight still appears inaccurate, ask clinical staff to compare the reading with an approved backup scale or recent verified patient weight, if clinically appropriate.

Do not use the integrated scale for patient weight decisions if the reading is questionable.

Expected outcome: The suspected scale error is confirmed or ruled out against a reliable reference.

Check for Repeated Error After Restart

If safe and allowed by facility procedure, power cycle or restart the device when it is not actively supporting a patient’s immediate care needs.

After restart, repeat the scale check with the mattress area properly prepared.

Expected outcome: A temporary software or initialization issue may clear after restart.

If the scale still will not zero or remains inaccurate, remove the scale function from clinical use and escalate.

Inspect for Obvious Damage or Contamination

Look externally for signs of fluid intrusion, cracked mattress support components, damaged scale area, loose panels, missing parts, or anything abnormal around the bed platform.

Do not disassemble the weighing mechanism or access internal load cell components during basic troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Any visible damage or contamination is identified for repair evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the integrated scale still will not zero or continues giving inaccurate weight readings after external setup, patient connection, mattress, accessory, and restart checks have been completed, the issue may involve the internal weighing system, load cell assembly, calibration, or control electronics.

The Babyleo TN500 should be:

  • Removed from service if the incubator/warmer function is also affected
  • Labeled Out of Service if the device cannot be safely used
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation
  • Clearly documented as having an unreliable integrated scale

If the incubator and warmer functions remain clinically usable but the scale is unreliable, follow facility policy for restricting only the scale function. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot the scale while the infant is actively dependent on the device unless clinical staff determine it is safe. Move the patient to a backup device first if troubleshooting could affect thermal support, positioning, or therapy continuity. If accurate weight is needed for medication, nutrition, or fluid decisions, use an approved alternate weighing method until the integrated scale is verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"NICU staff reported the Babyleo TN500 integrated scale would not zero and displayed inconsistent infant weight readings."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found mattress pad partially shifted and patient tubing pulling against the mattress surface during the zeroing process."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Repositioned mattress pad, relieved tubing tension, repeated zero process successfully, and verified stable scale reading with staff present."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the unit was occupied or empty during troubleshooting
  • Whether the scale would not zero or gave unstable readings
  • Mattress and pad position
  • Extra blankets, gel pads, or accessories present
  • Tubing or cable tension observed
  • Whether a backup scale was used for comparison
  • Power behavior or whether restart changed the behavior
  • Environmental factors such as unit movement, uneven floor, leaning on the device, or accessories contacting the scale surface
  • Any displayed scale message, alarm, indicator light, prompt, or error
  • Any visible damage, fluid exposure, or contamination
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Scale issues on an infant incubator should be handled carefully because inaccurate weight can affect clinical decisions. Start with external causes such as mattress position, accessories, tubing tension, and improper zeroing conditions before assuming internal failure. If the scale cannot be trusted after basic checks, remove it from use or escalate the device for repair, and document the work clearly using the CCR method.

That is successful troubleshooting.
