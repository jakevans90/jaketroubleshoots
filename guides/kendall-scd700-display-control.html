Kendall SCD 700 Series

Display / Control Panel Malfunction

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the device is actively being used on a patient: Transfer the patient to another functioning SCD unit first.
  • Do not troubleshoot a malfunctioning control panel on an active patient unless therapy is confirmed safe and uninterrupted.
  • Patient safety takes priority over equipment evaluation.

Confirm the Unit Has Power

  • Verify the unit is plugged into a known good outlet.
  • Confirm the power switch is in the ON position.
  • Listen for internal startup sounds (pump initialization or fan noise).
  • Observe any LED indicators.
  • If there are no lights and no startup sounds, the issue may be power-related rather than display-related. Refer to the “Will Not Turn On” troubleshooting process if needed.
  • A blank screen is often a power issue.

Assess Screen Visibility

  • Observe the screen closely in normal lighting.
  • Shine a flashlight at an angle across the screen.
  • Look for faint text or graphics.
  • If text becomes visible under a flashlight, this strongly suggests backlight failure rather than full display failure.
  • If the screen briefly lights and then goes dark, this may indicate a failing backlight or power regulation issue.

Check for a Frozen Interface

  • Press multiple buttons.
  • Observe whether indicator LEDs respond.
  • Confirm whether sleeves continue inflating and cycling.
  • If therapy continues but controls are unresponsive, this may indicate:
    • Software logic freeze
    • Keypad membrane failure
    • Control board issue
  • If nothing responds at all, suspect a deeper display/control failure.

Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit OFF.
  • Unplug from the wall (if applicable).
  • Wait at least 10 seconds.
  • Restore power and allow full startup sequence.
  • Temporary logic faults can cause display freezing or keypad lockout. A proper power cycle may restore normal function.
  • Observe startup behavior carefully:
    • Does the screen briefly flash?
    • Is there a normal startup beep?
    • Are partial segments visible?

Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Visually inspect the front panel:
    • Cracks in the display window
    • Signs of liquid intrusion
    • Warping or pressure marks
    • Sticky or physically stuck buttons
  • Gently press each button to check for tactile feedback.
  • Physical damage commonly causes intermittent or non-responsive controls.

Check for Intermittent Behavior

  • With the unit powered on:
    • Gently move the device.
    • Lightly tap near the display area.
    • Observe whether the screen flickers or partially returns.
  • Intermittent flickering may indicate a loose internal ribbon cable or failing connection.
  • Do not open the unit unless authorized and within scope of practice.

If the Display or Controls Remain Malfunctioning

  • After completing the steps above, you have:
    • Verified incoming power
    • Ruled out brightness/backlight visibility issues
    • Attempted power cycling
    • Checked for physical damage
    • Observed startup behavior
    • Confirmed therapy operation status
  • At this point, the issue is likely internal, such as:
    • Display panel failure
    • Backlight failure
    • Keypad membrane failure
    • Internal control board malfunction
    • Ribbon cable connection issue
  • These are not bedside-correctable issues.
  • The unit should be:
    • Removed from service
    • Clearly labeled Out of Service
    • Sent for bench evaluation by Clinical Engineering

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never rely on a unit with a non-functional display or control panel for active therapy.
  • If settings cannot be verified or adjusted, therapy parameters cannot be confirmed. Always transfer the patient to a functioning device before removing a malfunctioning unit from service.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

  • Complaint: Document what was reported by clinical staff.
    • “Display blank; unit powers on but no visible text.”
    • “Control buttons unresponsive; sleeves inflate but panel frozen.”
  • Cause: Document what you verified and observed.
    • “Power verified. Unit power cycled. No visible backlight or response from control panel. Suspected display/backlight failure.”
    • “Therapy running but control panel non-responsive after power cycle. Suspected internal control board or keypad failure.”
  • Resolution: Document the final action taken.
    • “Removed device from service; labeled Out of Service; sent to CE shop for bench evaluation.”
    • “Tagged Out of Service due to display/control malfunction; awaiting internal repair.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet verified functional
  • Startup beep present (yes/no)
  • Screen completely blank or faint image visible
  • Buttons responsive (yes/no)
  • Therapy running despite display issue (yes/no)
  • Visible physical damage present (yes/no)
  • Unit removed from service (yes/no)
  • Final device status

That is successful troubleshooting.
