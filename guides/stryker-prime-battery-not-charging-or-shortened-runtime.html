Stryker Prime

Battery not charging or shortened runtime

Asset Type

Stretcher

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

Prime

What This Guide Helps With

Battery not charging, rapid battery drain, or stretcher losing powered drive due to charging, connection, or external power issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the stretcher from active patient transport before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient safely transferred or alternate stretcher in use.

Verify Charging Attempt

  • Confirm the stretcher is properly connected to its charging source or docking station.
  • Expected: Charging initiated when connected.
  • Why it matters: Misalignment or incomplete docking is a common cause of no charge.

Check Power Source (Outlet or Dock)

  • Plug another device into the outlet or verify dock power indicator (if applicable).
  • Expected: Confirmed working power source.
  • If no power → move stretcher to a known-good outlet or powered dock.

Inspect Charging Cable / Dock Contacts

  • Check for bent pins, debris, or worn contacts on charger or docking interface.
  • Expected: Clean, intact contacts with proper alignment.
  • If dirty → clean contacts and reconnect.
  • If damaged → remove from service.

Observe Charging Indicators

  • Check battery indicator lights or display status when plugged in.
  • Expected: Charging indicator active (light or display confirmation).
  • If no indication → continue troubleshooting.

Power Cycle the Stretcher

  • Turn the stretcher off (if applicable), wait 10–15 seconds, then power back on while connected to charger.
  • Expected: Charging status resets and begins.
  • If charging starts → issue resolved.

Inspect Battery Seating and Connection

  • Access battery compartment (no disassembly beyond access panel) and ensure battery is fully seated and secured.
  • Expected: Firm connection with no looseness.
  • Why it matters: Loose batteries prevent charging and can cause intermittent runtime.

Evaluate Battery Condition (Runtime Check)

  • If charging occurs but runtime is short, operate the stretcher under normal load.
  • Expected: Battery holds charge for typical operational duration.
  • If runtime is significantly reduced → battery likely degraded.

Swap with Known Good Battery (If Available)

  • Install a known good battery and test charging and runtime.
  • Expected: Normal charge and runtime restored.
  • If resolved → original battery is defective.

If the Problem Persists

If charging does not occur or runtime remains poor after verifying power source, connections, and battery condition, the issue is likely internal (charging circuit, power board, or battery interface).

Remove the stretcher from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and reflects proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a powered stretcher while transporting a patient. Always move the patient to a safe alternative before evaluating battery or drive issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stretcher battery not charging and losing power during transport."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Worn battery with reduced capacity confirmed by swap test."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced battery and verified normal charging and runtime."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet or dock tested and verified
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Battery swapped with known good unit
  • Condition of charging contacts
  • Runtime observed under load
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent out)

Final Thought

Battery issues are often external and straightforward when approached logically. Always verify power sources and connections first before assuming internal failure. Prioritizing patient safety and documenting clearly ensures reliable equipment and strong clinical support.

That is successful troubleshooting.
