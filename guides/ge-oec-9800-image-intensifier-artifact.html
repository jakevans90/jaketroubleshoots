GE Healthcare OEC 9800

Image Intensifier Artifact / Distorted Image

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9800

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a GE Healthcare OEC 9800 C-Arm producing distorted or artifact-laden images. Symptoms may include streaking, shadowing, uneven brightness, or warping of the image. Most image distortions on this system are caused by external, easily verifiable issues such as incorrect positioning, loose connections, or interference, rather than internal X-ray tube or intensifier failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Confirm Patient and C-Arm Position

  • Ensure the C-Arm is properly aligned with the patient and table.
  • Misalignment can create geometric distortions or clipping artifacts.

Check Image Intensifier Connection

  • Inspect the cable connecting the image intensifier to the main system.
  • Ensure connectors are fully seated and not bent or corroded.
  • A loose connection can result in signal loss and distorted images.

Inspect Input/Output Ports

  • Verify video output and input ports for damage or contamination.
  • Clean connectors if dust or residue is present.

Verify Power and Warm-Up

  • Confirm the system has completed full warm-up.
  • An un-warmed image intensifier can produce uneven brightness or artifacts.

Check Display Settings and Calibration

  • Ensure the monitor brightness, contrast, and edge enhancement settings are correct.
  • Perform software-based calibration if the system provides an option.

Observe for Electromagnetic Interference

  • Identify nearby equipment that could cause interference (e.g., surgical lights, electrosurgical units).
  • Move or power down sources temporarily to see if artifact resolves.

Swap Cables / Connectors if Available

  • If a spare cable is available, replace the cable connecting the image intensifier to the processor.

If the Problem Persists

External factors and basic system calibration have been ruled out. The image intensifier or associated electronics may be failing.

  • Remove the C-Arm from service.
  • Label it “Out of Service.”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation by a certified GE service provider.

Knowing when to escalate prevents patient risk and unnecessary troubleshooting attempts.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not image patients if the displayed image is distorted, as this may compromise diagnostic quality.
  • Use an alternate C-Arm or imaging method until the issue is resolved.
  • Always ensure patient positioning is optimal before concluding the artifact is hardware-related.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"The C-Arm image is distorted with streaks and uneven brightness when viewing the patient."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
External checks indicate proper patient positioning, warm-up complete, but artifacts persist. Loose or damaged cables, or interference, were ruled out.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service and labeled “Out of Service.” Sent for bench evaluation by GE Healthcare service.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Date and time artifact observed.
  • Any recent moves or adjustments to the C-Arm.
  • Display images or screenshots showing the artifact.
  • Any unusual sounds or smells during operation.

Final Thought

Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures patient safety and avoids unnecessary service costs. External checks, positioning, and calibration often resolve or clarify the source of image artifacts. Accurate documentation provides a clear trail for repair and regulatory compliance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
