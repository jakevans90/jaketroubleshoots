Siemens Healthineers Multix Impact

Exposure Inhibit, Generator Fault, or Failed X-Ray Exposure

Asset Type

Radiographic System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

Multix Impact

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting exposure inhibit, generator fault, failed exposure, or X-ray not ready conditions before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm whether a patient is positioned at the table, wall stand, or near the tube stand.

If the system will not expose, do not repeatedly attempt exposures while the patient remains positioned. Have clinical staff safely move the patient away or use another radiographic room if imaging is urgent.

Expected outcome: The patient is not held in position or exposed to repeated failed exposure attempts.

Confirm the Exact Exposure Complaint

Ask the technologist what occurred and when.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Exposure inhibit
  • Generator fault
  • Prep but no exposure
  • No prep or no exposure
  • Exposure switch not responding
  • X-ray tube not ready
  • Detector not ready
  • Bucky not ready
  • Door interlock or room ready warning
  • Collimator, SID, tube angle, or positioning inhibit
  • Fault occurs on table only, wall stand only, or all exams

Expected outcome: The issue is separated into workflow, positioning, detector, room interlock, exposure switch, or generator-related behavior.

Check for Active Error Messages or Inhibit Conditions

Review the system console, acquisition workstation, generator status, and any visible room indicators.

Look for messages such as exposure inhibited, system not ready, generator fault, detector not ready, Bucky not selected, door open, or positioning not accepted.

Expected outcome: The displayed message guides troubleshooting instead of guessing at the cause.

Verify the Correct Exam, Detector, and Image Receptor Are Selected

Confirm the selected exam protocol matches the intended table, wall stand, detector, or Bucky.

Make sure the technologist has selected the correct image receptor and that the detector status is ready.

Why this matters: Radiographic systems may inhibit exposure if the selected receptor does not match the physical setup.

Expected outcome: The selected exam and receptor match the actual patient setup. If this resolves the inhibit, complete a test exposure per site policy and stop.

Check Room Door and Exposure Safety Interlocks

Confirm the X-ray room door is fully closed and that any door-closed indicator is active.

Inspect the door switch area externally for obvious obstruction or damage. Do not bypass interlocks.

Expected outcome: The system recognizes the room as safe for exposure. If the door was not fully closed and exposure readiness returns, stop.

Verify Tube Stand, Table, Wall Stand, and Bucky Positioning

Confirm the tube stand, table, wall stand, and Bucky are positioned within normal operating ranges.

Check for obvious misalignment, extreme angulation, improper SID, or a tube/detector position that does not match the selected exam.

Expected outcome: The system accepts the mechanical position and no longer reports a positioning-related inhibit.

Check Collimator and Field Setup

Confirm the collimator is powered, responsive, and not displaying a fault.

Verify the light field operates normally and that the collimator is not reporting a blade, filter, or lamp-related error.

Expected outcome: Collimator status does not prevent exposure readiness.

Inspect the Exposure Hand Switch and Prep/Expose Behavior

Check the exposure hand switch for physical damage, loose connection, stuck button feel, or strain at the cable.

Have the technologist describe whether the system reaches prep but fails at exposure, or whether prep never starts.

Expected outcome: A damaged or nonresponsive hand switch is identified as a likely external cause. If a known-good approved exposure switch is available and site policy allows, swap it for testing.

Check Emergency Stop and Safety Stop Controls

Confirm no emergency stop button is engaged at the operator console, table, wall stand, or room control area.

Reset only according to site procedure and only after confirming the area is safe.

Expected outcome: The system is not being held in a safety-stop condition.

Verify System Power and Generator Readiness

Confirm the room system, generator, operator console, acquisition workstation, and detector components are powered normally.

Look for abnormal indicator lights, tripped local breakers, shutdown warnings, cooling warnings, or generator status messages.

Expected outcome: All major system components show normal powered status.

Check for Tube Heat, Cooling, or Warm-Up Messages

Review the system for tube heat, cooling delay, warm-up required, or exposure not permitted messages.

If the system indicates a required warm-up or cooling delay, follow the approved clinical workflow and site policy. Do not force repeated exposure attempts.

Expected outcome: Exposure is attempted only when the system reports the tube and generator are ready.

Confirm Detector Readiness

Check whether the selected detector is detected, connected, charged if wireless, and ready for acquisition.

If the detector is not ready, troubleshoot the detector issue separately before continuing exposure troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Exposure is not being inhibited by an unavailable detector or image receptor.

Restart Only When Safe and Appropriate

If no patient is present and clinical workflow allows, restart the acquisition workstation or system using the normal shutdown and startup process.

Do not power-cycle during image acquisition, active patient positioning, or while unsaved study data may be at risk.

Expected outcome: Temporary software or communication-related inhibit clears after a controlled restart.

Perform a Controlled Test Exposure if Cleared

After correcting an external issue, coordinate with Radiology staff to perform an approved test exposure or system readiness check according to site policy.

Confirm that prep, exposure, image acquisition, and image display complete normally.

Expected outcome: The Multix Impact exposes successfully and returns to clinical use only after safe function is verified.

Stop if the Fault Repeats or Indicates Generator Failure

If generator faults, exposure aborts, failed prep/expose behavior, tube readiness faults, or unexplained exposure inhibits continue after external checks, stop troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: The system is treated as potentially having an internal generator, tube, high-voltage, control, or interlock issue requiring qualified service evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the Multix Impact still shows an exposure inhibit, generator fault, or failed X-ray exposure after external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal to the generator, tube, exposure control circuit, interlock chain, workstation communication, or system control hardware.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench/vendor evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not bypass interlocks, defeat safety circuits, or continue repeated exposure attempts on a system with unresolved generator or exposure faults.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot failed exposures while a patient is still positioned for imaging. Move the patient to another room or device first when clinically appropriate, then troubleshoot with the system clear and safe.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the Siemens Multix Impact displayed an exposure inhibit and would not complete an X-ray exposure at the wall stand."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified the room door interlock was not showing ready because the door was not fully latched; no generator fault remained after the door was secured."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Secured the room door, confirmed system ready status, performed a test exposure with Radiology, and returned the room to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exam type selected
  • Table, wall stand, or free detector use
  • Detector selected and ready status
  • Exposure inhibit or generator fault message
  • Whether prep occurred
  • Whether exposure occurred
  • Door interlock status
  • Emergency stop status
  • Tube heat or warm-up messages
  • Collimator behavior
  • Hand switch condition
  • Bucky or receptor selected
  • Indicator lights observed
  • Any unusual sound, heat, smell, or shutdown
  • Whether restart was performed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Exposure failures should be approached logically and safely. Confirm the patient is protected first, then work through room interlocks, detector readiness, selected receptor, positioning, power, and exposure controls before suspecting a generator or internal system fault. Clear CCR documentation helps show what was checked, what was found, and why the system was either returned to service or escalated.

That is successful troubleshooting.
