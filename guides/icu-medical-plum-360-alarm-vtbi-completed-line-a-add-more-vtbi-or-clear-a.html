ICU Medical Plum 360

Alarm: VTBI Completed Line A! Add more VTBI or Clear A.

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the “VTBI Completed Line A! Add more VTBI or Clear A.” alarm, which indicates the programmed volume to be infused (VTBI) has been fully delivered and Line A infusion has stopped until further action is taken.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Assess the patient and confirm therapy status
  • If continuous infusion is required, transition to a backup pump if needed
  • Ensure no interruption to critical medication delivery

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm message is displayed exactly
  • Check if Line A infusion has stopped
  • Determine if the alarm occurred at expected completion of infusion

Confirm VTBI Completion

  • Review programmed VTBI settings
  • Compare delivered volume to programmed VTBI
  • Check infusion history if available

Determine Clinical Intent

  • Confirm with clinical staff whether therapy is complete
  • Verify if additional infusion volume is required
  • Avoid assumptions, especially with high-risk medications

Add Additional VTBI if Needed

  • Enter additional VTBI per clinical order
  • Restart Line A infusion
  • Verify infusion resumes normally

Clear or Stop Line A if Therapy is Complete

  • Stop Line A infusion
  • Clear programming or open cassette door if required
  • Confirm alarm clears and pump resets for next use

Check for Unexpected Early Completion

  • Verify correct VTBI was entered
  • Check flow rate settings
  • Inspect for programming errors or unintended fast infusion

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues despite correct VTBI entry or clearing, external and programming causes have been ruled out. This may indicate a keypad, software, or control issue.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or further evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before addressing alarms or programming changes.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"VTBI Completed Line A! Add more VTBI or Clear A alarm displayed and infusion stopped."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"VTBI fully delivered as programmed; normal completion of infusion."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Confirmed therapy plan with staff, added additional VTBI and restarted infusion, or cleared Line A and verified normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

This alarm typically reflects normal operation rather than device failure. Proper interpretation ensures therapy continuity while avoiding unnecessary troubleshooting or device removal.

That is successful troubleshooting.
