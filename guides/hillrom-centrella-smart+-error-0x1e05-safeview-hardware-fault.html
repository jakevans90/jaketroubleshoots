Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x1E05 SafeView Hardware Fault

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0x1E05 SafeView Hardware Fault indicating a failure within the SafeView system hardware, potentially causing loss of under-bed lighting functionality or triggering a broader system fault condition.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use if SafeView lighting or system behavior is compromised
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed
  • Ensure adequate room lighting if visibility is affected

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error: 0x1E05 SafeView Hardware Fault is displayed
  • Observe when the error appears (startup vs during use)
  • Check if SafeView lights are non-functional or intermittent
  • Note any additional system errors or abnormal behavior

Perform a Basic Power Reset

  • Turn the bed off
  • Unplug from AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart the bed

Inspect External Cabling

  • Check all accessible SafeView-related cables and connectors
  • Ensure connectors are fully seated
  • Look for pinched, cut, or damaged wiring
  • Reseat any loose connections

Check for Physical Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect SafeView lighting areas and surrounding components
  • Look for liquid intrusion, debris, or impact damage
  • Check for signs of overheating or burnt components

Isolate Cable vs Hardware Issue

  • Gently manipulate external harnesses while the system is powered
  • Observe for any intermittent clearing or reappearance of the error
  • Determine if the issue changes with cable movement

Confirm System Function if Error Clears

  • Verify SafeView lighting operates normally
  • Test basic bed functions
  • Return the bed to service if no issues remain

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0x1E05 continues after completing all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • The issue likely involves internal SafeView hardware or control boards
  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace affected components (SafeView board, control board, or related hardware)

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or rely on SafeView lighting while the bed is in active patient use. Always transfer the patient to a backup device before performing troubleshooting steps.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x1E05 SafeView Hardware Fault; under-bed lighting not functioning."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Error persisted after power reset and cable inspection, indicating likely internal SafeView hardware or control board failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated external cables with no change. Removed bed from service and sent for repair and board replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error 0x1E05 indicates a likely hardware-level issue within the SafeView system. Effective troubleshooting focuses on ruling out external causes before escalating to repair, ensuring both patient safety and efficient equipment handling.

That is successful troubleshooting.
