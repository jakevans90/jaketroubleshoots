Mortara ELI Series

Thermal Printer Failure

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Mortara

Model

ELI Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies external causes of thermal printer failure, including paper jams, incorrect paper type, cable connections, and power issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm no patient is actively undergoing an EKG.

Expected outcome: Safe environment for troubleshooting without affecting patient care.

2. Check Printer Power

Verify the printer is powered on and indicator lights are normal.

Expected outcome: Printer powers on without error lights; ensures printer receives electricity.

3. Inspect Paper Supply

Ensure proper thermal paper is installed and seated correctly.

Check for jams or misaligned paper.

Expected outcome: Paper feeds smoothly; resolves most feed issues.

4. Verify Printer Cables and Connections

Inspect the printer’s data and power cables for secure connection and damage.

Re-seat connections if necessary.

Expected outcome: Printer communicates properly with EKG; eliminates connection faults.

5. Test Printer with a Self-Test or Demo Print

Use device menu to run a printer self-test (if available).

Expected outcome: Printer prints test page; confirms printer hardware functionality.

6. Check Device Settings

Verify printer selection and paper type in EKG settings.

Expected outcome: Correct configuration ensures print commands are sent properly.

7. Power Cycle the EKG and Printer

Turn both units off, wait 30 seconds, and power back on.

Expected outcome: Resolves temporary software or communication glitches.

If the Problem Persists

External causes are likely ruled out; the issue may be internal to the printer mechanism or control board.

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents further damage and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to print on an active patient if printer issues are unresolved.

Use another EKG or printer for patient recordings until the issue is resolved.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Printer fails to print EKG strips."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Paper jam and misaligned feed roller identified during inspection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleared jam, reseated paper, verified successful self-test print."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Printer power light status
  • Paper type and alignment
  • Cable connections inspected
  • Any error codes displayed
  • Test print success/failure
  • Final device status (ready for patient use or OOS)

Final Thought

Thermal printer troubleshooting requires a logical, stepwise approach, starting with the simplest external checks. Document each step clearly, escalate when internal issues are suspected, and prioritize patient safety. Proper CCR entries support accountability and future reference.

That is successful troubleshooting.
