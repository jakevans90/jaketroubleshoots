Siemens Healthineers CLINITEK Advantus

Printer / Internal Report Output Failure

Asset Type

Urine Analyzer

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

CLINITEK Advantus

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting internal printer or report output failures caused by paper, cover, settings, power, or printer mechanism issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the CLINITEK Advantus is not actively being used to process a patient specimen.

If testing is in progress, allow the current test sequence to complete when safe, or have laboratory staff use an alternate analyzer if reporting is delayed.

Expected outcome: Patient testing and result reporting are not dependent on an analyzer with unresolved printer output failure.

Verify the Reported Printer Failure

Ask laboratory staff what is happening during report output.

Common symptoms may include:

  • No report prints
  • Blank report output
  • Partial or faded print
  • Paper does not feed
  • Printer noise without paper movement
  • Printer error message
  • Reports available on screen but not printed

Expected outcome: The issue is confirmed as an internal printer or report output problem, not a testing, LIS, or operator workflow issue.

Check Whether Results Are Still Available on the Analyzer

Confirm that patient or QC results are visible on the analyzer display or stored in the result memory.

Expected outcome: Results are retained and can be reviewed, reprinted, or transmitted if supported.

Why it matters: A printer failure should not be treated the same as lost test data. Protecting result availability comes first.

Inspect Printer Paper Supply

Open the printer paper compartment and verify that paper is installed.

Check for:

  • Empty roll
  • Incorrect paper type
  • Paper installed backward
  • Paper not feeding into the printer path
  • Paper roll jammed or restricted

Reload paper using the correct orientation and close the compartment securely.

Expected outcome: Paper feeds normally and the report prints. If resolved, stop troubleshooting.

Check for Paper Jam or Obstruction

Inspect the paper path for torn paper, folded paper, adhesive residue, or debris.

Remove only visible loose paper or debris without forcing the mechanism.

Expected outcome: The printer path is clear and paper can advance normally.

If the printer begins feeding and printing correctly, stop troubleshooting.

Confirm Printer Door or Cover Is Fully Closed

Verify that the printer cover is seated correctly and not warped, loose, or blocked by the paper roll.

Open and close the cover again, making sure it latches completely.

Expected outcome: Any cover-related printer interlock or feed issue clears.

Perform a Controlled Power Cycle

Confirm no specimen is actively being processed.

Power the analyzer off, wait briefly, then power it back on.

Allow the analyzer to complete startup before attempting another report print.

Expected outcome: A temporary printer communication or output state clears.

If printing returns to normal, document the power cycle and stop.

Attempt a Reprint or Internal Report Print

From the analyzer menu, attempt to reprint a recent result, QC report, or other available internal report.

Expected outcome: The analyzer sends a print command and the printer responds.

Why it matters: This helps separate a printer problem from a specific test workflow or user action.

Check Print Quality

If paper feeds but output is blank, faint, or unreadable, confirm the correct thermal paper is installed and oriented properly.

Thermal paper loaded backward may feed normally but produce no readable print.

Expected outcome: Correctly loaded thermal paper produces readable printed output.

Check Analyzer Settings Related to Printing

Review available report or printer settings with laboratory staff or the site configuration record.

Confirm whether automatic printing is enabled, disabled, or configured only for specific result types.

Expected outcome: The analyzer is configured to print the expected reports.

Why it matters: A disabled auto-print setting may look like a printer failure even when the printer hardware is working.

Compare Internal Printing vs External Result Workflow

Determine whether the issue affects only the internal printer or also LIS/result transmission.

If LIS transmission works but the internal report does not print, focus on printer output.

If both printing and LIS/reporting fail, evaluate broader analyzer communication or system configuration issues.

Expected outcome: The failure is narrowed to internal print output or a broader reporting problem.

Inspect for External Damage or Contamination

Look for signs of liquid exposure, reagent strip dust, paper debris, cracked printer cover, loose hinges, unusual heat, or burning smell.

Do not continue operating the printer if there is odor, heat, visible damage, or suspected liquid intrusion.

Expected outcome: The analyzer is safe to continue basic troubleshooting or is removed from service for repair.

Test With Known-Good Paper

If available, install a known-good, correct paper roll from verified stock.

Avoid using old, damp, non-thermal, or questionable paper.

Expected outcome: Printer output improves if the original paper roll was defective or incorrect.

If resolved, document the paper replacement and return the analyzer to service.

Determine Whether the Printer Mechanism Responds

Attempt one print command while observing whether the printer motor runs, paper advances, or an error appears.

Expected outcome: The printer either responds normally, fails to feed, prints poorly, or does not respond at all.

Why it matters: A nonresponsive printer after paper, cover, and settings checks may indicate an internal printer assembly, sensor, or control issue.

If the Problem Persists

If the printer still does not print, paper does not feed, output remains blank with correct paper orientation, or printer errors continue after basic checks, common external causes have been ruled out.

The CLINITEK Advantus should be:

  • Removed from service if printed reports are required for clinical workflow
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Do not disassemble the printer mechanism beyond basic paper-path inspection. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting, especially when result reporting and documentation integrity are affected.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot report output failure while patient specimens are actively being processed. If printed reports are required for the lab workflow, have staff use another analyzer or an approved alternate reporting process until the printer issue is resolved.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Laboratory staff reported that the Siemens CLINITEK Advantus was not printing internal patient result reports."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Printer paper was installed backward, causing the analyzer to feed paper with blank output."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reinstalled correct thermal paper in the proper orientation, verified successful result reprint, and returned the analyzer to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Analyzer powered on successfully
  • Internal results still visible on display
  • Paper roll present or empty
  • Paper orientation checked
  • Known-good paper tested
  • Printer cover fully latched
  • Paper path checked for jam or debris
  • Print command attempted
  • Paper feed behavior observed
  • Print quality noted
  • Any printer error message recorded
  • LIS transmission status, if relevant
  • Unusual heat, smell, liquid exposure, or damage
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Printer failures on a urine analyzer should be handled logically: protect patient result availability first, then check paper, covers, settings, and basic output behavior before assuming an internal printer problem. Clear CCR documentation helps show whether the issue was corrected at the point of service or appropriately escalated for repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
