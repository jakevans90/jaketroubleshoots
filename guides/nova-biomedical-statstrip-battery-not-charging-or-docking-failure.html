Nova Biomedical StatStrip

Battery Not Charging or Docking Failure

Asset Type

Blood Glucose Meter

Manufacturer

Nova Biomedical

Model

StatStrip

What This Guide Helps With

Meter not charging, low battery despite docking, or no charging indicator when placed in base station.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the meter from clinical use if battery is critically low or unreliable. Use a backup device for patient testing.
  • Expected: No patient care interruption
  • Why it matters: Power loss during testing can delay care or cause inaccurate documentation

Verify Charging Indicator Behavior

  • Place the StatStrip in the docking station and observe for charging indicators (LED or on-screen icon).
  • Expected: Charging indicator appears within a few seconds
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the device recognizes the dock and is receiving power

Check Power to Docking Station

  • Ensure the docking station is plugged in and powered. Verify outlet functionality using another device.
  • Expected: Dock has confirmed power
  • Why it matters: A dead outlet or unplugged dock is a common and easily missed cause

Inspect Docking Station Connections

  • Check the power adapter connection to the dock and wall. Look for loose or damaged cables.
  • Expected: All connections secure and intact
  • Why it matters: Intermittent or failed connections prevent consistent charging

Clean Charging Contacts (Common Issue)

  • Inspect and clean the metal charging contacts on both the meter and the docking station using approved wipes (e.g., alcohol).
  • Expected: Contacts are clean, dry, and free of residue
  • Why it matters: Glucose meters frequently accumulate debris that blocks electrical contact

Reseat the Meter in the Dock

  • Remove and firmly reseat the meter in the docking station. Ensure it is properly aligned and fully seated.
  • Expected: Charging indicator activates
  • Why it matters: Misalignment prevents proper contact engagement

Try a Known Good Dock or Meter

  • Place the meter in a different working dock, or place a known working meter in the current dock.
  • Expected:
    • If meter charges in another dock → original dock is faulty
    • If another meter fails in this dock → dock or power supply issue
  • Why it matters: Isolates whether the issue is with the meter or docking station

Check for Battery Age or Degradation

  • If the meter only holds a charge briefly or fails to charge consistently, the internal battery may be worn.
  • Expected: Battery maintains charge during normal use
  • Why it matters: Rechargeable batteries degrade over time and eventually fail

Restart the Meter

  • Power cycle the device and return it to the dock.
  • Expected: Charging resumes after reboot
  • Why it matters: Clears minor software or charging recognition issues

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely due to internal battery failure or charging circuitry.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary downtime and avoids unsafe device use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot charging issues while the meter is needed for active patient testing. Always switch to a charged backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"StatStrip meter not charging in docking station; battery remains low."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Dirty charging contacts preventing electrical connection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned contacts and verified proper charging function in dock."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed working
  • Dock power LED status
  • Charging icon behavior
  • Contacts condition (clean/dirty)
  • Results with alternate dock or meter
  • Battery runtime after charge
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent out)

Final Thought

Charging issues are often caused by simple external factors like dirty contacts or power loss. Always rule these out first before assuming battery or internal failure. Proper escalation and clear documentation support both patient safety and efficient equipment management.

That is successful troubleshooting.
