Stryker FL28 / FL36

Scale Inaccuracy or Drift

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

FL28 / FL36

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses inaccurate weight readings or drifting scales due to power, calibration, connection, or environmental factors.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

  • Confirm the bed is unoccupied or the patient is safely moved to another bed. Never attempt scale calibration or testing with a patient on the bed if readings may be inaccurate.

Check Bed Level and Placement

  • Ensure the bed is on a flat, stable surface. Uneven floors can cause scale drift or incorrect weight readings. Adjust bed position if needed.

Verify Power and Battery Status

  • Confirm the bed is connected to a functional power source.
  • Check battery backup; low battery can affect scale accuracy.
  • Observe scale display behavior after power cycling.

Inspect Cables and Connectors

  • Examine any scale-specific cabling between the bed frame and control module.
  • Look for loose connections, frayed wires, or corrosion. Secure or replace as needed.

Check for Interference or Load Issues

  • Remove any non-patient weight (linens, equipment) from the bed.
  • Ensure side rails, IV poles, or accessories are not causing uneven load.

Perform Scale Calibration or Tare Function

  • Use the bed’s calibration or zeroing function per manufacturer instructions.
  • Confirm scale reads “0” when bed is empty.
  • Place a known weight (e.g., calibration test weight) to verify accuracy.

Observe Indicator Lights or Error Codes

  • Check for scale or system error indicators on the control panel.
  • Document any persistent warnings or abnormal readings.

If the Problem Persists

External factors and simple calibration issues have been ruled out.

The scale assembly or internal electronics may be faulty.

Action: Remove bed from service, label “Out of Service,” and send for repair or bench evaluation. Stopping at this stage prevents unsafe use and unnecessary troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on inaccurate bed scales for patient medication dosing or fluid balance.

Move patients to an alternative scale or bed for weight-dependent care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported bed scale reading fluctuates and drifts over time."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Bed on uneven floor; calibration not recently performed; minor connector looseness observed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Repositioned bed, secured cables, recalibrated scale; verified accuracy with test weight; returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Floor level and bed position
  • Battery and power status
  • Calibration steps performed
  • Test weight verification results
  • Error codes or indicator lights

Final Thought

Accurate bed scales are critical for patient safety. Follow a structured approach: check placement, power, cables, and calibration first. Escalate to repair when external issues are ruled out, and document all actions. Proper documentation supports safe patient care and reduces repeated service calls.

That is successful troubleshooting.
