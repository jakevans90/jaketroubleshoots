Mortara ELI Series

Touchscreen Calibration or Errors

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Mortara

Model

ELI Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies external causes of touchscreen issues, including calibration errors, screen misalignment, dirt or debris, software settings, and input response problems.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm no patient is actively undergoing an EKG procedure.

Expected outcome: Safe environment for troubleshooting.

Inspect the Touchscreen Surface

Wipe the screen with a lint-free cloth and approved cleaning solution.

Expected outcome: Remove debris that can interfere with touch detection.

Check for Protective Covers or Screen Shields

Remove any screen protectors or films that may impede touch sensitivity.

Expected outcome: Restored proper touch response.

Restart the Device

Power cycle the EKG machine to reset software and touchscreen drivers.

Expected outcome: Temporary glitches or calibration errors may be cleared.

Verify Device Settings

Navigate to touchscreen settings (if accessible) and ensure calibration mode is correctly configured.

Expected outcome: Device recognizes touch inputs accurately.

Perform a Touchscreen Calibration Routine

Follow manufacturer-recommended calibration procedure from the settings menu.

Expected outcome: Touch input aligns with displayed interface.

Test Touch Response Across the Entire Screen

Use a calibration or test pattern to check responsiveness in all areas.

Expected outcome: No dead zones, erratic behavior, or delayed response.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. Device may have internal touchscreen or controller failure. Actions:

  • Remove from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to escalate ensures patient safety and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt touchscreen troubleshooting while a patient is connected. Move patient to another unit first. Ensure safe, controlled environment for device interaction.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Touchscreen unresponsive and misaligned reported by staff."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Dirt on screen and calibration drift identified during troubleshooting."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned screen and recalibrated touchscreen; device now responsive."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Check for screen cracks or damage
  • Verify software version
  • Note if protective film or overlay was removed
  • Document full screen responsiveness test

Final Thought

Touchscreen issues are often due to external or software causes. Following a stepwise approach protects patients, resolves most problems, and ensures proper documentation for escalation if needed.

That is successful troubleshooting.
