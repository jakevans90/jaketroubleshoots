Medtronic Valleylab FT10

Error: E322 Check Instrument

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Valleylab FT10

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error E322 Check Instrument on the Medtronic Valleylab FT10 Electrosurgical Unit. This condition indicates the Mono 2 handswitch input is being detected as pressed, which may prevent normal ESU readiness or create concern for unintended activation behavior.

This error should be treated as a patient safety issue because an ESU activation input problem can affect cutting, coagulation, or reliable instrument control during a procedure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Valleylab FT10 from patient use if the error appears during setup, activation, or an active procedure.
  • Stop using the affected Mono 2 port or connected handswitch instrument.
  • Use another known-working ESU if electrosurgery is clinically needed.
  • Do not continue activation attempts while the ESU is reporting a handswitch input problem.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the Valleylab FT10 on and observe the display.
  • Confirm the exact message is Error E322 Check Instrument.
  • Note whether the error appears at startup, after connecting the instrument, when using the Mono 2 port, or intermittently during use.
  • Ask clinical staff whether the issue occurred during setup, before activation, while plugging in the instrument, or during a case.

Check for a Pressed or Stuck Handswitch

  • Inspect the connected monopolar handswitch pencil or instrument.
  • Confirm the Cut and Coag buttons are not pressed, jammed, taped down, contaminated, or trapped under a drape or cable.
  • Gently release and manipulate the handswitch buttons.
  • Disconnect and reconnect the instrument after confirming the switch is not physically stuck.

Disconnect the Mono 2 Instrument

  • Remove the instrument from the Mono 2 port.
  • Observe whether the E322 error clears after the instrument is disconnected.
  • If the error clears, reconnect the same instrument and see whether the error returns.
  • If the error follows the instrument, suspect the accessory or handswitch circuit first.

Replace the Instrument or Pencil

  • Connect a known-good compatible monopolar handswitch instrument to the Mono 2 port.
  • Do not reuse the suspect instrument during patient care.
  • Confirm whether the FT10 operates normally with the known-good accessory.
  • If the error does not return, document the suspect instrument as the likely cause.

Inspect the Mono 2 Port and Accessory Connection

  • Inspect the Mono 2 receptacle for bent contacts, debris, fluid contamination, damaged plastic, looseness, or signs of arcing.
  • Verify the instrument plug seats fully and does not feel loose or intermittent.
  • Check for moisture, residue, or visible damage around the accessory input area.
  • Do not use the unit if the connector appears damaged or contaminated.

Compare Mono 1 and Mono 2 Behavior

  • During bench testing only, compare behavior using a known-good compatible instrument on Mono 1 and Mono 2.
  • Observe whether the error only appears when using the Mono 2 port.
  • If Mono 1 works normally but Mono 2 repeatedly reports E322 with known-good accessories, suspect a Mono 2 input path problem.

Check for External Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect the ESU exterior, front panel, accessory ports, power cord, and surrounding area.
  • Look for signs of fluid intrusion, impact damage, burned odor, excessive heat, cracked housing, or contamination from the OR field.
  • If damage or contamination is found, remove the device from service for evaluation.

Power Cycle Only After Accessories Are Removed

  • Disconnect all monopolar instruments from the generator.
  • Power the FT10 off, wait briefly, and power it back on.
  • Observe whether Error E322 returns with no instrument connected.
  • If E322 appears with no accessory connected, the issue is unlikely to be caused by a user instrument.

Isolate the Likely Cause

  • If E322 clears when the instrument is removed, suspect the instrument, handswitch, or accessory cable.
  • If E322 follows one instrument, replace the instrument.
  • If E322 occurs with multiple known-good instruments on Mono 2, suspect the Mono 2 input circuit or internal generator fault.
  • If E322 appears with nothing connected, remove the unit from service for repair evaluation.
  • Do not continue into board-level repair during basic clinical troubleshooting.

If the Problem Persists

If Error E322 continues after the handswitch is released, the instrument is replaced, the Mono 2 port is checked, and known-good accessories are tested, external causes have been reasonably ruled out.

Remove the Valleylab FT10 from service.

Label the unit Out of Service.

Send the device for bench evaluation or vendor repair. Persistent E322 with known-good accessories may indicate an internal activation detection fault rather than a simple accessory issue.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a handswitch activation error on an active patient. Move the patient or procedure to a backup ESU first, then troubleshoot the Valleylab FT10 only after it is no longer needed for active clinical use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Medtronic Valleylab FT10 displayed Error E322 Check Instrument. Staff reported a Mono 2 handswitch or instrument issue and the unit would not clear reliably for use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Mono 2 handswitch input was detected as pressed. Error cleared after disconnecting the suspect instrument, indicating a likely handswitch or accessory fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed suspect instrument from use and tested FT10 with a known-good compatible monopolar instrument. Error did not return and unit passed functional check before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error E322 should be approached as a possible activation input problem. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact error, rule out a stuck or failed instrument, inspect the Mono 2 connection, and escalate only after external causes have been eliminated. Clear CCR documentation helps show whether the issue was accessory-related or a likely internal ESU fault.

That is successful troubleshooting.
