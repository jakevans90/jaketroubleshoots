ZOLL AED 3

Self-Test Failure or Status Indicator Fault

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

ZOLL

Model

AED 3

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses self-test failures or status indicator faults, focusing on battery, pad connections, device readiness, and external error conditions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the AED is not in active clinical use. If a patient requires resuscitation, provide a backup AED before troubleshooting.

Check Battery and Power

  • Ensure the battery is correctly seated.
  • Inspect for corrosion or debris on battery contacts.
  • Replace with a known good battery if the device reports “battery error.”

Expected Outcome: AED powers on and self-test progresses.

Verify Pad Installation

  • Ensure pads are connected firmly and correctly.
  • Inspect pad connectors and cables for damage or debris.
  • Replace pads if physical damage is noted.

Expected Outcome: Self-test recognizes pads.

Inspect Status Indicators

  • Observe the device status lights and display messages during power-on.
  • Compare to AED 3 standard readiness indicators.

Expected Outcome: Indicators display normal status (green).

Perform a Manual Self-Test (if available)

  • Follow AED prompts to initiate a manual self-test.
  • Record any error codes or abnormal indicator behavior.

Expected Outcome: Device completes self-test without faults.

Check Firmware and Device Updates

  • Confirm device firmware is current.
  • Some self-test or indicator faults may be resolved with manufacturer-recommended updates.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out.

Device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled “Out of Service”
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to escalate is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot on an active patient.

Always ensure a backup AED is available before performing checks.

Document all steps taken for compliance and safety tracking.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device fails self-test and status indicator shows fault on power-on."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery seated correctly, pads verified; self-test failure persists. Likely internal fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Device removed from service, labeled “Out of Service,” sent to manufacturer for evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery checked and swapped
  • Pad connectors inspected
  • Indicator lights observed
  • Error codes or messages recorded
  • Device status after attempted self-test

Final Thought

Patient safety comes first, and methodical, logic-based troubleshooting prevents wasted time and risk. Accurate documentation and timely escalation protect both patients and staff.

That is successful troubleshooting.
