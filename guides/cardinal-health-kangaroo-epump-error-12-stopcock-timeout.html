Cardinal Health Kangaroo ePump

Error: 12 Stopcock timeout

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo ePump

What This Guide Helps With

Error: 12 Stopcock timeout indicates a failure of the internal valve or stopcock mechanism to actuate within the expected time. This can interrupt feeding and prevent the pump from delivering nutrition safely.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use if actively infusing
  • Transition feeding to a backup pump or alternate method
  • Confirm feeding continues without interruption

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm “Error: 12” is displayed
  • Note when the error occurs (startup or during feeding)
  • Listen for internal movement or lack of mechanical actuation

3. Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the pump off
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power the pump back on
  • Observe if the error clears

4. Inspect Pump Set and Loading

  • Remove and reinstall the feeding set
  • Ensure tubing is properly seated in the pump channel
  • Verify a correct and compatible Kangaroo set is being used

5. Check for Obstructions or Resistance in Flow Path

  • Inspect tubing for kinks or occlusions
  • Ensure all clamps are open
  • Verify formula is able to flow freely

6. Observe Mechanical Function

  • Start a feed cycle
  • Listen for normal internal valve or motor movement
  • Note any abnormal sounds (clicking, grinding, or no movement)

If the Problem Persists

If Error: 12 continues after all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • The issue likely indicates internal stopcock or valve control failure
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the unit

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a feeding pump with internal control errors while connected to a patient. Always transition to a functioning device first to prevent interruption in nutrition delivery.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 12 Stopcock timeout and unable to deliver feeding."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Error persisted after power cycle, proper set loading, and tubing checks, indicating internal stopcock/valve control failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump from service after external checks; labeled Out of Service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error: 12 reflects a timing failure in a critical internal flow control component. After verifying setup and flow conditions, timely escalation prevents unnecessary troubleshooting delays and ensures patient safety.

That is successful troubleshooting.
