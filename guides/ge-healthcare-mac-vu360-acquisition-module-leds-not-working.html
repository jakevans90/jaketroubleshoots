GE Healthcare MAC VU360

Acquisition Module LEDs Not Working

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

MAC VU360

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Acquisition Module electrode-placement LEDs that do not illuminate, cycle, or indicate electrode status correctly.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the MAC VU360 is not actively connected to a patient before troubleshooting the Acquisition Module LEDs.

Expected outcome: Troubleshooting is performed only when it is safe and does not interrupt patient care.

If the device is needed for an active ECG, have staff use another ECG machine before continuing.

Confirm the Reported LED Problem

Ask staff what they observed.

Identify whether:

  • No Acquisition Module LEDs illuminate
  • Only one LED does not work
  • Several LEDs do not work
  • LEDs illuminate but do not match electrode status
  • LEDs worked intermittently
  • The Acquisition Module itself is not being recognized

Expected outcome: The failure pattern is understood before parts are suspected.

Check That the Acquisition Module Is Connected

Inspect the Acquisition Module connection to the MAC VU360.

Make sure the module cable is fully seated and not loose, strained, pinched, or damaged.

Expected outcome: A poor module connection is ruled out as the reason the LEDs are not functioning.

If reseating the connection restores LED function, document the loose connection and stop.

Inspect the Acquisition Module Externally

Check the Acquisition Module for cracks, fluid intrusion, impact damage, missing labels, damaged LED windows, or signs of contamination.

Expected outcome: Visible external damage is identified before diagnostics are run.

If there is fluid damage, burning smell, unusual heat, or physical damage, remove the device from service and escalate for bench evaluation.

Check the Module Cable and Connector

Inspect the cable and connector pins or contact areas for bent pins, debris, corrosion, looseness, or damaged strain relief.

Expected outcome: The module communication and power path appears physically intact.

If connector damage is found, do not continue using the module clinically until evaluated or repaired.

Power Cycle the MAC VU360

Turn the device off, wait briefly, and power it back on.

Observe whether the Acquisition Module initializes normally and whether the LEDs respond.

Expected outcome: A temporary software or communication issue is cleared.

If the LEDs begin working normally after restart, verify operation and return the device to service if no other issues are present.

Run Acquisition LED Diagnostics

From the Service > Diagnostics screen, run Acquisition LED Diagnostics.

Start the test and observe the Acquisition Module LEDs as the test cycles them on one by one.

Expected outcome: All Acquisition Module LEDs illuminate in the expected sequence during the diagnostic test.

If all LEDs cycle correctly, the LED hardware is likely functional. Continue checking electrode setup, leadwires, or staff-reported use conditions if the original complaint involved electrode status indication.

Identify Which LEDs Fail During Diagnostics

If the diagnostic does not pass, note the exact behavior.

Record whether:

  • No LEDs illuminate
  • Only one LED fails
  • LEDs are dim
  • LEDs flicker
  • LEDs do not cycle in order
  • The test does not start
  • The Acquisition Module is not detected

Expected outcome: The failure is documented clearly enough to support repair evaluation.

Check for Leadwire or Electrode Misinterpretation

If the LEDs illuminate during diagnostics but staff report incorrect electrode indications, check the patient cable, leadwires, electrodes, and electrode placement.

Poor skin prep, dry electrodes, damaged leadwires, or bad lead connectors can make LED behavior appear incorrect even when the LEDs themselves work.

Expected outcome: LED function is separated from electrode impedance or lead connection problems.

If replacing electrodes or leadwires resolves the reported issue, document the accessory fault and stop.

Do Not Replace Parts Until External Causes Are Ruled Out

Before replacing the Acquisition Module or related parts, confirm that the module connection, cable, connector, leadwires, electrodes, and diagnostic results have been checked.

Expected outcome: The repair path is based on verified failure, not assumption.

If the Problem Persists

If the Acquisition LED Diagnostics fail or the LEDs do not illuminate correctly after connection, cable, connector, and accessory checks, the issue is likely internal to the Acquisition Module, module cable path, connector, or related electronics.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue clinical use if the Acquisition Module LEDs cannot be verified and the device may not reliably support electrode-placement feedback.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot Acquisition Module LED failures on an active patient. If ECG acquisition is needed, move the patient to another available ECG machine first, then troubleshoot the affected MAC VU360 when safe.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the GE MAC VU360 Acquisition Module electrode-placement LEDs were not lighting during ECG setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Acquisition LED Diagnostics showed that the Acquisition Module LEDs did not cycle correctly, and external module cable and connector checks did not resolve the issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the MAC VU360 from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for bench evaluation of the Acquisition Module and connection path."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the issue affected all LEDs or only specific LEDs
  • Whether the Acquisition Module was recognized by the device
  • Whether Acquisition LED Diagnostics passed or failed
  • Whether LEDs cycled one by one during diagnostics
  • Module cable condition
  • Module connector condition
  • Any visible damage, fluid exposure, unusual heat, or smell
  • Whether leadwires or electrodes were swapped
  • Whether rebooting changed the symptom
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Acquisition Module LED problems should be approached logically. Confirm patient safety first, then check the module connection, cable, connector, and diagnostics before suspecting internal failure. Clear CCR documentation helps repair staff understand whether the problem is an LED failure, module communication issue, accessory issue, or intermittent connection problem.

That is successful troubleshooting.
