Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI

Alarm: Dead Battery Error

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo OMNI

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a Dead Battery Error on the Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI feeding pump when battery failure is imminent and feeding or flushing may stop due to battery loss. This alarm should be treated as an urgent power condition because the pump may stop delivering fluid if the battery can no longer support operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the Kangaroo OMNI is actively feeding or flushing and the Dead Battery Error is present, notify clinical staff immediately.
  • Do not allow the pump to continue operating on battery power with this alarm active.
  • If therapy must continue, transfer the feeding setup to a known-good pump or connect the current pump to AC power immediately.
  • Expected: Patient feeding or flushing is protected from interruption.
  • Why it matters: This alarm indicates the pump may stop delivering fluid very soon.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows Dead Battery Error or Dead Battery Warning.
  • Ask staff whether the alarm occurred during feeding, during flushing, while unplugged from AC power, shortly after being placed on battery, or while already connected to wall power.
  • Confirm whether the pump stopped delivering fluid or required operation to be restarted.
  • Expected: The alarm and associated loss-of-power behavior are clearly confirmed.
  • Why it matters: This separates a true dead battery condition from a low battery notice or charging complaint.

Connect the Pump to Wall Power Immediately

  • Plug the Kangaroo OMNI into a known-working wall outlet using the approved power adapter.
  • Confirm the adapter is fully seated in the pump power adapter port.
  • Confirm the outlet is live by using another device or an outlet tester if needed.
  • Expected: The pump recognizes AC power and can be restarted.
  • Why it matters: A dead battery alarm means battery loss is near, and AC power is the fastest way to prevent shutdown.

Restart Pump Operation

  • Once AC power is connected, restart pump operation as required.
  • Confirm that the pump powers on normally and allows feeding or flushing to resume.
  • Verify that the programmed therapy settings are correct before returning to clinical use.
  • Expected: Pump operation restarts normally on AC power.
  • Why it matters: The alarm may stop delivery, so therapy should not be assumed to continue automatically.

Check for Charging Response

  • Leave the pump connected to AC power and observe whether the battery indicator shows charging activity.
  • Confirm there are no repeated dead battery, low battery, or power-related alarms while connected.
  • Expected: The pump remains powered and begins charging.
  • Why it matters: If AC power is present but the battery does not recover, the battery pack may no longer be functional.

Inspect External Power Components

  • Check the power adapter, cord, plug, and pump power port for loose connections, bent or damaged plugs, frayed cords, cracked adapter housing, contamination in the power port, or intermittent power when the cord is moved.
  • Try a known-good Kangaroo OMNI power adapter if available and approved for use.
  • Expected: External power components are intact and provide stable AC power.
  • Why it matters: A poor power connection can mimic battery failure if the pump is not actually charging.

Allow Adequate Charging Time

  • If the pump powers normally on AC power, allow the battery to charge according to facility practice.
  • Do not return the pump for battery-dependent use until charging and battery operation are verified.
  • Expected: Battery level improves after being connected to AC power.
  • Why it matters: The dead battery alarm may occur shortly before battery loss, so the battery may be deeply discharged.

Test Battery Operation After Charging

  • After sufficient charge time, disconnect AC power briefly in a controlled bench or non-patient setting.
  • Confirm the pump remains powered without immediately alarming for Dead Battery Error.
  • Reconnect AC power after the test.
  • Expected: Pump remains powered on battery without immediate Dead Battery Error.
  • Why it matters: This helps determine whether the issue was a discharged battery or a failed battery pack.

Identify Battery Pack Failure

  • If AC power is confirmed good but the pump will not charge, immediately returns to dead battery, or cannot maintain battery operation, suspect a failed battery pack.
  • Remove the pump from clinical use and route it for qualified battery replacement or bench evaluation.
  • Expected: A pump with suspected battery failure is not returned to patient use.
  • Why it matters: A battery that cannot support operation may stop feeding or flushing unexpectedly during transport or unplugged use.

Stop When Resolved

  • If the pump charges normally, restarts correctly, and passes battery operation verification, document the findings and return the pump to service.
  • Confirm the final status with clinical staff if the pump was removed from the floor.
  • Expected: The pump is only returned to service after stable power and battery behavior are verified.
  • Why it matters: Dead battery complaints can recur if the pump is returned before the battery is proven functional.

If the Problem Persists

If the Dead Battery Error continues after confirming the wall outlet, power adapter, power connection, and charging response, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent dead battery condition strongly indicates battery pack failure or an internal charging or power issue.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for qualified battery replacement or bench evaluation.

Do not continue cycling power or repeatedly restarting the pump for patient use.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a dead battery alarm on an active patient while relying on the pump for feeding or flushing. Connect to AC power immediately or move the patient to a backup device first. A pump with unresolved battery failure should not be used for transport or unplugged operation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Kangaroo OMNI feeding pump reported Dead Battery Error during use. Staff stated pump was near battery loss and feeding or flushing operation had to be restarted."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery was fully depleted or battery pack failed to recover charge after confirmed AC power connection. External power source and adapter connection were checked."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Connected pump to known-good wall outlet, verified power adapter seating, restarted operation, and monitored charging response. Pump continued to show dead battery condition and was removed from service for battery replacement or bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Dead Battery Error on the Kangaroo OMNI should be treated as an immediate patient-care risk because delivery may stop when battery loss occurs. Clinical Engineering should verify the alarm, confirm AC power and adapter integrity, restart operation safely, and determine whether the battery can recover. If the battery cannot charge or support operation, remove the pump from service and escalate for qualified repair. Clear CCR documentation protects the patient, the device history, and the next technician.

That is successful troubleshooting.
