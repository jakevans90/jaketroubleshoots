Abbott Precision Xceed Pro

Battery or Power Failure

Asset Type

Blood Glucose Meter

Manufacturer

Abbott

Model

Precision Xceed Pro

What This Guide Helps With

Meter will not power on, shuts off unexpectedly, or shows low battery despite charging or battery replacement.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the meter from patient use and obtain a backup device before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: No delay in patient testing
  • Why it matters: Prevents interruption in critical glucose monitoring

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to power on the device. Observe:
  • No response
  • Intermittent shutdown
  • Low battery indicator
  • Expected: Issue is confirmed and reproducible
  • Why it matters: Rules out user error or temporary condition

Check Battery Installation (Most Common Issue)

  • Remove and reinsert the batteries, ensuring correct orientation and proper seating.
  • Expected: Meter powers on normally
  • Why it matters: Misaligned or loose batteries can interrupt power

Inspect Battery Contacts

  • Check battery terminals inside the compartment for:
  • Dirt or debris
  • Corrosion
  • Bent contacts
  • Clean gently if needed using approved methods.
  • Expected: Clean, intact contacts with solid connection
  • Why it matters: Poor contact prevents proper power delivery

Replace with Known Good Batteries

  • Install new, manufacturer-recommended batteries. Avoid mixing old and new batteries.
  • Expected: Meter powers on and remains stable
  • Why it matters: Weak or incompatible batteries are a frequent cause

Check Battery Compartment Door

  • Ensure the battery cover is fully closed and latched.
  • Expected: Secure closure with no looseness
  • Why it matters: Some units require proper closure to complete the circuit

Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Check for:
  • Cracks in housing
  • Signs of fluid intrusion
  • Dropping or impact damage
  • Expected: No visible damage
  • Why it matters: Internal damage can interrupt power even if batteries are good

Perform a Power Reset

  • Remove batteries, wait 30–60 seconds, then reinstall.
  • Expected: Device resets and powers on
  • Why it matters: Clears minor electronic faults or lockups

If the Problem Persists

All common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (power regulation circuit, internal connection, or board-level fault).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a non-powering glucose meter during active patient care. Always switch to a verified working unit first to avoid delays in treatment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Nursing reported Precision Xceed Pro would not power on during glucose testing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery contacts found corroded, preventing proper electrical connection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned contacts and installed new batteries; device powered on and passed functional check."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • New batteries installed
  • Battery orientation verified
  • Contacts inspected/cleaned
  • Battery door condition confirmed
  • Power-on behavior observed
  • Any signs of physical damage
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting starts with simple power checks and progresses logically. Many power failures are caused by batteries or contact issues. Prioritize patient safety, avoid unnecessary disassembly, and escalate appropriately when internal faults are suspected. Proper documentation ensures clear communication and supports ongoing reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
