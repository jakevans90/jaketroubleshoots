Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: INSP PRES NOT REACH !!

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

The INSP PRES NOT REACH !! alarm indicates that the inspiratory pressure is below the set PINSP value, which can lead to inadequate ventilation and failure to achieve target airway pressure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from patient use if adequate ventilation cannot be confirmed
  • Transition to manual ventilation or an alternate machine if needed
  • Verify the patient is safely ventilated by alternate means

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm INSP PRES NOT REACH !! is present
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup vs during ventilation)
  • Check ventilation mode (Pressure Control, Pressure Support, SIMV/PS)
  • Compare airway pressure readings to set PINSP
  • Look for weak or incomplete breaths

Check Fresh Gas Flow and Supply

  • Confirm O2 and air sources are connected and pressurized
  • Verify flowmeter settings and total fresh gas flow
  • Inspect supply hoses for kinks or disconnections

Inspect Breathing Circuit for Leaks or Disconnections

  • Check all connections from machine to patient
  • Inspect hoses, bag, and fittings for cracks or leaks
  • Ensure CO2 absorber and canister are properly seated

Check APL Valve and Circuit Configuration

  • Ensure APL valve is not fully open during mechanical ventilation
  • Confirm correct mode (Manual vs Ventilator mode)
  • Verify no unintended bypass or misconfiguration

Evaluate Ventilator Settings

  • Confirm PINSP is set appropriately
  • Check for excessively high or unrealistic pressure settings
  • Verify alarm limits are reasonable

Check for Obstruction or Compliance Issues

  • Inspect circuit for kinks or partial obstruction
  • Evaluate patient factors such as low compliance or airway leaks
  • Verify proper airway placement and seal

Perform System Leak Test (if available)

  • Run a leak test per standard user-level procedure
  • Confirm system passes within acceptable limits

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and the alarm continues, this indicates a likely internal ventilator or pressure control issue.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot pressure delivery issues on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before investigating.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit alarming INSP PRES NOT REACH !! during ventilation; unable to achieve set inspiratory pressure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Leak identified in breathing circuit connection causing loss of pressure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated circuit connections and verified proper pressure delivery; alarm cleared."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

This alarm reflects a mismatch between expected and delivered pressure. Always rule out leaks and setup issues first, but escalate persistent failures as potential internal faults.

That is successful troubleshooting.
