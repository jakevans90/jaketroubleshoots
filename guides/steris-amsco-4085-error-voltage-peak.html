STERIS STERIS AMSCO 4085

Error: VOLTAGE PEAK

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

STERIS AMSCO 4085

What This Guide Helps With

Error: VOLTAGE PEAK indicating battery voltage exceeding 31V, creating an overvoltage condition that can lead to electrical instability, unpredictable table behavior, or system shutdown.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the table from active patient use
  • If in a procedure, safely transfer the patient to another stable surface
  • Ensure no movement functions are actively engaged

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the table and confirm “VOLTAGE PEAK” is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup, charging, or operation)
  • Attempt basic table functions if safe

3. Check Power Source and Charging Conditions

  • Verify the table is connected to a known good outlet
  • Inspect the power cord for damage or loose connections
  • Disconnect from mains and operate briefly on battery (if safe)

4. Inspect Battery Condition (External Only)

  • Check for swelling, heat, or leakage
  • Listen for unusual sounds
  • Check for burning odor

5. Power Cycle the System

  • Turn the table OFF
  • Disconnect from mains power
  • Wait 60 seconds
  • Reconnect and power ON

6. Isolate Charging vs Internal Regulation Issue

  • Operate the table on battery only
  • Reconnect to mains and monitor for error return
  • Determine if error occurs only during charging or at all times

7. Stop if Issue is Resolved

  • If the error clears and does not return, return the table to service
  • Monitor closely during initial use

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out. Persistent VOLTAGE PEAK error indicates an internal power regulation failure.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or continue using a surgical table with electrical instability on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Table displaying VOLTAGE PEAK error; unit not trusted for use due to overvoltage condition."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery voltage exceeding safe threshold (>31V), indicating likely internal power supply failure after external checks."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified power source, inspected connections, performed power cycle, and isolated issue. Removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Voltage-related errors should always be treated as high-risk conditions. Proper troubleshooting means ruling out simple causes first, then confidently escalating when internal faults are indicated.

That is successful troubleshooting.
