GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00] Over-Temperature Thermostat Open

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00] when an open over-temperature thermostat prevents reliable radiant heat operation on the GE Healthcare Panda Warmer.

This error involves the heater over-temperature protection circuit. Radiant heat may be unavailable, interrupted, or unsafe to rely on for patient care until the condition is resolved and proper operation is verified.

This error applies to Main Application Processor Version 1.1.0 and later.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the warmer from patient use if Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00] is displayed or radiant heat operation is interrupted or unreliable.
  • Transfer the infant to another approved warming device according to clinical procedures before beginning troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on the affected warmer for patient thermal support while the heater safety condition remains unresolved.

Expected outcome: The infant is no longer dependent on a warmer with an unresolved heater safety condition.

Why it matters: Continued use may result in unavailable or unreliable warming support.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, confirm that the display shows Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00].
  • Determine whether the failure occurs immediately at startup, after the heater has operated, after prolonged use, or intermittently during warm-up or operation.
  • Observe whether radiant heat is unavailable, interrupted, or accompanied by other system-failure messages.
  • Document the displayed error and the operating condition when it appears.

Expected outcome: The error is reproduced or clearly documented, along with the operating condition when it appears.

Why it matters: This confirms a heater safety-related fault rather than an incorrect report or unrelated operational issue.

3. Inspect the Heater Head and Airflow Openings

  • Power the warmer off and allow the heater head to cool before inspection.
  • Inspect the heater duct and heater head vents for blankets, linens, covers, dust, debris, contamination, or other external airflow obstructions.
  • Inspect for visible physical damage to the heater head or vent openings.
  • Remove any external obstruction that can be safely cleared without disassembling the heater head.

Expected outcome: The heater duct and heater head vents are visibly clear and unobstructed.

Why it matters: Restricted airflow may contribute to overheating and cause the over-temperature thermostat to open.

4. Check the Operating Environment and Recent Use History

  • Ask whether the warmer had recently been operating at elevated heat output or whether the error appeared after extended warming use.
  • Confirm that nothing was placed over or near the heater head that could trap heat or restrict ventilation.
  • Allow the heater head to cool fully before any controlled retest.
  • Power the warmer on only while it remains out of clinical service and observe whether the error returns.

Expected outcome: A temporary overheating condition caused by an external obstruction may clear after cooling and removal of the blockage.

Why it matters: This helps distinguish an external overheating condition from a persistent thermostat safety-circuit or power-control fault.

5. Confirm Power and External Condition

  • Inspect the power cord for secure connection and visible damage.
  • Use a known-good outlet according to facility procedures.
  • Check for evidence of liquid intrusion, contamination, unusual odor, heat damage, discoloration, or visible damage near the heater head or base.
  • Do not continue testing if external damage or evidence of overheating is identified.

Expected outcome: No external electrical or physical condition explains the error or creates an additional safety concern.

Why it matters: Visible damage, overheating, or contamination may indicate a broader safety issue requiring immediate removal from service.

6. Determine Whether the Error Has Cleared

  • After confirming the airflow openings are clear and the heater head has cooled, operate the warmer only in a controlled test setting according to facility procedures.
  • Verify whether Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00] returns during startup or heater operation.
  • If the error does not return, complete required functional and safety checks before returning the warmer to service and document the corrected obstruction or overheating condition.
  • If the error returns, stop troubleshooting at the external-check level and proceed with removal from service and bench evaluation.

Expected outcome: The warmer either completes testing without recurrence or is clearly identified as requiring repair.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00] remains present after the heater duct and heater head vents are confirmed clear, the unit has cooled, and basic external power and physical conditions have been checked, common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent error is consistent with a problem involving the over-temperature thermostat safety circuit, heater safety monitoring, associated wiring, or Power PCA that requires authorized service evaluation.

  • Remove the warmer from service.
  • Label the warmer Out of Service.
  • Send the warmer for authorized repair or bench evaluation.
  • Do not return the warmer to clinical use with an unresolved heater safety error.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first. Do not rely on a warmer displaying an unresolved over-temperature thermostat error for infant thermal support.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00] during warming operation, and radiant heat was unavailable or considered unreliable for patient use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Heater duct and heater head vents were inspected for obstruction. Restricted airflow was identified as a possible overheating cause, or the error returned after cooling with airflow openings clear, consistent with a persistent heater safety-circuit fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from patient use and inspected heater airflow openings. Cleared external obstruction and completed required verification when the error resolved, or labeled the unit Out of Service and routed it for authorized bench evaluation when the error persisted."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Sys Fail 06 [0B XX 00 00] is a heater safety-related error that should be taken seriously. Begin with patient safety and external airflow restrictions, then escalate appropriately when the over-temperature thermostat condition persists. Clear documentation supports safe repair decisions and future trend review.

That is successful troubleshooting.
