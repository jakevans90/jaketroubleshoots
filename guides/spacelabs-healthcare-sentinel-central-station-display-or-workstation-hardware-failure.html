Spacelabs Healthcare Sentinel Central Station

Display or Workstation Hardware Failure

Asset Type

Central Monitoring Station

Manufacturer

Spacelabs Healthcare

Model

Sentinel Central Station

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes when the central station display or workstation fails, freezes, or shows visual artifacts, including hardware, power, or peripheral issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm patients are being actively monitored at the bedside. Move monitoring to another station if necessary.

Check Power and Cables

  • Verify AC power is connected and outlet is live. Inspect power cords for damage or loose connections. A display may fail if power is unstable.

Inspect Workstation Indicators

  • Check for LEDs, fan operation, and audible alerts. No lights or fan movement may indicate a power or motherboard issue.

Confirm Peripheral Connections

  • Ensure keyboard, mouse, and monitor cables are fully seated. Loose connections can cause workstation unresponsiveness or display failure.

Reboot the Workstation Safely

  • If safe, perform a controlled reboot. Observe if the display initializes correctly. Successful reboot may resolve temporary software or firmware hang-ups.

Swap Monitor or Workstation Components

  • If available, connect a known-good monitor or workstation to verify if the failure follows the hardware or remains with the station.

Check for Network or Server Impact

  • Confirm the central station is communicating with network servers. Some failures may appear as display issues but are caused by server or network communication problems.

If the Problem Persists

External and peripheral causes have likely been ruled out. Remove the station from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for vendor repair or bench evaluation. Do not attempt internal hardware repairs yourself unless trained and authorized.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot the workstation while patients are actively monitored. Always ensure alternate monitoring is in place before testing or rebooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Central station display frozen; no response from keyboard or mouse."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Power and peripherals verified; workstation unresponsive after reboot."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Station removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • Power cord inspected and reseated
  • Monitor and peripheral cables checked
  • LED indicators observed
  • Reboot attempt and result
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Patient safety is the priority. Follow a logical troubleshooting sequence, rule out external causes first, and escalate appropriately. Accurate documentation supports future maintenance and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
