Philips HeartStart MRx

Pads Connectivity / Pad Failure Alarms

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the HeartStart MRx defibrillator triggers:

  • “Pads not connected” or “Pad failure” alarms
  • Intermittent shock delivery warnings related to pads
  • False alarms despite pads being visibly attached

The focus is on external, logical troubleshooting steps before assuming internal board-level issues. Ensuring proper pad connection is critical for patient safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Pad Type and Expiry

  • Ensure pads are the correct type for the MRx (adult vs. pediatric).
  • Check expiration date; expired pads can fail to make proper contact.

Why: Expired or incorrect pads can cause connectivity errors.

Inspect Pads and Cables

  • Check pads for tears, wrinkles, or dried-out gel.
  • Examine cables for cuts, kinks, or broken connectors.

Why: Damaged pads or cables often trigger failure alarms.

Reconnect Pads Firmly

  • Remove and reattach pads ensuring connectors click securely into the device.
  • Avoid bending or twisting the cable at the connector.

Why: Loose connections are the most common cause of pad alarms.

Clean Connector Contacts

  • Use a lint-free cloth to remove dust, gel residue, or moisture from pad connectors.
  • Avoid liquids; only dry cleaning is recommended.

Why: Contaminated contacts can interrupt electrical continuity.

Check Device Self-Test and Battery

  • Run the MRx self-test with pads attached.
  • Ensure battery charge is sufficient; low voltage can cause pad errors.

Why: Power fluctuations can be misinterpreted as pad faults.

Swap Pads with Known Good Set

  • If available, attach a different set of pads to confirm whether the issue is pad-related.

Why: Isolates whether the alarm is due to the pads or the device itself.

Document Findings

  • Note pad type, expiry, condition, and any error codes during testing.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have ruled out pad damage, connection issues, and battery problems.

Device may have an internal connector fault or PCB issue.

  • Remove MRx from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send to Philips service or authorized repair bench for evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to deliver therapy on a patient while troubleshooting connectivity errors.

Move patient to another operational defibrillator if available.

Only use MRx once pad connectivity is verified and self-test passes.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Pad failure alarm” during patient care.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Pads were expired and connector contacts were dirty; self-test failed.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Replaced with new pads, cleaned connectors, and verified self-test passed before returning to service.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Pad expiration date and type
  • Cable condition and connector integrity
  • Self-test results
  • Battery charge level
  • Final device status: cleared for service

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount—never deliver therapy with a suspected pad failure. Systematic external checks often resolve pad alarms quickly. Proper documentation ensures traceability and reduces repeat issues. When external troubleshooting is exhausted, escalate appropriately to repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
