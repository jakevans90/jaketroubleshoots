STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Exhaust Rate Too Fast – Liquid cycle exhaust occurring faster than expected cooling rate

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Alarm: Exhaust Rate Too Fast condition where chamber exhaust occurs faster than the expected cooling rate during a liquid cycle, which can impact proper cooling and compromise sterilization integrity.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from active use if processing critical items
  • Do not rely on loads from a cycle with unresolved exhaust rate alarms
  • Ensure no questionable loads are released for patient use

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm Alarm: Exhaust Rate Too Fast is displayed
  • Verify it occurs during the exhaust phase of a liquid cycle
  • Observe chamber pressure dropping faster than expected

Check Cycle Selection and Configuration

  • Ensure a liquid cycle is selected
  • Confirm no incorrect cycle parameters were modified
  • Verify standard cycle configuration is in use

Inspect Chamber Load

  • Ensure liquid containers are appropriate and not overfilled
  • Confirm proper spacing between items
  • Verify no obstruction of drain or contact with chamber walls

Check Facility Steam Supply Stability

  • Verify consistent steam pressure
  • Check for recent facility steam interruptions or fluctuations

Listen and Observe Exhaust Operation

  • Listen for abnormal or continuous venting
  • Observe rapid pressure drop on display
  • Compare behavior to expected gradual exhaust

Check for Obvious Valve or Drain Issues

  • Inspect for visible steam discharge during exhaust
  • Check for abnormal noise or continuous venting
  • Look for signs of leakage at exhaust points

Rule Out Environmental or Installation Factors

  • Ensure drain system is not excessively assisted
  • Confirm no abnormal piping slope or vacuum effects
  • Verify installation meets standard conditions

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Likely causes include solenoid valve malfunction (S40 or S03), steam trap malfunction, or control calibration issues
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or calibration

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the load or process to a backup sterilizer before addressing this issue.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer displayed 'Exhaust Rate Too Fast' alarm during liquid cycle; chamber pressure dropped rapidly during exhaust phase."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"External factors ruled out; exhaust occurring faster than expected indicating possible solenoid valve or steam trap malfunction."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Unit removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and referred for repair and calibration."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Exhaust rate control is critical for proper liquid cycle cooling and sterilization. Always verify setup and environmental conditions first, then escalate when control components are suspected to maintain safety and reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
