Hamilton G5

Error: TF 5505 Tank pressure above 550 mbar indicating a leaking mixer valve

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Hamilton

Model

G5

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: TF 5505 Tank pressure above 550 mbar indicating a leaking mixer valve on the Hamilton G5 ventilator. This condition indicates excessive tank pressure consistent with a leaking mixer valve, activates a non-silenceable high-priority alarm, and causes the ventilator to enter ambient state rather than provide intended mechanical ventilation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately provide alternative ventilation using an approved backup ventilator or manual ventilation method according to clinical protocol.
  • Do not continue relying on the Hamilton G5 after TF 5505 occurs.
  • Do not perform troubleshooting while the device is connected to an active patient.

Expected outcome: The patient is safely ventilated using an approved alternative method before troubleshooting begins.

Why it matters: TF 5505 causes the ventilator to enter ambient state, meaning it cannot be relied upon to provide intended mechanical ventilation.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm that the ventilator displays Error: TF 5505.
  • Determine whether the fault occurred during startup, pre-use testing, or attempted ventilation.
  • Confirm whether the high-priority alarm is active and cannot be silenced.
  • Confirm whether the ventilator entered ambient state.

Expected outcome: The reported TF 5505 condition and associated loss of normal ventilation are confirmed.

Why it matters: Confirming the reported behavior identifies a true system-reported mixer pressure fault rather than an unrelated operating concern.

3. Inspect Power, Gas Sources, and External Connections

  • Confirm the ventilator is connected to a stable AC power source.
  • Verify that the air and oxygen hoses are correctly connected and are not crossed, kinked, visibly damaged, or contaminated.
  • Inspect facility gas connections and inlet fittings for secure attachment, visible damage, moisture, debris, or contamination.
  • Confirm that the external gas source setup is appropriate for the ventilator.

Expected outcome: External power and gas supply connections are secure, correct, and free of visible defects.

Why it matters: Although TF 5505 indicates excessive internal tank pressure, external setup concerns should be ruled out before repair escalation.

4. Inspect for Visible Damage or Environmental Factors

  • Examine the exterior of the ventilator for evidence of impact, recent transport damage, liquid ingress, or visible contamination.
  • Inspect the area around gas inlet fittings for obvious damage.
  • Document any unusual sound, odor, or heat reported when the fault occurred.

Expected outcome: No external damage or environmental condition is identified that would make further evaluation unsafe.

Why it matters: Damage or contamination may contribute to pneumatic malfunction and can support the need for immediate bench evaluation.

5. Review the Event Log

  • Review the ventilator event log according to facility procedure.
  • Confirm that TF 5505 was recorded.
  • Determine whether the fault occurred once or repeatedly.
  • Look for related mixer, pressure, gas supply, or other technical faults near the same time.

Expected outcome: Event history confirms the fault and helps determine whether it is isolated or part of a broader pneumatic failure.

Why it matters: Repeated or related technical faults strengthen the indication of an internal mixer valve or pressure-sensing problem.

6. Perform Approved Mixer Calibration and Checks

  • With the ventilator removed from patient use, perform the approved Mixer Calibration & Checks in Test Mode according to authorized Hamilton service documentation and facility policy.
  • Do not bypass alarms or attempt clinical operation while evaluating this fault.
  • Document whether testing passes, fails, or causes TF 5505 to recur.

Expected outcome: Approved testing either confirms normal operation without recurrence or identifies a persistent mixer pressure fault requiring repair evaluation.

Why it matters: TF 5505 is consistent with excessive tank pressure caused by a mixer valve that may not be closing correctly.

7. Confirm Final Device Status

  • If approved testing passes, TF 5505 does not recur, and all required operational verification is successfully completed, return the device to service only according to facility policy.
  • If TF 5505 recurs, remains unresolved, or approved testing fails, stop clinical troubleshooting and escalate the device for repair evaluation.

Expected outcome: The ventilator is either verified for safe return to service or correctly removed from service for repair.

Why it matters: A ventilator with an unresolved ambient-state technical fault must not be returned to patient use.

If the Problem Persists

If TF 5505 persists after external gas connections, visible condition, event history, and approved mixer checks have been evaluated, external causes have been ruled out and an internal mixer valve system or pressure-sensing failure is strongly indicated.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the ventilator for authorized repair or bench evaluation.
  • Document the suspected internal mixer valve or sensor-board-related failure for further service assessment.

Do not return the ventilator to patient use while TF 5505 remains unresolved.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this fault on an active patient. A Hamilton G5 displaying TF 5505 has entered ambient state and cannot be trusted to provide required ventilation. Move the patient to an approved backup ventilator or alternative ventilation method first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Hamilton G5 ventilator displayed Error: TF 5505, sounded a non-silenceable high-priority alarm, and entered ambient state during startup or attempted operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Event log confirmed TF 5505 indicating tank pressure above 550 mbar and suspected leaking mixer valve. External gas connections and visible condition were checked with no correctable external cause identified. Approved Mixer Calibration & Checks failed or the fault recurred."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed ventilator from service, labeled unit Out of Service, documented event log and test findings, and routed unit for authorized bench evaluation and repair due to unresolved TF 5505 mixer pressure fault."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

TF 5505 is a critical ventilation failure condition that requires immediate patient protection, logical external checks, review of recorded fault information, approved testing, and escalation when the fault remains unresolved. Clear documentation helps ensure the failure and repair decision are traceable.

That is successful troubleshooting.
