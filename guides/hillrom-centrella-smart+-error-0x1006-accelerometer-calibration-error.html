Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x1006 Accelerometer Calibration Error

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0x1006 indicating accelerometer calibration failure causing inaccurate bed position sensing or motion-related features not functioning properly.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use if position sensing or movement features are unreliable
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed if needed

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error: 0x1006 is displayed
  • Observe when the error appears (startup vs during operation)
  • Check for loss of movement tracking or incorrect angle readings

Perform a Power Reset

  • Unplug the bed from AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Restore power and reboot the system

Check Bed Position and Stability

  • Ensure bed is on a flat, level surface
  • Lock all wheels
  • Remove any tilt, obstruction, or uneven loading

Perform Accelerometer Calibration

  • Initiate calibration via service or user interface
  • Ensure bed remains completely stationary during calibration

Repeat Calibration if Needed

  • Repeat calibration once if the first attempt fails
  • Ensure environmental conditions are stable during retry

Inspect for External Interference or Damage

  • Check for loose or damaged frame components
  • Ensure no vibration or movement is affecting the bed
  • Confirm no mechanical instability is present

Verify MCB (Main Control Board) Part Number

  • Confirm correct MCB is installed
  • Verify compatibility with Centrella Smart+

If the Problem Persists

If calibration repeatedly fails and all external conditions are correct:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Error 0x1006 indicates likely internal sensor or control board failure
  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the MCB

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup bed before addressing calibration or sensor-related issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x1006 with inaccurate position readings."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Accelerometer calibration repeatedly failed despite stable conditions."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed calibration attempts and power reset. Verified stability and correct MCB. Error persisted; bed removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Accelerometer errors directly impact position accuracy and safety features. Always verify stability and calibration first before escalating to internal component failure.

That is successful troubleshooting.
