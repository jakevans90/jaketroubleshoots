3M Ranger 245

Temperature Sensor Malfunction Causing Inaccurate Warming

Asset Type

Fluid & Blood Warmer

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Ranger 245

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the 3M Ranger 245 fluid and blood warmer may be warming fluids inaccurately due to potential temperature sensor malfunction. Common external causes, including disposable cassette issues, power irregularities, or environmental conditions, are addressed before assuming internal sensor failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Device Power

  • Ensure the unit is plugged into a known, functioning outlet.
  • Check power indicator lights.

Why: Temperature sensors may not function properly if the unit receives inconsistent power.

Check Disposable Cassette Installation

  • Remove and reseat the disposable warming cassette according to proper alignment.
  • Inspect cassette for visible damage, contamination, or deformation.

Why: Misalignment or damaged cassettes can prevent correct temperature sensing.

Inspect Environmental Factors

  • Ensure adequate airflow around the device; avoid covering vents.
  • Confirm ambient temperature is within operational range (typically 18–30°C).

Why: Overheating or drafty conditions can skew sensor readings.

Monitor Device Operation

  • Run a warming cycle with a test fluid, observing the displayed temperature.
  • Note any temperature fluctuations or error messages.

Why: Observing behavior helps confirm if the issue is consistent and sensor-related.

Swap Cables and Power Sources (if applicable)

  • Try a different power cord or outlet if intermittent readings occur.

Why: Electrical noise or poor connection can affect sensor accuracy.

Reset or Power Cycle the Device

  • Turn off, wait 30 seconds, then turn on the device.
  • Observe if the temperature reading stabilizes.

Why: A system reset can clear temporary faults affecting sensor readings.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The temperature sensor or internal electronics are likely malfunctioning.

  • Remove the unit from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send the device to a qualified repair bench or manufacturer service for sensor replacement.

Clinical Engineering note: Do not attempt internal sensor repair beyond external verification—this ensures safety and preserves warranty.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not use the unit on a patient if temperature readings are unreliable.
  • Move patient fluids to another verified warming device before further use.
  • Always verify fluid temperature with an independent thermometer if there is any doubt.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ranger 245 warming unit displays inconsistent temperatures during fluid warming."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Device observed to have temperature fluctuations despite properly seated disposable cassette and stable power source; environmental factors confirmed acceptable.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for sensor inspection/replacement at service bench.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Cassette inspected and reseated
  • Observed temperature deviations and error messages
  • Device location and ambient temperature
  • Final status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Always confirm external causes before assuming internal sensor failure. Properly escalating the device for repair prevents harm and maintains clinical reliability. Thorough documentation in the CCR format ensures clear communication and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
