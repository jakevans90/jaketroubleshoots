Philips V60 Ventilator

Fails Self-Test / Flow Sensor Error

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

V60

Inspect the Patient Circuit

  • Ensure all tubing connections are secure and fully seated.
  • Check for cracks, holes, or loose fittings.
  • Confirm there are no kinks or obstructions.
    Why: Even a small leak or disconnection can trigger a flow sensor error.

Check the Flow Sensor Module

  • Locate the flow sensor (usually near the patient connection or ventilator port).
  • Verify it is properly installed and seated.
  • Inspect for visible damage, moisture, or condensation.
    Why: A loose or wet sensor prevents accurate flow readings.

Verify Humidifier and Additional Circuit Components

  • Ensure the humidifier is seated correctly and tubing is fully connected.
  • Check other inline devices (filters, adapters) for leaks or added resistance.
    Why: Extra resistance or leaks can trigger flow sensor alarms.

Observe the Ventilator Display

  • Note the error code and any accompanying messages.
  • Document the error for Clinical Engineering tracking.

Attempt a Simple Reset

  • Power off the ventilator.
  • Reseat the circuit and flow sensor connections.
  • Power on and rerun the self-test.
    Why: Sometimes a simple reseating clears false errors.

If the Flow Sensor Error Persists

  • After completing the above steps, if the ventilator still reports a flow sensor error:
  • Common external causes have been ruled out.
  • The issue is likely internal (flow sensor module, electronics, or calibration).
  • Next steps:
    • Remove the ventilator from clinical service.
    • Label as Out of Service.
    • Send for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Knowing when to stop troubleshooting is part of proper Clinical Engineering practice.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot on an active patient.
  • Move the patient to another ventilator before performing any checks.
  • Only interact with the circuit and ventilator when it is safe to do so.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Ventilator fails self-test; flow sensor error.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Circuit and flow sensor inspected and reseated, humidifier checked, no visible damage. Error persists. Suspected internal flow sensor or electronics issue.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Removed ventilator from service, labeled out of service, sent for repair.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Circuit inspected or replaced
  • Flow sensor reseated
  • Humidifier and inline devices checked
  • Error cleared or persisted
  • Any visible damage, moisture, or condensation
  • Final disposition (returned to service or sent for repair)

That is successful troubleshooting.
