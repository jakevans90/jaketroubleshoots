Philips BV Pulsera

Workstation / Monitor Cart Communication Problem

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

BV Pulsera

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting communication loss between the BV Pulsera C-arm and workstation/monitor cart using safe external checks first.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the system during an active procedure unless clinical staff confirm it is safe.

Action:

  • Confirm whether the C-arm is currently being used for patient imaging.
  • If the issue occurs during a case, notify clinical staff immediately.
  • If imaging is needed urgently, have staff move to another available C-arm if possible.
  • Do not repeatedly power-cycle the system during an active procedure without clinical approval.

Expected outcome: Patient care is protected before equipment troubleshooting begins.

Why it matters: A communication problem between the C-arm and monitor cart can affect image display, image transfer, system control, and procedural workflow.

Verify the Reported Problem

Confirm exactly what is failing.

Action:

  • Ask staff what they observed:
  • Monitor cart not displaying image
  • Workstation not responding
  • C-arm not detected by workstation
  • Live image not appearing
  • Stored images unavailable
  • Error message related to communication
  • Intermittent disconnect between stand and cart
  • Check whether the C-arm powers up normally and whether the monitor cart powers up normally.

Expected outcome: The failure is narrowed to communication between the C-arm stand and monitor/workstation cart, not a total power failure.

If the issue is actually no power to either the C-arm or cart, troubleshoot power first and stop this guide.

Check AC Power to Both the C-Arm and Monitor Cart

Verify both parts of the system are powered correctly.

Action:

  • Confirm the C-arm stand is plugged into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Confirm the monitor/workstation cart is plugged in if it uses separate AC power.
  • Check that power cords are fully seated and not damaged.
  • Verify outlet power using an outlet tester or another known-good device if needed.
  • Look for tripped breakers, loose wall receptacles, or switched outlets.

Expected outcome: Both the C-arm and monitor cart receive stable AC power.

If power is restored and communication returns, document the outlet or power connection issue and stop.

Inspect the Communication Cable Between the C-Arm and Cart

Check the external cable path between the C-arm stand and monitor/workstation cart.

Action:

  • Locate the communication/interface cable between the C-arm and monitor cart.
  • Inspect both ends for loose connections.
  • Reseat the cable at both the C-arm and cart ends.
  • Check for bent pins, damaged connectors, broken locking hardware, crushed cable jacket, or strain damage.
  • Verify the cable is routed safely and not pinched under wheels, doors, pedals, or cart movement points.

Expected outcome: The communication cable is fully seated and visibly undamaged.

If reseating the cable restores communication, perform a functional check and stop.

Confirm the Correct Cart Is Connected to the Correct C-Arm

Verify that the monitor cart belongs with the BV Pulsera system being used.

Action:

  • Check asset tags, serial numbers, labels, or department markings.
  • Confirm the cart has not been swapped with another Philips C-arm system.
  • Ask staff whether carts or stands were moved recently.

Expected outcome: The correct monitor/workstation cart is connected to the correct C-arm stand.

Why it matters: C-arm systems are often moved between rooms, and mismatched carts can create communication or configuration problems.

If a cart mismatch is found, reconnect the correct components, test the system, document the finding, and stop.

Check for Mechanical Damage From Movement

Inspect for obvious damage caused by transport or repositioning.

Action:

  • Look at the cable connectors, cart docking area, cable strain reliefs, monitor cart base, and rear connection panel.
  • Check whether the system was recently moved through doorways, elevators, or tight hallways.
  • Look for impact marks, cracked housings, loose panels, damaged wheels, or signs that a cable was pulled.

Expected outcome: No visible transport-related damage is found.

If damage is found, remove the system from service and send it for repair evaluation.

Power Down and Restart the System Safely

Perform a controlled restart only when it is safe to interrupt imaging.

Action:

  • Confirm with clinical staff that the system is not needed for an active patient.
  • Power down the C-arm and monitor/workstation cart using the normal shutdown process.
  • Wait long enough for the system to fully shut down.
  • Power the system back on in the normal sequence used by the site.
  • Observe startup behavior and check for communication errors.

Expected outcome: The workstation and C-arm re-establish communication after restart.

If the system communicates normally after restart, perform a basic functional check and document the software/communication reset.

Check the Monitor Cart Display and Workstation Behavior

Determine whether the cart is powered but not communicating, or if the cart itself is malfunctioning.

Action:

  • Check whether the monitors turn on.
  • Check whether the workstation computer boots normally.
  • Look for frozen screens, blank displays, startup errors, keyboard/mouse issues, or application lockups.
  • Confirm whether any live fluoroscopy image, stored image, or system status information is displayed.

Expected outcome: The monitor cart boots normally and only the C-arm communication path remains suspect.

If the cart does not boot, freezes, or has no display output, troubleshoot the monitor cart/workstation power or computer issue separately.

Check External Accessories and Connections

Verify that accessories are not creating confusion with the reported problem.

Action:

  • Inspect footswitches, hand controls, keyboard, mouse, image storage devices, printer connections, and network cables if present.
  • Disconnect non-essential external accessories only if approved by site policy and safe to do so.
  • Do not disconnect required imaging or safety-related connections during clinical use.

Expected outcome: No external accessory or loose peripheral connection is causing the reported workstation problem.

If removing or reseating a non-essential accessory restores normal operation, document the accessory connection issue and stop.

Confirm Whether the Problem Is Intermittent

Try to determine if the failure happens during movement or only at startup.

Action:

  • With the system safely out of patient use, gently observe whether communication drops when the cart or cable is moved.
  • Do not stress, bend, or pull cables aggressively.
  • Check whether the issue occurs after moving the system, turning the cart, locking wheels, or repositioning the C-arm.

Expected outcome: The issue is either stable or linked to movement/cable position.

If communication drops with movement, suspect cable, connector, strain relief, or internal connection damage. Remove the system from service.

Perform a Basic Functional Check After Communication Returns

Confirm the system is safe to return to service.

Action:

  • Verify the system boots without communication errors.
  • Confirm the monitor cart displays the expected system interface.
  • Confirm live image display is available using the site-approved test method.
  • Confirm image storage/review functions are available if normally used.
  • Check for unusual noises, heat, smell, flickering displays, or recurring error messages.

Expected outcome: The BV Pulsera communicates normally with the workstation/monitor cart and passes a basic operational check.

If the system passes, return it to service per site policy.

If the Problem Persists

If power, cable seating, cart matching, visible damage, restart behavior, and basic workstation function have been checked and the communication problem remains, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal communication interface, workstation hardware, system software, internal cabling, connector damage, or control electronics.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Do not continue repeated resets or cable manipulation if the communication failure returns. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a BV Pulsera communication problem while the C-arm is needed for an active procedure unless clinical leadership confirms it is safe. If imaging is required, move the patient or procedure workflow to another verified C-arm before continuing. Communication issues can delay image display and should be treated as a clinical workflow risk, not just a computer problem.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Philips BV Pulsera monitor cart was not communicating with the C-arm and no live image was displaying on the workstation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the communication cable between the C-arm stand and monitor cart partially seated at the cart connection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated the communication cable, restarted the system, verified live image display and workstation response, and returned the C-arm to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • C-arm power status
  • Monitor cart power status
  • Startup behavior
  • Exact error message, if displayed
  • Whether the cart monitors were blank, frozen, or working
  • Communication cable reseated
  • Cable or connector damage found
  • Cart and C-arm asset tags verified
  • Issue occurred after transport or room move
  • Intermittent or constant failure
  • Unusual sounds, heat, smell, or display flicker
  • Final device status

Final Thought

For a Philips BV Pulsera workstation or monitor cart communication issue, start with patient safety, then verify power, cable seating, cart matching, and visible damage before assuming an internal failure. C-arm systems are moved frequently, so loose or damaged external connections are always worth checking first. If the fault remains after basic external troubleshooting, remove the system from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
