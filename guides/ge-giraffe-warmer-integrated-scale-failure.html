GE Healthcare Giraffe Warmer

Integrated Scale Failure

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Giraffe Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting an integrated in-bed scale that will not weigh, displays incorrect weight, drifts, or reports a scale-related fault.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot or verify scale accuracy with an infant on the warmer.

If the scale reading is clinically needed, have clinical staff use another approved weighing method or transfer the patient to appropriate equipment before troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: The patient is safely managed without relying on an inaccurate or failed scale.

Why it matters: Weight data may influence clinical decisions, and GE identifies the integrated in-bed scale as the warmer’s bedside patient weighing option.

2. Confirm the Reported Failure

Obtain the exact complaint from staff and attempt to reproduce the issue with the warmer unoccupied.

Determine whether the reported problem is:

  • Scale option unavailable or not recognized.
  • Weight cycle will not start or complete.
  • Displayed weight is obviously inaccurate.
  • Weight reading drifts or fluctuates while the bed is undisturbed.
  • Scale-related message or fault appears on the display.

Document any displayed message, intermittent behavior, or conditions under which the failure occurs.

Expected outcome: The scale issue is clearly identified and reproducible before parts or accessories are considered.

If the issue cannot be reproduced: Document the reported symptom, perform a safe functional check, and return the device only if scale function is verified acceptable under facility procedure.

3. Inspect the Scale Platform, Mattress Area, and Bed Surface

Remove linens and accessories as needed to inspect the in-bed scale area.

Check for:

  • Debris or fluid beneath the mattress or scale platform.
  • Damaged, cracked, warped, or incorrectly seated scale components.
  • Objects contacting the mattress, scale platform, bed frame, side panels, or accessory trays.
  • Any accessory or cable applying force to the bed surface.

Reinstall the mattress and applicable bed components correctly after inspection.

Expected outcome: The scale platform is clean, seated correctly, undamaged, and free to measure weight without mechanical interference.

Why it matters: Physical contact or interference can cause inaccurate, unstable, or failed weight readings.

4. Verify the Bed Is Level and Undisturbed

Position the warmer on a stable floor surface and confirm the bed is level using the warmer’s level indicator.

Ensure no one is leaning on the warmer, touching the bed, moving attached cables, or contacting accessories during a weight attempt.

Expected outcome: The scale produces a stable reading without movement-related variation.

Why it matters: The Giraffe Warmer includes continuous bed tilt with a bubble level indicator, and an out-of-level or disturbed bed can affect accurate weight verification.

5. Inspect the Scale Cable and External Connection

With the warmer removed from patient use, inspect the accessible in-bed scale cable and connector area.

Check for:

  • Loose or partially seated scale connection.
  • Bent pins, damaged connector housing, or contamination.
  • Pinched, pulled, cut, or strained cable.
  • Scale cable routed across or against a corner tray or other bed component.

Reseat an accessible external connection only when permitted by department procedure and the connector is undamaged.

Expected outcome: The scale cable is intact, properly routed, and securely connected without mechanical strain.

Why it matters: GE service documentation identifies loose/damaged scale connectors, bent pins, and scale-cable interference with bed components as scale inspection points.

6. Power Cycle and Recheck Scale Recognition

After completing external inspection, power the warmer off according to facility procedure, wait briefly, then restart it.

Observe startup for scale-related faults and determine whether the scale function becomes available.

Expected outcome: The warmer recognizes the integrated scale and no scale fault remains present.

If the scale is restored: Perform an accuracy verification before returning the device to clinical use.

7. Perform a Safe Functional Weight Check

With the bed empty, correctly assembled, level, and free of interference, perform a scale check using an approved known test weight according to Clinical Engineering procedure.

Observe whether the displayed weight:

  • Appears stable.
  • Corresponds appropriately to the known test weight.
  • Repeats consistently when the test is performed again.

Expected outcome: The scale displays a stable and accurate value within the acceptance criteria established by approved GE documentation or facility procedure.

Why it matters: GE service material includes center and off-center scale verification using a known test weight; failed verification indicates the scale is not suitable for clinical use.

8. Determine Whether Calibration or Repair Evaluation Is Required

If the scale is recognized but does not pass an approved accuracy verification, do not attempt to compensate for the error clinically.

If authorized by your department and appropriate for the device configuration, proceed only with the approved scale calibration or performance-verification process from current GE service documentation.

Do not perform internal load-cell, circuit-board, or wiring repairs as part of frontline troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: A scale that passes approved verification may be returned to service; a scale that fails remains out of service for repair evaluation.

If the scale does not calibrate or repeatedly fails testing: Stop troubleshooting and escalate for repair.

If the Problem Persists

If the integrated scale remains unavailable, displays unstable or inaccurate weight, fails known-weight verification, or shows persistent scale faults after external checks, common external causes have been ruled out and the failure is likely within the scale assembly, connection path, or control system.

The warmer should be:

  • Removed from service for any use requiring the integrated scale.
  • Labeled Out of Service according to facility policy if the scale cannot be isolated from clinical reliance.
  • Sent for bench evaluation, approved repair, or manufacturer/service-provider support.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. An infant warmer with an unreliable scale must not be used when clinical staff could depend on that weight measurement.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot, calibrate, or verify the integrated scale with an infant on the warmer. Move the patient to an appropriate safe care location or use an alternate approved weighing method first.

Maintain therapy continuity by ensuring the infant remains supported on appropriate equipment throughout troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the GE Giraffe Warmer integrated scale would not provide a stable infant weight and readings varied during repeated attempts."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found the in-bed scale cable routed against the corner tray, creating mechanical interference with the scale platform."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Rerouted the scale cable away from the bed tray, leveled the bed, completed known-weight verification successfully, and returned the warmer to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the integrated scale option was visible on the display.
  • Any scale-related fault message or abnormal screen behavior.
  • Condition of the mattress, clear plate, and scale platform.
  • Whether fluid, debris, or mechanical interference was found.
  • Bed-level verification completed.
  • Scale cable condition and connector inspection results.
  • Approved test weight used and verification result.
  • Whether readings were stable, drifting, or intermittent.
  • Whether calibration or repair evaluation was required.
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed from service.

Final Thought

Integrated scale troubleshooting on a Giraffe Warmer should begin with patient safety, careful inspection of the bed and scale setup, and elimination of cable or mechanical interference. When the scale cannot be verified as accurate, appropriate escalation protects the patient and ensures that the work order clearly reflects the failure and corrective action.

That is successful troubleshooting.
