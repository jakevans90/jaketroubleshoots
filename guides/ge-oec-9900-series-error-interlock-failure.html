GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: INTERLOCK FAILURE

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm that displays INTERLOCK FAILURE during power-up. This error means the interlock circuits are in an incorrect state during startup, and the system shuts down to prevent operation under an unsafe or unresolved safety condition.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot a C-Arm interlock failure during an active procedure.
  • Remove the OEC 9900 from patient use if the system shuts down, will not complete startup, or displays INTERLOCK FAILURE.
  • Provide another imaging system if imaging is needed for patient care.
  • Do not attempt to bypass, tape, defeat, or override any interlock circuit.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the system on and observe the startup sequence.
  • Confirm the display shows INTERLOCK FAILURE.
  • Note whether the system shuts down immediately, powers partially, or fails only during startup.
  • Document whether the error is constant or intermittent.

Check Basic Power and Startup Conditions

  • Confirm the C-Arm and workstation are connected to the correct power source.
  • Verify the power cord is fully seated and not damaged.
  • Check for loose power connections, damaged plugs, tripped breakers, or facility power issues.
  • Test the wall outlet if there is any doubt about power quality.

Check for Obvious Mechanical or Cover Issues

  • Look for loose, open, misaligned, or damaged panels, covers, doors, or access areas.
  • Check whether any covers were recently removed for service or cleaning.
  • Inspect for bent brackets, missing screws, broken latches, or covers that are not fully seated.
  • Gently reseat user-accessible panels or covers that appear loose.

Check C-Arm Positioning, Locks, and Movement-Related Conditions

  • Verify the C-Arm is parked or positioned normally for startup.
  • Inspect mechanical locks, brakes, carriage position, orbital movement, and column movement for anything abnormal.
  • Look for signs of impact damage, binding, broken handles, or misaligned components.
  • Do not force movement if the system appears mechanically jammed.

Inspect External and Accessible Cable Connections

  • Power the system down before reseating connectors.
  • Inspect accessible cables between the C-Arm, workstation, monitor cart, power components, and system interface areas.
  • Look for loose connectors, bent pins, damaged cable jackets, crushed cables, or strain at connection points.
  • Reseat only external or safely accessible connectors.
  • Power the system back on and check whether INTERLOCK FAILURE returns.

Check for Recent Service, Transport, Cleaning, or Impact

  • Ask staff whether the system was recently moved, cleaned heavily, serviced, impacted, or stored in an unusual position.
  • Ask whether panels were opened or cables were pulled, stretched, or disconnected.
  • Inspect the area connected to any recent event.
  • Document anything that may have disturbed an interlock switch, cable, connector, latch, or bracket.

Inspect Accessible Interlock Areas Without Deep Disassembly

  • Look for visible interlock switches, actuator tabs, magnets, latch points, or switch brackets near accessible covers and safety panels.
  • Check for broken switch actuators, misaligned tabs, loose mounting hardware, contamination, bent brackets, disconnected wiring, or pinched wiring.
  • Do not perform board-level repair or deep internal teardown as part of basic troubleshooting.
  • Do not bypass interlocks to keep the C-Arm operational.

Power Cycle After Correcting Simple Issues

  • If a loose cover, connector, or obvious mechanical condition was corrected, power the OEC 9900 fully off.
  • Wait briefly for shutdown to complete.
  • Power the system back on and observe whether INTERLOCK FAILURE returns.
  • If the error clears, complete required operational checks before returning the device to service according to facility policy.

Determine Whether the Issue Is External, Mechanical, or Device-Related

  • A loose cover, panel, or latch may indicate a cover or access interlock issue.
  • A loose external cable may indicate a connection-related interlock issue.
  • Recent impact or transport may indicate switch, bracket, or wiring damage.
  • If no visible external cause is found, suspect an internal interlock circuit, wiring, switch, connector, or control hardware issue.

If the Problem Persists

If INTERLOCK FAILURE continues after checking power, covers, mechanical position, accessible cables, connectors, and obvious interlock switch areas, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Remove the GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm from service. Label it Out of Service and send it for repair, bench evaluation, or qualified service support.

Do not bypass the interlock circuit to keep the C-Arm operational.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this error on an active patient or during an active procedure. Move the patient or procedure to a backup imaging system first, then troubleshoot the OEC 9900 after patient care is protected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE OEC 9900 Series C-Arm displays INTERLOCK FAILURE during power-up and shuts down before completing startup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Interlock circuit state incorrect during startup. External checks performed for power, covers, mechanical position, cables, connectors, and accessible interlock areas. Persistent fault indicates likely interlock switch, wiring, connector, or internal interlock circuit failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified reported INTERLOCK FAILURE. Checked power source, visible panels and covers, mechanical positioning, external connections, and accessible interlock components. Error persisted. Removed C-Arm from service, labeled Out of Service, and referred for repair or qualified service evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

An INTERLOCK FAILURE on the GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series should be treated as a safety-related startup fault. Start with simple external checks, confirm covers and connections, inspect for obvious mechanical or switch issues, and never bypass safety circuits. If the error persists, remove the system from service and escalate for repair with clear documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
