Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI

Alarm: Low Battery Notice

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo OMNI

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Low Battery Notice alarms on the Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI feeding pump when the pump has about 30 minutes of battery life remaining. The pump may continue feeding or flushing during this alarm, but the condition should be corrected before the battery becomes depleted and therapy is interrupted.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the pump is actively feeding or flushing, confirm with clinical staff before interrupting therapy.
  • The pump may continue delivering fluid during the Low Battery Notice, but it should not be allowed to run until battery depletion.
  • Expected: Patient feeding or flushing continues safely while power is restored.
  • Why it matters: A low battery can eventually stop therapy if not corrected.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the displayed alarm is specifically Low Battery Notice.
  • Check whether the alarm occurs while running on battery, during feeding, during flushing, immediately after being unplugged, or while plugged into wall power.
  • Confirm whether the pump continues operating or powers down shortly after the alarm.
  • Expected: The alarm is confirmed and associated with low remaining battery life.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a battery or power condition rather than a feeding set, occlusion, or pump inactivity issue.

Connect the Pump to Wall Power

  • Plug the Kangaroo OMNI into a known-good wall outlet using the approved power adapter.
  • Confirm the adapter is connected securely to the pump power adapter port.
  • Expected: The pump recognizes external power and begins charging.
  • Why it matters: The Low Battery Notice is usually resolved by restoring external power and allowing the battery to recharge.

Verify the Wall Outlet

  • If the pump does not appear to charge, test the wall outlet with another approved device or outlet tester.
  • Move the pump to a known-good outlet if needed.
  • Expected: The outlet supplies power properly.
  • Why it matters: A dead outlet can make the pump appear to have a battery or charger problem.

Inspect the Power Adapter and Cord

  • Check for a loose connection at the pump.
  • Inspect the cord for cuts, cracks, strain relief damage, or intermittent charging when the cord is moved.
  • Check for a bent or damaged plug.
  • Confirm the adapter is approved and compatible with the Kangaroo OMNI.
  • Reseat the connector fully into the pump power adapter port.
  • Expected: The connection is secure and the pump charges normally.
  • Why it matters: A partially inserted adapter can cause the alarm to continue even while the pump appears plugged in.

Allow Adequate Charging Time

  • Leave the pump plugged into wall power for at least 12 hours to fully charge the battery.
  • Do not judge battery condition from a short charging period unless the pump still fails to recognize AC power.
  • Expected: Battery charge improves after a full charge cycle.
  • Why it matters: The alarm may return quickly if the pump was only briefly connected to power.

Recheck Operation After Charging

  • After charging, unplug the pump briefly and confirm whether it operates normally on battery without immediately displaying the Low Battery Notice.
  • Reconnect the pump to wall power after testing.
  • Expected: The pump holds enough charge for normal battery operation.
  • Why it matters: This helps separate a normally depleted battery from a battery that no longer holds charge.

Isolate Adapter Versus Pump or Battery Issue

  • If available, test with a known-good compatible power adapter.
  • If facility policy allows, test the reported adapter on another compatible Kangaroo OMNI pump.
  • Expected: The issue follows either the adapter or remains with the pump.
  • Why it matters: This helps determine whether the likely failure is accessory-related or pump-related.

Stop When Resolved

  • If the alarm clears after proper connection to wall power and the pump charges normally, return the pump to service after basic functional verification.
  • Document the outlet, adapter connection, charging status, and final condition.

If the Problem Persists

If the Low Battery Notice continues while the pump is connected to a known-good outlet with a known-good adapter, external causes have been ruled out.

Persistent low battery alarms may indicate a failed or weak internal battery, faulty charging circuit, damaged power adapter port, or internal power management issue.

Remove the pump from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first if therapy may be interrupted, if the pump cannot remain connected to AC power, or if battery reliability is uncertain.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Cardinal Health Kangaroo OMNI feeding pump displayed Low Battery Notice during feeding or flushing. Staff reported pump was low on battery with approximately 30 minutes of battery life remaining."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Pump battery was depleted. Power adapter connection and wall outlet were checked. Pump accepted wall power after adapter was fully seated and was placed on charge."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Connected pump to known-good wall outlet, verified power adapter was fully inserted into pump power adapter port, and charged pump. Alarm cleared and pump operation verified. Returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

The Low Battery Notice is usually a straightforward power condition, but it still affects therapy reliability. Start with patient safety, confirm the alarm, check the outlet and adapter connection, allow a full charge, and escalate if the pump cannot maintain or accept battery charge.

That is successful troubleshooting.
