Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000012 Five or More SMBUS Errors Over a 1-Minute Period Occurred

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000012 indicating repeated SMBUS communication failures causing intermittent operation, sensor issues, or system instability.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use if vital signs accuracy or device stability is in question.
  • Expected: Patient safely transferred to another monitoring device.
  • Why it matters: Communication errors can affect readings and system reliability.

Verify the Reported Error/Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error #000000012 is present.
  • Observe when the error appears (startup, during measurement, intermittent).
  • Check for associated issues such as missing parameters, freezing, reboots, or accessory failures.
  • Expected: Error is reproducible or clearly observed with system instability.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true communication fault between internal components.

Perform a Power Reset

  • Turn the unit off, unplug from AC power, and remove the battery (if accessible).
  • Wait 30–60 seconds, then reconnect and power on.
  • Expected: Device boots normally and error does not return.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary communication faults or bus lock conditions.
  • If resolved → Return device to service.

Check External Accessories and Modules

  • Disconnect all non-essential accessories (SpO₂ probes, NIBP hose/cuff, temperature probes, modules).
  • Restart the device with minimal configuration.
  • Expected: Device operates without error when accessories are removed.
  • Why it matters: Faulty or incompatible accessories can introduce communication noise or faults on shared data lines.
  • If error clears → Reconnect accessories one at a time to identify the cause.

Inspect Accessory Ports and Connections

  • Inspect ports for bent pins, debris, contamination, or loose connectors.
  • Reconnect accessories securely.
  • Expected: Clean, undamaged ports with solid connections.
  • Why it matters: Poor connections can disrupt SMBUS communication signals.
  • If damage is found → Remove from service or replace affected accessory.

Check Power Source Stability

  • Ensure connection to a known good outlet and/or fully charged battery.
  • Avoid power strips if possible.
  • Expected: Stable power with no fluctuation or interruption.
  • Why it matters: Voltage instability can disrupt internal communication buses.

Observe for Environmental or Interference Factors

  • Check for electromagnetic interference, high humidity, contamination, or recent fluid exposure.
  • Expected: Device operating in a clean, stable environment.
  • Why it matters: Environmental factors can interfere with low-voltage communication systems like SMBUS.

Attempt Operation Without Battery (if applicable)

  • Remove the battery and operate on AC power only.
  • Expected: Device operates without error.
  • Why it matters: A failing battery can interfere with internal communication circuits.
  • If resolved → Replace battery.

If the Problem Persists

If the error continues after external checks:

  • External accessories, power, and environment have been ruled out
  • Persistent SMBUS errors indicate internal communication failure (main board, internal modules, or data bus)

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Do not rely on a monitor showing communication-related errors. Always transfer monitoring before troubleshooting to avoid inaccurate patient data.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying Error #000000012 (SMBUS communication errors). Staff reported intermittent readings and occasional system freezing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal communication fault suspected after verifying power, removing accessories, and performing reset with no resolution."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Device removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent to Clinical Engineering/vendor for further evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior or error frequency
  • Accessories swapped or removed during testing
  • Power source behavior (AC vs battery)
  • Environmental conditions or interference sources
  • Indicator lights or system messages observed
  • Final device status (in service vs out of service)

Final Thought

SMBUS errors reflect communication breakdowns inside the system. Rule out external contributors first, but persistent errors should be escalated promptly to protect patient safety and data integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
