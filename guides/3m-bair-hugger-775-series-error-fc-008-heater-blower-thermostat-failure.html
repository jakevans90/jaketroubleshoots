3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-008 Heater / Blower / Thermostat Failure

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-008 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. FC-008 may be associated with rapid button pressing, blower failure, an open thermostat, or low-wattage heater failure. This fault can affect airflow, heat output, temperature regulation, or the unit’s ability to provide safe patient warming.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Bair Hugger from patient use if FC-008 appears during setup, treatment, or active warming.
  • Stop using the warming unit clinically until the issue is resolved.
  • Provide an alternate warming device if patient warming is still needed.
  • Do not continue therapy with an unresolved heater, blower, or thermostat-related fault.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the displayed error is FC-008.
  • Ask staff when the error occurred, such as startup, temperature selection, active warming, or after repeated button presses.
  • Note whether the blower starts, stops, runs weakly, or does not run at all.
  • Document whether the fault is constant or intermittent.

Power Cycle and Check for Button/Input Issue

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Disconnect power briefly.
  • Reconnect power and restart the unit.
  • Select settings normally without rapidly pressing multiple buttons.
  • Confirm whether FC-008 returns.

Check External Condition and Airflow

  • Inspect the unit for obvious physical damage.
  • Verify the hose is connected correctly.
  • Check that the hose is not kinked, crushed, blocked, or restricted.
  • Inspect the air intake and filter area for lint, debris, contamination, or blockage.
  • Make sure the unit is not positioned against bedding, walls, carts, or other equipment that may restrict airflow.

Verify Fan Operation in Ambient Mode

  • Power the unit on in a safe, non-patient-use setting.
  • Select Ambient mode.
  • Listen for normal blower operation.
  • Feel for airflow from the hose outlet.
  • If the blower does not run or airflow is weak, remove the unit from service for bench evaluation.

Check for Accessory or Setup Problems

  • Confirm the hose is fully seated at the unit.
  • Try a known-good compatible hose if available and allowed by facility procedure.
  • Check for signs of hose damage, loose fit, contamination, or abnormal restriction.
  • Do not return the unit to clinical use unless normal airflow and operation are verified.

Escalate for Bench Evaluation if FC-008 Returns

  • If FC-008 returns after power reset, airflow checks, hose checks, and Ambient mode verification, treat the unit as having an internal heater, blower, thermostat, or control issue.
  • Do not perform internal repair on the patient care floor.
  • Send the unit for repair or bench evaluation according to facility policy.
  • After repair, complete required calibration, functional testing, and safety checks before return to service.

If the Problem Persists

If FC-008 continues after external condition checks, airflow checks, hose checks, power reset, and Ambient mode fan verification, external causes have been ruled out. A persistent FC-008 may indicate an internal heater, blower, thermostat, or control board fault.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a patient warming system on an active patient when a heater, blower, thermostat, or temperature-control fault is present. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, then evaluate the faulty unit away from patient use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 Series reported FC-008 during operation. Staff stated the warming unit alarmed and could not be trusted for patient warming therapy."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified FC-008 fault. Checked power reset, button input behavior, hose connection, airflow path, filter area, and blower operation in Ambient mode. External restrictions were not found and fault returned during testing."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for bench evaluation due to suspected internal heater, blower, thermostat, or control circuit fault. Calibration, functional testing, and safety checks required before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

FC-008 should be treated as a real warming system fault until proven otherwise. Start with patient safety, confirm the error, rule out simple external causes, verify airflow and blower operation, and escalate the unit when the problem points beyond normal external troubleshooting. Clear documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
