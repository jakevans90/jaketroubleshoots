Fresenius Medical Care 5008

Blood Pump Speed or Motor Failure

Asset Type

Hemodialysis (HD) Machine

Manufacturer

Fresenius Medical Care

Model

5008

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where a Fresenius 5008 HD machine exhibits abnormal blood pump speed readings, erratic operation, or suspected motor failure. The focus is on checking external and easily verifiable issues before assuming internal pump or motor failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop dialysis and move the patient to another functioning machine.
  • Confirm that the blood lines are clamped to prevent blood loss.
  • Never troubleshoot the blood pump while the patient is connected.

Verify Power Supply and Machine Startup

  • Check that the machine is plugged into a verified working outlet.
  • Inspect the main power cord and connector for damage.
  • Restart the machine and monitor for normal startup sequence.

Inspect Blood Lines and Connections

  • Ensure bloodlines are properly installed without kinks or air bubbles.
  • Confirm that the arterial and venous lines are not obstructed.
  • Verify the transducer protector and tubing sensors are correctly seated.

Check Blood Pump Settings and Alarms

  • Review the prescribed blood flow rate; ensure it matches the prescription.
  • Observe if the pump speed matches the displayed value.
  • Listen for unusual noises indicating mechanical resistance or slipping.

Verify Pump Motor Function with Diagnostics

  • Use the machine’s built-in diagnostics menu to test the blood pump.
  • Observe if the motor starts, reaches target RPM, and runs smoothly.
  • Record any error codes displayed (e.g., motor drive, speed error).

Inspect External Mechanical Components

  • Check the roller head and pump segment for wear, debris, or misalignment.
  • Ensure the pump segment rotates freely without obstruction.
  • Replace pump tubing with a known good set if wear or cracking is present.

Swap or Test Related Components (if available)

  • Swap the pump tubing segment with a known functioning set to rule out tubing issues.
  • If another compatible blood pump module exists, test the motor with it to isolate the fault.

If the Problem Persists

External factors and tubing issues have been ruled out; the blood pump motor or drive electronics may be faulty.

  • Remove the machine from service.
  • Label as “Out of Service.”
  • Send to Fresenius service or internal repair bench for evaluation.
  • Recognize that further internal troubleshooting without manufacturer guidance may risk patient safety and machine integrity.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt to force or operate the blood pump with a patient connected if irregular speed or motor failure is suspected.
  • Always ensure another machine is ready for patient treatment before beginning troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Blood pump not reaching prescribed speed; machine displays motor error.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Blood tubing and connections verified; pump segment rotates freely. Diagnostics indicate motor drive error.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Machine removed from service, labeled “Out of Service,” and sent for bench evaluation or manufacturer repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Tubing checked for kinks or wear
  • Alarm codes recorded
  • Unusual pump noises or vibration
  • Final device status (removed from service)

Final Thought

Effective troubleshooting focuses on patient safety, systematic checks, and knowing when to escalate. Proper documentation ensures accurate records for future reference and supports timely repair. Blood pump failures are critical; never compromise safety for testing.

That is successful troubleshooting.
