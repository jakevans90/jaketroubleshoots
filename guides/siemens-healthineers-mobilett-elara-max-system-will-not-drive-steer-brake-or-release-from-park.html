Siemens Healthineers MOBILETT Elara Max

System Will Not Drive, Steer, Brake, or Release From Park

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

MOBILETT Elara Max

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting mobile X-ray drive, steering, brake release, park lock, and transport readiness issues before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the MOBILETT Elara Max is not positioned near a patient, bed, doorway, slope, or crowded hallway before troubleshooting drive or brake issues.

If the unit is stuck in a patient care area, secure the area and do not force movement.

Expected outcome: The system is stable, and no patient, staff member, or equipment is at risk from unexpected movement.

Confirm the Exact Mobility Complaint

Ask the operator what happened and when the issue started.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Unit will not drive forward or reverse
  • Steering is stiff or unresponsive
  • Brake will not release
  • Unit will not come out of park
  • Drive assist does not engage
  • Brake releases briefly then re-engages
  • Unit powers on but will not move
  • Issue occurs only after charging or after long battery use

Expected outcome: The problem is narrowed to drive, steering, brake release, park status, power, or operator-control behavior.

Check for Immediate Physical Obstructions

Inspect around the wheels, drive base, casters, and lower frame.

Look for power cords, linens, trash, tape, floor mats, bed sheets, IV tubing, or debris caught near the wheels.

Expected outcome: The system is free of external obstructions that could prevent movement or steering.

If removing an obstruction resolves the issue, verify safe movement and stop.

Confirm the Unit Is Fully Powered On

Check that the system is awake, powered on, and not in shutdown, standby, transport lockout, or startup mode.

Allow the system to complete its startup sequence before testing drive or brake release.

Expected outcome: Mobility controls are only tested after the system is fully ready.

If normal operation returns after startup completes, document the finding and stop.

Check Battery Charge Status

Review the displayed battery charge level or power status.

A low battery condition may prevent drive assist, brake release, imaging readiness, or normal transport behavior.

Expected outcome: The unit has enough charge to support powered movement.

If the battery is low, connect the system to a known-good AC outlet and allow charging before retesting.

Verify AC Power Cord Is Disconnected Before Transport

Confirm the MOBILETT Elara Max is not still plugged into the wall outlet or charging station.

Check that the AC cord is fully stored and not stretched, trapped, or pulling against the unit.

Expected outcome: The unit is physically free to move and not inhibited by the power cord.

If disconnecting and properly storing the cord resolves the issue, verify drive and brake function, then stop.

Check for Active Error Messages or Inhibit Indicators

Review the system display for brake, drive, park, movement, battery, door, detector, or system-ready messages.

Do not clear messages without recording them first.

Expected outcome: Any active mobility-related fault is captured before reset or escalation.

Confirm the Operator Controls Are Being Used Correctly

Verify that the operator is using the intended drive handle, enable control, brake release, or transport controls according to normal site workflow.

Check whether the issue is repeatable with Clinical Engineering operating the unit safely in an open area.

Expected outcome: The problem is confirmed as equipment-related rather than an incomplete control sequence.

If proper operation resolves the complaint, provide user education and document the result.

Inspect the Drive Handle and Control Area Externally

Check the drive handle, grip area, buttons, switches, and control surfaces for obvious physical damage, liquid contamination, sticking, cracks, loose parts, or impact marks.

Do not disassemble the handle assembly during floor troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: No obvious external control damage is present.

If damage is found, remove the unit from service and escalate for repair.

Check Emergency Stop or Safety Stop Status

Verify whether any emergency stop, safety stop, or movement inhibit control has been activated.

Reset only if it is safe and allowed by site procedure.

Expected outcome: The unit is not being held in a safety-inhibited state.

If resetting the safety stop restores movement, verify normal brake and drive behavior before returning the unit to service.

Verify All Covers, Doors, and Storage Areas Are Secure

Check that detector storage, accessory compartments, panels, and any transport-related doors or covers are fully closed and latched.

Some systems may inhibit movement or report readiness issues if a required component is not secured.

Expected outcome: No open or unsecured external component is preventing safe transport.

Test Movement in a Safe Open Area

With the area clear, test forward movement, reverse movement, steering response, brake release, and stopping behavior.

Move the unit only a short distance.

Expected outcome: The unit moves smoothly, steers normally, and stops predictably.

If movement is jerky, delayed, uncontrolled, noisy, or inconsistent, stop testing and remove the unit from service.

Check for Brake Drag or Wheel Resistance

If the unit attempts to move but feels held back, listen and feel for dragging, scraping, uneven rolling, or one wheel resisting movement.

Do not force the system to roll if brakes appear engaged.

Expected outcome: Wheels rotate freely when the system is properly released for transport.

If brake drag or wheel resistance remains, escalate for bench or vendor evaluation.

Check for Unusual Sounds, Heat, or Smell

During limited testing, check for grinding, clicking, motor strain, electrical smell, hot surfaces, or abnormal vibration near the drive base.

Expected outcome: No signs of mechanical binding, drive motor strain, or electrical overheating are present.

If any of these are present, stop immediately and remove the unit from service.

Perform a Controlled Power Cycle if Safe

If the system is not in active clinical use and no patient data or procedure is at risk, perform a normal shutdown and restart.

Retest drive, steering, brake release, and park release after the system fully boots.

Expected outcome: Temporary software or readiness lockups are cleared.

If the issue returns or remains, do not repeatedly power cycle the unit.

Confirm Final Operational Status

Before returning the unit to service, verify:

  • Unit drives forward and reverse
  • Steering responds normally
  • Brake releases correctly
  • Brake re-engages correctly
  • Park function works as expected
  • No active mobility-related error remains
  • Battery status is acceptable
  • Unit stops safely and predictably

Expected outcome: The system is safe for mobile transport and clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If the MOBILETT Elara Max still will not drive, steer, release the brake, come out of park, or stop correctly after external checks, common user-accessible causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal drive, brake, steering, battery, sensor, control handle, motor controller, or safety interlock fault.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Secured so it cannot be moved unexpectedly
  • Sent for repair, bench evaluation, or Siemens service evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A mobile X-ray system with unreliable braking or drive control should not remain available for clinical transport.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot drive, steering, or brake concerns while the unit is close to a patient, bed, stretcher, wall, elevator, or doorway. If imaging is needed, have clinical staff use another mobile X-ray system first. Movement faults can create staff injury, patient injury, collision, or equipment damage risk.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the MOBILETT Elara Max would power on but would not release from park or drive during transport."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Clinical Engineering found no wheel obstruction, AC cord issue, or operator-control error; brake release remained inactive after restart."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the MOBILETT Elara Max from service, labeled it Out of Service, documented the brake/drive fault, and escalated for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery level at time of failure
  • Whether the unit was plugged in or recently removed from charge
  • Whether AC outlet or charging cord was checked
  • Any active drive, brake, park, or system-ready message
  • Whether forward and reverse both failed
  • Whether steering worked normally
  • Whether brake release clicked, attempted, or stayed locked
  • Whether wheels were obstructed
  • Any unusual sound, vibration, heat, or smell
  • Whether the issue occurred near a slope, elevator, or tight space
  • Whether a power cycle changed the symptom
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Mobility issues on a mobile X-ray system should be handled cautiously because the hazard is physical movement, not just imaging downtime. Start with patient safety, external obstructions, power, battery state, cords, controls, and visible errors. If the unit cannot move or stop predictably after basic checks, remove it from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
