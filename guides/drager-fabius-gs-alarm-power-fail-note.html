Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: POWER FAIL !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

POWER FAIL ! alarm indicating loss of mains power, causing the anesthesia machine to operate on battery and creating risk of complete device shutdown if power is not restored.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately confirm the patient is being ventilated safely
  • Switch to manual ventilation (bagging) or an alternate anesthesia machine if needed
  • Ensure oxygen delivery is maintained

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the POWER FAIL ! alarm is displayed
  • Check if the unit is unplugged from wall power
  • Observe if the machine is running on battery
  • Monitor battery indicators for discharge

Check Power Cord Connection

  • Ensure the AC power cord is firmly connected to the machine
  • Ensure the cord is fully inserted into the wall outlet
  • Reseat both ends of the connection

Verify Wall Outlet Functionality

  • Plug in another device to confirm outlet power
  • Move the machine to a known working outlet if needed
  • Confirm consistent power at the outlet

Check Power Strip or Extension (If Used)

  • Ensure power strip is turned ON
  • Bypass the strip and plug directly into the wall
  • Remove any unnecessary intermediaries

Inspect Power Cord Condition

  • Check for cuts, fraying, or exposed wires
  • Inspect for loose connectors or damaged plug
  • Replace cord if damage is found

Confirm Internal Power Recognition

  • Verify the machine indicates AC power is present
  • Confirm the battery begins charging
  • Ensure the alarm clears after power restoration

If the Problem Persists

If the POWER FAIL ! alarm continues despite confirmed external power:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • This indicates a potential internal power supply or input failure
  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before addressing power-related issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"POWER FAIL ! alarm observed; unit running on battery with loss of mains power."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Power cord disconnected from wall outlet or outlet found to be non-functional."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reconnected power cord to verified working outlet; confirmed AC power restored, battery charging, and alarm cleared. Unit returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power-related alarms are often simple but critical. Always start with external checks, confirm power availability, and prioritize patient safety before escalating to internal failures.

That is successful troubleshooting.
