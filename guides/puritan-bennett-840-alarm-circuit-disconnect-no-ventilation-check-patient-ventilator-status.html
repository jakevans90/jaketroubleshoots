Puritan Bennett 840

Alarm: CIRCUIT DISCONNECT No ventilation. Check patient/ventilator status.

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Puritan Bennett

Model

840

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: CIRCUIT DISCONNECT indicating loss of ventilation due to disconnected circuit, power recovery state, or airflow pathway interruption. This condition results in no delivered breaths and poses immediate risk to the patient.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient
  • Provide manual ventilation or switch to a backup ventilator
  • Call for clinical support if needed

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm CIRCUIT DISCONNECT alarm is active
  • Observe absence of delivered breaths
  • Check for audible and visual alarm
  • Note if ventilator is in idle or standby mode

Check Patient Circuit Connections

  • Ensure circuit is fully connected at ventilator ports
  • Inspect wye, filters, humidifier, and patient interface
  • Reseat any loose or partially connected fittings

Inspect Circuit Integrity

  • Look for cracks, holes, or disconnected tubing
  • Replace circuit if damage is found

Check for Recent Power Loss or Recovery

  • Determine if AC power was recently lost
  • Verify ventilator did not revert to idle mode
  • Confirm system resumed proper operation

Run Extended Self-Test (EST)

  • Remove ventilator from patient use
  • Perform EST per manufacturer procedure
  • Verify system passes all checks

Swap with Known-Good Circuit

  • Replace breathing circuit with a known-good set
  • Check if alarm clears with replacement

If the Problem Persists

If external causes such as loose connections, damaged circuits, and power recovery conditions are ruled out:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device “Out of Service”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a ventilator circuit disconnect alarm on an active patient. Always establish backup ventilation before performing any checks.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator alarming 'CIRCUIT DISCONNECT,' no ventilation delivered to patient."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose connection identified at patient wye causing loss of circuit integrity."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Re-secured circuit connections and verified normal ventilation restored."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Circuit disconnect alarms represent an immediate loss of ventilation. Always prioritize patient safety, verify all external connections thoroughly, and escalate appropriately if the issue persists beyond simple causes.

That is successful troubleshooting.
