GE Healthcare Datex-Ohmeda S5

Display or touchscreen failure

Asset Type

Anesthesia Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Datex-Ohmeda S5

What This Guide Helps With

Unresponsive touchscreen, blank or dim display, frozen screen, or inability to interact with monitor controls during operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the monitor is actively in use, immediately transition patient monitoring to an alternate device.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring continues without interruption.
  • Why it matters: Loss of display or control removes visibility of critical patient parameters.

Verify the Reported Behavior

  • Confirm the issue:
    • Is the screen completely blank, dim, or frozen?
    • Does the touchscreen not respond to input?
    • Are waveforms still updating (if visible)?
  • Expected: Clear identification of whether this is a display issue, touchscreen issue, or full system freeze.
  • Why it matters: Differentiates between UI failure and full system failure.

Check Power Status and Indicators

  • Inspect:
    • Power LED indicators
    • Screen backlight presence
    • Any audible alarms
  • Actions:
    • Confirm the unit is powered on
    • Adjust brightness settings if accessible
    • Verify AC power connection
  • Expected: Device is powered with visible indicators.
  • Why it matters: A dim or blank screen may be a brightness or power issue, not a failed display.

Perform a Hard Reboot

  • If the screen is frozen or unresponsive:
    • Power off the unit using the main power switch
    • Disconnect AC power briefly (10–15 seconds if safe to do so)
    • Restart the monitor
  • Expected: System boots normally and display functionality returns.
  • Why it matters: Software lockups are common causes of touchscreen or display failure.

Check External Display Conditions

  • Inspect for environmental or physical issues:
    • Excessive glare or lighting affecting visibility
    • Screen contamination (fluids, gel, debris)
    • Physical damage (cracks, impact marks)
  • Actions:
    • Clean the screen using approved methods
    • Reassess visibility and responsiveness
  • Expected: Screen becomes visible and responsive if contamination was the cause.
  • Why it matters: Touchscreens may fail due to surface interference.

Test Touchscreen Responsiveness

  • After reboot:
    • Attempt to navigate menus
    • Try multiple areas of the screen
  • Observe:
    • Partial responsiveness (only some areas work)
    • Complete lack of response
  • Expected: Touch input is consistent across the screen.
  • Why it matters: Partial response often indicates touchscreen digitizer failure.

Check for Peripheral or Module Interference

  • Disconnect any non-essential accessories:
    • External modules
    • Data connections
    • Peripheral cables
  • Restart and retest.
  • Expected: Display and touchscreen function without interference.
  • Why it matters: External components can occasionally cause system instability.

Observe for System Performance Issues

  • Look for:
    • Slow response before freezing
    • Repeated lockups after reboot
    • Graphical artifacts or flickering
  • Expected: Stable system performance.
  • Why it matters: These signs may indicate deeper system or graphics hardware issues.

If the Problem Persists

If all external and basic causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal (display panel, touchscreen digitizer, or processing hardware).

Remove the device from service.

Label it Out of Service.

Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and reflects proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot a display or touchscreen issue while the monitor is actively being used on a patient. Always transition monitoring first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor touchscreen unresponsive and unable to navigate menus during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Touchscreen remained unresponsive after reboot; likely touchscreen digitizer failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed monitor from service and sent to Biomed shop for further evaluation and repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Power indicators verified
  • Reboot attempted
  • Screen brightness checked
  • Touch response behavior (partial vs none)
  • Physical screen condition
  • Accessories disconnected/tested
  • Final device status (removed from service)

Final Thought

Display and touchscreen failures directly impact patient visibility and control of the monitor. Always prioritize patient safety, rule out simple causes first, and escalate appropriately when internal failure is suspected. Proper documentation ensures clarity and continuity of care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
