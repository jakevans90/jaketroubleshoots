Smiths Medical Medfusion 4000

Keypad or Touchscreen Input Failure

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Medfusion 4000

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting keypad or touchscreen input failures on the Medfusion 4000. Such failures prevent user input for programming therapy, adjusting rates, or acknowledging alarms. Often, these issues are caused by external factors such as dirt, moisture, or connection issues rather than internal electronic failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure the Pump is Powered Properly

  • Verify AC power is connected and the pump is turned on.
  • Check the battery level; low battery can cause erratic touchscreen behavior.

Why: A weak power source can prevent proper input detection.

Inspect the Keypad / Touchscreen Surface

  • Look for debris, liquid, or adhesive residue on buttons or touchscreen.
  • Clean with a lint-free cloth and manufacturer-approved cleaning solution.

Why: Contaminants can block proper contact or capacitive sensing.

Check for Visible Damage

  • Examine the keypad or touchscreen for cracks, wear, or signs of delamination.

Why: Physical damage can impair input functionality.

Verify Device Connections

  • Ensure the front panel or control board ribbon cable (if accessible without disassembly) is seated properly.

Why: Loose connectors can cause intermittent input response.

Cycle Power and Reset the Device

  • Turn the pump off, wait 10–15 seconds, and turn it back on.
  • Observe if the keypad or touchscreen responds after reboot.

Why: Some software glitches can be cleared with a simple power cycle.

Test Input with Different Settings

  • Try entering multiple simple commands or programming steps to see if any keys or screen areas respond.

Why: Pinpoints partial failures vs. total failure.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have likely been ruled out.

  • Remove the pump from service.
  • Label as “Out of Service.”
  • Send for bench evaluation or manufacturer repair.

Note: Attempting internal repairs beyond connector checks is not recommended for safety and warranty reasons.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not troubleshoot on a patient actively receiving therapy.
  • Move the patient to a functioning pump before any input testing.
  • Ensure keypad/touchscreen issues are addressed before returning the pump to service.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Pump touchscreen does not respond; keypad entries fail to register.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
External inspection revealed no debris or visible damage; device reboot did not resolve issue. Likely internal touchscreen or control board failure.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Pump removed from service, labeled “Out of Service,” and sent for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Cleaning method and solution used.
  • Observed response after power cycle.
  • Battery status and AC connection verified.
  • Any partial input responses or intermittent behavior.

Final Thought

Troubleshooting input failures requires logical, stepwise evaluation, prioritizing external checks before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation and escalation preserve patient safety and reduce repeat service issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
