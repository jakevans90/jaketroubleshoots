Pedigo P-2055

Blankets Not Warming Evenly

Asset Type

Blanket Warmer

Manufacturer

Pedigo

Model

P-2055

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting uneven blanket warming caused by loading, airflow, door sealing, settings, or temperature-control issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

Do not place cold, overheated, or unevenly warmed blankets into patient use until the warmer is verified.

Action:

  • Remove questionable blankets from service.
  • Check for blankets that feel excessively hot, cold, damp, or unevenly warmed.
  • Use blankets from another approved warmer if patient care requires immediate use.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without using blankets from a warmer that may not be heating consistently.

Why it matters: Uneven blanket warming can lead to patient discomfort, delayed care, or unsafe heat exposure if some blankets are much warmer than others.

2. Verify the Reported Problem

Confirm whether the issue is truly uneven warming or a loading/use issue.

Action:

Ask clinical staff:

  • Was the warmer recently restocked?
  • Were blankets added cold or damp?
  • Are only the front blankets warm?
  • Are the top, bottom, or rear blankets staying cooler?
  • Has the door been opened frequently?

Expected outcome: You identify whether the problem follows a specific shelf, area, or usage pattern.

If the issue is explained by recent restocking or frequent door opening, allow the warmer time to recover and recheck.

3. Check the Temperature Setting

Verify that the warmer is set within the facility-approved blanket warming range.

Action:

  • Check the displayed temperature or thermostat setting.
  • Confirm the setting has not been turned down, changed accidentally, or left below the expected range.
  • Do not raise the temperature above facility policy or manufacturer limits to compensate for poor warming.

Expected outcome: The warmer is set appropriately and is not being operated outside safe limits.

If the setting was incorrect, correct it, allow the unit to stabilize, and recheck blanket temperature.

4. Inspect Blanket Loading

Check whether the cabinet is packed too tightly.

Action:

Open the warmer and inspect how blankets are stacked.

Look for:

  • Overfilled cabinet
  • Blankets pressed tightly against walls
  • Blankets blocking vents or internal air paths
  • Heavy stacks preventing heat movement
  • Blankets loaded unevenly on one side

Expected outcome: Air and heat can circulate around the blankets.

If overloading is found, reduce the blanket load, restack loosely, allow recovery time, and recheck.

5. Check for Blocked Airflow or Heat Paths

Uneven warming often happens when airflow or heat distribution is blocked.

Action:

Inspect the inside of the cabinet for:

  • Blankets covering vents
  • Liners, bags, or packaging left inside
  • Items other than blankets stored in the warmer
  • Debris near internal openings

Expected outcome: Internal airflow and heat transfer areas are clear.

If blocked, remove the obstruction, reload properly, and allow the warmer to stabilize.

6. Inspect the Door Seal and Door Closure

A poor door seal can allow heat loss, especially near the front of the warmer.

Action:

Check that the door closes fully.

Inspect the gasket for:

  • Tears
  • Flattened areas
  • Loose sections
  • Gaps
  • Debris preventing proper sealing

Expected outcome: The door seals evenly around the cabinet.

If the door does not seal correctly, remove the warmer from service if temperature cannot be maintained consistently.

7. Check Door Opening Frequency

Frequent door opening can make the front or upper blankets feel cooler than the rest of the cabinet.

Action:

  • Ask staff how often the warmer is accessed.
  • Observe whether the door is being left open during restocking or patient care.

Expected outcome: The warmer has enough closed-door recovery time to maintain stable cabinet temperature.

If usage pattern is the cause, educate staff to minimize door-open time and avoid leaving the door ajar.

8. Allow Recovery After Restocking

Cold blankets can take time to warm evenly, especially after a large restock.

Action:

  • Confirm when the warmer was last loaded.
  • If newly loaded, allow adequate warm-up time with the door closed.
  • Recheck blankets from different areas after the recovery period.

Expected outcome: Blanket temperatures become more consistent after the cabinet stabilizes.

If uneven warming remains after adequate recovery time, continue troubleshooting.

9. Compare Actual Temperature in Different Cabinet Areas

Use an approved temperature check method if available.

Action:

Check temperature in several areas of the cabinet, such as:

  • Top shelf
  • Bottom shelf
  • Front area
  • Rear area
  • Left and right sides

Compare findings to the displayed temperature or expected operating range.

Expected outcome: Cabinet temperature is reasonably consistent throughout the warmer.

If one area is significantly cooler or hotter, suspect airflow, door seal, heater, sensor, or control issue.

10. Check for Abnormal Signs

Inspect for signs that the warmer may have an internal heating or control problem.

Action:

Look and listen for:

  • No heat production
  • Unusual clicking
  • Burning smell
  • Excessive heat
  • Discolored interior surfaces
  • Damaged power cord
  • Loose plug
  • Intermittent display or indicator lights

Expected outcome: No unsafe electrical, overheating, or control symptoms are present.

If unusual heat, smell, damaged wiring, or erratic operation is found, remove the warmer from service immediately.

11. Power Cycle Only if Appropriate

If the warmer has a display or control issue, a controlled reset may help confirm behavior.

Action:

  • Remove blankets if needed for safe evaluation.
  • Turn the warmer off.
  • Wait briefly.
  • Turn the unit back on.
  • Observe whether the display, indicators, and heating behavior return normally.

Expected outcome: The warmer restarts normally and begins heating consistently.

If the issue returns or the warmer does not recover properly, stop troubleshooting and send for bench evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the Pedigo P-2055 still warms blankets unevenly after checking loading, airflow, door seal, temperature setting, restocking behavior, and power condition, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal heater, thermostat, sensor, circulation, control, or wiring problem.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not keep adjusting the temperature upward to overcome uneven warming.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot blanket temperature concerns while staff are actively using the warmer for patient care. Move patient care needs to another blanket warmer first, then evaluate the Pedigo P-2055 only when it is safe to remove from use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that blankets in the Pedigo P-2055 were not warming evenly, with some blankets remaining cooler than others."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Cabinet was found overfilled with blankets packed tightly against the internal heat circulation areas, limiting even heat distribution."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reduced blanket load, restacked blankets to allow airflow, verified door closure, allowed recovery time, and confirmed improved warming before returning unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Outlet tested
  • Power cord condition
  • Temperature setting observed
  • Displayed temperature, if available
  • Actual temperature readings from different cabinet areas
  • Whether cabinet was overfilled
  • Whether vents or airflow paths were blocked
  • Door gasket condition
  • Door closure condition
  • Frequency of door opening
  • Whether blankets were recently restocked
  • Any unusual smell, heat, sound, or indicator behavior
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Uneven blanket warming should be approached logically. Start with patient safety, then check loading, airflow, temperature setting, door sealing, and use pattern before suspecting an internal failure. If the warmer cannot maintain consistent heat after external causes are corrected, remove it from service and document the findings clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
