Drager Evita V500

Alarm: Oxygen measurement unreliable inconsistent or inaccurate oxygen concentration readings

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Evita V500

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the alarm condition where oxygen measurement is unreliable, resulting in inconsistent or inaccurate FiO₂ readings. This can lead to improper oxygen delivery and increased risk to patient safety due to incorrect monitoring.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Assess the patient’s oxygenation status immediately
  • Confirm adequate ventilation and oxygen delivery
  • Transfer to a backup ventilator if readings cannot be trusted

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the message “Oxygen measurement unreliable” is displayed
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup, during use, intermittent)
  • Check for fluctuating or inconsistent FiO₂ readings
  • Note any additional oxygen-related alarms

Check Oxygen Supply Source

  • Verify oxygen hose is securely connected
  • Confirm correct gas source is connected
  • Check wall outlet pressure is within normal range

Inspect Breathing Circuit and Gas Path

  • Check for loose or disconnected tubing
  • Inspect for condensation or contamination in the circuit
  • Ensure proper circuit assembly

Perform Oxygen Sensor Calibration

  • Run the ventilator’s oxygen sensor calibration procedure
  • Follow manufacturer protocol for calibration

Re-test Device Function

  • Monitor FiO₂ readings for stability
  • Confirm readings match expected set values

Replace Oxygen Sensor (If Needed)

  • Replace the oxygen sensor with a known good unit
  • Verify alarm clears and readings stabilize

If the Problem Persists

If external causes and sensor replacement do not resolve the issue, an internal measurement or control fault is likely.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup ventilator or confirm oxygen delivery with alternate monitoring before continuing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator displaying Oxygen measurement unreliable with unstable FiO₂ readings during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Oxygen sensor failed calibration and produced inconsistent readings indicating sensor drift."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed calibration and replaced oxygen sensor. Verified stable readings and returned device to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Accurate oxygen measurement is critical for safe ventilation. Always rule out external causes, perform calibration, and replace faulty sensors when needed. Escalate promptly if accuracy cannot be restored.

That is successful troubleshooting.
