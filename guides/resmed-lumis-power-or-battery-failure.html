ResMed Lumis Ventilator

Power or Battery Failure

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

ResMed

Model

Lumis

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting power or battery failures on a ResMed Lumis ventilator. Symptoms may include the device not turning on, failure to charge, rapid battery drain, or unexpected shutdowns. The focus is on external checks and verifiable causes before escalating to internal repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify AC Power Source

  • Ensure the wall outlet is live using a known working device or outlet tester.
  • Check the ventilator power cord for damage, proper connection, and secure seating.
  • Why: A faulty outlet or loose cord is a common cause of “power failure” errors.

Check the Ventilator’s Power Switch and Indicators

  • Confirm the power switch is in the “ON” position.
  • Observe any indicator lights on the device when connected to AC.
  • Why: Some Lumis models require a full press or hold to power on, and indicator lights signal basic functionality.

Inspect the Battery Module

  • Remove the battery and inspect for corrosion, physical damage, or improper seating.
  • Reinsert the battery securely until it clicks.
  • Why: A poorly seated battery can prevent charging or device startup.

Test with a Known Good Battery

  • If available, replace the battery with a fully charged, verified functional battery.
  • Observe if the device powers on or charges.
  • Why: This isolates whether the original battery is the source of failure.

Check Charging Behavior

  • With AC connected, monitor battery charging indicators.
  • If the battery fails to charge over a reasonable period, note any error messages.
  • Why: Proper charging confirms the internal charging circuit is functioning.

Power Cycle the Device

  • Disconnect AC power and battery, wait 30–60 seconds, then reconnect AC and battery and attempt to power on.
  • Why: Some electronic errors can be cleared by a simple power reset.

If the Problem Persists

All external power sources, battery modules, and cabling have been ruled out.

The device may have an internal power supply or board failure.

Action:

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label “Out of Service.”
  • Send for bench evaluation or manufacturer repair.
  • Note: Knowing when to escalate prevents further damage or patient risk.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt to troubleshoot power or battery issues while the patient is connected.
  • Always move the patient to another functioning ventilator before testing the Lumis.
  • Ensure backup ventilation is available at all times.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Ventilator will not power on; battery not charging.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Battery was found loose and corroded; AC power confirmed functional.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Cleaned battery contacts, reseated battery, and verified device powers on and charges. If unresolved, device sent for manufacturer repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • AC outlet tested with a multimeter or known device.
  • Battery seating checked; swapped with known good battery if available.
  • Power indicators observed.
  • Device error codes noted.
  • Device status after power cycle.

Final Thought

Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures patient safety while avoiding unnecessary internal repairs. Proper documentation and timely escalation protect both the patient and the device. Always confirm the ventilator is functioning fully before returning to service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
