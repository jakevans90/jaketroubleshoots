Smiths Medical Medfusion 4000

Syringe Recognition or Calibration Errors

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Medfusion 4000

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting syringe recognition or calibration errors on the Medfusion 4000 pump. These errors often prevent therapy initiation or cause inaccurate infusion rates. Common external causes include incorrect syringe placement, incompatible syringe type, or sensor obstruction. Internal failure is less common but may require escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power and Start-Up

  • Ensure the pump is powered on and battery is charged or unit is connected to AC.
  • Confirm the pump completes its startup self-test without error.
  • Why: Power fluctuations can prevent proper sensor initialization.

Inspect Syringe Type and Compatibility

  • Verify the syringe is a compatible model per manufacturer guidelines.
  • Check for expiration or damaged syringes.
  • Why: The pump may reject incompatible syringes or fail calibration.

Check Syringe Loading

  • Ensure syringe is seated correctly in the pump mechanism.
  • Confirm that the plunger and barrel align with pump sensors.
  • Why: Misalignment can prevent recognition or cause calibration failure.

Clean Syringe and Sensor Contacts

  • Remove the syringe and inspect for residue, blood, or fluid contamination.
  • Wipe sensor areas with approved cleaning solution.
  • Why: Dirt or residue can interfere with mechanical or optical sensors.

Cycle Power and Retry Recognition

  • Power the pump off, remove the syringe, wait 30 seconds, then reinsert syringe and power on.
  • Observe whether the pump recognizes the syringe.
  • Why: Simple resets often clear minor sensor or software glitches.

Verify Calibration Settings

  • Ensure the pump software is set for the syringe size in use.
  • Run any recommended calibration routine per manufacturer instructions (external checks only).
  • Why: Incorrect software settings can trigger recognition or calibration errors.

If the Problem Persists

Common external factors have been ruled out. The issue may now be due to:

  • Faulty syringe detection sensor
  • Internal pump electronics or software error

Action:

  • Remove the pump from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for manufacturer repair or Clinical Engineering bench evaluation

Note: Stopping here is proper troubleshooting; avoid internal repair.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt syringe calibration on a pump currently infusing a patient.

Move the patient to a different pump before troubleshooting.

Only troubleshoot in a safe, controlled environment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Pump displays “Syringe Not Recognized” or fails calibration with every attempt.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Syringe was misaligned; sensor contacts contaminated.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Cleaned sensor contacts, properly seated syringe, and verified calibration routine. Therapy successfully initiated.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Syringe type and lot number
  • Sensor contact condition
  • Power source used (battery/AC)
  • Software version
  • Error messages displayed
  • Any unusual sounds during recognition attempt

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Start with logical, external checks before assuming internal failure. Document all steps and escalate when appropriate. Accurate CCR documentation ensures clear communication and proper follow-up.

That is successful troubleshooting.
