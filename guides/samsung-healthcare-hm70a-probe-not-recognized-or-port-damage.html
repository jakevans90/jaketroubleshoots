Samsung Healthcare HM70A

Probe Not Recognized or Port Damage

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

Samsung Healthcare

Model

HM70A

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting when the HM70A does not recognize a probe or shows intermittent connection due to probe, cable, or port-related issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the ultrasound system from active patient use if imaging is required. Move the patient to a functioning system before troubleshooting.

Expected: No impact to patient care
Why it matters: Probe recognition issues prevent reliable imaging and diagnosis

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Connect the probe and power on the system.
  • Observe for:
    • “Probe not recognized” message
    • No image display
    • Intermittent connection when moving cable

Expected: Issue is clearly present and repeatable
Why it matters: Confirms this is not user error or intermittent misuse

Check Probe Connection (Most Common Cause)

  • Disconnect and firmly reconnect the probe to the port.
  • Ensure:
    • Connector is fully seated
    • Locking mechanism (if present) is engaged

Expected: Probe is detected after reseating
Why it matters: Loose connections are the most frequent cause

Inspect Probe Connector Pins

  • Visually inspect the probe connector for:
    • Bent or missing pins
    • Contamination (gel, dust, debris)
    • Corrosion

Expected: Pins are straight, clean, and intact
Why it matters: Damaged pins prevent proper communication

Inspect System Port for Damage

  • Use a flashlight to inspect the port on the system:
    • Bent or recessed pins
    • Debris or foreign material
    • Signs of wear or physical damage

Expected: Port is clean and undamaged
Why it matters: Port damage is common with repeated probe changes

Clean Connector and Port (If Needed)

  • If contamination is present:
    • Use approved cleaning methods (dry, lint-free wipe or manufacturer-approved cleaning tool)
    • Do NOT insert anything that could damage pins

Expected: Clean connection surfaces
Why it matters: Even small debris can interrupt signal transmission

Test with a Known-Good Probe

  • Connect a different compatible probe.

Expected:
If new probe works → original probe is faulty
If no probes work → likely system port or internal issue
Why it matters: Quickly isolates probe vs system fault

Test the Suspect Probe on Another System (If Available)

  • Connect the original probe to another compatible ultrasound system.

Expected:
If probe fails again → probe is defective
If probe works → issue is with HM70A port or system
Why it matters: Confirms root cause without internal disassembly

Check for Intermittent Connection

  • Gently move the probe cable near the connector while observing system response.

Expected: Stable connection with no dropouts
Why it matters: Intermittent signal suggests cable strain or internal wire break

Reboot the System

  • Power the system off, wait 15–30 seconds, and restart.

Expected: Probe may be recognized after reboot if caused by software glitch
Why it matters: Clears temporary communication faults

If the Problem Persists

If probes are not recognized after swapping probes and verifying connections, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely due to:

  • Internal port failure
  • Mainboard/interface board issue

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or further bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot probe connection issues while actively scanning a patient. Always transition to a working system first to avoid delays in diagnosis.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ultrasound probe not recognized on Samsung HM70A; no image displayed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Damaged probe connector pins causing loss of communication with system."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed defective probe from service and replaced with known-good probe; confirmed normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Probe reseated and tested
  • Known-good probe tested
  • Port condition (clean/damaged)
  • Presence of bent pins or debris
  • Intermittent vs constant failure
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Probe recognition issues should always be approached from the outside in—connections, probes, and ports first. Knowing when to escalate protects both the equipment and patient care. Proper documentation ensures repeat issues are tracked and resolved efficiently.

That is successful troubleshooting.
