Spacelabs Healthcare Sentinel Central Station

Alarm Not Propagating from Bedside to Central

Asset Type

Central Monitoring Station

Manufacturer

Spacelabs Healthcare

Model

Sentinel Central Station

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes when bedside alarms do not appear on the central station, including connectivity, configuration, or external interference issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm patients are being actively monitored at the bedside. If alarms are silenced or inhibited, move patient care to a safe monitor before troubleshooting.

2. Verify Bedside Monitor Alarm Settings

  • Action: Check that the bedside monitor’s alarms are enabled and volume is audible.
  • Expected Outcome: Alarms should be active; disabled alarms will not propagate.

3. Inspect Network Connectivity

  • Action: Confirm bedside monitor is connected to the correct network and shows a valid IP.
  • Expected Outcome: No network errors; a disconnected monitor cannot send alarms to central.

4. Check Physical Connections

  • Action: Inspect network cables and patch panels; ensure connections are secure and undamaged.
  • Expected Outcome: Solid connection; loose or damaged cables can interrupt alarm signals.

5. Review Central Station Configuration

  • Action: Verify the central station has the correct bedside monitor assigned and is configured to receive alarms.
  • Expected Outcome: Correct assignments; misconfigured central stations will not display alarms.

6. Examine Software or Server Status

  • Action: Check central station and associated servers for software errors, status indicators, or messages.
  • Expected Outcome: No errors; software issues may prevent alarm propagation.

7. Swap Bedside Monitor (if possible)

  • Action: Replace with a known working monitor.
  • Expected Outcome: If alarms propagate with a different monitor, bedside unit may require repair.

8. Check for Intermittent Network Issues

  • Action: Ping bedside monitor from central station or network tool; observe packet loss or high latency.
  • Expected Outcome: Stable network; intermittent network issues can block alarm signals.

If the Problem Persists

External and configuration causes are likely ruled out.

  • Remove the affected bedside monitor from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send for bench evaluation or vendor repair.

Knowing when to escalate prevents unsafe troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot alarms on a patient actively relying on monitoring.

Move patient to a functional monitor before testing or swapping devices.

Confirm alarm visibility at central before returning to clinical use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bedside monitor alarms not appearing on central station."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Bedside monitor correctly connected, alarms enabled, but central station not receiving signals; network stable."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Network outlet tested
  • Bedside monitor swapped
  • Central station assignment verified
  • Alarm status and volume checked
  • Indicator lights normal
  • Device labeled Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is the priority. Follow logical troubleshooting from simple settings and connections to external network checks. Escalate promptly when internal device issues are suspected. Accurate documentation supports continuity of care and future repairs.

That is successful troubleshooting.
