STERIS AMSCO Century V Series

Drain failure, water retention, or chamber flooding

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO Century V Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of slow or failed drainage, water retention, or chamber flooding that prevent cycle completion and compromise sterilization safety.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient and Staff Safety

  • Confirm the sterilizer cycle is complete and chamber is fully depressurized and cooled.
  • Verify no staff are near the chamber to avoid burns or scalding.

2. Inspect Chamber Drain Strainer and Trap

  • Open the drain access panel.
  • Check for debris, lint, or foreign objects in the strainer/trap.
  • Expected outcome: Water should flow freely when cleared.
  • Why: Blocked drains are the most common cause of retention or flooding.

3. Verify Drain Line and Floor Connection

  • Inspect the drain hose/piping for kinks, bends, or clogs.
  • Confirm the floor drain is not obstructed.
  • Expected outcome: Water exits the chamber freely; no pooling.
  • Why: Proper drainage relies on unobstructed plumbing downstream.

4. Check Solenoid Valve Operation

  • Activate a manual drain cycle if available.
  • Listen for valve actuation and observe water flow.
  • Expected outcome: Valve opens and water drains.
  • Why: A stuck solenoid prevents chamber drainage.

5. Inspect Water Level Sensors and Float Switches

  • Ensure sensors are clean and properly seated.
  • Expected outcome: No false “full chamber” indications.
  • Why: Faulty sensors can prevent drain cycles from initiating.

6. Examine Inlet Water Supply

  • Confirm water supply is at correct pressure and flow.
  • Expected outcome: Sterilizer fills and drains correctly.
  • Why: Low or fluctuating supply can cause incomplete evacuation.

7. Run a Test Cycle

  • Observe fill, sterilization, and drain phases.
  • Confirm no flooding or residual water remains in chamber.
  • If resolved, document and return unit to service.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have been completed. Likely internal pump, valve, or controller issue.

  • Remove device from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Knowing when to escalate avoids further damage and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot a pressurized or hot chamber.
  • Move instruments or patients to another sterilizer before inspection.
  • Use PPE to prevent burns when accessing the drain area.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Chamber retains water after cycle, causing flooding."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Drain strainer partially blocked with lint and debris, valve actuation delayed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleared strainer and verified drain line; test cycle completed with normal drainage."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Drain strainer inspected and cleaned
  • Solenoid valve audible and functioning
  • Floor drain unobstructed
  • Water sensors verified clean
  • No unusual alarms or sounds
  • Chamber cleared of residual water

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting begins with external checks to prevent escalation. Patient safety and logical investigation are paramount. Accurate documentation ensures traceability and supports future maintenance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
