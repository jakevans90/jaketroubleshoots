Linet Multicare

Side Rail Control Not Responding

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Linet

Model

Multicare

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of unresponsive side rail controls, including power, lockout functions, cable connections, or panel faults before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the patient is safely transferred or fully supported. Never attempt side rail control troubleshooting with the patient relying on the bed.

2. Verify Bed Power

  • Check that the bed is connected to a live outlet.
  • Confirm that any battery backup is charged.
  • Expected Outcome: Bed electronics should have power; indicator lights are on.

3. Inspect Side Rail Lockout Settings

  • Confirm the side rail is not in a mechanical or electronic lockout mode.
  • Expected Outcome: Control should be active if lockout is disabled.

4. Check Control Panel and Cables

  • Inspect the side rail control panel for visible damage, dirt, or stuck buttons.
  • Verify connections between the panel and the bed’s control box.
  • Expected Outcome: Secure cables and a clean panel may restore function.

5. Test Alternate Control

  • Use bed-mounted or remote nurse control if available.
  • Expected Outcome: If alternate controls work, the issue is likely the side rail panel itself.

6. Observe for Indicator Feedback

  • Look for lights or error messages on the side rail panel or bed display.
  • Expected Outcome: Indicators may help identify panel-specific faults.

If the Problem Persists

External causes are ruled out. Remove the bed from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Do not attempt internal electronic repairs on-site.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a malfunctioning side rail for patient safety. Move the patient to a fully operational bed before further troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Side rail control unresponsive when attempting to raise or lower the rail."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Control panel cable loose; lockout mode engaged."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Secured cable connection, disabled lockout; verified side rail movement restored."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and functional
  • Lockout setting checked
  • Panel buttons inspected
  • Alternate control tested
  • Final device status: functional or sent for repair

Final Thought

Patient safety is the priority. Troubleshoot logically from external causes to panel-specific issues, and escalate appropriately. Document all findings for accountability and future reference.

That is successful troubleshooting.
