Fresenius Medical Care 2008T

Error: High Flow Error

Asset Type

Hemodialysis (HD) Machine

Manufacturer

Fresenius Medical Care

Model

2008T

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting High Flow Error when the balancing chamber switches faster than 1.8 seconds, causing dialysate heating to stop and preventing dependable dialysis operation until the abnormal flow condition is resolved.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If High Flow Error occurs during treatment, notify clinical staff immediately and follow dialysis-unit procedures for safe alternate treatment support or appropriate clinical intervention.
  • Do not continue relying on a Fresenius Medical Care 2008T with an unresolved dialysate-flow error or heater shutdown during patient treatment.
  • Remove the affected machine from patient use before troubleshooting continues.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not dependent on equipment with unreliable dialysate-flow control or disabled heating operation.

Why it matters: High Flow Error indicates abnormally rapid balancing chamber switching and causes the heater to turn off.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the machine removed from patient use, confirm that High Flow Error appears on the display.
  • Document whether the error occurs during startup, preparation, rinse, or operational testing.
  • Observe whether the condition is continuous or intermittent.
  • Confirm whether the heater turns off when the error occurs.
  • Record the selected dialysate flow setting at the time of failure.

Expected outcome: The reported error and associated operating behavior are confirmed and clearly documented.

3. Check the Selected Flow Setting and Configuration

  • Verify that the selected dialysate flow rate is appropriate for the intended test or operating condition.
  • Confirm that no unintended flow setting or unusual setup condition has been selected.
  • Note whether the error appears at one flow setting or at multiple flow settings.
  • Use the applicable manufacturer procedure or approved facility process when evaluating operational performance.

Expected outcome: The machine is configured for an expected dialysate flow rate without an incorrect setting contributing to the error.

Why it matters: Balancing chamber cycle time is related to flow rate. At 800 mL/min, expected switching timing is identified as 2.25 seconds ±10%; switching faster than 1.8 seconds is outside normal maximum-flow timing.

4. Inspect External Dialysate and Hydraulic Connections

  • Inspect accessible external tubing and connections associated with the dialysate and hydraulic flow path.
  • Confirm that tubing is correctly routed, fully connected, and not kinked, collapsed, damaged, or leaking.
  • Check for visible contamination, loose fittings, bypassed routing, or incorrect external connection.
  • Confirm that no external testing accessory or temporary setup is altering normal flow behavior.

Expected outcome: Accessible external flow-path connections are correct, secure, and free of visible abnormalities.

Why it matters: External routing or setup problems should be ruled out before an internal hydraulic or sensor failure is suspected.

5. Restart and Observe Error Recurrence

  • After external checks are completed and the machine remains out of patient use, restart or rerun the applicable preparation or test sequence according to facility procedure.
  • Observe whether High Flow Error returns immediately or only at a specific selected flow rate.
  • Document whether the heater shuts down again when the error occurs.
  • Note whether the abnormal condition is repeatable or intermittent.

Expected outcome: The error either clears after correction of an external or configuration issue, or remains reproducible and requires escalation.

6. Identify the Likely Failure Category

  • If proper setup and accessible external connections have been confirmed, consider whether the error is likely related to abnormal flow-pump operation.
  • Consider whether the chamber full switch signal may be falsely indicating rapid chamber filling.
  • Consider abnormal balancing chamber cycling or valve operation as possible causes.
  • Do not perform internal disassembly or component-level repair unless authorized, qualified, and following applicable service procedures.

Expected outcome: External and simple configuration causes are ruled out, leaving a likely internal hydraulic, pump, valve, or chamber full switch condition for bench evaluation.

7. Verify Resolution Before Return to Service

  • If correcting a flow setting, tubing condition, external connection, or setup issue eliminates High Flow Error, complete applicable operational verification before returning the machine to service.
  • Confirm that the error does not recur during the approved verification process.
  • Document the confirmed cause, corrective action, verification completed, and final equipment status.

Expected outcome: The machine completes operational verification without recurrence of High Flow Error before return to clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If High Flow Error remains after selected flow settings, external hydraulic connections, accessible tubing, and configuration have been checked, external causes have been ruled out and the remaining condition suggests an internal problem involving the flow pump, chamber full switch signal, balancing chamber cycling, valve operation, or internal hydraulic flow-control function.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the device for qualified repair or bench evaluation.
  • Do not return the machine to clinical use with recurring High Flow Error or unresolved heater shutdown.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a recurring High Flow Error on an active patient. Move the patient to an approved backup device or ensure safe alternate dialysis support first, because unresolved dialysate-flow control and heater shutdown may compromise dependable therapy delivery.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Fresenius 2008T displayed High Flow Error during preparation or operational testing, with heater shutdown observed when the condition occurred."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed selected dialysate flow setting and inspected accessible external hydraulic tubing and connections. High Flow Error remained reproducible, indicating suspected internal flow-pump, chamber full switch, balancing chamber, valve, or hydraulic flow-control malfunction."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and labeled Out of Service for qualified bench evaluation and repair. Unit not returned to patient use pending successful operational verification."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A High Flow Error on the Fresenius Medical Care 2008T indicates that balancing chamber switching is occurring faster than expected, with heater shutdown as a protective response. Begin with patient safety, verify the selected flow and accessible external connections, escalate when the condition remains reproducible, and clearly document the complaint, cause, and resolution.

That is successful troubleshooting.
