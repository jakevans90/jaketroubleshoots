Edwards Lifesciences HemoSphere Advanced Monitoring System

Pressure Cable, Sensor, or Module Not Detected

Asset Type

Hemodynamic Monitor

Manufacturer

Edwards Lifesciences

Model

HemoSphere Advanced Monitoring System

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting when the HemoSphere system does not recognize a pressure cable, sensor, module, or connected hemodynamic accessory.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Before troubleshooting, confirm whether the monitor is being used for active invasive hemodynamic monitoring.

Action:

  • Do not troubleshoot cable or module connections on an active patient unless clinical staff confirm it is safe.
  • If pressure or hemodynamic data is clinically needed, have staff move monitoring to another available device or backup method.
  • If the monitor is not displaying required parameters during patient care, treat the issue as clinically significant.

Expected outcome: Patient monitoring is maintained safely while the equipment issue is investigated.

Why it matters: A missing pressure cable, sensor, or module can prevent arterial pressure, central venous pressure, cardiac output, or other advanced parameters from displaying.

Verify the Reported Detection Issue

Confirm what the HemoSphere system is failing to detect.

Action:

  • Ask clinical staff what was connected when the issue occurred.
  • Identify whether the issue involves a pressure cable, pressure transducer or sensor, FloTrac / Acumen IQ sensor, HemoSphere module, patient cable, smart cable, or interface cable.
  • Check whether the message appears at startup, after connection, during patient monitoring, or after moving the monitor.

Expected outcome: The exact failed item and timing are identified.

Why it matters: A missing module message, cable detection error, and sensor recognition issue can have different causes.

Check the Module Seating

Confirm the module is fully installed in the monitor.

Action:

  • Remove the monitor from patient use first if needed.
  • Power the unit down if required by facility practice before reseating modules.
  • Inspect the module bay.
  • Remove and reseat the module firmly.
  • Confirm the module locks or seats evenly without being partially inserted.
  • Restart the monitor and check whether the module is recognized.

Expected outcome: The HemoSphere system detects the installed module.

If resolved, stop troubleshooting and document the reseated module.

Why it matters: A partially seated module may appear physically installed but fail electrical communication with the monitor.

Inspect External Cable Connections

Check all external connections before suspecting equipment failure.

Action:

  • Confirm the pressure cable is connected to the correct port or module.
  • Disconnect and reconnect the cable.
  • Check for loose, angled, or partially inserted connectors.
  • Inspect the connector ends for bent pins, damaged contacts, fluid contamination, cracked plastic, or broken locking tabs.
  • Confirm no adapter or extension cable is missing from the setup.

Expected outcome: The cable connects securely and the monitor recognizes the accessory.

If resolved, stop troubleshooting and document the loose or corrected connection.

Why it matters: Detection issues are often caused by connection problems, damaged connector contacts, or incorrect cable routing.

Confirm the Correct Sensor or Disposable Is Being Used

Verify that the connected sensor matches the intended HemoSphere setup.

Action:

  • Confirm the disposable pressure sensor, FloTrac sensor, Acumen IQ sensor, or related accessory is compatible with the monitor configuration.
  • Check that the sensor packaging is correct for the intended parameter.
  • Confirm the sensor is not expired, damaged, contaminated, or previously used in a way that could affect recognition.
  • Replace the disposable sensor with a new compatible sensor if there is any doubt.

Expected outcome: The monitor detects the correct sensor after connection.

If resolved, stop troubleshooting and document the defective, incorrect, or replaced sensor.

Why it matters: Some detection problems come from the wrong disposable, damaged sensor contacts, or a sensor that cannot communicate properly with the cable/module.

Swap With a Known-Good Cable

Rule out the pressure cable or smart cable as the cause.

Action:

  • Use a known-good compatible cable from another working HemoSphere setup if available.
  • Connect the known-good cable to the same module and sensor.
  • Check whether the monitor now detects the cable or sensor.
  • If the known-good cable works, tag the suspect cable for replacement or repair.

Expected outcome: The monitor recognizes the known-good cable.

If resolved, stop troubleshooting and remove the failed cable from service.

Why it matters: Cables are high-wear items and may fail from repeated cleaning, bending, pulling, or connector damage.

Swap With a Known-Good Sensor or Module When Available

Narrow the failure to the sensor, cable, module, or monitor.

Action:

  • Test the suspect cable with a known-good compatible sensor.
  • Test the suspect sensor with a known-good compatible cable if appropriate.
  • If the system uses a removable module, test with another known-good compatible module if one is available.
  • Do not interchange parts unless they are confirmed compatible and safe for that monitor configuration.

Expected outcome: The failed component is isolated.

If resolved, stop troubleshooting and remove the failed accessory or module from service.

Why it matters: Controlled swapping helps avoid replacing the monitor when the issue is actually a failed cable, module, or disposable sensor.

Check for Contamination or Cleaning Damage

Look for signs that fluid or cleaning solution affected the connector or module area.

Action:

  • Inspect cable ends, module ports, and the monitor connection area.
  • Look for moisture, residue, corrosion, sticky buildup, or damaged seals.
  • Do not connect wet or contaminated accessories.
  • Allow only approved cleaning and drying methods per facility policy.
  • Remove any accessory from service if contamination or corrosion is found.

Expected outcome: Connectors are clean, dry, undamaged, and safe to reconnect.

Why it matters: Fluid intrusion or residue can prevent accessory detection and may create unreliable monitoring.

Restart the Monitor

Clear a possible temporary software or communication hang.

Action:

  • Disconnect from patient use first if the monitor is being used clinically.
  • Power the HemoSphere system off.
  • Wait briefly.
  • Power the monitor back on.
  • Reconnect the module, cable, and sensor.
  • Confirm whether the device detects the accessory after restart.

Expected outcome: The accessory is detected after the system reinitializes.

If resolved, stop troubleshooting and document that the issue cleared after restart and reconnection.

Why it matters: Some accessory communication errors may persist until the monitor restarts and rechecks connected hardware.

Check Monitor Configuration and Parameter Setup

Confirm the monitor is set up for the intended monitoring mode.

Action:

  • Verify the expected parameter is enabled or selected.
  • Confirm the correct module or cable type is being used for that parameter.
  • Check whether the patient profile, monitoring mode, or connection screen shows the accessory as unavailable, disconnected, or unsupported.
  • Have clinical staff confirm the intended monitoring setup if needed.

Expected outcome: The monitor configuration matches the connected hardware and intended parameter.

Why it matters: The monitor may not display or recognize expected data if the wrong mode, setup path, or parameter configuration is selected.

Check Power and General System Health

Make sure the detection problem is not part of a larger system issue.

Action:

  • Confirm the monitor powers on normally.
  • Check for boot errors, system alerts, battery issues, touchscreen problems, or repeated restarts.
  • Confirm the module bay and display respond normally.
  • Listen for unusual sounds and check for unusual heat or smell.

Expected outcome: The monitor operates normally except for the specific detection issue.

Why it matters: Multiple system problems may indicate a larger internal fault rather than a single cable or sensor issue.

Perform a Known-Good Setup Test

Use a complete known-good setup to confirm whether the monitor itself is the problem.

Action:

  • On the bench, connect a known-good compatible module, cable, and sensor.
  • Power on the monitor.
  • Confirm whether the HemoSphere system detects the full setup.
  • If the known-good setup fails, suspect the monitor, module bay, or internal communication path.

Expected outcome: The monitor detects the known-good setup.

If the known-good setup works, the original accessory chain is the likely issue. If it fails, escalate the monitor for repair.

Why it matters: A complete known-good setup is one of the cleanest ways to separate accessory failure from monitor failure.

If the Problem Persists

If the HemoSphere system still does not detect the pressure cable, sensor, or module after external checks, reseating, accessory swaps, restart, and known-good setup testing, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal to the monitor, module, module bay, communication path, or accessory electronics.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue clinical use when the monitor cannot reliably detect required hemodynamic monitoring components.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot invasive pressure monitoring accessories while the patient depends on that monitor unless clinical staff confirm it is safe. If pressure or advanced hemodynamic data is required, move the patient to another working monitor or backup monitoring method before continuing equipment troubleshooting. Therapy continuity should be maintained before equipment troubleshooting continues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Edwards HemoSphere monitor would not detect the connected pressure cable and sensor."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found the pressure cable was not recognized on the original setup, but the monitor detected a known-good cable and sensor during bench testing."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the failed pressure cable from service, verified the HemoSphere monitor recognized a known-good cable/sensor setup, and returned the monitor to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact message or detection error displayed
  • Whether issue involved pressure cable, sensor, module, or smart cable
  • Parameter affected, such as arterial pressure, CVP, cardiac output, SVV, or advanced monitoring value
  • Whether issue occurred at startup, during setup, or during patient monitoring
  • Alarm behavior or alerts present during the issue
  • Accessories swapped, including known-good cable, sensor, or module
  • Power behavior, restart results, boot errors, battery issues, or repeated restarts
  • Environmental factors such as fluid exposure, cleaning residue, moisture, or connector contamination
  • Indicator lights, system messages, connection screen status, or module status indicators
  • Final device status
  • Any accessory removed from service

Final Thought

Pressure cable, sensor, or module detection problems should be approached logically from the outside in. Start with patient safety, then verify the setup, connections, accessories, and known-good swaps before suspecting internal monitor failure. Clear documentation helps show what was ruled out, what failed, and why the device was returned to service or escalated for repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
