Getinge 8666 / 8668 Series

Temperature not reaching setpoint

Asset Type

Washer-Disinfector

Manufacturer

Getinge

Model

8666 / 8668 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Cycle runs but fails to reach programmed temperature due to water supply, loading, sensor, or heating-related external issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Operational Safety

Confirm no critical instruments are dependent on this unit.

Expected: Backup reprocessing method available.

Why it matters: Inadequate temperature compromises disinfection effectiveness and patient safety.

Verify the Reported Issue

Run a cycle and observe temperature readings and any alarms.

Expected: Temperature stalls below setpoint or alarms out.

Why it matters: Confirms a true heating issue and identifies when failure occurs.

Check Incoming Water Supply Temperature

Verify facility hot water supply temperature to the unit.

Expected: Incoming water meets manufacturer requirements (typically pre-heated).

Why it matters: Low inlet temperature increases heating demand and may prevent reaching setpoint.

If low → escalate to facilities and retest.

Inspect Water Supply Flow and Pressure

Ensure adequate water pressure and flow to the unit (no partially closed valves or restrictions).

Expected: Strong, consistent water flow.

Why it matters: Insufficient flow affects heating efficiency and circulation.

If restricted → restore flow and retest.

Check Chamber Loading and Rack Configuration

Inspect load size and placement of trays/instruments.

Expected: Proper loading without overloading or blocked spray arms.

Why it matters: Overloading reduces heat distribution and prevents uniform temperature rise.

If overloaded → adjust load and retest.

Inspect Spray Arms for Rotation and Blockage

Check that spray arms rotate freely and are not clogged.

Expected: Free movement with clear nozzles.

Why it matters: Poor circulation limits heat transfer throughout the chamber.

If blocked → clean and retest.

Verify Detergent and Chemical Dosing

Confirm proper detergent dosing and correct chemical type.

Expected: Proper dosing during cycle.

Why it matters: Incorrect chemistry can affect heat transfer and cleaning performance.

If dosing issue found → correct and retest.

Check Door Seal Integrity

Inspect door gasket for damage, debris, or improper sealing.

Expected: Tight seal with no visible gaps.

Why it matters: Heat loss from poor sealing prevents reaching temperature setpoint.

If seal compromised → clean/adjust or remove from service if damaged.

Observe for Heating Indicators or Alarms

Monitor for heating-related alarms or lack of temperature increase during heating phase.

Expected: Gradual and steady temperature rise.

Why it matters: Lack of heating response suggests heater or control-related issue after external causes are ruled out.

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal (heater element, temperature sensor, relay, or control system failure).

Remove the unit from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not rely on a washer-disinfector that is not reaching temperature. Always move instruments to a verified functioning unit before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Washer-disinfector not reaching programmed temperature during cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Incoming hot water temperature below required range due to partially closed supply valve."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Restored water flow, verified inlet temperature, and confirmed unit reached setpoint during test cycle."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Incoming water temperature verified
  • Water pressure/flow checked
  • Spray arms inspected and cleared
  • Load configuration reviewed
  • Door seal condition checked
  • Any temperature alarms or codes observed
  • Final cycle test results
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Temperature issues should always be approached from external factors first—water supply, loading, and circulation. Proper escalation prevents unnecessary downtime and ensures safe instrument reprocessing. Strong documentation supports both compliance and repeatability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
