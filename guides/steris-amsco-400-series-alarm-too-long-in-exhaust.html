STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Too Long In Exhaust - chamber does not exhaust to 4 psig within the allotted time

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the “Too Long In Exhaust” alarm condition where the chamber fails to reach approximately 4 psig within the expected time, resulting in extended exhaust phases and potential incomplete sterilization cycles.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not use instruments from a failed or alarmed cycle
  • Remove the sterilizer from service if cycle reliability is uncertain
  • Ensure alternative sterilization methods are available

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm “Too Long In Exhaust” alarm is displayed
  • Observe that it occurs during the exhaust phase
  • Check that chamber pressure does not drop to ~4 psig within expected time
  • Note if the unit attempts to extend exhaust time automatically

Check Chamber Drain Strainer (Most Common Cause)

  • Inspect for debris, scale, or buildup
  • Remove and clean thoroughly if obstructed
  • Reinstall securely after cleaning

Inspect Drain and Exhaust Path

  • Check for kinks or restrictions in piping
  • Look for partial blockages downstream
  • Verify proper flow through drain system

Evaluate Load Conditions

  • Ensure chamber is not overloaded
  • Verify proper tray spacing
  • Avoid dense packs that restrict airflow

Verify Door Gasket and Seal Condition

  • Inspect gasket for debris or residue
  • Check for wear or damage
  • Ensure proper seating and seal integrity

Attempt a Test Cycle

  • Run a cycle after cleaning and inspection
  • Verify chamber reaches ~4 psig within normal exhaust time
  • Confirm alarm does not recur

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out:

  • Possible solenoid valve malfunction (S03)
  • Control system out of calibration
  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or calibration

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or release instruments from a sterilizer that alarmed during a cycle. Always move to a backup sterilizer before continuing clinical operations.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer alarming 'Too Long In Exhaust'; chamber not reaching ~4 psig and extending exhaust phase."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Chamber drain strainer clogged, restricting exhaust flow and slowing depressurization."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned drain strainer and verified exhaust path; test cycle completed successfully without alarm."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

This alarm indicates a failure to properly depressurize the chamber, which directly impacts sterilization effectiveness. Always rule out flow restrictions first, then escalate to internal component or calibration issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
