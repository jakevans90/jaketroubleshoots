Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: FLOW SENSOR FAIL !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

FLOW SENSOR FAIL ! alarm indicating loss of flow measurement, resulting in inaccurate or absent ventilation monitoring and potential inability to safely deliver or verify patient breaths.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use if ventilation accuracy is uncertain
  • Transfer patient to an alternate ventilator or anesthesia machine

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm “FLOW SENSOR FAIL !” is displayed
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup vs during ventilation)
  • Check if tidal volume or flow readings are missing or inaccurate
  • Look for any additional related alarms

3. Check Flow Sensor Cable Connection

  • Ensure the cable is fully seated at both the sensor and machine
  • Inspect for bent pins, loose connectors, or damage
  • Reconnect securely if needed

4. Inspect the Flow Sensor and Breathing System

  • Verify proper installation in the breathing circuit
  • Check for moisture, condensation, or contamination
  • Inspect for cracks or physical damage
  • Clean or replace sensor as needed per protocol

5. Perform Flow Sensor Calibration

  • Initiate calibration via device menu
  • Ensure no patient is connected
  • Follow on-screen prompts carefully

6. Substitute with a Known Good Flow Sensor

  • Replace with a known working sensor if available
  • Repeat calibration procedure

7. Inspect Environmental and Setup Factors

  • Check for excessive condensation in the circuit
  • Ensure proper hose and circuit assembly
  • Confirm no airflow obstructions

If the Problem Persists

If external causes have been ruled out and the alarm continues after cable checks, cleaning/replacement, and calibration:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or further bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or calibrate a flow sensor while connected to a patient. Always transition ventilation support before addressing this alarm.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed FLOW SENSOR FAIL ! alarm with no tidal volume readings during setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Defective flow sensor identified after reseating cable and failed calibration."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced flow sensor, completed calibration successfully, and verified normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Flow sensor alarms directly impact ventilation accuracy. Always rule out simple external causes like connections and contamination before escalating to internal failure, and prioritize patient safety throughout.

That is successful troubleshooting.
