Cardinal Health Kangaroo ePump

Error: 10 Flash write error

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo ePump

What This Guide Helps With

Error: 10 Flash write error indicates a failure in the pump’s internal memory system. This can prevent proper startup, stop the device from saving programmed settings, and lead to unreliable or interrupted enteral feeding.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the pump if actively infusing
  • Transfer feeding to a backup pump
  • Ensure continuous nutrition delivery using alternate equipment

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and observe the display
  • Confirm Error: 10 Flash write error is shown
  • Note whether the pump fails to boot, freezes, or cannot save settings

3. Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the pump OFF
  • Disconnect AC power
  • Allow full shutdown (remove battery if accessible without disassembly)
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart

4. Check Power Source Stability

  • Plug into a known-good wall outlet
  • Avoid power strips or extension cords
  • Verify AC indicator lights function normally

5. Observe for Reoccurrence During Use

  • Attempt to program a feed
  • Verify if settings can be saved
  • Monitor for repeat occurrence of the error

If the Problem Persists

If Error: 10 Flash write error continues after external checks and power cycling:

  • Internal flash memory or control board failure is likely
  • Device cannot reliably store or execute operating data
  • Remove the pump from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or control board replacement

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a feeding pump on an active patient. Always move the patient to a properly functioning backup device before performing any troubleshooting steps.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error: 10 Flash write error and unable to save feeding settings."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Error persisted after power cycle; device failed to retain programmed settings, indicating internal memory failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair with suspected control board replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Flash memory errors directly impact device reliability and safe operation. Recognizing internal failures early and removing the device from service prevents unsafe feeding conditions and ensures patient care is not compromised.

That is successful troubleshooting.
