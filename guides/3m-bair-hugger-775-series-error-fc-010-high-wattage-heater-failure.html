3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-010 High-wattage heater failure

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-010 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. FC-010 indicates a high-wattage heater failure that may affect heat output, temperature control, or safe warming operation.

A heater-related fault can prevent the unit from maintaining the selected warming temperature or may indicate a problem in the heater/control system. Patient safety should come first, and the unit should not remain in clinical use with an unresolved heater fault.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Bair Hugger warming unit from patient use if Error FC-010 is active, recurring, or affecting heat delivery.
  • Do not troubleshoot the unit while it is actively warming a patient.
  • Have clinical staff transfer warming support to another approved warming device if patient warming is still needed.
  • Do not continue patient use with an unresolved heater-related fault.
  • Allow the unit to cool before handling or bench evaluation if it was recently operating.

2. Verify the Reported Error

  • Confirm the exact displayed error is FC-010.
  • Ask staff when the error occurred, such as during startup, after selecting a temperature setting, during active warming, or intermittently during use.
  • Note whether the error clears after restart or returns immediately.
  • Check whether the blower runs and whether the unit produces heat at any temperature setting.

3. Check Power Source and Basic Setup

  • Confirm the power cord is fully seated at the unit and wall outlet.
  • Inspect the cord and plug for damage, looseness, bent blades, discoloration, or signs of overheating.
  • Plug the unit into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Avoid power strips, extension cords, or questionable outlets during testing.
  • Confirm the unit powers on normally.

4. Inspect External Condition

  • Inspect the hose for kinks, blockage, cracks, damage, contamination, or improper connection.
  • Check the air inlet and filter area for heavy lint buildup or blocked airflow.
  • Inspect the warming blanket connection if a blanket was in use when the fault occurred.
  • Look for physical damage, liquid intrusion, burning smell, discoloration, or signs of overheating.
  • Remove any obvious external blockage or unsafe accessory condition before retesting.

5. Verify Fan Operation in Ambient Mode

  • Place the unit in a safe non-patient-use test area.
  • Set the unit to Ambient mode.
  • Confirm the fan or blower starts and runs normally.
  • Listen for abnormal noise, weak airflow, grinding, intermittent operation, or no airflow.
  • Confirm airflow is present at the hose outlet.

6. Check Whether the Error Appears When Heat Is Requested

  • After confirming Ambient mode operation, select a warming temperature.
  • Observe whether Error FC-010 appears immediately when heat is requested.
  • Check whether the unit attempts to heat or remains cool.
  • Do not perform repeated heat testing if the fault returns.
  • If the error returns during heat demand, remove the unit from service for repair evaluation.

7. Remove From Service for Heater/Control Evaluation

  • If power, hose, filter, airflow, and basic setup concerns have been ruled out, treat the issue as an internal heater/control system fault.
  • Label the unit Out of Service.
  • Send the unit for bench evaluation by trained service personnel.
  • Do not perform internal disassembly unless authorized, trained, and following approved service procedures.
  • Do not return the unit to service until repair, calibration, functional testing, and safety checks are completed.

8. Verify After Repair

  • Confirm the unit runs normally in Ambient mode.
  • Confirm each warming temperature setting operates properly.
  • Confirm Error FC-010 does not return.
  • Verify airflow is normal.
  • Perform required calibration after heater or control-related repair.
  • Perform required electrical safety testing and post-repair checks per facility procedure and service requirements.
  • Document final test results before returning the unit to service.

If the Problem Persists

If Error FC-010 continues after patient safety actions, power verification, hose inspection, filter and airflow checks, fan verification, and non-patient-use testing, external and common setup causes have been ruled out.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. A persistent FC-010 error strongly indicates an internal heater/control system failure that should not be ignored.

Do not keep returning a patient warming unit to service with an unresolved heater fault.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, then evaluate the Bair Hugger unit away from patient care. A warming unit with an unresolved FC-010 error should not be trusted for patient temperature management.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 Series warming unit reported with Error FC-010. Staff stated the unit alarmed during warming and heat output was not reliable."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified Error FC-010 high-wattage heater failure. Power source, hose connection, filter area, airflow, and fan operation were checked. Error returned when heat was requested."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for heater/control system repair evaluation. After repair, calibration, functional testing, airflow verification, and required safety checks were completed before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error FC-010 should be treated as a heater-related fault until proven otherwise. Start with patient safety, confirm the error, verify power and airflow, then escalate the unit for proper heater/control evaluation when external causes are ruled out. A warming device with an unresolved heater failure should not remain in patient use.

That is successful troubleshooting.
