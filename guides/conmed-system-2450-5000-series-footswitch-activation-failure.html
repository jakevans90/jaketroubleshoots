CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series

Footswitch Activation Failure

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

CONMED

Model

System 2450 / 5000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a CONMED ESU when the footswitch fails to activate energy delivery or other device functions. The focus is on verifying simple, external issues—such as cabling, connectors, and user interaction—before considering internal or generator faults. Patient safety and proper escalation are emphasized.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure ESU is Powered On and Idle

  • Verify the generator is turned on and has completed its self-check.
  • Confirm the device is not in a lockout mode or alarm state, which can prevent footswitch activation.

Why: Footswitchs will not activate if the ESU is not ready to deliver energy.

Inspect Footswitch Connection

  • Disconnect and reconnect the footswitch cable.
  • Examine the connector for bent pins, debris, or damage.
  • If using a multi-function footswitch, ensure the correct port is being used.

Why: Poor connection is a common cause of activation failure.

Test with Alternate Footswitch (If Available)

  • Swap with a known-good footswitch to isolate the issue to the footswitch or the generator.

Why: Confirms whether the problem is the accessory or the ESU input.

Check Footswitch Functionality

  • Depress the footswitch while visually observing the generator’s indicators.
  • Listen for the click or tactile feedback from the footswitch.

Why: Confirms that the mechanical action is being properly transmitted.

Inspect Cables for Damage or Kinks

  • Look for fraying, cuts, or sharp bends in the footswitch cable.
  • Replace the cable if any damage is found.

Why: Broken wiring inside the cable can prevent activation.

Verify Generator Input Settings

  • Ensure the ESU is configured for footswitch operation in the system menu.
  • Some models allow selection between handpiece activation, footswitch, or dual.

Why: Incorrect settings can disable footswitch input.

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks are complete and the footswitch still fails:

  • Remove the ESU from service.
  • Label it “Out of Service.”
  • Send for vendor repair or bench evaluation.

Why: Continuing use without resolution can compromise patient safety and may cause energy delivery failure during procedures.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot the footswitch while the ESU is connected to an active patient.
  • Move the patient to an alternative ESU if available.
  • Only test the footswitch when the generator is idle and safe.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Footswitch does not activate generator energy output when depressed.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Footswitch cable found connected properly but activation failed; mechanical action not registering on generator.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Tested with alternate footswitch; issue persisted. Device removed from service and labeled Out of Service. Sent for vendor repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Footswitch type and model.
  • ESU serial number.
  • Any error codes displayed.
  • Condition of cables and connectors.
  • Observed indicator lights during testing.
  • Confirmation of generator settings for footswitch operation.

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting requires starting with simple, verifiable external checks before assuming internal failure. Always prioritize patient safety, know when to escalate, and document thoroughly to ensure accurate work history and minimize repeat issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
