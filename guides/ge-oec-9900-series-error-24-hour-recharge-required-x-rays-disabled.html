GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: 24-HOUR RECHARGE REQUIRED - X RAYS DISABLED

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: 24-HOUR RECHARGE REQUIRED - X RAYS DISABLED on a GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm. This condition disables generator functions and prevents X-ray operation because the C-Arm battery system requires an extended recharge period, commonly after long-term storage or being unplugged for an extended time.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not attempt to use the C-Arm for a procedure while this error is active.
  • Remove the OEC 9900 from clinical use if X-ray capability is needed.
  • Notify the clinical team that generator functions are disabled and the unit cannot produce X-rays until recharge is complete.
  • Provide another C-Arm or approved imaging method if imaging is needed for a case.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the system enough to observe the displayed error.
  • Confirm the message reads: 24-HOUR RECHARGE REQUIRED - X RAYS DISABLED.
  • Confirm whether X-ray or generator functions are disabled.
  • Ask staff whether the unit had been stored, unused, or unplugged for an extended period.

Understand What the Error Means

  • Treat this as a required battery recharge condition, not a user setting problem.
  • Do not repeatedly reboot the system expecting the error to clear immediately.
  • Understand that the system disables X-ray functions during this condition to prevent use before the battery system is ready.

Connect the Workstation to AC Power

  • Plug the workstation power cord into a known-good AC outlet.
  • Confirm the power cord is fully seated at the workstation and wall outlet.
  • Check for facility power issues, damaged cords, loose plugs, or tripped outlets.
  • Use a tested outlet if there is any doubt about power availability.

Verify the Interconnect Cable Is Connected

  • Inspect the interconnect cable between the workstation and C-Arm.
  • Verify the cable is fully seated and latched, if applicable.
  • Look for bent pins, damaged connectors, loose strain reliefs, crushed cable sections, or contamination.
  • Reconnect the cable securely if it is loose.

Leave the System Plugged In for the Full Recharge Period

  • Leave the workstation power cord plugged into AC power.
  • Keep the interconnect cable connected.
  • The workstation does not need to be turned on during this recharge period.
  • Attach a charging or do-not-use note if needed so the unit is not unplugged early.
  • Allow the full 24-hour recharge period to complete.

Recheck the System After 24 Hours

  • Power the system on after the full 24-hour recharge period.
  • Confirm the error message is no longer displayed.
  • Verify normal startup behavior.
  • Perform any required operational checks according to facility procedure before returning the device to service.

Check for Repeat or Persistent Recharge Errors

  • Confirm the unit remained plugged into AC power for the full recharge period.
  • Confirm the outlet had power the entire time.
  • Confirm the interconnect cable stayed connected.
  • Inspect accessible AC power, interconnect, and battery-related connections without disassembly.
  • If the error remains after verified recharge conditions, remove the device from service for further evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the OEC 9900 still displays Error: 24-HOUR RECHARGE REQUIRED - X RAYS DISABLED after a verified 24-hour AC recharge with the interconnect cable connected, remove the unit from service.

At that point, external and common causes have been ruled out. A persistent recharge-required condition may indicate failed batteries, charging circuit failure, power supply issues, interconnect communication problems, or another internal system fault.

  • Remove the device from clinical use.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not keep cycling power or attempting clinical use when X-ray functions remain disabled.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this condition on an active patient or during an active procedure. Move the patient to a backup device or approved alternate imaging method first.

Do not schedule or stage an OEC 9900 for a procedure immediately after long-term storage unless battery recharge status has been verified. If this error is active, the unit cannot be relied on for patient imaging until the recharge period is complete and normal operation is confirmed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE OEC 9900 C-Arm displayed Error: 24-HOUR RECHARGE REQUIRED - X RAYS DISABLED. Staff reported the unit could not perform X-ray functions after being unused or stored for an extended period."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"C-Arm battery system required extended recharge. Workstation AC power and interconnect cable were checked. Error condition was consistent with depleted batteries after long-term storage."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Connected workstation to known-good AC power, verified interconnect cable was fully seated, and allowed required 24-hour recharge period. After recharge, system was powered on and error cleared. Operational checks completed and unit returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

This error is usually a controlled system lockout caused by depleted C-Arm batteries after extended storage. The correct approach is to protect patient care first, verify the exact error, confirm AC power and interconnect connection, allow the full recharge period, and escalate only if the error remains after proper recharge conditions are verified.

That is successful troubleshooting.
