GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 10 Self-Calibration Failure

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 10 [XX XX XX XX] when the GE Healthcare Panda Warmer reports a self-calibration failure. This system failure prevents reliable warmer operation and requires the device to be removed from clinical use until the fault is evaluated and corrected.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately remove the Panda Warmer from patient use if Sys Fail 10 occurs while the warmer is occupied, prepared for use, or needed for patient care.
  • Transfer the infant to a known-working warming device according to clinical procedure before performing troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on a warmer displaying an unresolved system failure for thermal support.

Expected outcome: The infant is no longer dependent on a warmer with an unresolved system failure.

Why it matters: A self-calibration failure means the warmer cannot be trusted to provide reliable monitoring or controlled heat output.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, confirm that the display shows Sys Fail 10 [XX XX XX XX].
  • Document the displayed hexadecimal values exactly as shown.
  • Determine whether the error occurs during startup, immediately after power-on, intermittently after operation begins, or every time the unit is restarted.
  • Observe whether the warmer completes startup, responds to controls, or remains in a system-failure condition.

Expected outcome: The error is reproducible or clearly observed and documented.

Why it matters: Accurate fault confirmation preserves useful service information and verifies that the issue is a system-reported self-calibration failure.

3. Check the Power Source and External Condition

  • Inspect the power cord, plug, strain relief, and warmer power-entry area for visible damage, looseness, contamination, overheating, or signs of liquid intrusion.
  • Connect the warmer to a verified hospital-grade outlet and restart the unit only after it has been removed from patient use.
  • Document any abnormal power-cord, plug, receptacle, or power-entry findings.

Expected outcome: The warmer receives stable power without visible external power-related defects.

Why it matters: Although Sys Fail 10 indicates a self-calibration failure, obvious external power conditions should be ruled out before repair escalation.

4. Inspect the Unit for External Damage or Contamination

  • Check for fluid intrusion near the control panel or electrical areas.
  • Check for recent impact, transport damage, cracked housing, or loose external assemblies.
  • Note any burnt odor, unusual heat, abnormal sounds, damaged display, or control-panel damage.
  • Do not open the unit or perform internal board-level inspection during initial clinical-area troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Any visible external condition affecting safe operation is identified and documented.

Why it matters: External damage or contamination may contribute to electronic failure and affects the repair evaluation.

5. Perform a Controlled Power Restart

  • With the warmer out of service and disconnected from patient care, power the unit off.
  • After a brief wait, power it back on using the verified outlet.
  • Observe whether Sys Fail 10 [XX XX XX XX] returns during startup or before the warmer becomes ready for use.

Expected outcome: The error either clears and the warmer completes startup normally, or the error returns consistently.

Why it matters: A recurring self-calibration failure after a controlled restart supports an internal control-system fault rather than a temporary operating condition.

6. Determine Whether the Failure Requires Repair Evaluation

  • If the power source is verified, no external damage or contamination explains the condition, and Sys Fail 10 returns after restart, classify the issue as a device-related failure.
  • The reported condition indicates that the warmer did not complete required self-calibration.
  • Manufacturer corrective information identifies Control PCA replacement as the indicated repair action for this failure.
  • Control PCA replacement and post-repair verification must be completed through qualified repair procedures.

Expected outcome: The warmer is correctly routed for repair or bench evaluation rather than continued troubleshooting in the clinical area.

7. Confirm Safe Disposition

  • If the error does not return after evaluation, do not return the warmer to patient use solely because one restart was successful.
  • Complete the facility-required functional inspection, alarm verification, and safety testing appropriate for an infant warmer that reported a system failure.
  • If required checks pass and the error remains cleared, document the findings and return the unit to service according to departmental policy.
  • If the error remains present or returns, remove the unit from service and escalate for qualified repair.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 10 [XX XX XX XX] remains present or recurs after verifying the power source, inspecting the unit externally, and performing a controlled restart, external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent self-calibration failure indicates that the Panda Warmer requires qualified repair or bench evaluation. Manufacturer corrective information identifies Control PCA replacement as the indicated repair action for this failure.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Document the displayed Sys Fail 10 values and the conditions under which the error occurred.
  • Send the warmer for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Complete required post-repair inspection and verification before return to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. If Sys Fail 10 appears while the Panda Warmer is supporting an infant, move the patient to a verified backup warming device first, then evaluate the affected warmer.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displayed Sys Fail 10 [XX XX XX XX] during startup, indicating self-calibration failure. Unit was not considered safe for patient use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified hospital-grade power source and inspected external condition with no correctable external issue identified. Sys Fail 10 returned after controlled restart, indicating likely internal Control PCA failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed Panda Warmer from service, labeled unit Out of Service, documented the displayed fault values, and routed the device for qualified bench repair and Control PCA evaluation/replacement as indicated."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Sys Fail 10 indicates that the Panda Warmer failed its required self-calibration and should not be trusted for clinical warming until evaluated. Prioritize infant safety, confirm the displayed fault, rule out correctable external conditions, escalate appropriately for internal repair, and document the findings clearly using the CCR method.

That is successful troubleshooting.
