Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: FRESH GAS LOW !!

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

FRESH GAS LOW !! alarm indicating insufficient fresh gas flow, which can lead to inadequate ventilation delivery and risk of patient hypoventilation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the machine is in use, immediately assess ventilation
  • If gas delivery is inadequate, switch to manual ventilation or alternate anesthesia machine
  • Ensure continuous patient support

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the FRESH GAS LOW !! alarm is displayed
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup vs during ventilation)
  • Check flowmeter readings for low or zero flow
  • Observe bellows movement or lack of volume delivery
  • Check for additional related alarms

Check Medical Gas Supply Sources

  • Confirm wall gas connections (O₂, Air, N₂O if used) are connected
  • Verify pipeline pressures are within normal range
  • Check backup cylinders are open and adequately pressurized

Check Flowmeter and Gas Settings

  • Ensure flowmeters are not set to zero
  • Confirm proper gas mixture settings
  • Adjust flow controls and observe response

Inspect Fresh Gas Hoses and Connections

  • Check hoses for kinks, compression, or blockage
  • Ensure all connections are secure
  • Look for visible damage or disconnections

Check Breathing Circuit for Leaks or Disconnections

  • Inspect all circuit connections
  • Check for cracks, holes, or worn seals
  • Verify all components are properly connected

Perform a Leak Test

  • Run a system leak test per standard procedure
  • Verify results are within acceptable limits

Stop When Resolved

  • Confirm normal gas flow is restored
  • Verify stable ventilation
  • Return device to service if functioning properly

If the Problem Persists

If all external gas sources, hoses, and circuit issues have been ruled out and the alarm continues, an internal flow regulation or sensing failure is likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot gas supply issues on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before continuing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed FRESH GAS LOW !! alarm with low delivered tidal volume during ventilation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Kinked oxygen supply hose restricting fresh gas flow to the machine."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Repositioned and replaced hose, verified normal gas flow and cleared alarm. Performed leak test and returned unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Fresh gas flow is essential for safe anesthesia delivery. Always begin with supply and external causes before considering internal faults, and prioritize patient safety at every step.

That is successful troubleshooting.
