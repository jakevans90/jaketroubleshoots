Philips SureSigns VM Series

ECG Waveform Artifact / Lead-Off Issue

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

SureSigns VM Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting ECG artifact, noisy waveform, intermittent tracing, lead-off messages, cable problems, and electrode connection issues before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the Philips SureSigns VM monitor is not the only active source of cardiac monitoring before disconnecting ECG leads, removing electrodes, or swapping cables.

If continuous monitoring is required, have clinical staff move the patient to another verified monitor first.

Expected outcome: Patient monitoring is maintained safely while the ECG issue is evaluated.

Confirm the Exact ECG Complaint

Ask clinical staff what they observed. Determine whether the issue is:

  • ECG artifact
  • Wandering baseline
  • Lead-off message
  • Intermittent ECG dropout
  • Flatline or absent waveform
  • One lead missing
  • All ECG leads missing
  • Heart rate reading incorrectly
  • Artifact only during movement
  • Issue follows the cable, patient, or monitor

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to patient setup, electrodes, lead wires, ECG cable, settings, or monitor hardware.

Check the Patient Connection First When Safe

Have clinical staff verify that ECG electrodes are properly attached to clean, dry skin and are placed in the correct locations.

Loose electrodes, poor skin prep, sweat, lotion, hair, or dried electrode gel can cause artifact or lead-off messages.

Expected outcome: If the waveform stabilizes or the lead-off message clears, the issue was patient connection related. Stop troubleshooting.

Replace Questionable ECG Electrodes

Ask staff to replace the electrodes with fresh, non-expired electrodes from a known-good package.

Electrodes can dry out or lose adhesion, even if they appear usable.

Expected outcome: A stable ECG waveform returns and the lead-off message clears. Stop troubleshooting.

Check Lead Wire Attachment at Each Electrode

Confirm each snap or clip connection is secure and not loose, corroded, bent, or contaminated.

Gently move the wires near the electrode connections while observing the waveform.

Expected outcome: No artifact should appear when the lead wires are lightly moved. If movement causes artifact, replace the lead set.

Inspect the ECG Lead Set

Check the patient lead wires for cuts, stretching, exposed conductors, damaged snaps, bent clips, missing labels, or strain relief damage.

Pay close attention to areas near the patient end and connector end.

Expected outcome: The ECG lead set is physically intact. If damaged, replace it and retest.

Swap with a Known-Good ECG Lead Set

Install a known-good compatible ECG lead set and repeat ECG monitoring on a simulator or appropriate test setup.

Expected outcome: If the artifact or lead-off issue clears, the original lead set was defective. Replace the accessory and stop troubleshooting.

Inspect the ECG Trunk Cable or Main ECG Cable, If Used

If the SureSigns VM configuration uses a separate ECG trunk cable, inspect it for bent pins, cracked housing, loose strain relief, liquid contamination, or intermittent connection.

Expected outcome: Cable and connector are clean, secure, and undamaged. If damage is found, replace the cable and retest.

Verify the ECG Cable Is Fully Seated at the Monitor

Disconnect and reconnect the ECG cable at the monitor, ensuring it is fully inserted and secure.

Do not force the connector. Check for obvious bent pins, debris, or cracked connector housing.

Expected outcome: The monitor recognizes the ECG connection and displays a stable waveform.

Check for Incorrect Lead Selection or Display Settings

Confirm the displayed ECG lead is appropriate and that the monitor is not set to a lead that is unavailable, disconnected, or producing poor signal quality.

Change to another available lead to see whether the issue is isolated to one lead.

Expected outcome: A usable ECG waveform is displayed when a valid lead is selected.

Check ECG Gain, Filter, and Monitoring Mode Settings

Review ECG size/gain, filter mode, and monitoring configuration. Incorrect settings can make artifact appear worse or make the waveform difficult to interpret.

Expected outcome: ECG waveform appears appropriately scaled and stable after settings are corrected.

Rule Out Patient Movement or External Interference

Ask whether artifact occurs during patient movement, shivering, transport, bed movement, procedure activity, or contact with other equipment.

Also check for nearby sources of electrical interference when practical.

Expected outcome: If artifact occurs only during movement or interference, the monitor may be functioning normally and the cause should be documented.

Test with an ECG Simulator

Remove the monitor from patient use and connect a compatible ECG simulator using known-good ECG cables.

Verify whether the SureSigns VM displays a clean waveform and stable heart rate.

Expected outcome: A clean simulator waveform indicates the monitor ECG input is likely functional and the issue is probably patient setup, electrodes, or accessories.

Compare Against Another Monitor if Needed

If the complaint involves inaccurate heart rate or persistent artifact, compare the same simulator signal or known-good lead set on another verified monitor.

Expected outcome: If the problem follows the cable or setup, replace the accessory. If it stays with the SureSigns VM, escalate the monitor for repair.

Check for Physical Damage or Contamination at the ECG Input

Inspect the monitor ECG connector area for cracked plastic, loose connection, bent pins, fluid residue, corrosion, or signs of impact damage.

Expected outcome: Connector should be clean, stable, and undamaged. If damage or contamination is present, remove the device from service.

Power Cycle Only When Safe and Appropriate

If the ECG issue appears related to a monitor freeze, configuration hang, or intermittent recognition, power cycle the monitor after confirming it is not actively needed for patient monitoring.

Expected outcome: ECG function returns normally after restart. If the issue returns, continue escalation.

Confirm Final Function

After replacing electrodes, cables, or correcting setup, verify ECG waveform quality, heart rate display, lead selection, and absence of lead-off messages.

Expected outcome: The SureSigns VM displays a stable ECG waveform and reliable heart rate.

If the Problem Persists

If ECG artifact or lead-off messages continue after checking patient connection, electrodes, lead wires, ECG cable seating, settings, simulator testing, and visible connector condition, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

At that point, the issue may involve the ECG input connector, internal acquisition circuitry, isolation components, or monitor hardware. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot ECG lead-off or artifact problems on an active patient if it could interrupt required monitoring. Move the patient to another verified monitor first, then evaluate the Philips SureSigns VM with known-good accessories or a simulator.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported ECG artifact and intermittent lead-off messages on a Philips SureSigns VM Series patient monitor."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found worn ECG lead wires with intermittent connection near the patient snap leads; monitor displayed a stable waveform with a known-good lead set."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced the ECG lead set, verified clean ECG waveform and stable heart rate using simulator testing, and returned the monitor to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the issue occurred on one patient or multiple patients
  • Which ECG lead showed artifact or lead-off
  • Whether electrodes were replaced
  • Whether the lead set was swapped
  • Whether a trunk cable was used and inspected
  • ECG cable connector condition
  • Simulator test result
  • Heart rate stability
  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Any visible damage, fluid residue, or corrosion
  • Whether artifact occurred during movement
  • Final device status

Final Thought

ECG troubleshooting should begin with patient safety and simple external causes before suspecting monitor failure. Electrodes, skin prep, lead wires, cable seating, and settings cause many ECG complaints. If those checks do not resolve the issue, remove the monitor from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
