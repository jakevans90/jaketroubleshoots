Spacelabs Healthcare Xprezzon

No display / monitor will not power on

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Spacelabs Healthcare

Model

Xprezzon

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a Spacelabs Xprezzon patient monitor with no display, no power, or failed startup from external causes.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

If the monitor is connected to a patient and the display is blank or the monitor will not power on, do not continue relying on it for patient monitoring.

Move the patient to a known-good monitor or bedside monitoring setup according to clinical procedure.

Expected outcome: Patient monitoring is restored using a reliable device before troubleshooting begins.

Why it matters: A patient monitor with no display cannot be trusted to provide alarms, waveforms, numerics, or parameter status.

Confirm the Reported Power Failure

Press the power button and observe the monitor closely.

Check for:

  • Any screen backlight
  • Spacelabs startup screen
  • Power indicator light
  • Battery or charging indicator
  • Audible tones
  • Fan noise, relay click, or internal startup sound

Expected outcome: You determine whether the monitor is completely dead or powering internally with no usable display.

If the monitor starts normally, verify operation and return it to service if no fault repeats.

Check the Wall Outlet

Confirm the AC outlet is live using an outlet tester or another known-good device.

Avoid assuming the monitor is faulty until the power source is verified.

Expected outcome: The outlet provides proper power.

If the outlet is dead or controlled by a wall switch, move the monitor to a verified outlet and retest. If the monitor powers on, stop troubleshooting and document the outlet issue.

Inspect the AC Power Cord

Check that the power cord is fully seated at both the wall outlet and the monitor or external power supply.

Inspect the cord for:

  • Cuts
  • Loose plug blades
  • Crushed insulation
  • Bent connector
  • Evidence of liquid exposure
  • Missing hospital-grade plug integrity

Expected outcome: The power cord is secure and physically safe to use.

If the cord is damaged, remove it from use, replace it with an approved known-good cord, and retest.

Swap with a Known-Good Power Cord

Use a verified compatible hospital-grade power cord.

Make sure the cord fits securely and does not wiggle at the monitor connection.

Expected outcome: The monitor powers on with the known-good cord.

If the monitor powers on, the original cord was the likely cause. Replace the cord, verify startup, and stop.

Check for an External Power Supply or Power Module Issue

If the Xprezzon configuration uses an external AC adapter or power module, inspect it for:

  • Loose input or output cable
  • No power indicator light
  • Cracked housing
  • Burnt smell
  • Unusual heat
  • Damaged strain relief

Expected outcome: The power supply appears intact and is providing power.

If available, test with a known-good compatible power supply approved for that monitor configuration.

If the monitor powers on with the known-good supply, remove the failed supply from service and replace it.

Check Battery Status

If the monitor has a removable or internal battery-supported configuration, connect it to verified AC power and allow it to sit briefly before attempting startup again.

Observe for a charging indicator or battery status message.

Expected outcome: The monitor recognizes AC power and begins charging or starts normally.

If the monitor only fails on battery but powers on with AC, document reduced battery operation and send for battery evaluation or replacement according to facility procedure.

Remove Nonessential Accessories

Disconnect external accessories that are not required for basic startup, such as:

  • External display cables
  • USB devices
  • Recorder modules
  • Network cable
  • Parameter cables
  • External keyboard or barcode accessories, if present

Then reconnect only AC power and attempt startup.

Expected outcome: The monitor powers on without accessory interference.

If the monitor powers on after an accessory is removed, reconnect accessories one at a time to identify the cause. Remove the suspect accessory from service.

Inspect for Physical Damage or Fluid Intrusion

Look over the monitor housing, screen, connectors, handle, and rear panel.

Check for:

  • Cracked case
  • Damaged power inlet
  • Sticky residue
  • Signs of cleaning fluid intrusion
  • Corrosion at connectors
  • Burnt odor
  • Unusual heat

Expected outcome: No visible safety concern is present.

If fluid intrusion, smoke odor, overheating, or power inlet damage is found, do not continue power testing. Remove the monitor from service.

Check Display Behavior Separately from Power Behavior

If the monitor appears to power internally but the screen remains blank, look for signs that the device is running:

  • Alarm sounds
  • Button response
  • Indicator lights
  • Network activity
  • External display output, if configured
  • Parameter module activity

Expected outcome: You determine whether the issue is no power or possible display/backlight failure.

If the monitor is running but the display remains blank, remove it from service for bench evaluation. Do not return a patient monitor with an unreliable display.

Perform a Safe Power Reset

Disconnect AC power.

If the battery is externally removable and facility policy allows removal, remove it.

Wait briefly, reconnect AC power, reinstall the battery if removed, and attempt startup again.

Expected outcome: The monitor restarts normally after a basic power reset.

If the monitor powers on and completes startup normally, verify stable operation, charging status, and display function before returning it to service.

Verify Full Startup Before Returning to Service

If the monitor powers on, confirm:

  • Display is bright and stable
  • Startup completes without error
  • AC power indicator works
  • Battery indicator behaves normally
  • Basic controls respond
  • Alarm audio is present
  • No burning smell, overheating, or intermittent shutdown occurs

Expected outcome: The monitor operates normally and safely.

If the issue cannot be reproduced after correcting an external cause, document the checks performed and return to service only if the monitor passes functional verification.

If the Problem Persists

If the Spacelabs Xprezzon still has no display or will not power on after checking the outlet, power cord, power supply, battery behavior, accessories, and visible damage, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as a power supply, display, battery circuit, internal connection, or main electronics fault.

The monitor should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A patient monitor with unreliable power or display operation should not be returned to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a blank or non-powering patient monitor while it is the active source of monitoring for a patient. Move the patient to another verified monitor first, then troubleshoot the failed unit only when patient monitoring has been safely restored.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Spacelabs Xprezzon patient monitor had a blank screen and would not power on at the bedside."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified wall outlet was functional, but the monitor remained off with the original power cord and powered on normally with a known-good hospital-grade cord."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced the defective power cord, confirmed normal startup, stable display, AC power indication, and returned the monitor to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result
  • Power cord condition
  • Known-good cord used
  • AC adapter or power supply indicator status
  • Battery or charging indicator behavior
  • Whether the screen was completely blank or dimly lit
  • Any startup tones, fan noise, or indicator lights
  • Accessories removed during testing
  • Signs of fluid intrusion, heat, smell, or physical damage
  • Whether issue occurred on AC power, battery power, or both
  • Final device status

Final Thought

For a Spacelabs Xprezzon patient monitor with no display or no power, start with patient safety and external power checks before suspecting internal failure. A verified outlet, known-good cord, accessory isolation, and careful observation often separate simple setup issues from true device faults. Clear CCR documentation helps show what was checked, what was corrected, and why the final status was appropriate.

That is successful troubleshooting.
