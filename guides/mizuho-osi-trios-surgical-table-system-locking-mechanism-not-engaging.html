Mizuho OSI Trios Surgical Table System

Locking Mechanism Not Engaging

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

Mizuho OSI

Model

Trios Surgical Table System

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of the table’s locking mechanism failing to engage, including power, control, actuator, or safety interlock issues before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the table is not supporting a patient. Do not attempt locking mechanism troubleshooting while a patient is on the table.

Visual Inspection of Locking Mechanism

Examine the lock lever, pins, or mechanical parts for obstruction, debris, or visible damage. Clear any debris and ensure proper alignment.

Expected outcome: unobstructed, smooth movement.

Check Table Power

Verify the table is powered on and battery or AC supply is functional. Locking actuators may require electrical power.

Expected outcome: table powers normally.

Inspect Control Panel / Handset Commands

Attempt engaging the lock from the hand control or pendant. Observe indicator lights or audible clicks confirming activation.

Expected outcome: lock engages or indicator responds.

Confirm Safety Interlocks

Ensure all safety interlocks are properly seated (e.g., brakes, caster locks, accessories). Many tables will not engage the lock if interlocks detect unsafe conditions.

Expected outcome: lock engages when all interlocks satisfied.

Test Actuator Function

If safe, listen for actuator movement when lock command is given. No movement may indicate actuator or wiring issue.

Expected outcome: audible actuator operation.

Swap or Inspect Cables

Check connections between control panel and lock actuator for loose or damaged cables. Swap with known-good cables if available.

Expected outcome: lock functions normally with proper connection.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have been completed and the locking mechanism still fails to engage. The table should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop avoids patient risk and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot the lock mechanism under patient load.
  • Move the patient to another table first.
  • Verify all brakes and casters are secured before engaging or testing locks.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported the table locking mechanism would not engage."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Safety interlocks were functional, actuator did not respond to lock command."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Table removed from service and sent for repair; Out of Service label applied."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • Indicator lights during lock attempt
  • Audible actuator response
  • Mechanical obstruction checked
  • Cable/connection integrity verified
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount when troubleshooting mechanical or electrical lock failures. Follow logical external checks first, document actions clearly, and escalate appropriately to repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
