Siemens Healthineers Multix Impact

Detector Not Detected, Not Ready, Or Wireless Detector Connection Fault

Asset Type

Radiographic System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

Multix Impact

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting detector recognition, readiness, charging, seating, and wireless communication issues before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the Multix Impact is not being used for an active patient exam.

If the detector becomes unavailable during a patient exam, stop the exam workflow and have clinical staff move the patient to another available imaging room or detector if imaging is urgent.

Expected outcome: No patient imaging depends on a detector that is not ready or not reliably connected.

Confirm the Exact Detector Complaint

Ask the radiographer what message or behavior was seen.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Detector not detected
  • Detector not ready
  • Wireless detector disconnected
  • Detector not charging
  • Detector battery low
  • Detector not assigned to the room
  • Detector works in the wall stand but not table Bucky
  • Detector works wired but not wirelessly
  • Intermittent loss during positioning or exposure setup

Expected outcome: The fault is narrowed to detector power, battery, charging, wireless connection, docking, assignment, or system communication.

Check Whether an Exam Is Still Open or Hung

Verify whether the workstation is stuck in an incomplete exam, exposure setup, or detector selection screen.

Have the user safely close or cancel the incomplete workflow if appropriate.

Expected outcome: The system returns to a normal ready state and allows detector selection.

If this resolves the issue, document the workflow fault and stop.

Verify the Correct Detector Is Selected

At the acquisition workstation, confirm that the selected detector matches the detector being used for the exam.

Check whether the system is expecting the table detector, wall stand detector, wireless detector, or another configured detector.

Expected outcome: The correct detector is selected and the system recognizes its expected location.

If this resolves the issue, stop.

Inspect Detector Power and Battery Status

Check the detector battery level, battery indicator, or detector status display if available.

Confirm the detector is powered on and not in a depleted or standby condition.

Expected outcome: The detector has adequate battery power and is awake.

If the detector was simply off, asleep, or depleted, charge or power it appropriately and recheck operation.

Check Detector Charging Contacts and Charger Seating

If the detector is not charging, inspect the charging bay, dock, or charging contacts externally.

Look for dirt, debris, damaged contacts, poor seating, or obstruction that prevents proper contact.

Expected outcome: The detector seats fully and shows normal charging behavior.

If cleaning external debris or reseating restores charging, stop and document the finding.

Reseat the Detector in the Bucky or Holder

Remove and reseat the detector in the table Bucky, wall stand, or detector tray.

Confirm the detector is fully inserted, aligned, and latched if applicable.

Expected outcome: The system recognizes the detector position and shows it ready.

If reseating resolves the issue, document detector seating as the cause and stop.

Inspect the Detector and Tray for External Damage

Visually inspect the detector housing, corners, handle area, connector area, tray guides, and Bucky slot.

Look for cracks, impact damage, bent guides, loose parts, missing labels, or signs the detector was dropped.

Expected outcome: No visible damage or obstruction is present.

If damage is found, remove the detector from service and escalate for repair evaluation.

Check for Wireless Communication Issues

Confirm the detector is within normal room range and not blocked by unusual shielding, storage carts, metal barriers, or being used outside its intended room.

Verify the issue is not caused by the detector being too far from the system or blocked from communicating.

Expected outcome: The detector reconnects when positioned normally within the imaging room.

If this resolves the issue, document the wireless range or location issue and stop.

Confirm Room Assignment or Detector Pairing Status

Check whether the detector appears assigned to the correct Multix Impact room or system.

A detector associated with another room, another workstation, or a different exam setup may not be available.

Expected outcome: The detector is assigned to the correct system and appears available for selection.

If detector assignment appears wrong or unavailable, escalate to imaging support, PACS/application support, or Siemens service depending on site workflow.

Power Cycle Only When Safe

If no patient exam is active and clinical staff approves downtime, perform a controlled restart of the acquisition workstation or detector according to site policy.

Do not interrupt active image acquisition or unsent patient images.

Expected outcome: Temporary communication or software lockup clears and the detector returns to ready.

If the issue clears after restart, document the restart and monitor for recurrence.

Test With Another Detector if Available

Use a known-good compatible detector, if available and approved for that room.

Determine whether the issue follows the detector or stays with the room/system.

Expected outcome:

  • If the known-good detector works, the original detector is suspect.
  • If no detector works, the room, workstation, Bucky interface, or wireless communication path is suspect.

Test the Suspect Detector in Another Approved Location if Allowed

If site configuration allows, check whether the suspect detector is recognized by another compatible system or charger.

Do not cross-use detectors in a way that violates department configuration, licensing, infection control, or clinical workflow.

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to the detector itself or the original imaging room.

Check for Related System Messages

Review the workstation status, detector readiness message, generator inhibit message, or system event notification visible to the user.

Do not clear or ignore repeated detector readiness faults without documenting them.

Expected outcome: Any specific fault message is captured for repair escalation.

Confirm No Obvious Environmental or Network Change Occurred

Ask whether wireless changes, access point work, room renovation, detector replacement, workstation updates, or network downtime occurred recently.

Expected outcome: Recent changes are identified as possible causes of detector communication failure.

If the issue started after a known change, escalate with that detail included.

If the Problem Persists

If the detector is still not detected, not ready, not charging, or losing wireless connection after external checks, common user-accessible causes have been ruled out.

The Multix Impact detector or room should be:

  • Removed from clinical use if detector readiness is unreliable
  • Labeled Out of Service if no backup detector or safe workflow is available
  • Sent for repair, bench evaluation, or Siemens service support
  • Evaluated for detector battery, charging hardware, wireless communication, Bucky interface, or workstation configuration issues

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated exposure attempts or force clinical use with an unreliable detector.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot detector readiness while a patient is positioned for imaging. Move the patient to another available room or detector first, then troubleshoot when the system is safe and clinical workflow is protected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the Siemens Multix Impact wireless detector was not detected and remained unavailable for patient exams."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Clinical Engineering found the detector battery was depleted and the detector was not fully seated in the charging dock."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated the detector in the charger, verified charging indication, confirmed detector recognition at the workstation, and returned the room to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Detector battery level checked
  • Detector charging indication verified
  • Detector reseated in Bucky, wall stand, tray, or charger
  • Correct detector selected at workstation
  • Wireless connection status observed
  • Error message or detector status captured
  • Known-good detector tested
  • Suspect detector tested in another approved location
  • Any recent network, workstation, or room configuration changes
  • Visible detector damage, cracks, bent contacts, or signs of impact
  • Final device status: returned to service, detector removed, or room labeled Out of Service

Final Thought

Detector communication issues should be approached logically: protect the patient first, confirm the correct detector and workflow, check power and charging, inspect seating and damage, then evaluate wireless or configuration causes. If the detector remains unreliable after external checks, escalation is appropriate. Clear CCR documentation helps repair teams identify whether the problem follows the detector, the room, or the communication path.

That is successful troubleshooting.
