Philips Brilliance Series

Tube Warm-Up or Exposure Errors

Asset Type

CT Scanner

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

Brilliance Series

What This Guide Helps With

Tube warm-up, exposure inhibit, or scan start errors caused by readiness, power, cooling, or system interlock issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm no patient is actively being scanned or positioned for an exam before troubleshooting.

Action:

  • Stop the scan workflow.
  • Move the patient off the table if the issue delays imaging.
  • Notify CT staff that the scanner is not ready for patient use.

Expected Outcome: Patient care is safely continued using another CT scanner or alternate imaging plan if needed.

If this resolves the immediate clinical risk, continue troubleshooting only when the scanner is safe to evaluate.

Verify the Reported Error and Scan Behavior

Confirm the exact message or behavior reported by CT staff.

Action:

  • Ask whether the issue occurred during tube warm-up, scout scan, helical scan, axial scan, or exposure attempt.
  • Note the displayed message, exposure inhibit, tube not ready status, or aborted scan.
  • Check whether the gantry, console, or operator interface shows additional warnings.

Expected Outcome: The issue is confirmed and tied to tube readiness or exposure generation, not a general workflow issue.

Confirm the Correct Warm-Up Procedure Was Attempted

CT tubes often require warm-up after idle time, startup, or room restart.

Action:

  • Ask CT staff whether the normal tube warm-up sequence was started.
  • Confirm the scanner was not taken directly into patient scanning after extended idle time.
  • Restart the warm-up only if the system allows it and no patient is on the table.

Expected Outcome: Warm-up completes successfully and the scanner returns to ready status.

If the warm-up completes and exposure is restored, document and return the unit to service.

Check for Tube Overheat or Cooling Delay Messages

Exposure may be inhibited if the tube is too hot or cooling calculations have not cleared.

Action:

  • Review console messages for tube heat, cooling delay, exposure inhibit, or tube unavailable status.
  • Ask whether multiple high-dose or repeated scans were performed before the error.
  • Allow the system to cool if the console indicates a cooling delay.

Expected Outcome: Scanner clears the inhibit after the tube cooling period.

If cooling clears the issue, verify normal scan readiness before returning to service.

Verify Gantry and Room Power Status

Unstable power or incomplete system startup can prevent exposure readiness.

Action:

  • Confirm the CT scanner, console, gantry, and associated equipment are powered normally.
  • Check for tripped room emergency power-off conditions or facility power events.
  • Verify no breaker, wall disconnect, or room power control was recently operated by Facilities.

Expected Outcome: Scanner power is stable and the system is fully initialized.

If a facility power issue is found, coordinate with Facilities before returning the scanner to service.

Check Emergency Stop and Interlock Conditions

Exposure may be blocked if an emergency stop, door interlock, or safety circuit is active.

Action:

  • Verify all emergency stop buttons are released.
  • Confirm room doors are fully closed if door interlocks are installed.
  • Check for warning indicators at the console, gantry, or control area.
  • Ask whether staff recently pressed an emergency stop or opened the room door during scanning.

Expected Outcome: No active safety interlock is preventing exposure.

If resetting an external safety condition clears the issue, perform a normal readiness check before clinical use.

Inspect Console and Operator Workflow

Some exposure errors are caused by incomplete protocol selection, scan prescription, or system state.

Action:

  • Confirm the exam is properly selected and the scanner is not waiting for a required user action.
  • Verify the system is not paused, held, or in service mode.
  • Have CT staff exit the scan setup and reselect the exam if safe to do so.

Expected Outcome: Scanner accepts the scan workflow and allows exposure when ready.

If the issue was workflow-related, document the finding and return the scanner to service.

Restart the CT Application if Allowed by Site Procedure

A software state issue can sometimes prevent tube warm-up or exposure readiness.

Action:

  • Confirm no patient is loaded for scanning.
  • Have authorized CT staff close and reopen the scan application if permitted.
  • If needed, perform a controlled console restart according to site procedure.

Expected Outcome: System reloads normally and tube readiness can be rechecked.

If the error clears after restart, monitor for recurrence and document the restart.

Perform a Controlled System Reboot if Appropriate

If the system remains inhibited and no active patient care is affected, a full controlled reboot may clear communication or readiness faults.

Action:

  • Notify CT leadership before rebooting.
  • Follow site-approved shutdown and startup procedure.
  • Allow the system to fully initialize.
  • Attempt tube warm-up again if prompted by the system.

Expected Outcome: Scanner initializes normally and completes warm-up without exposure errors.

If the error returns after reboot, suspect a deeper system or tube/generator-related issue.

Check Environmental and Cooling Conditions

Tube and generator errors can occur if the room environment or cooling support is abnormal.

Action:

  • Check for excessive room temperature.
  • Listen for abnormal cooling sounds from the gantry or equipment room.
  • Look for water leaks, coolant alarms, unusual heat, or burning smell.
  • Ask Facilities if HVAC or chilled water service recently changed.

Expected Outcome: No environmental or cooling issue is found.

If heat, odor, leakage, or cooling failure is present, remove the scanner from service and escalate immediately.

Review Error Logs if Available to Clinical Engineering

Clinical Engineering may be able to confirm whether the error is recurring or escalating.

Action:

  • Review accessible system event logs or error history.
  • Note repeated tube warm-up failures, exposure inhibits, generator errors, rotor errors, or cooling-related messages.
  • Avoid clearing logs before recording useful details.

Expected Outcome: Error trend is documented for service escalation.

If repeated tube, generator, rotor, or cooling faults are present, stop troubleshooting and escalate.

Do Not Attempt Repeated Exposures

Repeated exposure attempts can worsen tube stress or create unnecessary radiation-related risk.

Action:

  • Do not repeatedly force scan attempts.
  • Do not bypass exposure inhibits, interlocks, or safety warnings.
  • Stop after reasonable external checks are complete.

Expected Outcome: The scanner is protected from further damage and patient/staff safety is maintained.

If the Problem Persists

If tube warm-up or exposure errors continue after checking workflow, power, interlocks, cooling delay, environment, and controlled restart, common external causes have been ruled out.

At that point, the issue may involve the x-ray tube, generator, rotor/stator function, heat calculation, cooling system, high-voltage components, or internal communication faults.

The scanner should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Escalated for Philips service, imaging specialist, or qualified bench/system evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not bypass safety interlocks or continue repeated exposure attempts.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot exposure errors with a patient waiting on the table unless the scanner is safe and the delay is minimal. Move the patient first, then troubleshoot. CT exposure faults affect both patient care timing and radiation-producing hardware, so safety comes before scan recovery.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"CT staff reported the Philips Brilliance scanner would not complete tube warm-up and displayed an exposure inhibit before scanning."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified the error at the console and ruled out active emergency stop, door interlock, workflow setup, room power, and cooling delay conditions."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed a controlled restart and repeated tube warm-up; error returned, so the scanner was removed from service and escalated for Philips service evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact warm-up or exposure error message
  • Whether the issue occurred during warm-up, scout, axial, or helical scan
  • Whether the tube had been idle for an extended period
  • Recent high-volume scanning or repeated exposures
  • Tube cooling delay or heat status
  • Emergency stop status
  • Door interlock status
  • Console restart or full system reboot performed
  • Room temperature or HVAC concerns
  • Cooling sounds, leaks, unusual heat, or odor
  • Any recent power event
  • Final scanner status

Final Thought

Tube warm-up and exposure errors should be handled carefully because they involve radiation-producing hardware and patient care availability. Start with simple external checks, confirm no safety interlock or workflow issue is blocking exposure, and avoid repeated scan attempts once common causes are ruled out. Strong documentation helps imaging service identify whether the issue is tube, generator, cooling, or control related.

That is successful troubleshooting.
