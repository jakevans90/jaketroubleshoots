Smiths Medical Medfusion 4000

Alarm: Depleted Battery

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Medfusion 4000

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Depleted Battery alarm conditions on the Smiths Medical Medfusion 4000 infusion pump. This alarm indicates the battery is depleted or unable to support operation, which may prevent the pump from safely operating on battery power during transport, unplugging, or AC power interruption.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the Medfusion 4000 is actively infusing and a Depleted Battery alarm is present, notify clinical staff immediately.
  • Do not allow the pump to continue operating on battery power if the battery cannot support operation.
  • Connect the pump to AC power or have clinical staff transfer the infusion to another verified pump if reliable power cannot be restored.
  • Expected outcome: Patient therapy is not dependent on a pump with unreliable battery support.
  • Why it matters: A depleted or failing battery can interrupt infusion therapy if AC power is lost or disconnected.

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact alarm shown on the pump display is Depleted Battery.
  • Check whether the issue is a depleted battery alarm, low battery condition progressing to depleted battery, battery not charging, shutdown when removed from AC power, or battery indicator not increasing while connected to AC power.
  • Observe whether the alarm occurs at startup, during infusion, when unplugged from AC, after charging, or intermittently.
  • Expected outcome: The Depleted Battery alarm or related battery failure behavior is clearly confirmed.
  • Why it matters: This separates a true battery support issue from a power cord, outlet, or charging connection problem.

3. Remove the Pump From Battery-Dependent Use

  • Do not return the pump to normal clinical use until battery operation is verified.
  • Keep the pump connected to AC power during evaluation.
  • If the pump cannot remain powered reliably, label it Out of Service.
  • Expected outcome: The pump remains powered safely during troubleshooting.
  • Why it matters: A pump with a depleted or failed battery may appear functional on AC power but fail when unplugged.

4. Verify the AC Power Source

  • Plug the pump into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Confirm the outlet is live and not controlled by a wall switch, power strip, or failed emergency power circuit.
  • If needed, test the outlet with an outlet tester or known-good device.
  • Expected outcome: The pump receives reliable AC power.
  • Why it matters: A battery alarm can worsen if the pump was never actually charging due to a failed outlet or poor AC connection.

5. Check the AC Power Cord and Connection

  • Inspect the power cord for damage, looseness, bent prongs, cuts, strain, or missing ground pin.
  • Confirm the cord is fully seated into the pump and wall outlet.
  • If available, test with a known-good compatible power cord.
  • Expected outcome: AC power is stable and the pump recognizes the external power source.
  • If the alarm clears and the pump begins charging normally after correcting the cord or outlet issue, allow the battery to charge and verify operation before returning the pump to service.

6. Verify AC and Battery Indicators

  • With the pump connected to AC power, check the AC power indicator and battery or charging indicator.
  • Confirm whether the pump shows AC power connected, battery charging, battery not charging, battery depleted, battery fault, or abnormal battery status.
  • Expected outcome: The pump correctly recognizes AC power and begins charging the battery.
  • Why it matters: If AC is present but the battery does not charge, the issue is more likely battery pack, charging circuit, or internal power path related.

7. Allow Charging Time and Recheck

  • If the pump had been stored unplugged or used on battery for an extended period, leave it connected to AC power long enough for charging to begin.
  • Recheck the display and battery indicator after charging time has passed.
  • Expected outcome: Battery status improves and the depleted battery alarm clears.
  • If the battery remains depleted after charging, continue troubleshooting.

8. Check Battery Status in Biomed Diagnostics

  • Access the Biomed diagnostics menu and review Biomed > Diagnostics > Monitor Battery Status.
  • Check battery capacity, charge state, charge cycles, and any abnormal battery information available.
  • Expected outcome: Battery data is readable and shows whether the battery can still support operation.
  • Why it matters: The Medfusion 4000 battery full-capacity value decreases with use, and battery replacement is required when capacity falls below the specified threshold.

9. Check for Charge-Cycle Limit

  • Review the battery charge-cycle count in the battery status screen if available.
  • The battery should be replaced when the charge-cycle count reaches the manufacturer’s stated limit of 400 cycles.
  • Expected outcome: Battery cycle count is below replacement limit.
  • If the charge-cycle count has reached 400, replace the battery pack according to facility procedure.

10. Evaluate Battery Capacity

  • Review the reported full-capacity value.
  • If the full-capacity value has dropped below the manufacturer’s specified threshold, the battery is no longer suitable for normal use.
  • Expected outcome: Battery capacity remains within the acceptable range.
  • If capacity is low, replace the battery pack.
  • Why it matters: A battery may still accept some charge but no longer have enough usable capacity to safely support infusion pump operation.

11. Replace the Battery Pack if Indicated

  • If battery capacity is low, charge-cycle limit is reached, or the battery will not recover after charging, replace the battery pack.
  • After replacement, connect the pump to AC power and verify that the new battery is recognized.
  • Expected outcome: Battery status is normal, charging behavior is correct, and the depleted battery alarm clears.
  • If the alarm clears after battery replacement, complete the required operational checks and return the pump to service.

12. Verify Battery Operation Before Return to Service

  • After charging or replacing the battery, confirm the pump operates properly on AC power.
  • Briefly verify that the pump remains powered when disconnected from AC, according to facility policy.
  • Confirm that no battery alarm returns.
  • Expected outcome: The pump transitions between AC and battery operation without immediate alarm or shutdown.
  • Why it matters: The repair is not complete until the pump can safely support normal power transition behavior.

If the Problem Persists

If the Depleted Battery alarm remains after confirming the outlet, power cord, AC recognition, charging behavior, battery status, and battery replacement, external and common battery causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Depleted Battery alarm may indicate an internal charging, battery communication, power management, battery connector, or main board issue.

Remove the pump from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot battery reliability on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first if reliable power cannot be maintained.

A pump with an unresolved Depleted Battery alarm should not be trusted for transport, temporary unplugging, or any use where battery operation may be required.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Medfusion 4000 infusion pump reported with Depleted Battery alarm. Staff stated pump would not reliably support operation on battery power and alarm appeared when disconnected from AC."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery status checked in Biomed diagnostics. Battery capacity was below acceptable level and/or charge-cycle count reached replacement limit. AC outlet and power cord were verified good."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced battery pack, connected pump to AC power, verified charging indication and battery status, confirmed alarm cleared, and performed functional power checks. Pump returned to service after passing verification."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery alarms should always be treated as patient safety concerns because infusion therapy may be interrupted if power support fails. Start with the outlet, cord, and charging indicators before replacing parts. Use the battery status screen to confirm capacity and cycle count, and escalate when the alarm remains after normal battery replacement or charging checks. Clear CCR documentation helps show whether the issue was caused by external power, normal battery wear, or a likely internal charging failure.

That is successful troubleshooting.
