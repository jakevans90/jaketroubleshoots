Siemens Healthineers MOBILETT Elara Max

Exposure Inhibit, Generator Fault, or Failed X-Ray Exposure

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

MOBILETT Elara Max

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting exposure inhibit, failed X-ray exposure, generator fault, readiness, and external setup issues before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the MOBILETT Elara Max is not actively positioned over a patient before troubleshooting exposure faults.

If the exposure failed during a patient exam, have clinical staff stop the exam, protect the patient, and move to another available X-ray system if imaging is urgent.

Expected outcome: No patient remains dependent on a mobile X-ray system that cannot safely complete an exposure.

Confirm the Exact Exposure Complaint

Ask the technologist what happened and when.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Exposure inhibit before attempting exposure
  • Rotor or prep failure
  • X-ray exposure button not responding
  • Exposure started but aborted
  • Generator fault message
  • Tube warmup or tube readiness issue
  • Detector not ready preventing exposure
  • Battery or charging state preventing exposure
  • Intermittent failure on certain techniques only

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to readiness, exposure control, detector status, power state, or generator-related behavior.

Check Whether the System Is Safe and Ready for Use

Verify the unit is powered on normally and not in a startup, shutdown, charging-only, locked, service, transport, or error state.

Confirm the system shows ready status before attempting any exposure-related testing.

Expected outcome: The unit is in a normal operating state and not inhibited by a basic status condition.

Review the Displayed Error or Inhibit Message

Read the exact message shown on the workstation or system display.

Record whether the message references generator, exposure inhibit, detector, battery, emergency stop, tube, collimator, communication, or system readiness.

Expected outcome: The fault is identified by the system message instead of guessing at the cause.

Check Emergency Stop and Safety Interlocks

Confirm any emergency stop, drive lock, brake, or safety control is not engaged.

If an emergency stop or safety lock was activated, reset it according to normal operator workflow and restart the system if required by site procedure.

Expected outcome: Exposure is not blocked by an active safety interlock.

Verify Battery and AC Power Status

Check battery charge level, charging indicator, and AC connection if the unit is plugged in.

A low battery or unstable power condition may prevent exposure even if the workstation appears powered.

Expected outcome: The system has sufficient power available for X-ray generation.

Confirm the Detector Is Ready

Verify the correct detector is selected, connected, charged, and ready.

If the detector is wireless, confirm it is awake, associated with the system, and not showing a communication or battery fault.

Expected outcome: Exposure is not inhibited because the detector is offline or unavailable.

Check Exam and Technique Setup

Confirm the exam is properly selected and patient/exam information is entered as required by the department workflow.

Verify the selected technique is appropriate and not outside normal limits.

Expected outcome: The system has a valid exam and exposure setup.

Check Collimator and Positioning Readiness

Confirm the tube head, collimator, and detector are positioned normally for the intended exposure.

Verify the collimator light, field alignment, and SID setup appear reasonable.

Expected outcome: Exposure is not blocked by setup, positioning, or readiness conditions.

Inspect the Exposure Switch or Hand Switch

Check that the exposure switch is connected, seated, and not physically damaged.

Inspect the cord for cuts, strain, crushed sections, or loose connection points.

If available and approved by site practice, test with a known-good compatible exposure switch.

Expected outcome: The exposure command is being sent reliably to the system.

Check for a Stuck or Damaged Exposure Button

Inspect the prep and expose positions of the exposure switch.

Make sure the button is not sticking, binding, cracked, or failing to return normally.

Expected outcome: The exposure control is not mechanically preventing prep or exposure.

Power Cycle the System Only When Safe

If no patient is active and no image acquisition is in progress, restart the MOBILETT Elara Max using normal shutdown and startup workflow.

Do not repeatedly cycle power if the same generator fault returns.

Expected outcome: A temporary software or readiness state clears, or the fault repeats and confirms a persistent issue.

Attempt a Controlled Functional Check

If permitted by site policy and radiation safety procedure, have qualified imaging staff perform a controlled test exposure using proper shielding, area control, and approved test setup.

Clinical Engineering should not create unnecessary radiation exposure or bypass normal safety workflow.

Expected outcome: The system either completes exposure normally or the fault is reproduced under controlled conditions.

Look for Repeated Fault Patterns

Determine whether the issue occurs:

  • On every exposure
  • Only after startup
  • Only on battery power
  • Only on AC power
  • Only with one detector
  • Only at higher technique settings
  • Only when using the hand switch
  • Only after system movement

Expected outcome: The problem is narrowed to power delivery, detector readiness, exposure control, generator load, or intermittent connection behavior.

Check for External Damage or Environmental Concerns

Inspect the mobile unit for signs of impact, loose panels, damaged cables, liquid intrusion, unusual odor, excessive heat, or abnormal sounds.

If burning smell, arcing sound, smoke, or unusual heat is present, stop troubleshooting immediately.

Expected outcome: Obvious unsafe conditions are identified before further operation.

Stop if Generator or Exposure Fault Returns

If the MOBILETT Elara Max continues to show generator fault, exposure inhibit, failed prep, failed exposure, or exposure abort after external checks, remove the unit from service.

Do not attempt internal generator, high-voltage, tube, inverter, or board-level troubleshooting in the clinical area.

Expected outcome: The device is escalated before unsafe or unreliable imaging use continues.

If the Problem Persists

If the exposure inhibit, generator fault, or failed X-ray exposure continues after checking patient safety, power status, detector readiness, exposure switch, setup, and visible external conditions, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal to the generator, high-voltage system, tube circuit, exposure control path, detector communication path, or system control electronics.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Repeated exposure attempts on a unit with a generator or exposure fault can create patient delays, repeat imaging, radiation safety concerns, and unreliable clinical workflow.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot exposure failure while the unit is positioned for an active patient exam. Move the patient to another available imaging system first if the image is clinically needed. Exposure-related faults should be treated as patient safety and radiation safety concerns until the unit is verified safe for use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Technologist reported the Siemens MOBILETT Elara Max displayed an exposure inhibit message and would not complete a portable chest X-ray exposure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Clinical Engineering found the detector ready and battery charged, but the exposure fault returned after checking setup, hand switch connection, and system restart."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the MOBILETT Elara Max from service, labeled it Out of Service, documented repeated exposure failure, and escalated the unit for generator/exposure system evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact exposure inhibit or generator fault message
  • Whether exposure failed during prep or during expose
  • Battery charge level at time of failure
  • AC power connected or battery-only operation
  • Detector selected, connected, and ready status
  • Exposure switch condition and connection
  • Whether a known-good exposure switch was tested
  • Exam type and technique selected
  • Whether fault occurs with one detector or all detectors
  • Any abnormal sound, odor, heat, or visible damage
  • Whether system restart changed the symptom
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Exposure faults on a mobile X-ray system should be approached carefully and logically. Start with patient safety, then verify power, detector readiness, exam setup, exposure controls, and visible external conditions. If the fault repeats after those checks, escalation is appropriate. Clear CCR documentation helps protect patients, supports radiation safety, and gives repair personnel the details needed to evaluate the system efficiently.

That is successful troubleshooting.
