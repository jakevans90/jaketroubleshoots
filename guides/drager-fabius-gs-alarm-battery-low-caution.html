Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: BATTERY LOW !!

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

BATTERY LOW !! alarm indicating loss of mains power with battery under 10%, creating risk of imminent shutdown and loss of ventilation capability.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the unit is in use, immediately connect to a verified power source or transition the patient to alternate ventilation.
  • Confirm continuous patient ventilation.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm BATTERY LOW !! is displayed.
  • Check if the unit is unplugged.
  • Verify battery level is below 10%.
  • Look for additional power-related alarms.

Check Mains Power Connection

  • Ensure power cord is securely connected to the device and outlet.
  • Inspect for loose or damaged connections.
  • Test outlet with another device if needed.

Verify Power Source and Facility Supply

  • Try a known-good outlet.
  • Check for switched outlets or breaker issues.
  • Avoid power strips or faulty extension cords.

Inspect Power Indicators on the Device

  • Confirm AC power indicator is illuminated.
  • Verify battery charging indicator is active.

Allow Battery to Charge

  • Keep device connected to mains power.
  • Allow time for battery to charge above critical level.

Evaluate Battery Condition

  • Check for rapid battery drain.
  • Verify battery is accepting and holding charge.
  • Note if alarm persists despite AC power.

If the Problem Persists

If external causes such as power connection and outlet issues are ruled out and the alarm remains active, remove the device from service. Label the unit Out of Service and send for repair or battery replacement.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before addressing power-related alarms.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying BATTERY LOW !! alarm with loss of mains power and battery under 10%."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Unit unplugged from AC power with battery fully discharged."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reconnected to AC power, verified outlet, confirmed charging, and returned unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power alarms should always be addressed immediately. Start with external power checks, but recognize that persistent battery issues typically indicate component failure requiring escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
