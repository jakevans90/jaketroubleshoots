GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: 4-HOUR WARM-UP REQUIRED - PRESS ANY KEY

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: 4-HOUR WARM-UP REQUIRED - PRESS ANY KEY on a GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm. This condition appears when the system has been inactive for an extended period and requires a four-hour warm-up before generator functions can be used. During the warm-up period, X-ray and generator functions are disabled, so the unit should not be relied on for active clinical imaging.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot this condition during an active procedure.
  • Remove the C-Arm from clinical use if X-ray imaging is needed immediately.
  • Provide another available C-Arm or approved imaging method if the room is preparing for a case.
  • Notify clinical staff that generator functions are disabled until the required warm-up is complete.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the OEC 9900 and observe the startup display.
  • Confirm the displayed message reads: Error: 4-HOUR WARM-UP REQUIRED - PRESS ANY KEY.
  • Confirm whether X-ray or generator functions are disabled.
  • Ask staff when the unit was last used, stored, powered on, or moved from backup inventory.

Confirm Extended Inactivity

  • Check department history, equipment history, or work order records to determine whether the C-Arm has been inactive for more than 60 days.
  • Look for clues such as long-term storage, backup inventory status, low use history, recent relocation, or extended downtime.
  • If the unit has been unused for an extended period, the warm-up message is consistent with the expected system behavior.

Do Not Attempt to Bypass the Warm-Up

  • Do not repeatedly power cycle the unit to try to clear the message early.
  • Do not continue pressing exposure controls or attempting X-ray operation while generator functions are disabled.
  • Advise staff that the four-hour delay must complete before the unit can return to normal use.

Allow the Four-Hour Warm-Up Period to Complete

  • Keep the unit connected to a proper power source.
  • Allow the full four-hour warm-up period to complete.
  • Avoid moving, unplugging, or shutting down the unit unless required for safety.
  • Record the start time and expected completion time in the work order if the device is being held out of service.

Press Any C-Arm Key After Warm-Up

  • After the four-hour warm-up finishes, press any key on the C-Arm control panel.
  • Observe whether the error clears and the system proceeds to normal operation.
  • If the message clears, continue with functional verification before returning the unit to service.

Verify Basic Operation After the Error Clears

  • Perform normal operational checks according to facility procedure.
  • Verify the C-Arm powers up normally.
  • Confirm the control panel responds correctly.
  • Confirm no warm-up error returns.
  • Verify generator functions are available.
  • Confirm fluoroscopy or X-ray operation is normal during approved testing.
  • Check that no additional startup, generator, or image-chain errors appear.

Check for Repeat or Abnormal Behavior

  • If the error returns after the warm-up has completed, treat it as a device issue.
  • Confirm whether the same message returns after the unit is restarted.
  • Check whether system date, usage history, or startup behavior appears abnormal.
  • Look for other errors related to generator operation, image chain, control panel function, or system initialization.

If the Problem Persists

If the four-hour warm-up completes but the error does not clear, or if generator functions remain disabled, external and normal-use causes have been ruled out.

Remove the C-Arm from service. Label it Out of Service. Document that the required warm-up was completed. Send the unit for repair or bench evaluation.

Persistent warm-up lockout may indicate an internal control, software, image intensifier-related, or system status detection issue requiring service-level evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first if imaging is required.

Backup or low-use C-Arms should be powered on and checked before they are needed clinically. If this error appears before a case, do not wait at bedside with the patient ready. Swap to another imaging system and let the affected unit complete its required warm-up away from active patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"User reported GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm displayed Error: 4-HOUR WARM-UP REQUIRED - PRESS ANY KEY during startup. Generator functions were disabled and the unit could not be used for X-ray imaging."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"System indicated extended inactivity condition requiring four-hour image intensifier warm-up before generator operation could resume. No external power, cable, accessory, or user setup issue was identified."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from clinical use during warm-up. Allowed required four-hour warm-up period to complete. Pressed C-Arm control panel key after warm-up and verified error cleared. Performed functional check and confirmed normal startup and generator availability before returning unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

The 4-hour warm-up message on the GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm is a system-reported condition that should not be treated like a simple nuisance error. Clinical Engineering should confirm the message, protect patient care, allow the required warm-up to complete, verify normal operation afterward, and escalate only if the error does not clear or generator functions remain disabled.

That is successful troubleshooting.
