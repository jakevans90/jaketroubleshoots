Drager Infinity Kappa Series

Battery Not Charging or Power / UPS Failure

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Infinity Kappa Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Infinity Kappa monitor battery is not charging, the unit fails to power on, or UPS/power-related alarms occur. Symptoms may include low battery warnings, failure to charge, unexpected shutdowns, or inability to operate on battery power. Focus is on external checks and safe escalation rather than internal electronics repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify AC Power Source

  • Ensure the monitor is plugged into a live outlet or approved UPS.
  • Check outlet with a known working device to confirm power availability.
  • Why: No power input will prevent battery charging or operation.

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Examine the power cable for damage, fraying, or bent connectors.
  • Confirm the connector is fully seated in the monitor’s power port.
  • Why: Loose or damaged cables are common causes of charging failures.

Check UPS or Surge Protector

  • If using a UPS, ensure it is powered on and functioning.
  • Bypass the UPS and connect directly to a known live outlet.
  • Why: Faulty UPS can prevent proper charging or trigger power alarms.

Verify Battery Status and Seating

  • Remove and reseat the battery according to manufacturer guidelines.
  • Inspect battery contacts for corrosion, dirt, or damage.
  • Why: Improperly seated or faulty batteries will not charge.

Power Cycle the Monitor

  • Disconnect AC power, remove battery (if removable), wait 30 seconds, reconnect, and attempt power-on.
  • Why: Resetting electronics can clear minor faults and restore charging recognition.

Check for Error Messages or LED Indicators

  • Note any battery, UPS, or system fault messages on the screen.
  • Compare against the monitor manual for specific warnings.
  • Why: Provides clues whether the issue is battery, UPS, or internal electronics.

If the Problem Persists

All external power, battery, and UPS causes have been ruled out.

The issue may be internal (battery controller, power board, or charging circuit).

Action:

  • Remove monitor from service.
  • Label as “Out of Service – Battery/Power Fault.”
  • Send to manufacturer-authorized service or internal bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and protects both patient safety and equipment integrity.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt troubleshooting on a patient.

Move patient monitoring to another functional unit before working on the affected monitor.

Always confirm backup monitoring is active before disconnecting power.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
User reports the Infinity Kappa monitor battery will not charge and the unit shuts down when unplugged.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
AC power and UPS verified; power cable intact; battery properly seated; monitor still fails to charge.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled “Out of Service – Battery/Power Fault,” and sent to Biomedical Service for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested with a known device.
  • Power cord and connector condition.
  • UPS function verified.
  • Battery removed, inspected, and reseated.
  • Error messages or LED indicators noted.
  • Final device status: Out of Service.

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Follow a logical, stepwise approach to isolate external causes before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation ensures accountability and speeds repair while reducing risk to patients. Escalation to service when needed preserves equipment integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
