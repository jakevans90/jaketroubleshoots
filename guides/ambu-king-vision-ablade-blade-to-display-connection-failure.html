Ambu King Vision aBlade

Blade-to-display connection failure

Asset Type

Video Laryngoscope

Manufacturer

Ambu

Model

King Vision aBlade

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting poor blade connection, no image, split image, frozen image, or display shutdown caused by blade, adapter, or contact issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

If the King Vision aBlade is being prepared for an airway case and the image is not reliable, do not continue with that device.

Provide clinical staff with another ready video laryngoscope or alternate airway device before troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not delayed by an unreliable display or blade connection.

Confirm the Reported Failure

Ask staff what was observed:

  • No image
  • Static or snow
  • Split image
  • Frozen image
  • Image drops out when the blade is moved
  • Display powers off after blade movement
  • Blade not recognized

Expected outcome: The failure mode is confirmed before parts are swapped.

Ambu quick-reference guidance notes that blade connection timing can affect image behavior, including split or frozen image conditions.

Check That the Blade Is Connected Before Power-On

Power the display off.

Remove the blade and adapter, then reconnect them fully before powering the display back on.

Expected outcome: The display powers up with a live moving image.

If the image returns, document improper connection sequence or incomplete blade seating and stop.

Inspect the Disposable aBlade

Check the blade for:

  • Cracked plastic
  • Bent or damaged connector area
  • Contamination on the connector
  • Blood, cleaning residue, or moisture near the connection point
  • Packaging damage
  • Wrong blade size or wrong blade type for the adapter

Expected outcome: The blade is clean, intact, and compatible.

If damage or contamination is found, discard the disposable blade and retest with a new blade. Ambu describes the King Vision aBlade system as using single-use blades.

Inspect the aBlade Adapter

Remove the aBlade adapter and inspect it externally.

Look for:

  • Cracked housing
  • Loose fit
  • Bent connector contacts
  • Debris in the connection channel
  • Signs of liquid intrusion
  • Excessive wear from repeated blade insertion

Expected outcome: The adapter seats securely and has no visible damage.

If the adapter is damaged, remove it from use and replace or send for evaluation.

Reseat the Display, Adapter, and Blade

Reconnect the components in this order:

  • Display off
  • Adapter fully seated to display
  • aBlade fully seated to adapter
  • Power display on

Gently move the handle and blade while watching the screen.

Expected outcome: The image remains stable during light movement.

If the image cuts out during movement, suspect a loose adapter, worn connection, or damaged display connection point.

Test With a Known-Good Blade

Use a new or known-good compatible aBlade.

Power the display off before connecting the replacement blade.

Expected outcome: A stable live image appears.

If the issue resolves, the original blade was defective, contaminated, or improperly seated. Discard the failed disposable blade and stop.

Test With a Known-Good Adapter

If available, test the display with a known-good compatible aBlade adapter.

Expected outcome: The display recognizes the blade and maintains a stable image.

If the issue resolves, replace the original adapter and document adapter failure.

Inspect the Display Connection Area

With the device removed from patient use, inspect the display stem connection area.

Look for:

  • Bent pins or contacts
  • Cracked plastic
  • Worn connector retention
  • Moisture or residue
  • Evidence of cleaning fluid exposure
  • Loose fit between display and adapter

Expected outcome: The display connection area is clean, dry, and mechanically secure.

Do not probe aggressively or attempt internal repair at the bedside.

Check for Cleaning or Liquid Exposure

Ask whether the display was recently cleaned, dropped into fluid, or stored while wet.

The King Vision display is not intended to be submerged, and connector openings should be protected from liquid exposure during cleaning.

Expected outcome: No liquid contamination is present at the electrical connection.

If liquid exposure is suspected, remove the device from service.

Verify Battery Condition

Confirm the display has adequate battery power.

Low battery can complicate troubleshooting by causing shutdowns or unstable display behavior.

Expected outcome: Battery condition is sufficient for testing.

If battery status is low or questionable, replace batteries or use a known-good display according to facility practice, then retest.

Perform a Final Functional Check

With a known-good blade and adapter installed before power-on, confirm:

  • Display powers on
  • Live image appears
  • Image does not freeze
  • Image does not split
  • Image remains stable with light handling
  • No visible damage is present
  • Device is clean and ready for clinical use

Expected outcome: The King Vision aBlade system passes a basic functional check.

If the issue is resolved, return the device to service per facility policy.

If the Problem Persists

If the blade, adapter, connection sequence, battery condition, and visible connector condition have been checked and the issue remains, the likely cause is internal display connection failure, adapter interface failure, or damage that requires bench evaluation.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, replacement evaluation, or vendor-supported assessment

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A video laryngoscope with intermittent image loss should not be returned to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot blade connection failures during an active airway attempt. Move clinical staff to another ready device first, then evaluate the King Vision only when patient care is protected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Ambu King Vision aBlade display showed a frozen image after the blade was connected."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the disposable aBlade did not maintain a secure connection to the aBlade adapter, while a known-good blade worked normally."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the failed disposable blade from use, verified stable live image with a replacement blade, and returned the display and adapter to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Blade size and type used
  • Whether the blade was connected before power-on
  • Whether the image was blank, split, frozen, or intermittent
  • Whether a known-good blade was tested
  • Whether a known-good adapter was tested
  • Battery condition
  • Connector condition
  • Evidence of fluid exposure
  • Any visible cracks or loose fit
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Blade-to-display connection failures should be handled logically and safely. Start with setup sequence, disposable blade condition, adapter seating, and battery condition before suspecting the display. If the image is intermittent or unreliable after external checks, remove the device from service and document clearly using CCR.

That is successful troubleshooting.
