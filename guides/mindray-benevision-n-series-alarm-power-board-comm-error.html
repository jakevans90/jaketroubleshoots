Mindray BeneVision N Series

Alarm: Power Board Comm Error

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Mindray

Model

BeneVision N Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the Power Board Comm Error alarm on a Mindray BeneVision N Series patient monitor. This alarm means communication with the power board has failed. The condition may affect AC power detection, battery charging, battery status reporting, power stability, or continued monitor operation.

Because this is a power-related communication alarm, the monitor should not be trusted for active patient monitoring until patient safety is confirmed and the device is verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the patient is being monitored safely before troubleshooting.
  • If the monitor is actively in use and the alarm is active, recurring, or associated with power instability, move the patient to another verified monitor.
  • Do not rely on a monitor that may lose power or fail to report power status correctly.
  • Confirm required monitoring parameters are available on a backup device if continued monitoring is needed.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact displayed alarm is Power Board Comm Error.
  • Check whether the alarm appears during startup, after AC power is connected, during battery operation, or intermittently during use.
  • Ask clinical staff whether the monitor restarted, froze, shut down unexpectedly, or failed to recognize AC power.
  • Check for related symptoms such as battery not charging, incorrect battery status, abnormal power indicator behavior, or AC power not recognized.

Check the AC Power Source

  • Connect the monitor to a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Verify the outlet has power.
  • Try another verified outlet if the power source is questionable.
  • Inspect the AC power cord for damage, loose fit, bent prongs, or signs of overheating.

Check the Power Cord and Monitor Power Connection

  • Disconnect and reconnect the AC power cord.
  • Confirm the cord is fully seated at the monitor or power adapter connection.
  • Check for looseness without forcing the connector.
  • Inspect the power input area for damage, contamination, liquid residue, corrosion, or overheating marks.

Check Battery Status and Basic Power Behavior

  • Observe whether the monitor powers on normally.
  • Check whether the battery icon appears normal.
  • Confirm whether the battery charges when AC power is connected.
  • If safe and appropriate, determine whether the alarm appears on AC power only, battery power only, or both.

Restart the Monitor

  • Power the monitor down using the normal shutdown process.
  • Disconnect AC power.
  • Allow the monitor to fully power down.
  • Reconnect AC power.
  • Power the monitor back on and check whether the Power Board Comm Error returns.

Inspect for Physical Damage or Environmental Causes

  • Inspect the monitor housing for cracks, impact damage, or loose panels.
  • Check near the power input area for liquid residue, corrosion, contamination, heat marks, or odor.
  • Ask whether the monitor was dropped, cleaned heavily, exposed to fluid, or recently involved in a power issue.

Isolate Docking or External Power Setup Issues

  • If the monitor is used with a dock, rack, transport module, or external power setup, remove the monitor from that setup if appropriate.
  • Test the monitor with direct AC power.
  • Check whether the alarm follows the monitor or only appears in a specific dock or rack setup.
  • Inspect external docking contacts for bent pins, debris, poor seating, or visible damage.

Perform Basic Functional Verification

  • Confirm the monitor powers on normally.
  • Confirm AC power is detected.
  • Confirm the battery charges if installed.
  • Confirm there are no active power-related alarms.
  • Allow the monitor to run long enough to confirm the alarm does not immediately return.

Determine Whether the Fault Is Internal

  • If the alarm returns after external power checks and restart, treat it as a likely internal communication failure.
  • Confirm the outlet, power cord, battery status, docking setup, and external power connections have been ruled out.
  • Record whether the alarm occurs immediately at startup or intermittently.
  • Remove the monitor from service and send it for bench evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the Power Board Comm Error persists after restart, AC power checks, power cord inspection, battery checks, and docking or external setup checks, the common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent alarm may indicate an internal fault involving the power board, main control board, internal communication path, or related internal power circuitry.

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the monitor for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Do not keep the monitor in clinical use with an unresolved internal power communication alarm.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this alarm on an active patient if the monitor is needed for continuous monitoring. Move the patient to a backup monitor first. A power communication fault can affect monitor reliability even if the screen is currently on.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Mindray BeneVision N Series patient monitor displays Power Board Comm Error during startup and may not reliably report power or battery status."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"External AC outlet, power cord, battery status, and docking setup were checked. Alarm persisted after controlled restart, indicating likely internal communication failure between the main control system and power board."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed monitor from patient use, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench repair evaluation. Power board or main control board replacement may be required depending on service findings."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Power Board Comm Error should be handled as a reliability issue, not just a nuisance alarm. Start with patient safety, verify the exact alarm, rule out external power and connection problems, then escalate when the alarm persists. Proper documentation shows that troubleshooting was logical and that the device was removed from service before becoming a patient safety risk.

That is successful troubleshooting.
