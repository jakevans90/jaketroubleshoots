Medtronic McGRATH MAC

Blurred, Obscured, or Poor-Quality Camera Image

Asset Type

Video Laryngoscope

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

McGRATH MAC

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a blurred, fuzzy, obstructed, or degraded video image caused by blade condition, camera contamination, damage, or device failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot poor image quality during active airway management. If the image becomes unreliable during patient use, clinical staff should follow their airway backup plan and use an appropriate alternative device when required.

Remove the video laryngoscope from clinical use for evaluation before attempting further troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: No patient depends on a video image that cannot be clearly interpreted.

Why it matters: A degraded airway image can delay visualization and may affect safe intubation.

2. Confirm the Device Is Eligible for Use

Identify the unit model and serial number before proceeding. Certain earlier McGRATH MAC 300-000-000 and McGRATH MAC EMS 300-200-000 devices were included in an FDA-listed removal involving serial numbers 366170 through 405673. The next-generation 301-000-000 devices were not removed, but received updated battery-handling instructions. Follow facility recall-management procedures for affected inventory.

Expected outcome: The unit is confirmed as acceptable for evaluation, or it is removed from service according to recall or corrective-action requirements.

If the device is affected by a removal notice, stop troubleshooting and process it according to institutional procedure.

3. Verify the Reported Image Problem

With the device removed from patient use, power it on and observe the display image. Document whether the complaint is:

  • Blurred or out of focus
  • Cloudy, hazy, or obstructed
  • Dark or poorly illuminated
  • Intermittently degraded
  • Present with one blade or every blade tested

Expected outcome: The reported poor-quality image is reproduced or identified as an accessory-specific condition.

If the image is clear and stable during evaluation, document that the issue could not be duplicated and return the device only according to department policy.

4. Inspect the Device for External Damage

Inspect the monitor, screen hinge, handle, CameraStick area, LED/camera tip, and blade attachment point for cracks, looseness, bending, residue, moisture, or other visible damage.

Do not use the device if any component is loose, physically damaged, or fits poorly. The manufacturer’s troubleshooting information directs that damaged or poorly fitting equipment be returned for repair or replacement.

Expected outcome: No external damage is found that could compromise image quality or patient safety.

If damage is found, stop troubleshooting and remove the unit from service.

5. Check the Disposable Blade

Verify that an appropriate McGRATH disposable laryngoscope blade is installed, that it is unused for patient care, and that the blade is correctly seated over the CameraStick. Medtronic identifies McGRATH disposable blades as single-use accessories intended for use with the device.

Inspect the blade for:

  • Cloudiness, scratches, distortion, or contamination in the optical path
  • Damaged packaging before use
  • Poor fit, looseness, or incomplete engagement
  • Rough edges or protrusions

Expected outcome: The blade is clear, securely fitted, undamaged, and does not obstruct the camera image.

If the blade appears defective or questionable, discard it and test with a new approved blade. If the image clears, the blade was the likely cause and troubleshooting may stop.

6. Remove the Blade and Isolate the Camera Image

Remove the blade and observe the camera image without the blade installed.

The McGRATH MAC troubleshooting instructions specifically direct removal of the blade to determine whether the image becomes clear.

Expected outcome:

  • Image becomes clear without the blade: suspect a defective, contaminated, or obstructed blade. Replace the blade and retest.
  • Image remains blurred without the blade: continue to inspect and clean the camera tip.

If a new blade restores a clear image, document the blade-related cause and stop.

7. Inspect and Clean the Camera Tip

Examine the camera at the end of the CameraStick for visible residue, haze, moisture, lint, or dried contamination.

When needed, wipe the camera tip using a lint-free wipe in accordance with the device’s approved cleaning and disinfection procedures and hospital infection-control policy. The manufacturer identifies this as a troubleshooting action for a blurred or fuzzy image.

Allow the device to be appropriately dry before functional testing.

Expected outcome: The image becomes clear after visible contamination is removed.

If the image is restored, complete required cleaning/disinfection handling, document the finding, and stop.

8. Test With a New Approved Blade

Install a new, compatible McGRATH disposable blade after the camera tip has been inspected and cleaned. Confirm that it fully engages and then observe the display image.

Expected outcome: The camera view is clear, adequately illuminated, and stable with the new blade installed.

If image quality is acceptable, the device may be returned to service only after required cleaning/disinfection and normal department functional verification are completed.

9. Evaluate Remaining External Image Concerns

If the image is still poor, inspect for other externally verifiable conditions:

  • Screen surface contamination or protective film affecting viewing
  • Persistent haze or damage at the camera window
  • Abnormally dim or uneven LED illumination
  • Image flicker, discoloration, or instability while the device remains stationary
  • Battery indication or startup behavior that suggests abnormal operation

Do not open the housing or attempt internal camera, display, or electronics repair.

Expected outcome: Any remaining external cause is identified, or the complaint is confirmed as an unresolved device-level failure.

If the image remains blurred, dark, unstable, or otherwise unsuitable for airway visualization, stop troubleshooting.

If the Problem Persists

If a new approved blade and appropriate external camera-tip cleaning do not restore a clear image, common external causes have been ruled out and the issue is likely internal to the camera, illumination, display, or related electronics.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, replacement, or bench evaluation through the appropriate Medtronic/service process

The manufacturer’s troubleshooting guidance states that if blade replacement and CameraStick camera cleaning do not correct poor picture quality, the unit should be returned for diagnostics and repair.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A video laryngoscope with unreliable image quality should not be returned for clinical airway use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a blurred or obstructed McGRATH MAC image while the device is actively needed for a patient airway. Ensure clinical staff have transitioned to a reliable alternative airway visualization method before evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Medtronic McGRATH MAC displayed a blurred and hazy airway image during pre-use inspection."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found that the image remained blurred with the original blade but became clear after removing the blade and installing a new McGRATH disposable blade."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the defective disposable blade, verified a clear and stable image with a new approved blade, completed required device handling checks, and returned the laryngoscope to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Patient use status when the complaint occurred
  • Device model and serial number verified
  • Recall or corrective-action status checked
  • Battery indicator or startup behavior
  • Original blade condition and fit
  • Image quality with blade removed
  • Camera tip condition before and after cleaning
  • New blade installed and tested
  • LED illumination appearance
  • Visible physical damage, looseness, haze, or contamination
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed for repair

Final Thought

Clear airway visualization is essential for safe device use. Begin with patient safety, rule out the disposable blade and external camera contamination, and escalate the unit when image quality cannot be reliably restored. Clear CCR documentation captures both the troubleshooting logic and the final safety decision.

That is successful troubleshooting.
