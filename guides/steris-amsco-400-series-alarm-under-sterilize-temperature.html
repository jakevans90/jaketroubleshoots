STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Under Sterilize Temperature - chamber temperature dropped below sterilize temperature

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the alarm condition where the chamber temperature drops below the required sterilization temperature during the cycle. This condition can result in incomplete sterilization and automatic cycle restart or extension, impacting patient safety and workflow.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not use any instruments from the affected cycle.
  • Remove the sterilizer from clinical use if the alarm persists.
  • Verify backup sterilization methods are available.

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm displays: “Under Sterilize Temperature.”
  • Observe when it occurs (during sterilize phase).
  • Check if the unit restarts sterilize time automatically.
  • Determine if the issue is intermittent or continuous.

3. Check Steam Supply (Most Common Cause)

  • Verify the steam supply valve is fully open.
  • Confirm steam pressure is adequate (≥50 PSIG if available).
  • Ensure consistent steam delivery during operation.

4. Inspect Steam Supply Piping

  • Check for kinks, restrictions, or leaks in piping.
  • Look for excessive condensation buildup.
  • Ensure piping is properly insulated and unobstructed.

5. Observe Chamber Behavior During Cycle

  • Run a test cycle with the unit out of service.
  • Monitor temperature rise and stability.
  • Look for slow recovery or fluctuating temperatures.

6. Check for Signs of Steam Trap Issues

  • Listen for abnormal steam discharge behavior.
  • Check for excessive condensate or poor drainage.
  • Identify continuous venting or lack of cycling.

7. Rule Out Facility or External Factors

  • Confirm no recent facility steam interruptions or changes.
  • Check if other sterilizers are affected.
  • Verify building steam system stability.

8. Restore Service if Resolved

  • If correcting steam supply resolves the issue, return unit to service.
  • Monitor for recurrence during subsequent cycles.

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and the alarm continues, internal component issues such as steam trap failure, solenoid valve malfunction, or control calibration problems are likely.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for qualified repair or calibration.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or rely on a sterilizer during an active clinical need. Always transfer sterilization needs to a backup unit before addressing the issue.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Sterilizer alarming ‘Under Sterilize Temperature’ during cycle with restart observed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Steam pressure found below required level or suspected internal component issue after external checks."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified steam supply and piping. Issue persisted. Removed unit from service and escalated for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Consistent sterilization temperature is critical for safe operation. Always prioritize verifying steam supply and external conditions first, and escalate promptly when internal faults are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
