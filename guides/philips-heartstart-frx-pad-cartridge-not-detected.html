Philips HeartStart FRx

Pad Cartridge Not Detected or Improperly Connected

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart FRx

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses pad cartridge detection issues, focusing on connection integrity, cartridge seating, and external inspection before internal service.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the defibrillator is not in active use. If a patient requires resuscitation, provide a backup AED immediately.

Inspect Pad Cartridge

Remove the pad cartridge. Check for visible damage, expiration date, and ensure the adhesive pads are intact. Damaged or expired cartridges may fail detection.

Verify Cartridge Orientation

Reinsert the pad cartridge according to the device’s orientation guides. Proper seating ensures electrical contact with the defibrillator.

Check Connector Contacts

Inspect the cartridge connector for dirt, debris, or corrosion. Clean contacts gently with a lint-free cloth if needed. Poor contact can prevent pad detection.

Listen for Device Confirmation

When the cartridge is correctly inserted, the HeartStart FRx typically emits a brief beep and updates the status indicator. Confirm the “Ready” light or icon is illuminated.

Try a Known Good Cartridge

If available, insert a different, verified working pad cartridge. If the AED detects this cartridge, the original pad cartridge is likely faulty.

Power Cycle the Device

Turn the AED off and back on after reinserting the cartridge. Some detection errors clear after a power cycle.

If the Problem Persists

External causes (cartridge damage, seating, contacts) have been ruled out. The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent to Philips service or qualified repair for evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unintended damage or patient risk.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt troubleshooting during active patient use. Always ensure a backup AED is immediately available and confirm pad cartridges are within their expiration date.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"AED displays “Pad Not Detected” upon power-on."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Pad cartridge connector contacts dirty and/or cartridge improperly seated."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned contacts, reseated cartridge, and verified device Ready indicator illuminated."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Status indicator behavior
  • Beeps or audible alerts
  • Pad cartridge expiration date
  • Any unusual odors or damage to pads
  • Final device status after troubleshooting

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting prioritizes patient safety, checks external factors first, and uses logic before internal repair. Documenting each step ensures accountability and prevents repeat issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
