STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Door Seal A Switch Malfunction – Seal switch closed out of cycle (switched neutral relay failure)

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the Alarm: Door Seal A Switch Malfunction – Seal switch closed out of cycle (switched neutral relay failure), which causes cycle abort due to the system falsely detecting seal pressure when the seal should be inactive. This condition prevents safe sterilizer operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the sterilizer from service if the alarm occurs
  • Do not use any instruments from an aborted cycle
  • Ensure no questionable loads are released

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows “Door Seal A Switch Malfunction”
  • Observe if the cycle aborts automatically
  • Check if alarm occurs outside of normal sealing phase
  • Verify system indicates seal switch closed when it should be open

Check for Obvious Door Seal or Mechanical Issues

  • Ensure the door is fully open or correctly positioned when idle
  • Inspect seal area for debris, damage, or deformation
  • Verify seal moves freely without obstruction

Inspect External Seal Switch Indications

  • Check if the unit indicates seal pressure while idle
  • Listen for unexpected seal actuation
  • Verify no pressure is retained when not in a cycle

Check Accessible Wiring and Connections

  • Inspect wiring associated with Door Seal A switch
  • Look for loose, pinched, or damaged cables
  • Check connector P19 for possible short circuit

Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the sterilizer OFF
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Power the unit back ON

Isolate Likely Failure Source

  • Consider failed seal pressure switch (LPS1/PS2) stuck closed
  • Evaluate for shorted wiring in seal circuit
  • Identify possible incorrect input to control system

If the Problem Persists

After ruling out external causes, a persistent alarm indicates internal component failure such as a faulty seal pressure switch or shorted wiring.

  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send for qualified repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or operate the sterilizer on an active load. Always move instruments to a backup sterilization process before addressing the issue.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying Door Seal A Switch Malfunction alarm. Cycle aborts and indicates seal switch closed out of cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Seal pressure switch stuck closed or shorted wiring at connector P19 causing false closed signal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified alarm condition. Inspected door seal and wiring. Removed unit from service and referred for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Seal switch alarms indicate critical feedback failures that directly impact pressure safety and sterilization integrity. Always rule out simple causes first, but escalate quickly when false signals persist. Proper troubleshooting and documentation ensure safe and reliable operation.
