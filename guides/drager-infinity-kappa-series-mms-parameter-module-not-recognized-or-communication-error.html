Drager Infinity Kappa Series

MMS / Parameter Module Not Recognized or Communication Error

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Infinity Kappa Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Infinity Kappa monitor does not recognize the MMS (Multi-Measurement Server) or parameter module, or displays communication errors. Symptoms may include missing vital signs parameters, “module not detected,” intermittent dropouts, or communication alarms. The focus is on verifying external connections, proper module seating, and safe escalation, rather than internal electronics repair. Patient safety and accurate monitoring are the priorities.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Patient Safety

  • Ensure the patient is on another functional monitor or appropriate monitoring device.
  • Do not troubleshoot on an actively monitored patient.

Check Module Connection

  • Confirm the MMS/parameter module is properly seated in the monitor slot.
  • Remove and reseat the module to ensure firm connection.
  • Inspect for bent pins, debris, or visible damage.

Inspect Cables and Connectors

  • Check all module cables and patient leads for wear, kinks, or loose connections.
  • Swap with a known working cable if available to rule out cable failure.

Power Cycle the Monitor

  • Safely power down the monitor.
  • Wait 10–15 seconds, then power it back on.
  • Observe if the monitor recognizes the module on reboot.

Verify Module Compatibility and Configuration

  • Confirm the module is supported by the Infinity Kappa monitor.
  • Check the monitor’s software version; some modules require specific firmware.

Check Alarm and Communication Settings

  • Review monitor menus for module recognition settings.
  • Ensure no “module off” or “parameter disabled” settings are active.

Swap Modules (if available)

  • Replace with a known working module to see if the problem follows the module or the monitor.
  • If the replacement works, the original module may be faulty.

If the Problem Persists

If external checks (cables, module seating, power cycle, compatibility) do not resolve the issue:

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send both the monitor and module for bench evaluation or repair.
  • Avoid further internal troubleshooting to maintain safety and warranty compliance.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Always confirm patient safety first; move the patient to a functioning monitor before troubleshooting.
  • Document any intermittent communication errors to assist repair and prevent missed alarms.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Monitor does not recognize MMS/parameter module; parameters missing.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Module was not seated properly and initial cable inspection showed no damage.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Reseated module and verified recognition; monitor functioning normally. If issue recurs, remove from service for repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes/No
  • Cables swapped: Yes/No
  • Module LED indicators: Normal/Abnormal
  • Alarm behavior: Any intermittent or continuous alarms
  • Unusual sounds: Clicking or beeping from module
  • Final device status: Functioning / Out of Service

Final Thought

Troubleshooting module communication errors requires a methodical approach—start with external checks, verify proper seating and connections, and escalate appropriately. Maintaining patient safety and documenting each step ensures continuity of care and proper device management.

That is successful troubleshooting.
