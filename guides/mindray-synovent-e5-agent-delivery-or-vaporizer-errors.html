Mindray SynoVent E5

Agent Delivery or Vaporizer Errors

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Mindray

Model

SynoVent E5

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting agent delivery or vaporizer errors on a Mindray SynoVent E5. These errors can prevent the safe delivery of inhaled anesthetic agents and are often caused by external factors such as vaporizer misalignment, empty or incompatible agent bottles, improper connections, or sensor issues rather than internal ventilator faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power and Setup

  • Confirm the ventilator is powered on and functioning normally.
  • Check that all gas supplies (O₂, air, N₂O if applicable) are connected and at correct pressures.
  • Ensure the vaporizer is seated correctly and securely in the backbar or mounting slot.

Check Vaporizer Level and Compatibility

  • Verify that the correct agent type is installed. Using the wrong agent can trigger errors.
  • Check the fill level; empty or near-empty vaporizers can cause delivery faults.
  • Ensure the vaporizer is unlocked and not in a transport or “OFF” position.

Inspect Connections

  • Confirm agent line connections between the vaporizer and ventilator are secure.
  • Look for kinks, leaks, or blockages in the agent delivery tubing.
  • Replace O-rings or seals if visibly damaged or dried.

Perform Self-Test / Calibration

  • Run the ventilator’s pre-use self-test with the vaporizer installed.
  • Observe for any vaporizer or agent delivery fault messages.
  • Document any specific error codes for reference.

Check Environmental Factors

  • Ensure room temperature and pressure are within the vaporizer operating range.
  • Excessive humidity or very low temperature may trigger sensor errors.

Replace Consumables If Needed

  • Swap in a known working vaporizer or agent bottle to rule out device-specific issues.
  • Replace any suspect tubing, O-rings, or connectors.

If the Problem Persists

If all external checks and swaps fail, the issue may be internal to the ventilator’s vaporizer interface, agent delivery sensors, or electronic control circuits. At this stage:

  • Remove the ventilator from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send the unit to the Biomedical Engineering bench or manufacturer service for evaluation.

Note: Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot agent delivery while a patient is connected.
  • Move the patient to an alternative ventilator or anesthesia device first.
  • Always verify gas delivery and alarm functionality before patient use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

“Vaporizer error displayed; agent delivery not functioning.”

Cause

Observed that the vaporizer was misaligned and nearly empty; connections checked.

Resolution

Realigned vaporizer, refilled agent, replaced seals, and verified proper operation during pre-use check.

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Vaporizer model and serial number
  • Specific error codes displayed
  • Fill level of agent
  • Pre-use self-test results
  • Connection integrity and seal condition

Final Thought

Patient safety must remain the top priority. Systematic troubleshooting, starting with external factors and moving to consumables and connections, minimizes unnecessary equipment downtime. Proper CCR documentation ensures clarity for follow-up and continuous quality improvement.

That is successful troubleshooting.
