Cardinal Health Kangaroo ePump

Error: 4 No ADC Interrupt for Motor Current

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo ePump

What This Guide Helps With

Error 4 indicating motor current feedback failure, resulting in pump not running, stopping unexpectedly, or inability to deliver enteral feeding.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use and stop any active feeding
  • Transition to a backup feeding method or pump

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error: 4 is displayed
  • Note when it occurs (startup, priming, or during feeding)
  • Observe if the pump attempts to run or remains inactive

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the pump off
  • Disconnect from AC power
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart

Check Power Source and Battery Condition

  • Plug into a known-good outlet
  • Verify AC power indicator is on
  • Check battery status and ensure it is not critically low

Inspect Pump Mechanism for Obstruction or Resistance

  • Open and inspect the pump door
  • Check for misloaded tubing or debris
  • Ensure tubing is correctly seated and not pinched

Attempt Operation with Known-Good Set

  • Load a new, correct feeding set
  • Attempt to prime or run the pump

Determine if Issue Persists

  • Confirm power cycle did not resolve the issue
  • Verify power source and setup are correct
  • Ensure no mechanical obstruction is present
  • Recognize this indicates internal motor control or sensing failure

If the Problem Persists

After external causes have been ruled out, remove the device from service and label it Out of Service.

Send the pump for repair or bench evaluation. Persistent Error 4 indicates an internal hardware failure involving motor control or current sensing.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup feeding pump before evaluating the device to ensure uninterrupted nutrition delivery.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 4 and not running during feeding."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Persistent Error 4 after power cycle and setup verification indicating internal motor current sensing failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error codes like this point directly to internal system failures. Efficient troubleshooting means ruling out external causes quickly, then escalating appropriately to protect patient safety and workflow.

That is successful troubleshooting.
