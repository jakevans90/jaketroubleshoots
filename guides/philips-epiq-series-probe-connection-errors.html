Philips EPIQ Series

Probe Connection Errors

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

EPIQ Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Philips EPIQ Series ultrasound system displays probe connection errors. Common manifestations include:

  • Probe not detected or “No Probe Connected” messages
  • Intermittent probe recognition
  • System does not allow imaging when the probe is connected
  • Error codes related to probe type or communication

These issues are often caused by external factors such as cable connection problems, probe port contamination, or probe incompatibility before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Power Down and Inspect Connections

  • Turn off the system.
  • Inspect the probe connector for debris, bent pins, or visible damage.
  • Re-seat the probe firmly.
  • Why: Loose or contaminated connections are a common cause of detection errors.

Try an Alternate Probe Port (if available)

  • Connect the probe to a different compatible port.
  • Observe if the error persists.
  • Why: Eliminates a potential port failure as the cause.

Swap Probes

  • Connect a known-working probe to the same port.
  • Connect the suspect probe to another working system.
  • Why: Confirms whether the probe or system is at fault.

Inspect Probe Cable

  • Check the probe cable for kinks, cuts, or excessive wear.
  • Ensure the cable is fully intact from connector to probe tip.
  • Why: Damaged cables can intermittently prevent recognition.

Check Software and Firmware

  • Ensure the system firmware is up to date.
  • Check for probe compatibility with current software version.
  • Why: Incompatible firmware can cause recognition issues.

System Reboot and Diagnostic Check

  • Power cycle the ultrasound system.
  • Run the built-in hardware or probe diagnostics.
  • Document any error codes generated.

If the Problem Persists

External connections, cables, ports, and software have been ruled out. The probe or system interface may have an internal failure.

  • Remove the system or probe from clinical service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to Philips-certified repair or internal biomedical bench evaluation.

Note: Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt to troubleshoot a probe while actively scanning a patient.
  • Move the patient to another functional system first.
  • Only connect or disconnect probes when the system is powered down to avoid electrical damage.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Ultrasound system shows probe connection error and will not detect the probe.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed debris in probe connector; suspect probe not recognized on multiple ports; cable integrity verified; software version compatible.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Cleaned probe connector, reseated probe, attempted alternate ports; problem persisted. Device labeled Out of Service and sent for repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes
  • Probe port swapped: Yes
  • Alternate probe tested: Yes
  • Error codes recorded: Include screenshots or printout
  • Cable integrity checked: Yes
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting protects patient safety and prevents damage to expensive probes. Always progress logically from external checks to internal evaluation and document all steps carefully. This approach reduces unnecessary downtime and supports accurate repair escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
