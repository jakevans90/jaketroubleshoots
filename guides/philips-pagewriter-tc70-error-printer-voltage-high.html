Philips PageWriter TC70

Error: Printer voltage high

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Printer voltage high errors on the Philips PageWriter TC70. This condition may affect printer power regulation, ECG printing reliability, or overall cardiograph dependability during clinical use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the cardiograph is being used for active patient testing and the error appears, stop use of the affected unit.
  • Move ECG acquisition to another verified cardiograph if patient testing is needed.
  • Do not rely on a cardiograph with an unresolved printer power fault for clinical ECG documentation.

Expected: Patient care is not delayed or dependent on a device with a printer power fault.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the PageWriter TC70 on and confirm the exact message: Printer voltage high.
  • Observe whether the error appears during startup, when attempting to print, intermittently during operation, or after the unit has been moved or powered from battery.
  • Check whether the cardiograph can still acquire ECGs, whether printing is disabled, or whether additional printer or power-related errors are displayed.

Expected: The error is confirmed and the associated behavior is clearly identified.

Remove the Device From Clinical Use if the Error Is Active

  • If the error is present or returns after restart, remove the cardiograph from service.
  • Label the unit Out of Service.
  • Do not continue clinical use while a printer voltage fault is unresolved.

Expected: The device is not used clinically while a printer voltage fault is active.

Check the External Power Source

  • Plug the cardiograph directly into a known-good hospital-grade AC outlet.
  • Avoid extension cords, power strips, loose receptacles, or shared outlets with questionable power quality.
  • Confirm the AC power indicator behaves normally.

Expected: The cardiograph powers normally from a verified outlet.

Check Battery-Only and AC-Powered Behavior

  • If safe to do so, compare whether the error appears on AC power, battery power, or both.
  • Do not continue repeated testing if the error is persistent.
  • Document whether the issue appears AC-only, battery-only, or in both conditions.

Expected: Power-source behavior is identified and documented.

Inspect the Printer Area Externally

  • Open the printer paper drawer and inspect for obvious external issues.
  • Check for jammed paper, incorrectly loaded paper, debris in the printer path, damaged printer door or latch, signs of liquid intrusion, burnt smell, heat, or discoloration.
  • Close the printer drawer fully and restart the cardiograph.

Expected: The printer compartment is clean, properly closed, and free of obvious external damage.

Check for Physical Damage or Liquid Exposure

  • Inspect the exterior of the cardiograph, printer housing, AC inlet, battery area, and surrounding casing.
  • Look for cracks, impact damage, residue, staining, loose panels, or signs the device was dropped.
  • Document any visible damage or contamination before repair escalation.

Expected: No visible damage or contamination is present, or any findings are clearly documented.

Restart the Cardiograph Once

  • Power the cardiograph off.
  • Disconnect AC power briefly.
  • Reconnect to a verified AC outlet and power the unit back on.
  • Confirm whether Printer voltage high returns.

Expected: The error clears and does not return, or the error remains reproducible.

Perform a Basic Printer Function Check if the Error Clears

  • If the message does not return after restart, perform a controlled print test.
  • Confirm paper feeds correctly and print quality is acceptable.
  • Verify that no printer voltage or printer controller message returns.
  • Check for abnormal noise, smell, heat, or other signs of printer malfunction.

Expected: Printer operation is normal and the error does not return.

Do Not Perform Deep Internal Troubleshooting in Clinical Areas

  • Do not open the cardiograph for internal board-level inspection while it is in a patient care area.
  • Do not attempt to bypass the printer, defeat safety protections, or continue using the device with an active printer voltage error.
  • Escalate persistent faults for bench evaluation or manufacturer service support.

Expected: Troubleshooting remains limited to safe external checks and proper escalation.

If the Problem Persists

If Printer voltage high returns after restart and external power, printer drawer condition, paper path, and visible damage have been checked, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent high printer supply voltage condition strongly indicates an internal electrical fault, likely involving the printer power supply path or main board.

The cardiograph should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation. Escalate to Philips service support as appropriate.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this error while the cardiograph is being used on an active patient. Move the patient or ECG acquisition workflow to a backup device first.

Avoid relying on a cardiograph with unresolved printer voltage errors, even if ECG acquisition appears to work, because printing and internal power regulation may be unreliable.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips PageWriter TC70 displayed Printer voltage high error. Staff reported printer function was unavailable or unreliable during ECG use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified Printer voltage high error. External AC outlet, printer drawer, paper path, and visible physical condition were checked. Error persisted after restart, indicating likely internal printer supply voltage or main board fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed cardiograph from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation/repair. Printer voltage condition could not be cleared through external troubleshooting."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A printer voltage high error should be treated as more than a paper or user-operation issue. Clinical Engineering should verify the message, rule out simple external causes, protect patient workflow with another device, and escalate when the error persists. Clear CCR documentation helps show that the issue was handled logically and safely.

That is successful troubleshooting.
