Hillrom CareAssist

Power Supply or Battery Backup Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

CareAssist

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of bed power loss or failure of battery backup, including outlet issues, power cords, fuses, and external connections before assuming internal electronics failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

  • Confirm the patient is safely transferred to another bed or fully supported. Do not attempt power troubleshooting while the patient depends on bed functions.

Verify AC Power Source

  • Check that the wall outlet is live using a verified device or outlet tester.
  • Expectation: Outlet is functional; if not, reset breaker or use a different outlet.

Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Check the bed’s power cord for visible damage, kinks, or loose plugs.
  • Ensure connectors to the bed are fully seated.
  • Expectation: Secure, undamaged cord; loose or damaged cords may cause intermittent or total power failure.

Check Fuses and Circuit Breakers on Bed

  • Locate bed’s internal fuses or circuit breakers per external access points (no disassembly required).
  • Expectation: Fuses intact; tripped breakers reset.

Test Battery Backup

  • If the bed has a removable or built-in battery, verify it is installed correctly and shows charge.
  • Expectation: Bed functions on battery power; if not, battery may be discharged or failed.

Power Cycle Bed

  • Disconnect AC power and battery (if applicable), wait 1–2 minutes, then reconnect.
  • Expectation: Bed powers on normally. This can reset minor control board errors without internal repair.

Observe Indicator Lights and Bed Response

  • Confirm bed control panels, side rails, and actuators respond when power is applied.
  • Expectation: Lights illuminate and actuators operate; absence indicates persistent electrical issue.

If the Problem Persists

All external causes have been ruled out. Likely internal failure (control board, power module, or wiring harness).

  • Remove the bed from service.
  • Label Out of Service.
  • Send for bench evaluation or manufacturer repair.

Knowing when to stop protects patient safety and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on battery backup during troubleshooting with an active patient. Always move the patient to a functioning bed first. Only test power functions when the bed is unoccupied.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed fails to power on and battery backup does not operate."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Power cord, AC outlet, and battery checked; all appear functional, but bed remains unresponsive."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Bed removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for manufacturer evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested with known-good device
  • Power cord visual inspection
  • Battery presence and charge status
  • Indicator lights observed
  • Bed response to power cycle
  • Final bed status noted

Final Thought

Always prioritize patient safety and confirm external power sources before assuming internal failure. Stepwise troubleshooting ensures accurate diagnosis and reduces unnecessary repair costs. Proper documentation supports clinical and engineering communication.

That is successful troubleshooting.
