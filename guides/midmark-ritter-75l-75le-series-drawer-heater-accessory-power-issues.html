Midmark Ritter 75L / 75LE Series

Drawer Heater or Accessory Power Issues

Asset Type

Exam Table

Manufacturer

Midmark

Model

Ritter 75L / 75LE Series

What This Guide Helps With

Drawer heater not warming, accessory outlets not working, or intermittent accessory power due to external power, settings, or connection issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the table from active patient use before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient safely relocated.
  • Why it matters: Electrical issues can create unsafe conditions or unexpected behavior.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to use the drawer heater and any powered accessories (outlets, lights, etc.).
  • Expected: Confirm no heat, no power, or intermittent operation.
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is isolated to one function or affects all accessory power.

Check Main Power to the Table

  • Ensure the table is plugged into a known working outlet. Test outlet with another device.
  • Expected: Reliable power present.
  • Why it matters: Accessory systems depend on main power—partial failures can still occur with weak or unstable power.
  • If no power → restore power and retest

Inspect Power Cord and Connection

  • Check for loose plugs, frayed wiring, or damage at the cord entry point.
  • Expected: Cord intact and firmly connected.
  • Why it matters: Intermittent power to accessories often comes from poor connections.
  • If damaged → remove from service

Check Drawer Heater Switch or Control

  • Verify the heater switch (if equipped) is turned ON and functioning.
  • Cycle it OFF and ON several times.
  • Expected: Indicator light (if present) activates and heater responds.
  • Why it matters: Heater circuits are often separately controlled and may be turned off or have a faulty switch.
  • If switch resolves issue → stop troubleshooting

Inspect Drawer and Accessory Area

  • Open the drawer and check for:
    • Obstructions
    • Damaged wiring (visible only, no disassembly)
    • Loose connections to heater plate
  • Expected: No visible damage or loose components.
  • Why it matters: Physical stress or drawer movement can affect wiring over time.
  • If damage observed → remove from service

Check Accessory Outlet Function (If Equipped)

  • Plug a known working device into the accessory outlet.
  • Expected: Device powers on consistently.
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is heater-only or affects all accessory power.
  • If outlet works but heater does not → heater-specific issue
  • If outlet does not work → broader accessory power issue

Cycle Table Power

  • Turn the table OFF (if switch present) or unplug for 30 seconds, then restore power.
  • Expected: Accessory functions reset and operate normally.
  • Why it matters: Resets internal control circuits that may affect accessory power.
  • If resolved → return to service

Check for Overload or External Devices

  • Ensure no high-draw devices are connected to accessory outlets.
  • Expected: Only appropriate low-power accessories connected.
  • Why it matters: Overloading can disable or trip internal accessory circuits.
  • If overloaded → remove excess load and retest

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (heater element, wiring harness, transformer, or control circuitry).

Remove the table from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not rely on the drawer heater for patient comfort if functionality is questionable. Always verify safe operation before use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Drawer heater not warming and accessory outlet not working on exam table."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Accessory power failure not resolved after verifying power source, cord, and controls."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed table from service and sent to vendor for internal repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed working
  • Power cord condition
  • Heater switch/indicator behavior
  • Accessory outlet test results
  • Any visible wiring concerns
  • Final device status (removed from service vs operational)

Final Thought

Accessory power issues should always be approached from the outside in. Rule out power supply, controls, and load conditions before assuming internal failure. Patient comfort features still require safe operation. Proper escalation and clear documentation ensure safe and efficient resolution.

That is successful troubleshooting.
