Getinge 8666 / 8668 Series

Door not sealing or latch failure

Asset Type

Washer-Disinfector

Manufacturer

Getinge

Model

8666 / 8668 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Door not sealing, latch not engaging, or cycle not starting due to door closure, gasket, or latch-related external issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Ensure no instruments needed for immediate patient care are dependent on this unit.
  • Expected: Backup reprocessing method available.
  • Why it matters: Delays in instrument processing can impact patient care readiness.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to close the door and start a cycle. Observe for gaps, resistance, or latch failure.
  • Expected: Door does not fully close, latch does not engage, or unit alarms.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true sealing or latching issue.

Inspect Door Closure Path for Obstructions

  • Check door edges, hinges, and chamber opening for trays, racks, or debris blocking closure.
  • Expected: Clear path with no obstruction.
  • Why it matters: Even small obstructions prevent proper sealing and latch engagement.
  • If obstruction is found → remove and retest.

Check Door Gasket Condition

  • Inspect the door gasket for cracks, flattening, buildup, or displacement.
  • Expected: Gasket is intact, clean, and evenly seated.
  • Why it matters: A damaged or misaligned gasket prevents a proper seal.
  • If dirty → clean gasket and sealing surface, then retest.
  • If damaged → remove from service for replacement.

Inspect Door Alignment and Fit

  • Close the door slowly and observe alignment with the chamber frame.
  • Expected: Door sits flush and evenly without shifting or binding.
  • Why it matters: Misalignment prevents proper latch engagement and sealing.
  • If misaligned → check hinges for looseness or wear.

Check Door Latch Mechanism

  • Operate the latch and observe for smooth engagement. Listen for clicking or locking action.
  • Expected: Latch engages fully and holds door securely.
  • Why it matters: Worn or stuck latches are a common cause of sealing failure.
  • If stiff or sticking → inspect for debris and clean.
  • If latch does not engage → likely mechanical failure.

Inspect Latch Receiver/Strike Plate

  • Check the strike plate or receiving mechanism inside the chamber frame.
  • Expected: Proper alignment with latch and no visible damage.
  • Why it matters: Misalignment or wear prevents latch from securing the door.
  • If loose → tighten if accessible.
  • If worn/damaged → remove from service.

Check for Door Interlock or Sensor Feedback (if applicable)

  • Observe if the unit recognizes the door as closed (no “door open” alarm).
  • Expected: System acknowledges door closure.
  • Why it matters: Sensor/interlock issues can mimic latch failure.
  • If door appears closed but not recognized → possible interlock issue.

Inspect for Signs of Heat or Mechanical Stress

  • Check for warped door edges, discoloration, or uneven sealing surfaces.
  • Expected: No warping or distortion.
  • Why it matters: Repeated heat cycles can deform components and prevent sealing.
  • If warping is present → remove from service.

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely due to internal latch mechanism failure, interlock failure, or structural misalignment.

Remove the unit from service
Label Out of Service
Submit for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unsafe operation and protects sterilization integrity.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to force the door closed on an active unit. Always move instruments to another washer before troubleshooting to maintain proper infection control workflow.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Washer-disinfector door not sealing and cycle will not start."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Worn door gasket and latch not fully engaging due to alignment shift."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned sealing surface, confirmed gasket wear, removed unit from service for gasket and latch repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Door fully closing or not
  • Presence of debris or obstruction
  • Gasket condition (cracked, worn, intact)
  • Latch engagement behavior (clicking, loose, not engaging)
  • Any door-related alarms
  • Signs of warping or misalignment
  • Final device status (in service or removed)

Final Thought

Door sealing and latching are critical for proper washer-disinfector operation and infection control. Always rule out simple mechanical and environmental causes first, and escalate appropriately when structural or latch failures are identified. Clear documentation supports faster repair and maintains compliance.

That is successful troubleshooting.
