Drager Vamos 2

No CO₂ Reading

Asset Type

Anesthesia Monitor

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Vamos 2

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting missing CO₂ readings caused by sampling line, airway adapter, moisture, flow, setup, or sensor issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not troubleshoot the Vamos 2 on an active patient if CO₂ monitoring is required for anesthesia care.

Action:

  • Confirm the patient is being monitored safely.
  • Verify ventilation using the anesthesia machine, reservoir bag movement, chest rise, SpO₂, airway pressure, and any alternate EtCO₂ source if available.
  • If EtCO₂ is clinically required and the Vamos 2 is not displaying CO₂, move the patient to another verified monitor or module before continuing.

Expected outcome: The patient remains safely monitored while the CO₂ issue is investigated.

Why it matters: Loss of CO₂ monitoring during anesthesia can delay recognition of apnea, disconnection, airway obstruction, or ventilation problems.

If patient safety is restored by using another monitor, continue troubleshooting the affected Vamos 2 off the patient.

2. Verify the Reported Problem

Confirm exactly what is missing.

Action:

  • Check whether the monitor shows:
  • No CO₂ waveform
  • No EtCO₂ numeric value
  • A flat CO₂ waveform
  • A dashed or blank CO₂ value
  • A sampling-line or gas measurement alarm
  • A startup or warm-up message

Expected outcome: The issue is confirmed as a CO₂ measurement problem rather than a display, alarm limit, or patient setup issue.

If CO₂ appears after normal startup or warm-up, verify operation and stop.

3. Check That CO₂ Monitoring Is Enabled and Displayed

Confirm the Vamos 2 is configured to show the CO₂ parameter.

Action:

  • Check the screen layout and parameter display.
  • Verify CO₂ has not been hidden, disabled, or removed from the active view.
  • Confirm the unit is in the correct monitoring mode for the clinical area.

Expected outcome: CO₂ waveform and numeric fields are visible on the display.

If CO₂ was hidden and restoring the display resolves the issue, document the finding and stop.

4. Inspect the Sampling Line Connection

A missing CO₂ reading is commonly caused by an open, disconnected, kinked, or blocked sample line.

Action:

  • Check that the sampling line is firmly connected to the Vamos 2 gas sample inlet.
  • Check the patient end of the sampling line or airway adapter connection.
  • Verify the line is not loose, cracked, stretched, kinked, pinched, or disconnected.

Expected outcome: The sampling path is fully connected from the airway circuit to the monitor.

If reconnecting or replacing the line restores CO₂, verify stable waveform and stop.

5. Replace the Sampling Line

Even if the sampling line looks normal, it may be blocked internally by moisture, secretions, or debris.

Action:

  • Remove the current sampling line.
  • Install a known-good compatible sampling line.
  • Ensure the replacement line seats correctly at the monitor and airway connection.
  • Allow the monitor time to draw a sample and update the CO₂ reading.

Expected outcome: CO₂ waveform and EtCO₂ numeric value return.

If a new line resolves the issue, the original sampling line was likely blocked, leaking, or damaged. Stop and document the accessory replacement.

6. Check for Moisture or Contamination

Moisture in the sample path can prevent proper gas measurement.

Action:

  • Inspect the sampling line, water trap if present, and sample inlet area for visible fluid, condensation, or contamination.
  • Replace wet or contaminated disposable accessories.
  • Do not force liquid into the monitor.
  • Do not blow compressed air into the gas inlet unless approved by facility procedure and manufacturer guidance.

Expected outcome: The gas sample path is dry, clean, and unobstructed.

If removing wet accessories restores CO₂, verify stable operation and stop.

7. Verify the Airway Adapter or Sample Port

A proper airway sample point is required for sidestream CO₂ measurement.

Action:

  • Check that the sampling line is connected to the correct airway adapter or sample port.
  • Confirm the adapter is not installed backward, blocked, capped, cracked, or disconnected from the breathing circuit.
  • Verify the sample port is exposed to exhaled gas and not connected to the wrong circuit location.

Expected outcome: The Vamos 2 receives a valid airway gas sample.

If correcting the adapter or sample location restores CO₂, document the setup issue and stop.

8. Check for Circuit Leak or No Exhaled Sample

The monitor cannot display CO₂ if it is not receiving exhaled gas.

Action:

  • Confirm ventilation is occurring.
  • Check for a disconnected breathing circuit, loose airway connection, open sampling port, leaking adapter, or excessive circuit leak.
  • Confirm the patient is not disconnected from the anesthesia circuit.

Expected outcome: A valid exhaled gas sample reaches the sampling line.

If the CO₂ reading returns after correcting a circuit or adapter issue, stop and document the external cause.

9. Check the Sample Inlet and Filter Area

The monitor-side gas inlet or filter area may be blocked or improperly seated.

Action:

  • Inspect the sample inlet connection for damage, debris, or loose fit.
  • Confirm any required filter, water trap, or connector is installed correctly.
  • Remove and reseat the accessory if appropriate.
  • Replace questionable disposable components with known-good compatible parts.

Expected outcome: The monitor can pull a gas sample without obstruction or leakage.

If reseating or replacing the inlet accessory restores CO₂, verify the reading and stop.

10. Listen for Sampling Pump Activity

A sidestream gas monitor generally requires sample flow through the unit.

Action:

  • With the monitor powered on and gas monitoring active, listen for normal sampling pump activity if it is normally audible on this device.
  • Check for alarms related to sample flow, blocked line, or gas measurement failure.
  • Compare behavior to a known-good unit if available.

Expected outcome: The monitor appears to be drawing sample normally.

If there is no apparent sample flow and external accessories have already been ruled out, remove the device from service.

11. Restart the Monitor When Safe

A temporary software or initialization issue may prevent the CO₂ parameter from updating.

Action:

  • Only perform a restart when the device is not being used for active patient monitoring.
  • Power the Vamos 2 off.
  • Wait briefly.
  • Power it back on and allow the gas measurement system to initialize.
  • Reconnect a known-good sampling line and verify CO₂ function.

Expected outcome: CO₂ measurement initializes normally.

If CO₂ returns after restart, perform a functional check before returning the monitor to service.

12. Compare With a Known-Good Setup

Use comparison testing to separate monitor failure from accessory or setup failure.

Action:

  • Use a known-good sampling line, airway adapter, and test setup.
  • If available, compare with another working gas monitor using the same external accessories.
  • Verify whether the issue follows the accessory setup or stays with the Vamos 2.

Expected outcome: The fault is isolated to either the external setup or the monitor itself.

If the issue follows the accessories, replace the faulty accessory.

If the issue stays with the Vamos 2, remove the monitor from service.

If the Problem Persists

If the Vamos 2 still has no CO₂ reading after checking the sampling line, airway adapter, moisture, sample inlet, setup, display configuration, and restart behavior, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal sampling pump, gas measurement module, flow sensor, internal blockage, calibration issue, or electronic failure.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Do not continue troubleshooting into internal components during clinical response. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot loss of CO₂ monitoring on an active anesthesia patient unless another qualified person is maintaining patient safety and ventilation monitoring. If CO₂ monitoring is required, move the patient to another verified monitor first, then troubleshoot the Vamos 2 off the patient.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Drager Vamos 2 anesthesia monitor displayed no CO₂ reading during setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the CO₂ sampling line connected but blocked with moisture, preventing the monitor from drawing a valid gas sample."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced the sampling line, verified EtCO₂ waveform and numeric reading returned, and confirmed the monitor operated normally before returning it to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet or power source verified
  • Sampling line replaced
  • Airway adapter checked
  • Sample inlet inspected
  • Water trap or filter condition checked, if applicable
  • Any CO₂-related alarms observed
  • Whether waveform, numeric value, or both were missing
  • Whether pump/sample flow appeared normal
  • Restart attempted
  • Known-good accessories used
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

No CO₂ reading on an anesthesia monitor should be handled with patient safety first, then logical external checks. Most issues are caused by the sampling line, airway adapter, moisture, setup, or sample path obstruction. Once those causes are ruled out, the correct action is to remove the monitor from service and escalate for repair with clear CCR documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
