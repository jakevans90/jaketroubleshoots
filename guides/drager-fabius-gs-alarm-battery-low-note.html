Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: BATTERY LOW !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

BATTERY LOW ! alarm indicating loss of mains power with battery below 20%, creating risk of device shutdown, loss of ventilation, and interruption of anesthesia delivery.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately confirm patient ventilation
  • Switch to manual ventilation if needed
  • Move patient to backup device if stability is uncertain

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm BATTERY LOW ! alarm is displayed
  • Check if unit is unplugged from mains power
  • Observe battery indicator level (<20%)
  • Look for additional power-related alarms

Check Mains Power Connection

  • Ensure power cord is securely connected to device and outlet
  • Inspect for loose or partially disconnected cable

Verify Wall Outlet Functionality

  • Test outlet with another device
  • Try a different known working outlet

Check Power Indicators on the Machine

  • Confirm AC power indicator is illuminated
  • Verify battery charging indicator is active

Inspect Power Cord and Plug

  • Check for damage, fraying, or bent prongs
  • Replace with a known good cord if needed

Allow Battery to Charge

  • Keep unit connected to AC power
  • Monitor battery level increasing above alarm threshold

Evaluate Battery Performance

  • Confirm battery percentage increases over time
  • Check if battery holds charge
  • Identify signs of battery degradation

If the Problem Persists

If external causes such as power connection, outlet, and cord are verified and the alarm remains:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or battery replacement

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before performing any troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit alarming BATTERY LOW ! with loss of mains power indication during setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Power cord disconnected from outlet or battery failed to charge despite confirmed AC power."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reconnected to working outlet and confirmed charging or removed from service for battery replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power-related alarms are critical on anesthesia machines. Always restore mains power first and verify battery health to prevent unexpected shutdowns and patient risk.

That is successful troubleshooting.
