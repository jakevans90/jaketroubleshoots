GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: CONTROL PANEL KEY STUCK - RELEASE, THEN PRESS ANY KEY TO CONTINUE

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the CONTROL PANEL KEY STUCK - RELEASE, THEN PRESS ANY KEY TO CONTINUE error on a GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm. This condition indicates that a control panel key is detected as pressed or stuck during startup. Generator functions may be disabled and X-ray operation may be unavailable until the stuck-key condition is cleared.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the C-Arm from patient use if generator functions or X-rays are disabled.
  • Confirm the device is not being used for an active procedure.
  • If imaging is needed, provide another C-Arm or approved imaging method.
  • Do not continue trying to use the unit while the control panel key fault is active.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the OEC 9900 on and observe the startup sequence.
  • Confirm the displayed message is exactly: CONTROL PANEL KEY STUCK - RELEASE, THEN PRESS ANY KEY TO CONTINUE.
  • Check whether the error appears immediately during bootup, after the splash screen, or intermittently.
  • Confirm whether generator functions are disabled, X-rays are unavailable, the system is waiting for keypad input, or one or more keys do not respond normally.

Check for Anything Pressing the Control Panel

  • Inspect the C-Arm control panel area before assuming keypad failure.
  • Remove anything touching or pressing the keypad, including drapes, covers, tape, labels, gel residue, accessories, cleaning wipes, or objects resting on the panel.
  • Confirm that no object or material is holding a key down.

Free the Suspected Stuck Key

  • Gently press and release each C-Arm control panel key.
  • Look for any key that does not click normally, does not return fully, feels sticky, is physically damaged, sits lower than surrounding keys, or responds differently than the others.
  • If a key appears stuck, gently work it free without forcing or damaging the panel.
  • Confirm that all keys move normally and return to their resting position.

Press Any C-Arm Control Panel Key to Continue

  • After removing the stuck-key condition, press any C-Arm control panel key as instructed by the display.
  • Observe whether the system continues through startup.
  • If the error clears, complete functional checks before returning the C-Arm to service.

Power Cycle and Recheck

  • If the message clears, power the system down using the normal shutdown procedure.
  • Restart the C-Arm and observe the boot sequence again.
  • Confirm that the stuck key error does not return during startup.
  • If the error immediately returns, suspect a recurring keypad or control panel input issue.

Inspect for Cleaning Residue, Fluid, or Physical Damage

  • Inspect the control panel for cracked membrane material, damaged buttons, fluid residue, sticky cleaning chemical buildup, loose keypad overlay, evidence of impact, or keys that remain partially depressed.
  • Clean only according to facility-approved cleaning practices and manufacturer-safe methods.
  • Confirm the keypad surface is clean, dry, intact, and all keys move normally.

Determine Whether the Issue Is External or Device-Related

  • If the error clears after removing an object or freeing a key, document the external cause.
  • If the error returns with no obstruction, no contamination, and no visible stuck key, suspect control panel, keypad, membrane switch, or related input hardware failure.
  • Do not repeatedly power cycle the system to force it into clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If the error continues after removing anything pressing the panel, freeing all keys, checking for residue or fluid, and restarting the system, external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent CONTROL PANEL KEY STUCK - RELEASE, THEN PRESS ANY KEY TO CONTINUE error may indicate a keypad, control panel, membrane switch, or related input hardware failure.

Remove the C-Arm from service.

Label it Out of Service.

Send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a stuck control panel key on an active patient when X-ray operation is needed. Move the patient or procedure to a backup imaging device first. A C-Arm with disabled generator functions should not be relied on for patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE OEC 9900 Series C-Arm displays CONTROL PANEL KEY STUCK - RELEASE, THEN PRESS ANY KEY TO CONTINUE during bootup. Generator functions and X-rays are disabled."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Control panel key input was detected as stuck during startup. No drape, tape, cover, residue, or object was found pressing the keypad. Error returned after power cycle, indicating likely keypad or control panel hardware fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Inspected control panel, checked all keys for sticking, removed external causes, power cycled the system, and verified the error persisted. Removed C-Arm from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A stuck control panel key may look minor, but on the OEC 9900 it can disable generator functions and prevent X-ray use. Start with patient safety, confirm the displayed error, check for anything physically pressing the keypad, free the stuck key, and only escalate after simple external causes are ruled out. Accurate CCR documentation helps show whether the issue was corrected at the point of use or required repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
