Pedigo P-2040

Temperature Too High

Asset Type

Blanket Warmer

Manufacturer

Pedigo

Model

P-2040

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a Pedigo P-2040 blanket warmer that is overheating, reading high temperature, or warming above the expected set range.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not continue using blankets or linens from a warmer suspected of overheating.

Action:

  • Remove the Pedigo P-2040 from active clinical use if the cabinet temperature is above the expected range, if blankets feel excessively hot, or if staff report a temperature alarm or overheating concern.
  • Remove heated contents if safe to do so.
  • Check whether any patient-contact items may have been overheated.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without risk of thermal injury from overheated blankets or linens.

Why it matters: Blanket warmers can cause patient burns if contents are overheated or applied directly to sensitive skin.

2. Verify the Reported Temperature Condition

Confirm whether the unit is actually overheating or if the concern is based on display behavior.

Action:

  • Check the displayed temperature.
  • Compare it to the set temperature.
  • Ask staff what they observed, including high temperature display, alarm condition, blankets too hot to touch, cabinet running continuously, or unusual smell or heat from the unit.

Expected outcome: You confirm whether the issue is a true high-temperature condition, a display concern, or a user-reported comfort issue.

If the displayed temperature is normal and blankets are not excessively hot, document the finding and continue monitoring if needed.

3. Remove the Unit From Service if Temperature Is Unsafe

Do not try to “watch it for a while” in patient care areas if the warmer is already above a safe operating range.

Action:

  • If the cabinet temperature is significantly above the setpoint, unplug the warmer.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Allow the cabinet to cool before further inspection.

Expected outcome: The overheating condition is stopped and the unit is controlled before further use.

4. Check the Temperature Setpoint

Verify that the set temperature was not accidentally adjusted too high.

Action:

  • Review the temperature setting on the control panel.
  • Confirm it matches the facility’s approved blanket warmer temperature policy.
  • Adjust only within approved clinical limits.

Expected outcome: If the setpoint was too high, correcting it should allow the warmer to stabilize at the proper temperature.

If this resolves the issue, return to service only after confirming stable temperature operation.

5. Check for Blocked Airflow Inside the Cabinet

Overloading the cabinet can trap heat and cause uneven or elevated temperatures.

Action:

  • Inspect the cabinet contents.
  • Look for blankets packed tightly against vents, linen blocking air circulation, items stacked too close to the temperature sensor area, or non-approved items being warmed.
  • Remove excess contents and reload the cabinet with space for airflow.

Expected outcome: Cabinet air can circulate normally and temperature should stabilize.

If the temperature normalizes after correcting the load, educate staff and document the cause.

6. Check Door Closure and Door Gasket

A door or gasket issue can cause the heater to run longer than expected or create unstable cabinet temperatures.

Action:

  • Inspect the door for proper closure.
  • Check the gasket for tears, flattened sections, gaps, debris, or misalignment.
  • Confirm the door seals evenly.

Expected outcome: The cabinet maintains temperature without abnormal heat cycling.

If the gasket is damaged or the door does not seal properly, remove the unit from service for repair.

7. Inspect the External Condition of the Warmer

Look for obvious signs of overheating or damage before continuing.

Action:

  • Inspect the warmer for burning smell, discolored cabinet areas, melted plastic, unusual heat around controls, loose power cord, damaged plug, buzzing, or abnormal sounds.

Expected outcome: No obvious electrical or heat-damage condition is present.

If heat damage, burning odor, or electrical damage is found, stop troubleshooting and send the unit for repair evaluation.

8. Verify the Power Source

A poor or improper power connection can contribute to abnormal operation.

Action:

  • Confirm the warmer is plugged directly into an approved hospital-grade outlet.
  • Avoid extension cords or power strips unless specifically approved by facility policy.
  • Check the outlet for physical damage.
  • If needed, test the outlet with an approved outlet tester.

Expected outcome: The warmer is connected to a proper power source.

If outlet problems are found, move the warmer to a known-good outlet and notify facilities as appropriate.

9. Compare Display Temperature With an Independent Thermometer

Confirm whether the displayed temperature matches the actual cabinet temperature.

Action:

  • Place an appropriate calibrated thermometer or temperature probe inside the cabinet.
  • Allow enough time for the reading to stabilize.
  • Compare the independent reading with the warmer’s displayed temperature.

Expected outcome: If both readings are high, the unit may truly be overheating. If the independent reading is normal but the display reads high, the issue may involve sensing, calibration, or display/control circuitry. If the display is normal but the independent reading is high, the control system may not be regulating properly.

If readings do not agree or the cabinet temperature continues rising above setpoint, remove the unit from service.

10. Observe Temperature Recovery After Cooling

Only perform this step if the unit shows no signs of burning, damage, or unsafe electrical condition.

Action:

  • After the warmer cools, plug it into a known-good outlet.
  • Set it to the approved temperature.
  • Monitor the temperature rise.
  • Confirm the heater cycles off and the cabinet stabilizes near the setpoint.

Expected outcome: The unit heats normally, stops heating near setpoint, and maintains stable temperature.

If the temperature continues climbing beyond setpoint, stop testing and send for repair.

11. Do Not Bypass Temperature Protection

Do not bypass thermostats, sensors, thermal limits, relays, or control circuits to keep the warmer running.

Action:

  • If the unit will not regulate temperature correctly, remove it from service.

Expected outcome: The warmer is not returned to patient care with a possible failed control or safety circuit.

If the Problem Persists

If the Pedigo P-2040 continues to run hot, overshoots the set temperature, has inaccurate temperature readings, or shows signs of heat damage, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device must be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Likely internal causes may include a failed temperature sensor, control board issue, stuck heater relay, thermostat problem, or thermal protection failure.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A blanket warmer with uncontrolled heat should not remain in clinical service.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot an overheating blanket warmer while staff are actively pulling patient-use blankets from it. Remove the unit from service first, provide another warmer if available, move patient care to safe backup warmed blankets if needed, and verify safe blanket temperatures before returning the unit to use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported that the Pedigo P-2040 blanket warmer temperature was too high and blankets felt hotter than normal."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Cabinet temperature exceeded the setpoint during inspection, and external checks found no blocked airflow, incorrect setpoint, or outlet issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the blanket warmer from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for bench evaluation for temperature regulation failure."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status
  • Outlet tested
  • Set temperature found
  • Displayed cabinet temperature
  • Independent thermometer reading
  • Blankets overloaded or properly loaded
  • Door gasket condition
  • Door closure verified
  • Any unusual smell, heat, sound, or discoloration
  • Whether heater cycled off normally

Final Thought

Temperature-too-high complaints on blanket warmers should be handled conservatively. Start with patient safety, verify the setpoint and loading conditions, confirm actual cabinet temperature, and escalate when the warmer cannot regulate heat reliably. Clear CCR documentation protects patients, staff, and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
