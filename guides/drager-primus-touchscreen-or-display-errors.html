Drager Primus

Touchscreen or Display Errors

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Primus

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting Drager Primus ventilators that exhibit touchscreen or display errors. Display issues can prevent the user from seeing ventilator settings, alarms, or patient data, potentially affecting patient care. Most causes are external or easily verifiable before assuming internal electronic failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Never attempt touchscreen troubleshooting on an actively ventilated patient. Move the patient to another ventilator before testing or powering off the device.

Check power connection

  • Ensure the ventilator is plugged into a verified working outlet.
  • Verify the power cable is fully seated on both ends.
  • Why: Inconsistent or low voltage can cause display malfunctions.

Verify battery status

  • If operating on battery, confirm sufficient charge.
  • Plug into AC power to see if the issue persists.
  • Why: Low battery can cause display dimming or unresponsiveness.

Inspect the touchscreen surface

  • Clean with a soft, lint-free cloth and approved disinfectant.
  • Remove any moisture or debris that may interfere with touch input.
  • Why: Dirt, liquid, or residue can cause false or no touch responses.

Check for visible damage

  • Examine the screen for cracks, chips, or signs of impact.
  • Inspect connector areas at the display module if accessible.
  • Why: Physical damage can prevent proper display operation.

Perform a soft reboot

  • Power cycle the ventilator: turn off, wait 30 seconds, turn on.
  • Observe if the display initializes correctly.
  • Why: Temporary software glitches can be cleared with a reboot.

Check ventilator firmware and settings

  • Confirm the device firmware is current if information is available.
  • Reset display settings to default if user-accessible.
  • Why: Corrupt settings or outdated firmware may cause display errors.

Swap external peripherals

  • Disconnect any external modules, sensors, or data interfaces.
  • Test if the display functions normally with minimal setup.
  • Why: Peripheral conflicts can sometimes freeze or corrupt the touchscreen interface.

If the Problem Persists

External and software-related causes have been ruled out. Device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to escalate prevents further risk and preserves patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt touchscreen troubleshooting on an actively ventilated patient. Move the patient to another ventilator before testing or powering off the device. Ensure patient safety is maintained at all times.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Touchscreen unresponsive and display shows flickering or blank screen.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed that the ventilator operates normally on power cycle with no peripheral connections, but display fails intermittently. Cleaning and soft reboot did not correct the issue.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from clinical service, labeled Out of Service, and sent to biomedical engineering bench for display module evaluation or replacement.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet and power cable tested
  • Battery charge status verified
  • Screen cleaned and inspected
  • Soft reboot attempted
  • External peripherals disconnected
  • Any visible damage noted
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety and logical, stepwise troubleshooting are paramount. Start with simple external checks, document every action with CCR, and escalate to repair when internal failure is suspected. Clear documentation supports safe, efficient operations and reduces repeat issues.

That is successful troubleshooting.
