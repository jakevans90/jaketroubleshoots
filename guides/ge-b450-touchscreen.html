GE Carescape B450

Touchscreen Not Responding

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

B450

Verify Power and Connections

  • Ensure the monitor is properly plugged into a working outlet.
  • Confirm the AC adapter or battery is seated correctly and operational.
  • Why: Touchscreen may appear unresponsive if the device is underpowered.

Check for Obstructions

  • Clean the touchscreen with a soft, lint-free cloth and approved cleaner.
  • Remove any screen protectors or films that may interfere.
  • Why: Dirt, smudges, or residue can prevent proper touch recognition.

Restart the Monitor

  • Power cycle the device: turn it off, wait 30 seconds, then turn it back on.
  • Why: Temporary software or firmware glitches can cause touchscreen freezes.

Verify Input Method

  • Ensure you are using a bare finger or approved stylus; other objects may not register.
  • Why: The B450 touchscreen is capacitive and may not respond to non-approved objects.

Check Display and Calibration Settings

  • Navigate (if possible via physical buttons) to touchscreen calibration settings and re-calibrate.
  • Why: Miscalibration can make the touchscreen appear unresponsive or inaccurate.

Environmental Check

  • Confirm the monitor is not exposed to excessive heat, moisture, or magnetic interference.
  • Why: Environmental conditions can affect touchscreen sensitivity.

Test with External Interface (if available)

  • If the monitor supports external input (mouse or keyboard), test responsiveness.
  • Why: Determines if the issue is software-related or strictly touchscreen hardware.

If the Problem Persists

  • Common external causes have been ruled out; the touchscreen or internal display controller may be failing.
  • Action: Remove monitor from service. Label “Out of Service.” Send to Biomedical Engineering or vendor repair.
  • Note: Knowing when to escalate is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not troubleshoot on an active patient. Move patient monitoring to a backup device before any troubleshooting.
  • Only interact with the touchscreen when the monitor is safely offline.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

  • Complaint: “Touchscreen not responding during routine patient monitoring.”
  • Cause: “Monitor touchscreen remained unresponsive after confirming power, cleaning, and restarting the device.”
  • Resolution: “Monitor removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for vendor evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • AC adapter or battery checked
  • Screen cleaned and verified
  • Monitor power-cycled
  • Calibration attempted
  • Environmental conditions noted
  • Monitor removed from patient use

That is successful troubleshooting.
