Cincinnati Sub-Zero Blanketrol Series

Display or control panel malfunction

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

Cincinnati Sub-Zero

Model

Blanketrol Series

What This Guide Helps With

Unresponsive buttons, blank or flickering display, or incorrect readings caused by power issues, loose connections, or external environmental factors.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use and transition to an alternate warming method.
  • Expected: No patient connected during troubleshooting.

Verify Reported Issue

  • Confirm symptoms such as blank screen, frozen controls, or incorrect display behavior.
  • Expected: Panel is unresponsive or not displaying correctly.
  • Why it matters: Confirms true control/display issue vs normal operation misunderstanding.

Check Power Source

  • Ensure the unit is plugged into a known-good hospital-grade outlet. Test outlet if needed.
  • Expected: Stable power supply.
  • If outlet is faulty → move to a working outlet and retest.

Inspect Power Cord and Connection

  • Check for damage, loose plug, or partially seated cord at the unit.
  • Why it matters: Inconsistent power can cause display failure or erratic behavior.
  • If corrected and display returns → issue resolved.

Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit off, wait 10–15 seconds, then power it back on.
  • Expected: Display initializes normally and controls respond.
  • If normal operation returns → issue resolved.

Check for Locked or Stuck Buttons

  • Inspect the control panel for physically stuck keys or debris under buttons.
  • Expected: Buttons move freely and respond when pressed.
  • If obstruction found → clean and retest.

Inspect for Fluid Exposure or Contamination

  • Look for signs of spilled fluid, cleaning residue, or condensation on the panel.
  • Why it matters: Moisture can interfere with touch or button input.
  • If present → clean/dry per facility guidelines and retest.

Check Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure the unit is not exposed to excessive heat, cold, or humidity.
  • Expected: Unit operating within normal room conditions.
  • If relocated and functioning → issue resolved.

Observe Startup Behavior

  • Restart the unit and watch for abnormal startup (no display, partial display, error codes).
  • Why it matters: Helps determine if issue is intermittent or consistent hardware failure.

If the Problem Persists

All common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (display module, control board, or wiring).

Remove the device from service, label Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot the control panel while connected to a patient. Always transfer the patient to another warming device first to maintain safe temperature management.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit display blank and controls unresponsive during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Suspected control panel/display failure after verifying power and connections."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent to Biomed/vendor for repair."

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Intermittent control panel freezing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Power cord not fully seated causing intermittent power disruption."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated power cord and verified normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Power cord condition
  • Display behavior (blank, flickering, partial)
  • Button responsiveness
  • Presence of fluid or residue
  • Startup behavior
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Display and control issues can often appear serious but are frequently caused by simple external factors like power instability or contamination. Always approach troubleshooting methodically, prioritize patient safety, and escalate appropriately when internal faults are suspected. Proper documentation ensures clarity and supports effective repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
