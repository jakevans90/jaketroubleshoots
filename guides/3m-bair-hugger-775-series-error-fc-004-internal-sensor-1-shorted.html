3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-004 Internal Sensor 1 Shorted

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-004 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. FC-004 indicates that internal sensor 1 is shorted, which can create a permanent sensor-related fault condition and prevent the warmer from safely controlling temperature.

A shorted temperature sensor fault should be treated as a patient safety concern because unreliable temperature sensing can affect heating control, alarm behavior, and the unit’s ability to maintain safe warming therapy.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the Bair Hugger from patient use if Error FC-004 is active or returns after reset.

  • Stop use of the warming unit.
  • Disconnect the warming blanket from the patient if clinically appropriate.
  • Provide an alternate warming method if patient warming is still needed.
  • Do not continue using the unit with an active sensor fault.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without relying on a warming unit that may not safely control temperature.

Why it matters: A shorted temperature sensor can affect how the unit detects and controls heat. Patient warming equipment must not be used when temperature sensing is unreliable.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

Confirm that the displayed fault is Error FC-004.

  • Check the display for the exact error code.
  • Confirm whether FC-004 appears at startup, during operation, or immediately after selecting a temperature setting.
  • Ask staff whether the unit stopped heating, alarmed, shut down, or failed to clear after restart.
  • Note whether the fault is intermittent or permanent.

Expected outcome: Error FC-004 is clearly observed or reproducible.

Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported sensor fault instead of a user setup issue, temporary overheating event, or unrelated operating problem.

3. Remove the Unit From Power and Allow a Short Reset Period

Disconnect the unit from AC power and allow a brief reset period.

  • Turn the unit off.
  • Unplug the power cord from the wall outlet.
  • Wait at least 30–60 seconds.
  • Reconnect power and turn the unit back on.

Expected outcome: The unit powers up normally without Error FC-004.

Why it matters: A short reset can clear temporary electronic faults, but a returning FC-004 should be treated as a real sensor-related problem.

4. Inspect the Power Source and Power Cord

Check the external power setup before focusing only on the sensor fault.

  • Confirm the unit is connected to a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord for cuts, loose strain relief, bent blades, or signs of heat damage.
  • Avoid using extension cords or questionable power strips.
  • Try another known-good outlet if the power source is suspect.

Expected outcome: The unit receives stable power and powers on normally.

Why it matters: While FC-004 points toward a sensor fault, poor power or damaged cords can create confusing startup behavior and should be ruled out early.

5. Check the Hose Connection

Inspect the hose connection at the warming unit.

  • Turn the unit off and disconnect power.
  • Confirm the hose is fully seated and properly connected.
  • Check that the hose is not loose, partially inserted, twisted, crushed, or visibly damaged.
  • Look for damage at the hose connection point.
  • Reconnect the hose securely and retest the unit.

Expected outcome: The hose connection is secure and the error clears.

Why it matters: FC-004 is grouped with sensor, hose, and elbow-related permanent fault conditions. A poor hose or sensor connection can cause the unit to detect a fault.

6. Inspect the Hose and Elbow Area for Damage or Contamination

Check the hose and elbow area closely.

  • Inspect the hose for cracks, kinks, crushed sections, or pulled wiring.
  • Check the elbow area for damage, looseness, contamination, or signs of moisture.
  • Look for fluid intrusion, residue, or physical abuse.
  • Replace any visibly damaged hose assembly or sensor-related component according to your facility process.

Expected outcome: No damage, moisture, or contamination is found.

Why it matters: Moisture, damaged wiring, or a failed sensor path can cause temperature sensor faults. A shorted sensor is often not something that can be corrected by normal user operation.

7. Isolate Accessory-Related Causes

Determine whether the issue follows the hose or remains with the warming unit.

  • If available, test the unit with a known-good compatible hose assembly.
  • Make sure the replacement hose is appropriate for the Bair Hugger 775 Series.
  • Power the unit back on and observe whether Error FC-004 returns.
  • Do not use patient-connected accessories during testing.

Expected outcome: The error clears when a known-good hose or sensor path is used.

Why it matters: This helps separate a hose or elbow sensor problem from a device-side internal failure.

8. Check for Repeat Fault After Reset

After external checks are complete, retest the unit.

  • Reconnect the verified hose.
  • Power the unit on.
  • Select operating modes as appropriate for functional testing.
  • Observe whether FC-004 returns immediately or during operation.
  • Listen for abnormal fan behavior or unexpected shutdown.

Expected outcome: The unit operates without FC-004 and without abnormal behavior.

Why it matters: A single cleared fault may not be enough. The device should remain stable through functional testing before being returned to patient use.

9. Perform Required Calibration and Functional Verification

If a hose or sensor-related component is replaced, complete required service checks before return to use.

  • Perform operating temperature calibration as required by your facility procedure and the applicable service requirements.
  • Verify normal fan operation.
  • Confirm the unit heats appropriately at selected settings.
  • Confirm no fault codes return during testing.
  • Complete any required safety or post-repair checks before returning the device to service.

Expected outcome: The unit passes calibration, functional testing, and safety checks.

Why it matters: Sensor-related repairs affect temperature control. The device should not be returned to service until heating performance and safety are verified.

If the Problem Persists

If Error FC-004 remains after power reset, hose inspection, connection checks, and testing with a known-good hose, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent FC-004 strongly indicates a sensor-related fault, hose or elbow sensor failure, or internal device failure requiring repair-level evaluation.

  • Remove the unit from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Do not return it to patient use.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Replace the hose or elbow sensor as indicated by testing.
  • Calibrate and verify operation before returning the device to service.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Continued reset attempts are not a repair when the device continues to report a permanent sensor fault.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a Bair Hugger on an active patient. If warming is needed, move the patient to a backup warming device first, then remove the faulty unit for Clinical Engineering evaluation. Never rely on a patient warming system with an unresolved temperature sensor error.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 Series reported Error FC-004. Unit displayed internal sensor 1 shorted fault and was removed from patient use due to unresolved temperature sensor-related alarm condition."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found FC-004 sensor-related fault. Hose connection and external condition checked. Fault appears related to hose, elbow sensor, sensor wiring path, or internal sensor circuit depending on test results."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service. Inspected power cord, outlet use, hose connection, hose condition, and elbow sensor area. Retested after power reset. If fault cleared, completed functional testing and calibration before return to service. If fault persisted, labeled Out of Service and sent for repair or bench evaluation for hose or elbow sensor replacement as needed."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error FC-004 should be treated as a temperature sensor reliability issue, not just a nuisance alarm. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact fault, check the hose and external connections first, then escalate when the fault persists. Proper documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the repair history.

That is successful troubleshooting.
