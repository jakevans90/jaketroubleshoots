Cardinal Health Kangaroo ePump

Error: 0 Internal Timer Stopped

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo ePump

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses Error: 0 Internal Timer Stopped, which indicates a failure in the pump’s internal timing system. This condition can cause the pump to shut down and prevents safe and reliable enteral feeding delivery.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the pump if actively infusing
  • Transition feeding to a backup pump or approved alternative method
  • Verify patient feeding continues without interruption

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error: 0 is displayed
  • Observe if the error occurs at startup or during operation
  • Note if the pump is completely non-functional or intermittent

Check Power Source and Basic Operation

  • Verify AC power connection is secure
  • Inspect power adapter for damage
  • Test operation on both battery and AC power

Perform a Full Power Cycle (Primary Corrective Step)

  • Turn the pump off
  • Disconnect from AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart the device

Observe Post-Reset Behavior

  • Allow the pump to run briefly if safe
  • Monitor for recurrence of Error: 0
  • Confirm stable operation without faults

Stop if Resolved

  • If the error clears, return the device to service
  • Monitor closely during initial use

If the Problem Persists

If Error: 0 Internal Timer Stopped continues after power cycling, external causes have been ruled out and an internal control or timing failure is likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the unit

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a feeding pump while actively connected to a patient. Always transition to a backup pump first to maintain continuous nutrition delivery.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 0 and not delivering enteral feeding."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal timer fault persisted after power cycle; unit non-functional."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Power cycled unit with no resolution. Removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error: 0 Internal Timer Stopped indicates a critical internal system failure. Proper troubleshooting involves confirming the fault, attempting a safe reset, and escalating appropriately to maintain patient safety and device reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
