Sysmex UF-1000i

Sheath Fluid / Reagent / Waste Error

Asset Type

Urine Analyzer

Manufacturer

Sysmex

Model

UF-1000i

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting UF-1000i sheath fluid, reagent, waste level, container, tubing, and fluid management errors before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the UF-1000i is not actively processing patient specimens before opening fluid areas, replacing containers, moving waste lines, or restarting the analyzer.

If urine testing must continue, have laboratory staff use another validated analyzer or follow the approved downtime process.

Expected outcome: No patient specimen is interrupted, delayed, or resulted from a device with unresolved fluid handling issues.

Stop Additional Sample Processing

Ask laboratory staff not to load additional specimens until the sheath fluid, reagent, or waste error is cleared.

Confirm whether any specimens were in process when the error occurred and whether results were completed, flagged, aborted, or need repeat testing per lab policy.

Expected outcome: Incomplete or questionable urine results are not released.

Confirm the Exact Fluid Error

Review the analyzer screen, IPU/workstation message, and staff report. Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Sheath fluid empty or low
  • Reagent empty or low
  • Waste full
  • Waste container not detected
  • Fluid replacement error
  • Bubble or flow-related message
  • Reagent pack or container recognition issue
  • Repeated fluid errors after replacement

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to supply level, waste level, container setup, tubing, detection, or internal fluidics.

Check Sheath Fluid Level and Container Placement

Verify the sheath fluid container has adequate volume and is properly seated in its assigned location.

Confirm the container is not expired, empty, damaged, collapsed, or incorrectly installed.

Expected outcome: A low or empty sheath fluid condition is corrected. If the error clears, document the action and stop.

Check Reagent Level and Correct Reagent Type

Confirm the correct Sysmex-compatible reagent is installed and that the bottle, pack, or container matches the analyzer requirement.

Look for expired reagent, improper storage, empty container, loose cap, damaged connector, or incorrect bottle position.

Expected outcome: Incorrect, empty, expired, or mispositioned reagent is identified and corrected. If resolved, return the analyzer to service after lab verification.

Inspect Waste Container Level

Verify the waste container is not full or near full.

Empty or replace the waste container according to laboratory safety procedure and biohazard handling policy.

Confirm the cap and tubing are reconnected securely afterward.

Expected outcome: A full waste condition is corrected and the analyzer can resume normal operation.

Confirm Waste Container Is Properly Seated

Check that the waste container is in the correct location and that any sensor, cap, float, or detection connection is properly positioned.

A container that is slightly misaligned can trigger a waste error even when it is not full.

Expected outcome: Waste detection returns to normal. If the message clears, stop troubleshooting.

Inspect External Fluid Tubing

Visually inspect user-accessible sheath, reagent, and waste tubing. Look for:

  • Kinks
  • Pinches
  • Loose fittings
  • Cracked tubing
  • Disconnected lines
  • Fluid leakage
  • Air bubbles
  • Tubing routed under the analyzer or container

Expected outcome: External tubing restrictions or disconnections are corrected before assuming internal failure.

Check for Leaks or Fluid Residue

Inspect around the analyzer base, reagent area, waste container, and tubing connections for fluid residue, wet surfaces, crystallized deposits, or odor.

Do not continue operation if active leakage is present.

Expected outcome: Any leak is identified and the device is removed from service if fluid containment is compromised.

Verify Container Caps and Pickup Tubes

Confirm pickup tubes are inserted fully into the correct containers and are not sitting above the fluid level.

Make sure caps are not cross-threaded and that tubing is not swapped between sheath, reagent, and waste positions.

Expected outcome: The analyzer can draw fluid and drain waste through the correct external paths.

Check Recent Fluid Replacement History

Ask lab staff whether sheath fluid, reagent, or waste was replaced shortly before the error appeared.

Confirm whether the correct replacement sequence was followed and whether the analyzer was allowed to recognize the new container.

Expected outcome: Errors caused by recent container replacement, loose tubing, or missed confirmation steps are identified.

Run the Appropriate User-Level Prime, Rinse, or Fluid Initialization Function

If allowed by site procedure and operator access, have qualified lab staff initiate the appropriate user-level fluid replacement, prime, rinse, or initialization function from the analyzer or IPU.

Do not bypass alarms or repeatedly force operation if fluid movement fails.

Expected outcome: Air is cleared from the line and the analyzer recognizes normal fluid status.

Observe for Recurring Error After Reset

After correcting external fluid or waste issues, clear the alarm using normal operator workflow and observe whether the same error returns.

Do not return the analyzer to service based only on a cleared message if the same error repeats immediately.

Expected outcome: A one-time container or tubing issue is separated from a recurring fluidics or sensor problem.

Check for Unusual Pump or Valve Behavior

Listen for abnormal sounds during user-level fluid priming or startup, such as repeated clicking, no pump sound, strain, grinding, or prolonged cycling.

Do not open internal covers or attempt board-level or pump repair during field troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: Abnormal internal fluid movement is recognized as a repair-level issue.

Confirm Analyzer Status With Laboratory Staff

Have lab staff confirm whether the analyzer passes its required startup checks, background checks, QC, or site-required verification after the fluid issue is corrected.

Expected outcome: The UF-1000i is only returned to service after the lab confirms acceptable operation.

If the Problem Persists

If the sheath fluid, reagent, or waste error continues after checking container levels, correct reagent type, waste level, tubing, caps, pickup lines, leaks, and user-level priming, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve an internal fluid sensor, pump, valve, pressure path, waste detection circuit, reagent detection mechanism, or internal tubing problem. The UF-1000i should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue running patient specimens on a urine analyzer with unresolved fluid handling errors.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot fluid, reagent, or waste errors while patient specimens are actively running. Move testing to another validated analyzer or downtime workflow first. Fluid handling errors can affect sample processing, result reliability, and biohazard containment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Laboratory reported the Sysmex UF-1000i displayed a sheath fluid/reagent/waste error and stopped urine sample processing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Found the waste container full and the waste tubing slightly kinked behind the analyzer, preventing normal waste drainage."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Emptied the waste container per lab biohazard procedure, corrected tubing routing, cleared the error, and had lab staff verify analyzer operation with required checks."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact fluid or waste error message displayed
  • Whether sheath fluid was low, empty, expired, or recently replaced
  • Reagent lot, expiration, and correct container placement
  • Waste container level and condition
  • Whether waste tubing was kinked, blocked, or disconnected
  • Any visible leaks, residue, odor, or fluid under the analyzer
  • Whether air bubbles were seen in external tubing
  • Whether user-level prime, rinse, or fluid initialization was performed
  • Any abnormal pump, valve, or clicking sounds
  • Whether startup checks, background checks, or QC passed afterward
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed from service

Final Thought

Fluid management errors on the Sysmex UF-1000i should be approached logically and safely. Start with supply levels, waste condition, tubing, caps, and recent container changes before suspecting internal failure. If the error repeats after external causes are corrected, remove the analyzer from service and escalate for repair. Clear CCR documentation helps protect patient results and supports accurate follow-up.

That is successful troubleshooting.
