Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x2006 EEPROM Write Error

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0x2006 EEPROM Write Error indicating a failure to store system settings, which can lead to lost configurations, inconsistent behavior, or unreliable operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use if critical functions or settings are affected
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error 0x2006 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup, during adjustments, intermittently)
  • Check for loss of saved settings or unexpected resets

3. Perform a Full Power Reset

  • Unplug AC power from the bed
  • Remove battery if externally accessible
  • Wait at least 60 seconds
  • Restore power and reboot the bed

4. Check Power Stability

  • Verify the bed is connected to a known-good outlet
  • Test the outlet with another device
  • Inspect power cord for damage or loose connection
  • Watch for flickering power or resets

5. Inspect External Connections

  • Check visible cables and harnesses for damage or looseness
  • Ensure no cables are pinched or stressed
  • Reseat accessible connections if needed

6. Evaluate Configuration Retention

  • Change a setting on the bed (if accessible)
  • Power cycle the bed
  • Verify whether the setting is retained after reboot

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0x2006 continues after reset, power checks, and connection verification:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • The issue indicates an internal EEPROM or control board failure
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Replace the System Control Board (SCB) or send for repair

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before addressing EEPROM or system memory issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x2006 and not saving settings."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"EEPROM write failure confirmed after power reset and stable power verified."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and replaced System Control Board."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

EEPROM write errors indicate the device cannot reliably store critical configuration data. After ruling out power and connection issues, prompt escalation ensures patient safety and prevents unreliable operation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
