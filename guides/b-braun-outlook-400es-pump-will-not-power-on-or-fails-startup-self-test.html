B. Braun Outlook 400ES

Pump Will Not Power On or Fails Startup Self-Test

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

B. Braun

Model

Outlook 400ES

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting no-power, failed startup, battery, AC power, and startup self-test issues on the B. Braun Outlook 400ES infusion pump.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

If the pump is connected to a patient, do not troubleshoot it while therapy is active.

Transfer the infusion to another approved pump according to clinical policy before continuing.

Expected outcome: The patient is safely supported by another infusion device.

Why it matters: A pump that will not power on or cannot pass startup checks should not be trusted for medication or fluid delivery.

Verify the Reported Problem

Press the power button and observe the pump behavior.

Check whether the pump:

  • Does not respond at all
  • Starts and shuts down
  • Displays an error during startup
  • Fails a self-test
  • Only works on AC power
  • Only works on battery power
  • Beeps but does not complete startup

Expected outcome: The failure mode is confirmed before parts or accessories are blamed.

Check the AC Outlet

Plug another approved device or outlet tester into the same wall outlet.

Expected outcome: The outlet provides proper power.

If the outlet is dead, damaged, loose, or controlled by a wall switch, move the pump to a known-good outlet and retest.

If the pump powers on normally, stop troubleshooting and document the outlet issue.

Inspect the Power Cord

Check the power cord for cuts, bent prongs, loose strain relief, cracked insulation, or signs of overheating.

Confirm the cord is fully seated at the pump and wall outlet.

Expected outcome: The cord is intact and securely connected.

If the cord is damaged, remove it from use and replace it with an approved compatible cord.

Try a Known-Good Power Cord

Connect the Outlook 400ES to AC power using a known-good compatible power cord.

Expected outcome: The pump powers on and begins normal startup.

If the pump powers on with the replacement cord, the original cord was the likely cause. Stop and document the replacement.

Check for Charging or AC Power Indicators

With the pump connected to AC power, observe whether any charging, AC, or display indicator appears.

Expected outcome: The pump shows some sign that external power is present.

If there is no indication at all, the issue may involve the power cord, inlet, fuse, internal power supply, or battery/power management circuit.

Inspect the AC Power Inlet

Visually inspect the pump’s power inlet for looseness, cracks, bent contacts, debris, liquid residue, or burn marks.

Do not insert tools into the inlet.

Expected outcome: The inlet is clean, secure, and undamaged.

If the inlet is loose, damaged, discolored, or smells burnt, remove the pump from service and send it for repair.

Check Battery Condition

If the pump shows signs of life only when connected to AC power, allow it to charge per facility process and retest startup.

Expected outcome: The pump starts normally after charging.

If the pump shuts down immediately when unplugged, does not retain charge, or reports a battery-related failure, the battery may be depleted, aged, disconnected, or failed.

Test Startup on AC Power

Connect the pump to a known-good outlet using a known-good power cord.

Power the pump on and allow it to complete startup without pressing other buttons.

Expected outcome: The pump completes its startup self-test without alarm or shutdown.

If the pump passes startup on AC power, document the result and continue with required functional checks before returning it to service.

Test Startup on Battery Power

After confirming the pump operates on AC power, disconnect AC power and attempt startup on battery power if facility policy allows.

Expected outcome: The pump starts and remains powered on using battery.

If it fails only on battery, suspect battery condition or battery power path rather than a general no-power issue.

Check for Stuck or Damaged Keypad Buttons

Inspect the power button and front keypad area for sticking, cracking, fluid residue, or physical damage.

Expected outcome: Buttons move normally and are not stuck.

A stuck key can cause startup failure, self-test failure, or abnormal boot behavior.

If keypad damage or fluid intrusion is suspected, remove the pump from service.

Inspect for Fluid Intrusion or Physical Damage

Check the case, display, keypad, power inlet, battery area, pole clamp area, and seams for cracks, impact damage, residue, corrosion, or signs of cleaning-fluid entry.

Expected outcome: No evidence of damage or contamination is found.

If fluid intrusion, impact damage, smoke smell, unusual heat, or corrosion is present, do not continue operational testing. Remove the pump from service.

Note Any Startup Error Message

If the pump powers on but fails startup self-test, record the exact displayed message, alarm tone, or failure code.

Expected outcome: The failure is documented clearly for bench evaluation or vendor support.

Do not clear or ignore repeated startup self-test failures unless the cause is confirmed and corrected.

Power Cycle Once After External Checks

Disconnect AC power, wait briefly, reconnect to known-good AC power, and attempt startup again.

Expected outcome: The pump completes startup normally.

If the same failure returns, treat it as a repeatable device fault rather than a one-time startup interruption.

Perform Required Safety and Functional Checks Before Return to Service

If the issue was corrected by outlet change, power cord replacement, charging, or cleaning of an external obstruction, perform the facility-required inspection, electrical safety check if applicable, battery check, and basic pump operational verification.

Expected outcome: The Outlook 400ES passes required checks before being returned to clinical use.

If it cannot pass startup self-test or any required verification, do not return it to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the B. Braun Outlook 400ES still will not power on or continues to fail startup self-test after outlet, cord, battery, keypad, connection, and visible damage checks, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as a failed power supply, battery charging circuit, internal connection, keypad/display assembly issue, or main control electronics fault.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A pump that fails startup self-test should not be returned to patient care until it has been evaluated, repaired, and verified according to facility procedure.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot an infusion pump while it is delivering therapy to a patient. Move the infusion to another approved pump first, especially when the issue involves power, startup, battery operation, alarms, or self-test failure. Maintain therapy continuity by using a verified backup device before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the B. Braun Outlook 400ES infusion pump would not power on and failed to complete startup when connected to AC power."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Troubleshooting confirmed the wall outlet and power cord were functional, but the pump showed no reliable startup response and failed repeated power-on checks."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Pump was removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation due to suspected internal power or startup self-test failure."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result
  • Known-good power cord used
  • Whether AC power indicator appeared
  • Battery charging behavior
  • Startup error message or alarm behavior
  • Whether the pump worked on AC, battery, both, or neither
  • Display behavior during startup
  • Any stuck keypad buttons
  • Any signs of fluid intrusion
  • Unusual heat, smell, smoke, or discoloration
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power and startup failures on infusion pumps should be handled carefully because they directly affect therapy reliability. Start with simple external causes, verify AC power and accessories, observe the exact startup behavior, and escalate when the pump cannot safely complete startup. Clear CCR documentation helps repair teams identify whether the failure is external, battery-related, or likely internal.

That is successful troubleshooting.
