Cardinal Health Kendall SCD 700 Series

Error: E8 Vent Error

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error E8 Vent Error where garment pressure remains above 20 mmHg after the venting phase, preventing proper deflation and disrupting normal compression therapy cycles.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the device from active patient use if sleeves are not inflating or deflating properly
  • Transfer therapy to a known working SCD unit

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error E8 Vent Error is displayed
  • Observe if the error occurs after a compression cycle
  • Check if garments remain partially inflated
  • Note if deflation is slow or incomplete

Check Tubing for Kinks or Occlusions

  • Inspect all tubing from device to garments
  • Straighten any kinks or bends
  • Ensure tubing is not pinched under bed rails or mattress
  • Disconnect and reconnect tubing securely

Inspect Garment Application and Fit

  • Ensure sleeves are snug but not overly tight
  • Avoid loose application that may trap air pockets
  • Reapply garments correctly

Check for Obstructions or Contamination

  • Inspect tubing connectors and ports for debris or moisture
  • Look for lint, fluid, or blockages
  • Clean or replace tubing if needed

Test with Known-Good Accessories

  • Replace tubing set with a known-good one
  • Try a different garment sleeve

Power Cycle the Device

  • Turn the device off
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power back on and restart therapy

If the Problem Persists

If Error E8 Vent Error continues after all external checks and accessory swaps are completed, internal venting or pressure control components are likely faulty.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot compression issues on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup device before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Device displaying Error E8 Vent Error. Garments not fully deflating after compression cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Kinked tubing causing restricted airflow and inability to vent pressure below 20 mmHg."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced tubing set, verified proper garment fit, and confirmed normal operation. Device returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error E8 Vent Error reflects a real airflow and pressure release issue. Focus on tubing, garment fit, and airflow path first, then escalate if venting cannot be restored through external corrections.

That is successful troubleshooting.
