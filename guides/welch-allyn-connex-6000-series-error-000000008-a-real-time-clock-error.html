Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000008 A Real-Time Clock Error

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000008 indicating RTC failure causing incorrect date/time, logging issues, or startup errors affecting patient data accuracy.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use if time/date accuracy affects documentation or EMR integration.
  • Expected: Patient safely transferred to another monitor.
  • Why it matters: Incorrect timestamps can compromise patient records and clinical decisions.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error #000000008 appears. Observe if:
  • Time/date resets on power cycle
  • Device shows incorrect or default time
  • Error appears at startup or intermittently
  • Expected: Error is reproducible and timekeeping is incorrect.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true RTC-related fault versus a transient glitch.

3. Check Date and Time Settings

  • Navigate to system settings and verify current date and time. Attempt to manually set the correct values.
  • Expected: Device accepts and retains updated date/time.
  • Why it matters: Misconfiguration can mimic RTC failure.
  • If settings correct and hold → return to service
  • If settings reset after reboot → continue

4. Perform a Power Reset

  • Power down the unit, unplug from AC power, and remove battery (if accessible). Wait 60 seconds, then restore power and restart.
  • Expected: Device boots normally and retains correct time.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary faults affecting system clock behavior.
  • If resolved → return to service
  • If error persists → continue

5. Check Battery Status (External Check Only)

  • Reconnect AC power and ensure the device battery is installed and charging properly (check battery indicator/status).
  • Expected: Battery is recognized and charging normally.
  • Why it matters: Some RTC functions rely on internal backup power stability.
  • If battery not detected or faulty → replace battery and retest
  • If battery is good → continue

6. Check for Recent Power Loss or Storage Conditions

  • Ask if the device was:
  • Stored without power for an extended period
  • Fully discharged for a long time
  • Expected: Normal usage conditions.
  • Why it matters: Extended loss of power can reset RTC and trigger errors.
  • If yes → recharge fully, set time, and retest
  • If no or issue persists → continue

7. Inspect for External Signs of Damage

  • Check for signs of:
  • Liquid intrusion
  • Physical impact
  • Corrosion around battery compartment
  • Expected: No visible damage.
  • Why it matters: RTC circuitry can be affected by environmental damage.
  • If damage found → remove from service
  • If no damage → continue

If the Problem Persists

If the error continues after verifying settings, power reset, and battery condition:

External causes have been ruled out

Persistent RTC errors strongly indicate internal clock circuit or internal backup battery failure

Action:

Remove device from service

Label as Out of Service

Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a monitor with incorrect time/date—this can lead to inaccurate charting, trending errors, and EMR documentation issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying Error #000000008 (real-time clock error). Time/date not retaining after power cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"RTC failure suspected after confirming settings reset, power cycle ineffective, and battery functioning properly."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

RTC errors may seem minor but directly impact patient data integrity. Always verify timekeeping first, rule out power-related causes, and escalate appropriately when persistence indicates internal failure.

That is successful troubleshooting.
