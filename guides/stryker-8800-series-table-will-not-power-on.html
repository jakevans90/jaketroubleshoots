Stryker 8800 Series

Table Will Not Power On

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

8800 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of no power, including power source, cord, battery, and connection issues before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

Confirm the table is not in use with a patient. If in use, safely transfer the patient to another table before proceeding.

2. Check Main Power Source

  • Verify the wall outlet is live using a known working device.
  • Ensure any circuit breakers controlling the outlet are on.

Expected Outcome: Outlet provides proper voltage.

3. Inspect Power Cord

  • Check for visible damage, frays, or kinks.
  • Ensure cord is fully seated in both the table and wall outlet.

Expected Outcome: Table powers on or shows initial LED indicators.

4. Verify Battery Condition (if equipped)

  • Inspect battery charge level and connections.
  • If battery is removable, reseat it.
  • Attempt powering table from AC while battery is installed.

Expected Outcome: Table powers on or shows charging indicator.

5. Check Table’s Main Power Switch

  • Ensure power switch is in the ON position.
  • Toggle the switch off and on to test.

Expected Outcome: LEDs illuminate and control panel becomes active.

6. Examine Fuses and Circuit Protection

  • Check accessible fuses for continuity.
  • Replace blown fuses with correct rating only.

Expected Outcome: Table responds to power.

7. Test Control Panel Indicators

  • Look for any error lights or messages.
  • Document unusual indicators for service escalation.

Expected Outcome: Normal startup sequence begins.

If the Problem Persists

External power and connection issues have been ruled out.

  • Remove the table from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send for vendor repair or in-house bench evaluation.

Do not continue attempting to power on, as this could damage electronics or compromise patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot power issues while a patient is on the table. Always ensure patient safety by transferring them first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Table will not power on in the OR."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Power cord and outlet verified; table does not respond to main power or battery input."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Table removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested with multimeter or known device
  • Power cord inspected and reseated
  • Battery charge level checked
  • Switch and fuses inspected
  • Any LED indicators or error codes noted
  • Final table status documented

Final Thought

Patient safety and logical troubleshooting are paramount. Start with external checks before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation ensures accountability and efficient repair workflow.

That is successful troubleshooting.
