Karl Storz Image1 S

Image quality degradation

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Karl Storz

Model

Image1 S

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses causes of blurry, distorted, or low-contrast images from the camera system, ensuring optimal visualization during procedures.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the system is not in use on an active patient. Move the procedure to a backup camera system if necessary.
  • Why: Prevents patient risk while troubleshooting.

Check Camera Head and Lens

  • Inspect the camera head lens and light guide for debris, condensation, or scratches. Clean with approved lens wipes.
  • Expected Outcome: Clear lens improves image quality.

Verify Cable Connections

  • Ensure camera head, coupler, and hub connections are secure. Disconnect and reconnect each cable.
  • Expected Outcome: Resolves signal issues that can cause image artifacts or flickering.

Check Light Source Intensity

  • Confirm the light source is functioning at proper intensity. Adjust settings as needed.
  • Why: Insufficient illumination often causes low-contrast or dark images.

Inspect Hub / Processor Settings

  • Verify image processing modes (HD/SD, color settings, brightness, contrast) are correct for the procedure.
  • Expected Outcome: Correct settings restore normal image appearance.

Swap Cables or Camera Head if Available

  • Use a known-good camera head and cables to test image quality.
  • Expected Outcome: Determines if the issue is with the camera head, hub, or cabling.

Restart System

  • Power down the hub, camera head, and light source. Wait 30 seconds and power back on.
  • Expected Outcome: Resolves temporary glitches in the imaging system.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out.

Remove the device from service, label Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Note: Escalating at this point avoids further procedural delays and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot on an active patient; always move the patient to an alternate system first.

Monitor light intensity carefully; prolonged low illumination can compromise visualization.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Surgeon reports blurry and low-contrast image during laparoscopic procedure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Camera head lens slightly contaminated and light source intensity below standard."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned lens, verified light source, adjusted hub image settings; image quality restored."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Lens cleanliness and inspection result
  • Cable condition and connection integrity
  • Light source intensity reading
  • Hub settings (brightness, contrast, processing mode)
  • Image quality after each troubleshooting step

Final Thought

Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures patient safety and minimizes unnecessary equipment downtime. Thorough documentation aids future maintenance and supports cost avoidance. Escalation is appropriate once external causes are ruled out.

That is successful troubleshooting.
