Midmark 630 / 641 Series

Upholstery Damage Affecting Positioning / Support

Asset Type

Procedure Chair

Manufacturer

Midmark

Model

630 / 641 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Damaged, torn, or compressed upholstery causing poor patient support, instability, or improper positioning during procedures.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the chair from active patient use before inspection.
  • Expected: Patient safely relocated to another chair.
  • Why it matters: Damaged upholstery can compromise patient stability and increase risk of injury or infection.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Inspect the affected area (seat, backrest, armrests, or leg section) for tears, compression, or separation.
  • Expected: Visible damage or loss of cushioning integrity.
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is cosmetic or affecting support and positioning.

Assess Severity of Upholstery Damage

  • Check for:
  • Surface cracks or minor tears
  • Deep tears exposing foam
  • Flattened or uneven cushioning
  • Expected: Clear understanding of damage level.
  • Why it matters: Minor cosmetic issues may be manageable; structural cushion failure impacts support.
  • If minor surface wear only → continue to monitor
  • If foam is exposed or cushioning compromised → proceed to next steps

Check Cushion Integrity and Support

  • Apply light pressure to the damaged area and compare with unaffected sections.
  • Expected: Even resistance and cushioning across all sections.
  • Why it matters: Uneven or collapsed foam affects patient positioning and comfort.
  • If uneven or collapsed → upholstery replacement required

Inspect Upholstery Attachment Points

  • Check seams, edges, and mounting points for separation or loosening.
  • Expected: Upholstery securely attached with no lifting or shifting.
  • Why it matters: Loose upholstery can shift during use, affecting positioning and safety.
  • If loose → do not attempt repair in field; escalate for replacement

Evaluate Infection Control Risk

  • Determine if damage creates a surface that cannot be properly cleaned (cracks, exposed foam).
  • Expected: Smooth, intact, cleanable surface.
  • Why it matters: Breached surfaces harbor contaminants and violate infection control standards.
  • If not cleanable → remove from service immediately

Check for Associated Mechanical Impact

  • Ensure upholstery damage is not interfering with movement (hinges, articulation points).
  • Expected: Full range of motion without binding or obstruction.
  • Why it matters: Torn material can catch in mechanisms and worsen damage.
  • If interference present → remove from service

If the Problem Persists

If upholstery damage affects support, positioning, or cleanability, external causes have been ruled out. The issue is structural and requires repair.

  • Remove the chair from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for upholstery replacement or vendor repair

Knowing when to stop prevents unsafe patient use and further equipment damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not use chairs with compromised upholstery on patients. Always transfer the patient to a safe, intact surface before troubleshooting or evaluation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported torn seat upholstery causing patient discomfort and instability during procedures."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Seat cushion upholstery torn with exposed foam and loss of structural support."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed chair from service and sent for upholstery replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Location of damage (seat, backrest, armrest, leg section)
  • Depth of tear (surface vs exposed foam)
  • Cushion firmness comparison
  • Infection control concern noted
  • Any interference with movement
  • Final device status (in service vs out of service)

Final Thought

Upholstery issues may appear cosmetic but often impact patient safety, positioning, and infection control. Address external condition first, and escalate promptly when structural integrity is compromised. Proper documentation ensures accountability and continuity of care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
