ICU Medical Plum 360

Alarm: Data was cleared. Re-enter all programming.

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the alarm indicating cleared programming data, resulting in loss of infusion settings and requiring full re-entry before therapy can safely resume.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the infusion immediately if active
  • Transfer therapy to a backup infusion pump if needed

Expected: Patient continues receiving prescribed therapy safely

Why it matters: Loss of programming removes all infusion parameters, making delivery unsafe

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the pump
  • Confirm the alarm message is displayed
  • Check if all infusion settings are blank or reset
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup or during use)

Expected: Alarm is present and programming is cleared

Why it matters: Confirms true data loss versus intentional clearing

3. Acknowledge Alarm and Attempt Reprogramming

  • Clear the alarm
  • Re-enter infusion parameters (rate, VTBI, etc.)
  • Attempt to start infusion

Expected: Pump accepts programming and operates normally

Why it matters: Determines if issue was a one-time event

4. Check Power Stability

  • Ensure connection to a known good AC outlet
  • Verify battery charge level
  • Check for recent unplugging or power interruption

Expected: Stable and uninterrupted power supply

Why it matters: Power disruptions can cause memory corruption

5. Inspect for Repeated Data Loss

  • Power cycle the pump
  • Check if alarm reappears
  • Verify if programmed data is retained after restart

Expected: Programming remains intact after restart

Why it matters: Repeated clearing suggests internal fault

6. Check for Environmental or Handling Factors

  • Inspect for fluid exposure or contamination
  • Check for physical damage
  • Review exposure to extreme temperatures

Expected: No environmental damage present

Why it matters: External conditions can impact internal electronics

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues or programming does not retain after power cycles:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Internal memory or control board failure is likely

Remove the device from service, label it as Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or reprogram a pump while connected to a patient. Always transition to a backup device before making adjustments.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displayed 'Data was cleared. Re-enter all programming.' and all infusion settings were lost."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Programming cleared repeatedly after power cycle despite stable power source, indicating internal memory fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump from service and sent to Biomed/vendor for evaluation and repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Programming loss alarms directly impact patient therapy accuracy and safety. Always verify settings, confirm stability, and escalate repeated failures promptly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
