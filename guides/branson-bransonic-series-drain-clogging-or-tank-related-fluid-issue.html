Branson Bransonic Series

Drain Clogging or Tank-Related Fluid Issue

Asset Type

Ultrasonic Cleaner

Manufacturer

Branson

Model

Bransonic Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting drain clogs, slow draining, leaking, standing fluid, and tank-related issues on Branson Bransonic ultrasonic cleaners.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Verify the ultrasonic cleaner is not in active use and that no instruments or contaminated items are being processed.

Turn the unit off and unplug it before checking the tank, drain, hose, or exterior plumbing.

Expected outcome: The unit is safe to inspect without electrical, splash, or contamination risk.

Why it matters: Ultrasonic cleaners combine liquid, heat, and electrical components. Troubleshooting should not be performed while the unit is energized or clinically needed.

Confirm the Reported Fluid Issue

Ask staff what was observed:

  • Tank will not drain
  • Tank drains slowly
  • Fluid backs up into the tank
  • Fluid leaks near the drain
  • Fluid remains in the tank after draining
  • Tank appears stained, pitted, or contaminated
  • Drain valve is stiff, loose, or not closing fully

Expected outcome: The exact complaint is confirmed before troubleshooting begins.

Check the Tank for Debris or Instrument Obstruction

Inspect the tank for loose items, instrument fragments, labels, packaging, gauze, plastic, broken glass, or heavy sediment.

Remove any debris using appropriate PPE and facility cleaning precautions. Do not use sharp tools that could scratch or puncture the tank.

Expected outcome: The tank is free of visible debris.

If debris removal restores normal draining, clean the tank, test drain flow, document the finding, and stop.

Verify Fluid Level and Solution Condition

Check whether the tank is overfilled, filled with heavy residue, or loaded with solution that has excessive debris or buildup.

Drain and refill according to facility procedure if the solution is heavily contaminated or appears thick, cloudy, or full of solids.

Expected outcome: Fluid condition is suitable for normal draining and cleaning operation.

Why it matters: Heavy debris or residue can settle near the drain opening and cause repeated clogging.

Inspect the Drain Opening Inside the Tank

Look at the drain outlet from inside the tank. Check for visible blockage, mineral buildup, instrument debris, tape, labels, or residue around the drain screen or opening.

Clear only accessible debris. Do not force tools into the drain path.

Expected outcome: The drain opening is visibly clear.

If the drain begins flowing normally after clearing visible debris, run a controlled drain test and return the unit to service if no leaks are present.

Check the External Drain Hose or Plumbing Connection

Inspect the drain hose, tubing, or connection point if the unit is connected to external drainage.

Verify that the hose is:

  • Not kinked
  • Not pinched behind the unit
  • Not routed uphill
  • Not submerged in standing fluid
  • Not clogged with debris
  • Securely attached to the drain fitting

Expected outcome: The external drain path allows fluid to flow freely.

If correcting the hose route resolves the issue, test normal draining and stop.

Check the Drain Valve Operation

Open and close the drain valve through its normal range of motion.

Check whether the valve feels stuck, loose, gritty, or misaligned. Confirm that staff are using the correct valve position for draining.

Expected outcome: The valve opens fully, closes fully, and does not leak when closed.

If the valve does not move correctly or leaks when closed, remove the unit from service for repair evaluation.

Perform a Controlled Drain Test

With the unit unplugged and positioned safely, add a small amount of clean water to the tank if appropriate.

Open the drain and observe whether water drains smoothly without backing up, leaking, or pooling under the unit.

Expected outcome: Water drains at a steady rate through the intended drain path.

If the unit drains normally, dry the exterior, verify there are no leaks, and return it to service.

Inspect for External Leakage

Check around the drain fitting, valve area, underside, rear panel, and nearby countertop.

Look for:

  • Drips during draining
  • Fluid under the unit
  • Wet insulation or inaccessible internal areas
  • Corrosion around fittings
  • Cracks or deformation near the drain

Expected outcome: No fluid escapes outside the intended drain path.

If leakage is present from the unit body, drain assembly, or underside, remove the unit from service.

Inspect the Tank Surface

Visually inspect the tank for cracks, dents, pitting, staining, corrosion, or signs of improper cleaning chemicals.

Do not place the unit back into service if the tank appears compromised or if fluid may be entering internal areas.

Expected outcome: Tank condition is acceptable for safe use.

Why it matters: A compromised tank can leak into electrical components or create cleaning-performance and infection-control concerns.

Verify Proper Basket and Accessory Use

Confirm staff are using the correct basket or tray and are not placing instruments directly on the tank bottom.

Expected outcome: Instruments are supported properly and not blocking the drain or damaging the tank.

If improper loading caused the issue, educate staff, document the finding, and return the device to service after successful testing.

Confirm Final Operation

After corrections, verify:

  • Tank fills normally
  • Drain opens and closes properly
  • Fluid drains completely
  • No leaks are present
  • Tank is clean and free of obstruction
  • Unit powers on normally after exterior is dry

Expected outcome: The Bransonic cleaner is safe to return to service.

If the Problem Persists

If the drain remains clogged, the valve does not operate properly, fluid leaks from the unit, or the tank appears damaged, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue repeated draining attempts if fluid may be entering the unit or if the tank or drain assembly appears compromised. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a Bransonic ultrasonic cleaner while contaminated instruments are being processed. Ensure staff have another approved cleaning method or another ultrasonic cleaner available before removing the unit from service. Move instruments or processing needs to a backup device first so therapy continuity and instrument-processing workflow are maintained.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported the Branson Bransonic ultrasonic cleaner would not drain completely after instrument cleaning cycles."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found debris buildup at the tank drain opening and a partially kinked external drain hose."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleared accessible debris, corrected drain hose routing, performed a controlled drain test, and verified normal draining with no external leakage."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the unit was unplugged before inspection
  • Fluid level and solution condition
  • Visible debris in tank
  • Drain opening condition
  • Drain valve operation
  • External hose condition
  • Whether hose was kinked or routed uphill
  • Leak location, if present
  • Tank condition: stains, pitting, cracks, corrosion, dents
  • Whether accessories or baskets were being used correctly
  • Power behavior after the exterior was dry
  • Alarm behavior or indicator lights, if present
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Drain and tank-related issues should be approached with patient safety, contamination control, and electrical safety in mind. Start with visible debris, fluid condition, hose routing, and valve operation before suspecting internal failure. If leakage, tank damage, or drain assembly failure is found, remove the unit from service and document the work clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
