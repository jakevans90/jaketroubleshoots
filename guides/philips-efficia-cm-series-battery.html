Philips Efficia CM Series Patient Monitor

Battery Not Charging

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

Efficia CM Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the Efficia CM Series monitor battery is not charging. Symptoms may include:

  • Battery icon not increasing when monitor is plugged in
  • Monitor does not run on battery after being unplugged
  • Battery alarms or warnings
  • Monitor only operates when connected to AC power

The focus is on ruling out external and easily correctable issues before assuming internal battery or charger failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify AC Power Source

  • Ensure the outlet is live using a known working device.
  • Check that the monitor is plugged in securely.

Why: A monitor won’t charge if AC power is not available or the plug is loose.

Inspect Power Cord and Adapter

  • Look for frayed wires, bent pins, or loose connections.
  • If possible, try a known working Philips power cord/adapter.

Why: Damaged cords or adapters are a common cause of battery charging issues.

Check Battery Insertion

  • Remove and reseat the battery, ensuring it clicks into place.
  • Confirm there is no debris or corrosion on the contacts.

Why: Poor connection prevents charging.

Monitor Indicator Review

  • Check the monitor’s battery indicator and any on-screen messages.
  • Observe if the charging icon appears when AC is connected.

Why: Confirms whether the monitor recognizes the battery and charging status.

Perform a Monitor Restart

  • Power cycle the monitor while connected to AC power.

Why: Some software glitches can temporarily prevent battery charging.

Test with a Spare Battery

  • If available, insert a known good battery to determine if the issue is the battery itself.

Why: Helps differentiate between battery failure and monitor charging circuitry issues.

If the Problem Persists

All external checks have been performed. The battery may be faulty or the monitor’s internal charging circuitry may be failing.

  • Remove the monitor from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send the monitor and battery to repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not rely on a monitor with a non-charging battery for patient transport or bedside use.

Move the patient to another functional monitor before troubleshooting if the battery is critical.

Only attempt troubleshooting when it is safe for patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Monitor battery not charging; monitor only works on AC power.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Battery contacts clean and properly seated; AC power verified; monitor does not recognize battery. Spare battery tested with same result.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent to biomedical repair for battery or charging circuitry evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested with another device
  • Power cord swapped or inspected
  • Battery contacts inspected
  • Charging icon/LED behavior
  • Battery voltage if accessible
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Follow a logical, stepwise troubleshooting approach and avoid guessing at internal repairs. Document all findings clearly and escalate when external checks do not resolve the problem. Proper documentation ensures repeatable, safe, and efficient resolution.

That is successful troubleshooting.
