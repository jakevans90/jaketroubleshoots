Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x2001 Audio Codec Driver Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses Error 0x2001 Audio Codec Driver Failure, which indicates a failure in the bed’s audio system. This can result in loss of audible alarms, alerts, and feedback tones, creating a potential patient safety risk if critical alerts are not heard.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from active patient use if audible alarms are not functioning
  • Transfer the patient to a safe, fully functional bed if needed
  • Ensure alternate monitoring is in place

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error 0x2001 is displayed
  • Check for missing alarm sounds or button feedback tones
  • Observe if the error appears at startup or during use

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Disconnect AC power from the bed
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart the bed

Check External Connections and Accessories

  • Inspect pendant and side rail control connections
  • Look for damaged or pinched cables
  • Check for signs of fluid intrusion near controls

Inspect SCB ↔ MCB Cable Connections

  • Verify cables between the System Control Board (SCB) and Main Control Board (MCB) are secure
  • Reseat connectors if accessible
  • Inspect for bent pins or visible damage

Check SOM Board Seating

  • Verify the System on Module (SOM) board is properly seated if accessible
  • Reseat if loose or partially connected

Isolate to Internal Hardware

  • If the error persists after all checks, suspect internal SCB failure
  • Recognize that audio codec functionality is managed through internal hardware

If the Problem Persists

If all external connections, power cycling, and board seating checks are completed and the error remains, the issue is likely internal.

  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the System Control Board (SCB)

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a bed with disabled audio alarms while a patient is relying on it. Always move the patient to a backup device before continuing diagnostics.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x2001 with no audible alarms or tones."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Audio codec failure suspected after power cycle and cable reseating did not resolve issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated SCB connections and verified SOM seating. Error persisted. Bed removed from service and SCB replacement initiated."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Audio system failures directly impact patient safety by eliminating critical alerts. Always rule out simple external causes first, then escalate quickly when alarms are compromised. Clear documentation ensures accountability and efficient repair handling.

That is successful troubleshooting.
