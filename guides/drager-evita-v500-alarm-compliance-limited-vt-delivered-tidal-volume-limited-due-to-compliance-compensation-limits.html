Drager Evita V500

Alarm: Compliance limited VT delivered tidal volume limited due to compliance compensation limits

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Evita V500

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the “Compliance limited VT” alarm, which indicates that the ventilator is unable to deliver the set tidal volume due to circuit compliance or compensation limits. This can result in reduced effective ventilation and inaccurate volume delivery to the patient.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient’s ventilation status
  • If tidal volume delivery is inadequate, remove from ventilator
  • Provide alternate ventilation (bag-valve mask or backup ventilator)

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the ventilator is displaying “Compliance limited VT”
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (continuous or during inspiration)
  • Compare set VT vs delivered VT
  • Check for additional related alarms (low volume, pressure issues)

Check Breathing Circuit Connections

  • Inspect all connections from ventilator to patient
  • Ensure fittings are tight and fully seated
  • Look for partial disconnections

Inspect Circuit for Excess Compliance or Expansion

  • Check for overly flexible or worn tubing
  • Confirm correct circuit type is installed
  • Remove unnecessary extensions or add-ons

Check for Leaks in Circuit or Interfaces

  • Inspect tubing, humidifier, filters, and connectors
  • Check ET tube or mask seal
  • Listen for audible air leaks

Verify Ventilator Settings

  • Confirm set tidal volume is appropriate for patient
  • Review pressure limits and compliance compensation settings
  • Ensure settings are within realistic system capability

Run System Test (SST)

  • Perform system self-test per manufacturer procedure
  • Confirm test completes without errors

Swap Circuit if Needed

  • Replace with a known-good breathing circuit
  • Re-evaluate alarm condition

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes such as circuit issues, leaks, and settings have been ruled out, an internal compliance measurement or compensation failure is likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot compliance or volume delivery alarms on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup ventilator before making adjustments.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator alarming 'Compliance limited VT' with low delivered tidal volume during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Excess circuit compliance due to loose connection and worn tubing (or) no external issue found, suspected internal compliance compensation limitation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reseated connections and replaced breathing circuit; verified proper VT delivery and alarm cleared (or) removed from service after failed SST and persistent alarm."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Compliance-related alarms reflect real limitations in volume delivery. Always start with external components like circuits and connections before escalating to internal faults. Proper troubleshooting protects both patient safety and device reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
