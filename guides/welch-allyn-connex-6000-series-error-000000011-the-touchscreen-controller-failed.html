Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000011 The Touchscreen Controller Failed

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000011 indicating touchscreen controller failure causing unresponsive or inaccurate touch input, preventing normal device operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from active patient use if touchscreen input is required for operation.
  • Expected: Patient is safely transferred to another monitor.
  • Why it matters: Inability to interact with the device can delay or prevent patient assessment.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the device and confirm Error #000000011 appears. Observe:
  • Touchscreen not responding to input
  • Delayed or inaccurate touch response
  • Error appears at startup or during use
  • Expected: Error is reproducible and touchscreen functionality is impaired.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true touchscreen/controller failure rather than user interaction issue.

Inspect Screen Surface and Physical Condition

  • Check the touchscreen for:
  • Cracks, scratches, or visible damage
  • Fluid contamination, dirt, or residue
  • Presence of protective covers interfering with touch
  • Clean the screen using approved methods if needed.
  • Expected: Screen is clean, intact, and free of obstruction.
  • Why it matters: Contamination or overlays can mimic touchscreen failure.

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the device off, unplug from power (if applicable), wait 30–60 seconds, then restart.
  • Expected: Device reboots normally and touchscreen functionality is restored if issue was temporary.
  • Why it matters: Resets the touchscreen controller and clears transient faults.

Check for External Input Interference

  • Ensure:
  • No external USB devices or peripherals are connected that could interfere
  • No constant pressure on the screen (objects, mounting brackets, tight covers)
  • Expected: No external interference affecting touch input.
  • Why it matters: Continuous pressure or interference can cause controller faults.

Test Touchscreen Function Across the Display

  • Attempt interaction in multiple areas of the screen:
  • Menu navigation
  • Buttons at different corners
  • Different pressure levels
  • Expected: Either full failure or consistent dead zones are identified.
  • Why it matters: Helps determine if the issue is localized (screen damage) or controller-wide.

Evaluate Environmental Factors

  • Confirm the device is being used within normal conditions:
  • No excessive moisture or condensation
  • No extreme temperatures
  • Expected: Device is in a normal clinical environment.
  • Why it matters: Environmental factors can affect touchscreen performance and electronics.

Determine if Issue is Resolved

  • If touchscreen responds normally after cleaning, reset, or removing interference:
  • Return the device to service

If the Problem Persists

If Error #000000011 remains after all external checks:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • The error indicates a failure of the touchscreen controller or display interface

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to troubleshoot a touchscreen issue while the device is actively needed for patient care—transfer monitoring first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying Error #000000011 with unresponsive touchscreen preventing navigation and vital sign entry."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Troubleshooting ruled out contamination, external interference, and power reset; failure consistent with touchscreen controller fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Device removed from service, labeled out of service, and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Touchscreen-related errors directly impact usability and patient workflow. Always rule out simple causes first, but recognize that controller errors typically indicate hardware failure requiring escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
