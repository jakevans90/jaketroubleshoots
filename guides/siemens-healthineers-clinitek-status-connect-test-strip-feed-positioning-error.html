Siemens Healthineers CLINITEK Status Connect

Test Strip Feed / Positioning Error

Asset Type

Urine Analyzer

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

CLINITEK Status Connect

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting strip feed, strip placement, tray movement, and positioning errors before escalating for repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the analyzer is not actively processing a patient sample.

If testing is urgently needed, have staff use another available analyzer or approved backup testing method according to facility policy.

Expected outcome: Troubleshooting is performed only when no active patient result is at risk.

Confirm the Reported Error

Ask the user what happened when the error occurred.

Check whether the issue happens:

  • During strip insertion
  • During tray movement
  • Before reading
  • After the strip is placed
  • Intermittently with certain users or strip lots

Expected outcome: The failure is clearly linked to strip feed, strip position, or test table movement.

Remove the Analyzer From Active Testing

Stop additional patient testing until the strip feed or positioning issue is verified.

Expected outcome: No questionable urine results are produced while the analyzer may be misreading strip position.

Check for Improper Strip Placement

Verify the reagent strip is being placed correctly on the test table or strip holder area.

Confirm the strip is flat, aligned, fully seated, and not angled or hanging over the edge.

Expected outcome: A correctly placed strip should allow the analyzer to move and read without a positioning error.

If proper placement resolves the issue, document the finding and return the analyzer to service.

Inspect the Test Table / Strip Tray Externally

Remove the test table or strip tray only as allowed for normal operator cleaning.

Look for:

  • Dried urine residue
  • Crystals
  • Stray strip fragments
  • Paper towel fibers
  • Bent or damaged tray surfaces
  • Obstruction in the tray path

Expected outcome: The test table should be clean, smooth, and able to move normally.

Why it matters: Urine residue or debris can interfere with strip positioning and tray travel.

Clean the Test Table Area

Clean the test table, strip channel, and accessible exterior tray surfaces following facility-approved cleaning practices and manufacturer operator-level cleaning instructions.

Do not flood the analyzer, spray liquid into the instrument, or force fluid into openings.

Expected outcome: Residue is removed without damaging the analyzer.

If cleaning resolves the issue, run an appropriate QC or verification check before returning to service.

Check for Wet or Contaminated Strips

Verify the strips are not overly wet, dripping, curled, expired, contaminated, or exposed to humidity.

Confirm staff are blotting or handling strips according to the facility’s urine testing procedure.

Expected outcome: The strip should remain flat and readable during tray movement.

Why it matters: Excess sample or curled strips can shift during feed or reading.

Try a Fresh Strip From the Same Container

Use a new strip from the current container and place it correctly.

Expected outcome: If the analyzer works with a fresh strip, the original strip may have been damaged, over-wet, or mispositioned.

If the issue occurs repeatedly with the same container, continue troubleshooting.

Compare With Another Strip Lot or Container

If available, try a strip from a different valid container or lot approved for the analyzer.

Expected outcome: If the analyzer works with another strip container, suspect strip storage, lot condition, expiration, or contamination rather than analyzer failure.

Verify the Test Table Moves Freely

With the analyzer off and only within normal user-accessible handling limits, check that the test table is not visibly stuck, cracked, loose, or obstructed.

Do not force the tray or open the analyzer housing.

Expected outcome: The tray should not appear jammed, misaligned, or physically damaged.

Power Cycle the Analyzer

Turn the analyzer off, wait briefly, and power it back on.

Allow the analyzer to complete startup without touching the test table.

Expected outcome: The analyzer should initialize normally and return to ready status.

If the positioning error clears, perform a test or QC verification before returning to service.

Check Power Stability

Confirm the analyzer is connected to a stable outlet or approved power source.

Inspect the power supply connection for looseness, strain, or intermittent contact.

Expected outcome: The analyzer remains powered during tray movement and does not reset or stall.

Run QC or a Non-Patient Verification Test

After cleaning, strip replacement, or power cycling, perform the appropriate facility-approved QC or verification process.

Expected outcome: The analyzer completes the test cycle without feed, tray, or positioning errors.

If QC passes and no error returns, document and return the device to service.

Check for Repeated or Mechanical Symptoms

Listen and observe for:

  • Grinding
  • Clicking
  • Tray hesitation
  • Tray not extending or retracting
  • Tray moving unevenly
  • Recurring positioning errors after cleaning

Expected outcome: Repeated abnormal movement suggests an internal mechanical, sensor, or drive issue.

Stop Troubleshooting if the Error Persists

If the analyzer continues to show strip feed or positioning errors after proper strip placement, cleaning, strip replacement, power cycling, and QC verification, remove it from service.

Expected outcome: The device is escalated before unreliable testing can occur.

If the Problem Persists

Common external causes have been ruled out if the strip is properly placed, the test table is clean, strips are valid, power is stable, and the error still returns.

At that point, the issue is likely internal, such as a test table drive, position sensor, optical detection, or mechanical alignment problem.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not force the tray, bypass the error, or continue patient testing with repeated positioning failures.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot this issue while a patient sample is actively being processed. Strip positioning errors can affect result reliability. Move testing to another analyzer or approved backup method before continuing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the CLINITEK Status Connect displayed a test strip positioning error and would not complete urine strip testing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Test table area had dried urine residue and the strip was not seating flat during the read cycle."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned the test table and strip path, verified proper strip placement, ran QC successfully, and returned the analyzer to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact error message displayed
  • Whether the tray extended or retracted normally
  • Whether the strip was wet, curled, expired, or misaligned
  • Test strip lot and expiration date
  • Whether another strip container was tested
  • Whether the test table was cleaned
  • Any visible urine residue, crystals, or debris
  • Any unusual clicking, grinding, or tray hesitation
  • Power source checked
  • QC or verification result
  • Final device status

Final Thought

For a CLINITEK Status Connect strip feed or positioning issue, start with patient safety, strip placement, tray cleanliness, strip condition, and power stability. Most preventable failures involve residue, misalignment, or damaged strips. If the issue persists after external checks and QC verification, remove the analyzer from service and escalate for repair. Clear CCR documentation protects patient results and helps repair staff identify the failure pattern.

That is successful troubleshooting.
