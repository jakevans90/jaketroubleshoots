Philips DigitalDiagnost C90

X-Ray Generator Communication or Exposure Failure

Asset Type

Radiographic System

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

DigitalDiagnost C90

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting generator communication faults, failed exposure attempts, no X-ray output, or exposure-control problems before escalating for internal repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient and Radiation Safety First

Remove the room from clinical use until reliable exposure operation is confirmed. If a patient is present, have clinical staff move the patient to another appropriate radiographic system before troubleshooting.

Do not perform test exposures with a patient present. Any radiation-producing test must follow facility radiation-safety policy and be performed only by qualified personnel.

Expected outcome: No patient depends on a radiographic system with uncertain exposure capability.

Why it matters: The DigitalDiagnost C90 is designed for routine radiographic examinations, and an unresolved exposure-control or generator communication failure can prevent imaging or create an unsafe operating condition.

2. Confirm the Exact Failure Condition

Obtain the original complaint and observe the system startup and attempted workflow while the unit is out of patient service.

Document whether the problem is:

  • Generator communication error displayed at the workspot.
  • Exposure preparation begins but exposure does not occur.
  • Exposure hand switch does not initiate preparation or exposure.
  • System appears ready, but no image is acquired.
  • Failure is intermittent or occurs only with a specific detector, table, wall stand, or exam setup.
  • A specific error code or message is present.

Photograph or record the exact error message before restarting the system.

Expected outcome: The reported issue is clearly identified and repeatable, or the intermittent condition is documented for escalation.

3. Verify Room Power and System Startup

Confirm that the radiographic room has normal facility power and that all user-accessible system components complete startup normally.

Inspect for:

  • Dark or unresponsive acquisition workspot.
  • Abnormal startup messages.
  • Generator, detector, tube head, or room component not recognized.
  • UPS, breaker, or room power indication that suggests an external supply problem.

Do not open generator cabinets or reset internal breakers unless specifically authorized under your service procedures.

Expected outcome: The acquisition workspot and associated room components start normally without power-related indications.

If power restoration or a permitted reset resolves the issue: Verify safe system operation according to facility policy, document the result, and stop troubleshooting.

4. Check the Acquisition Workspot and Generator Communication Indications

Verify that the Eleva Workspot, which incorporates generator control, is responsive and displays the expected system-ready condition. The DigitalDiagnost C90 configuration includes an Eleva Workspot with integrated generator control and a hand switch for exposure release.

Check for:

  • Frozen or unresponsive user interface.
  • Generator shown as unavailable, disconnected, or not ready.
  • Repeating communication warning after startup.
  • Exam setup unable to progress to ready status.

Perform only the normal operator-accessible restart procedure permitted by facility practice. Do not bypass communication faults or attempt exposure while a generator warning remains active.

Expected outcome: The workspot recognizes the generator and allows the system to reach a normal ready condition.

If communication is restored and remains stable: Complete required operational verification and return the system only if it passes all safety requirements.

5. Inspect Accessible Exposure-Control Connections

With the system safely powered down or placed in the appropriate safe state, visually inspect accessible connections associated with exposure operation.

Check:

  • Exposure hand switch cable and connector for damage, strain, looseness, bent pins, or contamination.
  • Accessible workstation and peripheral cables for obvious disconnection or damage.
  • Any user-accessible detector or room-component cable involved in the failed workflow.
  • Signs of fluid intrusion, crushed cable jackets, exposed conductors, or damaged connectors.

Do not disconnect internal generator wiring or remove covers.

Expected outcome: External cables and connectors are secure and free of visible damage.

If a damaged accessory cable is identified: Remove the damaged accessory from service, replace it with an approved known-good equivalent when available, then retest according to facility policy.

6. Evaluate the Exposure Hand Switch

Inspect the hand switch for physical damage, sticking buttons, damaged strain relief, or intermittent operation.

Using a safe, non-patient workflow, determine whether:

  • The preparation stage responds when the hand switch is activated.
  • The exposure stage fails only when using that hand switch.
  • A known-good approved hand switch, when available and permitted, changes the behavior.

Expected outcome: The hand switch either functions normally or is identified as the likely external cause of the failed exposure request.

If replacement of a defective hand switch resolves the failure: Complete required exposure-performance verification before returning the room to service.

7. Confirm the Selected Exam and Detector Configuration

Verify that the selected examination, detector, and image receptor configuration are appropriate and recognized by the system.

Check for:

  • Detector not recognized or not ready.
  • Incorrect detector selected for the intended exposure.
  • Detector battery, wireless connection, backup cable, or docking condition preventing readiness, when applicable.
  • Failure occurring only at the table, wall stand, or free exposure configuration.

The DigitalDiagnost C90 may be configured with SkyPlate detectors and multiple room components; an exposure may be prevented when the intended acquisition path is not ready. Philips also identifies SkyPlate detector condition and backup cable checks as system maintenance items.

Expected outcome: The intended detector and exam pathway are properly recognized and ready for acquisition.

If the problem follows one detector or accessible detector cable: Remove that component from use and continue only with a verified functional configuration, when clinically and technically appropriate.

8. Inspect for Visible Conditions Preventing Exposure Readiness

Confirm that the system is not being prevented from reaching ready status by a visible external condition.

Check for:

  • Tube head, table, or wall stand not in the expected position for the selected exam.
  • Accessory, grid, detector, or bucky component not fully seated where applicable.
  • Active system warning requiring acknowledgement or correction.
  • Physical damage, collision evidence, unusual noise, overheating, odor, or fluid contamination.

Expected outcome: No visible mechanical, accessory, or readiness condition is preventing the requested exposure.

If any physical damage, unusual heat, odor, or evidence of collision is found: Stop immediately and keep the system out of service.

9. Perform a Controlled Non-Patient Exposure Verification Only When Authorized

Once external causes have been checked and the system reaches a normal ready condition, qualified personnel may perform the facility-approved non-patient exposure verification using appropriate radiation-safety controls.

Observe whether:

  • Preparation occurs normally.
  • Exposure is completed without generator communication error.
  • An image is successfully acquired.
  • The same fault immediately recurs.

Do not repeatedly expose in an attempt to clear an unresolved generator fault.

Expected outcome: A controlled verification exposure succeeds without repeating the fault.

If exposure succeeds and the system passes required quality and safety checks: Document the corrective action and return the system according to departmental procedure.

10. Review Preventive Maintenance Status and Recent Service History

Check whether the issue began after service, room power interruption, component replacement, detector change, software event, or intermittent communication complaint.

Verify whether generator and associated system checks are current. Philips lists generator functionality, cable connection, generator data backup, tube calibration, acquisition workspot functionality, and AWS remote connection checks within planned maintenance activities for the DigitalDiagnost C90.

Expected outcome: Relevant history is captured and overdue maintenance, recurring failures, or post-service patterns are identified.

If the exposure or communication failure remains unresolved: Stop troubleshooting and escalate.

If the Problem Persists

If power, accessible cables, hand switch, detector readiness, exam setup, visible system conditions, and authorized non-patient verification have been checked without resolution, common external causes have been ruled out.

The failure may involve internal generator operation, communication circuitry, generator control integration, internal cabling, tube/generator control, or system software requiring authorized service evaluation.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench/vendor evaluation

Do not bypass interlocks, repeatedly attempt exposures, open generator cabinets, or perform internal repair without appropriate authorization, training, and service documentation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an exposure failure while a patient is positioned for imaging or while the room is needed for active patient care. Move the patient to a functioning imaging system first, and perform any test exposure only under approved radiation-safety controls.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported that the Philips DigitalDiagnost C90 displayed a generator communication fault and would not complete an exposure from the Eleva Workspot."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified normal room power and detector recognition, but the exposure failure remained present after inspecting the accessible hand switch and external connections with no visible damage found."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the radiographic room from service, labeled it Out of Service, documented the displayed generator communication fault, and escalated the system for authorized Philips service evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Exact error message or code displayed
  • Whether failure occurred during preparation or exposure
  • Whether the exposure hand switch responded
  • Whether the detector was recognized and ready
  • Whether the problem occurred at table, wall stand, or free exposure setup
  • Workspot startup and generator-ready status
  • Accessible cables inspected or approved accessories swapped
  • Room power or UPS indication
  • Any unusual heat, sound, smell, damage, or collision history
  • Whether an authorized non-patient verification exposure was attempted
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A generator communication or exposure failure on a radiographic system must be handled with patient safety and radiation safety first. Logical external checks can identify simple causes, but unresolved exposure faults require prompt removal from service and authorized repair. Thorough CCR documentation supports safe follow-up and faster resolution.

That is successful troubleshooting.
