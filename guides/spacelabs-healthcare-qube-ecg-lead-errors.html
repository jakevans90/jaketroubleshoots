Spacelabs Healthcare Qube

ECG Lead Detection Errors / Waveform Issues

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Spacelabs Healthcare

Model

Qube

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a Spacelabs Qube patient monitor experiencing ECG lead detection errors or abnormal waveforms. Common signs include:

  • Missing or intermittent ECG waveforms
  • “Lead Off” or similar error messages
  • Erratic or noisy ECG traces

This guide focuses on verifying external connections and patient setup before considering internal device faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check patient connection

  • Verify all ECG electrodes are attached correctly to the patient.
  • Ensure skin is clean, dry, and free of oils or lotions.
  • Replace any old or dried electrodes.

Why: Poor electrode contact is the most common cause of lead detection errors.

Inspect ECG lead cables

  • Check for visible damage, frays, or broken connectors.
  • Ensure cables are fully seated at both the patient side and monitor connector.
  • Swap with a known good cable if available.

Why: Damaged leads or loose connections can mimic waveform or lead errors.

Confirm proper lead placement

  • Verify standard lead positions (RA, LA, RL, LL, V1-V6 if applicable) are correct.
  • Make sure limb leads are not crossed or touching each other.

Why: Misplacement can cause waveform distortion or lead-off errors.

Check monitor settings

  • Verify ECG mode (3-lead, 5-lead, or 12-lead) matches the applied electrode configuration.
  • Ensure filters are appropriately set (not excessively filtering small signals).

Why: Incorrect configuration can cause the device to misinterpret signals.

Observe for interference sources

  • Ensure no large electrical devices or patient-worn pumps are near the monitor leads.
  • Remove or reposition equipment if interference is suspected.

Why: Electrical noise can cause erratic or missing waveforms.

Power cycle the monitor

  • Turn the device off and back on to reset lead detection circuits.

Why: Software glitches may temporarily prevent proper lead detection.

If the Problem Persists

All external checks have been ruled out; the issue is likely internal to the Qube.

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to the Biomedical Engineering bench or authorized repair vendor.

Note: Stopping before internal repairs ensures patient safety and proper troubleshooting escalation.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never troubleshoot leads while the patient is unstable or actively monitored for arrhythmia.
  • Move the patient to a different monitor if continuous ECG monitoring is required.
  • Only reconnect or adjust leads when patient safety is ensured.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Nurse reports intermittent ECG waveform with “Lead Off” messages.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed dried electrodes and loose lead cable connections.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Replaced electrodes and reseated leads; verified normal waveforms.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Check if multiple leads are affected or just one
  • Document which cables were tested or swapped
  • Record waveform quality before and after intervention
  • Note any unusual noise or patient movement affecting the signal

Final Thought

Patient safety and proper lead placement are critical. Start with external connections and settings before assuming device failure. Clear documentation ensures a traceable troubleshooting path and appropriate escalation when necessary.

That is successful troubleshooting.
