Medtronic Valleylab FT10

Error: E329 Check Foot Pedal

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Valleylab FT10

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error E329 Check Foot Pedal on the Medtronic Valleylab FT10 electrosurgical unit. This error indicates invalid foot pedal input, which may prevent safe ESU activation or cause unreliable foot pedal control.

Because the foot pedal is an activation control, this condition should be treated as a patient safety issue until the pedal, cable, connector, and ESU response are confirmed safe.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Valleylab FT10 from patient use if the error appears during setup, activation, or a procedure.
  • Stop use of the ESU if activation control is unreliable.
  • Use another ESU if electrosurgery is clinically needed.
  • Do not continue troubleshooting on an active patient or during active energy delivery.

Patient care should be protected from unintended activation, failed activation, or unreliable foot pedal control.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the Valleylab FT10 and observe the display.
  • Confirm the message is Error E329 Check Foot Pedal Invalid Foot Pedal Input.
  • Ask staff whether the error occurred at startup, when the foot pedal was connected, when the pedal was pressed, during a procedure, or intermittently during use.
  • Check whether the error clears when the foot pedal is released.

This confirms a true system-reported foot pedal input fault instead of a setup issue or temporary activation condition.

Release the Foot Pedal and Check for Stuck Activation

  • Make sure no one is pressing the foot pedal.
  • Inspect the pedal for a stuck, wedged, or partially depressed switch.
  • Check under and around the pedal for cords, drapes, equipment, or debris holding the pedal down.
  • Gently press and release each pedal function to confirm normal mechanical movement.

If the error clears and does not return, complete facility-required function checks before returning the device to service.

Check the Foot Pedal Connection

  • Disconnect the foot pedal from the FT10.
  • Inspect the connector for bent pins, contamination, fluid residue, damage, or looseness.
  • Reconnect the pedal fully and securely.
  • Make sure the cable is not stretched, pulled, crushed, or routed where it can be stepped on.

A poor or damaged connection can cause the ESU to interpret the pedal signal incorrectly.

Inspect the Foot Pedal and Cable

  • Look over the full length of the foot pedal cable.
  • Check for cuts, crushed insulation, kinks, exposed conductors, strain relief damage, or fluid intrusion.
  • Inspect the pedal housing for cracks, sticky movement, missing parts, or signs of heavy impact.

The foot pedal is the most likely external source and should be ruled out before suspecting an internal ESU fault.

Swap with a Known-Good Compatible Foot Pedal

  • Remove the suspect foot pedal.
  • Connect a known-good compatible foot pedal approved for the Valleylab FT10.
  • Power cycle the ESU if needed and check whether Error E329 returns.
  • Test foot pedal activation only in a safe test setup, not on a patient.

If the error clears with a known-good pedal, replace the faulty foot pedal and return the ESU to service only after standard functional verification.

Check for Accessory or Port-Specific Behavior

  • Confirm whether the error occurs with only one foot pedal, one connector, or one mode of use.
  • Check whether the error appears immediately when the pedal is connected or only when pressed.
  • Verify that the correct foot pedal type is being used for the intended function.

A repeatable pattern helps separate an accessory issue from a device-side input fault.

Perform a Basic ESU Condition Check

  • Inspect the FT10 foot pedal port area for physical damage, contamination, or loose connection points.
  • Check that the unit powers on normally.
  • Confirm no other errors are displayed.
  • Listen for unusual sounds and check for odor, overheating, or visible damage.

Multiple symptoms may indicate a broader device issue rather than a simple foot pedal problem.

Determine Whether the Issue Is Resolved

  • If the error clears after releasing, reconnecting, or replacing the foot pedal, document the finding.
  • Complete normal safety and functional checks per facility procedure.
  • Return the ESU to service only if the error does not return and activation control is reliable.
  • If the error returns with a known-good foot pedal, remove the ESU from service and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Possible internal sources may include the steering relay PCBA or foot pedal PCBA.

If the Problem Persists

If Error E329 continues after the foot pedal is released, the connector is checked, and a known-good compatible foot pedal has been tested, external causes have been reasonably ruled out.

At that point, the condition may indicate a device-side input issue or internal hardware fault.

  • Remove the ESU from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or further bench evaluation.

Do not continue swapping accessories indefinitely once the error remains with the ESU. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an ESU foot pedal activation error on an active patient. Move the patient or procedure to a backup device first, then troubleshoot the affected Valleylab FT10 away from patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"User reported Medtronic Valleylab FT10 displaying Error E329 Check Foot Pedal Invalid Foot Pedal Input. Staff reported foot pedal activation issue during setup or use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Foot pedal input fault confirmed. Foot pedal and cable inspected. Error cleared after replacing suspect foot pedal with known-good compatible foot pedal."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced faulty foot pedal. Verified error no longer appears and foot pedal activation response is normal in test setup. Unit passed functional check and was returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error E329 should be approached as an activation-control safety issue. Start with the foot pedal, cable, connector, and setup before suspecting internal failure. If the error follows the pedal, replace the accessory. If it remains with the FT10 after a known-good pedal is tested, remove the unit from service and escalate appropriately.

Clear CCR documentation supports patient safety, repair follow-up, and a defensible troubleshooting process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
