Puritan Bennett 840

Alarm: VOLUME NOT DELIVERED Pressure exceeds allowable limits preventing delivery

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Puritan Bennett

Model

840

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: VOLUME NOT DELIVERED indicating target tidal volume not achieved due to high pressure limits or airway resistance.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient and ventilation status
  • Provide alternate ventilation if needed (bag valve mask or backup ventilator)

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the ventilator displays VOLUME NOT DELIVERED
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup vs during breaths)
  • Compare delivered tidal volume vs set tidal volume
  • Check peak pressure readings approaching or hitting limits

Check Patient Condition (Most Critical First Step)

  • Assess for sudden changes in lung compliance
  • Check for airway obstruction (secretions, biting tube, kinked ET tube)
  • Evaluate for bronchospasm or coughing

Inspect Patient Circuit for Obstructions or Restrictions

  • Check for kinks or compression in tubing
  • Look for water accumulation in the circuit
  • Inspect filters and humidifier for blockage

Verify Circuit Connections and Integrity

  • Ensure all connections are secure
  • Check for partially disconnected or misaligned components

Check 2PPEAK (Peak Pressure Limit) Setting

  • Compare peak pressure limit to patient needs
  • Determine if the limit is set too low

Review Ventilator Settings vs Patient Demand

  • Confirm tidal volume setting is appropriate
  • Evaluate ventilation mode and compliance requirements

Drain Circuit and Check Filters (If Applicable)

  • Remove condensation from tubing
  • Replace clogged filters if necessary

Stop if Resolved

  • Verify stable operation after corrections
  • Return ventilator to service if normal function restored

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after external causes and settings have been ruled out, this indicates a possible internal ventilator issue.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this alarm on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup ventilator before continuing diagnostics.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator alarming VOLUME NOT DELIVERED with low tidal volume observed during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"High airway pressure due to kinked circuit or restrictive peak pressure setting."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleared obstruction and adjusted pressure limit; verified normal volume delivery."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

This alarm reflects a mismatch between pressure limits and volume delivery. Always rule out patient condition and circuit issues first, then verify settings before considering device failure.

That is successful troubleshooting.
