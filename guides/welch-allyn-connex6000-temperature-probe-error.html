Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Temperature Probe Error

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Connex 6000 Series monitor reports a temperature probe error. Symptoms may include an error message when attempting a temperature reading, no temperature measurement appearing, or the monitor failing to recognize the probe. Most issues are caused by probe connection problems, damaged cables, or incorrect probe types rather than internal device failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Verify the device is not actively being used on a patient.
  • If the monitor is in use, move the patient to another monitor before beginning troubleshooting.
  • This ensures patient care continues without interruption while the device is evaluated.

Confirm the error message displayed on the monitor

  • Verify the monitor specifically indicates a temperature probe error.
  • This helps confirm the issue is related to the probe or module rather than a general system fault.

Inspect the temperature probe connection

  • Ensure the probe is fully seated in the temperature port on the side of the monitor.
  • Loose or partially connected probes are a common cause of detection errors.

Check for visible damage to the probe and cable

  • Look for frayed cables, bent connectors, cracked housings, or exposed wiring.
  • Damaged probes can trigger errors or prevent the monitor from detecting the probe.

Disconnect and reconnect the probe

  • Remove the probe from the monitor and reconnect it firmly.
  • This can correct intermittent connection issues caused by contact oxidation or incomplete insertion.

Verify the correct probe type is being used

  • Ensure the probe is compatible with the Connex 6000 Series monitor and matches the temperature module installed (for example, oral/axillary or rectal probe types).
  • Incorrect probes may not be recognized.

Check the probe well and connector port

  • Inspect the temperature probe well and port for debris, bent pins, fluid contamination, or physical damage that could interfere with the connection.

Swap with a known-good temperature probe

  • Connect a probe that is known to work properly on another Connex monitor.
  • If the new probe works, the original probe is likely faulty.

Power cycle the monitor

  • Turn the monitor off, wait approximately 10 seconds, and power it back on.
  • This resets the device and may clear temporary communication faults.

Observe monitor behavior during a temperature attempt

  • Attempt a temperature reading and watch for any alarms, error codes, or unusual behavior that may indicate whether the issue is probe-related or internal.

If the Problem Persists

If the temperature probe error continues after verifying connections and testing with a known-good probe, common external causes have likely been ruled out.

The issue may be related to an internal temperature module, connector port failure, or internal electronics.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or further bench evaluation

Recognizing when the issue is likely internal and escalating appropriately is an important part of proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot the temperature probe while it is in use on a patient. Ensure the patient is moved to another monitor or measurement device before testing probes or cycling power on the monitor.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Nursing reported the Connex 6000 monitor displays a temperature probe error and will not take a temperature reading.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Temperature probe cable found damaged near the connector. Monitor displayed probe error when connected.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Replaced faulty temperature probe with known-good probe. Verified temperature measurement functions correctly.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Probe connection inspected
  • Cable condition checked
  • Known-good probe tested
  • Temperature port inspected
  • Power cycle performed
  • Error message displayed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Many temperature probe issues are caused by simple external factors such as damaged probes, loose connections, or incorrect probe types. A systematic troubleshooting approach helps rule out these common causes quickly. Escalating the device appropriately when internal failure is suspected helps protect patient safety and ensures efficient repair processes.

That is successful troubleshooting.
