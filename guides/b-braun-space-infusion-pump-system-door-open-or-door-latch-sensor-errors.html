B. Braun Space Infusion Pump System

“Door Open” or Door Latch Sensor Errors

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

B. Braun

Model

Space Infusion Pump System

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting B. Braun Space Infusion Pumps that display “Door Open” warnings or door latch sensor errors, even when the pump door appears fully closed.

These alarms commonly occur due to external factors such as improper cassette installation, obstruction of the latch mechanism, debris in the door channel, or worn door components rather than internal electronics failure.

The goal is to verify simple mechanical and setup issues before assuming the pump requires repair.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Never troubleshoot the infusion pump while it is connected to a patient receiving medication.
  • If the alarm occurs during therapy, transfer the infusion to a backup pump.
  • Confirm medication delivery continues safely before evaluating the device.

Verify the Door is Fully Closed

  • Open and close the pump door several times to confirm proper engagement.
  • Ensure the latch clicks firmly into place.
  • Confirm the door sits flush against the pump housing.
  • Look for any gaps between the door and the frame.
  • A partially engaged latch can trigger the door sensor.

Inspect the IV Set Installation

  • Remove the IV set and reinstall it following normal loading procedures.
  • Check for tubing not seated correctly in the pump channel.
  • Check for kinks or twists near the cassette area.
  • Confirm excess tubing is not interfering with door closure.
  • Improper set placement can prevent the door from fully engaging the latch.

Check for Debris in the Door Channel

  • Inspect the pump door channel and latch area for obstructions.
  • Look for dried medication residue.
  • Look for tape fragments or packaging material.
  • Look for small pieces of tubing or plastic.
  • Even small debris can prevent the latch sensor from activating properly.
  • If debris is present, clean the area with an approved disinfectant wipe and allow it to dry.

Inspect the Door Latch Mechanism

  • Manually inspect the latch and hinge area.
  • Check for bent latch components.
  • Check for worn latch hooks.
  • Check for sticky or slow latch movement.
  • The latch should move freely and return smoothly when pressed.

Verify the Door Sensor Trigger

  • With the IV set removed, close the pump door firmly.
  • Observe whether the alarm clears.
  • Gently press the door inward to see if the alarm disappears.
  • If slight pressure clears the alarm, the latch alignment may be worn or misaligned.

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Power the pump off completely, then power it back on.
  • This allows the system to reset and recheck the door sensor status.
  • If the alarm clears after reboot but returns when the door is used again, the issue is likely mechanical.

Test with a Different IV Set

  • Install a different manufacturer-approved IV set.
  • Occasionally a damaged or warped cassette can prevent proper door closure.

If the Problem Persists

If the “Door Open” alarm continues after verifying door closure, IV set installation, and latch condition, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve:

  • Internal door sensor failure
  • Latch alignment failure
  • Internal switch malfunction

At this point the device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to escalate prevents unnecessary troubleshooting and protects patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot infusion pumps while they are actively infusing medication to a patient.

If a door alarm occurs during therapy:

  • Transfer the infusion to a backup pump
  • Secure the patient’s therapy
  • Then evaluate the faulty pump safely

Maintaining continuous medication delivery is the priority.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Nursing reported the infusion pump repeatedly alarming ‘Door Open’ even though the door appeared closed during infusion setup.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Door latch mechanism showed intermittent engagement due to debris in the latch channel preventing full closure.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Cleaned latch area, reinstalled IV set, verified proper door closure, and confirmed alarm cleared. Pump returned to service after functional check.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • IV set replaced or reinstalled
  • Door latch inspected
  • Debris found in latch area (if applicable)
  • Alarm cleared after cleaning or door adjustment
  • Pump passed operational check
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Infusion pump door alarms often appear serious but are frequently caused by simple mechanical or setup issues. By starting with basic checks—door closure, IV set installation, and latch condition—Clinical Engineering can resolve many issues quickly. When mechanical wear or sensor faults are suspected, removing the device from service and documenting findings ensures both patient safety and efficient repair workflows.

That is successful troubleshooting.
