HeartSine Samaritan PAD Series

Ready Indicator or Status LED Errors

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

HeartSine

Model

Samaritan PAD Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting when the status LED is not flashing green, shows red, or indicates the AED is not ready for use.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the AED is needed for an active emergency and is not showing ready:
  • Immediately retrieve a backup AED
  • Do not delay patient care while troubleshooting

Expected: Patient care continues without delay

Why it matters: A non-ready AED may not deliver therapy when needed

Verify LED Status Behavior

  • Observe the status indicator:
  • Flashing green = ready
  • Flashing red or no light = fault condition

Expected: Clear identification of LED status

Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is real or misinterpreted

Check PAD-Pak Installation (Most Common Cause)

  • Open the device and ensure the PAD-Pak is fully seated
  • Remove and reinstall firmly until it clicks into place

Expected: LED returns to flashing green

Why it matters: Improperly seated PAD-Pak prevents readiness checks

Check PAD-Pak Expiration Date

  • Inspect expiration date on PAD-Pak
  • Replace if expired or near expiration

Expected: Device recognizes valid PAD-Pak and returns to ready

Why it matters: Expired PAD-Paks trigger fault indicators

Inspect for Shipping Tab or Activation Issue

  • Ensure any battery isolation tab has been removed
  • Confirm device has been activated per initial setup

Expected: Device powers correctly and runs self-check

Why it matters: Unactivated units may not complete readiness checks

Perform a Manual Power-On Self-Test

  • Turn the AED ON and observe prompts
  • Listen for voice prompts and watch LED behavior

Expected: Device completes self-test without errors

Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is persistent or intermittent

Check Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure device is within proper temperature range
  • Inspect for moisture, dust, or contamination

Expected: Device operates within normal conditions

Why it matters: Environmental extremes can affect internal self-tests

Inspect Device for Physical Damage

  • Look for cracks, water ingress, or impact damage
  • Check speaker grille and casing integrity

Expected: No visible damage

Why it matters: Physical damage can cause internal faults and LED errors

Replace PAD-Pak if Issue Persists

  • Install a new, known-good PAD-Pak

Expected: LED returns to flashing green

Why it matters: Faulty PAD-Paks are a common root cause

If the Problem Persists

If the LED still does not show ready after verifying PAD-Pak, installation, and environment:

  • Internal fault is likely
  • Remove the AED from service
  • Label it Out of Service
  • Send for repair or manufacturer evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unsafe equipment from remaining in circulation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an AED during an active emergency. Always switch to a backup unit first, then evaluate the faulty device afterward.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"AED status indicator not flashing green and showing fault condition."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Expired PAD-Pak prevented successful self-test."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced PAD-Pak; device passed self-test and returned to ready status."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Status LED behavior (red, off, intermittent)
  • PAD-Pak expiration date
  • Whether PAD-Pak was reseated or replaced
  • Self-test results during power-on
  • Environmental conditions (temperature, moisture)
  • Physical condition of device
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

AED readiness is critical for patient survival. Always start with simple external checks like PAD-Pak condition and installation before assuming failure. Remove from service when readiness cannot be confirmed, and document clearly to support safe equipment management.

That is successful troubleshooting.
