BD Alaris

Error: 120.4620 Battery voltage too low when charging

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 120.4620 indicating low battery voltage during charging, causing failure to charge or loss of battery backup power.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use if battery backup is unreliable
  • Transfer infusion to a functioning device if needed

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and connect to AC power
  • Confirm Error 120.4620 is displayed
  • Check if battery does not charge or remains critically low
  • Observe if pump shuts off when unplugged
  • Note when the error occurs (startup, during use, intermittent)

Check AC Power Source

  • Plug the pump into a known working outlet
  • Test outlet with another device
  • Ensure proper hospital-grade power source

Inspect Power Cord and AC Inlet

  • Ensure power cord is fully seated at pump and outlet
  • Inspect for damage or fraying
  • Check for loose or intermittent connection

Confirm Charging Indicators

  • Observe battery icon and charging indicators
  • Check if no charging indication is present
  • Check if charging appears but battery percentage does not increase

Perform Power Cycle

  • Disconnect AC power and power down the pump
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect AC power and restart

Test Battery Function

  • Allow brief charging period if possible
  • Disconnect AC power and observe operation
  • Check if pump shuts off immediately
  • Check if battery level improves

Consider Battery Age and Usage

  • Evaluate battery age and service history
  • Consider frequent deep discharges
  • Assess long-term storage without charging

If the Problem Persists

If Error 120.4620 continues after external checks, battery failure or internal charging circuitry issues are likely.

  • Remove the pump from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for battery replacement or further repair

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before addressing battery or power issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"BD Alaris pump displaying Error 120.4620 with battery not charging and shutting off when unplugged."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery unable to reach proper voltage during charging, consistent with battery degradation or charging failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified AC power source and connections. Error persisted. Removed pump from service and sent for battery replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power-related errors directly affect infusion reliability. Always rule out simple external causes first, then escalate appropriately when battery or charging failure is indicated.

That is successful troubleshooting.
