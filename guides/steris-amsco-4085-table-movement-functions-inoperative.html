STERIS AMSCO 4085

Table Movement Functions Inoperative

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 4085

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a STERIS AMSCO 4085 surgical table when the table powers on but movement functions (raising, lowering, tilting, or lateral adjustments) do not operate. Common symptoms include:

  • Table powers on, but no response from hand or foot controls
  • No movement in any axis (height, tilt, Trendelenburg)
  • Audible relay clicks without motion

Focus is on external checks and safe troubleshooting for patient care.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Table Power

  • Confirm the table is plugged into a live outlet.
  • Check that the main power switch is on.
  • Observe any indicator lights on the table base.

Why: Even if the table powers on, insufficient or intermittent power can prevent movement.

Inspect Foot and Hand Controls

  • Ensure the pendant or hand control is securely connected.
  • Check for visible damage to buttons, wires, or connectors.
  • Swap with a known working pendant if available.

Why: Faulty controls or loose connections are common causes of inoperative movements.

Check Emergency Stop and Safety Locks

  • Confirm the emergency stop button is released.
  • Ensure any safety locks or brakes are disengaged.

Why: Engaged safety mechanisms prevent movement to protect patients.

Observe Table Base Indicators

  • Look for error codes, blinking lights, or alarms on the base.
  • Note any unusual sounds or vibrations.

Why: Internal relays may attempt to actuate without completing movement, indicating electrical or hydraulic issues.

Inspect Power Cables and Connections

  • Verify all cables between table base, control units, and pendant are secure.
  • Look for frayed wires or signs of damage.

Why: Intermittent connections can prevent motion despite system power.

If the Problem Persists

External and control checks have been completed; issue is likely internal (electrical relay, motor, or hydraulic actuator failure).

Action:

  • Remove the table from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send for manufacturer repair or in-house bench evaluation.

Knowing when to escalate is part of proper troubleshooting and patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt to troubleshoot movement functions with a patient on the table.
  • If urgent repositioning is needed, move the patient to another table first.
  • Ensure all staff are aware the table is inoperative before any surgical procedure.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Table powers on, but none of the movement functions respond to pendant or foot controls.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Pendant and foot controls verified; emergency stop disengaged; power confirmed; table still does not move.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Table removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent for repair evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and live
  • Pendant/cable swapped or tested
  • Emergency stop status
  • Indicator lights behavior
  • Audible relay clicks or unusual sounds
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety and logical, stepwise troubleshooting are paramount. External checks often resolve common issues. When a table remains inoperative despite these checks, timely escalation and clear documentation prevent patient harm and streamline repair workflow.

That is successful troubleshooting.
