Stryker S3

Error: 102 Stuck Safety Relay

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Error 102 Stuck Safety Relay, which may cause unreliable or disabled bed motion when the safety relay remains active without a pressed control. This condition affects the bed motion system and may prevent safe, predictable positioning operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Stryker S3 bed from patient use if motion control cannot be trusted, motion is disabled, or unintended movement is possible.
  • If a patient is currently on the bed, coordinate a safe transfer to a known-working bed before troubleshooting.
  • Do not test bed motion with a patient occupying the bed.

Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on a bed with an unresolved motion-system error.

Why it matters: Error 102 affects the bed motion system and may indicate that the control system is detecting a continuous motion-command condition.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Bed Behavior

  • With the bed removed from clinical use, power the bed normally and confirm the display shows Error 102 Call Service.
  • Document whether the error appears after approximately one minute with no motion button pressed.
  • Determine whether the error appears consistently after startup or after using a specific footboard, siderail, or pendant control.
  • Document whether bed motion is disabled, intermittent, or abnormal.
  • Do not rely only on an Auto-Cycle Mode test, because this condition is not checked during Auto-Cycle Mode.

Expected outcome: Error 102 is reproduced or clearly confirmed during normal operation, and the associated motion behavior is identified.

Why it matters: This confirms a true stuck safety relay condition rather than an isolated user-control complaint or incomplete functional test.

3. Check the Power Source and Basic Bed Condition

  • Verify the bed is connected to an appropriate grounded AC outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, and strain relief for visible damage.
  • Inspect the bed for impact damage, liquid contamination, cleaning-fluid intrusion, or damaged control areas.
  • If permitted by facility procedure, test the outlet or move the bed to a known-good outlet and restart the evaluation.

Expected outcome: The bed is receiving stable power and no obvious external damage is contributing to unreliable operation.

Why it matters: Power or contamination concerns should be identified before isolating a motion-control electronics fault.

4. Inspect for a Physically Stuck Motion Button

  • With no one touching the controls, inspect each available bed-motion control location, including footboard controls, siderail controls, and pendant controls if installed.
  • Press and release each motion-related button individually.
  • Look for buttons that remain depressed, feel sticky, fail to spring back, or appear damaged.
  • Pay special attention to any control location reported by staff as recently used before the error occurred.

Expected outcome: All buttons release normally and no motion command appears to remain mechanically active.

If resolved: If a stuck button is released or a contaminated accessory is removed and Error 102 no longer returns during normal testing, complete a full motion-function verification before returning the bed to service.

Why it matters: The safety relay signal remaining active may be caused by a control input that is physically stuck or continuously activated.

5. Inspect Control Overlays and External Control Surfaces

  • Examine footboard and siderail switch overlays for cracked, punctured, lifted, or worn membrane surfaces.
  • Check for fluid intrusion, sticky residue, or evidence of cleaning-fluid damage.
  • Look for buttons distorted by impact or controls that appear continuously activated.
  • Do not perform internal disassembly during this initial troubleshooting evaluation.

Expected outcome: External control surfaces are intact, clean, dry, and do not indicate a continuously active switch.

Why it matters: A damaged or contaminated control overlay can behave as though a motion button is continuously pressed.

6. Inspect Pendant Controls and Accessible Cabling

  • If a pendant is connected, inspect it for damage, stuck buttons, contamination, or a pinched cable.
  • Disconnect or substitute the pendant only when permitted by the bed configuration and facility service practice, then retest for Error 102 during normal operation.
  • Inspect accessible external cables and connectors associated with footboard, siderail, or pendant controls for loose connections.
  • Check for bent or damaged connector pins, pinched or stretched cables, cuts, fluid contamination, or evidence of intermittent connection.

Expected outcome: No external accessory, cable, or connector is found continuously driving a motion input.

If resolved: If the error clears after removing or replacing a damaged external control accessory, verify all remaining bed controls and document the failed accessory.

Why it matters: This helps isolate an accessory-related or cabling-related input from an internal safety-relay or control fault.

7. Retest the Bed Under Normal Motion-Control Conditions

  • After correcting any external condition found, restart the bed and leave all motion controls untouched for longer than one minute.
  • Confirm whether Error 102 Call Service returns.
  • If the error does not return, test each available motion function individually from the applicable control locations.
  • Observe for normal movement, stopping, and response when each control is released.

Expected outcome: The error remains cleared, and bed motion occurs only when intentionally commanded.

Why it matters: Error 102 may occur after the safety relay signal remains active for one minute without a button being pressed. Normal functional testing is required because Auto-Cycle Mode does not check this condition.

8. Identify Need for Repair Evaluation

  • If Error 102 returns after external controls, accessories, accessible cables, connectors, contamination, power conditions, and obvious physical damage have been ruled out, discontinue external troubleshooting.
  • Suspect a failure within the safety-relay or motion-control electronics path.
  • Continue evaluation only according to approved service documentation and qualified repair procedures.

Expected outcome: The bed is identified as requiring repair rather than continued external troubleshooting.

Why it matters: A persistent stuck safety relay error with no external control input present strongly suggests a device-related motion-control fault.

9. Verify Operation After Repair

  • Confirm Error 102 does not return during normal operation.
  • Verify that no motion occurs without an intentional button press.
  • Test all applicable footboard, siderail, and pendant motion controls.
  • Verify each movement stops correctly when the button is released.
  • Confirm the bed is clean, intact, and safe for clinical use.

Expected outcome: The bed passes functional testing without recurrence of Error 102.

If resolved: Document the repair and testing performed, then return the bed to service according to facility procedure.

If the Problem Persists

If Error 102 Call Service remains after external controls, pendant devices, accessible cables, connectors, contamination, power conditions, and visible damage have been checked, the common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Stuck Safety Relay error indicates that the safety-relay or motion-control electronics path requires qualified repair evaluation.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the device for repair or bench evaluation using approved service documentation.

Do not return the bed to patient use while motion control reliability remains unresolved.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first before testing bed motion, and never rely on a bed with an unresolved motion-system error.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displayed Error 102 Call Service / Stuck Safety Relay. Staff reported concern for unreliable bed movement. Error was confirmed during normal operation after approximately one minute without a motion button being pressed."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found no physically stuck footboard, siderail, or pendant motion button and no visible external cable, connector, contamination, or power-related issue. Error remained active, indicating likely failure within the safety-relay or motion-control electronics path."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from patient service and labeled Out of Service due to unresolved motion-control error. Forwarded unit for bench evaluation and component replacement as indicated. Bed must pass complete motion-function verification before return to clinical use."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error 102 should be treated as a motion-safety concern, not merely a display message. Begin with patient safety, confirm the fault during normal operation, rule out stuck or contaminated external controls, and escalate appropriately when the relay or control path remains suspect. Clear CCR documentation records both the safety risk and the reason for repair or return to service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
