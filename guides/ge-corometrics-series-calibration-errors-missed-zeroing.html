GE Healthcare Corometrics Series Fetal Monitor

Calibration Errors or Missed Zeroing

Asset Type

Fetal Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Corometrics Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting Corometrics fetal monitors that report calibration errors or fail to complete zeroing. These issues often prevent accurate readings of fetal or maternal parameters. Common causes are usually external or setup-related, such as sensor connection issues, improper placement, or environmental interference, rather than internal device failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check Power Supply

  • Verify the monitor is plugged into a known good outlet.
  • Ensure the battery (if present) is charged and seated properly.
  • Why: Unstable power can cause calibration routines to fail.

Inspect Sensors and Cables

  • Check all transducers and cables for damage, kinks, or loose connections.
  • Reconnect each sensor securely to the monitor.
  • Why: The monitor cannot zero or calibrate properly if a sensor is not communicating correctly.

Ensure Proper Sensor Placement

  • Confirm fetal and maternal sensors are positioned according to the manufacturer’s guidelines.
  • Remove excessive gel or debris that might affect readings.
  • Why: Misplaced sensors can cause the zeroing procedure to fail.

Verify Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure the monitor is not in an excessively hot, cold, or high-electrical-noise environment.
  • Move portable devices or cables that may introduce interference.
  • Why: Environmental factors can prevent successful calibration.

Restart Calibration Procedure

  • Power cycle the monitor and attempt zeroing again.
  • Follow the on-screen instructions carefully, ensuring no movement occurs during the procedure.
  • Why: Sometimes a simple reset resolves temporary software or sensor communication issues.

Swap Known-Good Sensors (if available)

  • Replace current sensors with known-good ones to determine if the original sensor is faulty.
  • Why: A defective sensor can consistently fail calibration.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out, so the issue may be internal (sensor board, electronics, or firmware).

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to manufacturer service or internal repair bench for evaluation.

Knowing when to escalate is a key part of safe and effective troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Never attempt calibration while the monitor is attached to a patient.
  • Move any patient monitoring to another verified device before troubleshooting.
  • Only perform calibration and sensor testing in a safe, controlled environment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Monitor displays “Calibration Error” or fails zeroing.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed loose sensor connections and improper sensor placement; external environment verified stable.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Reconnected sensors, repositioned according to guidelines, restarted calibration. If unresolved, device labeled out of service and sent for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes/No
  • Cables swapped: Yes/No
  • Sensor placement: Verified per guidelines
  • Indicator lights: Any unusual LEDs during zeroing
  • Audible alarms: Note if triggered
  • Final device status: Operational or Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Following a logical, stepwise approach ensures calibration errors are addressed without risking patient monitoring accuracy. Proper documentation using the CCR method provides a clear record of troubleshooting steps and escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
