Philips PageWriter TC70

Error: Check Printer

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a Check Printer error on the Philips PageWriter TC70. This condition may prevent ECG reports from printing, interrupt paper feed, cancel print jobs, or indicate a printer drawer, paper path, printer controller, or internal printer connection problem.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the PageWriter TC70 is needed for active ECG acquisition or timed testing, provide another working ECG machine before troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on a cardiograph that cannot print required ECG reports.
  • Expected outcome: Patient testing is not delayed or dependent on a device with unresolved printer errors.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the cardiograph and confirm the exact message: Check Printer.
  • Observe whether the message appears at startup, when opening or closing the printer drawer, when attempting to print, or intermittently during normal use.
  • Check the associated symptom, such as no ECG printout, no paper feed, print job cancellation, error clearing briefly and returning, or the printer drawer not seating correctly.
  • Expected outcome: The error is reproducible or clearly observed.

Remove the Device from Active Clinical Use if Printing Is Required

  • If ECG printing is required for clinical workflow, remove the unit from patient use until printer function is verified.
  • Use another ECG machine if immediate ECG documentation is needed.
  • Expected outcome: Clinical staff have a reliable method to acquire and print ECGs.

Check Printer Paper Drawer Position

  • Open the printer paper drawer fully.
  • Inspect that the drawer moves smoothly and is not stuck, misaligned, or partially latched.
  • Close the drawer firmly and evenly.
  • Expected outcome: The drawer closes completely and the error clears.
  • Why it matters: Opening and closing the printer paper drawer can clear a drawer seating condition that may trigger printer-related messages.

Check Paper Loading

  • Verify that the correct ECG paper is installed and sitting properly in the tray.
  • Confirm the paper is not jammed, folded, wrinkled, upside down, or preventing the drawer from closing.
  • Expected outcome: Paper sits flat, feeds correctly, and does not interfere with drawer closure.

Inspect for Paper Jam or Debris

  • With the drawer open, look for torn paper, labels, dust, adhesive residue, or foreign material around the paper path.
  • Remove only loose, accessible debris without forcing any printer components.
  • Expected outcome: The paper path is clear and the drawer closes normally.

Restart and Recheck the Error

  • Power the PageWriter TC70 off, wait briefly, then power it back on.
  • Confirm whether Check Printer returns at startup or only during printing.
  • If the message does not return after opening and closing the drawer, continue with a test print before returning the device to use.
  • Expected outcome: The cardiograph boots without the Check Printer message.

Perform a Test Print

  • Attempt a controlled ECG or system printout according to normal facility workflow.
  • Confirm the printer feeds paper, print quality is readable, no error returns, paper advances correctly, and the drawer remains latched.
  • Expected outcome: The printer completes the print job without error.
  • Why it matters: Clearing the message alone is not enough. Printing must be verified before returning the device to clinical use.

Check External Condition of the Printer Area

  • Inspect the printer drawer, latch area, paper tray, and exterior housing for cracks, impact damage, looseness, or signs the device was dropped.
  • If damage is found, remove the device from service and send it for repair evaluation.
  • Expected outcome: No physical damage is present.

Determine Whether the Issue Is External or Device-Related

  • If the error clears after reseating the drawer and the printer works normally, the issue was likely drawer seating or paper path related.
  • If the error returns with the drawer seated, correct paper installed, and no jam present, the issue is likely device-related.
  • Possible device-related causes include a printer controller fault, internal printer cable dislodgement, printer assembly connection issue, or internal cardiograph service fault.
  • Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to a simple paper or drawer problem, or an internal printer service condition.

If the Problem Persists

If Check Printer persists after the printer drawer has been opened and closed, paper has been checked, jams have been ruled out, and the printer still fails to operate, remove the PageWriter TC70 from service.

Label the device Out of Service and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

A persistent Check Printer error may indicate a printer controller problem or an internal printer cable that has become dislodged. Internal cable inspection requires service-level evaluation and should not be handled as a bedside troubleshooting step.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot printer problems on an active patient if the ECG result is clinically urgent. Move the patient to a backup device first so acquisition, review, and printed documentation are not delayed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips PageWriter TC70 reported Check Printer error. Staff reported ECG printer would not reliably print reports."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Printer drawer and paper path checked. Error persisted after drawer reseat and paper inspection, indicating likely internal printer controller or printer cable service condition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Opened and closed printer drawer, verified paper loading, checked for accessible paper jam or debris, restarted unit, and attempted test print. Error returned. Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Printer errors on an ECG machine can directly affect clinical workflow and documentation. Start with the drawer, paper, and obvious external causes first. If the Check Printer message persists, treat it as a real device fault and escalate appropriately. Clear documentation helps repair staff understand whether the issue was simple drawer seating or a likely internal printer controller or cable problem.

That is successful troubleshooting.
