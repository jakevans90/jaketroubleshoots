Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: O2 SENSOR CAL DUE !

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the O2 SENSOR CAL DUE ! alarm, indicating that oxygen sensor calibration is overdue. This condition can result in inaccurate oxygen concentration readings and unsafe gas delivery to the patient.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from active patient use or transition to a backup device
  • Ensure patient ventilation and oxygen delivery are maintained safely

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power on the unit and confirm the O2 SENSOR CAL DUE ! alarm is displayed
  • Observe if the alarm appears at startup or continuously
  • Check if O2 readings are present but may be inaccurate
  • Determine if the machine has been running without recent calibration

Check O2 Supply Before Calibration

  • Verify pipeline oxygen is connected and stable
  • Check cylinder supply (if applicable) is open and adequately pressurized
  • Inspect hoses for kinks, disconnections, or leaks

Perform O2 Sensor Calibration

  • Place the machine in Standby mode
  • Access the calibration menu
  • Perform room air (21%) calibration as prompted
  • Follow all on-screen instructions completely

Verify Post-Calibration Performance

  • Confirm the alarm is cleared
  • Verify O2 readings are stable and appropriate
  • Run a system check or self-test if available

Repeat or Inspect if Needed

  • Repeat calibration once if it fails initially
  • Inspect the sensor connection and cable externally
  • Check for contamination or moisture in the breathing system

If the Problem Persists

If calibration cannot be completed or the alarm continues:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Persistent calibration failure may indicate sensor degradation or internal fault
  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the O2 sensor per service procedures

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot or rely on oxygen readings on an active patient. Always transition the patient to a backup device before addressing calibration issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displayed O2 SENSOR CAL DUE ! alarm at startup; oxygen readings potentially unreliable."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"O2 sensor calibration overdue due to elapsed time since last calibration."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed O2 sensor calibration per procedure; alarm cleared and readings verified. Device returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Calibration alarms are early indicators that accuracy may be compromised. Addressing them promptly ensures safe oxygen delivery and reliable monitoring. Always verify system accuracy and escalate when necessary.
