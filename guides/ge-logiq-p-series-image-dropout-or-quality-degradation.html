GE Healthcare LOGIQ P Series

Image Dropout or Quality Degradation

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

LOGIQ P Series

What This Guide Helps With

Addresses poor image quality or intermittent dropout caused by probe issues, settings, connections, or environmental interference before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the probe from the patient and transition to another functioning system if imaging is needed.
  • Do not troubleshoot during active clinical use.

Inspect the Probe and Cable

  • Check for visible damage such as cracks, fluid intrusion, or frayed cable shielding.
  • Damaged probes commonly cause dropout or artifact.
  • If damage is found, remove probe from service.

Reseat the Probe Connection

  • Disconnect and firmly reconnect the probe to the system port.
  • Poor seating can cause intermittent signal loss.
  • Confirm the locking mechanism is fully engaged.

Try a Different Probe (If Available)

  • Connect a known working probe to the same port.
  • If image quality returns, the original probe is likely faulty.
  • If issue persists, continue troubleshooting.

Test the Original Probe on Another System

  • If possible, connect the probe to a different compatible ultrasound system.
  • Confirms whether the issue follows the probe or stays with the system.

Check Imaging Presets and Settings

  • Verify gain, depth, frequency, and focus settings are appropriate.
  • Incorrect settings can mimic dropout or poor image quality.
  • Reset to default preset if unsure.

Inspect Probe Port for Debris or Damage

  • Look for bent pins, debris, or fluid in the connector.
  • Contamination can disrupt signal transmission.
  • Clean only per manufacturer-approved methods.

Check for Environmental Interference

  • Ensure cables are not routed near strong electrical sources (e.g., power cords, other equipment).
  • Electrical interference can degrade signal quality.

Restart the System

  • Perform a full system reboot.
  • Clears temporary software or communication issues.

Observe for Consistency of Issue

  • Determine if dropout is constant or intermittent.
  • Constant issues are likely hardware-related.
  • Intermittent issues are likely connection or cable-related.
  • If the issue resolves at any step, return the device to service and document findings.

If the Problem Persists

If all probes, connections, and settings have been verified and image quality issues continue, the problem is likely internal (beamformer, port hardware, or processing issue).

Remove the system from service, label it Out of Service, and send for bench evaluation or vendor repair.

Recognizing when external causes have been ruled out is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot imaging issues on an active patient. Always transition care to a functioning system to avoid diagnostic delays or compromised imaging quality.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ultrasound system experiencing intermittent image dropout during scanning."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Faulty probe cable causing intermittent signal loss during movement."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced defective probe and verified normal image quality with functional testing."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Probe condition (cracks, cable wear)
  • Probe swapped and results
  • Port condition (clean/damaged)
  • Imaging preset adjustments
  • Behavior (constant vs intermittent dropout)
  • Any interference sources nearby
  • System reboot performed
  • Final system status

Final Thought

Image quality issues are often caused by probes, connections, or settings rather than internal system failure. A structured approach ensures patient safety, avoids unnecessary part replacement, and supports accurate diagnosis. Knowing when to escalate is just as important as resolving the issue.

That is successful troubleshooting.
