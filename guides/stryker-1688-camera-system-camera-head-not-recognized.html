1688 Camera System

Camera Head Not Recognized

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

1688 Camera System

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes when the camera head is not detected by the console, preventing video output and delaying surgical procedures.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm no patient is actively undergoing a procedure with the affected camera. Move the patient to a backup camera system if needed.

Verify Camera Head Connection

  • Disconnect and reconnect the camera head to the scope cable or console interface. Ensure the connection is fully seated; loose connections are a common cause. Expected outcome: camera is detected by console.

Inspect Cables and Adapters

  • Check for visible damage to the scope cable, camera head connector, and adapters. Swap with a known-good cable if available. Expected outcome: camera detection restored.

Check Console Settings

  • Ensure the console is set to the correct input/source for the 1688 camera head. Incorrect input selection can prevent recognition. Expected outcome: console recognizes camera head.

Power Cycle Console and Camera

  • Turn off the console and camera head, wait 30 seconds, and power back on. Expected outcome: system self-test completes and camera is recognized.

Test with Alternate Camera Head

  • If available, attach a different, known-good 1688 camera head. Expected outcome: if recognized, original camera head may be defective.

Firmware and Compatibility Check

  • Confirm console firmware supports the camera head model. Outdated firmware can cause recognition errors. Expected outcome: system operates normally if firmware is compatible.

If the Problem Persists

All external causes have been ruled out. The camera head or console interface may be internally defective.

  • Remove affected device from service.
  • Label as “Out of Service.”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt troubleshooting during an active procedure. Always have a backup camera ready to maintain patient safety and surgical workflow.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Camera head not detected by 1688 console during setup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Camera head connection loose; initial cable inspection and swap confirmed issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reconnected cable and replaced damaged cable; camera head recognized and tested successfully."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Cable checked and swapped
  • Connection ports inspected for debris or damage
  • Console input verified
  • Firmware version noted
  • Backup camera tested

Final Thought

Patient safety and surgical efficiency depend on properly functioning camera systems. Follow logical, stepwise troubleshooting to resolve external issues, escalate internal faults promptly, and document thoroughly for future reference.

That is successful troubleshooting.
