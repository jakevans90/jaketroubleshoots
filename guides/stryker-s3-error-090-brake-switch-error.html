Stryker S3

Error: 090 Brake Switch Error

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

S3

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Error 090 Brake Switch Error, indicating unreliable brake-status sensing due to disconnected or shorted brake-switch wiring. This condition may prevent the bed from accurately reporting brake status and can create an unintended movement or fall risk if the bed is used before the fault is corrected.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Stryker S3 bed from patient use if brake operation or brake-status indication cannot be trusted.
  • If the bed is occupied, coordinate with clinical staff to transfer the patient to a known-safe bed before troubleshooting.
  • Apply the brakes manually and physically verify that the bed does not roll before moving or servicing it.
  • Do not rely on electronic brake-status indication while Error 090 is active.

Expected outcome: The patient is not dependent on a bed with a potentially unreliable brake system or brake-status indication.

Why it matters: A brake-related fault can create unintended bed movement or increase fall risk.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Associated Behavior

  • With the bed removed from patient use, power the bed on and confirm that the display shows Error 090 Call Service.
  • Document whether the error appears immediately at startup or after the bed has been powered on.
  • Check whether brake-related indicators or alerts behave abnormally when the brake pedal is applied and released.
  • Manually apply and release the brake system and observe whether the bed physically locks and unlocks as expected.
  • Do not assume that normal brake indication means the fault has cleared.

Expected outcome: The error is reproduced or clearly confirmed, and actual brake function is documented.

Why it matters: Error 090 is associated with the brake-switch circuit and may affect brake-status reporting even when the mechanical brakes appear to operate.

3. Confirm Actual Brake Function Physically

  • With the bed unloaded and positioned safely, apply the brakes using the brake pedal.
  • Gently attempt to move the bed to confirm that the casters are held.
  • Release the brakes and confirm that the bed rolls normally when intended.
  • Inspect the brake pedal and visible linkage area for obvious physical damage, obstruction, or abnormal movement.

Expected outcome: The bed either brakes and releases normally, or a physical brake concern is identified.

Why it matters: Until the error is corrected, brake status must be verified physically rather than relying on the bed's indication system.

4. Check Power Condition and Fault-Clearing Requirements

  • Confirm that the bed is connected to an appropriate power source and note whether Error 090 remains displayed.
  • Recognize that this error may remain displayed after an intermittent condition stops.
  • Correct any identified brake-switch wiring or connection issue before attempting to clear the message by cycling power.

Expected outcome: The technician distinguishes an active brake-switch problem from a latched error requiring power cycling after correction.

Why it matters: Power cycling alone will not resolve a true brake-switch circuit fault.

5. Inspect Accessible Brake-Switch Wiring and Connections

  • Remove power as appropriate before inspecting accessible wiring and connections.
  • Inspect accessible brake-switch wiring, harness routing, and connectors for loose or disconnected connections.
  • Look for pinched, cut, stretched, or abraded wires.
  • Check for bent or damaged connector pins, contamination, or evidence of fluid intrusion.
  • Inspect visible harness routing near moving brake components for damage or interference.
  • Reseat any accessible loose brake-switch connector according to normal Clinical Engineering practice.

Expected outcome: Accessible brake-switch wiring and connections are secure, clean, undamaged, and properly routed.

Why it matters: Error 090 may occur when brake-switch wiring is disconnected or shorted, preventing reliable brake-status sensing.

6. Isolate Whether the Fault Is Correctable Externally

  • If visible damage, contamination, or a loose accessible connector is found, correct the issue when within repair scope.
  • After correction, continue to functional verification and power cycling.
  • If no accessible connection or wiring issue is identified and Error 090 remains present, treat the condition as requiring further repair evaluation.

Expected outcome: The technician determines whether an accessible connection issue was corrected or the bed requires additional evaluation.

7. Cycle Power and Verify Repair

  • After correcting any identified accessible brake-switch wiring or connection issue, power-cycle the bed.
  • Confirm that Error 090 Call Service no longer appears.
  • Verify that the brakes apply and release normally.
  • Confirm that the bed remains stationary when braked.
  • Verify that brake-status indication operates as expected.
  • Confirm that no related brake alarms or abnormal indicators remain.

Expected outcome: The error clears after correction and power cycling, with normal brake operation and indication verified.

Resolved condition: If the error clears and brake operation and indication test normally, document the repair and return the bed to service according to facility procedure.

If the Problem Persists

If Error 090 Call Service remains after power cycling and accessible brake-switch wiring, connections, brake pedal condition, and physical brake function have been evaluated, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Error 090 indicates that the brake-switch circuit, associated harness, or control electronics path requires further repair or bench evaluation.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A bed with unresolved brake-system error reporting should not be returned to clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first. While Error 090 is active, do not rely on brake-status indication; physically verify that the bed brakes are applied and holding before any transfer, positioning, or service activity.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Stryker S3 hospital bed displayed Error 090 Call Service - Brake Switch Error during power-on evaluation. Brake-status indication was considered unreliable pending inspection."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection identified a loose accessible brake-switch harness connection causing interruption of the brake-switch circuit. Error remained displayed until corrective action and power cycling were completed."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from patient use, verified physical brake holding function, reseated and secured the accessible brake-switch harness connection, cycled bed power, and confirmed Error 090 cleared. Verified normal brake apply/release operation and brake-status indication before returning the bed to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A brake-switch error must be treated as a safety concern because the bed may not accurately report its brake condition. Clinical Engineering troubleshooting should begin with patient safety and physical brake verification, proceed through accessible wiring and connection checks, and end with appropriate escalation when the error remains unresolved. Clear CCR documentation supports safe return-to-service decisions and future repair activity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
