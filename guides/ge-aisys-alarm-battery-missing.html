GE Healthcare Aisys

Alarm: BATTERY MISSING

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Aisys

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the BATTERY MISSING alarm on a GE Healthcare Aisys anesthesia machine. This alarm indicates the system may not detect usable battery backup during startup, which can affect safe operation if AC power is lost.

The condition may be caused by a missing battery, disconnected battery, deeply discharged battery, failed battery, battery harness issue, charge circuit problem, or internal power detection failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from patient use if the alarm appears during setup, checkout, or before a case.
  • If the machine is in active use, notify anesthesia staff immediately and make sure alternate ventilation, oxygen delivery, and monitoring are available.
  • Do not rely on the machine’s battery backup until the alarm is resolved and the machine passes required checks.
  • Use another anesthesia machine or approved backup equipment if reliable battery backup is required.

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power the system on and confirm the exact alarm displayed is BATTERY MISSING.
  • Confirm whether the displayed message states No battery backup.
  • Observe whether the alarm appears during POST/startup, system checkout, after power cycling, or continuously while connected to AC power.
  • Confirm whether the machine otherwise powers normally from AC power.

Understand the Real-World Problem

  • Treat this as a battery backup availability issue, not just a nuisance message.
  • The machine may be running normally from wall power while still lacking reliable backup power.
  • Possible causes include a missing battery, disconnected battery, deeply discharged battery, failed battery, wiring issue, or battery charge circuit fault.

Confirm AC Power Is Stable

  • Verify the anesthesia machine is plugged into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Check that the power cord is fully seated at the machine and wall outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, and strain relief for visible damage.
  • Confirm the outlet is powered and the machine recognizes AC power.
  • Avoid questionable extension cords, switched outlets, or failed power strips.

Confirm the Battery Is Installed and Connected

  • Verify the battery is physically present according to facility-approved service access procedures.
  • Check for loose battery leads, disconnected plugs, damaged connectors, corrosion, or swelling.
  • Do not bypass battery safety features or modify connectors.
  • If the battery is missing or disconnected, correct the issue and retest the machine.

Leave the System Plugged In to Charge

  • Connect the machine to stable AC power and allow the battery time to charge.
  • After charging, power cycle the machine and check whether the BATTERY MISSING alarm clears.
  • If the alarm clears, verify battery status and complete the required checkout before return to service.
  • A deeply discharged battery may need recovery time before the system detects it properly.

Check Whether the Alarm Returns After Power Cycling

  • Turn the system off and back on after the battery has had time to charge.
  • Watch for the BATTERY MISSING alarm during POST/startup.
  • Check for related battery, power, or charging alarms.
  • A recurring alarm points more strongly toward a failed battery, harness issue, charge circuit fault, or internal power detection problem.

Check Battery Charge Circuit Status

  • If the alarm continues, use the appropriate service diagnostics per facility policy.
  • Review battery voltage, charge status, and battery charge circuit indicators if available.
  • Confirm whether the system detects the battery and whether the charge circuit appears to function correctly.
  • Do not continue clinical use if the system cannot confirm usable battery backup.

Replace the Battery if Indicated

  • Replace the battery if it is connected but will not charge, reads abnormally, fails diagnostics, or continues to trigger the alarm.
  • Use the correct approved replacement battery for the device.
  • After replacement, reconnect AC power, allow charging as needed, power cycle the system, and verify the alarm clears.
  • Run required checkout and testing before returning the machine to service.

Determine Whether the Problem Is Battery-Related or Device-Related

  • If the alarm clears after reconnecting, charging, or replacing the battery, the issue was likely battery-related.
  • If the alarm remains after battery replacement and verified AC power, suspect the battery harness, charge circuit, power detection circuit, or internal electronics.
  • Stop external troubleshooting when the fault points to internal repair or board-level evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the BATTERY MISSING alarm remains after confirming AC power, battery installation, battery connection, charge time, and battery replacement if needed, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent BATTERY MISSING conditions may indicate an internal battery charge circuit, battery detection, wiring, or power-related failure.

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send the unit for repair, vendor service, or bench evaluation.
  • Document that the unit should not be used clinically until battery backup operation is verified.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot battery backup alarms on an active patient unless patient safety is already protected. Move the patient to a backup anesthesia machine or alternate approved ventilation setup first when continued machine reliability is in question.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Aisys anesthesia machine displayed BATTERY MISSING alarm during startup with No battery backup message."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified stable AC power. Battery backup was not detected during startup. Battery connection, charge condition, and battery charge circuit were checked."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Connected battery and left unit plugged into AC power to charge. Rechecked startup condition. Replaced battery after alarm persisted, completed required checkout, and verified BATTERY MISSING alarm cleared."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

BATTERY MISSING should be treated as a backup power safety issue, not just a startup message. Clinical Engineering should confirm the alarm, check AC power and battery connection first, allow charging, verify diagnostics, and escalate when the machine cannot reliably detect battery backup.

That is successful troubleshooting.
