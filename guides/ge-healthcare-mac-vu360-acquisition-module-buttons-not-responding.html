GE Healthcare MAC VU360

Acquisition Module Buttons Not Responding

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

MAC VU360

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting MAC VU360 Acquisition Module ECG, Stop, Rhythm, or barcode scan buttons that do not respond correctly.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm whether the MAC VU360 is connected to a patient or being used for an active ECG.

If the Acquisition Module buttons are not responding during patient use, have staff complete care using another available ECG machine before troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: The patient is not dependent on a device with unreliable acquisition, rhythm, stop, or barcode controls.

If this resolves the immediate clinical need, stop troubleshooting until the unit is safe to evaluate.

Confirm the Exact Button Complaint

Ask clinical staff which button failed and what the device did when pressed.

Identify whether the issue involves:

  • ECG button not starting acquisition
  • Rhythm button not starting rhythm printout or recording
  • Stop button not stopping a report or printout
  • Barcode scan button not initiating barcode scan function
  • Multiple Acquisition Module buttons not responding
  • Intermittent button response

Expected outcome: The failure is clearly defined before testing.

Check Whether the Main Unit Still Responds

Use the MAC VU360 touchscreen or main controls to confirm whether the ECG machine itself is operating normally.

Expected outcome: If the main unit responds but Acquisition Module buttons do not, the issue is more likely isolated to the Acquisition Module, its cable path, or button assembly.

If the entire ECG machine is locked up, frozen, or nonresponsive, address the system lockup or power issue first.

Inspect the Acquisition Module Cable Connection

Check that the Acquisition Module cable is fully seated and not loose, pulled, pinched, or strained.

Look for bent pins, damaged connectors, broken strain relief, or signs that the cable has been stretched during use.

Expected outcome: A secure cable connection supports button communication between the Acquisition Module and the MAC VU360.

If reseating the cable restores button response, verify operation and stop.

Inspect the Physical Buttons

Examine the ECG, Stop, Rhythm, and barcode scan buttons for sticking, cracking, missing button covers, depressed buttons, or inconsistent tactile feel.

Expected outcome: Buttons should move normally and feel consistent when pressed.

If a button is physically damaged or stuck, remove the device from service and escalate for repair.

Check for Fluid Intrusion or Contamination

Inspect the Acquisition Module for fluid residue, cleaning chemical buildup, dried gel, corrosion, or debris around the buttons and seams.

Do not continue using a module with suspected fluid intrusion.

Expected outcome: The module should be clean, dry, and free of contamination that could interfere with button operation.

If fluid intrusion, corrosion, unusual smell, or sticky residue is found, remove the device from service and escalate.

Confirm the Barcode Scanner Function Separately

If only the barcode scan button is reported as not working, confirm whether the barcode reader itself is connected and functional.

Try a known-good barcode and verify that the barcode reader is not the actual cause of the complaint.

Expected outcome: The issue is separated between the Acquisition Module scan button and the barcode reader function.

If the barcode reader is the problem, troubleshoot the barcode reader path before suspecting the Acquisition Module buttons.

Run Acquisition Pushbutton Diagnostics

Access the MAC VU360 diagnostic menu and run Acquisition Pushbutton Diagnostics.

Test the ECG, Stop, Rhythm, and barcode scan button functions as prompted.

Expected outcome: Each button press should register correctly during diagnostics.

If all buttons pass diagnostics, attempt normal operation again and confirm with staff.

If a button fails diagnostics after cable and physical checks, remove the device from service and escalate.

Check for Intermittent Operation

If diagnostics pass but the complaint was intermittent, gently reposition the Acquisition Module and cable while repeating button checks.

Do not force or excessively flex the cable.

Expected outcome: Button operation should remain consistent during normal handling.

If button response drops out when the cable or module is moved, suspect a cable, connector, or module fault and remove the unit from service.

Verify Final Function Before Returning to Use

Confirm that the ECG, Stop, Rhythm, and barcode scan button functions respond normally.

Expected outcome: The Acquisition Module buttons operate consistently, and the unit is safe to return to service.

If button response cannot be verified, do not return the ECG machine to clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If the Acquisition Module buttons still do not respond after checking patient safety, main unit response, cable seating, physical button condition, contamination, barcode function, and Acquisition Pushbutton Diagnostics, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal to the Acquisition Module, cable path, connector, or related control circuitry.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, bench evaluation, or Acquisition Module replacement

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. A device with unreliable ECG, Stop, Rhythm, or barcode control buttons should not remain in patient care use.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot Acquisition Module button failures while the ECG machine is connected to an active patient. Move the patient workflow to another ECG machine first, especially if the Stop button is unreliable or reports cannot be controlled safely.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the MAC VU360 Acquisition Module ECG button would not start an ECG acquisition."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Acquisition Module cable was secure, no fluid intrusion was found, but the ECG button failed Acquisition Pushbutton Diagnostics."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the MAC VU360 from service, labeled it Out of Service, and escalated for Acquisition Module repair or replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Which button failed: ECG, Stop, Rhythm, or barcode scan
  • Whether one button or multiple buttons were affected
  • Whether the main MAC VU360 controls still responded
  • Acquisition Module cable condition
  • Connector condition
  • Button physical condition
  • Evidence of fluid, residue, gel, corrosion, smell, heat, or damage
  • Barcode reader behavior, if barcode scan button was involved
  • Acquisition Pushbutton Diagnostics result
  • Whether the failure was constant or intermittent
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Acquisition Module button issues should be handled logically and safely. Confirm the exact failure, rule out cable damage, contamination, barcode reader issues, and simple connection problems before suspecting internal module failure. If the buttons cannot be verified through diagnostics, remove the ECG machine from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
