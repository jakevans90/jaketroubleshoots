Karl Storz Xenon Nova 300

Footswitch or Remote Not Responding

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Karl Storz

Model

Xenon Nova 300

What This Guide Helps With

Footswitch or remote control not activating light functions due to connection issues, incorrect settings, or accessory faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the footswitch or remote is required during a procedure, notify staff and transition to manual control or backup equipment
  • Ensure the procedure continues safely without interruption
  • Loss of control can delay adjustments during critical visualization

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to activate the light source using the footswitch or remote
  • Observe for no response, intermittent response, or delayed activation
  • Confirm the issue is reproducible
  • Confirms this is not user technique or timing-related

Check Footswitch/Remote Connection (Most Common Cause)

  • Inspect the cable connection at the light source
  • Ensure connector is fully seated
  • Disconnect and reconnect firmly
  • Check for bent pins or loose connectors
  • Loose or misaligned connections commonly prevent signal transmission

Inspect Cable and Physical Condition

  • Check the entire length of the footswitch or remote cable
  • Look for cuts, fraying, or crushed sections
  • Inspect connector ends for damage
  • Damaged wiring interrupts control signals

Confirm Correct Port Usage

  • Ensure the footswitch or remote is plugged into the correct control port on the unit
  • Incorrect port placement results in no response

Test with Known Good Footswitch or Remote

  • Swap with a known working accessory if available
  • Determine if the replacement works correctly
  • Isolates whether the issue is accessory-related or system-related

Check System Settings (If Applicable)

  • Review system configuration for remote or footswitch enablement
  • Confirm control input is enabled
  • Check for mode settings that disable external control
  • Some configurations can disable external inputs

Power Cycle the Light Source

  • Turn the unit off
  • Wait 10–15 seconds
  • Power back on
  • Clears temporary communication faults

Observe for Additional Symptoms

  • Check for no response from all external controls
  • Look for error indicators or warning lights
  • Monitor for unusual system behavior
  • Multiple failures may indicate internal control board issues

If the Problem Persists

If all external connections, accessories, and settings have been verified, the issue is likely internal (control input circuitry or interface failure).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and reflects proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot footswitch or remote issues during an active procedure. Always ensure the clinician has alternative manual control or backup equipment first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Footswitch not activating light source during endoscopic procedure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed footswitch cable identified after testing with known good unit."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced defective footswitch and verified proper operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior or lack of response from controls
  • Accessories swapped (footswitch or remote)
  • Power behavior during testing
  • Environmental factors affecting connections
  • Indicator lights or system responses
  • Final device status (operational or removed from service)

Final Thought

Start with simple external checks—connections, cables, and accessories—before suspecting internal failure. Maintaining control functionality is critical for procedural efficiency and safety. Escalate appropriately and document clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
