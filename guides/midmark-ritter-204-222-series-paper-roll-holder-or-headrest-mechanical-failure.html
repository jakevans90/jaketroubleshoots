Midmark Ritter 204 / 222 Series

Paper Roll Holder or Headrest Mechanical Failure

Asset Type

Exam Table

Manufacturer

Midmark

Model

Ritter 204 / 222 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Loose, broken, or non-functioning paper roll holder or headrest causing instability, inability to secure paper, or improper patient positioning.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the table from active patient use before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient safely relocated to another table.
  • Why it matters: Headrest or support failure can lead to patient discomfort or injury.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Inspect the paper roll holder and headrest for looseness, detachment, or improper function.
  • Expected: Identify whether issue is structural, alignment-related, or hardware-related.
  • Why it matters: Confirms exact failure point before adjustments.

Inspect Paper Roll Holder Mounting Points

  • Check brackets, rods, and mounting screws securing the paper roll holder.
  • Expected: All hardware present, aligned, and tight.
  • Why it matters: Loose or missing hardware is the most common cause of holder failure.
  • If loose → tighten and retest
  • If missing/damaged → replace hardware or remove from service

Check Paper Roll Rod Condition and Fit

  • Remove and inspect the paper roll rod for bending, warping, or improper seating.
  • Expected: Rod is straight and fits securely in holders.
  • Why it matters: A bent rod prevents proper rotation and paper feeding.
  • If damaged → replace rod

Inspect Headrest Adjustment Mechanism

  • Operate the headrest adjustment (manual lever, friction joint, or ratcheting mechanism).
  • Expected: Smooth movement with ability to hold position firmly.
  • Why it matters: Worn or slipping mechanisms lead to drifting or instability.
  • If slipping → check for loose fasteners or worn friction components

Check Headrest Mounting and Support Structure

  • Inspect hinges, brackets, and attachment points between headrest and table frame.
  • Expected: No excessive play, wobble, or separation.
  • Why it matters: Structural looseness directly impacts patient support.
  • If loose → tighten hardware
  • If cracked or bent → remove from service

Inspect Upholstery and Padding Integrity (Headrest Area)

  • Check for torn upholstery, compressed padding, or exposed internal structure.
  • Expected: Surface intact and supportive.
  • Why it matters: Damage may indicate deeper structural issues or create infection control concerns.
  • If damaged → send for upholstery repair or replacement

Check for Interference or Misalignment

  • Ensure no components are obstructing headrest movement or paper roll rotation (covers, accessories, debris).
  • Expected: Full, unobstructed range of motion.
  • Why it matters: Misalignment can mimic mechanical failure.

Functional Test

  • Reinstall paper roll and operate headrest through full range.
  • Expected: Paper feeds smoothly and headrest holds position without drift.
  • Why it matters: Confirms issue resolution under normal use conditions.
  • If resolved → return to service

If the Problem Persists

If all external hardware, alignment, and mounting issues have been ruled out, the problem is likely due to internal wear or structural failure.

Remove the device from service, label Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unsafe equipment use and unnecessary disassembly.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot headrest or support components with a patient on the table. Always transfer the patient first to prevent injury from sudden movement or collapse.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Paper roll holder loose and headrest not holding position during patient exams."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose mounting hardware at headrest hinge and misaligned paper roll bracket."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Tightened hardware, realigned components, and verified proper function."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Mounting hardware condition (loose/missing)
  • Headrest movement (drifting, slipping, stable)
  • Paper roll rotation (smooth vs binding)
  • Presence of bent rod or brackets
  • Upholstery condition
  • Final device status (returned to service or removed)

Final Thought

Mechanical issues like these are often simple but directly impact patient safety and usability. Always start with visible hardware and alignment before assuming deeper failure. Proper escalation and clear documentation ensure safe and efficient resolution.

That is successful troubleshooting.
