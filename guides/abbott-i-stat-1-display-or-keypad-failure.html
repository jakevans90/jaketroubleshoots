Abbott i-STAT 1

Display or Keypad Failure

Asset Type

Blood Gas Analyzer

Manufacturer

Abbott

Model

i-STAT 1

What This Guide Helps With

Unresponsive keypad, blank or dim screen, or incorrect display behavior caused by battery, contamination, or external damage.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

  • Do not troubleshoot while the analyzer is needed for active patient testing. Move testing to another analyzer.

Expected: No interruption to patient care
Why it matters: Delays in blood gas results can impact clinical decision-making

2. Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the analyzer and attempt normal operation.
  • Observe:
    • Blank or dim screen
    • Partial display or distorted text
    • Keypad buttons not responding or delayed response

Expected: Issue is reproducible
Why it matters: Confirms a true hardware/interface issue vs user error

3. Check Battery Condition (Most Common Cause)

  • Low or unstable battery power can cause display and keypad malfunctions.
  • Action:
    • Remove and reseat the battery
    • Replace with a known-good, fully charged battery

Expected: Device powers on with normal display and keypad response
Why it matters: Insufficient voltage can affect both screen and input functionality

If resolved, remove faulty battery from service.

4. Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Examine the screen and keypad for visible issues.
  • Action:
    • Check for cracks, impact damage, or worn keypad buttons
    • Look for signs of fluid intrusion

Expected: No physical damage present
Why it matters: Damage can prevent proper input or display function

5. Check for Contamination (Very Common in POC Devices)

  • Residue from gloves, fluids, or cleaning agents can affect keypad responsiveness.
  • Action:
    • Inspect keypad for sticky or unresponsive buttons
    • Clean surface using approved disinfectant wipes (per facility policy)
    • Allow device to fully dry before retesting

Expected: Keys respond normally after cleaning
Why it matters: Surface contamination can block electrical contact

6. Perform a Power Reset

  • Resetting the device can clear temporary faults.
  • Action:
    • Power off the analyzer
    • Remove battery for 30–60 seconds
    • Reinstall battery and power back on

Expected: Device boots normally with responsive display and keypad
Why it matters: Clears minor software/interface glitches

7. Check Operating Environment

  • Environmental factors can impact display visibility and keypad performance.
  • Action:
    • Verify device is within normal temperature range
    • Avoid direct bright light affecting screen visibility

Expected: Screen is clearly visible and responsive
Why it matters: Extreme conditions can degrade LCD performance

If the Problem Persists

If display or keypad issues continue after verifying battery, cleaning, and reset, common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (display module, keypad membrane, or internal circuitry).

Remove the analyzer from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and wasted troubleshooting time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never rely on a partially functioning keypad or unreadable display during patient testing. Always switch to a fully functional analyzer before proceeding.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Analyzer display blank and keypad unresponsive during blood gas testing."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery voltage low causing improper display and input function."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced battery with known-good unit; device returned to normal operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery swapped and tested
  • Display condition (blank, dim, distorted)
  • Keypad response (none, intermittent, delayed)
  • Signs of contamination or fluid exposure
  • Physical damage noted
  • Reset performed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Display and keypad issues on handheld analyzers are often power or contamination-related. Start with simple, external checks and avoid over-troubleshooting. When those are ruled out, escalate appropriately. Clear documentation ensures reliability and supports patient safety.

That is successful troubleshooting.
