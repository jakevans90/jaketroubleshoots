Philips SureSigns VS4

Display Not Powering On

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

SureSigns VS4

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the Philips SureSigns VS4 monitor display does not power on. Symptoms may include a completely black screen, no startup lights, and no audible alerts. Most issues are related to power source problems, battery depletion, or external cable/connectivity issues before considering internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Confirm the wall outlet is live by plugging in a known working device.
  • Check for tripped breakers or switched-off outlet.
  • Why: A dead outlet is a common external cause of no display power.

Check Power Cord and Connection

  • Ensure the power cord is fully seated in the monitor and the outlet.
  • Inspect the cord for cuts, frays, or kinks.
  • Why: Damaged cords can prevent the device from receiving power.

Test with Battery and AC Power Separately

  • Remove the battery and plug in the AC power. Attempt to power on.
  • If the battery is installed, remove and reseat it; ensure charge indicator shows.
  • Why: This isolates whether the issue is battery-related or AC power-related.

Look for Indicator Lights

  • Observe any LED activity on the front panel or near the power button.
  • Why: Some minor LED flashes indicate system diagnostics or error codes.

Perform a Soft Reset

  • If the device has a reset button or sequence (refer to the quick reference), press and hold as directed.
  • Why: Some monitors may hang in a low-power or unresponsive state that prevents powering on.

Swap Known-Good Power Accessories

  • If available, use a verified working power cord or battery from another VS4.
  • Why: Confirms whether the original accessory is faulty.

If the Problem Persists

External causes (outlet, cord, battery) have been ruled out.

The issue is likely internal, such as a failed power supply or internal board failure.

Next Steps:

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send to Philips-authorized repair or internal bench evaluation.
  • Do not attempt internal disassembly beyond safe external checks.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt to use this monitor on an active patient until power is confirmed.
  • Move patient care to a functioning monitor.
  • Only troubleshoot on a powered-off or safe device to avoid patient harm.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor will not power on; screen remains black, no lights or beeps."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet functionality, checked and reseated power cord and battery, attempted soft reset; device remains unresponsive."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed monitor from service, labeled Out of Service, submitted for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested with a known-working device
  • Power cord visually inspected
  • Battery status checked
  • Any LED activity or absence noted
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Always prioritize patient safety by removing non-functional devices from clinical use. Systematic, stepwise troubleshooting ensures minor issues are resolved efficiently while preventing unnecessary escalation. Proper CCR documentation provides a clear history for future maintenance or repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
