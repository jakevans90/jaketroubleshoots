GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B650

Alarm: Speaker Failure

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B650

What This Guide Helps With

Speaker Failure alarm at startup causing no audible alarms or tones due to speaker, cable, or internal audio hardware failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use if audible alarms are not functioning
  • Transfer monitoring to a working unit
  • Confirm patient is safely monitored with audible alarm capability

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Power cycle the monitor
  • Observe startup sequence carefully
  • Listen for alarm tones or speaker test sounds
  • Confirm the alarm appears during startup

Check Alarm Volume and Mute Settings

  • Navigate to alarm/audio settings after startup
  • Ensure volume is not set to minimum
  • Confirm alarms are not muted or silenced

Inspect External Speaker Area and Physical Condition

  • Check speaker grille for blockage such as tape or debris
  • Inspect for fluid contamination or damage
  • Ensure speaker opening is unobstructed

Check Speaker Cable Connection

  • Verify any accessible speaker connections are secure
  • Gently check for loose or partially connected cables
  • Do not disassemble the device

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn OFF the monitor
  • Disconnect AC power
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect and power ON

Evaluate Audio Behavior

  • No sound at all indicates likely speaker or cable failure
  • Distorted sound indicates possible speaker degradation
  • No startup tone with alarm present suggests internal audio circuit failure

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out, the alarm indicates internal hardware failure.

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a fully functional monitor before continuing.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Speaker Failure alarm during startup with no audible tones."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"No sound output during startup test after verifying settings and external inspection, indicating internal audio failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent for repair to evaluate speaker or internal audio circuitry."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Speaker failure alarms directly impact patient safety by eliminating audible alerts. Focus on ruling out simple external causes quickly and escalate when hardware failure is indicated. Clear documentation ensures efficient repair and safe return to service.

That is successful troubleshooting.
