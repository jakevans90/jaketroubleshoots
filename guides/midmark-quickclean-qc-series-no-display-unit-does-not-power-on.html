Midmark QuickClean QC Series

No Display / Unit Does Not Power On

Asset Type

Ultrasonic Cleaner

Manufacturer

Midmark

Model

QuickClean QC Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting a blank display or unpowered QuickClean caused by outlet, cord, rear switch, GFI, fuse, or internal power faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

Remove the ultrasonic cleaner from use while the unit has no display or will not power on.

Confirm that no instrument-processing cycle is needed immediately, and direct staff to another approved cleaning process or working ultrasonic cleaner according to facility policy.

If fluid is present, use caution around the power connection. Do not energize the unit if there is visible leakage, fluid near electrical components, a burnt odor, unusual heat, or cord damage.

Expected outcome: The unit is safely available for evaluation without creating an electrical or instrument-processing risk.

2. Verify the Reported Condition

Confirm the reported symptom:

  • Display remains completely blank.
  • Unit does not respond to keypad input.
  • Rear power switch does not illuminate, if applicable.
  • No normal startup indication occurs.

Ask staff whether the issue occurred after cleaning, moving the unit, a power interruption, a tripped GFI, or a fluid spill.

Expected outcome: A true no-power or no-display condition is confirmed rather than an interrupted cycle or operational misunderstanding.

3. Inspect the Unit Externally Before Applying Power

Inspect the ultrasonic cleaner for:

  • Damaged or wet power cord.
  • Loose power inlet connection.
  • Cracked housing or display area.
  • Fluid leakage beneath or around the unit.
  • Burnt smell, discoloration, or evidence of overheating.

Do not proceed with powered testing if electrical damage, leakage, or overheating is present.

Expected outcome: No visible condition is found that would make continued troubleshooting unsafe.

4. Verify the Power Cord Connection

Confirm that the power cord is fully seated at the rear power inlet and firmly connected to the wall outlet.

If the cord is detachable and a known-good compatible cord is available, substitute it for testing.

Expected outcome: The unit powers on and the display illuminates normally.

If resolved: Return the original damaged or unreliable cord from service as appropriate, document the correction, and stop troubleshooting.

5. Check the Rear Main Power Switch

Place the rear main power switch in the ON position and observe whether the switch illuminates and whether the display starts.

Midmark identifies the main power connection and rear main power switch as initial checks for a QuickClean with no display.

Expected outcome: The illuminated power switch and LCD display indicate normal startup.

If resolved: Confirm basic unit operation while empty of instruments, document the finding, and stop troubleshooting.

6. Test the Electrical Outlet and GFI Protection

Verify that the outlet is functional using an approved outlet tester or another appropriate test method.

If connected to a GFI-protected outlet, check whether the GFI has tripped. Reset it only when safe and in accordance with facility practice.

When possible, test the unit on a known-good compatible outlet to determine whether the issue follows the cleaner or remains with the outlet.

Midmark lists a tripped or weak/faulty GFI outlet among possible causes of a no-power condition.

Expected outcome: The unit powers on from a known-good outlet, identifying the original outlet or GFI as the likely cause.

If resolved: Keep the unit on an approved functional outlet and report the failed outlet or GFI for facilities/electrical correction.

7. Inspect the Accessible Power-Entry Fuse Condition, When Applicable

With the unit unplugged and drained or positioned safely, inspect the externally accessible rear power-entry fuse location, if present on the specific QuickClean configuration.

Check for an open fuse only using approved Clinical Engineering procedures. Replace a failed fuse only with the exact approved type and rating.

Midmark identifies rear bath/IEC fuses as a possible cause of a QuickClean no-display or no-power condition.

Expected outcome: Correctly rated replacement fuses restore normal display and power operation without immediately failing again.

If resolved: Perform a functional verification and document the fuse replacement.

If a replacement fuse opens again: Stop testing. A repeated fuse failure suggests an internal electrical fault requiring repair evaluation.

8. Perform a Final External Function Check

If power and display operation return, verify that:

  • The display remains stable.
  • Keypad controls respond normally.
  • No unusual noise, heat, odor, or leakage is present.
  • The unit can begin and stop a test cycle according to department practice, using proper fluid level and no patient-care instruments.

The QuickClean uses a digital LCD control panel and includes fluid-level sensing, so a stable display and safe basic cycle response are appropriate functional observations following a power-related correction.

Expected outcome: The cleaner demonstrates normal basic operation and can be returned to service according to departmental policy.

If the Problem Persists

If the power cord, outlet, GFI condition, rear power switch position, and accessible fuse condition have been checked and the display remains blank, common external causes have been ruled out.

The failure is likely related to an internal power supply, display, control connection, power switch, or associated circuitry. Midmark’s service troubleshooting chart identifies internal ribbon cable, power-supply fuse, missing operating voltage, and LCD faults as additional no-display causes; these conditions require repair-level evaluation rather than further external troubleshooting.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service.
  • Labeled Out of Service.
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation according to facility procedure.

Knowing when to stop after ruling out external causes is proper troubleshooting and protects staff from electrical hazards and unreliable instrument-processing equipment.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not allow staff to rely on an ultrasonic cleaner that will not power on or has an unstable display. Confirm an alternate approved instrument-cleaning process is available before removing the unit for repair.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Midmark QuickClean QC Series ultrasonic cleaner had a blank display and would not begin a cleaning cycle."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Unit remained without display operation after confirming a working outlet, secure power cord connection, rear power switch position, and normal external fuse condition."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the ultrasonic cleaner from service, applied an Out of Service label, and routed the unit for bench evaluation of the internal power/display circuit."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result.
  • GFI status and whether it was reset.
  • Power cord condition or whether a known-good cord was substituted.
  • Rear power switch illumination status.
  • Accessible fuse condition and replacement rating, if replaced.
  • Presence of fluid leakage, unusual heat, or burnt odor.
  • Whether the display returned during testing.
  • Final device status: returned to service or removed for repair.

Final Thought

A blank display begins with basic electrical safety and external power checks, not assumptions of internal failure. Once the cord, outlet, switch, GFI, and accessible fuse conditions are addressed, proper escalation and clear CCR documentation complete the troubleshooting process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
