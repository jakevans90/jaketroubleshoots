GE Healthcare CARESCAPE B850

Error: 0xHOST1001 Internal temperature out of spec

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE B850

What This Guide Helps With

Error 0xHOST1001 indicating internal overheating causing shutdowns, instability, or unsafe operation due to temperature outside normal limits.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use if actively alarming or shutting down
  • Transition monitoring to a backup device

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the monitor and confirm Error: 0xHOST1001
  • Note when the error occurs (startup, after running, intermittent)
  • Check for symptoms such as excessive heat, fan noise, shutdowns, or sluggish performance

3. Allow Device to Cool Down

  • Power OFF the monitor
  • Disconnect from power
  • Allow the unit to sit powered off for 15–30 minutes

4. Check Environmental Conditions

  • Verify room temperature is within normal clinical range
  • Ensure the monitor is not near heat sources (blankets, vents, direct sunlight)
  • Confirm the device is not enclosed or tightly packed with other equipment

5. Inspect Airflow and Ventilation

  • Check all air vents for blockage (dust, linens, wall placement)
  • Ensure proper clearance around the monitor
  • Listen for internal fan operation during power-on

6. Inspect Physical Condition

  • Check for dust buildup around vents
  • Look for signs of liquid intrusion or contamination
  • Observe for unusual heat, odor, or noise

7. Use System Diagnostics (Webmin / Hardware Stats)

  • Access system diagnostics if available
  • Review internal temperature readings and hardware stats
  • Identify abnormal temperature values or sensor alerts

If the Problem Persists

If Error 0xHOST1001 continues after cooling and environmental checks, external causes have been ruled out. This indicates likely internal cooling failure such as fan malfunction, sensor fault, or board-level issue. Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always transition the patient to a backup monitor before evaluating overheating or instability issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor displaying Error 0xHOST1001 with intermittent shutdown and excessive heat during operation."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Internal temperature out of specification; airflow and environmental causes ruled out, indicating likely internal cooling or sensor failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Unit powered down and cooled. Airflow and environment verified. Error persisted. Device removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Temperature-related errors are protective but critical. Always rule out airflow and environmental causes first, but escalate quickly if overheating persists to protect patient safety and equipment integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
