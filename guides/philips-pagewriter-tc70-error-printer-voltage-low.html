Philips PageWriter TC70

Error: Printer voltage low

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the Printer voltage low error on the Philips PageWriter TC70 Electrocardiograph. This condition may disable ECG printing, interrupt printer initialization, or indicate that the printer supply voltage is below the expected range.

The goal is to protect patient care, verify the reported condition, rule out external power and printer-loading issues, and escalate the device for repair when the error persists.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the PageWriter TC70 is needed for active patient testing and printing is unavailable or unreliable, remove it from patient use.
  • Provide another verified ECG machine before continuing troubleshooting.
  • Do not depend on a cardiograph with unresolved printer power errors for required ECG printouts.
  • Expected result: Patient ECG acquisition and printing continue using a known-good device.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the cardiograph and confirm the exact message: Printer voltage low.
  • Check whether the message appears at startup, when the printer initializes, when attempting to print, or intermittently during operation.
  • Observe whether the printer fails to print, does not feed paper, starts then stops, sounds weak or abnormal, or leaves the unit usable on-screen but unable to print ECGs.
  • Expected result: The error is reproduced or clearly observed.

Connect to Known-Good AC Power

  • Plug the PageWriter TC70 directly into a known-good hospital-grade AC outlet.
  • Avoid power strips, extension cords, loose outlets, or battery-only operation during testing.
  • Confirm the AC power indicator shows the unit is receiving external power.
  • Expected result: The cardiograph powers normally from AC power before print testing continues.

Check Battery Status

  • Confirm the battery is installed, charged, and not showing a low battery condition.
  • If the battery is depleted, allow the cardiograph to charge on AC power before retesting the printer.
  • Expected result: The unit has adequate battery status or operates normally on AC power.

Restart the Cardiograph

  • Power the unit off while connected to AC power.
  • Wait briefly, then restart the cardiograph.
  • Watch for the error during bootup and printer initialization.
  • If the message clears and does not return, perform a test print and ECG print verification before returning the unit to service.
  • Expected result: The unit restarts without the Printer voltage low error.

Inspect the Printer Paper Drawer

  • Open and close the printer paper drawer fully.
  • Confirm the drawer latches securely and is not partially open, obstructed, cracked, or contaminated.
  • Expected result: The printer drawer closes properly and the printer initializes normally.

Check Printer Paper Installation

  • Verify the correct printer paper is installed.
  • Confirm the paper is loaded in the correct direction and is not jammed, wrinkled, wet, or damaged.
  • Reload or replace the paper if the paper path does not feed smoothly.
  • Expected result: Paper feeds without binding or excessive resistance.

Inspect the Visible Printer Area

  • With the unit removed from patient use, inspect the visible printer area for paper debris, torn paper, liquid contamination, broken drawer parts, or abnormal resistance.
  • Check for burnt smell, unusual heat, or visible damage.
  • If contamination, odor, heat, or damage is present, stop troubleshooting and remove the unit from service.
  • Expected result: No visible obstruction, contamination, odor, heat, or mechanical damage is found.

Perform a Test Print

  • Attempt a controlled test print or print a non-patient test ECG according to facility workflow.
  • Observe whether the printer prints normally, fails to feed, starts and stops, triggers the error again, or causes the unit to restart or freeze.
  • If the print succeeds and the error does not return after repeated checks, document the result before returning the device to service.
  • Expected result: The printer operates normally without the error returning.

Isolate Printer Load Versus General Device Function

  • Confirm whether the cardiograph can still perform non-printing functions such as startup, touchscreen operation, patient cable recognition, and ECG acquisition preview.
  • If non-printer functions work normally but printing fails, the issue is likely isolated to the printer power circuit, printer assembly, internal printer cable path, or main board printer supply.
  • Expected result: The failure pattern is clearly documented before repair escalation.

Do Not Perform Deep Internal Troubleshooting in Clinical Areas

  • Do not continue clinical-area troubleshooting if the error persists after AC power, battery, restart, printer drawer, paper loading, and visible printer checks.
  • Do not open the device or perform internal disassembly during clinical troubleshooting.
  • Expected result: The device is removed from service for bench evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If Printer voltage low continues after external checks, restart, AC power verification, battery review, printer drawer check, paper loading, and visible printer path inspection, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Printer voltage low error may indicate an internal printer power supply, printer assembly, internal cable, or main board-related failure condition.

Remove the PageWriter TC70 from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. Philips service support may be required.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first.

Do not rely on a PageWriter TC70 with unresolved printer voltage errors for active ECG workflow or required printed ECG documentation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips PageWriter TC70 reported Printer voltage low during printer initialization or ECG printing. Staff reported ECG printing was unavailable or unreliable."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified Printer voltage low error on startup or print attempt. AC power, battery status, printer drawer, paper loading, and visible paper path were checked. Error persisted, indicating likely internal printer supply voltage, printer assembly, cable path, or main board-related fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed PageWriter TC70 from patient use, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation or Philips service due to persistent Printer voltage low error."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Printer voltage errors should be treated as more than a paper issue once simple checks are complete. Confirm the error, protect patient workflow, rule out external causes, and escalate when the system continues reporting low printer supply voltage. Clear CCR documentation helps repair teams understand what was verified before service escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
