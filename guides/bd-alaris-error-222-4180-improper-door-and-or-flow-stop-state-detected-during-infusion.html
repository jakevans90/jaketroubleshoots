BD Alaris

Error: 222.4180 Improper door and/or flow stop state detected during infusion

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 222.4180 indicating a mismatch between door position and flow stop state, preventing infusion due to unsafe or unverified tubing conditions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the infusion immediately
  • Clamp the IV line if needed
  • Transfer the patient to another functioning pump if therapy must continue

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm Error 222.4180 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup, door closure, or during infusion)
  • Check for related alarms such as door open or flow stop alerts

3. Inspect Pump Module Door

  • Open and firmly close the module door
  • Ensure a full latch engagement (audible click)
  • Verify the door sits flush and closes without resistance

4. Check Roller Clamp / Flow Stop on IV Set

  • Ensure the roller clamp is open during operation
  • Verify tubing is correctly seated in the pumping channel
  • Confirm anti-free-flow (AFF) mechanism is properly aligned

5. Reseat the IV Set Properly

  • Remove and reload the tubing using proper routing
  • Ensure no twisting, pinching, or misalignment
  • Confirm AFF tab is correctly positioned in the door

6. Try a New IV Set

  • Replace with a known-good BD Alaris-compatible set
  • Ensure proper installation and alignment

7. Inspect Door and Latch Area (External Only)

  • Check for cracks, looseness, or wear in latch and hinge
  • Look for debris preventing full closure
  • Inspect AFF engagement area

8. Power Cycle the Module

  • Turn the module off and back on
  • Observe if the error clears during restart

9. Perform Test Infusion (Off Patient)

  • Run a controlled test infusion with proper setup
  • Verify normal operation without error

If the Problem Persists

If external causes have been ruled out and Error 222.4180 continues:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always transfer therapy to a backup device before investigating pump errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 222.4180 and infusion would not start."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Improper door/flow stop state detected. Verified proper tubing load and clamp position; issue persisted."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reloaded tubing and replaced IV set. Issue persisted, removed module from service for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Errors related to door and flow stop conditions are critical safety checks tied to anti-free-flow protection. Always verify setup and external factors first, but escalate quickly when safety sensing cannot be confirmed.

That is successful troubleshooting.
