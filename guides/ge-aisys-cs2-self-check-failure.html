GE Aisys CS2 Anesthesia Machine

System Self-Check Failure

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Aisys CS2

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the Aisys CS2 anesthesia machine:

  • Fails its automatic system self-check on startup
  • Displays error messages or alarms during the self-test
  • Does not transition to “ready for use” status

The focus is on verifying external factors and setup issues before assuming internal component failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify AC Power and Battery Backup

  • Ensure the machine is plugged into a working hospital-grade outlet.
  • Check that the AC power indicator is illuminated.
  • If the machine has battery backup, verify that it is charged.

Why: Power irregularities can cause self-check failures.

Inspect Main Circuit Breaker and Fuses

  • Confirm that the internal circuit breaker has not tripped.
  • Check accessible fuses for continuity if safe to do so.

Why: Electrical protection mechanisms can prevent startup.

Check Environmental Conditions

  • Ensure the machine is at the proper operating temperature (15–30°C / 59–86°F).
  • Avoid high humidity or condensation on internal sensors.

Why: Extreme conditions can prevent accurate self-checks.

Examine Gas Supplies and Connections

  • Confirm that oxygen, nitrous oxide, and air supplies are connected and turned on.
  • Check hoses and fittings for leaks or kinks.
  • Verify pipeline pressures are within manufacturer specifications.

Why: Gas supply failures can trigger system self-check errors.

Inspect Breathing Circuit and Vaporizer Installation

  • Ensure vaporizers are properly seated and locked.
  • Check the breathing circuit for correct assembly and no obstructions.

Why: Improper installation can prevent the machine from passing self-tests.

Perform a Restart Sequence

  • Power down the machine fully, wait 30 seconds, and power up again.
  • Observe if the self-check progresses further or displays the same error.

Why: Some self-test failures are transient and cleared by a full restart.

If the Problem Persists

External factors and setup errors have been ruled out. The self-check failure likely indicates an internal hardware or software fault.

  • Remove the machine from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send to GE Healthcare service or your biomedical repair bench for evaluation.

Escalation prevents patient risk and avoids further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt patient care on a machine that fails self-check. Move the patient to a verified working anesthesia machine before troubleshooting. Ensure patient safety is maintained at all times.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Self-check failed during machine startup; error displayed “System Self-Test Failure.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Confirmed all external power, gas, and circuit connections; machine continues to fail self-test.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Removed machine from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • AC power outlet tested
  • Battery backup checked
  • Gas pipeline pressures verified
  • Vaporizers seated properly
  • Breathing circuit checked
  • Restart attempted
  • Final status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Logical, stepwise troubleshooting ensures external causes are ruled out before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation allows follow-up repair and compliance with safety protocols. Escalating appropriately protects patients and equipment.

That is successful troubleshooting.
