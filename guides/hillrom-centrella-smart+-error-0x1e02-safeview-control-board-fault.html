Hillrom Centrella Smart+

Error: 0x1E02 SafeView Control Board Fault

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

Centrella Smart+

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error 0x1E02 indicating a SafeView control board fault, which can result in loss of SafeView functionality, monitoring features, or communication issues within the bed system.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the bed from patient use if SafeView monitoring is required
  • Transfer the patient to a functioning bed if needed
  • Do not rely on monitoring features during troubleshooting

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the bed and confirm Error 0x1E02 is displayed
  • Observe when the error appears (startup or during use)
  • Check if SafeView features are unavailable or intermittent

Perform a Basic Power Reset

  • Unplug the bed from AC power
  • Disconnect battery if accessible without disassembly
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Reconnect power and restart the bed

Check External Connections Related to SafeView

  • Inspect visible cables and harnesses associated with SafeView
  • Ensure all connectors are fully seated
  • Look for pinched, damaged, or disconnected wiring

Inspect for Environmental or Physical Issues

  • Check for signs of liquid intrusion
  • Inspect for physical damage to panels or housings
  • Look for debris or contamination affecting electronics

Isolate as Device-Level Failure

  • If error persists after reset and external checks, suspect internal failure
  • Confirm no external issues are contributing
  • Recognize this as a likely control board fault

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and the error remains, the SafeView control board is likely faulty.

  • Remove the bed from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or replace the SafeView control board

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a bed with an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a safe, functioning bed before addressing system faults.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Bed displaying Error 0x1E02 and SafeView system not functioning."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Persistent SafeView control board fault after power reset and cable inspection."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed bed from service and sent for SafeView control board replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

System-reported control board faults should be taken seriously. After ruling out power and connection issues, escalation ensures patient safety and efficient resolution.

That is successful troubleshooting.
