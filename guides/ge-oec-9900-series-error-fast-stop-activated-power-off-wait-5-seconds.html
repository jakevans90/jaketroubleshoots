GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: FAST STOP ACTIVATED – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot FAST STOP ACTIVATED – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS on the GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm. This error indicates that the interlocks are open, commonly because a FAST STOP switch was pressed. X-ray generation and most system functions, including column lift motors, may be disabled until the condition is cleared.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the C-Arm from patient use if the error appears during setup, positioning, or a procedure.
  • Confirm X-ray generation is disabled before attempting further use.
  • Notify clinical staff that the C-Arm should not be used until the FAST STOP condition is cleared.
  • If imaging is needed immediately, use another C-Arm or approved imaging method.
  • Do not continue clinical use with an active interlock shutdown condition.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Check the display for the exact message: FAST STOP ACTIVATED – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS.
  • Ask whether the error occurred during startup, during C-Arm movement, after pressing a FAST STOP switch, during procedure setup, or intermittently during use.
  • Check whether X-ray generation, system controls, or column lift motors are disabled.
  • Document the exact behavior before attempting repeated resets.

Power Down, Wait, and Reboot

  • Power the system off.
  • Wait at least 5 seconds.
  • Power the system back on.
  • Observe whether the system boots normally and whether the FAST STOP message returns.
  • If the error clears, verify basic system operation and confirm no FAST STOP switch remains engaged before returning the device to service.

Inspect the FAST STOP Switches

  • Locate the FAST STOP switches on the system.
  • Confirm that no switch is physically pressed in, stuck, cracked, loose, contaminated, or mechanically damaged.
  • Reset any activated switch according to normal use.
  • Power cycle the system again after confirming the switches are released.

Check for Accidental Activation or User Workflow Issue

  • Ask staff if a FAST STOP switch was pressed intentionally.
  • Check whether the C-Arm was being moved, cleaned, parked, or bumped when the error appeared.
  • Inspect the surrounding area for anything pressing against a FAST STOP switch.
  • If this was a one-time activation and the system resets normally, verify operation before returning to service.

Check External Cable and Connection Conditions

  • Verify the workstation and C-Arm interconnect cable is fully seated.
  • Check for cable strain, pinched sections, damaged jackets, bent pins, loose connections, or signs of fluid contamination.
  • Reseat accessible connectors only if safe and permitted by facility practice.
  • Do not open covers or perform internal disassembly during clinical troubleshooting.

Check for Physical Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect around the FAST STOP switch locations, column area, control panels, cable routing, and base.
  • Look for cracked housings, loose switch assemblies, fluid residue, damaged strain reliefs, or evidence of collision or rough transport.
  • If damage or contamination is found, remove the device from service for repair evaluation.

Evaluate for an Intermittent Interlock Condition

  • If the system sometimes boots normally, check whether movement near a specific switch, cable, or column area causes the error to return.
  • Do not continue using the unit clinically if the error is intermittent.
  • Document whether the issue appears tied to a specific switch area, cable path, or general interlock condition.

If the Problem Persists

If the error remains after power cycling, confirming FAST STOP switches are released, checking external cables, and inspecting for physical damage or contamination, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent FAST STOP ACTIVATED error may indicate an open interlock loop, failed FAST STOP switch, damaged wiring, connector issue, or internal interlock circuit failure.

Remove the C-Arm from service.

Label it Out of Service.

Send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not return the system to clinical use with an unresolved FAST STOP or interlock error.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. If the C-Arm shows a FAST STOP or interlock shutdown during a procedure, move the patient imaging need to a backup device first before attempting repeated resets, cable checks, or switch inspections.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE OEC 9900 displayed FAST STOP ACTIVATED – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS. X-ray generation and column lift functions were disabled during attempted use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"FAST STOP/interlock condition observed. FAST STOP switches and external interconnect cable were inspected. No physical damage found. Error cleared after proper power cycle."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Powered system off, waited 5 seconds, rebooted, verified FAST STOP switches were released, confirmed normal startup and basic system operation, and returned C-Arm to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A FAST STOP error should be treated as a safety-related shutdown first, not just a nuisance reset. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact message, check for an activated or stuck FAST STOP switch, inspect external connections, and escalate when the interlock condition remains unresolved. Clear documentation protects both the patient and the repair process.

That is successful troubleshooting.
