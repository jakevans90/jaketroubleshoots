Pedigo P-2055

Temperature Too High

Asset Type

Blanket Warmer

Manufacturer

Pedigo

Model

P-2055

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting high temperature complaints, overheating, incorrect setpoint, blocked airflow, sensor concerns, or unsafe blanket warmer heat conditions.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not continue using the blanket warmer if staff report unusually hot blankets, excessive cabinet heat, burning smell, or a high-temperature alarm.

Action:

  • Remove blankets from patient use until the temperature concern is verified.
  • If blankets feel excessively hot, notify clinical staff not to place them on patients.

Expected outcome:
Patient exposure to overheated blankets is prevented.

Why it matters:
Overheated blankets can cause patient burns, especially for sedated, elderly, neonatal, or low-sensation patients.

Verify the Reported Temperature Issue

Confirm whether the unit is actually overheating or if the complaint is related to setpoint expectations.

Action:

  • Check the displayed temperature, set temperature, actual cabinet temperature, and any alarm or warning behavior.
  • Ask staff whether the issue was constant or intermittent.

Expected outcome:
You know whether the warmer is above setpoint, heating continuously, or simply set higher than expected.

If the issue is explained by an incorrect setpoint, correct the setpoint, monitor operation, document findings, and stop.

Check the Temperature Setpoint

Verify the warmer is not set above the department’s approved temperature range.

Action:

  • Review the current temperature setting and compare it with facility policy for blanket warming.

Expected outcome:
The setpoint is appropriate for safe clinical use.

If the warmer was set too high, lower it to the approved range, allow the cabinet to stabilize, recheck temperature, document the user-setting issue, and stop.

Inspect for Overloading or Blocked Airflow

Overpacking the cabinet can trap heat and create uneven or excessive blanket temperatures.

Action:

  • Open the warmer and inspect how blankets are loaded.
  • Check for items blocking vents, covering internal airflow paths, or packed tightly against the temperature sensing area.

Expected outcome:
Blankets are loaded loosely enough for air circulation, and vents are not obstructed.

If airflow obstruction caused the issue, correct the loading condition, allow the warmer to stabilize, recheck temperature, document findings, and stop.

Confirm Only Approved Items Are Inside

Blanket warmers should not be used to warm fluids, unauthorized supplies, plastic-wrapped items, or equipment unless specifically approved.

Action:

  • Inspect the cabinet contents.
  • Remove any non-approved items and ask staff what had been stored in the warmer when the problem occurred.

Expected outcome:
The cabinet contains only approved warming items.

If inappropriate contents caused the high-temperature complaint, remove them, notify the department, document the finding, and stop.

Check Door Seal and Door Closure

A damaged gasket or door that does not close properly can cause unstable heating behavior and poor temperature control.

Action:

  • Inspect the door gasket, latch, hinges, and door alignment.
  • Confirm the door closes fully and seals evenly.

Expected outcome:
The door seals correctly with no obvious gaps, damage, or misalignment.

If the door does not seal correctly, remove the unit from service for repair evaluation.

Inspect the Area Around the Warmer

Poor ventilation around the unit can contribute to excess heat buildup.

Action:

  • Check that the warmer has adequate clearance around vents and is not placed near external heat sources, direct sunlight, covered vents, or restricted airflow.

Expected outcome:
The unit has proper surrounding ventilation and is not exposed to external heat sources.

If the location is causing heat buildup, correct the placement if allowed, monitor temperature, document the environmental cause, and stop.

Measure Cabinet Temperature Independently

Do not rely only on the display if the complaint is overheating.

Action:

  • Use a calibrated thermometer or temperature logger appropriate for equipment checks.
  • Place it in the cabinet and allow enough time for the reading to stabilize.

Expected outcome:
Independent temperature measurement is close to the warmer’s displayed temperature and within the approved operating range.

If the independent reading confirms excessive temperature, remove the unit from service.

Check for Continuous Heating Behavior

A warmer that continues heating past setpoint may have a control, relay, thermostat, or sensor issue.

Action:

  • Observe whether the heater cycles normally or appears to continue heating even after the cabinet reaches setpoint.

Expected outcome:
Heating cycles off or reduces appropriately as the unit reaches set temperature.

If heating appears uncontrolled, remove the unit from service and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Inspect for Obvious Damage, Smell, or Heat Stress

Look for external signs that the unit may be unsafe.

Action:

  • Check the power cord, plug, cabinet surfaces, controls, display, vents, and interior for discoloration, unusual smell, abnormal heat, melted material, or signs of electrical damage.

Expected outcome:
No obvious physical damage, burning smell, unusual heat, or unsafe condition is present.

If any damage, burning smell, or abnormal heat is found, unplug the warmer if safe to do so, label it Out of Service, and escalate for repair.

If the Problem Persists

If the Pedigo P-2055 continues to run hot after setpoint, loading, airflow, contents, location, and independent temperature checks are completed, the likely cause is internal temperature-control failure.

At that point, external causes have been ruled out and the device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Possible internal causes may include a failed temperature sensor, thermostat, control board, heater relay, or heating circuit issue. Do not continue clinical use until the warmer is verified safe. Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a suspected overheating blanket warmer while staff are actively pulling blankets for patient use. Remove the unit from service first, provide another warmer if available, move patient care to approved backup warming supplies when needed, and verify temperature safety before returning it to use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Pedigo P-2055 blanket warmer was running too hot and blankets felt hotter than normal."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Inspection found the cabinet was overloaded with blankets packed against the internal airflow path, causing poor heat circulation and elevated blanket temperature."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed excess blankets, restored proper airflow, verified cabinet temperature with an independent thermometer, and returned the warmer to service after temperature stabilized within the approved range."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Reported displayed temperature
  • Set temperature found on arrival
  • Independent temperature reading
  • Thermometer or logger used
  • Whether cabinet was overloaded
  • Whether vents were blocked
  • Door gasket condition
  • Any high-temperature alarm behavior
  • Any unusual smell, heat, discoloration, or melted material
  • Whether blankets were removed from patient use
  • Environmental factors, including nearby heat sources, direct sunlight, or restricted airflow
  • Power behavior and whether the heater appeared to run continuously
  • Indicator lights, display behavior, or warning messages observed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

High-temperature complaints on a blanket warmer should always be treated as a patient safety issue first. Start with setpoint, loading, airflow, contents, and location before assuming internal failure. If the warmer cannot control temperature safely, remove it from service and document the complaint, cause, and resolution clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
