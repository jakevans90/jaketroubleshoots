Mindray uMEC Series

Display or Touchscreen Malfunction

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Mindray

Model

uMEC Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting display or touchscreen malfunctions on Mindray uMEC series monitors. Issues may present as an unresponsive touchscreen, flickering or blank display, incorrect touch input, or display artifacts. Most problems are external or configuration-related and can often be resolved without opening the monitor.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Check Power Source

  • Verify the monitor is properly connected to a known working outlet or power strip.
  • Ensure the battery (if used) is charged and seated correctly.
  • Why: Many display issues mimic touchscreen failure when the device is underpowered.

Inspect the Display and Touchscreen Surface

  • Look for cracks, scratches, or residue on the touchscreen.
  • Clean the screen with a soft, lint-free cloth and approved cleaning solution.
  • Why: Debris or damage can prevent proper touch recognition.

Verify Cables and Connections

  • Confirm internal display connectors (if externally accessible) are seated firmly, especially after transport or maintenance.
  • Check any external monitor or USB cables if connected.
  • Why: Loose connections can cause flickering, blank screens, or unresponsive touch.

Perform a Monitor Restart

  • Power off the monitor, wait 30–60 seconds, and power it back on.
  • Why: Temporary software glitches may resolve with a full reboot.

Check for Software or Configuration Issues

  • Ensure the device is running the latest approved firmware.
  • Reset display or touch calibration settings through the monitor menu if available.
  • Why: Misconfigured settings can appear as hardware malfunction.

Test Touchscreen Functionality

  • Use a known working stylus or gloved/un-gloved finger depending on device spec.
  • Navigate through multiple menu screens to ensure touch registration is consistent.
  • Why: Confirms whether issue is full-screen or partial touch failure.

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out; the issue is likely internal (display panel, digitizer, or mainboard).

  • Remove the monitor from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Send it for repair or bench evaluation by authorized service.

Reminder: Stopping at this point is proper troubleshooting and ensures patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt to troubleshoot the touchscreen on a monitor attached to a patient.
  • Move the patient to a different monitor first.
  • Only test displays in a safe, controlled environment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Touchscreen is unresponsive, and display intermittently flickers.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Observed that the monitor is connected to a working outlet, cleaned screen, performed restart, but touchscreen remained unresponsive.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Monitor removed from service, labeled Out of Service, sent to vendor/biomed bench for evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Battery condition checked
  • Restart attempts made
  • Screen cleaned and inspected
  • Firmware version noted
  • Touch calibration attempted
  • Any unusual display artifacts observed

Final Thought

Troubleshooting logic helps differentiate between external factors and true internal failure. Document all steps carefully, prioritize patient safety, and escalate appropriately when internal repair is suspected. Clear documentation supports maintenance tracking and future problem resolution.

That is successful troubleshooting.
