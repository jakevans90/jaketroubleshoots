GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: HLF OVERTIME

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error: HLF OVERTIME on the GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm. This error occurs when High Level Fluoro or Digital Cine exceeds the preset time limit and the system terminates X-rays to help limit patient radiation dose.

The issue may be normal protective behavior after extended high-dose imaging, or it may point to a stuck X-ray control, footswitch issue, hand switch issue, cable problem, or abnormal exposure-control behavior if it happens repeatedly or unexpectedly.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not continue attempting fluoroscopy until the timeout condition is understood and cleared.
  • Remove the C-arm from active imaging use if X-rays will not re-enable or if the alarm cannot be cleared.
  • Confirm clinical staff are aware that High Level Fluoro or Digital Cine was terminated due to elapsed exposure time.
  • Use another imaging system if continued imaging is clinically urgent.
  • This alarm is directly related to radiation exposure control and patient dose protection.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the display shows Error: HLF OVERTIME.
  • Ask the operator what imaging mode was being used when the message appeared.
  • Confirm whether the mode was High Level Fluoro, Digital Cine, continuous high-dose imaging, or extended activation of the X-ray control.
  • Ask whether X-rays stopped automatically, whether an alarm sounded, and whether the message appeared after prolonged exposure.
  • Confirm whether X-rays were re-enabled after releasing the X-ray control.

Release the X-Ray Control

  • Have the operator release the active X-ray control, such as the hand switch or footswitch.
  • Allow the system to stop the exposure request fully.
  • Confirm the alarm silences and the message clears.
  • Confirm X-rays become available again after the control is released.
  • If the message clears normally and the unit operates correctly, this may have been normal timeout protection.

Confirm the X-Ray Control Is Not Stuck

  • Inspect the footswitch and hand switch for physical sticking or delayed release.
  • Check for a stuck pedal, jammed exposure button, damaged switch housing, fluid contamination, or debris around the control.
  • Inspect the control cable for strain, crushing, cuts, or signs of liquid exposure.
  • Disconnect and reconnect the control cable if appropriate and accessible.
  • Confirm the exposure control returns freely to its normal released position.

Check Operator Workflow

  • Ask whether High Level Fluoro or Digital Cine was held continuously for an extended period.
  • Confirm staff understand that the system may automatically terminate X-rays after the preset time limit.
  • Remind clinical staff to release and re-press the X-ray control only when clinically appropriate and within radiation safety practices.
  • Determine whether the alarm is related to expected high-dose imaging behavior rather than equipment failure.

Check for Repeated or Abnormal Alarm Behavior

  • Determine whether the alarm only occurs after extended High Level Fluoro or Digital Cine use.
  • Watch for abnormal behavior, such as the error appearing immediately when X-ray is requested.
  • Do not return the unit to service if the error appears without the X-ray control being pressed.
  • Do not return the unit to service if the error appears with no active exposure request.
  • Immediate or random HLF OVERTIME behavior may indicate an input-control, software, or system fault.

Inspect External Cables and Connections

  • Inspect the hand switch, footswitch, and related external cables.
  • Look for loose connectors, bent pins, damaged insulation, liquid contamination, crushed cables, or stretched cables.
  • Reseat accessible external connections where appropriate.
  • Confirm the X-ray controls communicate normally and the message does not return unexpectedly.
  • External exposure-control problems can create abnormal X-ray request behavior and repeated timeout alarms.

Verify Normal Imaging Recovery

  • Perform a basic functional check according to facility policy.
  • Confirm standard fluoro activates normally.
  • Confirm High Level Fluoro activates only when intentionally selected.
  • Confirm Digital Cine starts and stops properly.
  • Confirm the alarm is not present at idle.
  • Confirm X-rays stop when the control is released.

If the Problem Persists

If Error: HLF OVERTIME continues to appear after the X-ray control is released, or if it appears without extended High Level Fluoro or Digital Cine use, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent or abnormal HLF OVERTIME conditions may indicate a stuck or defective footswitch, stuck or defective hand switch, exposure-control input fault, software/control issue, generator issue, or system communication fault.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue using a C-arm with unresolved exposure-control or dose-protection behavior.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this error on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup imaging system first if imaging must continue and the alarm does not clear normally.

If the immediate issue is simply releasing the X-ray control and the message clears normally, verify proper operation before returning the system to clinical use.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Operator reported GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series displayed Error: HLF OVERTIME during High Level Fluoro or Digital Cine use. X-rays stopped and alarm sounded."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"High Level Fluoro or Digital Cine exceeded preset exposure time. System terminated X-rays as a dose-protection function. X-ray control was released and the message cleared."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Released X-ray control, confirmed alarm cleared, verified X-rays re-enabled, inspected footswitch and hand switch for sticking or damage, checked external cables, and confirmed normal imaging operation. Unit returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

HLF OVERTIME is a protective radiation-safety timeout, not automatically a hardware failure. Start by confirming the message, releasing the X-ray control, and checking for stuck or damaged exposure controls. If the error does not clear normally or returns without extended high-level imaging, remove the C-arm from service and escalate for repair. Clear documentation protects patient safety, staff safety, and the equipment service record.

That is successful troubleshooting.
