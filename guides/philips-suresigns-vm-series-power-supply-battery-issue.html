Philips SureSigns VM Series

Power Supply / Battery Issue

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

SureSigns VM Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting SureSigns VM power, charging, battery runtime, AC adapter, power cord, and startup issues before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the Philips SureSigns VM monitor is not the only active source of patient monitoring before removing power, disconnecting the battery, or rebooting the device.

If the patient requires active monitoring, have clinical staff move the patient to another working monitor first.

Expected outcome: Patient monitoring continues safely while the suspect monitor is evaluated.

Confirm the Exact Power Complaint

Ask clinical staff what they observed. Determine whether the issue is:

  • Monitor will not power on
  • Monitor powers on only with AC power
  • Monitor shuts off when unplugged
  • Battery will not charge
  • Battery runtime is short
  • Battery icon does not show charging
  • Power supply light is off or flickering
  • Unit turns off intermittently
  • Device was dropped, exposed to liquid, or has a damaged power connector

Expected outcome: The complaint is narrowed to AC power, external power supply, battery condition, charging circuit, or physical damage.

Check the Wall Outlet First

Verify the monitor is plugged into a known working hospital-grade outlet. Test the outlet with another approved device or outlet tester if appropriate.

Expected outcome: A failed outlet or switched outlet is ruled out before suspecting the monitor.

Inspect the Power Cord and AC Adapter

Check the power cord, brick-style power supply, and connector for cuts, bent pins, loose fit, exposed wiring, strain damage, heat discoloration, or missing ground pin if applicable.

Expected outcome: Damaged or unsafe external power components are identified and removed from use.

Verify the Power Supply Indicator Light

If the AC adapter has an indicator light, confirm it illuminates steadily when connected to the outlet and monitor.

Expected outcome: A steady indicator suggests the adapter is receiving AC power. No light or flickering may indicate outlet, cord, or adapter failure.

Confirm the Power Connector Is Fully Seated

Make sure the DC power connector is fully inserted into the SureSigns VM monitor and is not loose, angled, or easily interrupted when gently moved.

Expected outcome: The monitor receives stable external power without intermittent shutdowns.

Try a Known Good Power Supply if Available

Use a compatible, approved Philips power supply from a working SureSigns VM monitor if available. Do not substitute an unapproved adapter.

Expected outcome: If the monitor works normally with a known good adapter, the original power supply or cord is likely defective. Replace the external power supply and stop troubleshooting.

Observe the Monitor During Startup

Connect the monitor to verified AC power and attempt to power it on. Watch for display activity, indicator lights, fan or speaker activity if present, battery icon behavior, startup tones, or error messages.

Expected outcome: Startup behavior helps separate a no-power condition from a battery-only or charging-related complaint.

Check Battery Status on the Display

Once powered on, review the battery icon or battery status indicator. Look for low battery, charging symbol, battery missing, battery fault, or abnormal charge percentage behavior.

Expected outcome: The displayed battery condition helps identify whether the monitor recognizes the battery and charger.

Allow the Battery to Charge on Verified AC Power

If the monitor powers on with AC but has low battery, leave it connected to known good AC power long enough to confirm charging behavior.

Expected outcome: Battery percentage should increase or the charging indicator should remain active. If it does, return the device only after adequate charge and functional verification.

Check Whether the Monitor Runs on Battery Power

After confirming the battery has had time to charge, disconnect AC power only when the device is not being used on a patient. Observe whether the monitor remains powered.

Expected outcome: If the monitor immediately shuts off when unplugged, suspect a failed battery, unrecognized battery, or internal charging/power issue.

Compare Runtime to Expected Use

If staff report short runtime, confirm whether the battery was fully charged before use and whether the monitor has heavy parameter use, frequent alarms, or an aging battery.

Expected outcome: A repeatable short runtime after full charging supports battery replacement or further bench evaluation.

Inspect the Battery Area Externally

If the battery is externally accessible, inspect only to the level allowed by normal Clinical Engineering handling. Look for swelling, leakage, cracked housing, loose latch, corrosion, liquid residue, or overheating signs.

Expected outcome: Any battery with swelling, leakage, unusual heat, odor, or physical damage is unsafe and should be removed from service.

Check for Heat, Odor, or Electrical Safety Concerns

Feel near the power supply and monitor case for unusual heat. Check for burning smell, buzzing, visible damage, or evidence of liquid intrusion.

Expected outcome: Any sign of overheating, odor, liquid damage, or electrical damage requires immediate removal from service.

Perform a Controlled Power Cycle if Safe

With the monitor off-patient and connected to verified AC power, power the monitor off, wait briefly, and restart it.

Expected outcome: If the issue was a temporary startup or charging display glitch, the monitor may recover. If the power issue returns, continue evaluation.

Verify Basic Function After Power Is Restored

If power or charging is restored, confirm the monitor starts normally, displays battery/AC status correctly, and does not shut down when lightly repositioned.

Expected outcome: The device remains stable and may be returned to service if it passes normal operational checks.

Stop if the Issue Is Resolved

If the problem follows a damaged cord, failed AC adapter, bad outlet, or discharged battery that now charges normally, document the finding and corrective action.

Expected outcome: Troubleshooting ends once the external cause is confirmed and corrected.

If the Problem Persists

If the Philips SureSigns VM monitor still will not power on, will not charge, shuts down on battery, shows battery fault behavior, or has intermittent power after external checks are completed, common external causes have been ruled out.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Possible internal causes may include battery failure, charging circuit failure, DC input damage, internal power supply fault, or main board power management failure. Do not continue into internal disassembly during frontline troubleshooting unless following approved service procedures and facility policy.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot power or battery issues on an active patient monitor unless clinical staff have already moved the patient to another reliable monitoring source. A monitor with unstable power should not be trusted for continuous patient surveillance until the issue is corrected and verified.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported the Philips SureSigns VM monitor would shut off shortly after being unplugged from AC power."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified outlet and AC adapter were working, but the monitor would not sustain operation on battery after charging."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the monitor from service, labeled it Out of Service, and sent it for battery and charging system bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result
  • Power cord inspected or swapped
  • AC adapter indicator light status
  • Battery icon or charging symbol behavior
  • Whether monitor powers on with AC power
  • Whether monitor powers on with battery only
  • Approximate battery runtime
  • Any shutdown, reboot, or flickering behavior
  • Any unusual heat, odor, noise, or visible damage
  • Whether a known good power supply was tested
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power and battery issues should be approached from the outside in: outlet, cord, adapter, connector, battery indication, and runtime before suspecting internal failure. Patient safety comes first, and a monitor with unstable power should be removed from use until verified. Clear CCR documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the repair history.

That is successful troubleshooting.
