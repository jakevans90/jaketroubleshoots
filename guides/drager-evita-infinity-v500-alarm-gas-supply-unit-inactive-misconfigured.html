Drager Evita V500

Alarm: Gas supply unit inactive/misconfigured – gas supply not properly enabled or configured

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Evita V500

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses the alarm indicating the gas supply unit is inactive or misconfigured, which can prevent proper gas delivery and lead to inadequate or unavailable ventilation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient’s ventilation status
  • Switch to a backup ventilator or manual ventilation if needed
  • Do not troubleshoot on an unstable patient

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact alarm message is displayed
  • Observe when the alarm appears (startup or during operation)
  • Check if ventilation is limited or unavailable
  • Look for additional gas-related alarms

3. Check External Gas Supply Connections

  • Verify air and oxygen hoses are connected to correct outlets
  • Ensure all connections are secure and fully seated
  • Confirm adequate supply pressure is present

4. Verify Gas Supply Unit Configuration

  • Access the ventilator configuration or settings menu
  • Confirm the gas supply unit is enabled
  • Verify correct configuration for installed supply type
  • Check for recent configuration changes or resets

5. Power Cycle the Ventilator

  • Turn the ventilator off and back on
  • Observe if the alarm clears during startup
  • Watch for configuration prompts

6. Perform Device Check / System Test

  • Run a full device check or self-test
  • Ensure gas supply is connected during testing
  • Follow all on-screen prompts

7. Isolate Configuration vs. Device Issue

  • Compare settings with a known-good ventilator if available
  • Test with a known-good gas source
  • Confirm whether the issue follows the device or setup

8. Stop When Resolved

  • If the alarm clears, return the ventilator to service
  • Verify stable operation without recurring alarms

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues after verifying gas supply, configuration, and completing a device check, external causes have been ruled out. This indicates a likely internal configuration fault or gas supply module issue.

  • Remove the ventilator from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a ventilator with gas supply issues while connected to a patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup device first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator displayed Gas supply unit inactive/misconfigured alarm; unable to confirm proper gas delivery."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Gas supply unit found disabled or misconfigured; possible configuration mismatch or internal module fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Verified gas connections and pressure, corrected configuration, performed device check. Device removed from service when issue persisted."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Gas supply alarms directly impact ventilation performance and patient safety. Always start with external supply and configuration checks, then escalate appropriately if unresolved.

That is successful troubleshooting.
