STERIS AMSCO 400 Series

Alarm: Too Long To Close Door – Door switch does not make contact within allotted time

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 400 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Alarm: Too Long To Close Door indicating failure of the door to fully close and latch within the required time, preventing cycle start and proper chamber sealing.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not run sterilization cycles while the alarm is active
  • Do not consider any load sterile if the door did not fully seal
  • Keep unit out of service until resolved

Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm “Too Long To Close Door” is displayed
  • Observe if the door attempts to close but stops short
  • Check if the motor runs without full latch engagement
  • Determine if the issue is consistent or intermittent

Check for Door Obstruction

  • Inspect door opening and gasket area
  • Remove trays, wrappers, or debris blocking closure
  • Check gasket for folds, buildup, or damage
  • Attempt to close door again after clearing obstruction

Verify Proper Door Alignment and Closure

  • Ensure door is properly aligned with chamber
  • Check for smooth movement without binding
  • Look for visible misalignment or resistance
  • Attempt another close cycle

Check Door Switch (LS1 / LS2) Function

  • Observe if system recognizes door as closed
  • Watch for status change at full closure
  • Inspect accessible switch mounting and wiring
  • Check for loose or damaged connections

Evaluate Power Door Mechanism Operation

  • Observe motor performance during closure
  • Check for slow, weak, or stalled movement
  • Listen for abnormal noises (grinding, clicking)
  • Confirm consistent motor-driven closure

Stop if Resolved

  • If door closes properly and alarm clears, run a test cycle if appropriate
  • Return unit to service after confirming normal operation

If the Problem Persists

If obstruction, alignment, and external checks are normal but the alarm continues, internal causes are likely.

  • Remove the sterilizer from service
  • Label the unit as Out of Service
  • Escalate for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move workload to a backup sterilizer before continuing diagnostics.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displaying 'Too Long To Close Door' alarm. Door would not fully close and cycle could not be started."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Obstruction found in door gasket area preventing full closure and door switch activation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed obstruction, verified proper door closure, confirmed alarm cleared, and returned unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Door-related alarms directly impact sterilization safety. Always start with simple physical checks, confirm switch feedback, and escalate appropriately when mechanical or control failures are suspected.

That is successful troubleshooting.
