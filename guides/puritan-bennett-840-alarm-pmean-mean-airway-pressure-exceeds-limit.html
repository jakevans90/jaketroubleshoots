Puritan Bennett 840

Alarm: ↑PMEAN Mean airway pressure exceeds limit

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Puritan Bennett

Model

840

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the ↑PMEAN alarm, which indicates that the mean airway pressure is exceeding the configured limit. Elevated mean airway pressure can lead to lung overdistension, impaired venous return, and potential patient harm if not addressed promptly.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient’s respiratory status
  • Confirm adequate chest rise and oxygenation
  • If ventilation is compromised, provide manual ventilation or switch to a backup ventilator

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the ↑PMEAN alarm is displayed on the ventilator
  • Observe elevated mean airway pressure readings
  • Check if alarm occurs consistently during ventilation
  • Note any accompanying high pressure or volume alarms

3. Check Patient Condition

  • Assess for decreased lung compliance (e.g., ARDS, fluid overload)
  • Check for coughing, agitation, or breath stacking
  • Verify airway patency (ET tube position, secretions)

4. Inspect the Patient Circuit for Obstruction or Restriction

  • Check tubing for kinks, compression, or disconnections
  • Inspect for water accumulation in the circuit
  • Examine inspiratory and expiratory filters for blockage

5. Verify Ventilator Settings

  • Review PEEP settings
  • Check inspiratory time (I-time)
  • Evaluate tidal volume and pressure limits

6. Check for Expiratory Flow Issues

  • Inspect expiratory valve function
  • Check for obstruction in the expiratory limb
  • Ensure complete exhalation without air trapping

7. Run Ventilator Self Tests (SST/EST)

  • Run Short Self Test (SST)
  • Run Extended Self Test (EST) if available
  • Review results for internal faults

If the Problem Persists

If all external, patient-related, and configuration causes have been ruled out and the ↑PMEAN alarm continues, remove the ventilator from service. Label the unit as Out of Service and send it for bench evaluation and repair.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot pressure-related alarms while the patient is actively connected. Always transfer the patient to a backup ventilation source before performing further investigation.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ventilator alarming ↑PMEAN with elevated airway pressures during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"No external obstruction or setting issue identified; suspected internal pressure regulation issue."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed ventilator from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Mean airway pressure alarms directly impact patient safety and must be addressed immediately. Always start with patient and circuit checks before evaluating settings or internal issues. Proper escalation and clear documentation ensure safe and effective device management.
