GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Power Supply or Battery Issues

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting power supply or battery issues on the GE Panda Warmer. Symptoms may include the unit not powering on, intermittent shutdowns, alarms related to power or battery, or failure to charge the internal battery. The focus is on external checks before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify AC Power Source

  • Ensure the wall outlet is supplying power using a verified meter or a known working device.
  • Confirm the outlet is not controlled by a switch or GFCI that may have tripped.
  • Why: A failed or switched outlet is a common cause of “no power” issues.

Check Power Cord and Connections

  • Inspect the power cord for damage, fraying, or kinks.
  • Ensure the cord is fully seated in the warmer and the outlet.
  • Why: Damaged cords can prevent the unit from receiving power even if the outlet is live.

Inspect the Battery Indicator and Charge Status

  • Observe the on-screen battery icon or status LED when plugged in.
  • If the unit shows “charging,” allow at least 30 minutes and verify the indicator changes.
  • Why: Batteries can fail to charge due to poor connection or end-of-life cells.

Test with a Known Good AC Supply

  • If available, plug the warmer into a different verified outlet.
  • Why: Confirms the problem is not specific to the original power source.

Attempt Power-On Without Battery

  • If the battery is removable, try powering the unit directly from AC only.
  • Why: Determines if the issue is battery-related or affects the main power supply.

Reset the Unit (if safe and supported)

  • Perform a power cycle: turn off, unplug, wait 1–2 minutes, and restart.
  • Why: Sometimes minor electronic glitches can cause false power or battery alarms.

If the Problem Persists

External checks (outlet, cord, connection, battery seating) have been ruled out. The power supply or internal battery is likely failing.

  • Remove the warmer from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to authorized repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting—do not attempt internal board repair.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to troubleshoot or operate the warmer on a patient if the power supply or battery is unreliable. Transfer the infant to another verified warming device before performing troubleshooting. Ensure patient safety takes precedence over equipment troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Unit will not turn on; battery icon shows low despite being plugged in.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
External AC power verified; power cord and connections intact; battery not charging.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Unit removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair/bench evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested with meter or known device.
  • Power cord visually inspected and reseated.
  • Battery status observed (charging indicator, LED status).
  • Alarm messages noted.
  • Final device status recorded (OOS and sent for repair).

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting prioritizes patient safety and logical external checks first. Document each step clearly to ensure continuity and accountability, and escalate issues promptly when internal components fail. Removing the device from service when necessary prevents patient risk and protects staff.

That is successful troubleshooting.
