Smiths Medical Medfusion 4000

Battery Charging or Runtime Failure

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Medfusion 4000

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting battery charging failures or reduced runtime on the Medfusion 4000 infusion pump. These issues often prevent portable operation and can trigger low battery warnings or unexpected shutdowns. External factors such as power supply, battery connection, and environmental conditions are evaluated before assuming internal battery or electronics failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Confirm the pump is plugged into a working outlet using a verified device or meter.
  • Inspect the AC adapter and cord for damage or loose connections.
  • Why: Faulty outlets or power cords are common causes of charging failure.

Inspect Battery Installation

  • Ensure the battery is properly seated in its compartment and that contacts are clean.
  • Remove and reinsert the battery to confirm secure connection.
  • Why: Poor contact can prevent charging or reduce runtime.

Check Battery Indicator and Alarm Messages

  • Observe the battery icon or on-screen messages for status.
  • Record any error codes or warnings for documentation.
  • Why: Error messages indicate whether the battery is recognized, charging, or defective.

Test With Known Good Battery

  • If available, replace the pump battery with a fully charged, verified battery.
  • Observe if the pump charges and maintains runtime.
  • Why: Confirms whether the issue lies with the battery or the pump circuitry.

Environmental and Usage Considerations

  • Ensure the pump has not been exposed to extreme temperatures.
  • Avoid long-term storage with a depleted battery.
  • Why: Lithium-ion batteries lose capacity if stored improperly or exposed to temperature extremes.

Check Charger or Charging Dock (if applicable)

  • Use a verified charging dock or AC adapter.
  • Confirm charger indicators show proper operation.
  • Why: Charging accessories can fail and mimic battery issues.

If the Problem Persists

External and simple internal causes have been ruled out. The battery or internal charging circuitry may be failing.

  • Remove the pump from service.
  • Label “Out of Service – Battery/Charging Fault.”
  • Send for vendor repair or bench evaluation.

Stopping at this point ensures patient safety and prevents further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not use the pump on an active patient if the battery cannot reliably hold charge. Move the patient to a backup pump or wall-powered alternative before troubleshooting. Troubleshoot only when the pump is disconnected from therapy.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Pump battery will not charge and runtime is shorter than expected.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Battery failed to hold charge after testing; charger and power supply verified functional.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Pump removed from service, labeled, and sent for vendor repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested
  • AC adapter tested
  • Battery seating checked
  • Battery swapped with known good
  • Charging dock or charger tested
  • Observed runtime after charge attempt
  • Error codes or messages displayed

Final Thought

Proper troubleshooting ensures patient safety, preserves pump integrity, and avoids unnecessary replacement costs. Document all observations using the CCR method. Recognizing when to escalate to vendor repair is essential for effective Clinical Engineering practice.

That is successful troubleshooting.
