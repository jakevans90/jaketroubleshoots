Stryker FL28 / FL36

Side Rail Control Panel Failure

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

FL28 / FL36

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of side rail control panel failure, including power, connection, or configuration issues before assuming internal panel failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety

  • Verify the patient is safely out of the bed or in a stable position. Never troubleshoot controls while a patient is in bed to prevent accidental movement.

Check Bed Power

  • Confirm the bed is plugged in or battery is charged.
  • Expectation: Bed functions on other controls if power is adequate.

Inspect Side Rail Locking Mechanism

  • Ensure the side rail is fully latched; some panels are disabled when not fully engaged.
  • Expectation: Panel should respond once the rail is correctly positioned.

Examine Wiring Connections

  • Inspect wiring harness between side rail panel and bed electronics for loose or damaged connectors.
  • Expectation: Re-seating or securing connectors restores panel functionality.

Test Alternate Controls

  • Operate bed using the foot panel or nurse control.
  • Expectation: If alternate controls work, issue is isolated to side rail panel.

Check Panel for Visible Damage

  • Look for cracks, water damage, or stuck buttons.
  • Expectation: Minor debris removal may restore function; physical damage requires replacement.

Software/Reset Check

  • If bed supports a reset or power cycle, perform it per facility protocol.
  • Expectation: Panel may regain responsiveness after reset.

If the Problem Persists

All external and connection issues have been ruled out. The side rail control panel should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt troubleshooting with a patient in bed. Always move the patient first to another bed if needed. Only test controls when safe.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Side rail control panel unresponsive when patient attempted adjustment."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose wiring harness found between side rail panel and main electronics."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Re-seated connector; panel restored full function."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Bed plugged into verified outlet
  • Alternate controls tested successfully
  • No unusual sounds or smells
  • Side rail fully engaged and locked

Final Thought

Patient safety is paramount. Use a logical stepwise approach, ruling out power, connection, and mechanical issues before assuming panel failure. Proper documentation ensures traceability and supports timely repair.

That is successful troubleshooting.
