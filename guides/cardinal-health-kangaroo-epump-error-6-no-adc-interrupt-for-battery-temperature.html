Cardinal Health Kangaroo ePump

Error: 6 No ADC interrupt for battery temperature

Asset Type

Feeding Pump

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kangaroo ePump

What This Guide Helps With

Error 6 indicating battery temperature sensing failure, which can cause pump shutdown, charging issues, or inability to safely operate on battery power.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from patient use if actively infusing
  • Transition feeding to a backup pump

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error: 6 is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup or during use)
  • Check if the pump runs on battery
  • Look for abnormal charging behavior

Perform a Power Cycle

  • Turn the pump off
  • Disconnect from AC power
  • Wait 30 seconds
  • Restart the device

Check External Power and Charging Conditions

  • Plug into a known-good outlet
  • Verify power adapter connection
  • Check for charging indicators

Inspect Battery Compartment and Physical Condition

  • Look for signs of overheating or damage
  • Check battery seating and contacts
  • Ensure battery is properly installed

Attempt Operation on AC Power Only

  • Operate the pump while connected to AC power
  • Observe if error persists

Isolate Likely Failure Source

  • Battery temperature monitoring uses internal ADC circuitry
  • No ADC interrupt indicates loss of temperature feedback
  • Possible causes include:
    • Faulty battery temperature sensor
    • Battery pack communication failure
    • Internal control board failure

If the Problem Persists

External causes have been ruled out and the error remains.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before addressing equipment issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error: 6 and not operating on battery."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery temperature not detected due to internal ADC monitoring failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Power cycle performed, battery and AC verified. Error persisted. Device removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery monitoring errors are critical safety protections. Once external checks are completed, escalation is the correct and safe path.

That is successful troubleshooting.
