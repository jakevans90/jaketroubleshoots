GE Healthcare Vivid Series

Touchscreen or Control Panel Unresponsive

Asset Type

Ultrasound System

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Vivid Series

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting unresponsive touchscreen or control panel due to freezes, input failure, connection issues, or external hardware faults.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the system is in use, safely stop the exam and move the patient to another ultrasound system.

Expected: No patient care is dependent on the malfunctioning system.
Why it matters: Loss of control can prevent proper imaging or system shutdown.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Attempt to use the touchscreen and control panel.
  • Check if:
  • Entire screen is unresponsive
  • Only certain areas/buttons are not working
  • Trackball or keyboard still responds

Expected: Clear understanding of full vs partial failure.
Why it matters: Helps differentiate between software freeze vs hardware input failure.

Check for System Freeze or Software Lockup

  • Observe the display for:
  • Frozen image
  • No screen updates
  • No response to any inputs
  • Attempt a soft reset (if available) or reboot the system using the power button.

Expected: System reboots and regains responsiveness.
Why it matters: Temporary software lockups are common and often resolved with a reboot.

Inspect External Peripherals and Input Devices

  • Check the control panel components:
  • Trackball movement
  • Keyboard button response
  • Touchscreen surface condition (damage, contamination)
  • Clean the touchscreen if visibly dirty using approved wipes.

Expected: Inputs respond normally after cleaning or repositioning.
Why it matters: Dirt, fluid, or debris can block touchscreen responsiveness.

Check for Physical Damage or Obstructions

  • Inspect for:
  • Cracked touchscreen
  • Stuck buttons on control panel
  • Signs of liquid intrusion
  • Gently press different areas of the screen to check for inconsistent response.

Expected: No physical damage or obstruction affecting input.
Why it matters: Physical damage often causes partial or total input failure.

Verify System Connections (If Accessible Externally)

  • Ensure any external cables or modules related to the control panel are secure (if visible without disassembly).

Expected: All connections appear secure with no visible damage.
Why it matters: Loose connections can interrupt input signal communication.

Check for Overheating or Environmental Issues

  • Feel for excessive heat around the control panel or system base.
  • Ensure vents are not blocked.

Expected: System is within normal operating temperature.
Why it matters: Overheating can cause system instability or input failure.

Perform Full System Restart (Power Cycle)

  • Shut down the system completely.
  • Disconnect from power briefly (if appropriate), then restart.

Expected: System boots normally and inputs function.
Why it matters: Clears temporary system faults not resolved by soft reboot.

If the Problem Persists

If the touchscreen or control panel remains unresponsive after completing these steps, common external causes have been ruled out. The issue is likely internal (touchscreen digitizer, control board, or system electronics).

Remove the system from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or further bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and ensures proper escalation.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt to troubleshoot input issues while actively scanning a patient. Always transition to another system before troubleshooting to avoid delays in care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Ultrasound system touchscreen and control panel not responding during exam."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"System software lockup causing loss of input functionality."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed system reboot, restoring normal touchscreen and control panel operation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • System frozen vs partially responsive
  • Touchscreen vs control panel behavior
  • Trackball/keyboard response
  • Cleaning performed
  • Physical damage observed
  • Overheating or fan issues
  • Power cycle results
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Start with simple, external checks and confirm whether the issue is software-related before assuming hardware failure. Patient safety and controlled troubleshooting are always the priority. Proper escalation and clear documentation ensure efficient repair and continued reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
