Drager Fabius GS

Alarm: APNOEA VENTILATION !!

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

Drager

Model

Fabius GS

What This Guide Helps With

APNOEA VENTILATION !! alarm indicating stopped breathing or failed ventilation, typically caused by circuit disconnection, leaks, or incorrect ventilation settings leading to loss of effective ventilation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Immediately assess the patient and ventilation status
  • If ventilation is compromised, disconnect and begin manual ventilation (bagging)
  • Transition to an alternate anesthesia machine if needed

2. Verify the Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm APNOEA VENTILATION !! is displayed
  • Observe if ventilation has stopped or is intermittent
  • Check if apnea backup breaths are being delivered
  • Determine when the alarm occurs (startup, spontaneous mode, during ventilation)

3. Check Breathing Circuit Connections

  • Inspect entire circuit from machine to patient
  • Ensure all hoses, Y-piece, filters, and adapters are connected
  • Verify secure connection to airway device

4. Check for Leaks in the Breathing System

  • Inspect for loose fittings or damaged tubing
  • Check CO₂ absorber seating and seals
  • Inspect reservoir bag and valves
  • Perform a leak check if possible

5. Verify Gas Flow and Ventilator Operation

  • Ensure O₂/air supply is active
  • Confirm flowmeters are properly set
  • Verify ventilator is running and not in standby

6. Evaluate Ventilation Mode and Settings

  • Confirm correct ventilation mode is selected
  • Verify respiratory rate and apnea settings
  • Ensure apnea backup ventilation is enabled if required

7. Check Pressure Support Settings

  • Verify support pressure is adequate
  • Check trigger sensitivity settings
  • Ensure patient effort can be detected

8. Observe Breath Triggering

  • Determine if patient is triggering breaths
  • Check if machine is providing consistent backup breaths
  • Identify missed triggers or irregular ventilation

9. Confirm Resolution

  • Ensure normal ventilation is restored
  • Verify stable airway pressure and volume delivery
  • Confirm alarm has cleared

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes such as disconnections, leaks, gas supply, and settings have been ruled out:

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot ventilation alarms on an active patient. Always move the patient to a backup device before attempting troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit alarming APNOEA VENTILATION !!; ventilator not delivering effective breaths."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Loose breathing circuit connection causing loss of ventilation and apnea alarm."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Reconnected circuit, verified no leaks, confirmed proper ventilation and cleared alarm."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

APNOEA VENTILATION alarms indicate a critical loss of ventilation. Focus on simple external causes first, prioritize patient safety, and escalate appropriately if ventilation cannot be restored.

That is successful troubleshooting.
