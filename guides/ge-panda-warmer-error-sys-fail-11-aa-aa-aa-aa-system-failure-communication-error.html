GE Healthcare Panda Warmer

Error: Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] System Failure Communication Error

Asset Type

Infant Warmer

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Panda Warmer

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] when a processor communication failure prevents reliable infant warmer heat-control operation. This system failure indicates that the Heat Control Processor stopped communicating with the Main Application Processor because of a power-on self-test failure. The warmer should not be relied upon for patient thermal support until the condition is corrected and safe operation is verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the warmer is supporting an infant or prepared for immediate clinical use, notify clinical staff immediately.
  • Transfer the patient to a known-working warming device according to clinical procedure before troubleshooting.
  • Do not rely on a Panda Warmer displaying Sys Fail 11 for patient thermal support until the error is resolved and operation is verified.
  • Confirm that the infant is no longer dependent on equipment with an unresolved heat-control processor communication failure.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • With the warmer removed from patient use, power the unit on and confirm that the display reports Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA].
  • Document whether the error appears immediately at power-up or after part of the startup sequence completes.
  • Note whether heater output is unavailable, startup is interrupted, or the warmer was recently in use before the failure occurred.
  • Record whether the error appears approximately one minute after a power-on attempt.

3. Inspect the Warmer for External Heat or Ventilation Concerns

  • Inspect the heater head cooling vents for obstruction, dust buildup, linens, covers, or positioning against nearby equipment.
  • Remove any visible airflow obstruction before attempting further evaluation.
  • Check whether the heater head remains unusually warm from recent operation.
  • Do not handle hot heater components or continue operation if overheating is suspected.

4. Confirm the Incoming Mains Power Is Correct

  • Verify that the warmer is connected to the correct facility mains voltage for the unit configuration.
  • Inspect the power cord and plug for visible damage, discoloration, looseness, or evidence of overheating.
  • Test the outlet or approved power source according to facility procedure.
  • Do not continue operating the warmer on an outlet or power source with incorrect, unstable, high, or low voltage.

5. Reattempt Startup After External Conditions Are Corrected

  • After confirming correct mains voltage and clear heater head vents, perform a controlled power-up with the warmer out of clinical use.
  • Observe whether Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] returns.
  • If the error clears, complete functional and safety verification according to departmental procedure before returning the warmer to service.
  • Document any corrected external condition, such as obstructed vents or an abnormal power source.

6. Check for Related Overtemperature Error History When Applicable

  • If the warmer was recently operating and Sys Fail 11 appears approximately one minute after a power-on attempt, review the authorized Service Mode error history according to Clinical Engineering procedure.
  • Check for Sys Fail 09 0B or Sys Fail 06 in the Errors list.
  • Document whether the history indicates a possible overheated heater head or opened thermostat condition.

7. Refer for Qualified Bench Evaluation of the Power PCA Fuse Condition

  • If correct mains power, clear ventilation, and applicable error-history review do not resolve the failure, remove the warmer from service for qualified bench evaluation.
  • Authorized service personnel should evaluate Power PCA fuses F9 and F10 according to approved service procedure.
  • Relevant findings may include an open fuse, heat discoloration, visible damage, or loose fuse retention in the clip mount.
  • If either fuse is loose in its clip mount, the Power PCA requires replacement; the clip mounts should not be repaired by bending.

8. Confirm Resolution Before Returning the Warmer to Service

  • After correction or approved repair, confirm that Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] no longer appears during startup.
  • Complete required operational verification, alarm checks, heater-function testing, and electrical safety testing according to departmental and manufacturer-authorized procedures.
  • Return the warmer to service only after successful verification is documented.

If the Problem Persists

If Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] remains after confirming correct mains voltage, clearing heater head ventilation concerns, reviewing applicable error history, and referring identified Power PCA fuse concerns for qualified evaluation, the common external and readily verifiable causes have been ruled out.

A persistent error indicates an internal Power PCA, heat-control processing, thermostat-related, or communication failure requiring repair-level evaluation.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the warmer for qualified bench evaluation or manufacturer-authorized repair.

An unresolved processor communication failure affecting heater control is not appropriate for continued clinical use.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an infant warmer system failure while an infant depends on it for thermal support. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, then evaluate the affected warmer away from the patient.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Panda Warmer displays Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] during startup. Warmer does not complete normal operation and cannot be relied upon for infant heat support."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] processor communication failure during startup. Incoming mains voltage was verified, heater head vents were inspected, and related overtemperature error history was reviewed. Persistent failure requires qualified evaluation of the Power PCA, fuse condition, thermostat circuit, or processor communication path."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed warmer from patient use and labeled Out of Service. Verified power source and heater head ventilation condition, reviewed applicable error history, and referred unit for qualified bench repair evaluation. Device will not be returned to service until operational and safety verification is successfully completed."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

A Panda Warmer displaying Sys Fail 11 [AA AA AA AA] has reported a failure affecting communication with its heat-control system. Patient safety comes first: remove the device from clinical use, rule out power and cooling-related contributors, document relevant error history, and escalate persistent failures rather than returning an unreliable warmer to service. Clear CCR documentation preserves the reported error, troubleshooting findings, repair decision, and final safety status.

That is successful troubleshooting.
