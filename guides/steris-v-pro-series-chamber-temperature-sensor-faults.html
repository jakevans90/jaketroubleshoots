STERIS V-PRO Series Sterilizer

Chamber Temperature or Sensor Calibration Faults

Asset Type

Sterilizer

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

V-PRO Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting chamber temperature errors or sensor calibration faults on a STERIS V-PRO sterilizer. These issues may present as temperature alarms, failed cycles, or calibration-related error messages. The focus is on verifying environmental conditions, setup, and external factors before assuming internal sensor failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Unit Power and Warm-Up Status

  • Ensure the unit is fully powered on and has completed its warm-up cycle.
  • Temperature-related faults can occur if the system has not stabilized after startup.

Check Room Environmental Conditions

  • Confirm the room temperature and humidity are within manufacturer specifications.
  • Excessively cold or hot environments can cause inaccurate chamber readings or calibration faults.

Inspect Chamber for Load Issues

  • Verify the chamber is not overloaded or improperly loaded.
  • Items blocking airflow can affect temperature distribution and trigger sensor alarms.

Check for Residual Moisture or Condensation

  • Inspect the chamber for visible moisture or condensation.
  • Moisture can affect temperature sensors and lead to inaccurate readings.

Inspect Door Seal and Closure

  • Ensure the door is fully closed and the seal is clean and intact.
  • Poor sealing can affect internal chamber conditions and temperature stability.

Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit off, wait 30–60 seconds, then power it back on.
  • This can clear temporary sensor or calibration faults.

Verify No Recent Interruptions or Aborted Cycles

  • Check if the issue started after a cycle abort or power interruption.
  • Incomplete cycles can leave the system in an unstable state.

Check External Connections (If Applicable)

  • If the unit interfaces with external monitoring or recording systems, ensure connections are secure.
  • Communication interruptions can sometimes present as calibration-related faults.

Run an Empty Test Cycle

  • Run a cycle with an empty chamber to determine if the issue is load-related or system-related.
  • If the cycle passes empty but fails when loaded, the issue may be related to load configuration.

Observe for Abnormal Indicators

  • Look for persistent temperature alarms
  • Inconsistent readings
  • Unusual noises from internal components
  • These may indicate sensor drift or internal hardware issues.

If the Problem Persists

If all external and environmental causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely due to an internal temperature sensor, calibration drift, or control system fault.

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Referred for repair or further bench evaluation

Do not attempt internal calibration or sensor replacement in the field unless specifically trained and authorized. Knowing when to escalate is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot temperature or calibration faults while the sterilizer is needed for patient care. Always ensure alternate sterilization equipment is available before removing the unit from service.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“User reports sterilizer failing cycles with temperature alarm and calibration error message.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Unit powered and tested. Verified proper environment and load. Empty cycle still failed with temperature fault, indicating likely internal sensor or calibration issue.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Removed unit from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for vendor repair and calibration verification.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Room temperature and humidity verified
  • Chamber inspected for moisture or residue
  • Load configuration checked
  • Empty cycle test results
  • Alarm messages observed
  • Power cycle performed
  • Door seal condition verified
  • Final device status (removed from service or returned to use)

Final Thought

Temperature accuracy is critical to effective sterilization and patient safety. Always start with environmental and usage-related checks before assuming internal failure. When those factors are ruled out, timely escalation ensures the device is properly calibrated and safe for continued use. Clear documentation supports both safety and efficiency.

That is successful troubleshooting.
