Berchtold Chromophare Surgical Light

Light Head Power Supply Failure

Asset Type

Surgical Light

Manufacturer

Berchtold

Model

Chromophare Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a light head power supply failure in Berchtold Chromophare surgical lights. Symptoms may include the light head not turning on, flickering, or complete loss of illumination. The guide focuses on external and easily verified causes before escalating to internal repair or manufacturer service.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Main Power Supply

  • Ensure the surgical light is connected to a live power source.
  • Check circuit breakers and any outlet switches.
  • Why it matters: A simple power interruption is the most common cause of light failure.

Inspect Power Cables and Connections

  • Check the power cable for visible damage, kinks, or loose connections.
  • Confirm that the connectors to the light head and control panel are secure.
  • Why it matters: Faulty cables or loose plugs can cause intermittent or total power loss.

Check Control Console/Interface

  • Verify that the main control console or wall switch is operational.
  • Attempt to cycle power on/off to reset the system.
  • Why it matters: Some Chromophare models have safety interlocks that prevent light activation if the control console detects errors.

Inspect Light Head for Obvious Issues

  • Look for burned-out LED modules or unusual discoloration.
  • Listen for abnormal sounds from the internal fan or power module.
  • Why it matters: Some external symptoms may indicate power supply stress or failure.

Test Alternative Light Head (if available)

  • Swap the problematic light head with a known working one on the same control system.
  • Why it matters: Confirms whether the issue is with the light head or the power supply/control system.

Check Fuses or Resettable Protection

  • Some Chromophare series models have internal fuses or reset buttons accessible without disassembly.
  • Why it matters: A blown fuse or tripped protection circuit can mimic full power failure.

If the Problem Persists

External and easily verified causes have been ruled out. Likely the internal light head power supply has failed.

Action:

  • Remove the light head from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Escalate to manufacturer repair or qualified service personnel.

Note: Avoid opening the light head or attempting internal repair, as this can void warranties and create electrical hazards.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt to troubleshoot the light head during an active procedure.
  • Ensure alternative lighting is available before working on the device.
  • Confirm the OR is safely illuminated by another surgical light before removing the faulty head.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
Light head will not power on; no illumination observed.

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Checked power source, cables, control console, and connectors; all verified functional. Fault traced to internal power supply of light head.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Light head removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for manufacturer repair.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested: Yes
  • Cables swapped: Yes, no change
  • Indicator lights on console: Normal
  • Unusual sounds/heat: None
  • Final device status: Removed from service

Final Thought

Patient safety and proper lighting are critical. Logical troubleshooting avoids unnecessary downtime and ensures only trained personnel handle internal failures. Accurate documentation supports efficient repair and compliance with OR safety standards.

That is successful troubleshooting.
