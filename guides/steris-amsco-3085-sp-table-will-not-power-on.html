STERIS AMSCO 3085 SP

Table Will Not Power On

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

STERIS

Model

AMSCO 3085 SP

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting situations where the STERIS AMSCO 3085 SP surgical table will not power on. Common symptoms include:

  • No indicator lights when attempting to turn on
  • Table does not respond to foot or hand controls
  • System completely unresponsive

Focus is on external, verifiable issues first, avoiding internal disassembly.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Confirm the outlet is live using a multimeter or a known working device.
  • Check for tripped circuit breakers or blown fuses in the OR electrical panel.

Why it matters: A dead outlet is the most common reason for a table to appear “dead.”

Check Power Cord and Connections

  • Inspect the table’s power cord for cuts, frays, or loose connections.
  • Ensure the cord is fully seated in the wall outlet and the table’s input port.

Inspect Table Power Switch

  • Ensure the main power switch on the table is in the ON position.
  • Look for any debris, damage, or stuck switches that could prevent contact.

Check Emergency Stop or Safety Interlocks

  • Verify that any emergency stop buttons are released.
  • Confirm any safety interlocks (e.g., foot pedal engagement, bed locking mechanisms) are in the correct position.

Why it matters: Many tables disable power when interlocks are active for safety.

Test with Alternate Power Outlet

  • If possible, move the table to a different verified working outlet.
  • This isolates outlet or OR electrical issues.

Observe Table Indicators

  • Look for any blinking lights, fault codes, or audible alarms when attempting to power on.
  • Document any behaviors for work order entry and potential manufacturer troubleshooting.

If the Problem Persists

External power and interlocks have been verified.

Table likely has an internal electrical or control board failure.

  • Remove the table from service.
  • Label it Out of Service.
  • Escalate to STERIS service for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is critical for safety and avoiding further damage.

Clinical Use Tip

  • Do not attempt to operate or troubleshoot the table while a patient is on it.
  • Move the patient to another table before beginning work.
  • Only proceed when patient safety can be assured.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Surgical table will not power on; no lights or response from controls.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Verified outlet is live; power cord intact; main switch ON; emergency stops released; table remains unresponsive.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
“Table removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and scheduled for STERIS repair.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and confirmed live
  • Power cord visually inspected
  • Emergency stop and safety interlocks checked
  • Main switch position noted
  • Any indicator lights or alarms recorded
  • Final device status: Out of Service

Final Thought

Patient safety and methodical troubleshooting are paramount. Starting with external checks ensures you do not risk patient or staff safety and allows accurate documentation for service escalation. Proper CCR entries help track problem history and justify repair needs.

That is successful troubleshooting.
