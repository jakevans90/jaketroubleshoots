BD Alaris

Error: 222.4030 Motor stall detected

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

BD

Model

Alaris

What This Guide Helps With

Error 222.4030 Motor stall detected indicates the pump motor is unable to move as expected, causing infusion to stop due to tubing resistance, improper loading, or internal drive failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Stop the infusion immediately
  • Remove the pump from patient use
  • Transfer therapy to a functioning pump or module

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm Error: 222.4030 Motor stall detected is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (startup or infusion start)
  • Listen for abnormal sounds such as clicking or straining

Check for Improper Tubing Load (Most Common Cause)

  • Open the pump module door and remove the administration set
  • Inspect for misrouting, improper seating, or pinched tubing
  • Reload tubing correctly and ensure proper placement

Inspect for Occlusion or Downstream Resistance

  • Check for closed clamps or kinked tubing
  • Inspect for blocked filters or patient-side occlusion
  • Clear any restriction before retrying infusion

Inspect Pump Channel for Contamination or Obstruction

  • Look for fluid spills, residue, or debris
  • Check for restricted or sticky moving parts
  • Clean externally per facility protocol

Check Door Latch and Mechanism

  • Open and close the module door
  • Ensure smooth motion and full latch engagement
  • Check for misalignment or looseness

Test with Known Good Setup

  • Load a new administration set
  • Attempt a basic infusion
  • Observe if the motor engages normally

Swap Pump Module (Isolation Step)

  • Move setup to a different Alaris module if available
  • If issue resolves, original module is faulty
  • If issue persists, problem may still be external

If the Problem Persists

If Error 222.4030 continues after verifying tubing, clearing occlusions, and testing with a known good setup, internal motor or drive mechanism failure is likely.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot motor or flow-related errors on an active patient. Always transfer the patient to a backup device before troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Alaris pump displaying Error 222.4030 Motor stall detected and would not start infusion."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Tubing and occlusion ruled out; error persisted with known good setup, indicating internal motor failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed pump module from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Motor stall errors are most often caused by setup or resistance issues. Once external causes are ruled out, internal failure is likely. Recognizing when to escalate ensures safe and efficient troubleshooting.

That is successful troubleshooting.
