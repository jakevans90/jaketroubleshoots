Smiths Medical Medfusion 3500

Battery Charging Failure / Won’t Power On

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Smiths Medical

Model

Medfusion 3500

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting a Medfusion 3500 syringe infusion pump that will not power on or does not appear to charge. Symptoms may include a blank display, the pump shutting off when unplugged, or the battery not charging while connected to AC power. The guide focuses on verifying external power sources and easily correctable conditions before assuming internal battery or power supply failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify the AC power source

  • Confirm the outlet is functioning by testing with another device or outlet tester. If the outlet is controlled by a wall switch or power strip, ensure it is turned on.
  • Why this matters: The pump cannot charge or power on without a functioning AC source.

Inspect the power cord and adapter

  • Check the AC power cord and power supply brick for visible damage such as frayed insulation, bent pins, or loose connectors. Ensure the cord is firmly seated in both the outlet and the pump’s power input.
  • If possible, swap the power cord or power supply with a known-good one of the same type.

Check the power indicator lights

  • Connect the pump to AC power and observe any indicator lights or display activity. Some pumps will briefly illuminate the screen or charging indicator when power is applied.
  • Note any abnormal behavior such as flickering lights or no response at all.

Attempt a power cycle

  • Disconnect the pump from AC power, wait approximately 10 seconds, then reconnect it and attempt to power it on again using the power button.
  • This can sometimes reset minor power state issues.

Inspect the charging connector

  • Examine the DC input port on the pump for debris, bent contacts, or looseness. Gently reseat the power connector and ensure it fits securely.
  • A loose or damaged connector can prevent battery charging or AC operation.

Allow the pump to charge

  • If the pump powers on only while connected to AC power, allow it to remain plugged in for at least 30 minutes and observe whether the battery begins charging.
  • A deeply discharged battery may take time before normal operation resumes.

Check for battery-related symptoms

  • Signs of battery failure may include:
  • Pump shuts off immediately when unplugged
  • Battery indicator does not increase during charging
  • Pump requires AC power to operate
  • These symptoms suggest internal battery degradation.

If the Problem Persists

If external power sources, cables, and connectors have been verified and the pump still will not power on or charge, the issue is likely related to an internal battery failure, charging circuit fault, or internal power supply issue.

At this point the device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when external causes have been ruled out and escalation is appropriate is an important part of effective Clinical Engineering troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an infusion pump that is actively connected to a patient. Ensure the patient has been safely transferred to another functioning pump before beginning any troubleshooting steps.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Medfusion 3500 infusion pump would not power on and appeared completely dead.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Pump did not power on during testing. Verified outlet was functional and power supply was connected. Pump only briefly illuminated when plugged in and shut off immediately when unplugged, indicating battery failure.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Pump removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for battery replacement and internal power circuit evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and confirmed working
  • Power cord and power supply inspected or swapped
  • Charging indicator behavior observed
  • Display activity when connected to AC power
  • Any loose power connectors noted
  • Final device status (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Many infusion pump power issues are caused by simple external factors such as faulty outlets or damaged power cords. Systematically verifying these first prevents unnecessary repair actions. When those checks are complete and the pump still fails to charge or power on, removing the device from service and documenting the findings ensures both patient safety and efficient repair follow-up.

That is successful troubleshooting.
