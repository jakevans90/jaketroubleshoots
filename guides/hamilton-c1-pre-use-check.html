Hamilton C1 Ventilator

Pre-Use Check Failures

Asset Type

Ventilator

Manufacturer

Hamilton

Model

C1

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the Hamilton C1 ventilator:

  • Fails the pre-use checkout or self-test
  • Displays error messages during startup
  • Does not indicate readiness for patient use

Focus is on external, logical checks first—power, connections, accessories—before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify Power Source

  • Ensure the ventilator is plugged into a verified, live outlet.
  • Confirm the battery is charged if running on internal battery.

Why: Low or unstable power often causes pre-use check failures.

Check Main Power Switch & Startup Sequence

  • Power on the ventilator and observe the startup screen.
  • Ensure no buttons are stuck or pressed during startup.

Why: Improper startup or button press can interrupt self-tests.

Inspect Breathing Circuit & Patient Interfaces

  • Confirm that circuit tubing, filters, humidifiers, and sensors are properly connected.
  • Remove any circuit accessories and try running the pre-use check again.

Why: Circuit misconfiguration or disconnected sensors can cause automated checks to fail.

Check Oxygen and Air Supply (if applicable)

  • Verify oxygen and air lines are connected to the ventilator and supply pressures are within operational range.
  • Ensure no kinks or obstructions in supply hoses.

Why: Ventilator self-test includes gas flow verification.

Examine Sensors and Alarms

  • Look for any disconnected, loose, or damaged pressure, flow, or oxygen sensors.
  • Inspect for visible contamination or moisture in sensor ports.

Why: Faulty sensors prevent successful pre-use checks.

Run Diagnostics / System Test

  • Follow on-screen prompts for self-test or pre-use check.
  • Record any error codes displayed.

Why: Codes give insight into which system component is causing failure.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have been completed; failure is likely internal (hardware or firmware).

  • Remove the ventilator from patient use.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to Hamilton Service or in-house biomedical bench evaluation.

Note: Stopping at this point avoids patient risk and unnecessary internal troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not attempt pre-use checks on a ventilator attached to a patient.

Move the patient to an alternative ventilator before performing troubleshooting.

Only perform further diagnostics when the ventilator is disconnected from the patient circuit.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Ventilator fails pre-use checkout; displays ‘Self-Test Failed’ message.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed loose oxygen sensor and partially connected breathing circuit tubing during pre-use check.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Examples:
Reconnected all sensors and breathing circuit components, retested pre-use check. If still failing, ventilator marked Out of Service for service evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Outlet tested and confirmed live
  • Battery charge level
  • Oxygen and air supply pressures
  • Circuit assembly checked and reconnected
  • Error codes displayed during self-test
  • Final device status labeled (Out of Service if unresolved)

Final Thought

Patient safety and logical, stepwise troubleshooting are paramount. Always confirm external causes before assuming internal failure. Accurate documentation ensures traceability, facilitates repairs, and supports compliance with safety protocols.

That is successful troubleshooting.
