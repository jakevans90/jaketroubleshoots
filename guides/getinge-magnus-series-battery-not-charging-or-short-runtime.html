Getinge Magnus Series

Battery Not Charging or Short Runtime

Asset Type

Surgical Table

Manufacturer

Getinge

Model

Magnus Series

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of battery not charging or short runtime, including battery condition, charger function, connection, and load issues before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety

  • Confirm the table is not in use with a patient. If it is, safely transfer the patient to another table before troubleshooting.

2. Check Power Source

  • Verify the wall outlet is live using a known-good device.
  • Ensure any extension cords or power strips are functional.
  • Outcome: Table should detect incoming AC power.

3. Inspect Battery Connections

  • Turn off the table and disconnect power.
  • Remove the battery and check terminals for corrosion, debris, or loose connections.
  • Re-seat the battery securely.
  • Outcome: Proper contact ensures charging can occur.

4. Verify Charger Function

  • Confirm charger is correctly connected to the table or battery.
  • Check for indicator lights on the charger showing it is actively charging.
  • Swap with a known-good charger if available.
  • Outcome: Charger is confirmed operational or replaced.

5. Check Battery Status and Runtime

  • Observe battery level indicators on the table or hand control.
  • If the table powers on but runtime is short, the battery may be degraded.
  • Outcome: Determine if battery retains sufficient charge for normal use.

6. Review Load and Usage Conditions

  • Ensure table is not under excessive load or using high-energy movements continuously, which can shorten runtime.
  • Outcome: Confirm short runtime is not caused by operational overload.

If the Problem Persists

External checks are complete; the battery may be faulty or the internal charging system may be compromised.

  • Remove the table from service.
  • Label “Out of Service.”
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Stopping at this point avoids unsafe troubleshooting and preserves patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to operate the table on battery power with a patient until the battery is verified to hold a full charge. Always move the patient to a table with confirmed power for safety.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Table battery not charging and provides only short runtime."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery terminals were corroded, and battery capacity is below specification."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned terminals, tested charger, battery replaced, and table verified to hold full charge."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested
  • Charger swapped
  • Battery level indicators
  • Table load conditions
  • Final table status (Ready/Out of Service)

Final Thought

Battery issues are common in mobile surgical tables. A logical approach—checking power, connections, charger, and battery health—ensures patient safety and avoids unnecessary internal repairs. Document all findings for proper work order tracking.

That is successful troubleshooting.
