Baxter SIGMA Spectrum

Error: 111 Pulse dmnd too high

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

Baxter

Model

SIGMA Spectrum

What This Guide Helps With

Error 111 Pulse dmnd too high indicates the programmed interval infusion requires a delivery rate beyond the pump’s physical capability. This prevents infusion from starting or continuing and may interrupt therapy.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the pump from active patient use if infusion is interrupted or unreliable
  • Transfer infusion to another pump if therapy must continue
  • Confirm patient therapy is maintained without interruption

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the pump and confirm Error: 111 Pulse dmnd too high is displayed
  • Observe when the error occurs (programming, start, or immediately after entry)
  • Check if the error is consistent during interval infusion attempts

Check Infusion Mode and Programming

  • Verify the pump is operating in Interval mode
  • Review dose or volume per interval
  • Review interval timing
  • Calculate equivalent mL/hr delivery rate

Reduce Programmed Delivery Demand

  • Increase the interval duration
  • Decrease the volume per interval
  • Switch to continuous mode if clinically appropriate
  • Ensure calculated delivery rate is within pump limits

Verify Drug Library / Guardrails Settings

  • Confirm correct drug and concentration selection
  • Ensure limits are not forcing excessive delivery rates
  • Adjust programming if limits are driving unrealistic demands

Inspect Administration Set and Setup

  • Ensure correct Baxter-compatible tubing is used
  • Verify tubing is properly loaded and aligned
  • Check for kinks, occlusions, or resistance

Reattempt Infusion

  • Restart infusion after adjustments
  • Confirm error does not return
  • Verify stable operation

If the Problem Persists

If Error 111 continues after confirming correct programming and setup, external causes have been ruled out.

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label the device Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an infusion pump while connected to a patient. Always transition the patient to a backup device before making adjustments or attempting to resolve errors.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displaying Error 111 Pulse dmnd too high when attempting interval infusion."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Programmed interval delivery required pulse rate exceeding pump capability."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Adjusted interval settings to acceptable range or removed device from service for further evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error 111 Pulse dmnd too high is typically a programming limitation rather than a hardware failure. Always verify infusion parameters first, but escalate appropriately when the device cannot meet expected delivery requirements.
