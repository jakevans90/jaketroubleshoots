Siemens Healthineers MOBILETT Elara Max

Battery Runtime, Charging, or Power-On Failure

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

Siemens Healthineers

Model

MOBILETT Elara Max

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting power-on failure, poor battery runtime, charging issues, AC power problems, or mobile system power complaints before repair escalation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Confirm the MOBILETT Elara Max is not needed for an active patient exam.

If the unit loses power, shuts down unexpectedly, or cannot complete imaging, have clinical staff use another mobile X-ray system if imaging is urgent.

Expected outcome: Patient care is not delayed by a system with unreliable power.

Confirm the Exact Power Complaint

Ask the operator what happened and when.

Determine whether the issue involves:

  • Unit will not power on
  • Unit powers on only when plugged in
  • Battery runtime is short
  • Battery will not charge
  • Charging indicator does not appear
  • System shuts down during transport or exposure preparation
  • Unit powers on but displays a low-battery warning
  • Unit was left unplugged or unused for an extended period

Expected outcome: The issue is separated into power-on, battery runtime, charging, or AC input behavior.

Check Whether the System Is Safely Parked and Stable

Make sure the unit is parked in a safe location with the brake engaged.

Do not troubleshoot power or charging while the unit is blocking patient care areas, doorways, hallways, or emergency access.

Expected outcome: The unit can be evaluated without creating a transport or safety hazard.

Verify the AC Outlet

Plug the MOBILETT Elara Max into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.

Test the outlet with another approved device or outlet tester if appropriate for your facility workflow.

Expected outcome: The system receives reliable facility power. If the outlet is dead, have Facilities correct the outlet issue and stop troubleshooting the mobile X-ray system.

Inspect the Power Cord and Plug

Check the AC power cord for:

  • Loose connection
  • Damaged insulation
  • Bent or damaged plug blades
  • Evidence of overheating
  • Strain relief damage
  • Cord not fully seated at the device or wall outlet

Expected outcome: The cord is safe and properly connected. If the cord is damaged, remove it from use and replace or escalate according to site policy.

Confirm Charging Indication

With the system plugged into AC power, look for the expected charging indication on the system display or power/charging indicator.

Allow the unit to remain connected long enough to determine whether charging starts.

Expected outcome: The system recognizes AC power and indicates charging. If there is no charging indication on a known-good outlet, continue troubleshooting.

Check for Power-On Response

Attempt to power on the system using the normal power button sequence.

Observe whether the system:

  • Shows no response
  • Displays lights only
  • Starts booting then shuts down
  • Boots normally while plugged in
  • Shows a battery or charging warning

Expected outcome: The power-on behavior is documented clearly for escalation if needed.

Allow Time for a Deeply Discharged Battery

If the unit was stored unplugged or left unused, leave it connected to AC power and monitor for charging response.

A deeply discharged battery may not immediately provide normal runtime.

Expected outcome: The system begins charging and eventually powers on. If charging resumes and runtime returns to normal, document the issue and stop.

Check for User-Accessible Battery or Power Status Information

Review the system status screen, battery icon, charging message, or operator-visible alerts.

Look for warnings such as low battery, charging fault, battery service required, or power supply-related messages.

Expected outcome: The displayed status helps separate normal low charge from a battery, charger, or internal power fault.

Confirm the Unit Was Stored Correctly

Ask staff whether the system is routinely returned to its charging location after use.

Mobile X-ray systems can develop runtime complaints when they are left unplugged between exams or not connected fully to AC power.

Expected outcome: A workflow issue is ruled in or out before assuming equipment failure.

Check for Excessive Runtime Expectations

Ask how long the system is expected to operate between charges and whether the complaint occurs after normal use, heavy use, or repeated imaging.

Expected outcome: The complaint is compared against actual usage. If the device charges normally and runtime is acceptable after full charge, the issue may be user workflow rather than equipment failure.

Inspect for Heat, Odor, or Abnormal Sounds

While plugged in and during power-on attempts, check for:

  • Burning smell
  • Unusual heat near the power entry area
  • Clicking, buzzing, or repeated relay sounds
  • Fan noise without boot
  • Intermittent shutdown

Expected outcome: Any safety concern is identified early. If heat, odor, smoke, or electrical noise is present, unplug the unit if safe, remove it from service, and escalate immediately.

Verify Accessories Are Not Causing a Startup Delay

Check whether external accessories, detector charging components, USB devices, or network peripherals were recently connected.

Remove nonessential external accessories if allowed by site policy and attempt normal startup again.

Expected outcome: A peripheral-related startup issue is ruled out before deeper repair evaluation.

Check for Error Messages During Boot

If the system begins to boot, document any power, battery, charger, generator, detector, or system startup messages shown on the display.

Expected outcome: The exact message is captured for repair or vendor support.

Perform a Controlled Restart if the System Is Frozen

If the unit appears powered but unresponsive, follow the site-approved normal shutdown or restart process.

Avoid repeated hard power cycling unless required for safety or directed by approved service guidance.

Expected outcome: A software lockup is ruled out. If normal function returns and charging is confirmed, document and stop.

Verify Function After Charging

If the unit begins charging normally, allow it to charge sufficiently, then confirm:

  • System powers on
  • Battery indicator behaves normally
  • Unit remains on without immediate shutdown
  • No power or charging warnings remain
  • System can be safely returned to clinical use if all required checks pass

Expected outcome: The power issue is resolved and the device status is clear.

If the Problem Persists

If the MOBILETT Elara Max still will not power on, will not charge, has very short battery runtime, shuts down unexpectedly, or shows repeated power-related warnings after external checks are complete, common external causes have been ruled out.

The issue may involve the battery pack, charging circuit, power supply, internal cabling, power management electronics, or system control hardware.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service
  • Labeled Out of Service
  • Sent for repair, bench evaluation, or vendor service support

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue using a mobile X-ray system with unreliable power, charging, or battery performance.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot power or charging problems during an active patient exam. If the system cannot reliably power on or maintain charge, move imaging to another available unit before continuing patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the Siemens MOBILETT Elara Max would not hold a charge and powered down during mobile imaging rounds."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Clinical Engineering verified the unit was connected to a known-good outlet but showed poor battery runtime after charging and displayed a battery-related warning."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed the unit from service, labeled it Out of Service, documented charging behavior and warning message, and sent the system for battery and power subsystem evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Whether the outlet was tested
  • Whether the AC cord was inspected or swapped
  • Charging indicator behavior
  • Battery percentage or displayed battery status
  • Whether the unit powers on while plugged in
  • Whether the unit powers on while unplugged
  • Any boot or power-related error messages
  • Whether the issue occurs after full charge or only after extended use
  • Unusual heat, odor, clicking, buzzing, or fan behavior
  • Whether the system was stored unplugged
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Power issues on a mobile X-ray system should be approached carefully and logically. Confirm patient safety first, then verify outlet power, cord condition, charging indication, battery status, and operator workflow before assuming an internal failure. If the system remains unreliable, removing it from service protects patients, staff, and imaging operations. Clear CCR documentation also helps repair teams identify whether the issue is battery, charger, AC input, or power management related.

That is successful troubleshooting.
