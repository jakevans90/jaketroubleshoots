ICU Medical Plum 360

Alarm: Depleted Battery! Plug into AC now!

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

Plum 360

What This Guide Helps With

Depleted Battery alarm indicating critically low battery, risking sudden pump shutdown and interruption of infusion therapy.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If infusion is active, immediately plug the pump into AC power or transfer therapy to another pump
  • Confirm the patient continues receiving ordered therapy without interruption

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm message: “Depleted Battery! Plug into AC now!”
  • Observe when the alarm occurs (startup or during infusion)
  • Check if the pump is still operating or shutting down
  • Look for any additional power-related warnings

3. Connect to AC Power (Immediate Action)

  • Plug the pump into a known working AC outlet
  • Ensure the power cord is securely connected
  • Verify outlet functionality using another device if needed
  • Confirm AC power indicator is present on the pump

4. Inspect Power Cord and Connections

  • Check for damage, fraying, or loose connections
  • Try a known-good power cord if available
  • Ensure the cord is fully seated in the device

5. Confirm Battery Charging Behavior

  • Observe for battery charging indicators or icons
  • Allow the pump to remain plugged in for several minutes
  • Verify the alarm clears and battery status improves

6. Isolate Battery vs Device Issue

  • Try a different outlet
  • Try a different power cord
  • Monitor for intermittent loss of AC power

7. Stop When Resolved

  • If the alarm clears and battery begins charging, return the pump to service
  • Ensure adequate charge before unplugging for patient use

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm continues despite confirmed AC power:

  • External power sources and connections have been ruled out
  • Persistent depleted battery alarms indicate battery failure or internal power system fault
  • Remove device from service
  • Label Out of Service
  • Send for battery replacement or bench evaluation

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device before addressing battery or power issues.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Pump displayed 'Depleted Battery! Plug into AC now!' and could not maintain infusion on battery."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Battery voltage below threshold; condition persisted despite AC connection indicating battery degradation."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Connected to AC power to maintain operation; removed device from service and sent for battery replacement."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Battery-related alarms are critical and must be addressed immediately. Always verify external power sources first, ensure patient safety, and escalate when battery performance is unreliable.

That is successful troubleshooting.
