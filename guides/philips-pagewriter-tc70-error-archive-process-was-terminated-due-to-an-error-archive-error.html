Philips PageWriter TC70

Error: Archive process was terminated due to an error Archive error

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

PageWriter TC70

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot a Philips PageWriter TC70 archive error where the archive process is terminated before completion. This condition may prevent ECG storage, export, transfer, or completion of the expected archive workflow.

Even if ECG acquisition and printing appear normal, an archive failure should be treated seriously because it can affect record retention, ECG availability, and clinical documentation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the PageWriter TC70 is being used for an active ECG, complete patient care using another verified ECG machine if needed.
  • Do not continue relying on the cardiograph if ECG storage, archive completion, or transfer is uncertain.
  • Confirm that patient testing continues on a reliable device.
  • An archive error can affect ECG record retention or transfer, even if the ECG acquisition appears successful.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the displayed error is: “Archive process was terminated due to an error.”
  • Determine whether the error occurs during automatic ECG archive, manual save, network transfer, USB export, startup, shutdown, or intermittent use.
  • Check whether ECG acquisition still works.
  • Check whether reports print normally.
  • Check whether stored ECGs are visible in the archive list.
  • Confirm whether the archive error is repeatable or was a one-time interruption.

Restart the Cardiograph

  • Power the PageWriter TC70 off normally.
  • Wait briefly, then power it back on.
  • Allow the unit to complete startup.
  • Attempt the same archive action again.
  • If the archive process completes without the error, verify ECG storage or transfer before returning the device to service.

Check Power Source and Battery Condition

  • Connect the cardiograph directly to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Verify that AC power is present.
  • Confirm the unit is not operating from a depleted or unstable battery during archive attempts.
  • Avoid questionable outlets, power strips, loose receptacles, or unstable power connections.
  • Repeat the archive attempt while the unit is on stable AC power.

Check Available Storage and Archive Behavior

  • Review whether the internal archive appears full, slow to respond, or unable to display stored ECGs.
  • Confirm stored ECGs can be accessed normally.
  • Delete or export old ECGs only according to facility policy, retention requirements, and clinical workflow.
  • Retest the archive process after confirming available storage and normal archive access.

Confirm the Correct Workflow

  • Ask the user what action was being performed when the error occurred.
  • Confirm whether they were saving an ECG locally, sending to a network destination, exporting to USB, or repeating a failed archive action.
  • Verify patient data entry is complete if the workflow requires it.
  • Confirm the selected destination or archive action is valid.
  • Repeat the same workflow in a controlled setting to see whether the error returns.

Check Network Connection if Archiving to a Remote System

  • If the archive process sends ECGs to a server, MUSE system, shared destination, or network location, inspect the network connection.
  • Verify the Ethernet cable is fully seated.
  • Check the wall jack with a known-good connection if available.
  • Inspect the cable for visible damage.
  • Confirm the network icon or connection status appears normal.
  • Ask whether other ECG carts are transferring normally.
  • If only this device fails, continue troubleshooting the cardiograph or its configuration. If multiple carts fail, escalate the network or destination issue.

Check External Media if Exporting

  • If the error occurs during USB or removable media export, remove the media and inspect it.
  • Confirm the device is not write-protected, damaged, unsupported, or full.
  • Try a known-good approved USB drive if facility policy allows.
  • Repeat the export attempt.
  • If the error follows the USB drive, replace or reformat the media according to facility policy.

Check Date, Time, and Patient Data Fields

  • Verify that the cardiograph date and time are correct.
  • Confirm required patient demographic fields are entered if the archive workflow requires them.
  • Check whether the issue occurs only with one patient record or with all records.
  • Correct workflow or demographic issues before suspecting an internal fault.

Test With a New Sample ECG in a Controlled Setting

  • Create a test ECG according to facility policy or use an appropriate simulator/test workflow.
  • Attempt to save, print, and archive the test record.
  • Confirm whether the test ECG archives successfully without the error.
  • If the test record archives normally, the issue may be tied to one ECG record, one workflow, or one destination.
  • If the test record also fails, suspect a broader archive function issue.

Narrow Down the Failure

  • If the error only occurs during network transfer, suspect network configuration, destination availability, or communication workflow.
  • If the error only occurs during external media export, suspect the USB drive, media format, or export process.
  • If the error occurs during every local save or archive attempt after restart, suspect a device-side software, storage, database, or internal archive system fault.
  • Document the specific failure pattern before sending the unit for repair.

Stop When Resolved

  • If restarting the cardiograph or correcting an external cause resolves the error, verify ECG acquisition works.
  • Verify the report prints correctly.
  • Verify the ECG saves locally.
  • Verify archive or transfer completes.
  • Confirm the error does not return after repeat testing.
  • Return the PageWriter TC70 to service only after successful functional verification.

If the Problem Persists

If the archive error continues after restart, power verification, workflow checks, network checks, external media checks, and sample ECG testing, common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent “Archive process was terminated due to an error” condition may indicate a software, storage, configuration, database, or internal device fault.

Remove the cardiograph from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for bench evaluation or repair.

Do not continue using the cardiograph clinically if ECG records cannot be reliably stored, archived, or transferred.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot archive failures during active patient testing. If ECG records may not save or transfer correctly, move the patient workflow to another verified ECG machine first and protect the patient record process.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips PageWriter TC70 displayed Archive process was terminated due to an error during ECG archive attempt. User reported ECG could not complete archive process."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Archive error confirmed. Restart attempted. External checks performed for AC power, archive workflow, network or USB media connection, record handling, and sample ECG archive test."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Restarted cardiograph and retested archive function. Verified ECG acquisition, printing, local save, and archive completion. If unresolved, removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for bench evaluation or repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Archive errors should be treated as more than a minor inconvenience because they may affect ECG record storage, transfer, and clinical documentation. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact message, restart the cardiograph, check external causes, and escalate when the archive process remains unreliable.

That is successful troubleshooting.
