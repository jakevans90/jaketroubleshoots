Medtronic WarmTouch WT 6000

Keypad, display, or UI startup error

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

WarmTouch WT 6000

What This Guide Helps With

Troubleshooting an unresponsive keypad, blank or abnormal display, or startup/UI error preventing reliable warming unit operation.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Immediately remove the WarmTouch WT 6000 from patient use if the keypad, display, or startup interface is not functioning normally.

If warming is required, notify clinical staff and move the patient to a known-working warming device before troubleshooting.

Expected outcome: The patient is no longer dependent on a warming unit with unreliable controls or status information.

Why it matters: A malfunctioning display or keypad may prevent confirmation of temperature settings, alarms, or safe operating status.

Confirm the Reported Failure

With the unit removed from clinical use, inspect the front panel and document the symptom:

  • Display remains blank after attempting to power on.
  • Display illuminates but is distorted, incomplete, frozen, or shows abnormal colors.
  • One or more keypad buttons do not respond.
  • Unit displays a startup, POST, or UI-related error.
  • Unit powers on but cannot be placed into a normal operating mode.

Record any displayed alarm or error code exactly as shown.

Expected outcome: The complaint is clearly identified as a keypad, display, or startup/UI problem rather than a general warming failure.

Verify Normal Standby Versus a Blank-Display Failure

Connect the unit to an appropriate grounded hospital-grade outlet and observe the status LED.

On the WarmTouch WT 6000, Standby mode normally shows an amber status LED with the display blank. After the On/Standby key is pressed, Ready mode should show a green status LED with the display on.

  • If the LED is amber and the display is blank, press the On/Standby key once.
  • If the LED turns green and the display appears normally, the unit may have been in normal standby. Continue checking keypad response before returning it to use.
  • If the LED is green but the display remains blank or abnormal, stop and proceed toward escalation.
  • If no LED illuminates, evaluate the issue as a possible power-on failure rather than a display-only complaint.

Expected outcome: Normal standby behavior is distinguished from a true display or startup failure.

Check the Power Source and External Power Cord

Inspect the power cord and external connections before suspecting an internal UI failure.

  • Confirm the power cord is fully seated at the warming unit.
  • Inspect for bent pins, damaged insulation, loose strain relief, fluid contamination, or signs of overheating.
  • Test the outlet with another approved device or outlet tester according to department procedure.
  • Try a known-good compatible power cord when available.

Expected outcome: The unit powers into standby with an amber LED and then enters Ready mode with a functional display after pressing On/Standby.

If resolved: Document the external power issue and complete operational verification before returning the unit to service.

Inspect the Keypad and Display Externally

Examine the front panel without opening the unit.

Check for:

  • Cracked or peeling keypad membrane.
  • Stuck, depressed, or damaged buttons.
  • Dried fluid residue or cleaning solution around the keypad edges.
  • Cracked display window or visible moisture behind the display.
  • Signs of impact damage or excessive heat.

Do not continue using the unit if the keypad appears physically stuck or the display cannot reliably communicate settings and alarms.

Expected outcome: No visible external damage, contamination, or stuck key is present.

If damage is found: Remove the unit from service for bench evaluation.

Reattempt Startup Without Pressing Any Key During the Startup Sequence

Power the unit off, wait briefly, then press the On/Standby key once and allow the startup sequence to complete without touching any additional buttons.

A WarmTouch POST keypad error may occur when a key is pressed while the power-on self-test is running. The service documentation identifies error 133 as a keypad error during POST and indicates that repeated occurrence without a key being pressed suggests a keypad fault.

Expected outcome: The unit completes startup normally, the display becomes readable, and all required keys respond appropriately.

If resolved: Document that the startup error cleared after restarting without keypad input. Complete operational verification before return to use.

Check for Electromagnetic Interference or Location-Related Behavior

If the startup/UI issue is intermittent, evaluate whether it occurs only in one clinical location or near other powered equipment.

  • Move the unit away from nearby devices with high electrical activity.
  • Reposition external cabling so it is not bundled tightly with other powered equipment.
  • Try a different approved outlet circuit in a safe evaluation area.

The WarmTouch service documentation notes that certain communication-related faults may be associated with electromagnetic interference and recommends separating devices, repositioning cabling, or using separate outlet circuit branches.

Expected outcome: The device starts and operates normally without display freezing, UI errors, or repeated startup alarms.

If resolved: Document the location-related behavior and determine whether the unit requires further evaluation before clinical return.

Verify Basic Control Response Only if the Unit Starts Normally

If the display is readable and no error remains:

  • Press each necessary front-panel control once to confirm response.
  • Confirm the temperature setting displayed changes appropriately when commanded.
  • Verify the unit reaches a normal ready state without repeating a keypad, display, or UI error.
  • Observe for alarm tones, unexpected resets, or a frozen interface.

Do not place the unit back into patient use solely because it powers on once after a reported UI failure. Repeated operation should be stable and controls must be dependable.

Expected outcome: The display is clear, required keys respond consistently, and no startup/UI fault recurs.

If resolved: Follow department policy for final functional verification and document the result.

Capture Any Error Code Before Escalation

If the unit shows a startup or UI-related error, document:

  • Exact error code or alarm message.
  • LED color at the time of failure.
  • Whether the display was readable.
  • Whether keys responded.
  • Whether the error occurred during startup or after operating.
  • Whether restarting without pressing keys changed the outcome.

The WarmTouch service documentation associates multiple POST and UI faults with internal user-interface or communication failures that require further service evaluation rather than continued clinical use.

Expected outcome: The repair technician or vendor receives meaningful failure information for bench diagnosis.

If the Problem Persists

If the WarmTouch WT 6000 continues to show a blank or abnormal display, unresponsive keypad, repeated keypad POST error, startup failure, or UI communication error after external checks are completed, common external causes have been ruled out and the problem is likely internal.

The device should be:

  • Removed from service.
  • Labeled Out of Service.
  • Sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not open the unit or proceed into internal keypad, display, cable, or circuit-board repair as part of frontline troubleshooting.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting, especially when the device interface is responsible for communicating warming settings and alarm conditions.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot a WarmTouch warming unit while it is actively supporting a patient. Move the patient to another approved warming method first, then evaluate the device only when clinical care is protected.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Clinical staff reported that the Medtronic WarmTouch WT 6000 powered on but the display remained blank and the keypad would not respond."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified appropriate outlet and known-good power cord; unit entered standby but failed to provide a functional display after power-on, indicating an unresolved display/UI failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed WarmTouch WT 6000 from service, labeled unit Out of Service, documented LED behavior and startup symptoms, and routed the device for bench evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and result.
  • Power cord condition and whether a known-good cord was tried.
  • Status LED color in standby and after pressing On/Standby.
  • Whether the display was blank, distorted, frozen, or showing an error.
  • Exact error code or alarm message displayed.
  • Whether any key was pressed during startup.
  • Whether the keypad had stuck buttons, damage, or fluid residue.
  • Whether the issue changed in a different outlet or location.
  • Final device status and repair-routing information.

Final Thought

A keypad, display, or startup UI fault on a patient warming system must be taken seriously because the clinician depends on the interface to confirm operating status and temperature selection. Start with safe external checks, verify normal standby behavior, document any codes accurately, and escalate when reliable control cannot be confirmed.

That is successful troubleshooting.
