Philips HeartStart MRx

Error: Replace Clock Battery

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Philips

Model

HeartStart MRx

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the Replace Clock Battery error on the Philips HeartStart MRx. This condition may cause date/time loss, incorrect event timestamps, clock-retention problems, or unreliable code documentation records after the device has been powered down.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the defibrillator from patient care if the error is active or if date/time accuracy cannot be trusted.
  • Provide clinical staff with a ready backup defibrillator before troubleshooting.
  • Expected outcome: Patient care is protected with an available alternate device.
  • Why it matters: Defibrillators must be reliable, and date/time accuracy affects event records, code documentation, and clinical review.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the HeartStart MRx and confirm the exact message displayed is Replace Clock Battery.
  • Check whether the error appears during startup, after battery replacement, after the device has been powered off, intermittently, or repeatedly after date/time reset.
  • Confirm whether the unit has lost date and time settings.
  • Expected outcome: The Replace Clock Battery error is present or the clock fails to retain date/time.
  • Why it matters: This confirms a true clock backup battery condition rather than a one-time staff report or general configuration issue.

Check Main Power and Main Battery Condition

  • Verify the main battery is installed, charged, and accepted by the device.
  • Connect the MRx to AC power if available and confirm external power is recognized.
  • Confirm the device powers normally from main battery and/or AC power.
  • Why it matters: A weak or absent main battery can create confusion during troubleshooting, but it does not replace the need to correct the clock backup battery condition.

Confirm Date and Time Settings

  • Access the appropriate date/time setting screen according to facility procedure.
  • Check whether the date and time are incorrect, reset, or blank.
  • Correct the date and time if needed.
  • Power the device off briefly, then power it back on and verify whether the date/time is retained.
  • Expected outcome: Date/time remains correct after power cycling.
  • Why it matters: The clock backup battery supports retention of time settings when the device is powered down.

Inspect for Obvious External or Handling Issues

  • Check the device for signs of damage, fluid intrusion, cracked housing, or evidence the unit was dropped.
  • Inspect the main battery bay and external power connection for contamination or damage.
  • Expected outcome: No external damage or contamination is found.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage or fluid exposure may indicate a broader service concern beyond routine clock battery replacement.

Determine Whether Service Is Required

  • If the device powers normally but displays Replace Clock Battery, treat this as a service-level internal clock backup battery condition.
  • Do not ignore the message or return the device to service without correction.
  • Remove the device from clinical use and follow approved Philips HeartStart MRx service procedure and facility policy.
  • Expected outcome: The issue is identified as an internal clock battery service requirement.
  • Why it matters: Even if defibrillation functions appear normal, inaccurate time retention can affect event review and documentation.

Verify Correction After Approved Service

  • After qualified service is completed, power on the device.
  • Set the correct date and time.
  • Power the unit off, remove normal power as appropriate for the verification process, then power it back on and confirm the date/time remains correct.
  • Expected outcome: The Replace Clock Battery error clears and the date/time is retained.
  • Why it matters: Retention verification confirms the service action corrected the reported clock battery error.

Run Required Operational Checks

  • Perform required post-service checks before returning the HeartStart MRx to service.
  • Verify startup is normal.
  • Confirm no Replace Clock Battery message returns.
  • Verify date/time remains correct.
  • Confirm battery status is acceptable.
  • Confirm AC power is recognized.
  • Confirm no additional errors are present.
  • Confirm user checks or operational checks pass per facility procedure.
  • Expected outcome: The device passes required checks with no active errors.
  • Why it matters: A defibrillator should never be returned to service after service-level correction without confirming operational readiness.

Stop When Resolved

  • If the clock battery condition has been corrected, the date/time is retained, and required checks pass, document the work and return the device to service.
  • Expected outcome: The HeartStart MRx is ready for clinical use.

If the Problem Persists

If the Replace Clock Battery error remains after approved service, or the date/time still does not retain correctly, external and routine causes have been ruled out.

Persistent clock battery errors may indicate an internal board, connector, battery contact, or clock-retention circuit issue.

Remove the defibrillator from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a defibrillator on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup device first. A device with unresolved clock or system errors should not be relied on for patient care.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Philips HeartStart MRx displayed Replace Clock Battery error. Staff reported date/time issue after device startup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Replace Clock Battery error confirmed. Date/time retention issue observed after power cycle. Main battery and AC power were checked with no external power issue found."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from service. Clock backup battery condition corrected through approved service process. Reset date/time, verified time retention after power cycle, completed required operational checks, and returned unit to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

The Replace Clock Battery error is not usually a user-operation problem. Clinical Engineering should confirm the error, verify clock behavior, rule out external power concerns, complete approved service correction, and perform required checks before returning the defibrillator to service. Accurate documentation supports both patient safety and event record reliability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
