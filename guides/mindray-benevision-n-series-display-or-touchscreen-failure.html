Mindray BeneVision N Series

Display or Touchscreen Failure

Asset Type

Patient Monitor

Manufacturer

Mindray

Model

BeneVision N Series

What This Guide Helps With

Display blank, frozen, dim, or touchscreen not responding, often caused by power, settings, or external accessory issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the monitor is in use, transfer the patient to another functioning monitor before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient monitoring remains uninterrupted.
  • Why it matters: Loss of display or control prevents safe monitoring and alarm visibility.

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Observe the screen behavior:
    • Completely blank vs dim
    • Frozen image vs active but no touch response
    • Partial display or flickering
  • Expected: Clear understanding of whether issue is display, touchscreen, or both.
  • Why it matters: Helps narrow down whether this is a power, software, or interface issue.

Check Power Source and Indicators

  • Confirm AC power is connected
  • Check power LED indicators
  • Attempt to power cycle the monitor
  • Expected: Monitor powers on normally with display active.
  • Why it matters: Loss of display can be related to power instability or improper startup.

Perform a Full Reboot

  • Power off the monitor completely
  • Disconnect AC power briefly (10–15 seconds)
  • Reconnect and power back on
  • Expected: Display and touchscreen restore after reboot.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary software or interface lockups.

Check Brightness and Display Settings

  • Adjust brightness using physical buttons (if available)
  • Shine a flashlight on the screen to check for faint image
  • Expected: Display becomes visible or confirms backlight issue.
  • Why it matters: A dim or black screen may be a brightness or backlight problem, not full failure.

Inspect Touchscreen Functionality

  • Attempt to use touchscreen across multiple areas
  • Try physical buttons or rotary knob (if equipped)
  • Expected: Either touch works or alternate controls function.
  • Why it matters: Helps determine if failure is isolated to touchscreen vs full interface.

Check for External Interference or Accessories

  • Disconnect any external modules, USB devices, or network cables
  • Remove and reseat parameter modules
  • Expected: Display/touch returns to normal after removing interference.
  • Why it matters: Faulty accessories or modules can cause system instability.

Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Look for cracks, liquid intrusion, or delamination
  • Check for unresponsive areas or ghost touches
  • Expected: No visible damage affecting function.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage often explains touchscreen or display failure.

Test with Known Good Power Source

  • Plug into a verified working outlet
  • Avoid power strips or extension cords
  • Expected: Stable operation with known good power.
  • Why it matters: Eliminates facility power issues.

Observe Startup Behavior

  • Does the Mindray logo appear?
  • Does the screen fail before or after boot?
  • Expected: Normal boot sequence with functional display.
  • Why it matters: Failure during boot suggests internal hardware or system board issue.

If the Problem Persists

If display or touchscreen failure continues after verifying power, rebooting, and removing external causes, the issue is likely internal (LCD panel, touchscreen digitizer, or system board).

Remove the monitor from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation. Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and wasted time.

Clinical Use Tip

Never attempt to troubleshoot a display or touchscreen issue while the monitor is actively being used on a patient. Always transfer monitoring first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Monitor display blank and touchscreen unresponsive during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed to respond after reboot and external accessories removed; suspected internal display/touchscreen failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent for repair evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Power outlet tested
  • Reboot attempted
  • Brightness adjusted
  • Touchscreen response behavior
  • Physical buttons functional or not
  • Visible screen damage
  • Startup screen observed or not
  • Final device status (removed from service)

Final Thought

Display and touchscreen failures directly impact patient monitoring visibility and control. Always start with power and simple resets, but escalate quickly when basic checks fail. Clear documentation ensures efficient repair and accountability.

That is successful troubleshooting.
