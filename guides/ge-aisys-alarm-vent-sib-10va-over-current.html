GE Healthcare Aisys

Alarm: VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT

Asset Type

Anesthesia Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

Aisys

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot the GE Healthcare Aisys VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT alarm. This condition may be caused by incorrect Ventilator Interface Board current feedback, a possible VIB fault, or an ACB-related power or control failure.

Because this is a ventilator electronics-related alarm, the anesthesia machine should not be trusted for patient ventilation until the issue is evaluated, corrected, and the machine passes required checkout and functional testing.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the anesthesia machine from patient use if the alarm occurs during setup, checkout, or active operation.
  • Have anesthesia staff move to a backup anesthesia machine or alternate ventilation method as clinically appropriate.
  • Do not continue using the machine for patient ventilation with an unresolved ventilator electronics alarm.
  • Confirm patient ventilation and anesthesia delivery are maintained using safe alternate equipment.

2. Verify the Reported Alarm and Behavior

  • Confirm the alarm or logged event is exactly: VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT.
  • Check whether the alarm appears during startup, during system checkout, when ventilation is enabled, intermittently during use, or only in the error log.
  • Observe for associated symptoms such as ventilator failure, startup failure, checkout failure, repeated alarm after reboot, or loss of normal ventilation function.
  • Confirm whether this is an active alarm or a historic logged event.

3. Power Cycle and Check for Simple External Issues

  • Power the anesthesia machine down using the normal shutdown process.
  • Disconnect the machine from patient use and allow the system to fully power off.
  • Verify the machine is connected to a proper AC power source.
  • Check the power cord, outlet, and machine power entry area for obvious damage or loose connection.
  • Restart the system and observe whether the alarm returns.
  • If the alarm clears and does not return, complete checkout per hospital procedure before returning the device to service.

4. Check for Obvious Physical or Environmental Causes

  • Inspect the anesthesia machine externally for fluid intrusion.
  • Check for recent cleaning saturation or evidence of liquid entering the machine.
  • Look for signs of physical impact, loose panels, or damaged external connectors.
  • Check for burning smell, unusual heat, or visible contamination.
  • Ask whether recent service activity occurred before the alarm appeared.
  • If fluid intrusion, burning smell, or overheating is found, stop troubleshooting and remove the machine from service.

5. Review the Alarm Log

  • Access the machine’s error or alarm log if available through normal Clinical Engineering access.
  • Confirm whether VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT is current, repeated, or historic.
  • Look for related ventilator, VIB, SIB, power, or ACB events around the same time.
  • Repeated entries support a real hardware fault rather than a one-time event.

6. Isolate the VIB Power Harness

  • With the anesthesia machine powered off and removed from service, disconnect the VIB power harness according to approved service access procedures.
  • Restart the system and check whether the VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT error reappears in the log.
  • If the error does not reappear after disconnecting the VIB power harness, suspect the VIB.
  • If the error persists after disconnecting the VIB power harness, suspect the ACB or related internal control circuitry.

7. Replace or Escalate Based on the Isolation Result

  • If the error clears with the VIB power harness disconnected, replace the VIB and retest the system.
  • If the error persists with the VIB isolated, remove the machine from service for ACB replacement or higher-level bench evaluation.
  • Do not continue repeated restarts or return the machine to service with an unresolved ventilator electronics alarm.

8. Retest Before Returning to Service

  • After corrective action, restart the anesthesia machine.
  • Confirm the VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT alarm no longer appears.
  • Run the required anesthesia machine checkout and ventilator functional testing per facility procedure.
  • Verify ventilation operates normally.
  • Review the error log for repeat entries.
  • Do not return the machine to service based only on alarm silence after reboot.

If the Problem Persists

If the alarm persists after power cycling, external power checks, visible condition checks, VIB harness isolation, and VIB replacement, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT conditions strongly indicate an internal electronics failure, most likely involving the ACB or related ventilator control circuitry.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair, board replacement, or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated restarts or place the machine back into clinical use with an unresolved ventilator electronics alarm.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot this alarm on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup anesthesia machine or alternate approved ventilation method first. Avoid relying on an anesthesia machine with unresolved ventilator board, power, or overcurrent alarms.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE Healthcare Aisys anesthesia machine reported VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT alarm. Device removed from service due to ventilator electronics fault concern."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Alarm confirmed in system log. External power and visible condition checked. VIB power harness isolation performed. Alarm cleared with VIB disconnected, indicating likely VIB fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed anesthesia machine from patient use. Replaced VIB after isolation testing. Restarted system, completed checkout and ventilator functional testing, and verified VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT alarm did not return."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

VENT SIB 10VA OVER CURRENT should be treated as a ventilator electronics fault until proven otherwise. Start with patient safety, confirm the alarm, check simple external causes, then isolate logically between the VIB and ACB. Proper escalation and clear CCR documentation protect the patient, the technician, and the facility.

That is successful troubleshooting.
