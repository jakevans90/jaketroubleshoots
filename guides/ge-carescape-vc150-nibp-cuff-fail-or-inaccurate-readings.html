GE Healthcare CARESCAPE VC150

NIBP Cuff Fail or Inaccurate Readings

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

CARESCAPE VC150

What This Guide Helps With

This guide assists Clinical Engineering in troubleshooting non-invasive blood pressure (NIBP) failures or inaccurate readings on the CARESCAPE VC150. Symptoms may include cuff fail messages, repeated measurement errors, abnormally high/low readings, or inability to complete a BP cycle. The focus is on external components and setup before considering internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot while the device is in use on a patient.
  • Move the patient to another monitor before beginning troubleshooting.

Verify Patient Condition and Movement

  • Ensure the patient is still, relaxed, and not talking during measurement.
  • Movement, shivering, or arrhythmias can cause inaccurate readings or cuff failures.

Check Cuff Size and Placement

  • Confirm the correct cuff size is being used for the patient.
  • Ensure the cuff is wrapped snugly (not too loose or tight) and positioned at heart level.
  • Incorrect sizing or placement is one of the most common causes of errors.

Inspect the NIBP Cuff for Damage

  • Look for tears, worn Velcro, or internal bladder leaks.
  • If the cuff does not hold pressure or inflates unevenly, replace it.

Inspect NIBP Hose and Connections

  • Check the hose for cracks, kinks, or loose fittings.
  • Ensure the hose is fully seated at both the monitor and cuff connection points.
  • Air leaks will result in cuff fail alarms.

Swap with Known Good Accessories

  • Replace the cuff and hose with known good components.
  • This helps isolate whether the issue is accessory-related or device-related.

Check for Obstructions or Kinks

  • Ensure tubing is not pinched under the bed or wrapped tightly.
  • Obstructed airflow can prevent proper inflation/deflation.

Observe Pump Operation

  • Start an NIBP cycle and listen for the internal pump.
  • Note if the pump sounds weak, excessively loud, or fails to engage.

Check for Error Messages or Alarm Codes

  • Document any displayed messages (e.g., cuff fail, inflation error).
  • Repeated specific errors may indicate internal pneumatic issues.

Verify Settings and Mode

  • Ensure the correct patient type (adult/pediatric/neonate) is selected.
  • Incorrect mode can affect pressure limits and measurement accuracy.

Perform Multiple Test Cycles

  • Run several test measurements using a simulator or manual verification method if available.
  • Inconsistent results across tests may indicate internal issues.

If the Problem Persists

If all external components (cuff, hose, connections, setup) have been verified and the issue continues, the problem is likely internal (pump, valve assembly, or pressure sensor).

  • Remove the device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or perform bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop external troubleshooting and escalate is proper Clinical Engineering practice.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot NIBP issues on an active patient. Always move the patient to another monitor before testing. Inaccurate blood pressure readings can lead to inappropriate clinical decisions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“Nursing reported repeated “cuff fail” alarms and inability to obtain BP readings.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
“Inspection revealed a leaking NIBP hose causing loss of pressure during inflation.”

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
“Replaced NIBP hose with known good unit. Verified proper operation with multiple successful BP cycles.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Cuff size verified
  • Hose and cuff swapped
  • Pump sound observed
  • Alarm messages documented
  • Tubing checked for kinks/leaks
  • Multiple test cycles completed
  • Final device status (in service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Accurate NIBP readings depend heavily on proper accessories and setup. Most issues can be resolved through basic checks like cuff condition, hose integrity, and patient factors. When those are ruled out, escalation is appropriate. Always prioritize patient safety and document clearly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
