Hillrom VersaCare

Error: Err5 Scale Display Error

Asset Type

Hospital Bed

Manufacturer

Hillrom

Model

VersaCare

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Err5 on the Hillrom VersaCare bed scale display. Err5 may prevent normal scale operation and can make the bed scale unreliable for patient weight measurement. The condition may be related to European scale configuration settings or scale P.C. board failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not rely on the bed scale while Err5 is active.
  • Remove the bed from patient use if accurate bed weight is required for medication dosing, fluid monitoring, nutrition, or clinical documentation.
  • If the bed is occupied, do not troubleshoot DIP switch settings or internal scale components while the patient is in the bed unless facility policy allows safe non-invasive checks.
  • Use another approved weighing method if patient weight is clinically needed.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the scale display shows Err5.
  • Check whether the error appears during bed power-up, when scale is selected, after bed movement, after power interruption, or after service activity.
  • Confirm whether other bed functions operate normally, including bed articulation, side rail controls, brake or steer functions, and scale display response.
  • Verify the error is repeatable and not a one-time display issue.

Check External and Simple Causes First

  • Confirm the bed is connected to a known-good AC outlet.
  • Inspect the power cord for damage, loose connection, or signs of strain.
  • Confirm the bed powers normally and does not show low battery or intermittent power behavior.
  • Correct any obvious power issue before continuing scale troubleshooting.

Check for Obvious Physical or Environmental Issues

  • Inspect the scale display, side rails, base frame, load cell areas, cable pinch points, and under-bed components.
  • Look for fluid intrusion, impact damage, loose panels, damaged harnesses, debris under the frame, or anything contacting the bed frame during weighing.
  • Remove visible obstruction or debris if found.
  • Do not assume a board failure until obvious external causes have been checked.

Confirm the Error Context

  • Identify whether the bed has recently had scale service, scale board replacement, software or configuration changes, DIP switch changes, or prior scale troubleshooting.
  • The manual labels Scale Error 4 and Scale Error 5 as European Scale Only conditions.
  • Err5 may point to an incorrect scale configuration instead of an immediate component failure.

Check DIP Switch Configuration

  • Follow facility service procedure and access the scale configuration area as appropriate.
  • Turn DIP switch #4 to the OFF position.
  • Turn DIP switch #2 to the OFF position.
  • Power cycle or reset the bed as required by facility procedure.
  • Recheck the scale display and confirm whether Err5 clears.

If the Error Clears, Perform Function Checks

  • Verify the scale display powers normally.
  • Verify the scale function operates without Err5.
  • Check bed movement functions and control response.
  • Confirm no new alarms or errors appear.
  • Return the bed to service only after all required function checks pass.

If Err5 Does Not Clear

  • If DIP switch #4 and DIP switch #2 are OFF and Err5 remains, suspect scale P.C. board failure.
  • Remove the bed from service.
  • Label the bed Out of Service.
  • Send the bed for repair or bench evaluation.
  • Replace the scale P.C. board only if authorized by facility procedure and parts availability.

If the Problem Remains After Scale Board Replacement

  • Stop further basic troubleshooting.
  • Contact Hill-Rom Technical Support or qualified repair support.
  • Do not continue replacing parts without additional technical direction.
  • Persistent Err5 after scale board replacement may indicate a deeper configuration, harness, compatibility, or system-level issue.

If the Problem Persists

If Err5 remains after verifying stable power, checking for obvious damage, confirming DIP switch #4 and DIP switch #2 are OFF, and replacing the scale P.C. board if authorized, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Remove the bed from service, label it Out of Service, document the failure clearly, and send the bed for repair or bench evaluation. Escalate to Hill-Rom Technical Support or qualified repair support if the error remains after the specified correction path.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient when service access, configuration changes, or internal scale components are involved. Move the patient to a backup bed or use another approved weighing method first if accurate patient weight is needed.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Hillrom VersaCare bed scale display showing Err5. Scale function unavailable or not reliable for patient weight measurement."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Err5 confirmed on scale display. Power and visible condition checked. Manual identifies Scale Error 4 and Scale Error 5 as European Scale Only conditions. DIP switch #4 and DIP switch #2 required OFF position verification."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Set DIP switch #4 and DIP switch #2 to OFF. Err5 cleared. Performed bed and scale function checks. Bed passed checks and was returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Err5 on a Hillrom VersaCare bed should be approached as a scale configuration or scale electronics issue. Start with patient safety, confirm the displayed error, rule out simple power and physical causes, verify the DIP switch settings, and only then escalate to scale board replacement or Hill-Rom support. Clear documentation protects the patient, the technician, and the facility.

That is successful troubleshooting.
