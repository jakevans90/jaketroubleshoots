Medtronic Valleylab FT10

Error: E326 Check Instrument

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

Medtronic

Model

Valleylab FT10

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error E326 Check Instrument on the Medtronic Valleylab FT10 Electrosurgical Unit. This error occurs when the FT10 detects the LigaSure handswitch as pressed, stuck, or sending an unintended activation input, which can prevent normal ESU readiness or safe activation.

Because this condition involves an activation-control input, it should be treated as a patient safety issue until the instrument, accessory connection, and generator response are verified.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Valleylab FT10 from patient use if Error E326 appears during setup, activation, or a procedure.
  • Stop using the affected LigaSure instrument.
  • Have clinical staff switch to another ESU, another LigaSure device, or another approved method if the case must continue.
  • Do not repeatedly attempt activation while the error is present.
  • Confirm patient care continues without relying on an ESU accessory that may be falsely activating or stuck.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the Valleylab FT10 and observe the screen.
  • Confirm the displayed message is Error: E326 Check Instrument.
  • Ask staff whether the error appeared at startup, when the LigaSure instrument was connected, when the handswitch was pressed, during activation, or intermittently during the case.
  • Check whether the LigaSure instrument appears physically stuck, damaged, wet, contaminated, or difficult to actuate.
  • Document whether the error is reproducible or clearly linked to the LigaSure instrument or handswitch condition.

Release and Inspect the LigaSure Handswitch

  • Confirm the handswitch button is not being held down.
  • Release the handswitch completely.
  • Inspect the instrument handle for a stuck button, cracked housing, fluid contamination, debris, or mechanical binding.
  • Disconnect and reconnect the LigaSure instrument.
  • Restart the generator only after the instrument has been removed or reconnected properly.
  • If the handswitch was stuck or unintentionally pressed, the error should clear after the switch is released or the instrument is corrected.

Check for User Setup or Handling Issues

  • Ask staff whether the instrument was plugged in while the handswitch was already pressed.
  • Confirm the instrument cable is not pulled tight, pinched, wrapped around other equipment, or positioned in a way that presses the handswitch.
  • Verify the instrument is fully seated in the correct LigaSure receptacle.
  • Check that the ESU recognizes the instrument without immediately displaying E326.

Replace the LigaSure Instrument

  • Disconnect the suspected LigaSure instrument from the FT10.
  • Connect a known-good compatible LigaSure instrument.
  • Power cycle the FT10 if needed.
  • Observe whether E326 returns.
  • If the replacement instrument works normally, remove the original LigaSure instrument from use according to facility policy.

Inspect the Instrument Connection Port

  • Inspect the LigaSure port for obvious damage, bent contacts, contamination, fluid residue, or debris.
  • Do not insert tools into the connector.
  • Clean only according to facility-approved external cleaning procedures.
  • Reconnect a known-good instrument and verify whether the error returns.
  • If the port appears damaged or contaminated and the error persists, remove the generator from service.

Compare With Other ESU Functions

  • Determine whether the problem follows the instrument or stays with the generator.
  • Test the same LigaSure instrument on another compatible FT10 only if allowed by facility policy and the instrument is safe to test.
  • Test a known-good LigaSure instrument on the affected FT10.
  • Check whether monopolar or bipolar functions report separate errors.
  • If the error follows the instrument, replace or discard the instrument according to facility policy.
  • If the error stays with the FT10, suspect a generator-side detection issue.

Check for Intermittent Behavior

  • With no patient connected, gently reposition the instrument cable and handle.
  • Observe whether E326 reappears during normal cable or handle movement.
  • Do not activate energy output unless following approved test procedures and using proper test equipment or load.
  • If the error returns while the cable or handle is moved, suspect an intermittent handswitch or cable fault.

If the Problem Persists

If E326 remains after releasing the handswitch, replacing the LigaSure instrument, checking the connector, and testing with known-good accessories, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent E326 may indicate a generator-side detection problem involving the controller circuitry or instrument input path.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or bench evaluation.

Do not continue clinical use with an unresolved handswitch detection error.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a LigaSure handswitch error on an active patient. Move the patient or procedure to a backup ESU, approved energy source, or safe alternate setup before troubleshooting continues.

A device that falsely detects activation should not be trusted for patient use until the accessory or generator issue is resolved.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Medtronic Valleylab FT10 displayed Error E326 Check Instrument while staff were using a LigaSure instrument."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"LigaSure handswitch input was detected as active. Original instrument was inspected and replaced. Error cleared with a known-good LigaSure instrument, indicating a faulty or stuck LigaSure instrument."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed device from patient use, released and inspected LigaSure handswitch, checked instrument connection, power cycled generator, and tested with a known-good LigaSure instrument. FT10 returned to service after error cleared with replacement instrument."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Error E326 should be treated as an activation-detection problem, not just a nuisance message. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact error, check the LigaSure handswitch and instrument first, then isolate whether the issue follows the accessory or remains with the generator.

If known-good accessories do not clear the error, escalation to repair is appropriate. Clear CCR documentation protects the patient, the user, and the Clinical Engineering record.

That is successful troubleshooting.
