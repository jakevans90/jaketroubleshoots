Karl Storz Xenon Nova 300

Lamp Not Igniting or Low Output

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Karl Storz

Model

Xenon Nova 300

What This Guide Helps With

Lamp not turning on or producing weak illumination due to power, connection, lamp life, or external system setup issues.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • If the light source fails during a procedure, immediately transition to a backup light source or system.
  • Expected: Procedure continues safely with adequate visualization
  • Why it matters: Loss of illumination eliminates visualization and creates immediate patient risk

Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the unit and observe behavior:
  • No light output at all
  • Light attempts to start but fails
  • Dim or flickering illumination
  • Check for any status indicators, alarms, or fan activity.
  • Expected: Clear identification of whether the issue is no ignition vs low output
  • Why it matters: Helps differentiate between lamp failure and system/setup issues

Check Power Source and Unit Status (Most Common Cause)

  • Confirm unit is plugged into a known-good outlet
  • Verify power switch is ON
  • Observe front panel indicators or display
  • Test outlet if needed.
  • Expected: Unit powers on with normal indicators
  • Why it matters: No lamp ignition can simply be a power issue

Confirm Light Source is Enabled

  • Ensure the light output is not set to standby or minimum
  • Adjust brightness/output control
  • Verify system is not waiting for scope connection (if applicable setup)
  • Expected: Lamp attempts ignition when output is increased
  • Why it matters: Some systems will not activate lamp without proper enable conditions

Inspect Light Cable and Scope Connection

  • Ensure light cable is fully seated in the light source port
  • Check for proper locking/engagement
  • Inspect cable for damage (burn marks, cracks, discoloration)
  • Confirm proper connection to scope or camera head
  • Expected: Secure, undamaged connection
  • Why it matters: Poor coupling reduces or blocks light transmission

Check Light Cable for Damage or Degradation

  • Swap with a known-good light cable if available
  • Expected: Normal brightness restored with alternate cable
  • Why it matters: Fiber optic cables degrade over time, causing low output

Evaluate Lamp Life / Usage Hours (Very Common Cause)

  • Check lamp hour counter if available
  • Determine if lamp is near or beyond expected life
  • Expected: Lamp within usable life range
  • Why it matters: Xenon lamps lose intensity before complete failure

Attempt Lamp Re-Ignition Cycle

  • Power the unit OFF
  • Wait 30–60 seconds
  • Power ON and attempt ignition again
  • Expected: Lamp ignites normally
  • Why it matters: Xenon lamps sometimes require a reset cycle to ignite

Observe for Cooling or Fan Issues

  • Listen for internal fan operation
  • Check for overheating indicators or shutdown behavior
  • Expected: Fan running normally with no over-temp warnings
  • Why it matters: Overheating can prevent lamp ignition or reduce output

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out and the lamp still does not ignite or output remains low, the issue is likely internal (lamp module failure, igniter circuit, or power supply issue).

Remove the device from service
Label as Out of Service
Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and damage.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot illumination issues during an active procedure. Always transition the patient to a functioning light source before evaluating the equipment.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Light source not producing illumination during endoscopic procedure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Xenon lamp at end-of-life with degraded output."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Replaced lamp module and verified normal light output."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Outlet tested and verified
  • Light cable swapped
  • Lamp hours checked
  • Indicator lights and fan behavior observed
  • Any flickering or delayed ignition noted
  • Final device status (operational or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Start with simple external checks like power, connections, and accessories before assuming lamp or internal failure. Illumination issues are often caused by cables or lamp wear. Escalate appropriately once these are ruled out, and always prioritize patient safety and clear documentation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
