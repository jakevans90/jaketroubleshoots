Carestream DRX-Revolution

Exposure Inhibited Due to Generator, Tube, or System Interlock Fault

Asset Type

Mobile X-Ray System

Manufacturer

Carestream

Model

DRX-Revolution

What This Guide Helps With

Exposure inhibited conditions caused by readiness, generator, tube, battery, detector, door, motion, or safety interlock problems.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

Do not continue attempting exposures on a patient until the inhibit condition is understood.

Action:

  • Remove the DRX-Revolution from patient use if imaging cannot be completed safely.
  • Use another mobile X-ray system if the exam is urgent.
  • Do not repeatedly attempt exposures if the unit displays generator, tube, or system fault messages.

Expected outcome: Patient imaging is completed with a reliable system, and the affected unit is evaluated safely.

Why it matters: Exposure inhibit faults may prevent image acquisition, interrupt patient care, or indicate a safety condition that the system is intentionally blocking.

Verify the Exact Inhibit Message

Confirm what the system is actually reporting before troubleshooting.

Action:

  • Check the workstation display, system status area, generator status, and any warning or error messages.
  • Document whether the issue appears as exposure inhibited.
  • Document whether the issue appears as generator not ready.
  • Document whether the issue appears as tube not ready.
  • Document whether the issue appears as system interlock active.
  • Document whether the issue appears as detector not ready.
  • Document whether the issue appears as battery or power-related inhibit.
  • Document whether the issue appears as emergency stop or safety interlock condition.

Expected outcome: The exact symptom is identified instead of treating all exposure failures as the same issue.

If the issue clears after acknowledging a temporary message and the unit exposes normally, document the event and stop.

Confirm the Exam and Exposure Workflow

Make sure the unit is in a valid state to allow exposure.

Action:

  • Confirm a patient or test exam is selected.
  • Verify the correct anatomy, view, detector selection, and exposure technique are selected.
  • Confirm the system is not waiting for operator input, image acceptance, detector selection, or procedure confirmation.
  • Exit and re-enter the exam if the software appears stuck.

Expected outcome: The system allows exposure once the workflow is complete and no required selection is missing.

Why it matters: Mobile X-ray systems may inhibit exposure when the workstation, detector, or generator workflow is incomplete.

Check Emergency Stop and Safety Controls

A safety control may be active or partially engaged.

Action:

  • Inspect the emergency stop button and confirm it is not pressed.
  • Reset the emergency stop if it is engaged.
  • Check for any visible safety switch, brake, drive, or system lockout condition.
  • Confirm the unit is stationary and stable before attempting exposure.

Expected outcome: Exposure is enabled if the inhibit was caused by an active safety stop or motion-related interlock.

If the emergency stop will not reset or the system continues to show a safety interlock fault, remove the unit from service.

Verify Power and Battery Status

Low battery or unstable power can prevent generator readiness.

Action:

  • Check battery charge level.
  • Connect the unit to a known-good AC outlet if battery charge is low.
  • Confirm the charging indicator behaves normally.
  • Allow the system time to stabilize after connecting to AC power.
  • Avoid attempting exposure during a low-battery or shutdown warning condition.

Expected outcome: The generator becomes ready once adequate power is available.

Why it matters: A mobile X-ray generator requires enough stored energy to support exposure. Low or unstable battery conditions can trigger exposure inhibit messages.

Confirm the Unit Is Fully Booted and Ready

The generator, workstation, and detector must all be ready before exposure.

Action:

  • Restart the system if it appears partially booted, frozen, or stuck in a not-ready state.
  • Allow the DRX-Revolution to complete startup fully.
  • Check that the workstation is responsive.
  • Confirm the generator status changes to ready before attempting exposure.

Expected outcome: A normal restart clears temporary software or communication readiness issues.

If the same inhibit returns immediately after restart, continue troubleshooting.

Check Detector Readiness

A detector or detector communication issue may prevent exposure.

Action:

  • Confirm the wireless detector is powered on.
  • Check detector battery level.
  • Verify the correct detector is selected for the exam.
  • Confirm the detector is connected or recognized by the system.
  • Reseat or reconnect the detector battery if appropriate and safe.
  • Try a known-good compatible detector if available.

Expected outcome: Exposure is allowed once the selected detector is available and ready.

Why it matters: The system may inhibit exposure if it cannot confirm that the intended image receptor is ready.

Inspect the Exposure Switch

The exposure switch may be disconnected, damaged, or not completing the prep/expose sequence.

Action:

  • Inspect the hand switch and cable for visible damage.
  • Confirm the switch is fully connected.
  • Check whether the first-stage prep and second-stage exposure actions feel normal.
  • Try a known-good compatible exposure switch if available.

Expected outcome: The unit allows prep and exposure if the original switch or connection was the cause.

If the switch is damaged, remove it from use and replace it according to site policy.

Verify Tube Head and Arm Positioning

The system may block exposure if positioning, locks, or tube readiness are abnormal.

Action:

  • Confirm the tube head, column, and arm are positioned normally.
  • Check whether any mechanical locks, brakes, or detents are not fully seated.
  • Look for messages related to arm position, tube position, SID, or motion.
  • Reposition the tube head and arm, then attempt a test exposure in a safe test environment.

Expected outcome: Exposure is enabled if the inhibit was caused by an unresolved positioning or motion interlock.

Why it matters: Mobile X-ray systems may use interlocks to prevent exposure while the system is moving, unstable, or not in a valid imaging position.

Check Technique Settings

Invalid or extreme technique settings can prevent exposure.

Action:

  • Review kVp, mAs, exposure mode, and selected exam protocol.
  • Select a standard adult chest or general test protocol if permitted by local policy.
  • Avoid unusual manual settings during troubleshooting.
  • Confirm the selected technique is within normal operating range.

Expected outcome: Exposure is allowed if the inhibit was caused by an invalid or unsupported technique selection.

Check for Tube Heat or Cooling Delay

The tube may be temporarily inhibited due to heat or readiness timing.

Action:

  • Look for tube heat, cooling, or wait messages.
  • Ask clinical staff whether multiple exposures were attempted in rapid sequence.
  • Allow the unit to cool if a heat-related message is present.
  • Do not bypass or ignore tube heat warnings.

Expected outcome: Exposure becomes available after the tube returns to an allowable operating condition.

Why it matters: Tube heat protection prevents damage to the tube and protects against unsafe operating conditions.

Check for Recent Collisions, Drops, or Movement Issues

Physical impact or motion faults may create system interlocks.

Action:

  • Ask whether the unit recently hit a wall, door frame, bed, elevator threshold, or other obstruction.
  • Inspect the tube head, arm, detector, exposure switch, and workstation for obvious damage.
  • Check for unusual sounds, binding, loose covers, or warning messages.

Expected outcome: External damage or impact history is identified before assuming an internal generator failure.

If impact damage is suspected, remove the unit from service for inspection.

Perform a Safe Test Exposure Only If Appropriate

Test only when the area is controlled and radiation safety requirements are met.

Action:

  • Use the site-approved test process, phantom, or non-clinical test setup.
  • Follow radiation safety practices.
  • Verify whether prep occurs, whether exposure is attempted, and whether the inhibit appears before or during exposure.
  • Do not repeatedly force exposures after a generator or tube fault.

Expected outcome: The failure point is confirmed: before prep, during prep, at exposure command, or after exposure attempt.

Review Error History or Event Logs If Available

The system may contain useful fault history.

Action:

  • Check accessible system messages or event history if available to Clinical Engineering.
  • Record exact fault wording, fault codes, timestamps, and conditions.
  • Do not clear logs before documenting useful information.

Expected outcome: Repair evaluation has enough information to separate generator, tube, detector, battery, or interlock faults.

If the Problem Persists

If the DRX-Revolution still inhibits exposure after patient safety, power, battery, detector, workflow, exposure switch, positioning, and basic interlock checks have been completed, the fault is likely internal or service-level.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair, vendor service, or bench evaluation.

Possible internal causes may include generator fault, tube fault, high-voltage issue, internal interlock failure, control board problem, system communication fault, or battery/power delivery issue.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Repeated exposure attempts should not be used as a repair strategy.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not troubleshoot exposure inhibit faults while the unit is being used on an active patient. Move the patient to another mobile X-ray system first if imaging is clinically needed. Treat generator, tube, and interlock messages as safety-related until proven otherwise.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Radiology reported the Carestream DRX-Revolution would not expose and displayed an exposure inhibited message during a portable exam."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Clinical Engineering found the unit powered on normally, but exposure remained inhibited after checking AC power, battery status, detector readiness, exposure switch connection, positioning, and workflow setup."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Unit was removed from service, labeled Out of Service, fault details were documented, and the system was escalated for generator/tube/interlock service evaluation."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior, including the exact exposure inhibit message or fault code
  • Accessories swapped, including detector, detector battery, or exposure switch
  • Power behavior, including outlet tested, AC power verified, battery charge level, and charging behavior
  • Environmental factors, including whether the issue followed a collision or transport event
  • Indicator lights, including charging indicators and system readiness indicators
  • Final device status, including whether the unit was returned to service or labeled Out of Service

Final Thought

Exposure inhibit troubleshooting should follow a safe and logical order. Start with patient safety, then rule out power, workflow, detector, switch, positioning, and visible interlock issues before suspecting internal generator or tube failure. Clear documentation helps repair teams identify whether the issue is operational, accessory-related, power-related, or a true service fault.

That is successful troubleshooting.
