Physio-Control LIFEPAK 15

Failed Self-Tests on Power-Up

Asset Type

Defibrillator

Manufacturer

Physio-Control

Model

LIFEPAK 15

What This Guide Helps With

This guide addresses situations where the LIFEPAK 15:

  • Fails its automated self-test during power-on
  • Shows error messages, warnings, or beeps related to system checks
  • May display “Service Required” or “Check Battery” alerts immediately after powering on

Focus is on logical, external troubleshooting to verify common, fixable causes before assuming internal failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Verify battery status

  • Ensure the battery is fully seated and charged.
  • A low or poorly seated battery can cause self-test failure.
  • Why: LIFEPAK 15 self-tests check battery health and connection first.

Inspect battery and device contacts

  • Remove battery and inspect terminals for corrosion, dirt, or debris.
  • Clean contacts with a dry, lint-free cloth.
  • Reinstall battery and ensure a snug fit.
  • Why: Poor connection may trigger system errors during self-test.

Check charger and power source

  • Confirm the device is connected to a known working charger or AC outlet.
  • Test the outlet with another device to ensure proper voltage.
  • Why: Self-test may fail if battery charge is insufficient or unstable.

Remove external accessories

  • Disconnect ECG leads, paddles, and other attachments.
  • Power cycle device without accessories.
  • Why: Faulty or shorted accessories can cause self-test errors.

Observe error codes/messages

  • Take note of any messages displayed during the self-test (e.g., “Battery Low,” “Hardware Fault,” “Sensor Error”).
  • This helps identify whether the issue is battery, electronics, or accessory related.

Power cycle the device

  • Turn off, wait 30 seconds, then power back on.
  • Why: Temporary glitches can sometimes be cleared with a simple reset.

Try a known-good battery

  • If available, insert a fully charged, verified battery.
  • Repeat power-on self-test.
  • Why: Confirms whether the battery is the root cause.

If the Problem Persists

External checks have been exhausted.

The device may have internal hardware or firmware issues.

Action:

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label as Out of Service.
  • Send to authorized service for bench evaluation or repair.

Note: Knowing when to escalate is proper troubleshooting and maintains patient safety.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot a device on an active patient.

Move the patient to a working device first.

Only power-cycle or check self-test when the device is offline and safe to manipulate.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
“LIFEPAK 15 fails self-test on power-up, displaying error messages immediately.”

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
Observed fully seated battery, clean contacts, and no external accessory faults. Device still fails self-test. Likely internal hardware/firmware issue.

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
Device removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent to authorized repair vendor for bench evaluation.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Battery voltage and model
  • Any error codes or messages displayed
  • Charger and outlet tested
  • Accessories disconnected
  • Final device status before sending for repair

Final Thought

Patient safety and logical troubleshooting go hand-in-hand. Exhaust external and easily verified causes before assuming internal failure. Proper documentation ensures continuity of care and helps the service team quickly identify the issue.

That is successful troubleshooting.
