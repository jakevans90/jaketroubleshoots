3M Bair Hugger 775 Series

Error: FC-009 Open Thermostat or Medium-Wattage Heater Failure

Asset Type

Patient Warming System

Manufacturer

3M

Model

Bair Hugger 775 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Error FC-009 on the 3M Bair Hugger 775 Series Patient Warming System. FC-009 indicates an open thermostat or medium-wattage heater failure. This condition may prevent the unit from providing controlled heated airflow and may indicate a heater, thermostat, wiring, or control board fault.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the Bair Hugger 775 from patient use if Error FC-009 appears during setup or active warming.
  • Disconnect the warming blanket from the patient if warming therapy is affected and it is clinically safe to do so.
  • Use another approved warming device if patient warming is still needed.
  • Do not continue using the unit if it cannot provide controlled warming or if the error returns.
  • Do not troubleshoot heater operation while the unit is connected to an active patient.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Confirm the exact error shown is FC-009.
  • Power the unit on in a safe non-patient-care setting and observe whether the error appears at startup, during warm air operation, or after temperature selection.
  • Ask staff what happened when the error occurred.
  • Confirm whether the unit stopped heating, alarmed, failed to reach set temperature, or shut down.
  • Document whether the error is constant, intermittent, or only occurs during heating.

Check External and Simple Causes First

  • Confirm the power cord is secure and the outlet is working.
  • Inspect the hose for blockage, kinks, damage, contamination, or loose connection.
  • Verify the air intake and filter area are not blocked.
  • Check for signs of overheating, unusual smell, abnormal noise, visible damage, or liquid intrusion.
  • Run the unit only if it is safe to do so.

Verify Fan Operation in Ambient Mode

  • Power the unit on in a safe bench or test area.
  • Select Ambient mode.
  • Listen and feel for airflow from the hose outlet.
  • Confirm the fan runs normally without abnormal noise, vibration, or weak airflow.
  • If the fan does not run correctly, remove the unit from service and troubleshoot as a fan, power, or control issue before continuing heater-related checks.

Inspect for Airflow Restriction or Overheating Conditions

  • Check the filter condition.
  • Confirm the hose is fully attached and not obstructed.
  • Inspect the blanket connection area for restriction or improper setup.
  • Allow the unit to cool if it was recently running hot or shut down.
  • Confirm the airflow path is clear before assuming internal heater failure.

Check Heater/Thermostat Circuit Continuity at J4

  • Remove the device from patient care areas and perform service evaluation at the bench.
  • Access the control board area only as appropriate for Clinical Engineering service level.
  • Check continuity at the J4 connector on the control board.
  • If continuity is open, suspect an open thermostat or heater assembly circuit fault.
  • If continuity is present and the fault remains, continue evaluating for a control board or related internal control issue.

Determine Heater vs Control Board Fault

  • If continuity at J4 is open, replace the heater assembly according to approved service procedures.
  • If continuity is not open and Error FC-009 persists, suspect the control board.
  • Replace the control board if heater continuity is good but the FC-009 fault continues after appropriate testing.
  • Avoid unnecessary board replacement when the heater or thermostat circuit is actually open.

Calibrate and Test Before Return to Service

  • Perform operating temperature calibration after repair if required by service procedure.
  • Verify warming operation at available temperature settings.
  • Confirm Ambient mode still works properly.
  • Perform over-temperature detection testing if required after service.
  • Perform electrical safety testing if required by facility policy or post-repair procedure.
  • Run the unit long enough to confirm Error FC-009 does not return.

Stop When Resolved

  • Document the repair.
  • Confirm calibration and functional testing passed.
  • Clean the unit as needed.
  • Return the device to service only after the fault is cleared and testing is complete.

If the Problem Persists

If FC-009 remains after external checks, fan verification, airflow inspection, heater continuity checks, heater assembly replacement if open, and control board replacement if indicated, remove the unit from service.

At that point, common and external causes have been ruled out. A persistent FC-009 strongly indicates an internal heater circuit, thermostat, wiring, or control board failure that requires repair-level evaluation.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or further bench evaluation. Do not keep troubleshooting repeatedly in the clinical area.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot FC-009 on an active patient. Move the patient to a backup warming device first, then evaluate the Bair Hugger in a safe bench or non-patient-care setting. Do not rely on a patient warming unit with an unresolved heater or thermostat fault.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"3M Bair Hugger 775 reported with Error FC-009. Staff stated unit alarmed during warming setup and would not provide normal heated airflow."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified FC-009 fault. Fan operation checked in Ambient mode. External airflow path, hose, filter area, and power source inspected. J4 heater/thermostat circuit continuity found open, indicating heater/thermostat circuit failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed unit from service. Replaced heater assembly. Performed operating temperature calibration, functional warming test, and post-repair verification. Error FC-009 did not return. Unit passed testing and was returned to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

FC-009 should be treated as a heater and thermostat circuit fault until proven otherwise. Start with patient safety and simple external checks, then verify fan operation and isolate the heater circuit logically. If the fault points internal, remove the unit from service and document the findings clearly using CCR.

That is successful troubleshooting.
