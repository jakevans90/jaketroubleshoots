CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series

Error: Err 381 No tone feedback during POST

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

CONMED

Model

System 2450 / 5000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Err 381 on a CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series electrosurgical unit when the generator does not detect tone feedback during POST. This may indicate a speaker, volume control, cable, back panel, controller, or tone feedback circuit problem.

Because ESU tones provide important activation and alarm feedback, the unit should not be used clinically until the POST error is resolved and normal audible tone operation is confirmed.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the ESU from patient use if Err 381 appears during startup or if the unit does not complete POST normally.
  • Do not allow the generator to be used for a procedure while the error is present.
  • Notify clinical staff that the unit requires Clinical Engineering evaluation.
  • Provide a known working ESU if surgical support is needed.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on in a safe, non-patient-use setting.
  • Confirm that the displayed error is Err 381.
  • Listen for expected tones during POST.
  • Note whether tones are absent, faint, distorted, intermittent, or normal.
  • Confirm whether the error occurs every startup or only intermittently.

Check the Tone Volume Control

  • Check the back-panel tone volume control before assuming an internal failure.
  • Increase the volume setting.
  • Power cycle the ESU and listen again during POST.
  • If POST tones become audible and Err 381 clears, verify normal startup and complete required functional checks before returning the unit to service.

Check External Setup and Basic Conditions

  • Confirm the ESU is connected to a proper power source.
  • Inspect the unit for signs of impact damage, fluid intrusion, contamination, or loose external hardware.
  • Confirm the unit is not placed in a way that could block, cover, or muffle the tone output.
  • Power cycle the unit after the inspection.

Listen for Tone Output During Safe Activation Checks

  • Only perform activation checks outside patient use and according to facility policy.
  • Use appropriate ESU test equipment and do not connect the unit to a patient.
  • Listen for activation tones and alarm tones if safe testing is allowed.
  • Determine whether tone failure is completely absent, intermittent, faint, or limited to POST.

Check Accessible Cable Connections

  • Remove the device from service before opening any covers.
  • Follow facility policy and manufacturer service precautions.
  • Check accessible cable connections related to the back panel, speaker or tone circuit, and controller connections.
  • Look for loose, disconnected, pinched, damaged, or contaminated connectors.
  • Reseat only appropriate accessible connectors if allowed by your service process.

Escalate to Bench-Level Tone Circuit Evaluation

  • If volume, external condition, and accessible cable checks do not resolve the error, evaluate the tone drive and feedback path in an approved bench setup.
  • Configure the system for test mode if available and authorized.
  • Check whether the controller is sending the tone drive signal.
  • Check whether the TONE_MON feedback signal returns to the controller.
  • Evaluate whether the fault points toward the Controller assembly or Back Panel assembly.

Stop When Resolved

  • Confirm POST completes successfully.
  • Confirm tones are audible and consistent.
  • Perform required operational and safety checks.
  • Return the ESU to service only after it passes testing and Err 381 no longer appears.

If the Problem Persists

If Err 381 remains after checking the volume control, external conditions, tone behavior, and accessible cable connections, external and common causes have been ruled out.

Persistent Err 381 points toward an internal tone drive, tone feedback, back panel, controller, or related cabling fault.

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send it for repair or bench evaluation. Do not return an ESU to clinical use with unresolved POST errors or unreliable tone feedback.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. If Err 381 appears before or during clinical setup, move the patient or procedure support to a backup ESU first. Audible tones are part of safe ESU operation because they alert staff to activation and alarm conditions.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series ESU displayed Err 381 during POST. Staff reported no audible tone feedback during startup."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Confirmed Err 381 during startup. Back-panel tone volume checked and increased. External condition inspected. Accessible tone and back panel cabling checked. Error persisted, indicating likely internal tone drive, tone feedback, Back Panel assembly, or Controller assembly fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed ESU from service and labeled Out of Service for bench evaluation. Further troubleshooting needed in test mode to verify tone drive signal, TONE_MON feedback, Controller assembly, and Back Panel assembly."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Err 381 should be treated as more than a simple sound complaint. The ESU is reporting that expected tone feedback was not detected during POST. Start with patient safety, verify the error, check volume and connections first, then escalate when the fault points to the tone feedback circuit, Back Panel assembly, or Controller assembly. Clear documentation helps show that basic causes were ruled out before repair escalation.

That is successful troubleshooting.
