Stryker 1488 Camera System

White balance or color calibration failure

Asset Type

Endoscopic / OR Camera System

Manufacturer

Stryker

Model

1488 Camera System

What This Guide Helps With

Identifies causes of incorrect color, tint, or failed white balance affecting image accuracy during procedures.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Confirm the camera is not in use on an active patient. Move the procedure to a backup system before troubleshooting.

Verify Camera Has Proper Light Source

  • Ensure the light source is on, connected, and providing adequate brightness.
  • Low or inconsistent light will prevent proper white balance.
  • Expected: Bright, stable illumination

Check Scope and Coupler Cleanliness

  • Inspect the scope tip and coupler lens for debris, fluid, or fogging.
  • Contamination can distort color calibration. Clean using approved lens wipes if needed
  • Expected: Clear, unobstructed optics

Confirm Proper Scope and Coupler Connection

  • Ensure the scope is fully seated and locked into the coupler.
  • A loose connection can affect image signal and color processing.
  • Expected: Secure mechanical connection with stable image

Perform Manual White Balance

  • Use a proper white reference (sterile white gauze or approved card).
  • Fill the entire screen with the white target
  • Press the white balance button on the camera head
  • Expected: Image briefly adjusts and returns with natural color tones
  • If successful, stop here.

Check White Balance Conditions

  • Ensure:
    • No shadows or glare on the white surface
    • Camera is in focus
    • No colored objects in frame
  • Improper conditions will cause failed calibration.

Verify Camera Settings

  • Check for unusual image settings (gain, brightness, profiles).
  • Reset to default if needed.
  • Incorrect settings can skew color processing.
  • Expected: Neutral image settings

Swap Camera Head (If Available)

  • Connect a known-good camera head to the console.
  • If issue resolves → original camera head likely faulty
  • If issue persists → continue troubleshooting

Swap Coupler and Scope

  • Try a different coupler and scope to isolate the issue.
  • Optical components are common contributors to color distortion.
  • Expected: Normal color returns with known-good accessories

Inspect for Physical Damage

  • Check camera head cable, connectors, and lenses for damage.
  • Damage can affect signal quality and calibration ability.

If the Problem Persists

If all external components and conditions have been verified, the issue is likely internal (camera head sensor or processing circuitry).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Knowing when to stop prevents unnecessary risk and saves time.

Clinical Use Tip

Always perform white balance before the procedure begins. Never attempt calibration while the camera is in use on a patient.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Staff reported camera image had a yellow tint and would not white balance."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Contamination noted on scope tip affecting color calibration."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Cleaned scope and performed successful white balance; image returned to normal."

Helpful Details to Include

  • Light source output verified
  • White balance attempt behavior (success/failure)
  • Accessories swapped (scope, coupler, camera head)
  • Image appearance (tint, brightness, consistency)
  • Lens condition (clean/dirty/fogged)
  • Final device status

Final Thought

White balance issues are most often caused by lighting, optics, or user setup rather than internal failure. Start simple, verify conditions, and isolate components methodically. Proper escalation and clear documentation support both patient safety and efficient repairs.

That is successful troubleshooting.
