GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series

Error: AD CHANNEL %s FAIL Startup Failure

Asset Type

C-Arm

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

OEC 9900 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot AD CHANNEL %s FAIL – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS on the GE Healthcare OEC 9900 Series C-Arm. This startup error indicates that an analog-to-digital channel failed during system initialization. The actual failed channel may appear in place of %s. Because this fault can cause total system shutdown, the C-Arm should not be relied on for patient imaging until the issue is corrected.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot the C-Arm while it is positioned for active patient imaging.
  • Remove the OEC 9900 from patient use if the system shuts down, will not complete startup, or displays the AD CHANNEL failure.
  • Use another C-Arm or approved imaging method if imaging is needed for a procedure.
  • Do not continue repeated restarts during clinical use.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the system on and observe the startup sequence.
  • Confirm whether the display shows AD CHANNEL %s FAIL – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS.
  • Record the actual channel number or identifier shown in place of %s.
  • Note whether the system shuts down immediately or becomes unusable after the message appears.

Power Off and Wait 5 Seconds

  • Turn the C-Arm system power off.
  • Wait at least 5 seconds.
  • Power the system back on and observe whether the error returns.
  • If the system starts normally, verify basic operation before returning the device to service.

Check AC Power and Power Stability

  • Confirm the workstation is connected to a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Check for loose plugs, damaged power cords, tripped breakers, or poor outlet contact.
  • If appropriate, test the system from a verified outlet.
  • Look for startup behavior that suggests unstable power or inconsistent booting.

Check the C-Arm and Workstation Interconnect

  • Inspect the workstation-to-C-Arm interconnect cable.
  • Look for loose connectors, bent pins, damaged cable jackets, strain damage, contamination, or incomplete seating.
  • Reseat the cable if appropriate.
  • Restart the system and confirm whether the AD CHANNEL failure clears or returns.

Inspect for Obvious External Damage or Contamination

  • Inspect the C-Arm, control panels, cable ports, generator area covers, and workstation exterior.
  • Look for fluid intrusion, impact damage, damaged connectors, pinched cables, burning smell, unusual heat, or signs of recent cleaning fluid exposure.
  • If damage or contamination is found, remove the device from service for evaluation.

Identify Whether the Failure Is Repeatable

  • After confirming safe conditions, power cycle the system only as needed to verify the failure pattern.
  • Record whether the same channel fails every time.
  • Record whether the error appears intermittently.
  • Record whether the failed channel number changes.
  • Record whether the system shuts down before normal operation.

Isolate Simple External Contributors

  • Confirm no accessories, controls, cables, or external components are damaged or incorrectly connected.
  • Ask whether the issue began after cleaning, movement, storage, transport, or cable reconnection.
  • Focus inspection on areas recently moved, cleaned, impacted, or reconnected.
  • Rule out simple and visible causes before escalating to repair.

Determine Whether the Issue Is Device-Related

  • If the error returns after power cycling and external checks, treat the issue as a device-level fault.
  • Do not continue repeated restarts for clinical use.
  • Document the exact displayed channel and shutdown behavior.
  • Remove the C-Arm from service for bench evaluation.

If the Problem Persists

If the AD CHANNEL %s FAIL – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS error returns after proper power cycling, power verification, interconnect inspection, and external damage checks, common external causes have been ruled out.

A persistent A/D channel failure may involve the failed analog input channel, analog-to-digital conversion path, control circuitry, wiring, or related internal electronics.

The device should be removed from service, labeled Out of Service, and sent for repair or detailed bench evaluation.

Do not return the C-Arm to clinical use until the startup failure is corrected and normal operation is verified.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot on an active patient. A C-Arm that shuts down during startup is not available for imaging and should not be relied on for patient care. Move the patient imaging need to a backup C-Arm or approved alternate imaging method before continuing troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"GE OEC 9900 Series C-Arm displays AD CHANNEL %s FAIL – POWER OFF. WAIT 5 SECONDS during startup. System shuts down and cannot be used for imaging."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Startup self-test detected an analog-to-digital channel failure. Power cycle and external connection checks did not clear the error. Likely internal analog input, A/D channel, wiring, or related control circuitry fault."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Powered system off for more than 5 seconds and restarted. Verified error returned during startup. Checked AC power, interconnect cable, external connectors, and visible device condition. Removed C-Arm from service and sent for bench evaluation/repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

The AD CHANNEL %s FAIL message should be treated as a startup self-test failure that prevents safe clinical use. Start with patient safety, confirm the exact channel and shutdown behavior, check power and external connections, then escalate when the error remains. Clear documentation of the failed channel and troubleshooting steps helps repair teams isolate the fault faster.

That is successful troubleshooting.
