Roche cobas h 232

Display or Keypad Errors

Asset Type

Blood Gas Analyzer

Manufacturer

Roche

Model

cobas h 232

What This Guide Helps With

Unresponsive keypad, incorrect display, or screen issues caused by power instability, contamination, or external damage.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Do not troubleshoot while the analyzer is needed for active patient testing
  • Move testing to another analyzer
  • Expected: No interruption to patient care
  • Why it matters: Delayed cardiac marker results can impact urgent clinical decisions

2. Verify the Reported Issue

  • Power on the device and attempt normal operation
  • Observe for blank or dim display
  • Check for frozen or distorted screen
  • Assess keypad responsiveness or delayed input
  • Expected: Issue is reproducible
  • Why it matters: Confirms a true hardware/interface issue vs user workflow confusion

3. Check Battery Status (Most Common Cause)

  • Check battery level indicator
  • Replace with a fully charged or known-good battery
  • Power cycle the device
  • Expected: Device powers on with normal display and keypad response
  • Why it matters: Insufficient voltage can affect screen and input responsiveness
  • If resolved, remove faulty battery from service

4. Inspect for Contamination or Physical Obstruction

  • Inspect keypad surface for dried fluids or debris
  • Clean using approved disinfectant wipes (per facility policy)
  • Ensure no liquid has entered seams or edges
  • Expected: Keypad responsiveness improves
  • Why it matters: Contamination is a common cause of input failure in clinical environments

5. Check for Physical Damage

  • Inspect screen for cracks, discoloration, or dead areas
  • Check for loose housing or misaligned buttons
  • Expected: No visible damage
  • Why it matters: Physical damage often indicates internal failure requiring repair

6. Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Power off the device completely
  • Remove the battery (if removable)
  • Wait 30 seconds, reinstall battery, and power on
  • Expected: Device boots normally with responsive interface
  • Why it matters: Resets minor software or communication faults

7. Verify Operating Environment

  • Ensure device is within recommended temperature range
  • Avoid direct bright light or glare on the screen
  • Expected: Normal display visibility and operation
  • Why it matters: Environmental factors can mimic display issues

If the Problem Persists

If all external causes have been ruled out, the issue is likely internal (display module or keypad circuitry failure).

Remove the device from service, label it Out of Service, and send for repair or bench evaluation.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot display or keypad issues while a patient sample is pending. Always transition testing to a functioning analyzer first.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Analyzer display frozen and keypad not responding during use."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Failed display/keypad interface after ruling out battery and contamination."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed from service and sent to vendor for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Battery level verified or battery swapped
  • Display condition (blank, dim, distorted)
  • Keypad responsiveness
  • Evidence of contamination or fluid exposure
  • Physical damage observed
  • Power cycle performed
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Display and keypad issues are often caused by simple external factors like battery condition or contamination. Always start with those checks before escalating. When those are ruled out, prompt removal from service protects both patient safety and workflow efficiency. Proper documentation ensures clarity and continuity of care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
