Welch Allyn Connex 6000 Series

Error: #000000003 Graphics RAM POST

Asset Type

Vital Signs Monitor

Manufacturer

Welch Allyn

Model

Connex 6000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

Error #000000003 indicating Graphics RAM POST failure causing boot issues, display malfunction, or inability to initialize the monitor.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the monitor from patient use before troubleshooting.
  • Expected: Patient is safely transferred to another monitoring device.
  • Why it matters: Display failure prevents accurate vital sign monitoring.

Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power on the unit and observe startup. Confirm the exact error code (#000000003) appears.
  • Note whether the screen is blank, distorted, frozen, or fails during boot.
  • Expected: Error is consistently displayed during startup or POST.
  • Why it matters: Confirms a hardware-level self-test failure rather than a transient glitch.

Perform a Full Power Cycle

  • Shut down the device, disconnect AC power, and remove the battery (if accessible).
  • Wait 30–60 seconds, then reconnect power and restart.
  • Expected: Device either boots normally or reproduces the same error.
  • Why it matters: Clears temporary faults or corrupted initialization states.
  • If resolved → Return device to service

Check External Display Conditions

  • Inspect the screen for physical damage (cracks, pressure marks).
  • Check for signs of liquid intrusion.
  • Observe for abnormal brightness or flickering.
  • Expected: Screen appears physically intact with normal backlight behavior.
  • Why it matters: Physical damage can mimic or contribute to graphics-related failures.

Verify Power Stability

  • Plug the unit into a known working outlet and ensure the power adapter is secure.
  • Observe for consistent power-up behavior without flickering or resets.
  • Expected: Stable power with consistent startup attempt.
  • Why it matters: Unstable power can cause POST failures, including graphics initialization errors.

Disconnect External Accessories

  • Remove all non-essential accessories (USB devices, network cables, modules or attachments).
  • Restart the device.
  • Expected: No change, or device boots normally if an accessory was interfering.
  • Why it matters: External devices can occasionally interfere with boot processes.
  • If resolved → Identify faulty accessory and remove from service

Observe for Heat, Noise, or Additional Fault Indicators

  • During startup attempts, check for excessive heat.
  • Listen for unusual sounds.
  • Observe for repeated reboot cycles.
  • Expected: Normal startup attempt with consistent failure point.
  • Why it matters: Additional symptoms help confirm internal hardware failure.

If the Problem Persists

If the error continues after external checks and power reset:

  • External causes have been ruled out
  • Graphics RAM POST errors indicate failure of internal video/memory hardware

Action:

  • Remove device from service
  • Label as Out of Service
  • Send for repair or bench evaluation

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting.

Clinical Use Tip

Do not rely on a monitor with display or boot errors. Always transfer patient monitoring before troubleshooting or attempting restart.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"Unit displays Error #000000003 (Graphics RAM POST) during startup. Monitor does not boot and display is non-functional."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Likely internal graphics memory or system board failure indicated by POST error. External power and accessories ruled out."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Performed power cycle, verified power source, inspected display, and removed accessories. Error persisted. Unit removed from service and sent for repair."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • When the error appears (startup vs intermittent)
  • Screen condition (blank, distorted, flickering)
  • Power behavior during startup
  • Accessories connected or removed during testing
  • Any unusual sounds or heat observed
  • Indicator lights or boot sequence behavior
  • Final device status (removed from service, sent for repair)

Final Thought

POST errors like Graphics RAM failures are highly reliable indicators of internal hardware issues. Proper troubleshooting means confirming the error, ruling out external factors, and escalating without unnecessary disassembly to maintain patient safety and equipment integrity.

That is successful troubleshooting.
