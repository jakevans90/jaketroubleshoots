CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series

Error: Err 350 Monitor Output Channel Current Sensor Failure

Asset Type

Electrosurgical Unit (ESU)

Manufacturer

CONMED

Model

System 2450 / 5000 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide helps troubleshoot Err 350 on the CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series ESU. Err 350 indicates a monitor output channel current sensor failure, which may affect the generator’s ability to accurately monitor RF output current during activation.

Because this fault involves output current sensing, it should be treated as a serious ESU monitoring issue. If the unit cannot reliably monitor output current, delivered energy verification and internal safety monitoring may not be dependable.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Ensure Patient Safety First

  • Remove the ESU from patient use if Err 350 appears during setup, activation, or procedure use.
  • Notify clinical staff that the generator has reported an internal RF output monitoring fault.
  • Do not allow the unit to be used for cutting, coagulation, bipolar, or specialty output modes until evaluated.
  • Replace it with a known-good ESU if the case is active.
  • Label the unit appropriately if the error cannot be cleared safely.

Expected outcome: Patient care continues without relying on an ESU that may not accurately monitor output current.

Why it matters: Err 350 involves current sensing. If the device cannot correctly monitor RF output current, output verification and safety monitoring may be unreliable.

2. Verify the Reported Error and Behavior

  • Power the unit on in a safe, non-patient-care setting.
  • Confirm the exact displayed error is Err 350.
  • Determine whether the error occurs during startup, after power cycling, during activation, only under load, or intermittently.
  • Ask staff what they observed, including whether the unit failed to activate, alarmed during use, or displayed abnormal output behavior.

Expected outcome: Err 350 is reproducible or clearly documented.

Why it matters: This confirms a true system-reported current sensor fault instead of a mistaken complaint, accessory issue, or temporary startup condition.

3. Check Power Source and Basic External Conditions

  • Plug the ESU into a known-good hospital-grade outlet.
  • Avoid power strips or questionable outlets during testing.
  • Inspect the power cord, plug, strain relief, and ground pin.
  • Confirm the unit powers on normally aside from the reported error.

Expected outcome: The unit receives stable AC power and does not show signs of power-related instability.

Why it matters: Poor power or damaged cords can cause unusual startup or self-test behavior, even when the final error points deeper into the system.

4. Inspect External Connections and Accessories

  • Remove all patient cables, pencils, bipolar cords, return electrode cables, adapters, and accessories.
  • Inspect the output receptacles for bent contacts, contamination, damage, fluid intrusion, or debris.
  • Reconnect only known-good test accessories as needed for bench testing.
  • Do not test using questionable clinical accessories.

Expected outcome: No external accessory, cable, or connector damage is causing abnormal output sensing behavior.

Why it matters: ESU output faults should be isolated with known-good loads and accessories before assuming internal board failure.

5. Confirm the Test Setup Is Correct

  • Verify that the ESU analyzer or fixed load is appropriate for the output mode being tested.
  • Confirm the load is connected to the correct active and return or bipolar output path.
  • Confirm the selected power setting and output mode match the intended test.
  • Do not activate into an open circuit unless the approved test procedure specifically calls for it.

Expected outcome: The test setup is correct and not creating misleading output current readings.

Why it matters: Err 350 relates to current sensing. Incorrect load setup can make troubleshooting appear worse than it is.

6. Configure the System for Test Mode and Cycle Power

  • Configure the ESU for the appropriate service or test mode according to approved facility or service procedures.
  • Cycle power after entering the required test configuration.
  • Observe whether Err 350 clears, returns immediately, or returns during activation.

Expected outcome: The unit either passes startup in test mode or repeats Err 350.

Why it matters: If Err 350 repeats in test mode, the issue is less likely to be user setup and more likely related to the RF output current sensing path.

7. Test Output Into a Fixed Load

  • Connect the ESU to an appropriate ESU analyzer or fixed load.
  • Select a controlled output mode and constant power setting.
  • Activate the unit briefly while observing output behavior.
  • Compare displayed or measured output current against expected analyzer behavior.
  • Watch for failure to activate, abnormal output, unstable readings, or repeated Err 350.

Expected outcome: Output current should be stable and consistent with the selected mode, power setting, and load.

Why it matters: A fixed load helps isolate whether the current sensing failure is real, repeatable, and related to the output channel path.

8. Compare Output Channel Current With Output Current

  • Use approved service diagnostic tools, such as DACview if available and authorized.
  • Compare the output channel current sensor reading with the actual output current while activating at constant power into a fixed load.
  • Look for missing, frozen, incorrect, unstable, or mismatched current sensor values.

Expected outcome: The monitored output channel current should track reasonably with actual measured output current.

Why it matters: Err 350 specifically points to a monitor output channel current sensor failure. A mismatch supports a sensing circuit, RF output path, or controller-related failure.

9. Isolate Accessory, Setup, or Device Failure

  • If the error clears with known-good accessories and correct load setup, document the faulty accessory or setup condition.
  • If the error only occurs with one cable, handpiece, adapter, or output port configuration, remove that accessory from use.
  • If the error repeats with known-good loads and controlled test conditions, treat the fault as device-related.
  • Inspect for obvious external signs of damage, overheating, fluid intrusion, or contamination.

Expected outcome: The issue is narrowed to either an external setup/accessory problem or an internal ESU failure.

Why it matters: Clinical Engineering should rule out simple causes first, but repeated Err 350 under controlled bench conditions strongly indicates an internal sensing/output fault.

If the Problem Persists

If Err 350 returns after power cycling, test mode configuration, known-good accessories, correct load setup, and fixed-load testing, external and common causes have been ruled out.

A persistent Err 350 strongly indicates an internal failure in the RF output current monitoring path. Likely repair areas may include the RF Amplifier, RF Transformer board, RF Output board, Controller assembly, associated current sensing circuitry, or internal connections.

  • Remove the device from service.
  • Label the device Out of Service.
  • Send the unit for repair, bench evaluation, or vendor service.

Knowing when to stop is proper troubleshooting. Do not continue repeated activation testing on an ESU with unresolved output current sensing faults.

Clinical Use Tip

Never troubleshoot an ESU with Err 350 while it is actively being used on a patient. Move the patient or procedure to a known-good backup generator first if electrosurgery is still needed.

An unresolved RF output monitoring fault can affect confidence in delivered energy and safety monitoring.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

What was reported by the clinical staff.

Example:
"CONMED System 2450 / 5000 Series ESU reported Err 350. Staff stated unit alarmed or failed during use or setup. Error indicates monitor output channel current sensor failure."

Cause

What was observed during troubleshooting.

Example:
"Verified Err 350 during evaluation. External power, accessories, output connections, and test load setup checked. Error persisted under controlled test conditions, indicating likely internal RF output current sensing or output board pathway failure."

Resolution

What action was taken.

Example:
"Removed ESU from service and labeled Out of Service. Unit requires bench repair or vendor evaluation of RF Amplifier, RF Transformer board, RF Output board, Controller assembly, and current sensing circuitry before return to service."

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Alarm behavior
  • Accessories swapped
  • Power behavior
  • Environmental factors
  • Indicator lights
  • Final device status

Final Thought

Err 350 should be treated as a serious ESU output monitoring fault. The correct approach is to protect the patient first, confirm the exact error, rule out simple external causes, test under controlled load conditions, and escalate when the current sensing path cannot be trusted. Clear CCR documentation helps show what was checked, what was ruled out, and why the device was removed from service or repaired.

That is successful troubleshooting.
