Preventive Maintenance Procedure

Manual Reference

3M Bair Hugger Model 775 Temperature Management Unit Service Manual

Manufacturer Recommendation / AEM Status

The manufacturer-listed preventive maintenance and service activities for the 3M Bair Hugger Model 775 include:

  • Filter inspection
  • Operating temperature calibration
  • Over-temperature detection system test
  • Air filter replacement
  • Cleaning as needed
  • Hose replacement as needed
  • Safety inspection after repair

Manufacturer-listed frequencies and parts:

  • Operating temperature calibration and over-temperature detection system test: every 6 months or 500 hours of use, or whenever service is performed.
  • Air filter replacement: every 12 months or 500 hours of use.
  • Air filter part number: PN 90047.
  • Hose replacement: as needed.
  • Cleaning: as needed.
  • Post-repair safety inspection: before return to service.

The service manual lists post-repair safety inspection as including operating temperature calibration, over-temperature detection testing, leakage current testing, and safety ground continuity check before return to service.

AEM note: These are manufacturer-listed service activities and intervals. If a facility performs this PM at a different interval or changes the listed activities, that should be handled under the facility’s approved AEM program, risk assessment, maintenance history, and applicable regulatory requirements.

What This PM Procedure Covers

  • Visual inspection
  • Air filter inspection or replacement
  • Power-on self-test verification
  • Operating mode verification
  • Operating temperature calibration
  • Over-temperature detection system test
  • Electrical safety testing documentation
  • Final return-to-service decision

Required Tools and Equipment

  • 3M Patient Warming Model 22110 Temperature Test Unit
  • Calibrated thermometer compatible with K-style thermocouple input
  • Replacement air filter, PN 90047, if due or needed
  • #2 Phillips screwdriver
  • Electrical safety analyzer
  • Ground continuity / protective earth resistance test capability
  • Soft cloths
  • Mild detergent or antimicrobial spray, if cleaning is performed

The manual specifically lists the Model 22110 Temperature Test Unit for temperature testing and calibration. The air filter replacement procedure lists a replacement air filter and #2 Phillips screwdriver.

Step-by-Step PM Procedure

1. Verify Device Status and PM Basis

Action: Confirm the warming unit is available for PM and not in active clinical use. Review the last PM date, hour meter if used by the facility, recent repair history, and facility PM schedule.

Expected outcome: PM basis is clear: manufacturer-listed interval, operating hours, post-repair inspection, or facility-approved AEM interval.

2. Perform Visual Inspection

Action: Inspect the housing, power cord, plug, ground pin, hose, hose connection, filter cover, control panel, display, buttons, labels, mounting clamp if used, and overall cleanliness.

Expected outcome: No unsafe damage, missing hardware, loose parts, cracked hose, damaged power cord, contaminated surfaces, or obvious defect is present.

Why it matters: The manual warns not to use the warming unit when the warming unit, power cord, or any component appears damaged.

3. Inspect or Replace the Air Filter

Action: Disconnect the unit from power. Turn the unit upside down. Remove the two screws from the louvered filter cover. Remove the filter cover. Remove and inspect the air filter.

Replace the filter if it is due, damaged, contaminated, restricted, missing, or required by facility policy.

Install the replacement filter with the gasket toward the filter ledge. Reinstall the filter cover with the louvers facing the back of the unit. Reinstall the two screws.

Expected outcome: Filter is inspected and replaced if required.

Important note: Do not clean the air filter. The manual warns that the filter may contain hazardous microorganisms and should be discarded according to institutional biohazard protocol.

4. Verify Power-On Self-Test

Action: Connect the unit to a grounded power source and observe startup.

Confirm:

  • Indicator lights illuminate momentarily.
  • Display pixels illuminate momentarily.
  • Alarm sounds three low-level clicks.
  • Unit enters Standby mode.
  • High fan setting is selected by default.

Expected outcome: Unit completes power-on-reset and enters Standby without a fault. The manual describes this startup sequence for the Model 775.

5. Verify Operating Modes

Action: Verify the unit responds to:

  • Ambient mode
  • 32°C mode
  • 38°C mode
  • 43°C mode
  • Standby mode
  • High fan setting
  • Low fan setting, if checked by facility procedure

Expected outcome: Controls respond normally. Blower operates in operating modes. Heater activates in temperature modes. Standby turns blower and heater off.

Manufacturer-listed delivered air temperature ranges at the blanket end of the hose:

  • 32°C mode: 32°C ± 1.5°C
  • 38°C mode: 38°C ± 1.5°C
  • 43°C mode: 43°C ± 1.5°C

6. Calibrate Operating Temperature

Action: Attach the Model 22110 Temperature Test Unit to the end of the hose. Connect the warming unit to a grounded power source and allow it to enter Standby.

Select Ambient mode and allow the unit to run for at least 10 minutes. Verify the unit is set to high fan.

Enter Calibration mode by pressing the Alternative Mode button and Ambient button together for approximately 3 seconds.

Compare the warming unit display temperature to the temperature test unit reading.

If adjustment is needed:

  • Press 32°C to decrease displayed temperature by 0.1°C.
  • Press 38°C to increase displayed temperature by 0.1°C.
  • Adjust until the display matches the test unit as closely as possible.
  • Press the Alternative Mode button to save the calibration offset.

Expected outcome: Operating temperature display is calibrated to the test unit.

Remove from service condition: If an offset greater than ±3.0°C is required, the manual indicates there may be a hose-end sensor defect. Remove the unit from service until a new hose assembly can be substituted.

7. Test Over-Temperature Detection System

Safety warning: Do not perform the over-temperature detection system test while the unit is being used for patient temperature management therapy.

Action: Connect the Model 22110 Temperature Test Unit to the hose. Run the unit in Ambient mode for 5 minutes. Verify high fan setting.

Enter Over-Temperature Detection Test mode by pressing the Alternative Mode button and 43°C button together for approximately 3 seconds.

Allow the test to run.

Record:

  • PASS / FAIL result
  • Test time
  • Maximum temperature displayed by the test kit

After the test, allow the unit to cool by running it for at least 5 minutes. Unplug the unit to reset it to normal operation.

Expected outcome: Unit detects over-temperature within 150 seconds and displays PASS.

Pass criteria:

  • PASS shown on display
  • Over-temperature detected within 150 seconds
  • Maximum temperature recorded is ≤ 56°C

Fail criteria:

  • FAIL shown on display
  • Unit does not detect over-temperature within 150 seconds
  • Maximum temperature exceeds 56°C

The manual states not to place the unit back into service if it fails the over-temperature detection system test and to contact 3M Patient Warming technical service if the temperature is above 56°C.

8. Clean Unit if Needed

Action: Disconnect the unit from power before cleaning. Wipe the cabinet and outside of the hose with a soft cloth lightly dampened with water, mild detergent, or antimicrobial spray. Dry with a separate soft cloth.

Do not immerse the cabinet or hose. Do not use a dripping wet cloth. Do not use alcohol or other solvents on the cabinet.

Expected outcome: Unit is clean, dry, intact, and ready for final review.

Pass / Fail Criteria

PM Pass

Pass the PM if:

  • Visual inspection is acceptable.
  • Air filter is inspected/replaced as required.
  • Power-on self-test completes normally.
  • Operating controls respond correctly.
  • Operating temperature calibration is completed.
  • Calibration offset does not exceed the manufacturer-listed ±3.0°C adjustment limit.
  • Over-temperature detection system test passes.
  • Maximum temperature during over-temperature test is ≤ 56°C.
  • Electrical safety testing passes per facility policy and applicable criteria.
  • Unit is clean, dry, complete, and safe for clinical use.

Remove From Service

Remove from service if:

  • Unit has unsafe physical damage.
  • Power cord, plug, ground pin, hose, housing, or control panel is damaged.
  • Unit fails startup or has recurring fault codes.
  • Calibration requires an offset greater than ±3.0°C.
  • Unit fails the over-temperature detection system test.
  • Maximum temperature exceeds 56°C during over-temperature detection testing.
  • Unit fails electrical safety testing.
  • Unit cannot be verified safe for clinical use.
