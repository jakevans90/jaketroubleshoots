Kendall SCD 700 Series

Display / Control Panel Malfunction

Asset Type

Sequential Compression Device (SCD)

Manufacturer

Cardinal Health

Model

Kendall SCD 700 Series

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through simple checks when a Kendall SCD 700 Series unit has a display or control panel issue.

This may present as:

  • Blank screen but unit plugged in
  • Screen lights up but no text visible
  • Buttons not responding
  • Display frozen
  • Segments missing or distorted
  • Unit running but controls unresponsive

These steps focus on the most common and easily verified causes before assuming internal board or display failure.

You do not need advanced technical skills — just follow the steps and observe carefully.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Confirm the Unit Has Power

  • Ensure the unit is plugged into a live outlet.
  • Verify the power switch is ON.
  • Listen for internal sounds (fan or pump initialization).

If there are no sounds and no lights, refer to the “Will Not Turn On” troubleshooting guide.

A display issue may actually be a power issue.

2. Adjust Screen Visibility (If Applicable)

  • Check for brightness settings if adjustable.
  • Look closely for faint text that may indicate a backlight issue.
  • Shine a flashlight at an angle to see if text is present but dim.

If text is visible with a flashlight but not normally, suspect backlight failure.

3. Check for a Frozen Interface

  • Attempt to press multiple buttons.
  • Observe whether any LEDs respond.
  • Check whether sleeves are still inflating despite the frozen display.

If therapy continues but controls do not respond, the issue may be logic/software related.

4. Power Cycle the Unit

  • Turn the unit OFF.
  • Wait at least 10 seconds.
  • Turn it back ON and allow full startup.

Temporary logic faults can sometimes cause the display or keypad to freeze.

5. Inspect for Physical Damage

Check for:

  • Cracks in the display window
  • Liquid intrusion
  • Warping or pressure marks
  • Sticky or physically stuck buttons

Gently press each button to check for tactile response.

Physical damage can cause unresponsive or intermittent control issues.

6. Observe Startup Behavior

When powering on, note:

  • Does the screen briefly flash and then go blank?
  • Are partial segments visible?
  • Does the unit beep normally at startup?

These observations help determine whether the issue is:

  • Backlight failure
  • Display panel failure
  • Control board malfunction

7. Check for Intermittent Operation

  • Gently move the unit or lightly tap near the display area.
  • Observe whether the screen flickers or responds.

Intermittent flickering may indicate a loose internal ribbon cable or board connection.

(Do not open the unit unless authorized.)

If the Display or Controls Remain Malfunctioning

If the issue persists after completing the steps above, you have already:

  • Verified power source
  • Ruled out simple brightness issues
  • Attempted power cycling
  • Checked for physical damage
  • Observed startup behavior
  • Confirmed whether therapy continues

At this point, the issue is likely:

  • Display panel failure
  • Backlight failure
  • Keypad membrane failure
  • Internal control board malfunction
  • Ribbon cable connection issue

These cannot be corrected through bedside troubleshooting.

Next Steps

  • Remove the unit from clinical service
  • Clearly label it as Out of Service
  • Send for bench evaluation by Clinical Engineering

Knowing when to escalate is part of good troubleshooting.

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

Examples:

“Display blank; unit powers on but no visible text.”

“Control buttons unresponsive; sleeves inflate but panel frozen.”

Cause

Examples:

“Power verified, unit power cycled, no visible backlight or response from control panel. Suspected display/backlight failure.”

“Controls non-responsive despite therapy running; suspected internal control board fault.”

Resolution

Examples:

“Removed device from service; sent for bench evaluation.”

“Tagged Out of Service for display/control board repair.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Power verified (yes/no)
  • Any startup beep present (yes/no)
  • Screen completely blank or faint image visible
  • Buttons responsive (yes/no)
  • Therapy running despite display issue (yes/no)
  • Visible damage present (yes/no)
  • Final disposition

Final Thought

Most display/control issues are caused by:

  • ✔ power interruption
  • ✔ temporary software freeze
  • ✔ backlight failure
  • ✔ physical button damage

True control board failures are less common but possible.

Completing these steps ensures you have:

  • ✔ ruled out simple power and freeze issues
  • ✔ protected patient safety
  • ✔ documented properly
  • ✔ avoided unnecessary assumptions

That is successful troubleshooting.
