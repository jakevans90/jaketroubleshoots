CADD-Solis Ambulatory Infusion Pump

Cassette Not Recognized / “Cassette Error”

Asset Type

Infusion Pump

Manufacturer

ICU Medical

Model

CADD-Solis

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through simple checks when a CADD-Solis pump displays:

  • “Cassette Error”
  • “Cassette Not Recognized”
  • Pump will not start infusion after cassette installation
  • Audible alarm immediately after attaching cassette

These steps focus on the most common and easily verified causes before assuming a failed cassette, damaged pump latch mechanism, or internal sensor issue.

You do not need advanced technical skills — just follow the steps carefully and observe what happens.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Remove and Re-seat the Cassette

  • Stop the pump if running.
  • Remove the cassette completely.
  • Inspect the cassette connection area.
  • Reinstall the cassette firmly until it clicks/locks into place.

Make sure the cassette is:

  • Properly aligned
  • Fully seated
  • Locked securely

A partially engaged cassette is the most common cause of recognition errors.

2. Inspect the Cassette for Damage

Examine:

  • The cassette housing
  • The tubing channel
  • The cassette keying features
  • Any cracked plastic or warped edges

Look specifically for:

  • Bent or broken alignment tabs
  • Cracked pressure plate area
  • Signs of fluid contamination

If the cassette is damaged, replace it with a new one. Even minor warping can prevent proper recognition.

3. Confirm Cassette Compatibility

  • Verify the cassette type matches the programmed therapy.
  • Confirm cassette volume (50 mL / 100 mL / etc.) matches pump configuration.
  • Ensure it is an approved CADD-Solis compatible cassette.

Using the wrong cassette type can trigger recognition or validation errors. If unsure, compare with a known-good cassette.

4. Inspect the Pump Cassette Latch Area

  • Check for debris or dried medication residue.
  • Inspect the latch mechanism.
  • Ensure no plastic fragments are present.

Gently clean the area if contamination is present (per facility cleaning policy). Obstructions can prevent proper sensor engagement.

5. Power Cycle the Pump

  • Turn the pump off.
  • Wait at least 10 seconds.
  • Turn it back on.
  • Reinstall the cassette.

Temporary logic or sensor errors may clear after a reboot.

6. Test with a Known-Good Cassette

  • Install a new, unused cassette if available.
  • Attempt to start infusion.

If the error clears with a new cassette, the cause is likely a defective or warped cassette.

If the error persists with multiple cassettes, suspect internal pump issue (sensor, latch switch, pressure plate sensor, or PCB fault).

7. Observe the Exact Error Behavior

Document:

  • Does the error occur immediately on installation?
  • Does it occur only when starting infusion?
  • Is there an additional pressure or occlusion message?
  • Does the latch feel loose or not fully engaging?

If the Cassette Error Persists

If the pump continues to display “Cassette Error” after completing the steps above, you have already ruled out the most common causes.

You have already:

  • reseated the cassette
  • inspected for visible cassette damage
  • confirmed correct cassette type
  • cleaned the cassette interface area
  • power cycled the pump
  • tested with a known-good cassette

The issue is likely internal:

  • Cassette detection sensor failure
  • Latch microswitch malfunction
  • Pressure plate sensing issue
  • Main PCB fault

The best next step is to:

  • remove the pump from clinical service
  • clearly label it as Out of Service
  • send it for repair or bench evaluation

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

Complaint

Example: “Pump displaying ‘Cassette Error’ — cassette not recognized.”

Cause

Example: “Cassette reseated and replaced with known-good cassette. Error persists. Suspected internal cassette detection sensor or latch failure.”

Resolution

Examples:
“Removed pump from service, labeled out of service, sent for repair.”
“Tagged for CE bench evaluation.”
“Replaced defective cassette; pump tested and returned to service.”

Helpful Details to Include (If Known)

  • Cassette reseated (yes / no)
  • New cassette tested (yes / no)
  • Visible cassette damage
  • Latch engagement normal (yes / no)
  • Error immediate or during start
  • Final disposition (returned to service or sent for repair)

Final Thought

Most “Cassette Error” messages are caused by improper seating, warped or damaged cassettes, or debris in the latch area.

Completing these steps ensures you have ruled out simple supply or user causes, protected patient therapy continuity, and documented properly.

That is successful troubleshooting.
