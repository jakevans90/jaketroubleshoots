GE Healthcare MAC VU360

ECG Issue – Leads Not Detected / Noisy ECG

Asset Type

Electrocardiograph (EKG) Machine

Manufacturer

GE Healthcare

Model

MAC VU360

What This Guide Helps With

This guide walks you through common checks when a MAC VU360 displays:

  • “Lead Off” messages
  • Multiple leads not detected
  • Excessive artifact or noisy ECG waveform
  • Intermittent tracing loss

These steps focus on the most common and easily corrected causes before assuming cable or hardware failure.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

1. Check Electrode Placement and Skin Prep

  • Ensure electrodes are placed correctly according to standard ECG positioning.
  • Confirm skin is clean and dry.
  • If needed, gently prep skin to remove oil or lotion.
  • Replace any dried, expired, or loose electrodes.

2. Inspect Lead Wires and Connections

  • Verify each lead wire is firmly snapped onto the electrode.
  • Check for loose, bent, or damaged connectors.
  • Inspect the patient cable for cracks, exposed wires, or strain damage.
  • Ensure the trunk cable is securely connected to the device.

3. Reduce Electrical Interference

  • Move ECG cables away from power cords and other equipment.
  • Ensure patient is not touching metal surfaces.
  • Minimize cable looping or tangling.

4. Confirm Proper Lead Selection

  • Verify the correct lead set configuration (3-lead, 5-lead, or 10-lead).
  • Ensure the selected mode matches the cable type in use.

5. Test with Known-Good Cable

  • Swap with a known-good patient cable if available.
  • Reconnect electrodes and reassess waveform quality.

6. Perform Simulator Test

  • Connect an ECG simulator.
  • Run a standard rhythm simulation.
  • If waveform is stable and clear, issue is likely external (electrodes or cable).
  • If simulator tracing is still noisy or leads not detected, suspect internal issue.

If the Issue Persists

If leads remain undetected or ECG remains noisy after completing the above checks, the issue may involve the patient cable assembly or internal acquisition board.

You have already:

  • verified electrode placement
  • confirmed cable integrity
  • reduced interference sources
  • tested with a simulator

The next step is to:

  • remove the unit from service
  • tag as Out of Service
  • send for further diagnostic evaluation

Work Order Documentation (CCR Method)

CCR = Complaint, Cause, Resolution

Complaint

Example: “Multiple leads not detected and ECG waveform very noisy.”

Cause

Example: “Loose/dried electrodes and intermittent patient cable connection causing lead-off detection and artifact.”

Resolution

Examples:
“Replaced electrodes and secured cable connections. Tested with simulator — unit operating normally.”
or
“Failed simulator test. Removed from service for further evaluation.”

Helpful Details to Include

  • Electrode condition
  • Cable condition
  • Simulator test result
  • Specific leads affected
  • Final status

Final Thought

ECG acquisition issues are frequently related to setup, electrode quality, or connection integrity. A systematic approach prevents unnecessary equipment replacement and protects patient care.

That is successful troubleshooting.
